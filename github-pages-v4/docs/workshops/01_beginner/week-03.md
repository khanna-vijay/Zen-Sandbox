## 2. 🎲 Today's games

#### Warm-up — Degrees of Connection

> Rapidly shift emotional intimacy levels with passing classmates to build vulnerability and spontaneous connection.

`Players 4+` · `~3 min` · `Complexity 1/5` · `Energy medium` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Emotional Fluidity · _connection_

**How to play**

1. Instruct all participants to walk around the room at a moderate pace, filling the empty spaces and maintaining soft eye contact with those they pass.
2. Announce Phase 1: As players pass each other, they must make brief eye contact and say a simple, neutral greeting like 'Hello.'
3. After about 30 seconds, call out Phase 2: Instruct players to upgrade their greeting to a warm, positive statement like 'You're great' or 'I'm glad you're here.'
4. Transition to Phase 3: Instruct players to look each other in the eye as they pass and say, with genuine intent, 'I love you.'
5. Transition to Phase 4: Instruct players to shift completely and say, with equal conviction but without physical aggression, 'I hate you.'
6. Transition to Phase 5: Bring the exercise back to a positive, vulnerable state by having players pass each other and say 'I love you' once more.
7. Call a freeze to end the exercise, allowing players to take a deep breath and shake off the emotional residue.

#### Core game — The Epic Mundane

> Transform the most boring details of your life into moments of absolute, earth-shattering triumph.

`Players 3+` · `~5 min` · `Complexity 1/5` · `Energy medium` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Emotional Fluidity · _connection_

**How to play**

1. Instruct the group to stand in a circle where everyone has clear sightlines to one another.
2. Explain that each player will take turns stepping forward to state their name and one completely uninteresting, mundane fact about themselves.
3. Challenge the active player to deliver this boring fact with their emotional dial cranked to a 10—expressing absolute, breathless excitement, joy, or triumph.
4. Begin with the first player, who might say, 'My name is Alex, and I bought some grey socks yesterday!' with the passion of someone who just won a lifetime achievement award.
5. Immediately upon the player finishing their statement, the entire circle must erupt into a massive, roaring standing ovation, cheering as if they just witnessed a historic miracle.
6. Once the applause peaks and naturally subsides, the next player in the circle steps up to deliver their name and boring fact with the same level of maximum commitment.
7. Continue clockwise around the circle until every participant has shared and received a stadium-level ovation.

## 3. 💭 Self-reflection

**Deepen your improv**
1. Which phrase felt the most difficult or vulnerable to say out loud, and why?
2. How did your physical posture or breathing change as we shifted between love and hate?

**Beyond the stage**
3. Think of a moment you 'performed calm' while feeling something else. What changes when you let a real emotion be visible — at home, or in a hard conversation?

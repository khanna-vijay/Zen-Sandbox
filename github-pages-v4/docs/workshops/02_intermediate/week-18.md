## 2. 🎲 Today's games

#### Warm-up — Streaming Queue

> Two viewers control the remote while an ensemble physically manifests their chaotic television screen.

`Players 4+` · `~5 min` · `Complexity 3/5` · `Energy high` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Support Work · _comedy game_

**How to play**

1. Two players sit on the chairs as the 'viewers' holding an imaginary remote control, while the rest of the players form the 'ensemble' offstage.
2. The host gets an initial suggestion from the audience (e.g., a bizarre movie title or a specific snack) to inspire the first program.
3. The viewers begin the scene by pantomiming eating snacks, chatting, and turning on the television to the suggested program.
4. As soon as a program is mentioned or selected, two or more ensemble members must immediately run into the space in front of the couch and physically perform that show.
5. The viewers actively comment on what they are watching, offering specific details (e.g., 'I love how this main character always whispers' or 'Look at that giant monster in the background!').
6. The performing ensemble must instantly incorporate the viewers' commentary into their physical actions and dialogue, treating every comment as an absolute truth.
7. The viewers can use the imaginary remote at any time to pause the action, mute the sound (requiring the actors to mouth words silently), fast-forward (requiring high-speed physical comedy), or change the channel entirely.
8. When the channel is changed, the current actors must quickly exit, and a new group from the ensemble must immediately jump in to establish the next show based on the viewers' new selection.

#### Core game — The Shared Ascent

> Navigate a high-stakes crisis together where every action must build upon the last.

`Players 4–6` · `~15 min` · `Complexity 3/5` · `Energy medium` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Narrative Architecture · _narrative_

**How to play**

1. Begin the scene in media res (in the middle of the action), with all characters already fully aware of the crisis and actively dealing with its immediate pressure.
2. The first player initiates by establishing a specific, immediate physical obstacle or a critical piece of information related to the overarching crisis.
3. Subsequent players must make moves that directly build upon the established reality, alternating between introducing new sub-obstacles, offering resources, or taking physical actions.
4. Enforce the 'No Solo Heroics' rule: no single character can unilaterally resolve a major problem or save the day. Any attempt to do so must be met by other characters complicating the action or pointing out why it requires the whole team.
5. Practice the 'Selfless Hand-Off' by ending every line or physical action with an open-ended challenge or a clear physical cue that invites another player to step in and build.
6. Maintain narrative tension by tracking the group's progress; every successful action should reveal a new, logical complication (the 'if true, what else is true' principle applied to danger).
7. Conclude the scene organically when the group either definitively achieves their super-objective or conclusively fails, resulting in a shared, dramatic climax.

#### Core game — More or Less

> Calibrate scene length and narrative momentum by letting the audience vote to continue or cut.

`Players 5+` · `~30 min` · `Complexity 3/5` · `Energy medium` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Room Reading · _mixed_

**How to play**

1. Designate one player to step forward as the first Director, who will pitch a basic scene premise or ask the audience for a simple suggestion.
2. The Director casts other players from the sidelines to perform the scene, setting up the initial stage picture and starting the action.
3. The cast performs the scene while the Director stands at the edge of the stage, actively observing both the stage action and the audience's reactions.
4. After approximately one to two minutes—ideally at a cliffhanger, a high-energy beat, or a moment of narrative tension—the Director freezes the scene.
5. The Director addresses the audience directly and asks: 'More or Less?'
6. The audience responds by shouting either 'More!' or 'Less!' based on their interest in seeing where the story goes.
7. The Director must read the room's volume and enthusiasm: if 'More' wins, the Director unfreezes the scene and lets it continue for another beat before asking again later.
8. If 'Less' wins (or if the energy is lukewarm), the Director immediately ends the scene, thanks the cast, and steps back to the sidelines.
9. A new player steps forward to become the next Director, initiating a completely new scene with a different cast and premise.

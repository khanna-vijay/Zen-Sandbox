# Somatic Echo

![Somatic Echo — infographic](img/D2_P3_S3_T1_G1328__the-magnet.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Replicate your partner's physical pose using only touch and spatial awareness with eyes closed.

## Overview
In this quiet, high-focus partner exercise, one player freezes in a dynamic pose while their partner closes their eyes. The closed-eyed player gently explores the sculpture's shape through touch, then attempts to mirror the exact pose with their own body. It is a deeply connecting experience that shifts players from analytical thinking to physical empathy.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop somatic empathy, non-verbal connection, and precise physical mirroring by relying on tactile feedback rather than visual cues.

## Setup
Clear the room of any obstacles to ensure safety. Divide the players into pairs and have them find a comfortable space to stand with some distance from other pairs.

## How to Play
1. Designate one player in each pair as Player A (the Sculpture) and the other as Player B (the Explorer).
2. Player A strikes a still, three-dimensional physical pose that is expressive but comfortable enough to hold for a couple of minutes.
3. Player B closes their eyes completely and stands directly in front of Player A.
4. Using gentle, respectful touch, Player B explores Player A's pose, mapping the angles of their limbs, the tilt of their head, and the distribution of their weight.
5. Once Player B feels they have a complete mental map of the pose, they step slightly away while keeping their eyes closed.
6. Player B then attempts to recreate the exact same pose with their own body, matching every detail they felt.
7. Once Player B is frozen in their replicated pose, they open their eyes to visually compare their shape with Player A's original pose.
8. Both players shake out their bodies, switch roles, and repeat the process.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Move slowly and focus on the micro-details—the angle of the wrists, the bend of the knees, the direction of the gaze.'
- Pitfall: Players rushing through the exploration phase. Fix: Encourage at least 30 to 45 seconds of quiet tactile mapping before attempting to replicate.
- Coaching cue: 'Focus on the negative space. What shapes are created between their limbs and the floor?'
- Pitfall: Discomfort with touch. Fix: Establish clear boundaries before starting; players can agree on 'no-touch zones' or use a non-contact variation.

## Variations
- The Thermal Map: Play without actual physical touch; the exploring player hovers their hands one to two inches away from the partner's body, sensing their form through heat and air pressure.
- Dynamic Echo: The sculpture slowly changes their pose over ten seconds while the closed-eyed partner maintains light contact, trying to mirror the movement in real-time.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to rely entirely on touch rather than sight to understand your partner's physical state?
- What did you notice about your own spatial awareness when you tried to recreate the pose?
- How does this level of physical attention translate to supporting your partner in an active, spoken improv scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
This game requires physical contact and closed eyes. Before beginning, facilitate a brief boundary-setting conversation within pairs. Players must explicitly agree on where touch is comfortable (e.g., shoulders, arms, back only) and may opt out of touch entirely by using the 'Thermal Map' variation. Ensure the floor is completely clear of tripping hazards.

## Why It Works
By removing the dominant sense of sight, players must slow down and connect deeply with their partner's physical reality. This builds somatic empathy, making players highly attuned to their partner's physical offers and presence on stage, embodying the principle of making their partner a genius through absolute focus.

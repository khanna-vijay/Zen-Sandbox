# Bunny Bunny

![Bunny Bunny — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G648__bunny-bunny.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy, multi-layered rhythmic warm-up that demands total group focus and peripheral awareness.

## Overview
Players stand in a circle and pass a rhythmic 'bunny' energy around, while neighbors and the rest of the group support with synchronized chants and movements. It creates a chaotic, joyful symphony of physical and vocal layers that requires everyone to stay highly attuned to their surroundings. The game builds a shared group mind where mistakes are celebrated as moments of collective play.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Timing exercises
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop peripheral awareness and thread-tracking skills by managing multiple simultaneous layers of rhythm, movement, and vocal cues within a group.

## Setup
Have all players stand in a wide, clear circle with enough space to move their arms and step side-to-side. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Stand in a circle and establish a steady, collective pulse by having everyone gently sway or bounce to a shared tempo.
2. Introduce the core 'Bunny' pass: One player starts as the focus, making 'bunny ears' with their fingers next to their head while chanting 'Bunny, bunny' to themselves, then pointing their hands like paws toward another player while chanting 'Bunny, bunny' again.
3. The receiving player immediately takes over the focus, repeating the pattern: 'Bunny, bunny' to themselves, then 'Bunny, bunny' to pass it to a new player.
4. Once the basic pass is fluid, add the flanking layer: Whenever a player has the focus, the two players standing directly to their left and right must turn to face them, waving their hands in the air and chanting 'Ticky-tocky, ticky-tocky' in time with the focus player's rhythm.
5. Add the final background layer: All other players in the circle who are not currently the focus or the flankers must continuously sway from side to side, tapping their knees and chanting 'Ooh-ah, ooh-ah' to keep the collective rhythm alive.
6. Keep the energy high and the tempo steady; if the rhythm breaks or someone misses a cue, the group should cheer, laugh, and immediately restart the pattern from whoever has the focus.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes wide! Use your peripheral vision to see if your neighbor suddenly gets the bunny.'
- Common pitfall: Players focus so hard on waiting for the 'bunny' that they stop doing the background 'Ooh-ah' chant. Fix: Remind the group that the background rhythm is the engine of the game; it must never stop.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Celebrate the drops! If the chain breaks, throw your hands up, laugh, and start it right back up without hesitation.'
- Encourage players to make clear, deliberate eye contact when passing the 'bunny' to avoid confusion about who the next focus player is.

## Variations
- Speed Run: Gradually increase the tempo of the background beat, forcing players to pass and react at high speeds.
- Silent Bunny: Play the entire game using only the physical gestures (ears, flanking waves, background swaying) without any vocal chants.
- Double Bunny: Introduce a second 'bunny' pass into the circle simultaneously, requiring players to track two moving focus points at once.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to track your neighbors' actions while maintaining your own background rhythm?
- What happened to our collective focus when we stopped worrying about making mistakes and just embraced the chaos?
- How does keeping a wide, peripheral view of the entire circle help us support each other in this game?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries when turning and gesturing toward their neighbors. Players with limited mobility can adapt the flanking jump/sway to smaller upper-body movements or simple head nods while maintaining the vocal chants.

## Why It Works
This game works because it forces players out of their analytical minds and into a state of pure presence. By requiring three distinct, simultaneous activities (the pass, the flank, and the background rhythm), players cannot overthink their next move; they must rely on peripheral awareness and group mind to keep the system running. It reframes mistakes as joyful group resets rather than individual failures.

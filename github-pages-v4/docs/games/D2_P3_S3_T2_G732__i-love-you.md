# I Love You

![I Love You — infographic](img/D2_P3_S3_T2_G732__i-love-you.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-stakes scene-work exercise where players deliver and receive a genuine declaration of love.

## Overview
Two players improvise a grounded, relationship-focused scene. At some point during the scene, one player must say 'I love you' to the other with absolute sincerity, and the partner must receive it and respond in kind. The scene explores the emotional ripples of this vulnerability rather than treating it as a punchline.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Vulnerability; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Emotional Fluidity; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Offer Reception; Stakes / The 'Want'
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Emotional-echo drills; Endowment-acceptance; Objective-laddering
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep emotional connection, vulnerability, and active listening. It trains players to focus on their partner's emotional state, mirror and echo emotional stakes, and elevate the relationship over plot or conflict.

## Setup
Two chairs placed center stage. The rest of the group acts as supportive observers. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Pair up two players and have them establish a simple, everyday relationship and a basic physical activity.
2. Instruct the players to begin a grounded scene, focusing on their connection and how they feel about each other in the present moment.
3. At some point during the scene, when the emotional stakes feel earned, one player must look their partner in the eye and say, 'I love you,' with genuine sincerity.
4. The receiving partner must take a moment to let the statement land, absorbing the emotional impact before responding honestly.
5. The receiving partner must then echo or validate the emotional reality to maintain the shared vulnerability.
6. The scene continues after the declaration, exploring how this confession changes the dynamic, the status, and the immediate future of the characters.
7. The facilitator calls 'scene' once the emotional truth of the moment has been fully explored and integrated into the relationship.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Slow down. Look at your partner. Let the words land.'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Avoid making it a joke. Play the sincere reality of this relationship.'
- Pitfall: Players might use 'I love you' as a sudden, absurd twist. Fix: Remind them that the love must be appropriate to the established relationship (familial, platonic, professional, or romantic) and played with high stakes.
- Pitfall: Deflecting the declaration with humor or immediate denial. Fix: Side-coach the receiver to breathe, accept the offer, and let it change them.

## Variations
- Non-Romantic Love: Explicitly assign non-romantic relationships (e.g., two soldiers in a trench, a mentor and student, estranged siblings) to practice different flavors of deep platonic love.
- The Silent Declaration: The players must convey 'I love you' entirely through subtext, eye contact, and physical mirroring, without ever speaking the actual words.
- Vulnerability Tag: A line of players. When the facilitator claps, the active player must immediately make a vulnerable, high-stakes emotional confession to their partner.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to say those words sincerely without hiding behind a comedic shield?
- For the receiver, what physical or emotional shifts occurred when you allowed yourself to fully hear and accept the declaration?
- How does establishing a strong emotional baseline change the way we handle conflict or plot in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Although marked as not consent-sensitive, expressing deep affection can sometimes trigger discomfort or boundary issues. Remind players that 'love' takes many forms (platonic, familial, professional respect) and they should choose a level of intimacy that feels safe and respectful for both partners. No unwanted physical contact should be initiated without consent.

## Why It Works
By forcing a high-stakes emotional declaration, the game bypasses intellectual plotting and forces players into the present moment. It relies on emotional-echoing and mirroring; when one player steps into vulnerability, the partner must match that vulnerability to keep the scene balanced, instantly elevating the scene's stakes and making both players look brilliant.

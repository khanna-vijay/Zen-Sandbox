# Instant Game Engine

![Instant Game Engine — infographic](img/D3_P0_S1_T1_G751__kick-the-can-marco.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Spontaneously invent and play absurd, unwritten games by committing to the unknown together.

## Overview
A high-energy group warm-up where players instantly agree to play a completely fictional game suggested on the fly. Without planning, the group physicalizes and executes the game, discovering its rules through collective action and immediate agreement.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Yes, And; Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Offer Reception; Game Identification; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Finding & Playing the Game; If this is true, what else is true?
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop the ability to identify, establish, and commit to a comedic or structural game in real-time, fostering group mind, unfiltered spontaneity, and rapid offer reception.

## Setup
Players stand in a clear, open room with enough space to move around safely. No props or materials are needed.

## How to Play
1. Gather the group in an open space, standing in a loose circle or spread out comfortably.
2. Explain that anyone can initiate by stepping forward and enthusiastically shouting, 'Let's play [insert nonsense/made-up game name]!'
3. Immediately, the entire group must shout 'Yeah!' with absolute enthusiasm, instantly committing to the suggestion.
4. Without pausing to discuss or plan, all players must immediately begin playing this fictional game, using physical movement, sound, and interaction.
5. Players must observe each other closely, treating the first physical actions or vocalizations as the 'rules' of the game, and building upon them.
6. Continue playing the high-energy game for 30 to 60 seconds, allowing the group mind to organically establish a clear pattern or climax.
7. The facilitator calls 'Freeze!' to end the round.
8. Ask the players to state the rules of the game they just played, articulating the patterns they discovered and established together.
9. Repeat the process with a new initiator suggesting a different made-up game.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't think, just move! Follow the first physical offer you see!'
- Pitfall: Players standing around trying to verbally negotiate the rules. Fix: Ban talking about the rules during play; all rules must be established through physical action and immediate mimicry.
- Side-coaching cue: 'If someone does something weird, make it a rule! Everyone copy or react to it!'
- Pitfall: Hesitation or half-hearted commitment. Fix: Emphasize that there are no wrong moves, only unadopted rules. The faster the commitment, the easier the game becomes.

## Variations
- Silent Play: The entire game must be played in complete silence, forcing players to rely entirely on visual cues and physical patterns.
- Two-Player Scene Transition: Transition this into a two-person scene where one player initiates an unusual behavior, and the other immediately treats it as a game with established rules.

## Debrief
- How did we know what the rules were without anyone explaining them?
- What happened when we committed 100% to a teammate's random physical choice?
- How does this apply to finding 'the game' of a scene in long-form improv?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles. Remind players to be mindful of physical boundaries and varying mobility levels when initiating high-energy physical games.

## Why It Works
It bypasses the analytical brain by demanding instant physical commitment. By forcing players to 'yes-and' physical offers immediately, it demonstrates how easily a 'game' (a repeatable, escalating pattern) can be discovered when everyone agrees to treat accidental choices as deliberate rules.

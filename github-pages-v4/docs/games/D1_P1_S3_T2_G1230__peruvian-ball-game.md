# The Traveling Sphere

![The Traveling Sphere — infographic](img/D1_P1_S3_T2_G1230__peruvian-ball-game.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Track, pass, and reclaim unique imaginary spheres in a chaotic, high-commitment physical exchange.

## Overview
Players create and physically define a highly specific imaginary ball, establishing its weight, size, and texture. They then move through the space, passing their ball to others and receiving new ones, before ultimately tracking down and reclaiming their original creation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Object work; Weight & resistance mime; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop absolute physical commitment to object work, sharpen peripheral awareness, and practice receiving and gifting physical offers with precision.

## Setup
An open room with plenty of space for 8-20 players to walk around safely. No physical props are required.

## How to Play
1. Have all players disperse and begin walking around the room at a moderate pace, maintaining awareness of the space and others.
2. Instruct each player to silently conjure an imaginary ball in their hands, defining its specific size, weight, texture, temperature, and material through physical manipulation.
3. Direct players to interact with their ball as they walk—tossing it, rolling it, or feeling its weight—to fully commit to its physical reality.
4. On the facilitator's cue ('Pass!'), each player must make eye contact with a nearby peer and carefully pass their ball to them, ensuring the receiver feels the weight and size of the incoming object.
5. The receiver must instantly adapt their physical posture and hand placement to match the specific properties of the ball they just caught.
6. Repeat this passing sequence several times on the facilitator's cues, so that balls travel across multiple players and everyone is holding a ball they did not start with.
7. On the final cue ('Retrieve!'), players must navigate the space to find the person currently holding their original ball and successfully trade back until everyone has their initial object.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Show us the weight! If it's a bowling ball, don't let your hands float. If it's a bubble, don't crush it!'
- Pitfall: Players pass the ball carelessly without making eye contact. Fix: Remind them that a pass is a gift; ensure the receiver is ready and looking before releasing the object.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Track your original ball with your eyes even as you handle the new ones. Keep your peripheral vision active!'
- Pitfall: The physical properties of the ball degrade or change during the handoff. Fix: Encourage the receiver to exaggerate the transition, adjusting their hands and body to the new weight immediately.

## Variations
- Sound Effects: Add a distinct, non-verbal sound effect to each ball that must be passed along with the physical object.
- Blind Handoffs: Players must pass the ball to someone behind them or without looking, relying entirely on physical touch and vocal cues to complete the transfer safely.
- Varying Speeds: Change the tempo of the room (slow motion to fast-forward) while maintaining the integrity of the ball's physical properties.

## Debrief
- How did your physical commitment change when you had to pass your object to someone else?
- What did it feel like to receive an object? How did you know what its weight and size were?
- How did you maintain awareness of your original ball while focusing on the one in your hands?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the walking space is clear of tripping hazards. Players with limited mobility can participate from a stationary position, passing objects to those who approach them. Remind players to make clear eye contact before throwing or passing to prevent unexpected physical contact.

## Why It Works
This game works because it forces players to externalize their imagination through physical resistance and spatial awareness. By passing the objects, players must practice 'making their partner a genius' by delivering clear, readable physical offers, while the receiver must practice 'offer reception' by instantly committing to the established reality of the object.

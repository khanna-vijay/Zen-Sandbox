# The Ripple Effect

![The Ripple Effect — infographic](img/D3_P4_S3_T0_G080__the-ripple-effect.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Transform subtle, unspoken physical and verbal offers into deep, deliberate narrative choices.

## Overview
The Ripple Effect is an analytical scene-work game where players pause the action upon noticing a micro-offer, such as a hesitation, physical twitch, or specific word choice. Players step out of the scene momentarily to analyze the subtext, declare its narrative implications, and plan their next move before resuming. This structured process turns implicit subtext into explicit, story-driving choices that deepen character relationships.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Game Identification; World-Building; Justification; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** If this is true, what else is true?; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Endowment-acceptance
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops advanced active listening and narrative architecture by training players to recognize, justify, and expand upon subtle, non-verbal, or implicit offers, moving from intuitive reaction to conscious story construction.

## Setup
A simple performance space. Two players stand ready to perform a scene. The remaining players sit as active observers (spotters). No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Two players begin a scene based on a simple relationship premise, such as two coworkers packing up an office.
2. The rest of the group acts as Spotters, watching closely for subtle, implicit, or non-verbal offers like a micro-hesitation, a shift in posture, or a specific word choice.
3. At any point, a Spotter or the facilitator calls out 'Ripple!' to freeze the scene immediately.
4. The person who called 'Ripple' points out the specific micro-offer they observed, stating: 'I noticed that you [describe the physical or verbal detail].'
5. The observing player then applies the 'If this is true, what else is true?' principle, stating: 'This implies that [interpret the subtext or hidden emotion].'
6. The player receiving the offer declares their next move: 'To create a ripple, I will respond by [state the concrete action or line that addresses this subtext].'
7. On the facilitator's cue, the scene unfreezes, and the receiving player immediately executes their declared response, continuing the scene with this new narrative layer.
8. Repeat this process three to four times throughout the scene to build a rich, layered narrative before bringing the scene to a natural close.

## Facilitation Notes
- Focus on the micro: Encourage spotters to look for tiny, involuntary-looking physical adjustments or vocal inflections rather than obvious, loud choices.
- Avoid the correctness trap: Remind players there is no single right interpretation of a subtle offer; any justified choice that serves the story is valid.
- Keep the pause brief: Ensure the discussion phase is snappy and analytical, preventing players from overthinking or losing their performance energy.
- Pitfall - Planning ahead: Players might wait for a specific cue to call 'Ripple' rather than reacting to what actually happens. Fix: Challenge spotters to call it on the very next physical movement.

## Variations
- Internal Spotting: The active players in the scene call 'Ripple' on themselves or their partner, eliminating the external spotter to build internal awareness.
- The Blind Ripple: The player who made the subtle offer secretly writes down their internal subtext beforehand, and the spotter tries to guess it through the ripple process.
- Silent Ripples: Run the entire scene in gibberish or silence, forcing players to rely entirely on physical micro-offers and body language.

## Debrief
- How did pausing to analyze subtle offers change the depth of your character's relationship?
- What was the difference between reacting to what was said versus reacting to how it was said?
- How does the 'If this is true, what else is true' framework help prevent scenes from stalling?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game focuses heavily on close observation of physical cues and body language, establish a boundary check beforehand. Ensure players are comfortable with close physical proximity and being closely observed, and allow players to opt out of physical touch or intense eye contact.

## Why It Works
By forcing a conscious pause, this game interrupts the habit of rushing past subtle offers to get to the next plot point. It operationalizes the 'If this is true, what else is true' principle, teaching players that the most compelling narrative architecture is built from the micro-details of character behavior rather than external, arbitrary plot events.

# Emotional Pulse

![Emotional Pulse — infographic](img/D1_P1_S2_T1_G448__spectrum-pulse.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Instantly shift emotional states and intensities on a scale of one to ten.

## Overview
A high-energy, rapid-fire drill where a single active player repeats a simple physical action or neutral phrase while the rest of the group dynamically alters their emotional state. By calling out new emotions or adjusting an intensity dial from 1 to 10, the group pushes the performer to stretch their expressive range. The result is a fast-paced workout that builds immediate physical, vocal, and emotional agility.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Vulnerability; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Emotional substitution; Projection & breath support; Vocal characterization
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop emotional fluidity and full physical commitment by practicing instant transitions between diverse emotional states and varying levels of intensity (1 to 10) without hesitation.

## Setup
An open, distraction-free space. Have the group stand in a wide semi-circle facing a designated performance area. No props are required. Before starting, run a brief physical and vocal warm-up (such as gentle stretching and humming) to prepare players for high-energy expression.

## How to Play
1. Select one player to step into the performance space as the Responder, and another to act as the Conductor (initially the facilitator).
2. The Conductor provides the Responder with a simple, neutral phrase (like 'I found this on the floor') or a basic physical action (like folding a piece of paper).
3. The Conductor assigns an initial emotion (such as 'Grief' or 'Ecstasy') and a starting intensity level on a scale of 1 to 10.
4. The Responder begins performing the action or repeating the phrase, fully committing to the assigned emotion and intensity using their entire body, facial expressions, and vocal dynamics.
5. The remaining players in the semi-circle act as the 'Pulse Gallery.' At any point, any gallery player can call out a new parameter: either a new emotion (like 'Jealousy!') or a new intensity number (like 'Eight!').
6. The Responder must instantly adapt to the new cue without pausing, dropping the previous state entirely or sliding their current emotion to the new numerical level.
7. To prevent chaotic overlapping cues, gallery players must wait at least three seconds between tags, allowing the Responder to fully land in each new state.
8. After 30 to 45 seconds of continuous shifting, the Conductor calls 'Freeze!' and the Responder holds their final pose for a moment of internal reflection.
9. Rotate roles so that multiple players get a turn in the center, introducing a new neutral phrase or action for each round.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the Responder to avoid intellectualizing the transition; encourage them to let the physical posture or vocal tone lead the emotional shift.
- If the gallery becomes chaotic, establish a physical hand-raise or a designated 'tagging' spot to regulate the flow of cues.
- Remind players that an intensity of '1' is not 'nothing'—it is a highly concentrated, deeply felt internal emotion with minimal outward expression, whereas a '10' is explosive and all-consuming.
- Pitfall: Responders often delay their reaction to find the 'perfect' choice. Fix: Coach them to 'fail joyfully' by making the very first physical impulse their truth, even if it feels messy.

## Variations
- Dual Input: Allow two gallery members to tag simultaneously—one calling a new emotion and the other calling a new intensity level (e.g., 'Fear!' and 'Two!').
- Justified Transitions: After the Conductor calls 'Freeze,' the Responder must deliver a single, improvised line that logically explains why their character just underwent those rapid emotional swings.
- Silent Pulse: Run the entire exercise in complete silence, relying solely on physical movement, facial expression, and breath to convey the shifting emotions and intensities.

## Debrief
- How did it feel physically to transition instantly from a high-intensity emotion to a low-intensity one?
- What did you notice about the relationship between your breath and your ability to access different emotional states?
- How can we use the concept of the 1-to-10 dial to bring more nuance to our regular scenic work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they can pass on any emotion called out if it triggers personal discomfort. Encourage participants to scale their physical movements to their own comfort and mobility levels, emphasizing that high intensity (a '10') can be expressed vocally or facially without requiring strenuous physical exertion.

## Why It Works
By separating emotional quality (the 'what') from emotional volume (the 'how much'), this game demystifies emotional access. The rapid-fire external cues bypass the analytical mind, forcing the performer to rely on immediate physical and vocal impulses. This builds a strong mind-body connection, training the actor to treat emotions as dynamic, physical choices rather than precious internal states.

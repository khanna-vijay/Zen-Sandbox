# The Four-Stage Scene

![The Four-Stage Scene — infographic](img/D3_P4_S3_T2_G760__location-relationship-escalate-end.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Four players enter one by one, each executing a specific narrative duty to build a scene.

## Overview
This exercise breaks down a scene's narrative arc into four distinct, sequential responsibilities handled by four different players. Players enter the stage one at a time, each focusing on a single structural element: establishing the physical platform, defining the relationship, introducing a tilt or escalation, and bringing the narrative to a satisfying resolution. The experience is highly collaborative, teaching players how to pass the narrative baton and trust their partners' contributions.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Base Reality First; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; World-Building; Heightening & Exploration; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); The 'ladder' (escalating beats); Walk-ons
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop a practical understanding of narrative architecture by isolating the key phases of a scene—specifically establishing a stable platform (base reality), introducing a tilt (escalation), and finding a logical resolution.

## Setup
Set up a performance space at the front of the room with a clear entrance and exit area. The remaining players sit as an active audience. Divide players into groups of four, or simply have players line up ready to step in. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign four players to a lineup and designate their specific roles in order: Player 1 (Platform), Player 2 (Relationship), Player 3 (Escalation), and Player 4 (Resolution).
2. Get a simple, non-narrative suggestion from the audience, such as an everyday activity or a mundane location, to inspire the opening.
3. Player 1 enters the stage alone and establishes a clear physical environment (the platform) using silent object work, physical activity, and self-talk, setting a grounded base reality.
4. Player 2 enters the scene to establish a clear relationship with Player 1, defining who they are to each other and establishing an emotional connection or shared history.
5. Player 3 enters the scene with the specific goal of introducing a 'tilt' or escalating an existing pattern, emotion, or stakes within the established reality.
6. Player 4 enters the scene to initiate the resolution, drawing on established details, reincorporating elements, or bringing a sense of closure to the narrative arc.
7. Once Player 4 has delivered their closing contribution and the scene reaches a natural pause, the facilitator calls 'scene' or 'cut.'
8. Rotate roles so that every player experiences different stages of the narrative arc in subsequent rounds.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach Player 1 to focus on physical environment: 'Show us where we are before you speak. Use your body to define the space.'
- Side-coach Player 2 to focus on connection: 'How do you feel about Player 1? Establish the connection immediately to avoid transactional scenes.'
- Watch out for Player 3 introducing a completely unrelated conflict; remind them to escalate what is already there rather than inventing a brand-new problem.
- Watch out for Player 4 trying to start a new storyline; remind them that their job is to serve the story by bringing it to a landing, often by reincorporating a previously mentioned detail.

## Variations
- Two-Player Compression: Run the same four steps but with only two players; Player 1 does steps 1 and 3, and Player 2 does steps 2 and 4.
- Blind Entrances: Players 3 and 4 wait outside the room so they must read the stage instantly upon entering without knowing the setup.
- Variable Order: Allow players to enter when they feel the narrative needs their specific step, rather than in a strict, pre-determined order.

## Debrief
- How did isolating these four narrative steps change your focus when entering a scene?
- For Player 3, how did you distinguish between introducing a 'tilt' that serves the story versus one that derails it?
- For Player 4, what clues in the scene helped you find a satisfying way to bring the story to a close?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical space during entrances, especially when entering an active scene. Encourage players to verbally or physically check in if they plan to make sudden physical contact during their entrance, keeping the stage safe and accessible for all physical abilities.

## Why It Works
By dividing the narrative arc into discrete, sequential tasks, this game removes the pressure of having to build a whole story alone. It forces players to practice 'Base Reality First' before introducing a 'Tilt,' demonstrating that a strong platform makes escalation and resolution natural and effortless.

# The Consent Compass

![The Consent Compass — infographic](img/D2_P1_S6_T1_G463__the-consent-compass.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pause, check in, and navigate physical and emotional boundaries collaboratively before taking the leap.

## Overview
A structured scene-work drill where players use a physical freeze signal to pause action and explicitly negotiate upcoming physical contact or high-intensity choices. By utilizing a clear, four-tiered response system, players practice setting real-time boundaries without breaking the creative momentum of the workshop. It establishes a shared vocabulary that prioritizes player safety and authentic choice over the pressure to blindly "yes-and" every offer.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Consent & Boundaries; Truth Over Pandering
- **Skill(s):** Boundary Navigation; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Check-ins; Cut calls; Negotiating physical contact
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop proactive boundary navigation and explicit check-in techniques, ensuring players can confidently negotiate physical touch and emotional intensity while upholding the principle of truth over pandering.

## Setup
A moderate open space. Two players stand in the performance area, while one facilitator (acting as the "Compass Keeper") stands nearby to monitor. The rest of the group observes as active listeners. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Designate two active players to step into the scene space, and one facilitator or experienced player to act as the Compass Keeper.
2. Begin a standard, character-driven scene, establishing a clear relationship and environment without rushing into high-stakes conflict.
3. Before initiating any physical contact, escalating emotional intensity (like shouting), or introducing sensitive narrative topics, a player must pause the scene by placing an open hand flat on their own chest.
4. This hand-on-chest gesture instantly freezes the scene in place, signaling a temporary out-of-character check-in.
5. The initiating player verbally states their intended action and asks their partner how they feel about it (e.g., "I want to grab your hand in frustration, how does that feel?").
6. The receiving partner must respond honestly with one of four explicit readings: "Full Sail" (enthusiastic yes), "Proceed with Caution" (yes, with specific modifications), "Shifting Winds" (negotiating an alternative action), or "Storm Ahead" (an absolute, non-negotiable no).
7. If "Storm Ahead" is called, the initiator must immediately accept the boundary without question, and both players prepare to pivot the narrative in a different direction.
8. Once the boundary is negotiated and agreed upon, the Compass Keeper calls "Play On" to unfreeze the scene, and the players immediately integrate the agreed-upon boundaries into their action.
9. At any point, any player or the Compass Keeper can call "Cut!" to immediately halt the entire exercise if a boundary is crossed or if anyone feels genuinely unsafe.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: Remind players that "Storm Ahead" is not a failure of the scene; it is a successful calibration of safety that often leads to more creative, unexpected narrative choices.
- Common Pitfall: Players rushing through the check-in or giving a polite "Full Sail" when they actually want to set a boundary. Fix: Pause the scene and ask the receiver to take a breath and answer based on their actual comfort level, reinforcing "Truth Over Pandering".
- Ensure the hand-on-chest gesture is clean and visible. If a player begins a physical action without signaling, the Compass Keeper should gently call "Freeze" and prompt them to use the Compass Point.
- Encourage specific, actionable modifications during "Proceed with Caution" (e.g., "You can grab my shoulder, but please don't squeeze tightly").
- Keep the out-of-character check-ins brief and functional to maintain the energy of the scene while ensuring absolute clarity.

## Variations
- Silent Compass: For advanced players, run the check-in using only eye contact and micro-gestures, pausing the scene briefly to establish non-verbal consent before physical contact.
- Emotional Dial: Focus the exercise entirely on emotional escalation rather than physical touch, practicing checking in before raising voices, crying, or invading personal space.
- The Director's Compass: The Compass Keeper can proactively call "Compass Point" if they observe a player looking hesitant or if they anticipate a high-stakes physical choice, guiding them through the check-in process.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to explicitly pause the scene to ask for consent? Did it disrupt your flow, or did it give you more confidence to play boldly?
- For those who set boundaries (using "Storm Ahead" or "Shifting Winds"), did you feel any internal pressure to pander to your partner's offer? How did you overcome it?
- How did the initiator adapt when their original physical or emotional offer was modified or declined?
- How can we carry this proactive negotiation mindset into regular, unstructured scenes without needing the formal freeze mechanic?

## Safety & Inclusion
This exercise is highly safety-sensitive. It is designed to establish physical and emotional agency. Facilitators must emphasize that a 'Storm Ahead' (no) requires zero justification and must be met with immediate, positive compliance. Ensure all participants understand that they have absolute control over their own bodies and can call 'Cut!' at any moment without penalty or judgment.

## Why It Works
By formalizing the check-in process, this game removes the social awkwardness of boundary setting in creative spaces. It replaces implicit assumptions with explicit, structured communication, teaching players that respecting boundaries does not kill a scene, but rather builds the trust necessary for deeper, more authentic play.

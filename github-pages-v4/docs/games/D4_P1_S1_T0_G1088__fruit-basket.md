# Circle Swap

![Circle Swap — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T0_G1088__fruit-basket.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy physical warm-up that builds rapid group connection and sharp peripheral awareness.

## Overview
Players sit in a circle with one person standing in the center. The center player shares a personal truth, prompting everyone to whom it applies to scramble for a new seat, leaving a new player in the center. It is a fast-paced, joyful exercise in finding commonalities and moving with spontaneous urgency.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop peripheral awareness, physical responsiveness, and group mind by recognizing shared traits and navigating a crowded space safely and quickly.

## Setup
Arrange chairs in a tight circle facing inward. There must be exactly one fewer chair than the total number of players. One player starts standing in the center of the circle. Ensure the space inside and around the circle is clear of tripping hazards.

## How to Play
1. Gather all players into a seated circle, with one player standing in the center.
2. Explain that the center player will make a statement starting with 'The wind blows for anyone who...' followed by a true characteristic or experience.
3. Instruct all seated players for whom the statement is true to immediately stand up and find a vacant chair.
4. Clarify that players cannot move to a chair immediately adjacent to their current one; they must cross the circle.
5. The center player also attempts to claim one of the vacated chairs.
6. The player left standing without a chair becomes the new center player and initiates the next round with a new statement.
7. Introduce the chaos command: if the center player calls out 'Tempest!', every single player must stand up and find a new seat, regardless of the statement.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Move on your first impulse—don't overthink whether the statement applies to you!'
- Pitfall: Players sliding into adjacent seats to avoid running. Fix: Enforce the rule that you must move at least two seats away from your original spot.
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes up and use your peripheral vision to avoid collisions in the center.'
- Pitfall: Hesitation or over-filtering what to say in the center. Fix: Encourage the center player to say the very first true thing that comes to mind, no matter how simple.

## Variations
- Silent Swap: Play the entire game in complete silence, using only eye contact and physical gestures to indicate who is moving.
- Character Swap: Players must move and sit in the physical posture of a specific character archetype or emotion dictated by the center player.
- No Chairs: Use spots on the floor like tape or poly spots instead of chairs to reduce physical impact and increase safety.

## Debrief
- How did your physical awareness change as the speed of the game increased?
- What did you notice about how quickly you made the decision to move or stay?
- How does this game help us tap into Group Mind and shared experiences?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure chairs are sturdy and won't slide easily. Remind players to avoid physical tackling or shoving; the goal is to find an open seat, not to fight over one. Offer a fast-walking modification for players with mobility limitations to ensure equal participation.

## Why It Works
By forcing rapid physical relocation based on shared traits, the game bypasses the analytical brain and demands high spatial awareness to avoid collisions. The shared vulnerability of admitting commonalities builds instant ensemble connection.

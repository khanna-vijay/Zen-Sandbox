# Physical Effort Explorations

![Physical Effort Explorations — infographic](img/D1_P1_S3_T1_G1060__fast-food-laban.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Transform your physical presence and unlock instant characters using three simple axes of movement.

## Overview
This physical skill drill guides players through a simplified framework of movement dynamics, exploring variations in weight, timing, and spatial focus. By combining these physical elements, players instantly bypass intellectual planning to discover bold, committed characters rooted entirely in their bodies.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop full physical commitment and character generation through body centers and movement qualities, training players to show character traits rather than explain them.

## Setup
An open, clear room where players can safely walk around without colliding. No props or chairs are needed.

## How to Play
1. Gather the players in the open space and have them begin walking at a neutral, natural pace, filling the room evenly.
2. Introduce the first physical axis: Weight. Instruct players to explore moving with extreme Heavy weight, feeling gravity and sinking, and then shift to extreme Light weight, floating and stepping softly.
3. Introduce the second axis: Timing. Have players alternate between Sudden movement, which is quick and explosive, and Sustained movement, which is slow and continuous.
4. Introduce the third axis: Space. Guide players to move with Direct focus, walking in laser-pointed straight lines, and then Indirect focus, meandering in wavy, multi-directional paths.
5. Combine these axes to create specific movement qualities. For example, call out Sudden, Heavy, and Direct and have players move around the space embodying this punchy combination.
6. Once players are moving with a specific combination, prompt them to add a simple vocal sound or repetitive phrase that matches this physical quality.
7. Instruct players to let this combination of movement and voice settle into a distinct character, prompting them to interact briefly with others they pass in the space while maintaining their physical commitment.
8. Cycle through other combinations, such as Sustained, Light, and Indirect for a floating quality, to let players experience a wide spectrum of character archetypes.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: Lead from a different part of your body. If you are heavy, where is that weight centered? Your hips? Your forehead?
- Common Pitfall: Players intellectualize the combinations instead of feeling them. Fix: Encourage them to exaggerate the physical movement first, letting the mind catch up later.
- Side-coaching cue: Let the voice match the body. If your movement is sudden and light, your speech should not be slow and heavy.
- Ensure players do not make physical contact during high-energy combinations; emphasize that these are movement qualities, not literal combat.

## Variations
- Soundtrack Shift: Play different genres of music and ask players to identify which combination of Weight, Timing, and Space the music inspires, then move accordingly.
- The Dial: Have players find a character combination and then dial the intensity from 1 (barely noticeable) to 10 (highly theatrical and clown-like).
- Two-Person Scenes: Pair players up, assign each a different physical combination, and have them sit or stand to play a brief, grounded scene using their physical centers.

## Debrief
- Which combination felt the most natural to you, and which felt the most challenging or foreign?
- How did changing your physical weight or timing automatically change your emotional state or status?
- How does starting a character from a physical sensation differ from starting a character with a mental concept or accent?

## Safety & Inclusion
Encourage players to adapt all physical prompts to their own comfort and mobility levels. Heavy does not require stomping or straining joints; it can be represented by internal focus, posture, or intention. Ensure pathways remain clear to prevent collisions.

## Why It Works
By focusing on concrete physical variables like weight, time, and space, players bypass the analytical brain that worries about being funny or doing it right. The physical commitment naturally generates emotional states and vocal patterns, proving that character is an inside-out process driven by external physical choices.

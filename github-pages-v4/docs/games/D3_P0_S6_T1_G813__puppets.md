# Human Puppets

![Human Puppets — infographic](img/D3_P0_S6_T1_G813__puppets.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Actors speak but rely entirely on physical puppeteers to move their bodies.

## Overview
Two actors perform a standard scene but are physically paralyzed from the neck down. Two other players act as their puppeteers, physically manipulating the actors' limbs and bodies to create movement, forcing the actors to verbally justify every unexpected posture.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Endowment-acceptance
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops advanced justification, active listening, and physical offer reception by forcing players to find logical reasons for absurd, involuntary movements.

## Setup
Four players stand in a clear, moderate-sized performance space. Divide them into two pairs: each pair consists of one 'Puppet' and one 'Puppeteer.' No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign one Puppet and one Puppeteer to each of the two teams.
2. The Puppets stand in the performance space, remaining completely limp and static, unable to move any part of their bodies except their eyes and mouths.
3. The Puppeteers stand directly behind or beside their respective Puppets, ready to physically guide their arms, legs, and torsos.
4. To make a Puppet take a step, the Puppeteer gently taps the back of the Puppet's knee to bend it, then guides the foot forward.
5. The Puppets begin a dialogue-based scene based on a simple suggestion, speaking normally but remaining physically frozen until moved.
6. The Puppeteers actively listen to the dialogue and move their Puppets to match the context, or intentionally create awkward, challenging physical poses.
7. The Puppets must immediately justify their new physical positions through their dialogue, treating every movement as a deliberate, character-driven choice.
8. The scene continues as a continuous feedback loop where dialogue inspires physical movement, and physical movement inspires dialogue.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the Puppets to remain completely limp: 'Let your partner do all the physical work, even if you start to lean.'
- Encourage Puppeteers to listen to the scene: 'If your puppet says they are holding a glass, physically shape their hand to hold it.'
- Remind Puppets to justify the absurd: 'If your arm is suddenly raised high, explain why your character is reaching for the ceiling.'
- Watch out for Puppeteers being too passive; prompt them to make bold physical choices that challenge the actors.

## Variations
- Blind Puppeteers: The puppeteers close their eyes, moving the puppets completely at random without visual context.
- Crossed Puppeteers: Puppeteer A controls Puppet B, while Puppeteer B controls Puppet A, decoupling physical control from the immediate dialogue partner.
- Audience Puppeteers: Invite two willing audience members to act as the puppeteers, guiding them on how to safely move the actors.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your physical choices completely controlled by someone else?
- What strategies did you use to instantly justify an unexpected or awkward physical position?
- How did this game force you to pay closer attention to your partner's physical state?

## Safety & Inclusion
Establish clear physical boundaries before starting. Puppeteers must only touch non-sensitive areas (arms, shoulders, outer legs, back of the knees) and must use gentle, supportive force. Puppets should communicate any physical limitations or injury-related boundaries before play begins.

## Why It Works
By separating physical action from verbal intent, this game forces the brain to work in reverse. Instead of planning a movement to match a thought, the actor must invent a thought to match a movement, building high-level justification skills and deep commitment to physical reality.

# Infinite Eights

![Infinite Eights — infographic](img/D1_P1_S3_T0_G1041__eights.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Warm up your body and mind by tracing infinity symbols with every limb.

## Overview
Infinite Eights is a physical isolation warm-up that gets players out of their heads and into their bodies. By systematically tracing the figure-eight pattern with different body parts, players build physical awareness, coordination, and a sense of playful commitment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop physical presence, body isolation, and uninhibited commitment to physical movement, preparing players to use their entire physical instrument in scenes.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle or scattered throughout the room, ensuring they have at least one arm's length of space in all directions. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Have players stand comfortably with their feet shoulder-width apart and take a deep breath to center themselves.
2. Direct players to lift their left foot and begin tracing a horizontal figure-eight (infinity symbol) in the air with their big toe, then expanding the movement to the whole foot, and finally the entire leg.
3. Switch to the right side, repeating the progression from the right big toe, to the foot, to the entire right leg.
4. Move to the upper body, starting with the left index finger, expanding to the hand, the lower arm, and then the entire left arm.
5. Repeat the upper body sequence on the right side, starting with the right index finger and building to the full arm.
6. Challenge players to trace eights with both arms simultaneously, first in the same direction, then in opposite directions.
7. Guide players to isolate their pelvis, tracing horizontal and then vertical figure-eights, experimenting with changing directions.
8. Move the focus to the shoulders (left, right, then both) and then gently to the head, tracing small, controlled eights.
9. Conclude by asking everyone to trace eights with as many body parts as possible at the same time, resulting in a chaotic, full-body wiggle.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Keep the movement fluid and continuous—don't let the infinity loop break.'
- If players struggle with balance during the leg portion, encourage them to touch a wall or keep their heel lightly on the ground.
- Coaching cue: 'If you lose your balance or feel silly, laugh it off, reset, and commit right back to the movement.'
- Address self-consciousness during the pelvic or full-body segments by leading with high energy and zero judgment, reminding players that looking silly together builds trust.

## Variations
- Vocal Eights: Add a vocal slide (siren) that rises and falls in pitch matching the loops of the physical eights.
- Partner Mirroring: Play in pairs where one partner leads the 'eight' movement and the other mirrors it, switching leaders on a clap.
- Space Travelers: Players move around the room while tracing eights, letting the physical loops dictate their path through the space.

## Debrief
- How did isolating different parts of your body change your physical awareness?
- What did it feel like when we transitioned from small, controlled movements to the chaotic full-body eights?
- How does committing fully to a silly physical warm-up help prepare us for scene work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Remind players to honor their bodies' limits, especially with neck, back, or hip movements. Encourage modifications, such as keeping feet on the floor and tracing with knees, or sitting in a chair, to accommodate balance or mobility needs.

## Why It Works
By focusing on a specific, geometric physical task (drawing an eight), the analytical mind is occupied, allowing the physical body to loosen up. The progression from micro-movements to macro-movements builds a sense of physical agency and lowers inhibitions through shared, low-stakes vulnerability.

# The Hero's Chronicler

![The Hero's Chronicler — infographic](img/D2_P3_S5_T1_G560__my-partner-the-hero.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Elevate your partner to legendary status while practicing the art of fearless, high-status acceptance.

## Overview
A two-player narrative drill where one player acts as an adoring chronicler who relentlessly endows their partner with extraordinary skills, intelligence, and heroic achievements. The other player steps fully into this high-status spotlight, instantly accepting and embodying these brilliant traits without hesitation or false modesty. Together, they build a scene where one partner is systematically made to look like an absolute genius.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Assume Competence
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Status Modulation; Offer Reception; Active Gifting
- **Technique(s):** Status Seesaw; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Give them the answer
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop active gifting and high-status offer reception by training players to enthusiastically endow their partners with competence, and to help players overcome the instinct to deflect praise or downplay their character's capabilities.

## Setup
Players split into pairs and find a comfortable space in the room. No props or special staging are required. The facilitator explains the two roles: the Chronicler (the supportive champion) and the Hero (the celebrated genius).

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have them stand or sit facing each other.
2. Assign one player in each pair to start as the Chronicler and the other as the Hero.
3. Instruct the pair to quickly establish a simple, neutral location for their scene, such as a laboratory, a kitchen, or an archaeological dig site.
4. Begin the scene with the Chronicler delivering a high-stakes, positive endowment that establishes the Hero as an absolute master of a specific skill or task related to the setting.
5. Direct the Hero to immediately accept this endowment as absolute truth, embodying the high-status capability through confident physical choices and dialogue.
6. Have the Chronicler continuously narrate, praise, and frame every action or word from the Hero as a stroke of pure genius, acting as their ultimate hype-person.
7. Instruct the Hero to lean into this praise, performing the tasks with competence and treating their own brilliance as completely normal and expected.
8. After approximately four minutes, call out 'Switch!' so the players instantly swap roles and begin a new scene with the new Chronicler endowing the new Hero.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Don't shrug it off!' If the Hero deflects praise with false modesty, pause the scene and remind them that in this space, they are as brilliant as their partner says they are.
- Pitfall: The Chronicler 'pimps' the Hero by demanding they perform complex physical feats (e.g., 'Do a backflip!'). Fix: Remind Chroniclers to gift competence and status rather than demanding physical tricks; focus on intellectual, artistic, or professional genius.
- Coaching Cue: 'Lower your status to raise theirs.' Encourage Chroniclers to adopt a supportive, admiring posture to naturally elevate the Hero's presence.
- Pitfall: The scene stalls because there is no conflict. Fix: Remind players that the focus is a skill drill, not a dramatic narrative. The 'action' of the scene is the demonstration of the Hero's expertise and the Chronicler's celebration of it.

## Variations
- The Silent Genius: The Hero is entirely silent, communicating only through confident, high-status physical choices and object work, while the Chronicler translates and praises every movement.
- The Reluctant Sidekick: The Chronicler is actually a rival who is forced to admit the Hero's undeniable genius, adding a layer of subtext to the praise.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your partner relentlessly build you up, and did you feel an urge to deflect or downplay the praise?
- As the Chronicler, how did focusing entirely on making your partner look like a genius change your listening habits?
- What physical and vocal shifts did you make to project the high status of a celebrated expert?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players understand they can set boundaries on the types of genius or traits endowed. If a player is uncomfortable with a specific endowment (e.g., romantic charm or physical appearance), they can instantly pivot the focus to a professional or intellectual skill without breaking the flow of the scene.

## Why It Works
By structurally separating the roles of 'giver' and 'receiver,' this drill bypasses the social conditioning of modesty. The Chronicler is forced to practice active gifting, while the Hero is forced to practice radical offer reception. This builds trust, establishes a healthy status dynamic, and reinforces the core improv principle of making your partner look like a genius.

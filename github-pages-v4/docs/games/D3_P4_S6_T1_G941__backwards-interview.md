# Reverse Interview

![Reverse Interview — infographic](img/D3_P4_S6_T1_G941__backwards-interview.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Conduct a talk show in reverse, justifying answers before the questions are even asked.

## Overview
Two players perform a talk-show style interview starting from the final sign-off and working backward to the opening introduction. Players must use reverse-chronological logic, meaning every line spoken must serve as the logical cause or setup for the line that was just delivered.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Narrative Architecture; Active Listening; Active Gifting
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Last Word Response; Endowment-gifting drills
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops advanced justification, active listening, and narrative architecture by forcing players to find logical setups for pre-existing conclusions.

## Setup
Two chairs are placed on stage facing slightly inward toward each other in a talk-show layout. No props are needed. The facilitator obtains a suggestion for a bizarre area of expertise or a unique profession for the guest.

## How to Play
1. Position two players on stage: one as the interviewer and one as the expert guest.
2. Begin the scene at the very end of the interview. The interviewer delivers a standard closing sign-off based on the suggestion.
3. The guest must then deliver the line that immediately preceded that sign-off, which must logically lead to the interviewer's closing statement.
4. Play continues backward with the interviewer asking a question that would logically prompt the answer the guest just gave.
5. Incorporate physical and emotional gifts by referencing how a line was delivered, forcing the other player to retroactively justify that behavior in their next chronologically prior line.
6. The scene concludes when the players successfully reach the opening introduction of the show.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to focus on cause and effect in reverse. If an effect is established, the next line must be the cause.
- Watch out for players trying to tell the story forward. Remind them that every line must answer the question: What happened immediately before this?
- Encourage active gifting. If the interviewer says, 'Thank you for crying through that explanation,' the guest's preceding line must be delivered with tears.
- Keep the pace deliberate. Because this requires high cognitive load, allow players a moment to process the reverse logic before speaking.

## Variations
- Reverse Scene: Apply the same reverse-chronological mechanic to a standard relationship-based scene rather than an interview format.
- Physical Reverse: Players must also reverse their physical actions, performing movements backward alongside the dialogue.

## Debrief
- How did reversing the chronology change how you listened to your partner's offers?
- What strategies did you use to justify an absurd statement or emotional state that was forced upon you?
- How does this game illustrate the concept of working backward to find a satisfying narrative setup?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical offers or emotional states gifted to partners remain within comfortable boundaries. Players can decline or modify high-intensity emotional gifts if they feel unsafe.

## Why It Works
By reversing the timeline, players cannot rely on pre-planned narrative arcs. They must practice deep active listening to dissect the exact implications of their partner's line, then use justification to construct a plausible past that makes the present moment make sense.

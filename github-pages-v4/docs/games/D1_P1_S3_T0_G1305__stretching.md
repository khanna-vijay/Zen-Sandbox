# Pass the Stretch

![Pass the Stretch — infographic](img/D1_P1_S3_T0_G1305__stretching.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A physical follow-the-leader warm-up that builds group connection and full-body commitment.

## Overview
In this physical warm-up, players stand in a circle and take turns leading a physical stretch. Every participant mirrors the leader's movement with absolute commitment, shifting the leadership seamlessly around the circle to engage the entire body and build group focus.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop physical commitment, active mirroring, and peripheral awareness by sharing leadership and fully embodying physical movements.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle with enough space to stretch their arms and legs comfortably without making accidental contact. No props or materials are needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle with comfortable spacing between all participants.
2. Establish a silent, focused atmosphere to prepare the group for physical mirroring.
3. The facilitator or a designated starting player initiates a simple physical stretch, such as reaching for the ceiling or rolling the shoulders.
4. Every other player in the circle immediately mirrors the stretch, matching the leader's intensity, speed, and physical form as closely as possible.
5. After holding the stretch for a few seconds, the leader passes the leadership to the person next to them in the circle.
6. The next player introduces a new stretch targeting a different part of the body, which the entire group immediately mirrors.
7. Continue passing the leadership sequentially around the circle until every player has led a stretch.
8. Encourage players to explore unconventional or playful stretches, such as stretching the face, jaw, or fingers, to increase physical commitment.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Commit 100% to the exact shape and energy of the leader, matching their physical tension.'
- Coaching cue: 'Move as one cohesive organism. Try to eliminate the delay between the leader's movement and your reaction.'
- Pitfall: Players might rush through their turn or offer low-energy stretches. Fix: Encourage leaders to hold their stretch for at least three deep breaths before passing.
- Pitfall: Players might attempt physically unsafe or overly strenuous stretches. Fix: Remind the group that the goal is connection and warm-up, not extreme flexibility.

## Variations
- Organic Passing: Instead of going in order around the circle, leadership is passed across the circle using clear eye contact and a physical nod.
- Sound and Movement: The leader accompanies their stretch with a vocal sound or sigh, which the group must mirror along with the physical movement.
- Abstract Shapes: Move away from traditional athletic stretches and lead the group in abstract, expressive physical postures and emotional gestures.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to fully commit to someone else's physical movement without hesitation?
- What did you notice about the group's focus when we all moved in perfect unison?
- How does committing 100% physically prepare us for committing to choices in an improv scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Remind players to honor their own physical boundaries and modify any stretch to suit their personal mobility levels. No one should feel pressured to perform a movement that causes pain or discomfort.

## Why It Works
By physically mirroring another person, players bypass intellectual overthinking and enter a state of active, empathetic connection. The 'follow the follower' dynamic builds a shared group mind, while the physical commitment primes the body and voice for active space work.

# Secret Currents

![Secret Currents — infographic](img/D2_P2_S4_T1_G361__the-panopticon-narrative-shared-secrets-shifting-influences.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Co-create a rich digital narrative shaped by shared imagery and hidden, shifting storytelling directives.

## Overview
In this virtual, turn-based storytelling game, players collaboratively build a cohesive narrative one sentence at a time. Guided by a sequence of shared visual backdrops, players must also secretly integrate private narrative constraints. The result is a layered, unpredictable story where players must balance visible narrative offers with invisible, shifting influences.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Narrative Architecture; World-Building; Thematic Synthesis
- **Technique(s):** Yes, And… sentence games; Story Spine; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop advanced offer reception and 'Yes, And' skills by requiring players to simultaneously synthesize multiple layers of input—the previous line, a shared visual environment, and a private, secret prompt—into a single, seamless narrative contribution.

## Setup
Players join a video conferencing platform in gallery view with screen-sharing and chat enabled. Establish a clear speaking order. The facilitator prepares 3 to 5 evocative, open-ended images to share. The facilitator also prepares three distinct prompt banks. Bank 1 (Internal Quirks): 1. Deeply suspicious of others, 2. Obsessed with a lost item, 3. Overly formal, 4. Seeking constant approval. Bank 2 (Sensory Details): 1. Smell of ozone, 2. Distant rhythmic ticking, 3. Sudden drop in temperature, 4. Rough, gritty texture. Bank 3 (Action Imperatives): 1. Urge everyone to hurry, 2. Inspect something closely, 3. Shift physical posture, 4. Whisper the line.

## How to Play
1. Step 1: The facilitator posts the three pre-made prompt banks into the main chat so all players have access to them throughout the game.
2. Step 2: Establish a fixed speaking order for all players to ensure smooth transitions and eliminate verbal overlap in the virtual space.
3. Step 3: The facilitator shares their screen to display the first evocative image, establishing the initial setting of the story.
4. Step 4: For the first round, all players secretly select one prompt of their choice from Bank 1 (Internal Quirks) without revealing it to anyone else.
5. Step 5: In the established order, each player contributes exactly one sentence to the story, seamlessly weaving together the previous player's line, the shared image, and their chosen secret prompt.
6. Step 6: Players must not explicitly state their secret prompt; instead, they must let the prompt naturally color their character's perspective, physical behavior, or dialogue.
7. Step 7: Once the rotation is complete, the facilitator shares the next image and announces the next bank (Bank 2 for Round 2, Bank 3 for Round 3), allowing players to secretly select a new prompt from that bank.
8. Step 8: Continue this cycle through 3 rounds, wrapping up the narrative on the final image with a collaborative resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- To eliminate pacing lag, use the round-based bank selection method instead of privately messaging individual prompts to players mid-game.
- If a player explicitly states their prompt, gently side-coach them to 'show, not tell' by letting the secret influence manifest in their tone or descriptive choices.
- Remind players to take a breath and fully absorb the previous line before speaking, using the virtual format's natural pause to synthesize their inputs.
- If players focus too hard on their secret prompt and ignore the previous speaker, remind them that the 'Yes, And' connection to the prior line is the absolute priority.

## Variations
- Exhaustion Mode: Once a player uses a specific prompt from any bank, they must cross it off and cannot use it again for the rest of the game.
- The Detective Variant: At the end of each round, players have 30 seconds to guess which specific prompts their peers chose based on their spoken sentences.
- Silent Echoes: Non-speaking players can use the chat to add silent, atmospheric sound effects or environmental descriptions that match the current image.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to balance three distinct sources of inspiration—the previous line, the visual, and your secret prompt—at the same time?
- What strategies did you use to make your secret prompt feel like a natural extension of the story rather than a forced interruption?
- When a partner's sentence took an unexpected turn due to their secret prompt, how did you justify and accept that shift in your own turn?
- How does this game mirror the real-world improv challenge of balancing your own internal ideas with the external offers of your partner?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the prompt banks do not contain themes of physical violation, extreme phobias, or non-consensual touch. Establish a 'skip' protocol where any player can instantly choose an alternative prompt from the bank if their selected one feels uncomfortable or inaccessible.

## Why It Works
This game works because it gamifies the cognitive multitasking of high-level improvisation. By using a shared visual and structured prompt banks, it introduces hidden variables that prevent the story from becoming predictable. Players cannot plan ahead because they must wait for the immediate 'Yes, And' offer of the preceding sentence, forcing deep active listening. Synthesizing a visual cue, a verbal offer, and an internal constraint trains the brain to find unexpected, creative connections and build highly textured narrative worlds.

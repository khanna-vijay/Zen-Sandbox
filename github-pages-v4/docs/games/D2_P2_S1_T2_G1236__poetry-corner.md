# Poetry Corner

![Poetry Corner — infographic](img/D2_P2_S1_T2_G1236__poetry-corner.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Co-create spontaneous poetry where your partner's final word becomes your very first step.

## Overview
Poetry Corner is a collaborative verbal drill where players construct a cohesive poem line-by-line. By requiring each player to begin their line with the exact final word of the previous player's line, the game eliminates pre-planning and demands absolute presence. The result is a rhythmic, surprising, and deeply connected spoken-word piece created in real time.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Last Word Response; Yes, And… sentence games; Timing exercises
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening and immediate offer reception by utilizing the Last Word Response technique, preventing players from planning ahead and encouraging unfiltered spontaneity.

## Setup
Players stand in pairs facing each other, or in a small circle of up to 10 players. No props, stages, or writing materials are required; the focus is entirely on vocal delivery and auditory focus.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs facing each other, or form a single circle if playing with a smaller group.
2. Establish the core constraint: the first word of every new line must be the exact last word spoken in the previous line.
3. Player A initiates the poem by delivering a single, expressive line of poetry (e.g., 'The winter wind begins to bite').
4. Player B must immediately listen for the final word ('bite') and use it as the first word of their line (e.g., 'Bite into the apple of your dreams').
5. Player A then takes the last word of Player B's line ('dreams') and begins the next line (e.g., 'Dreams are only shadows in the night').
6. Continue this rapid-fire exchange back and forth, maintaining a steady, rhythmic cadence without pausing to plan.
7. Allow the poem to find a natural conclusion after 8 to 12 lines, or when a satisfying thematic resolution is reached.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Listen to the very end!' Remind players not to start formulating their line halfway through their partner's sentence.
- Pitfall: Players often drop the constraint and use a word associated with the last word instead of the exact word. Fix: Gently pause the game and have them repeat the last word to restart their line.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Let the rhythm carry you.' Encourage a steady, metronomic beat to build group mind and pacing.
- Pitfall: Trying to make the poem 'good' or logical, which causes hesitation. Fix: Remind players that 'the first thought is a gift' and nonsense poetry is highly encouraged.

## Variations
- Rhyming Couplets: Add the constraint that every two lines must rhyme, while still maintaining the last-word-to-first-word transition.
- Haiku Chain: Players build a continuous chain of haikus, where each line must adhere to the 5-7-5 syllable structure while passing the last word forward.
- Group Circle: Instead of pairs, the entire circle passes the poem around clockwise, requiring players to track the rhythm across multiple voices.

## Debrief
- How did knowing you had to use your partner's last word change how you listened to them?
- What did it feel like to let go of planning your next line ahead of time?
- How did the rhythm of the exchange help you find your next line without overthinking?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are aware they can pass or take a breath if they feel stuck, keeping the environment low-pressure. For hearing-impaired players, this can be played with visual gestures or written/typed exchanges in a virtual space.

## Why It Works
By locking the starting word of a player's line to the ending word of their partner's, the game mechanically short-circuits the brain's tendency to plan ahead. Players cannot formulate their response until the absolute last second, forcing them into a state of pure active listening and immediate, unfiltered 'yes-and' acceptance.

# Resonant States

![Resonant States — infographic](img/D1_P1_S2_T2_G467__the-inner-world-weaver.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Embody raw emotional impulses while carrying the subtle physical residue of the player before you.

## Overview
A somatic ensemble exercise where players take turns fully embodying abstract feeling words using their entire body, breath, and non-verbal voice. Each performer must ground themselves in their own prompt while allowing the physical and emotional residue of the previous player's performance to subtly color their expression. The result is a fluid, connected chain of complex, layered human states.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Vulnerability; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Emotional substitution; Character Walks/Centers; Object work; Weight & resistance mime; Gibberish
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep emotional fluidity and physical commitment by practicing emotional substitution, allowing players to transition smoothly between contrasting internal states while maintaining a high level of vulnerability and physical presence.

## Setup
An open, quiet room with enough space for players to move freely. The group stands or sits in a wide circle, leaving the center clear as the performance space. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Gather the group in a circle and select one player to step into the center to begin the chain.
2. Provide the first player with an evocative feeling or sensory word (e.g., 'Lethargy', 'Vibrancy', 'Shattered').
3. The active player takes a moment to connect internally with the word, using emotional substitution to find a personal, physical, and vocal expression of that state.
4. For 45 to 60 seconds, the player fully embodies this state in the center, using non-verbal sounds, gibberish, breath, and dynamic movement with absolute commitment.
5. The player slowly winds down their expression, returning to a neutral, still stance, and makes direct eye contact with another player in the circle to invite them in.
6. The second player steps into the center, and the facilitator provides them with a completely new, contrasting stimulus word.
7. The second player must embody their new word while allowing a subtle 'echo' or physical residue of the first player's final state to color their performance, creating a layered, synthesized expression.
8. Repeat this process around the circle, with each subsequent player receiving a new word and integrating the energetic echo of the performer who immediately preceded them.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage players to avoid intellectualizing the 'echo'; it should be a physical sensation or energetic residue left in the space, not a literal mimicry of the previous player's actions.
- If a player struggles to connect, coach them to focus on their breath and physical weight first, using the 'Emotional Dial' technique to slowly turn up the intensity from a 1 to a 7.
- Pitfall: Players might try to act out a narrative or scene. Fix: Remind them that this is a pure somatic drill; focus on abstract movement, gibberish, and raw physical expression rather than storytelling.
- Keep the transitions clean. Ensure the departing player fully returns to neutral before the incoming player begins, allowing a clear handoff of the energetic 'thread'.

## Variations
- Rapid Fire: Shorten the embodiment time to 20 seconds, forcing players to make instant, high-commitment physical choices without time to overthink.
- Contrasting Currents: Instruct the incoming player to choose an internal state that is the exact emotional opposite of the previous player's state, while still integrating their physical residue.
- Silent Echoes: Run the entire exercise in complete silence, relying purely on physical posture, weight, and movement without any vocalization or gibberish.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to let the previous player's physical state influence your own? Did it make your own prompt easier or harder to access?
- What did you notice about the relationship between your physical posture and the ease of accessing a genuine emotional state?
- How did you use emotional substitution to make an abstract word feel personally authentic and committed?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game involves deep emotional exploration and physical vulnerability, remind players that they are always in control of their physical boundaries. They can scale the intensity of their emotional substitution to a level that feels safe and manageable for them, and they should avoid physical movements that cause strain or discomfort.

## Why It Works
By combining a new, immediate stimulus with the physical residue of a previous state, this game bypasses the analytical mind and forces players to rely on somatic intuition. It trains emotional fluidity by requiring the nervous system to hold and transition between complex, layered feelings, while the solo spotlight demands 100% commitment to the physical and vocal choices made in the moment.

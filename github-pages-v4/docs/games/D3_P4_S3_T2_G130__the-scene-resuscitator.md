# The Scene Resuscitator

![The Scene Resuscitator — infographic](img/D3_P4_S3_T2_G130__the-scene-resuscitator.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Freeze stagnant scenes, diagnose the narrative stall, and inject immediate dramatic action to drive the story.

## Overview
The Scene Resuscitator is an active diagnostic exercise where players learn to recognize and cure narrative stagnation from within a scene. When a scene loses momentum, a player pauses the action to identify the missing narrative element and immediately proposes a targeted dramatic choice to revive it. The players then unfreeze and play out the injection, transforming passive observation into active, collaborative storytelling.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; World-Building; Justification; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); What do they stand to lose? reps; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop a keen sense of narrative architecture, specifically mastering the 'Platform and Tilt' technique. Players learn to identify when a platform is stable but stagnant, and how to introduce a deliberate tilt (stakes, conflict, or a shift in wants) to serve the story and propel the scene forward.

## Setup
Arrange the group in a semi-circle facing a performance space. No props or special materials are required. This game can be played in-person or in a virtual gallery view. Two players step into the performance space to begin a scene, while the remaining players act as active observers.

## How to Play
1. Two players step forward and begin a standard, relationship-focused scene, establishing a stable platform (who, what, where).
2. The remaining players and the facilitator observe the scene closely, tracking its narrative momentum and structural health.
3. If a player inside the scene feels the narrative has stalled—due to circular arguments, excessive agreement, or a lack of clear objectives—they call out 'Resuscitate!' and both players freeze.
4. The player who called the freeze must clearly state the diagnosis: what specific narrative element is missing (e.g., 'We have no stakes,' 'We are missing a tilt,' or 'Our characters have no clear wants').
5. The diagnosing player then proposes a specific 'narrative injection'—a concrete line of dialogue, physical action, or emotional shift designed to resolve the stall.
6. Once the injection is declared, the player calls 'Action!' to unfreeze the scene, and both players immediately commit to and justify the new narrative direction.
7. If the players in the scene fail to notice a stall, the facilitator or an observing player can call 'Resuscitate!' and prompt one of the active players to diagnose and inject a solution.
8. The scene continues with its newly injected energy for another minute or two to explore the consequences of the tilt before editing out.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: If a player struggles to diagnose, prompt them with: 'What does your character want right now, and what happens if they don't get it?'
- Pitfall: Players calling 'Resuscitate!' too early before a stable platform is established. Fix: Remind players that a scene needs a clear baseline (who, what, where) before a tilt can be effectively introduced.
- Coaching Cue: Encourage active, physical injections rather than passive verbal explanations. Instead of saying 'I am sad,' show a physical breakdown or make a high-stakes confession.
- Pitfall: The non-calling player resisting the injection. Fix: Coach the partner to practice 'Yes, And' by immediately justifying the new reality, making their partner look like a genius.
- Coaching Cue: Keep the diagnostic phase brief (under 15 seconds) to maintain the physical and emotional energy of the freeze.

## Variations
- The External Medic: Allow observing players on the sidelines to call 'Resuscitate!', step into the scene to deliver the diagnosis and injection, and then step back out to let the original players play it.
- The Tilt Roulette: The facilitator calls 'Resuscitate!' and throws out a specific narrative constraint (e.g., 'Inject a secret,' 'Raise the stakes,' 'Shift the power dynamic') that the players must immediately justify.
- Silent Resuscitation: For advanced players, try to execute the 'resuscitation' silently without calling a freeze, using purely physical or emotional shifts to tilt the scene when it stalls.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to consciously analyze the scene's structure while actively performing in it?
- What are the most common warning signs that a scene's platform has become stagnant?
- How does introducing a clear 'want' or 'stake' instantly change the physical and emotional energy of a scene?
- What makes a narrative injection successful versus one that feels jarring or disconnected?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players feel safe making high-stakes emotional or physical choices. Remind the group that narrative injections should respect established personal boundaries and physical safety, avoiding sudden non-consensual physical contact or highly sensitive personal topics unless explicitly agreed upon beforehand.

## Why It Works
This game works because it demystifies narrative structure by turning meta-cognitive analysis into an active, in-the-moment gameplay mechanic. By freezing the scene, players bypass the panic of stalling and systematically apply the 'Platform and Tilt' technique. It forces players to actively listen for narrative gaps and teaches them that a scene is never truly dead—it just needs a clear, justified shift in stakes or objectives to bring it back to life.

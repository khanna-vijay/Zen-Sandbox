# Sky Catch

![Sky Catch — infographic](img/D1_P4_S1_T2_G1218__open-your-hand.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Bypass your inner editor by catching and naming imaginary objects falling from the sky.

## Overview
Players move through the space, periodically stopping to open their hands and catch an imaginary object falling from above. Without hesitating or planning, they immediately name whatever landed in their palms, set it down, and continue. This rapid-fire exercise trains players to trust their immediate, unfiltered impulses over calculated ideas.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Object work
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and trust in one's first thoughts. By removing the time to plan, players learn to accept the very first image that enters their mind as a valuable gift, cultivating a playful relationship with failure.

## Setup
An open room with enough space for all participants to walk around freely without colliding. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to begin walking around the room at a relaxed, comfortable pace, keeping their eyes open and maintaining awareness of the space.
2. Explain that when you call out 'Catch!', everyone must immediately freeze, cup one or both hands together, and imagine an object falling from the sky directly into their palms.
3. The moment their hands 'catch' the object, players must instantly speak its name aloud without pausing to think, judge, or refine the idea.
4. Once named, players must physically place the imaginary object on the floor or set it aside, then resume walking around the room.
5. Repeat this cycle multiple times, encouraging players to speed up the transition between catching and naming.
6. Reassure players that repetitive or absurd answers (such as catching the same item multiple times) are completely acceptable and should be spoken with confidence.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't look for the perfect answer, just say the first word that hits your tongue!'
- Pitfall: Players freeze or hesitate because they want to be clever. Fix: Encourage them to make a nonsense sound or name the most boring object possible to break the paralysis.
- Pitfall: Players get stuck in a loop of naming the same object. Fix: Validate this loop. Tell them to lean into it until their brain naturally tires of it and offers something else. Repetition is a valid first thought.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Feel the weight and texture of the object in your hands to help trigger the word.'

## Variations
- Pocket Discovery: Instead of catching from the sky, players reach into their actual pockets, feel the fabric, and pull out an imaginary object, naming it instantly.
- Spatial Levels: Instruct players to catch objects at different heights—high above their heads, low to the ground, or far to the side—to see how physical posture influences their mental associations.
- Pass the Catch: Players stand in pairs. One player mimics throwing an object to their partner, who catches it and must instantly name what they received.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to speak before you had time to decide if the idea was 'good' or 'bad'?
- Did anyone experience a 'blank' mind, and how did you move past it?
- How did changing your physical posture or hand position affect the types of objects that came to mind?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the walking pace remains safe and accessible for all physical abilities. Players who prefer not to walk can participate from a stationary position, focusing entirely on the hand gestures and vocalization.

## Why It Works
This drill works by pairing a physical trigger (the closing of the hands) with an immediate vocal response, leaving no cognitive gap for the analytical mind to edit the impulse. By treating the first thought as an inevitable physical reality rather than a creative choice, it lowers the stakes of improvisation and builds confidence in spontaneous generation.

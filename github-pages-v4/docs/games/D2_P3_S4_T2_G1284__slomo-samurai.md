# Slow-Motion Duelists

![Slow-Motion Duelists — infographic](img/D2_P3_S4_T2_G1284__slomo-samurai.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Defend and fall in hyper-slow motion to make your partner look like a master.

## Overview
In this physical warm-up, players move through the space in continuous slow motion, using one forearm as a simulated lethal blade. The goal is not to win, but to receive physical offers, block safely, and accept a dramatic, slow-motion defeat when tagged.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Physicality & Space Work; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Weight & resistance mime
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops physical offer reception and endowment-acceptance by prioritizing the partner's success and committing to a shared physical reality.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players stand spaced out across the floor. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to spread out in the space and adopt a slow-motion physical state, moving at roughly ten percent of their normal speed.
2. Designate each player's dominant forearm, from elbow to fingertips, as a poisoned blade.
3. Explain that the only safe way to block an incoming blade is by making contact with one's own dominant forearm.
4. If a player's blade touches any other part of an opponent's body, that opponent is struck and must immediately begin a dramatic, slow-motion death sequence.
5. Emphasize that players must never speed up to avoid a hit or to strike someone; the slow-motion tempo must remain perfectly consistent.
6. When a player is struck, they must fully accept the endowment of the fatal blow, reacting physically and collapsing to the floor in slow motion.
7. Once on the floor, players remain still as obstacles that active players must navigate around, maintaining peripheral awareness.
8. The game continues until only one or two players remain, or until the facilitator calls a reset.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep it at ten percent speed! If you speed up to win, you lose the magic of the game.'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Make your partner look amazing. If their blade is coming toward your torso, let it land and die beautifully.'
- Pitfall: Players speeding up when they get competitive. Fix: Pause the game and have everyone practice moving their arm from left to right over a slow count of five to recalibrate the tempo.
- Pitfall: Players ignoring a touch or arguing about whether they were hit. Fix: Remind them of the principle 'Make Your Partner a Genius'—it is always more fun to die dramatically than to survive by arguing.

## Variations
- Soundtrack of Doom: Add slow-motion vocal sound effects, requiring players to make low-pitched, stretched-out groans and swooshes that match their physical speed.
- Rebirth: Once a player dies, they can wait five seconds on the ground, then resurrect as a new duelist, keeping the game continuous and high-energy.
- Eye Contact Only: Players can only attack someone they are actively making eye contact with, ensuring high connection and preventing blindside collisions.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to prioritize dying beautifully over winning the fight?
- What did you have to do physically to ensure your partner's attack looked convincing?
- How does this game apply to verbal scenes when a partner endows us with a trait or a choice?

## Safety & Inclusion
Remind players to maintain light, controlled contact. Since the game is played in slow motion, there should be no hard impacts. Players with mobility limitations can play from a stationary or seated position, and their partners must adapt to match their physical level.

## Why It Works
By removing the competitive urge to win and replacing it with the joy of a dramatic slow-motion failure, players practice radical acceptance. It physically instills the concept of endowment-acceptance: when a partner offers a physical hit, accepting it fully makes the partner look skilled and advances the physical narrative.

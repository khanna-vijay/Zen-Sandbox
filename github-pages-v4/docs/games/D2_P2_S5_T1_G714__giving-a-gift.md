# Unwrapping Endowments

![Unwrapping Endowments — infographic](img/D2_P2_S5_T1_G714__giving-a-gift.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Transform simple physical offers into meaningful relationship-building moments through active gifting and playful justification.

## Overview
A paired physical and verbal drill where players practice giving and receiving imaginary gifts. Players learn to accept physical offers with enthusiasm, define them instantly, and justify the choice to deepen the connection between characters.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Offer Reception; Physicality & Space Work; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment-acceptance; Object work; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop active gifting, physical offer reception, and justification skills, training players to enthusiastically 'yes-and' their partner's physical choices and build immediate narrative depth.

## Setup
Divide the group into pairs. Partners stand facing each other with comfortable space to move and mime. No physical props are required.

## How to Play
1. Have players stand in pairs, facing each other, designating one as Partner A and the other as Partner B.
2. Partner A mimes holding and presenting an imaginary object to Partner B, focusing on physical details like weight, size, shape, and texture without speaking.
3. Partner B receives the imaginary object, matching its physical properties with their hands, and immediately names it aloud (e.g., 'Thank you for this vintage typewriter!').
4. Emphasize that Partner B's definition of the object is absolute law; Partner A must instantly accept this definition, even if they originally imagined giving something completely different.
5. Swap roles so Partner B mimes giving a gift, and Partner A receives, names, and thanks them, repeating this basic cycle 2-3 times to build physical confidence.
6. Introduce the 'Justification Beat': Partner A mimes giving the gift, Partner B receives and names it, and then Partner A must immediately explain why they chose this specific gift for Partner B.
7. Ensure the justification makes the receiver look good, reveals something about their relationship, or highlights a shared history, reinforcing the principle of making your partner look like a genius.
8. Swap roles again, practicing the three-step sequence (Mime, Name/Thank, Justify) several times, encouraging players to make bold, specific choices.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach physical commitment: Remind players to show, not tell, during the initial mime phase. If a gift is heavy, their muscles should tension up.
- Avoid the Charades Pitfall: The receiver must not try to guess what the giver was thinking. The receiver has 100% authority to name the object whatever they want, and the giver must happily accept it.
- Side-coach relationship building: Encourage the giver's justification to be supportive and revealing of a positive relationship, rather than a gag that undermines the receiver.
- Combat overthinking: Coach players to say the very first object that pops into their head the moment their hands touch the imaginary item.

## Variations
- Emotional Endowment: The giver delivers the gift with a specific, unspoken emotion (e.g., guilt, pride, secrecy), which the receiver must mirror or react to.
- The White Elephant: The receiver must name something incredibly bizarre or inconvenient (e.g., 'a bucket of warm soup'), and the giver must still justify it as the absolute perfect, thoughtful gift.
- Continuous Scene: After the justification beat, partners continue the scene for 30-60 seconds, using the gift and the established relationship dynamic as the foundation.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your physical offer named as something completely different than what you had in mind? How did you adapt?
- What strategies helped you come up with a justification that made your partner look good?
- How does establishing a clear 'why' behind a gift instantly build a relationship between two characters?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical gifts do not require uncomfortable physical contact. Remind players to respect personal space boundaries when handing over imaginary items, and allow players with limited mobility to adapt the physical scale of their gifting.

## Why It Works
This game bypasses the analytical brain by separating the physical action of giving from the verbal action of naming. By forcing the giver to justify whatever the receiver names, it reinforces the core 'Yes, And' philosophy: accepting your partner's reality and immediately building upon it to discover the scene's underlying relationship.

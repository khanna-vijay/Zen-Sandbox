# Granular Week

![Granular Week — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T0_G882__week-in-detail.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Share the tiniest details of your week and watch your partner recall them all.

## Overview
Players work in pairs to share a hyper-detailed, mundane account of their recent week, focusing on the smallest possible actions. The listening partner then attempts to reconstruct the narrative in chronological order, celebrating the speaker's everyday life.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening and recall, while practicing valuing the mundane and making your partner look brilliant by remembering their specific details.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other in a shared space. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and designate one person as Player A and the other as Player B.
2. Instruct Player A to describe their past week to Player B, focusing on extreme, granular, and mundane details rather than major highlights.
3. Encourage Player A to include tiny actions, such as struggling with a plastic seal, waiting for a kettle to boil, or the specific texture of a doorknob.
4. Player B must listen actively and silently, without taking notes or interrupting, focusing entirely on absorbing the details.
5. After two minutes, signal the pairs to pause.
6. Player B now retells Player A's week back to them in chronological order, aiming to include as many of the specific, tiny details as they can remember.
7. Player A listens and supports Player B, nodding or gently validating when they recall details correctly.
8. Swap roles so Player B shares their granular week and Player A listens and recalls.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach speakers to go slower and focus on the physical, sensory details of their day rather than listing calendar events.
- Remind listeners that this is not a test with a grade, but an exercise in generous attention; if they forget something, they can guess or ask for a hint.
- Pitfall: Speakers talk about big emotional events or abstract thoughts. Fix: Gently redirect them to physical actions and sensory details (e.g., 'What did the coffee cup feel like?').
- Pitfall: Listeners freeze up trying to memorize perfectly. Fix: Coach them to relax and trust their natural memory, focusing on the story's flow rather than rote memorization.

## Variations
- The Five-Minute Day: Instead of a whole week, focus on just one single hour of a day in even more microscopic detail.
- Emotional Echo: The recalling partner must not only repeat the details but also mimic the subtle physical gestures and emotional tone the speaker used.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have someone listen so intently to the most mundane parts of your life?
- What strategies did you use to remember the details without taking notes?
- How does focusing on tiny, physical details help us build richer scenes and characters?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they do not have to share deeply personal or private details; they can focus entirely on safe, mundane, everyday tasks like making tea or brushing teeth.

## Why It Works
By focusing on the hyper-mundane, players bypass the pressure to be 'interesting' or 'funny' (The First Thought Is a Gift). The listener's sole job is to make the speaker feel heard and valued, which builds trust and demonstrates how active listening naturally makes your partner look like a genius.

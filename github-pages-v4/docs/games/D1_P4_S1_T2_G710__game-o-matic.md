# Instant Game Engine

![Instant Game Engine — infographic](img/D1_P4_S1_T2_G710__game-o-matic.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pitch, invent, and instantly perform a brand-new improv game based on audience initials.

## Overview
A high-energy, high-stakes short-form game where players pitch game titles based on an audience member's initials. Once the audience votes on their favorite title, the pitching player must instantly invent the rules on the spot, and the ensemble must immediately play it. It is a thrilling exercise in unfiltered spontaneity, rapid-fire format creation, and absolute commitment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; The First Thought Is a Gift; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Format Literacy; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Longform vs. shortform mechanics; Make the choice readable
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and rapid format literacy by forcing players to trust their first instinct, confidently command the stage, and instantly structure a playable comedic game from a random prompt.

## Setup
An in-person performance space with a clear stage area and an audience. 4 to 8 players stand in a line facing the audience. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. The host asks an audience member for their first and last initials.
2. Each player in the lineup must immediately pitch a game title using those initials as an acronym, speaking their first thought without filtering or hesitating.
3. The host repeats the pitched titles to the audience and conducts a live vote by applause to select the winning title.
4. The player who pitched the winning title must immediately step forward and confidently explain the rules of this newly invented game to both the audience and the other players.
5. The inventing player asks the audience for any necessary starting suggestions to kick off the game.
6. The ensemble immediately plays the newly created game, adhering to the rules just established and committing 100% to the format's success.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't think, just speak!' Encourage players to pitch the very first title that pops into their head, even if it sounds absurd.
- Pitfall: The inventing player overcomplicates the rules, making it impossible for the cast to play. Fix: Coach them to keep the rules simple, such as establishing a single physical or verbal trigger.
- Pitfall: The cast hesitates or questions the rules during play. Fix: Remind the ensemble to treat the invented rules as absolute law and support their teammate's creation with total commitment.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Make the rules active!' Encourage rules that involve physical actions, verbal constraints, or clear comedic triggers.

## Variations
- Solo Challenge: A single player must pitch three different titles for the initials, the audience picks one, and that player must perform a solo scene under the rules they invent.
- Genre Engine: Instead of general games, the initials must represent a new film genre or theatrical style, and the cast performs a multi-scene narrative in that style.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to pitch a title without any time to pre-plan or self-censor?
- What strategies did you use to invent rules that were actually playable for your castmates?
- How does committing 100% to a silly or chaotic rule make it look brilliant to the audience?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that the voting process remains supportive and lighthearted. If a player struggles to come up with rules on the spot, allow them to 'call a friend' for a quick assist from a teammate, keeping the environment collaborative rather than high-pressure.

## Why It Works
This game bypasses the analytical brain by demanding immediate output. By forcing players to pitch titles and invent rules on the fly, it trains the 'first thought is a gift' principle. The pressure of a live audience voting forces the ensemble to rely on pure spontaneity and mutual support, turning potential panic into comedic gold through shared commitment.

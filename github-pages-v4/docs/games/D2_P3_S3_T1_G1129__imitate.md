# Character Echo

![Character Echo — infographic](img/D2_P3_S3_T1_G1129__imitate.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Step into a shop where the merchant mirrors your every physical and vocal quirk.

## Overview
A two-player scene-based drill where a customer enters a shop with a highly distinct, physically or vocally inspired character. The shopkeeper must immediately observe, adopt, and mirror these exact character traits, creating an instant, shared comedic reality. It is a playful exercise in deep observation, physical empathy, and radical agreement.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Character Walks/Centers; Vocal characterization; Mirror exercise; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep physical and vocal mirroring skills, active listening, and character generation. It trains players to make their partner look brilliant by instantly validating and adopting their creative choices.

## Setup
Two players stand facing each other in a designated performance space. One is designated as the Customer, the other as the Shopkeeper. The facilitator or group provides a simple shop type (e.g., a hat shop, a bakery) and a physical or vocal prompt for the Customer to build their character.

## How to Play
1. Assign roles: Player A is the Customer, and Player B is the Shopkeeper.
2. Establish the setting: Ask the group or select a simple retail environment (e.g., a shoe store, a fruit stand) and a basic item to purchase.
3. Generate the Customer's character: Give Player A a physical, vocal, or emotional prompt (e.g., leading with the chin, speaking in a high-pitched rasp, or feeling overwhelming suspicion).
4. Player A takes a moment to fully embody this prompt, letting it shape their posture, voice, and attitude before entering the scene.
5. Player A enters the shop as the Customer and initiates a simple transaction (e.g., trying to buy a single apple).
6. Player B, as the Shopkeeper, must immediately observe Player A's physical posture, vocal tone, tempo, and emotional state.
7. Player B instantly adopts these exact same physical and vocal traits, matching Player A's character choices as closely as possible while conducting the transaction.
8. The players play out the short scene, maintaining this mirrored dynamic until the transaction is complete and the Customer exits.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Match the breath!' Encourage the Shopkeeper to match the Customer's breathing pattern to naturally align their vocal rhythm and physical tension.
- Pitfall: The Shopkeeper tries to be funny by doing an exaggerated parody. Fix: Remind them that this is an exercise in empathy and validation, not mockery. The goal is to match, not mock.
- Coaching cue: 'Lead with the body.' If the Customer is struggling to find a character, have them walk around the space leading with a specific body part (like the nose or knees) and freeze in that shape before starting.
- Pitfall: The scene becomes too plot-heavy. Fix: Keep the transaction incredibly simple (e.g., buying a newspaper) so 90% of the players' cognitive load can be dedicated to physical and vocal mirroring.

## Variations
- Status Swap: The Shopkeeper mirrors the Customer's physical traits but plays the exact opposite social status.
- Emotional Infection: Instead of mirroring physical traits, the Shopkeeper mirrors the Customer's exact emotional subtext, which escalates as the scene progresses.
- The Echo Chain: Run this in a line where Player A passes a character to Player B, who mirrors it, then Player B passes it to Player C, like a physical game of telephone.

## Debrief
- For the Shopkeeper: What physical or vocal cues were the easiest to spot and mirror immediately?
- For the Customer: How did it feel to have your character choices instantly validated and reflected back to you?
- How did matching each other's physicality affect the emotional connection and agreement in the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries and comfort levels when mirroring. If a player has physical limitations, the mirroring should focus on vocal qualities, facial expressions, or upper-body posture to keep the exercise accessible and safe.

## Why It Works
By forcing the Shopkeeper to mirror the Customer, the game operationalizes the principle of 'making your partner a genius.' The Customer's choices are instantly validated as the correct choices for the scene's reality. Physically embodying another person's posture and vocal patterns naturally triggers emotional empathy, leading to effortless comedic chemistry and deep scenic agreement.

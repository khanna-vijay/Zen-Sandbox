# Impulse Jump

![Impulse Jump — infographic](img/D1_P4_S3_T1_G1140__jump.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Leap into an instant physical shape and discover a character from the outside in.

## Overview
A high-energy physical warm-up where players move dynamically through the space and instantly freeze into random, exaggerated postures on a cue. By committing fully to these accidental shapes, players bypass intellectual planning to discover unique characters based entirely on their physical centers. It is an active, playful exploration of physical character generation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Character Walks/Centers; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop physical spontaneity and character generation using the outside-in method, training players to trust their first physical impulse as a creative gift.

## Setup
An open, obstacle-free room where players can move around safely at various speeds. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to begin walking around the room, filling the empty spaces and maintaining a steady, neutral pace.
2. Explain that when you call out 'Jump!', every player must immediately leap into the air and land in a frozen, exaggerated physical shape.
3. Emphasize that the landing shape must be completely unplanned; players should embrace whatever posture, balance, or asymmetry they land in.
4. Once frozen, direct players to hold the shape for a moment and notice where their weight is distributed, where their chest or hips are pointing, and how their limbs are positioned.
5. Instruct players to let this physical shape inspire an immediate character attitude, emotion, or point of view, treating the physical sensation as their first thought.
6. On your cue ('Go!'), have players begin walking around the space again, but this time maintaining the physical center, posture, and attitude of the character they just discovered.
7. Encourage players to interact briefly with others they pass, using a single line of dialogue or a non-verbal greeting that fits their new character.
8. Call 'Neutral' to have players shake out the character and return to a normal walk, then repeat the cycle with new physical prompts.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't think about who you are—let the shape tell you who you are!'
- Side-coaching cues: Call out modifiers right before the jump, such as 'Heavy!', 'Light!', 'High!', 'Low!', or 'Asymmetrical!' to force players out of their physical comfort zones.
- Pitfall: Players landing in safe, neutral, standing positions. Fix: Encourage them to explore different levels (crouching, reaching high) and off-balance stances.
- Pitfall: Players dropping the physical shape as soon as they start walking. Fix: Remind them to exaggerate the physical center (e.g., leading with the chin, knees, or chest) as they move.

## Variations
- Vocal Impulse: Once players land in their shape, have them make a single vocal sound or sigh that matches the physical tension of the posture before they start walking.
- Emotional Catalyst: Add an emotional prompt to the jump (e.g., 'Jump... and land suspicious!' or 'Jump... and land joyful!') to combine physical shape with immediate emotional commitment.
- Partner Mirroring: Have players jump in pairs, landing in complementary shapes, and immediately initiate a two-person scene based on their physical relationship.

## Debrief
- How did changing your physical posture first make it easier to find a character's voice or attitude?
- What did it feel like to trust your body's accidental shape rather than planning a character in your head?
- How can we use this 'outside-in' physical approach when we feel stuck or in our heads during a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is clear of hazards. Encourage players to adapt the 'jump' to their own physical comfort level; a 'jump' can be a sudden shift in weight, a sharp gesture, or a rapid change in level for those who prefer not to leave the floor. Remind players to respect their own physical boundaries and joints.

## Why It Works
By forcing an immediate physical commitment ('the jump'), the game bypasses the analytical mind. The body lands in a shape before the brain can plan, forcing the player to use the physical reality as the starting point. This 'outside-in' approach teaches that physical centers naturally dictate emotional states and character voices, proving that the first physical thought is indeed a gift.

# Establishing the Ordinary

![Establishing the Ordinary — infographic](img/D3_P2_S1_T1_G790__ordinary-scene-until-ridiculous.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Build a rock-solid base reality until the first unusual thing naturally reveals itself.

## Overview
Two players initiate a highly realistic, mundane scene, focusing entirely on believable behavior and honest relationships. The moment an organic absurdity, mistake, or unusual choice occurs, the observing group celebrates the discovery, highlighting the exact seed of the scene's game.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Game Identification; World-Building; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Finding & Playing the Game; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To train players in establishing a strong base reality first, resisting the urge to force comedy, and collectively identifying the precise moment a comedic game is born.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with two chairs. The remaining players sit as an active audience. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Invite two players to the stage and ask the audience for a mundane, everyday location or activity.
2. Instruct the players to begin the scene by committing fully to the reality of the situation, focusing on realistic dialogue and detailed physical object work.
3. Emphasize that players must actively avoid trying to be funny, making jokes, or introducing wacky premises.
4. The observing players watch closely, listening for the first moment of organic friction, a slight exaggeration, an accidental slip-of-the-tongue, or an unusual reaction.
5. As soon as this first unusual thing occurs, the entire audience and facilitator immediately cheer and clap to freeze the scene.
6. The facilitator briefly highlights what the unusual thing was and how it could serve as the foundation for a comedic game.
7. Rotate players quickly, bringing up a new pair with a new mundane suggestion to repeat the process.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to slow down and focus on physical tasks if they start rushing into heavy dialogue to find the funny.
- If players deliberately introduce a wacky character right away, pause the scene and remind them to let the unusual thing happen accidentally.
- Ensure the audience's cheer is supportive and celebratory, not a buzzer of failure; the goal is to train the collective muscle of spotting the game's origin.
- If a scene goes on for more than two minutes without an unusual thing, gently side-coach a slight emotional escalation to help surface an organic pattern.

## Variations
- Play the Pattern: Once the audience cheers for the unusual thing, instead of stopping, the players must immediately yes-and that specific behavior and repeat it to play the game.
- Silent Start: The first minute of the scene must be entirely silent object work, forcing players to establish physical reality before any verbal choices can create the unusual thing.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to strip away the pressure of having to be funny at the start of the scene?
- What made the unusual thing stand out so clearly once a normal reality was established?
- How does having a strong base reality make it easier to discover and play a comedic game?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that the mundane suggestions requested do not touch on personal trauma or highly sensitive domestic situations. Keep the focus on low-stakes, everyday tasks.

## Why It Works
By removing the burden of comedy, players relax into authentic behavior, which naturally breeds organic, human absurdity. A strong base reality acts as a contrast medium: when everything is normal, the slightest deviation stands out clearly, making game identification effortless for both players and audience.

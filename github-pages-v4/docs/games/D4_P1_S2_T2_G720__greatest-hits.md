# Greatest Hits

![Greatest Hits — infographic](img/D4_P1_S2_T2_G720__greatest-hits.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Hosts pitch a hilarious compilation album while singers instantly bring the fictional tracks to life.

## Overview
Two charismatic hosts sell a themed compilation album to the audience, inventing absurd song titles on the fly. Behind them, a vocal ensemble stands ready to instantly perform high-energy snippets of these songs as soon as they are announced. The game relies on rapid-fire comedic banter, spontaneous musical composition, and seamless group support.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Make Your Partner a Genius; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Support Work; Vocal Craft; Active Gifting; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** Tap-ins; Vocal characterization; Endowment-gifting drills; Projection
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops group mind, active gifting, and vocal support by requiring players to instantly harmonize, back up, or lead musical numbers based on minimal information.

## Setup
Two hosts stand downstage left and right. A lineup of four singers stands upstage. No props or instruments are required; the singers perform a cappella.

## How to Play
1. The hosts ask the audience for a mundane suggestion (e.g., 'appliances' or 'dentistry') to serve as the theme for the compilation album.
2. The hosts initiate a high-energy, late-night infomercial style banter, introducing the album's theme and setting up the first song title.
3. Host A or B announces a specific, funny song title from the album (e.g., 'The Leaky Gasket Blues').
4. Upon hearing the title, one or more singers from the upstage lineup immediately step forward to the center stage to perform.
5. The active singers perform a short, 15-30 second snippet of the song, establishing a rhythm, melody, and chorus on the fly.
6. Upstage singers can step in to provide backing vocals, harmonies, or physical choreography to support those leading the song.
7. Once the song snippet reaches a high point or a natural comedic button, the hosts cut back in with their infomercial banter, signaling the singers to step back into the lineup.
8. The hosts continue pitching tracks, encouraging different combinations of singers (solos, duets, trios, or the full ensemble) to step forward for subsequent songs.
9. After four or five song snippets, the hosts pitch the final, epic track of the album, bringing all singers forward for a big, high-energy ensemble finish.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Hosts, give your singers a clear genre or style to help them start!'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Singers, support the lead! If someone steps out with a strong melody, join in with harmonies or a backing rhythm.'
- Pitfall: Singers hesitate to step forward, leaving the stage empty. Fix: Encourage the hosts to explicitly name who sings the track (e.g., 'And now, sung beautifully by our tenor duo...') to force a specific pairing forward.
- Pitfall: Songs drag on too long. Fix: Hosts must be aggressive with their edits. Cut the song off after one solid verse/chorus or a great punchline.
- Coaching: Emphasize physical commitment. Even if the singing is silly, confident staging and choreography make the performance land.

## Variations
- Genre Roulette: The hosts must specify a wildly different musical genre for every single track (e.g., heavy metal, opera, reggae).
- The Soloist Challenge: The hosts call out a specific singer by name or character trait to perform a solo, testing their individual spontaneity.
- Instrumental Backing: If an improvising musician is present, they instantly provide the backing track, and singers must match the tempo and key provided.

## Debrief
- How did the singers support each other musically when they didn't know where the song was going?
- Hosts, how did your descriptions of the songs help or challenge the singers?
- What did it feel like to step forward without a plan, trusting your ensemble to back you up?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are comfortable with singing in public; offer non-singing support roles (like beatboxing or rhythmic movement) for those anxious about vocal pitch. Keep the physical space clear of obstacles so singers can step forward safely and quickly.

## Why It Works
This game builds Group Mind by forcing singers to instantly align on key, tempo, and lyrical theme without pre-planning. The 'Tap-in' mechanic teaches players to read their ensemble's physical cues, stepping forward to support a teammate or holding back to let a solo shine, embodying the principle of making your partner look like a genius.

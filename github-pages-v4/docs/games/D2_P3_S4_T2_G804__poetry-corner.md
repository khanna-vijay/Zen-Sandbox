# The Poetry Broadcast

![The Poetry Broadcast — infographic](img/D2_P3_S4_T2_G804__poetry-corner.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Elevate absurd, improvised verses into profound literary masterpieces through unwavering support and high-status praise.

## Overview
In this comedic performance game, a high-brow host interviews an eccentric poet about their latest collection. As the poet introduces various titles, ensemble members step forward to perform these improvised poems on the spot. Regardless of how silly, simple, or bizarre the verses are, the host and poet treat them as absolute strokes of genius.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; The First Thought Is a Gift; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Vocal Craft; Support Work; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; First Thought drills; Vocal characterization; Walk-ons
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop endowment-acceptance and radical support. Players learn to treat their partner's spontaneous offers as brilliant choices, building comedic heightening through intellectual justification.

## Setup
Place two chairs downstage for the Host and the Poet. The remaining players (the Ensemble) stand in a line upstage or off to the side, ready to step forward to an imaginary microphone center stage. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign one player as the Host and another as the Poet. The rest of the players form the Ensemble.
2. Ask the group for a fictional, slightly pretentious name for the Poet to establish their literary persona.
3. The Host begins a high-brow, intellectual interview, asking the Poet about the inspiration behind their latest, highly anticipated poetry collection.
4. The Poet introduces the title of their first poem and briefly explains the deep, often mundane, inspiration behind it (e.g., 'I wrote this after staring at a half-eaten sandwich').
5. An Ensemble player immediately steps forward to the center-stage 'microphone' and improvises the poem based on the title and inspiration provided.
6. The Ensemble player delivers the poem using distinct vocal choices, rhythm, or physical styling, then steps back.
7. The Host and Poet react with profound awe, dissecting the poem's 'brilliance' and justifying why its simple or absurd choices are actually masterclasses in literature.
8. Repeat the cycle three to four times with different Ensemble members performing new poems introduced by the Poet, before wrapping up the interview.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Treat every word like gold!' Remind the Host and Poet that their job is to make the poem sound like a masterpiece, especially if the performer struggled or kept it incredibly simple.
- Pitfall: The interview drags on too long. Fix: Coach the Host to transition to the first poem within 60 seconds to keep the momentum high.
- Coaching Cue: 'Vary the style!' Encourage the Ensemble to contrast the previous poem. If the last one was a rhythmic rap, make the next one a slow, dramatic whisper or a classical sonnet.
- Pitfall: The poem-readers try to write 'good' poetry and freeze up. Fix: Remind them that bad poetry is comedy gold; trust the first thought and let the Host/Poet do the heavy lifting of making it genius.

## Variations
- The Translation: The Poet claims the poem was originally written in an obscure foreign language. The Ensemble player performs it in gibberish, and a second Ensemble player translates it line-by-line.
- Physical Poetry: The Ensemble player must incorporate expressive, interpretive movement that matches the rhythm of their spoken words.
- The Critic's Corner: Add a third seated player as a cynical literary critic who tries to find fault, forcing the Host and Poet to defend the poem's genius even more passionately.

## Debrief
- How did it feel as a reader to know that whatever you said would be treated as a work of genius?
- For the Host and Poet, what strategies did you use to find deep meaning in simple or silly lines?
- How does this game demonstrate the power of 'making your partner look good' in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players feel comfortable with vocal and physical expression. If a player has vocal strain or prefers not to speak, they can perform a silent, movement-based 'visual poem' while another player reads, or focus on the seated Host/Poet roles.

## Why It Works
This game operates on the 'Game Engine' of comedic justification. By taking low-stakes, unfiltered, and often clumsy improvised poetry and treating it with high-status intellectual reverence, it creates a hilarious contrast. It removes the fear of failure for the performer because the mechanics guarantee that their 'mistakes' will be framed as intentional brilliance.

# Gibberish Commands

![Gibberish Commands — infographic](img/D1_P1_S4_T3_G1093__gibberish-commands.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Decode and execute un-translated commands using vocal tone, physical context, and absolute commitment.

## Overview
A paired vocal and listening exercise where players communicate specific instructions using entirely made-up language. Instead of relying on literal words or heavy charades, players must use vocal inflection, emotional subtext, and environmental cues to make their meaning clear.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Vocal Craft; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Gibberish
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop vocal expressiveness, active listening, and the ability to commit fully to nonsense sounds while treating your partner's offer as a brilliant, decipherable message.

## Setup
Players stand in pairs facing each other in a shared space. No props or special staging required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have them stand facing each other with comfortable space in between.
2. Designate Player A as the Commander and Player B as the Executor for the first round.
3. Player A thinks of a simple, actionable command such as 'tie my shoe' or 'hand me that book'.
4. Player A delivers this command to Player B using entirely nonsense words (gibberish), focusing on vocal tone, volume, rhythm, and eye contact.
5. Player B must actively listen to the vocal cues and observe the physical context, then attempt to execute the action they believe was commanded.
6. Player A must not use explicit charades or point directly at objects; they must rely on the vocal delivery and the immediate environment to convey the message.
7. If Player B guesses incorrectly, Player A continues to repeat or adjust the gibberish command with different vocal emphasis until Player B successfully performs the intended action.
8. Once the command is successfully executed, the players swap roles and repeat the process with a new command.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Use your voice, not just your hands! Let the pitch, volume, and urgency do the heavy lifting.
- Pitfall: Players resort to silent charades or sign language. Fix: Remind them that the voice must remain active and be the primary driver of the communication.
- Coaching cue: Make your partner a genius. If they do something close or highly committed, find a way to accept it as the correct action or gently guide them vocally.
- Pitfall: Giving overly complex commands. Fix: Instruct players to keep commands to a single, immediate physical action.

## Variations
- Status Shift: Deliver the gibberish command from a position of extreme high status or extreme low status, which changes the vocal quality.
- Emotional Gibberish: The facilitator calls out an emotion that the Commander must use to color their gibberish delivery.

## Debrief
- How did changing your vocal pitch or rhythm help convey meaning when actual words were unavailable?
- What did it feel like to make your partner a genius by accepting their interpretation of your gibberish?
- How did active listening change when you couldn't rely on literal vocabulary?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries when executing commands, and encourage commands that respect personal space and physical accessibility.

## Why It Works
By stripping away literal language, players are forced to commit 100% to their vocal delivery and emotional intent. It trains active listening because the receiver cannot passively wait for words; they must read the entire vocal and physical presence of their partner.

# Chivalrous Duos

![Chivalrous Duos — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T0_G985__chivalrous-couples.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy, physical pairing game that sharpens peripheral awareness and rapid group coordination.

## Overview
Players move dynamically through the space until a caller on an elevated platform shouts out one of three royal pairings. Instantly, players must find a partner and assume the corresponding physical pose. The last pair to successfully form their pose is temporarily sidelined, keeping the energy high and the focus sharp.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Make Your Partner a Genius; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening; Boundary Navigation
- **Technique(s):** Negotiating physical contact
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop rapid physical responsiveness, peripheral awareness, and immediate non-verbal agreement with a partner under high-energy conditions.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. One sturdy chair or low, stable platform placed at the edge of the space for the facilitator or caller. All players start scattered throughout the room.

## How to Play
1. Designate one person (initially the facilitator) to stand on the elevated platform as the Caller.
2. Instruct all other players to mingle actively throughout the space, maintaining eye contact and moving at a brisk pace.
3. Explain the three distinct duos and their physical poses: 'Knight and Queen' (one kneels, the other sits on their knee), 'Prince and Princess' (one cradles the other in their arms), and 'Horse and Rider' (one offers a piggyback ride, the other mounts).
4. Introduce low-impact, non-lifting alternatives for safety (e.g., Knight and Queen can be a deep bow and curtsy; Prince and Princess can be a mirrored hand-heart; Horse and Rider can be one person holding imaginary reins behind the other).
5. When the Caller shouts one of the three duos, players must immediately find the nearest partner and assume the designated pose.
6. The last pair to successfully complete the pose, or any player left without a partner, is temporarily eliminated ('takes five') and joins the sidelines to cheer.
7. After each round, the remaining players resume mingling, and the Caller commands them to find a different partner for the next round to prevent exclusive pairing.
8. Continue play until only one winning pair remains, then reset the game with a new Caller.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Eyes up! Don't just look at the floor; scan the entire room to see who is open.'
- Pitfall: Players might hesitate or get picky about partners. Fix: Remind them of the 'Make Your Partner a Genius' principle—grab the closest person immediately without hesitation.
- Coaching Cue: 'Commit 100% to the shape! Make it clear and stable.'
- Pitfall: Physical safety issues with lifting. Fix: Strongly emphasize that players must mutually agree on who takes which role in the pose, and use the low-impact modifications if there is any doubt about physical capability.

## Variations
- Silent Court: The Caller uses non-verbal gestures or holds up visual signs instead of shouting, forcing players to keep their eyes on the Caller at all times.
- Modern Corporate: Change the archetypes to modern office roles (e.g., 'CEO and Assistant', 'IT Support and Broken Computer', 'Co-workers at the Watercooler') with safer, non-lifting physical poses.
- Continuous Play: Instead of elimination, the last pair to form the pose must perform a quick 5-second improvised scene based on their characters before the next round begins.

## Debrief
- How did your peripheral awareness change as the number of active players decreased?
- What non-verbal cues did you use to instantly negotiate who would play which role in the pair?
- How does committing 100% to a physical choice help your partner make their decision?

## Safety & Inclusion
This game involves significant physical contact and lifting. It is vital to establish a 'safety first' rule before starting. Introduce low-impact, non-contact alternatives for every pose (e.g., gesturing or posing near each other without touching) and explicitly state that players can opt for these alternatives at any moment without penalty. Ensure the platform for the caller is completely stable.

## Why It Works
By combining high physical energy with rapid decision-making, the game bypasses the analytical mind. Players must rely on immediate physical commitment and peripheral awareness to survive, fostering a deep sense of 'Group Mind' as the room self-organizes in seconds.

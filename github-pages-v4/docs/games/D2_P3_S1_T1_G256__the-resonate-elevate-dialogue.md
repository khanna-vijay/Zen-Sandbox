# Resonate and Elevate

![Resonate and Elevate — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T1_G256__the-resonate-elevate-dialogue.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Echo your partner's emotional truth, then build a brilliant justification to make them shine.

## Overview
A structured, two-player scene engine where improvisers must explicitly validate their partner's contribution before adding new information. By dividing every response into a 'Resonate' phase (mirroring and acknowledging) and an 'Elevate' phase (amplifying and justifying), players build deep relational attunement and practice radical generosity.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Assume Competence
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Status Modulation; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Meisner Repetition; Last Word Response; Mirror exercise; Emotional-echo drills; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Give them the answer; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening, emotional mirroring, and the habit of making your partner look like a genius by treating every offer they make as brilliant and absolutely correct.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other. No props or special staging are required. This can be run in a physical classroom or in virtual breakout rooms.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs. One pair will perform a scene while the rest observe, or all pairs can practice simultaneously.
2. Establish a simple relationship or starting scenario, such as two coworkers organizing a desk or friends waiting for a bus.
3. Player A begins the scene with a single, clear line of dialogue that carries an emotional tone or physical posture.
4. Player B must respond by first entering the 'Resonate' phase: they must verbally or physically echo Player A's offer. This can be a direct verbal repetition of a key phrase, mirroring their physical posture, or naming the underlying emotion.
5. Immediately after resonating, Player B enters the 'Elevate' phase: they must build upon Player A's offer by justifying it, amplifying its importance, or gifting Player A a positive endowment that makes their initial statement look brilliant.
6. Player A must then receive Player B's elevated response and repeat the cycle: first 'Resonating' (mirroring/echoing Player B's contribution) and then 'Elevating' (building and justifying).
7. Continue this strict alternating pattern of Resonate-then-Elevate for each line of dialogue throughout the scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Watch out for players who rush past the 'Resonate' phase to deliver their own pre-planned joke. Side-coach: 'Stop, feel their emotion first, echo it, then build.'
- If the 'Elevate' phase feels flat, encourage players to treat their partner's offer as the most important thing in the world. Ask: 'Why is what they just said absolutely genius?'
- Ensure players don't just repeat words mindlessly like a robot; the 'Resonate' phase must carry genuine emotional weight and empathy.
- For virtual play, encourage players to lean into the camera to capture subtle facial expressions and vocal inflections during the mirroring phase.

## Variations
- Physical Only: Run the exercise entirely silently, where players must physically mirror their partner's movement (Resonate) and then expand the movement into a larger, justified physical choice (Elevate).
- The Last-Word Resonate: Force the 'Resonate' phase to start with the exact last word or phrase spoken by the partner, adapting classic last-word response techniques.
- Status Seesaw: During the 'Elevate' phase, players must deliberately adjust their status, either raising their partner's status or lowering their own to make the partner look superior.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your partner immediately validate and amplify every single thing you said?
- What was harder: truly listening to echo (Resonate), or finding a way to make your partner's offer look brilliant (Elevate)?
- How did this structure change the speed and emotional depth of your scene compared to a standard free-form scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game requires close attention and mirroring, remind players to respect physical boundaries. If physical mirroring is used, players should mirror posture and gestures without invading personal space or making physical contact unless explicitly agreed upon.

## Why It Works
By separating the response into two distinct, mandatory phases, this game short-circuits the common improviser habit of planning ahead. The 'Resonate' phase forces active listening and emotional attunement (drawing on Meisner repetition principles), while the 'Elevate' phase operationalizes 'Make Your Partner a Genius' by requiring immediate, positive justification of the partner's choices.

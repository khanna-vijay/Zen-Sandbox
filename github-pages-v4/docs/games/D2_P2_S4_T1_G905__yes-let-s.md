# Yes, Let's!

![Yes, Let's! — infographic](img/D2_P2_S4_T1_G905__yes-let-s.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy physical agreement game where every suggestion is instantly embraced and acted out.

## Overview
In this fast-paced, whole-group warm-up, players take turns proposing physical actions to the room. Instantly, the entire group enthusiastically agrees in unison and immediately acts out the suggestion together. This cycle repeats rapidly, building a shared physical narrative and training immediate, unhesitating acceptance of offers.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Yes, And; Serve the Story; Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Offer Reception; Narrative Architecture; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Object work; Yes, And… sentence games; Story Spine
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To cultivate radical agreement, immediate physical commitment, and collaborative narrative building by removing the analytical filter that causes players to hesitate or block.

## Setup
Players stand in a clear, open room with plenty of space to move around safely. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Gather the entire group in the center of the playing space, standing and ready to move.
2. Explain that any player can call out an action starting with the phrase 'Let's...', such as 'Let's paint a giant mural!'
3. Immediately upon hearing the offer, the entire group must shout back in unison, 'Yes, let's!' with maximum enthusiasm.
4. Every player must immediately begin physically acting out the suggested action in the space, committing fully with their entire body.
5. After a few seconds of physical play, any other player calls out a new 'Let's...' suggestion (e.g., 'Let's step back and admire our work!').
6. The group instantly responds with 'Yes, let's!' and immediately transitions into the new physical action.
7. Encourage players to let each new offer naturally build upon or transition from the current action, creating a collaborative, physical narrative arc.
8. Continue this rapid cycle of offer, enthusiastic agreement, and physical action for several minutes until the energy peaks.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Shout "Yes, let's!" before your brain has time to judge or analyze the suggestion!'
- Coaching cue: 'Commit with your whole body, not just your voice. Let the movement lead your mind.'
- Pitfall: Players hesitate, waiting for the 'perfect' or funniest suggestion. Fix: Side-coach the group to shout out the very first simple action that comes to mind; mundane actions often lead to the best physical play.
- Pitfall: The physical energy drops or players merely mimic each other. Fix: Encourage players to find their own unique way of performing the suggested action while maintaining high physical commitment.
- Coaching cue: 'Listen to where the story wants to go next. Let the physical reality of the moment inspire your next offer.'

## Variations
- Narrative Thread: Restrict offers so that each 'Let's...' must logically and sequentially follow the previous action to tell a cohesive story (e.g., walking in a forest -> finding a key -> opening a chest).
- Emotional Shift: Each new offer must also introduce a specific emotional state that the entire group must instantly adopt (e.g., 'Let's look for our lost keys while feeling incredibly panicked!').
- Silent Yes, Let's: Players make physical offers without speaking, and the group mirrors the movement immediately without the verbal cue, relying purely on visual connection and group mind.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have every single one of your suggestions instantly and enthusiastically accepted by the entire room?
- What happened to our collective energy when we stopped overthinking and committed to the physical action immediately?
- How did physicalizing the offers help us build a shared story without needing a script or verbal negotiation?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the space is clear of tripping hazards before starting. Remind players to be mindful of their physical boundaries and those of others, adapting high-energy movements to suit their own physical comfort, mobility levels, and personal space.

## Why It Works
By forcing an immediate, vocalized, and physicalized 'Yes,' this game bypasses the analytical filter that often blocks or stalls improvisers. It physically programs the habit of active support, builds trust through shared vulnerability, and demonstrates how easily a narrative can unfold when everyone commits to the same reality.

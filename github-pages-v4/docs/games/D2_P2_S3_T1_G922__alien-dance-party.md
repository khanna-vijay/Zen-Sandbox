# Cosmic Groove Relay

![Cosmic Groove Relay — infographic](img/D2_P2_S3_T1_G922__alien-dance-party.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass a high-energy physical dance around the circle by mirroring and evolving unique movements.

## Overview
A high-energy physical warm-up where players stand in a circle and pass a unique dance movement to their neighbors. Each player must precisely mirror their partner's movement before evolving it into a brand-new groove to pass along.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Yes, And; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops deep physical listening, non-verbal agreement, and the ability to commit fully to bold physical choices.

## Setup
Clear a moderate-sized space so players can stand in a wide circle with enough room to move dynamically without colliding. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle with all players facing inward.
2. The facilitator designates a starting player to initiate the first dance movement.
3. The active player begins a repetitive, high-energy dance move, committing to it fully with their whole body.
4. The active player turns to face the person to their right, directing the dance move directly at them.
5. The receiving player immediately attempts to mirror the dance move as accurately as possible, matching the rhythm, energy, and physical shape.
6. Once the initiator feels their partner has successfully matched and locked into the groove, the initiator stops dancing and relaxes.
7. The receiving player continues the inherited dance for a few beats, then smoothly transitions it into a completely new, distinct dance movement.
8. The new active dancer turns to the person on their right to pass the new movement, repeating the cycle until the groove has traveled all the way around the circle.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to match facial expressions and posture, not just hand and leg movements.
- If an initiator stops dancing before the receiver has actually matched the movement, pause and coach them to wait for that moment of true physical connection.
- Encourage speed and immediate physical commitment over overthinking or trying to make the dance look 'good'.
- Remind players to keep the energy high even when they are waiting for the dance to reach them.

## Variations
- Sound and Motion: Add a distinct vocalization or sound effect to the dance move that must also be mirrored and evolved.
- Cross-Circle Pass: Instead of passing to the immediate right, players make eye contact across the circle and travel across the space to pass the dance.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your physical choices completely accepted and mirrored by your partner?
- What did you notice about the transition from mirroring someone else's movement to creating your own?
- How does physical mirroring build trust and connection without using words?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries and personal mobility levels. Encourage participants to adapt movements to their own comfort and physical capabilities; mirroring can be about matching the energy and intent rather than exact physical replication.

## Why It Works
This game bypasses the analytical brain by forcing immediate physical action. By mirroring, players practice physical 'Yes, And,' validating their partner's choices through total commitment and building a shared group mind.

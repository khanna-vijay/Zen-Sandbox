# The Silent Scenario

![The Silent Scenario — infographic](img/ISR020.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two improvisers compete in an alternating, high-energy mime challenge before the dividing wall drops for a head-to-head finale.

## Overview
Two improvisers from opposing teams compete in a high-energy mime challenge, navigating a suggested environment to complete a specific objective. Instead of playing simultaneously, the Referee uses a whistle to alternate focus between the players in rapid 15-second intervals. In the final phase, the imaginary wall between them drops, and they must interact to compete for the exact same objective in a shared space.

## Setup
Requires two players (one from each team) and a Referee with a whistle. No props or chairs are used. The stage is initially split down the middle by an imaginary line. The Referee gets an Environment (e.g., Space Station) and an Objective (e.g., Catch the escaped alien) from the audience.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets an Environment and an Objective from the audience. Both players will attempt the same objective in their own version of the environment.
2. The Referee explains the rules: players must remain completely silent, and they only move when the Referee points to them.
3. The Referee blows the whistle and points to Player A. Player A has 15 seconds to establish the environment and begin tackling the objective using only mime and object work.
4. The Referee blows the whistle and points to Player B. Player A must instantly freeze. Player B now has 15 seconds to establish their own environment and tackle the objective.
5. The Referee alternates focus back and forth 3 to 4 times, increasing the pace and urgency of the whistle blows as the players get closer to their goals.
6. The Twist: For the final 30 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle twice and yells 'Head to Head!' The imaginary dividing line drops. Both players unfreeze and are now in the SAME environment, interacting and physically competing to achieve the objective first.
7. The Referee remains silent during the action. There is no play-by-play commentary, allowing the audience to focus entirely on the physical storytelling and mime.
8. The Referee awards 1-3 points per turn for exceptional object work and physical comedy. Fouls are called for speaking (Chatterbox Foul, -3 points) or sloppy mime (Ghosting Foul, -1 point). The audience's final applause determines the overall winner of the match if the points are tied.

## Coaching Notes
- Ensure the Referee remains completely silent during the action to allow the audience to focus entirely on the physical storytelling.
- Award 1-3 points per turn for exceptional object work, paying attention to weight, texture, and consistency.
- Call a Chatterbox Foul (-3 points) if a player speaks, and a Ghosting Foul (-1 point) for sloppy mime.
- Use the alternating spotlight mechanic to prevent split focus and build comedic tension.
- Increase the pace and urgency of the whistle blows as the players get closer to their goals to heighten the energy.

## Variations
- Tag-Team Mime: Teammates can tag in during their team's 15-second window to take over the physical narrative and escalate the physical comedy.
- Foley Artist: A teammate offstage provides live sound effects for their miming player's actions, adding an audio layer without breaking the silence rule.

## Why It Works
The alternating spotlight mechanic prevents split focus and builds comedic tension. It forces 100 percent commitment to physical storytelling and object work, culminating in a surprise escalation when the dividing line drops for player interaction.

## Safety & Inclusion
During the 'Head to Head' interactive phase, players must maintain physical safety; no actual striking, tackling, or unsafe lifting is allowed. All object work and physical gestures must remain family-friendly (a clean-content foul applies for inappropriate mime). For players with mobility restrictions, the game can be easily adapted to focus on upper-body object work, facial expressions, and seated environmental interaction.

# Echo Orbit

![Echo Orbit — infographic](img/IS139.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A cooperative game reimagining Keepy-Uppy through imagined sensory properties and non-verbal communication.

## Overview
Players collaboratively sustain an imaginary Energy Orb imbued with distinct sensory characteristics. Each receiver must physically and audibly echo the orb's current traits, then transform exactly one characteristic before passing it to the next person.

## Setup
Participants stand in a loose circle or open space, maintaining awareness of everyone else.

## How to Play
1. One player begins with an imaginary 'Energy Orb' of their own creation, giving it at least two distinct sensory characteristics (e.g., 'a heavy, cold sphere' or 'a light, buzzing cloud').
2. The initiating player physically and audibly embodies holding this orb, clearly demonstrating its characteristics (e.g., straining with weight, shivering with cold, making a soft buzzing sound).
3. The player 'passes' this orb to another player in the space using a clear physical gesture (e.g., a toss, a gentle push, a levitation, a delicate placement).
4. The receiving player must immediately and demonstrably 'Echo' the orb's current sensory characteristics through their own body and voice.
5. As the receiver 'Echoes' the orb, they subtly or overtly transform exactly one of its characteristics (e.g., 'heavy and cold' becomes 'heavy and warm').
6. The player passing must clearly embody all of the orb's new traits as they pass it. They may add a slight verbal cue if helpful (e.g., 'Now it's... spiky!').
7. The game continues with the orb 'orbiting' around the group. The goal is to keep the orb in continuous, flowing motion without 'dropping' it.
8. If a player 'drops' the orb (their echo is unclear, they don't transform, or they seem lost), the game briefly pauses. That player immediately initiates a new Energy Orb with fresh, distinct characteristics to restart the flow.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): To receive and physically/audibly echo the exact sensory characteristics of the imaginary orb, and then to transform only one of those characteristics while embodying all new traits before passing it on.
- Intuition Over Intellect: The swift, continuous flow demands immediate, unthinking physical and vocal responses. There is no time to analyze; players must trust their gut reactions to the 'feel' of the orb.
- There is no 'right' or 'wrong' way to embody 'heavy' or 'warm'; commitment is key.
- Remind players that the 'reset' mechanism ensures 'drops' are not failures, but opportunities to re-engage with renewed focus, fostering a safe space for experimentation.
- Encourage full presence. Players must constantly observe the incoming offer, listen to its characteristics, and respond instantly.

## Why It Works
It trains sensory embodiment, active non-verbal listening, spontaneous responsiveness, and ensemble trust by requiring precise and intuitive reactions to a constantly evolving, shared imaginary object.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the play space is clear of physical hazards so players can safely embody different physicalities. Remind players to respect physical boundaries and avoid aggressive passes that might force unsafe physical reactions.

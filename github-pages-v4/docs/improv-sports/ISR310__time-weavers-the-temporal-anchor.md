# Time Weavers (The Temporal Anchor)

![Time Weavers (The Temporal Anchor) — infographic](img/ISR310.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams build a connected story across vastly different time periods by justifying a shared object passed between eras.

## Overview
A competitive narrative game where two teams build a connected story across vastly different time periods. A judge (the 'Chronoscribe') selects a specific object or detail from one scene and forces the next team to justify its existence in a completely new era. It tests a team's ability to seamlessly integrate unexpected offers and track narrative details across time.

## Setup
Two teams of 2-4 players. A Host to manage audience suggestions, pacing, and strict time limits. A Judge (the 'Chronoscribe') to select the objects and score the scenes.

## How to Play
1. The Host gets a starting time period (e.g., '1920s Paris') and an ordinary object (e.g., 'a silver spoon') from the audience.
2. Team A performs a fast-paced, 1 to 2-minute scene in that era, incorporating the object while establishing strong characters and relationships.
3. At the 1.5-minute mark, the Host calls 'Scene!' The Judge immediately identifies a new specific object or distinct physical detail that naturally emerged in Team A's scene (e.g., 'The dusty gramophone'). This becomes the 'Temporal Anchor'.
4. The Host asks the audience for a completely different time period (e.g., 'Ancient Egypt' or 'A Cyberpunk Future').
5. Team B steps up and performs a 1 to 2-minute scene in this new era. They must physically or verbally incorporate the 'Temporal Anchor' (the gramophone), justifying why it exists in this time and how it connects to the unfolding lore.
6. The Judge selects a new Anchor from Team B's scene, and play passes back to Team A for a new era.
7. Cap the game at exactly 3 or 4 scenes total to ensure a tight, punchy narrative arc without exhausting the audience.

## Coaching Notes
- Maintain strict 1-2 minute scene limits to keep high energy and prevent dragging.
- The Judge awards up to 5 points per scene based on three criteria: 1) How seamlessly the Temporal Anchor was justified in the new era, 2) Commitment to the physical object work, and 3) Narrative connection to the previous scenes.
- Encourage players to focus on collaborative storytelling, even though it is disguised as a competitive challenge.
- Remind players to establish strong characters and relationships quickly, rather than just talking about the object.

## Variations
- Genre Weavers: Instead of jumping through time periods, the Host asks the audience for wildly different film or theater genres (e.g., from Noir to Musical to Horror).
- Ensemble Montage: Remove the competitive aspect and teams. A single cast of 6-8 players performs this as a 10-minute long-form format, with a director calling the edits and naming the anchors.

## Why It Works
It forces deep listening, object permanence, and strong justification skills. The simple, clear mechanic of justifying a passed object in a new context creates a highly collaborative narrative disguised as a competitive challenge.

## Safety & Inclusion
When playing in different historical eras or cultures, players must avoid relying on harmful stereotypes, offensive accents, or caricatures. Focus on universal human relationships, status dynamics, and physical object work rather than mocking the time period. Ensure physical safety by establishing clear mime weight and dimensions for the Temporal Anchors so they are handled respectfully and safely.

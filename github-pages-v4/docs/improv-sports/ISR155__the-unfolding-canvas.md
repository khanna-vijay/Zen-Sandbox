# The Unfolding Canvas

![The Unfolding Canvas — infographic](img/ISR155.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A blindfolded Architect gently sculpts Clay Figures into an evolving, continuous human tableau.

## Overview
A non-competitive, facilitator-led ensemble exercise focusing on physical listening, consent, and continuous group creation. A blindfolded 'Architect' gently sculpts 'Clay Figures' into an evolving human tableau. As new figures are added to the connected sculpture, the oldest figures periodically melt away, creating a continuous ebb and flow of physical collaboration that forces players out of their heads and into their bodies.

## Setup
Participants stand loosely scattered in a clear, safe playing space. The facilitator mandates a rigorous 'Boundary Check-in' where every player demonstrates where they are comfortable being touched (e.g., 'shoulders and arms only' or 'no touch today, please'). One participant volunteers as the 'Architect' and closes their eyes or wears a blindfold. The facilitator acts as the dedicated 'Spotter.' All other players are 'Clay Figures.'

## How to Play
1. The Genesis: The facilitator (acting as Spotter) gently guides the blindfolded Architect through the space by the elbow to ensure absolute physical safety and prevent collisions.
2. Consent & Contact: The Spotter guides the Architect to a willing Clay Figure. Before making any physical contact, the Architect must ask, 'May I sculpt you?' The Clay Figure responds 'Yes' or 'No'. If no, the Spotter guides the Architect to another player.
3. The First Pose: Upon receiving verbal consent, the Architect gently touches the Clay Figure (respecting established boundaries). The Clay Figure immediately adopts a distinct, held physical pose as an intuitive, visceral response to the touch.
4. Shaping the Canvas: The Architect uses their hands to gently manipulate the Clay Figure's pose (e.g., adjusting an arm, tilting a shoulder). The Clay Figure yields to these adjustments, committing fully to the modified shape.
5. Expanding the Canvas: The Spotter guides the Architect to a new Clay Figure. After getting verbal consent, the Architect shapes them. This new figure must then physically connect to the first figure (e.g., a hand on a shoulder, a foot against a shoe) to begin forming a connected group sculpture.
6. The Rule of Release (The Melt): To keep the canvas dynamic and prevent physical fatigue, a 'release point' is established. Once the sculpture reaches four connected people, the addition of a fifth person cues the very first person to gently 'melt' out of their pose. They silently disengage and return to the pool of available Clay Figures.
7. Continuous Cycle: The process repeats continuously. The Architect shapes, the new figure connects, and the oldest figure melts. The sculpture slowly travels and morphs across the room.
8. Rotation: After 2-3 minutes, the facilitator calls 'Freeze,' names a new Architect, and the game resumes seamlessly from the existing sculpture.

## Coaching Notes
- The focus is entirely on the ensemble's internal process, physical listening, and mutual trust.
- Side-coach to maintain focus, ensure safety, and encourage players to surrender to the physical impulses rather than overthinking their poses.
- Point of Concentration: The quality of physical touch received/given, and the intuitive bodily response to shape and be shaped.

## Variations
- Air Sculpting (No-Touch Variation): For groups requiring a strict no-touch policy, the Architect keeps their eyes open. They 'sculpt' the Clay Figures from a few inches away using mime/proximity, and the Clay Figures mirror the implied manipulation. The connection to the sculpture is also done via close proximity rather than physical contact.
- Thematic Canvas: The facilitator provides a one-word prompt (e.g., 'Storm', 'Machinery', 'Grief', 'Joy') before a new Architect begins. The Architect and the Clay Figures allow this theme to subtly influence the types of shapes and connections they create.

## Why It Works
It forces players to rely entirely on non-verbal, physical connection to sustain the group creation. The Melting Mechanic prevents physical fatigue and keeps the exercise dynamic, ensuring the sculpture is an ever-evolving entity rather than a static pile-up. It also normalizes asking for and receiving verbal consent and respecting physical boundaries in real-time.

## Safety & Inclusion
Safety and consent are the foundational pillars of this game. A pre-game boundary check-in is mandatory. The Architect must ask for verbal consent ('May I sculpt you?') before every single interaction. The facilitator must act as a dedicated Spotter, physically guiding the blindfolded Architect to prevent trips, falls, or accidental inappropriate contact. Players who prefer not to be touched can opt to be 'Air Sculpted' or simply observe.

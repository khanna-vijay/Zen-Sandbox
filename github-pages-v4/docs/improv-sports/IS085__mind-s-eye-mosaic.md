# Mind's Eye Mosaic

![Mind's Eye Mosaic — infographic](img/IS085.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Three improvisers collaboratively portray a single character grappling with a dilemma by embodying their physical self, logical mind, and emotional core.

## Overview
Mind's Eye Mosaic is a collaborative improv game where three improvisers portray a single character grappling with an internal or external dilemma. One player acts as the 'Core Self,' physically embodying the character, while the other two articulate the character's distinct 'Logical' and 'Emotional' voices. The objective is to collaboratively forge a cohesive yet conflicted narrative, making the character's internal struggle tangible.

## Setup
Three performers stand in a tight huddle or line, facing the audience. The Core Self stands slightly forward or centrally, while the Logical and Emotional Voices flank them, ideally making slight eye contact with the Core Self. Obtain audience suggestions for a Character, a Location/Situation, and a Dilemma/Goal.

## How to Play
1. The Core Self establishes the character's initial posture and general emotional state based on the audience suggestion.
2. As the dilemma unfolds, the Logical Voice and Emotional Voice speak aloud, often in rapid succession or simultaneously, portraying the character's internal thoughts and feelings directly to the Core Self.
3. The Core Self must react physically and emotionally to every internal suggestion, synthesizing these conflicting inputs into actions, facial expressions, and body language.
4. The Logical and Emotional Voices actively listen to each other, using 'Yes, And' to build on, contradict, or escalate the dilemma from their respective perspectives.
5. The Core Self can optionally speak aloud to an imaginary scene partner or narrate their actions, but only as a result of their internal voices' influence or synthesis.
6. The scene concludes when the character makes a pivotal decision, reaches a crisis point, or resolves their immediate dilemma, directly driven by the internal struggle portrayed.

## Coaching Notes
- The Core Self doesn't simply pick one voice; they must embody the struggle, the compromise, or the overwhelming nature of the internal conflict.
- Internal Voices must use distinct vocal qualities, pacing, and intensity to differentiate their 'voices' and emotional weight.
- Remind the Internal Voices to 'Yes, And' each other's ideas (even when opposing) to deepen the character's dilemma.
- The Physical Self is constantly being changed by the dynamic internal landscape, and Internal Voices must adapt their arguments based on the evolving scene and physical responses.
- Maintain a dynamic pace; the game's energy comes from the rapid-fire internal debate and the Core Self's agile physical responses.

## Why It Works
It promotes a deep, internal character arc driven by inner conflict and choice, moving beyond superficial plots. Performers are forced to rapidly 'Yes, And' disparate impulses, transforming potential contradictions into rich, nuanced character moments or comedic internal chaos, allowing the audience to witness the genesis of character decisions.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical boundaries are respected when standing in a tight huddle. Players should be mindful of the emotional intensity of the internal voices to avoid overwhelming the Core Self player, maintaining a supportive collaborative environment.

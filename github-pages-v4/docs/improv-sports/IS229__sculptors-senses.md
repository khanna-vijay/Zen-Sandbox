# Sculptors & Senses

![Sculptors & Senses — infographic](img/IS229.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two Sculptors silently collaborate to physically embody a bizarre audience suggestion before a Critic interrogates it with rapid-fire sensory questions.

## Overview
Two 'Sculptors' silently create a complex physical tableau based on a bizarre audience suggestion. A third player, the 'Critic,' then interrogates their 'sculpture' with rapid-fire sensory questions (smell, sound, touch, taste), forcing the Sculptors to verbally justify and physically embellish their creation on the spot. It's a game of ultimate 'Yes, And', shared hallucination, and quick-witted sensory justification under pressure.

## Setup
3 players take center stage: two 'Sculptors' center-stage and one 'Critic' slightly off to the side. The rest of the competing team (red/blue) acts as the bench. No props are used; all objects and environments are mimed. The Referee asks the audience for a wild, imaginative concept for the Sculptors to embody.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a wild, imaginative concept from the audience for the Sculptors to embody.
2. The two Sculptors have a strict time limit (45-60 seconds, enforced by the Referee) to silently and physically create a shared tableau or a single, cohesive 'sculpture' based on the suggestion.
3. After the timed sculpting phase, the Critic steps in and begins a rapid-fire interrogation without touching the Sculptors, who must hold their basic pose.
4. The Critic asks highly specific sensory questions exploring sight, sound, touch, smell, and taste about different parts, functions, or elements of the sculpture.
5. For every question, the Sculptors must provide an immediate, funny, and consistent verbal answer, often accompanied by a small physical embellishment or facial reaction.
6. The Sculptors' answers must 'Yes, And' previous descriptions and their own physical choices without contradicting anything already established.
7. The game ends either when the Critic has exhausted their questions or the Referee calls time after 2-3 minutes of interrogation.

## Coaching Notes
- Sculptors must use their bodies, facial expressions, and collaborative 'Yes, And' physical offers to bring the suggestion to life non-verbally.
- The Critic should fire questions quickly to maintain pace, and Sculptors must match that pace without hesitating.
- Scoring for Sculptors: +5 points for a clear initial sculpture, +2 points per swift/witty sensory answer, +3 bonus points for brilliant physical embellishments.
- Scoring for Critic: +2 points per challenging sensory question, +5 points for maintaining high energy/pacing, +5 bonus points for ingenious insights.
- Fouls (Referee Calls): -5 points for 'Content Foul' (inappropriate humor), -5 points for 'Groaner' (clichéd response), -10 points for 'No, But...' (contradicting offers), -5 points for 'Frozen Brain' (hesitating 4+ seconds), and -5 points for 'Vague Vision' (Critic asking overly general questions).

## Why It Works
This high-pressure game hinges on ultimate 'Yes, And' principles, demanding shared hallucination and quick-witted sensory justification from the performers.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Critic must interrogate the sculpture without physically touching the Sculptors. The Referee enforces content boundaries via the content foul for off-color, inappropriate, or suggestive humor.

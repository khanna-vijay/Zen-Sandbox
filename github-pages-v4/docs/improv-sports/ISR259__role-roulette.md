# Role Roulette

![Role Roulette — infographic](img/ISR259.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive guessing game where two players subtly embody secret character endowments while their benched teammates try to deduce them.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive guessing game where two players (one from each team) start a mundane scene, then secretly receive audience-suggested character endowments. They must subtly embody these traits through physical action and dialogue without saying them out loud, while their respective benched teammates try to deduce the endowments for points.

## Setup
One player from the Red Team and one from the Blue Team take the stage. The remaining team members (the 'Guessers') put on soundproof headphones or step into an isolated booth so they cannot hear the audience. The Referee asks the audience for a mundane scene location (e.g., 'a laundromat' or 'a DMV waiting room') and a category for endowments (e.g., 'Quirky Professions' or 'Bizarre Phobias').

## How to Play
1. Isolate the Guessers: The Referee ensures the guessing players from both teams cannot hear or see the stage (using soundproof headphones or sending them offstage).
2. Gather the Endowments: The Referee gets the mundane location from the audience, then asks for several energetic suggestions in a specific character category (e.g., 'Give me weird hobbies!').
3. Assign the Roles: The Referee selects two distinct suggestions. They explicitly announce to the audience and the two performing players which endowment belongs to the Red player and which belongs to the Blue player.
4. Bring Back the Guessers: The guessing teammates remove their headphones and return to their benches. The Referee tells them the mundane location, but keeps the endowments a secret.
5. Play the Scene: The Referee yells 'Go!' The two performers begin the scene. They must organically integrate their secret endowments into their physicality, object work, and dialogue, interacting with each other while dropping clues for their benches.
6. The Guessing Phase: After 60-90 seconds of scene work, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Freeze!'
7. Scoring the Guesses: The Red Team bench has 10 seconds to guess the Red player's endowment. Then, the Blue Team bench has 10 seconds to guess the Blue player's endowment. Award 5 points for a correct first guess, 3 points for a second guess, and 1 point for a third guess.

## Coaching Notes
- Emphasize physicality over exposition: force players to use object work, posture, and behavior rather than just talking about their trait.
- Encourage cross-team collaboration: even though they are competing, the two performers must 'Yes, And' each other to keep the scene grounded while dropping their respective clues.
- Leverage dramatic irony: the audience knows the secrets, making the performers' subtle clues and the guessers' reactions highly entertaining.
- Enforce fouls: call a 'content foul' (-1 point) for inappropriate/blue content, or an 'explicit foul' (-1 point) if a player simply says their endowment out loud instead of acting it out.

## Variations
- The Roulette Pool (No Headphones): If you don't have soundproof headphones, the Referee gets 6 suggestions from the audience and writes them on a visible whiteboard. The Referee then secretly hands a card to each performer with one of the 6 items. The guessers stay in the room the whole time, but must deduce WHICH of the 6 options their teammate is playing.
- Emotion Escalator: Instead of professions or hobbies, players are endowed with an emotion that must escalate from a level 1 (barely noticeable) to a level 10 (completely unhinged) by the time the Referee calls Freeze.

## Why It Works
It forces players to use object work, posture, and behavior rather than just talking about their trait. The audience is highly engaged through dramatic irony, as they are 'in on the secret' and get to watch the performers struggle to convey the clues subtly to their confused teammates.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee strictly controls the suggestion categories to ensure they remain family-friendly and avoid harmful stereotypes (e.g., asking for 'Hobbies' or 'Phobias of inanimate objects' rather than 'Accents' or 'Mental Illnesses'). If a player's physical embodiment becomes unsafe, relies on punching down, or uses offensive tropes, the Referee should immediately blow the whistle, call a foul, and pivot the endowment to maintain a safe, inclusive environment.

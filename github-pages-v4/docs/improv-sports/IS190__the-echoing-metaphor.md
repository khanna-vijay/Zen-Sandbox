# The Echoing Metaphor

![The Echoing Metaphor — infographic](img/IS190.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers build a scene whose explicit plot subtly and powerfully echoes the narrative arc of an abstract concept without ever explicitly referring to it.

## Overview
The Echoing Metaphor is a collaborative improvisational game where performers are given an abstract concept with a clear progression. They must collaboratively build a scene whose explicit plot and characters' interactions subtly and powerfully echo the narrative arc and essential qualities of that concept, from its beginning to its resolution, without ever explicitly referring to it. This challenging exercise demands deep listening, intricate subtext, and unified group mind as players embody the concept's changing nature.

## Setup
Ask the audience to provide abstract concepts that imply a clear process or journey (e.g., 'The Growth of a Sapling', 'The Flow of a River', 'A Forgotten Memory'). The host or a judge selects one and reveals it to the performers and audience simultaneously. Set a clear time limit for the scene, typically 3-4 minutes.

## How to Play
1. The Starting Echo: Players immediately begin a scene where their initial characters, setting, and conflict subtly reflect the beginning state or quality of the abstract concept.
2. Metaphorical Progression: As the scene unfolds, players must collectively guide the narrative to implicitly mirror the natural progression of the abstract concept, including its beginning, middle, a significant turning point/climax, and a resolution/end.
3. Embodied Qualities, Not Literal Portrayal (The Golden Rule): Performers are strictly forbidden from explicitly mentioning the abstract concept or using direct terms related to it. They must instead embody the qualities, emotions, and physicality associated with that concept's arc through their characters, dialogue, and movement.
4. Collaborative Sensing: All players must be highly attuned to the evolving metaphorical state. Each offer and acceptance should subtly nudge the scene further along the abstract concept's journey, interpreting 'mistakes' as obstacles inherent to the metaphor.
5. Metaphorical Resolution: The scene must reach a conclusion that metaphorically aligns with the end state of the abstract concept (e.g., arrival, completion, transformation, decay, or new potential).

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: The entire game hinges on performers building a metaphorical narrative arc that mirrors the provided concept.
- Embracing Mistakes: Unforeseen turns in the scene become opportunities to reflect the struggles, diversions, or challenges inherent in the abstract concept's journey.
- Active Support (Making Your Partner Look Good): Players must not only advance their own character but actively contribute to the overall thematic resonance, ensuring their partners' contributions fit the evolving metaphor.
- Being Changed: Players must constantly be receptive to their partners' contributions, allowing the abstract concept's arc to change their characters and the scene's direction.
- Subtext, Physicality & Voice: Performers are challenged to use their bodies and voices to embody the qualities and changes inherent in the abstract concept (e.g., vulnerability, expansion, turbulence, stillness, decay).
- Scoring/Evaluation: Judges evaluate teams based on Narrative Arc Fidelity, Metaphorical Depth & Consistency, Collaborative Resonance, and Audience Engagement & Clarity.

## Why It Works
It pushes improvisers to think on multiple layers simultaneously, fostering profound narrative and collaborative connection. The abstract concept provides a powerful, unspoken layer of subtext that informs every character choice, relationship, and scene progression, demanding deep listening and group mind.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical choices used to embody abstract concepts (like turbulence or erratic movement) are performed safely without risking injury to oneself or scene partners. Maintain emotional check-ins if the abstract concept leads to intense emotional themes.

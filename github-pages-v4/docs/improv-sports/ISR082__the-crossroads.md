# The Crossroads

![The Crossroads — infographic](img/ISR082.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Improvisers build a scene to a high-stakes climax, freezing so an audience member can make a strict binary choice that dictates the outcome.

## Overview
In this narrative-driven game, improvisers build a scene toward a high-stakes climax. Just as the characters face a massive dilemma, the Director freezes the action and turns to a seated audience member, presenting them with a strict binary choice. The audience member's decision dictates the outcome, and the improvisers must instantly unfreeze, justify the choice, and play out the consequences.

## Setup
Requires 2-4 improvisers and a Director or Host to call the freeze and interact with the audience. Fits competitive improv (Narrative Long-Form). Use a standard stage where the Director has easy access to the front row of the audience.

## How to Play
1. 1. The Suggestion: The Director gets a suggestion (e.g., a location or a relationship) to inspire a grounded, narrative scene.
2. 2. Building the Stakes: The improvisers begin the scene, focusing on establishing strong characters, clear relationships, and a brewing conflict. Their goal is to drive the narrative toward a significant, high-stakes dilemma or climax.
3. 3. The Freeze: Right at the peak of the tension, just before the crucial decision is made, the Director yells 'Freeze!' or 'Crossroads!' The improvisers immediately freeze in their physical positions.
4. 4. The Binary Choice: The Director steps forward, addresses a single audience member in the front row, concisely summarizes the dilemma, and offers two distinct, binary options.
5. 5. The Verdict: The seated audience member simply states their choice. No acting, character work, or stage time is required from the volunteer.
6. 6. The Resolution: The Director yells 'Action!' The improvisers unfreeze and must immediately execute the audience member's choice, fully committing to the emotional and narrative consequences.
7. 7. The End: The scene concludes shortly after the resolution is fully played out, ending on a strong dramatic or comedic beat.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus heavily on narrative structure, stakes, and emotional commitment to earn the climax.
- Preserve scene momentum by limiting the interruption to exactly one major climax point.
- Improvisers must immediately justify, adapt, and Yes-And the unexpected offer from the audience.
- In a judged competitive improv format, score the scene (1-5 points) based on how well the improvisers built the narrative tension, the clarity of the dilemma, and how brilliantly they justified the chosen resolution.

## Variations
- The Moral Compass: Instead of a plot-based choice, the Director asks the audience member a moral question (e.g., 'Do they do the right thing and turn in the money, or the selfish thing and keep it?').
- The Genre Shift: The audience chooses between two distinct genres for the resolution (e.g., 'Does this scene end like a Shakespearean tragedy or a slapstick comedy?').

## Why It Works
It tests the improvisers' ability to build tension and adapt instantly, while giving the audience direct control over the story's resolution without the pressure of performing. It forces improvisers to immediately justify, adapt, and Yes-And unexpected offers.

## Safety & Inclusion
Keeping the audience member in their seat removes the anxiety and physical accessibility barriers of coming on stage. The Director acts as a safety buffer, ensuring the binary choices offered are appropriate, all-ages friendly, and do not involve crossing physical boundaries or introducing offensive content.

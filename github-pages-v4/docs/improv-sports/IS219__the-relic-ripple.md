# The Relic Ripple

![The Relic Ripple — infographic](img/IS219.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Non-verbal emotional and historical transfer through a shared object.

## Overview
The Relic Ripple is a novel improv game where players collectively build an evolving, non-verbal emotional narrative around a single, unremarkable object. Inspired by Hot Potato and object transformation, participants sit in a circle, passing a Relic. Each player non-verbally imbues the object with an emotion or implied history through their physical interaction, then conveys that accumulated weight through the manner of the pass.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle. A single, common, and unremarkable object is chosen (e.g., a smooth stone, a simple key, a small piece of wood, a plastic bottle cap). This object is designated 'The Relic'.

## How to Play
1. One player volunteers to start. They take The Relic and, for a few moments, physically imbue it with an unspoken emotion, a memory, or the resonance of a significant past event entirely non-verbally.
2. The initiating player passes The Relic to the person immediately to their left or right. They attempt to convey the emotional weight and implied history through the manner of the pass itself (speed, tension, delicacy, reluctance, or urgency).
3. The receiving player intuitively perceives and accepts the emotional charge, physically reflecting their gut understanding of its current state or history (e.g., recoiling, cherishing, examining suspiciously).
4. The new player quickly internalizes the perceived history and adds their own layer to The Relic's accumulating emotional journey through their unique physical interaction with it, still entirely non-verbally.
5. This process continues around the circle. Each player receives, interprets, internalizes, amplifies or transforms, and passes the object.
6. Absolutely no verbal communication about the story, emotion, or interpretation is allowed at any point.
7. After several rounds, or when a distinct narrative arc has organically emerged, stop the game and optionally debrief, inviting players to share what they felt The Relic represented.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Intuitively receive the unspoken emotional/historical weight of The Relic from the manner of its pass, and then authentically contribute a new, layered chapter solely through physical interaction.
- Focus on how the 'relic' is experiencing the transfer during the pass.
- Do not announce or vocalize anything about its 'story' or 'emotion'. Facial expressions should be with the object, not at others.
- Bypass intellectual analysis and self-censorship; rely purely on gut feelings and physical impulses.
- The game requires no 'acting' in the traditional sense; leverage natural human empathy, observation, and physical response.

## Why It Works
It develops intuitive and embodied storytelling, empathic resonance, and the ability to read subtext. It deepens the understanding of object transformation and relationship, demanding full presence and spontaneous responsiveness. It builds profound group connection and ensemble listening through the co-creation of a shared, evolving narrative, relying on collective intuition and non-verbal attunement.

## Safety & Inclusion
The clear focus on The Relic and the playful structure of passing a history removes the pressure of personal performance or getting it wrong. Each contribution, regardless of interpretation, becomes a valid part of the ongoing ripple, fostering a safe space for experimentation and discovery.

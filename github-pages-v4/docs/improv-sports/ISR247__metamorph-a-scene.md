# Metamorph-A-Scene

![Metamorph-A-Scene — infographic](img/ISR247.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must instantly adopt and justify extreme, audience-suggested emotions or physical ailments assigned mid-scene by the Referee.

## Overview
A high-energy, competitive short-form game where players perform a scene while the Referee periodically interrupts to assign them new, audience-suggested emotions or physical ailments. Players must instantly adopt the new trait and creatively justify the sudden change within the ongoing narrative, testing their ability to balance extreme character shifts with scene continuity.

## Setup
Two players from a single team take the stage. The Referee stands downstage or to the side with a whistle. Before the scene begins, the Referee asks the audience for a mundane location or relationship. Then, the Referee asks the audience to rapid-fire shout out 6 to 8 extreme emotions or physical ailments (e.g., 'paranoia', 'uncontrollably itchy', 'furious', 'legs made of jelly'), which the Referee writes down or memorizes.

## How to Play
1. The Referee establishes the audience-suggested location and relationship, then yells 'Play Ball!' to start the scene.
2. The players begin a grounded scene, establishing their base characters, the environment, and a clear objective.
3. After 45 to 60 seconds of solid scene work, the Referee blows the whistle, points at one specific player, and yells 'METAMORPH: [Insert Emotion/Ailment]!' (e.g., 'METAMORPH: Terrified of wood!').
4. The targeted player must instantaneously adopt this new physical or emotional state.
5. Crucially, the players must immediately justify this sudden change using the existing reality of the scene. They cannot invent a random new character or break the reality; they must find a reason why their current character suddenly feels this way (e.g., if they become 'furious' at a bakery, it's because the cupcakes are slightly asymmetrical).
6. The scene continues with this new dynamic. The Referee waits at least 45 to 60 seconds before making another call, giving the players time to explore the new reality, heighten the emotion, and advance the narrative.
7. The Referee continues to call 'METAMORPH!' periodically, assigning new traits from the audience's list to either player.
8. The game concludes after 3 to 4 minutes, ideally ending on a high-energy justification or a natural narrative resolution.
9. Scoring: The Referee awards 5 points for every 'Metamorphosis' that is instantly adopted and logically justified within the scene's narrative. An additional 5 bonus points can be awarded at the end of the game if the team successfully maintained a coherent story arc despite the chaotic changes.

## Coaching Notes
- Referee-driven pacing guarantees high energy while preventing player panic. Be sure to wait the full 45 to 60 seconds between calls to let the players explore.
- Focus on immediate, grounded justification of absurd or sudden changes.
- Maintain narrative continuity by restricting changes to emotions and physical states rather than entirely new characters.
- The audience's laughter and applause help the Referee gauge the success of the justifications.
- Highly accessible with zero props or setup required.

## Variations
- Infected Metamorph: The Referee assigns a physical ailment to one player. Whenever that player physically touches the other player, the 'infection' passes to them, and the first player is cured.
- Metamorph-A-Prop: Instead of emotions, the Referee calls out 'METAMORPH!' and hands the players a random, bizarre prop mid-scene. The players must instantly justify why this object is vital to their current situation.

## Why It Works
It develops the skill of immediate, grounded justification. By restricting changes to emotions and physical states rather than entirely new characters, players learn to balance extreme character shifts with scene continuity and narrative logic.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee must strictly filter audience suggestions for ailments. Explicitly ban the use of real-world medical conditions, mental illnesses, or marginalized identities as 'traits.' Keep physical endowments to universal, cartoonish, or sensory states (e.g., 'bouncy,' 'heavy,' 'itchy,' 'smells something terrible'). The Referee enforces a clean-content call for any inappropriate content, ensuring the game remains clean and all-ages.

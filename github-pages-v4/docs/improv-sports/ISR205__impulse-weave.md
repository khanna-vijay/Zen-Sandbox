# Impulse Weave

![Impulse Weave — infographic](img/ISR205.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players pass a sound and movement around a circle, letting the impulse physically affect them and transforming it completely with each pass.

## Overview
A fast-paced, non-verbal warm-up where players pass a sound and movement around a circle, transforming it completely with each pass. Instead of mimicking what they receive, players let the impulse physically affect them and immediately send a brand-new sound and movement to someone else. It trains the ensemble to stop overthinking, make strong eye contact, and react physically in the moment.

## Setup
Players stand in a clear, open circle to ensure everyone has perfect sightlines. No props or audience required. A facilitator stands outside the circle to side-coach and maintain the pace.

## How to Play
1. Form a circle. The facilitator asks the group to stand comfortably with their arms loose, ready to move.
2. Player A initiates by making strong eye contact with Player B across the circle.
3. Player A sends a distinct sound and physical motion toward Player B (e.g., stomping a foot and shouting 'Hey!').
4. Player B receives the impulse and lets it physically affect them, rather than just watching it or mimicking it. They must immediately transform it into a completely NEW sound and motion.
5. Example of Transformation: Player A sends a sharp clap and a 'Ha!'. Player B reacts by recoiling slightly from the imaginary force, which they immediately turn into a sweeping arm motion and a long 'Whoosh!' sound.
6. Player B then makes eye contact with Player C and sends this new 'Whoosh!' impulse to them.
7. The chain continues rapidly. Players must not plan their response in advance; they must rely on their immediate physical reaction to whatever is thrown at them.

## Coaching Notes
- The facilitator side-coaches to keep the pace fast: 'Don't think!', 'Let it change!', 'Strong eye contact!', 'React with your body!'
- Remind players there are no points, judges, or audience. The goal is ensemble connection and process over product.
- The 'win' is achieving a seamless, unthinking flow of physical reactions that bypasses the intellect.

## Variations
- Scattered Weave (Advanced): Once the circle is mastered, players walk freely around the room. To pass the impulse, they must catch someone's eye across the moving space, requiring heightened spatial awareness and clear sightlines.
- Emotion Weave: Players infuse their sound and motion with a distinct emotion. The receiver lets that emotion impact them, which naturally sparks a different emotional response to pass on (e.g., receiving 'anger' might make the next player physically react with 'fear', which they then pass to the next person).

## Why It Works
It isolates non-verbal communication and eye contact, forcing physical reactions over intellectual planning. This bypasses the intellect and builds ensemble focus and listening before scene work.

## Safety & Inclusion
No physical contact is required. Players should be encouraged to use whatever range of motion and vocal volume is comfortable and safe for their bodies. Facilitators should ensure the space is clear of tripping hazards, especially if using the scattered variation. The game relies on eye contact, so for players who find direct eye contact inaccessible, looking at the sender's forehead or shoulders is a perfectly acceptable alternative.

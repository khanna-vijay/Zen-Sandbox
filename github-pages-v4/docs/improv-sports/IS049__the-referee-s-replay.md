# The Referee's Replay

![The Referee's Replay — infographic](img/IS049.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A dynamic scene frequently halted by a referee who provides sports-commentary-style narration, assigns new character traits, or introduces game-changing plot twists.

## Overview
The Referee's Replay is a dynamic, short-form game where improvisers perform a scene that is frequently paused by the referee. During these 'replays,' the referee offers live, sports-commentary-style narration, challenges players with new character traits, or introduces game-changing scene details, which the improvisers must instantly integrate when the scene resumes.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 players divided between two teams. Use a standard competitive short-form stage layout with no props (all mimed). Get an initial suggestion from the audience (e.g., a location, relationship, or conflict) and encourage them to react loudly to the referee's commentary and the players' adaptations.

## How to Play
1. The referee takes a suggestion from the audience and the players begin an improvised scene.
2. At any point, the referee can shout, 'INSTANT REPLAY!' All players must immediately freeze in place, holding their positions and expressions.
3. While players are frozen, the referee steps forward and delivers an intervention from the 'Replay Menu' in a sports commentator's voice.
4. The referee can use 'Play-by-Play Commentary' to narrate, add internal thoughts, or offer a comedic prediction.
5. The referee can use a 'Player Challenge' to assign a specific player a new trait or action to perform.
6. The referee can use a 'Situation Update' to introduce a new, often absurd, piece of information or external factor.
7. The referee can use a 'Skill Spotcheck' to call out a specific improv fundamental for a player to immediately demonstrate (e.g., better object work).
8. After delivering the intervention, the referee shouts, 'PLAY ON!'
9. Players unfreeze and immediately continue the scene, incorporating the new information, challenge, or commentary as if it had been part of the scene all along.
10. The scene continues with potential for multiple 'Instant Replays' until the referee calls 'Time!' or a natural comedic conclusion is reached.

## Coaching Notes
- Award points for Seamless Integration (5 points), Character Embodiment (3 points), and Quick Wit/Strong Physicality (2 points).
- Call 'The Double Take Foul' (-3 points) if a player visibly pauses, looks confused, or struggles excessively before integrating the referee's input.
- Call 'Ignoring the Call Foul' (-5 points) for clearly failing to acknowledge or incorporate the referee's specific challenge or situation update.
- Call 'Missed Opportunity Foul' (-2 points) if the referee's commentary sets up a clear comedic or narrative opportunity that players entirely miss.
- Standard competitive short-form fouls (clean-content foul, groaner foul, etc.) still apply.
- The referee should act as a creative catalyst, dynamic narrator, and real-time improv coach, using their 'replay' power strategically to elevate the comedy and drive the scene in unexpected directions.

## Why It Works
The game demands instantaneous adaptation and pure 'Yes, And...' as players must accept the referee's new reality and build upon it without question. It promotes active listening to grasp specific instructions, quick wit to generate humorous justifications in real-time, and strong physical choices when responding to situation updates or skill spotchecks.

## Safety & Inclusion
Keep it Family-Friendly: All referee commentary and challenges should be designed to escalate the comedy, not to be crude or exclusionary. The focus remains on imaginative play and clever wit, ensuring the content is always appropriate for all ages.

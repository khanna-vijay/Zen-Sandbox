# Skill Shift Scenes

![Skill Shift Scenes — infographic](img/ISR218.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players perform scenes hyper-focused on a single improv fundamental, followed by targeted ensemble feedback.

## Overview
A facilitator-led workshop exercise where players perform scenes while hyper-focusing on a single, rotating improv fundamental, such as object work, emotional heightening, or status. Instead of points, the ensemble provides targeted feedback at the end of each scene, allowing players to practice specific muscles without mid-scene interruptions.

## Setup
A clear playing space. A facilitator with a list or deck of 'Focus Cards' (e.g., Physicality/Object Work, Emotional Escalation, Status Shifts, Silence/Pacing, Yes-And/Agreement). A whiteboard or display to show the current focus to the players and the observing ensemble.

## How to Play
1. Set the Focus: The facilitator reveals the first Focus Card and briefly defines what success looks like for this specific skill.
2. Get a Suggestion: Two to three players step up. The facilitator gets a simple suggestion (location or relationship) to ground the scene.
3. Play the Scene: The players begin the scene, integrating the active Focus Card into their scene work as much as possible while maintaining a coherent narrative.
4. Uninterrupted Flow: The facilitator allows the scene to run its natural course (usually 2 to 3 minutes) without stopping the action or shouting out feedback.
5. End and Debrief: The facilitator calls 'Scene!' and leads a discussion with the observing ensemble specifically through the lens of the Focus Card.
6. Rotate and Shift: New players step up, the facilitator draws a new Focus Card, and the process repeats.

## Coaching Notes
- Keep feedback constructive, focusing on what was added rather than what was missed.
- Use the post-scene debriefs to train the ensemble to observe and articulate improv mechanics.
- The game is highly modular; create custom Focus Cards based on the group's current weaknesses.
- If performed in a showcase, ask the audience to shout out which 'Focus' they want the players to tackle next.

## Variations
- Mid-Scene Shift: For advanced players, the facilitator rings a bell mid-scene and holds up a new Focus Card. Players must instantly pivot their playstyle while justifying the shift in the narrative.
- Secret Focus: The players in the scene know the Focus Card, but the observing ensemble does not. After the scene, the ensemble must guess what the focus was based on the players' choices.

## Why It Works
It isolates specific improv fundamentals for targeted practice, while uninterrupted scene work builds stamina and flow. The debriefs also train the observing ensemble to actively watch and articulate improv mechanics.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure Focus Cards are accessible to all players (e.g., adapt 'Physicality' to focus on facial expressions or vocal variation for players with limited mobility). When using 'Emotional Escalation,' remind players of emotional safety boundaries and establish a tap-out mechanic if a scene becomes too intense.

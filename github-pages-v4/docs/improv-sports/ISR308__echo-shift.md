# Echo Shift

![Echo Shift — infographic](img/ISR308.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Recontextualize memorable lines into brand-new scenes using completely contrasting emotions.

## Overview
Echo Shift is a fast-paced ensemble game that practices emotional justification and active listening. Players perform a series of short, unconnected scenes. After each scene, the facilitator grabs a memorable line of dialogue, asks the audience for a completely contrasting emotion, and challenges the next pair of improvisers to build a brand-new scene around that exact line delivered with the new emotion.

## Setup
A facilitator stands downstage or to the side. The ensemble of players stands on the wings or in a backline, ready to rotate in. No props or chairs are strictly necessary, though two chairs can be kept upstage for scene work.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator brings two players center stage and gets a basic suggestion (e.g., a location or relationship) to start a 1- to 2-minute scene.
2. The players perform the scene. The facilitator actively listens for a distinct, memorable line of dialogue (the 'Echo').
3. The facilitator calls 'Scene!' to end the first scene, then turns to the audience and announces the Echo line (e.g., 'The line is: I never want to see that dog again.').
4. The facilitator asks the audience for an emotion that sharply contrasts with how the line was originally delivered (e.g., 'They said it with disgust. Shout out a completely different emotion!' -> Audience yells 'Lust!').
5. Two new players step in for the next scene. They must use the Echo line, delivered with the newly assigned emotion, as a prominent feature of their completely new, unrelated scene.
6. The players must emotionally justify why the line is being said with that specific feeling, establishing a new who/what/where based entirely on that justification.
7. The facilitator lets the scene run for 1-2 minutes, pulls a new Echo line from this second scene, gets a new emotion from the audience, and brings up two new players. Repeat until the whole ensemble has played.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus on emotional justification rather than plot continuity.
- Train active listening and recognizing 'hook' lines in scene partners.
- Keep the game fast-paced with zero downtime for secret instructions or whispering.
- Lean into the dramatic irony and comedic payoff of watching a familiar line take on a wildly unexpected meaning.

## Variations
- First Line Echo: The new scene MUST begin with the Echo line and the assigned emotion as the very first words spoken, forcing immediate justification and high stakes.
- Genre Shift: Instead of asking the audience for an emotion, the facilitator asks for a film or theatre genre (e.g., Western, Horror, Soap Opera) to recontextualize the line.

## Why It Works
It strips away complex narrative weaving in favor of immediate, highly entertaining emotional recontextualization, training players to actively listen for 'hook' lines and prioritize emotional justification over plot continuity.

## Safety & Inclusion
When asking for intense emotions (like anger or fear), remind players to maintain physical safety and boundaries; loud emotions do not require physical aggression. The facilitator must act as a filter, ensuring the chosen 'Echo' lines are appropriate and avoiding lines that could be problematic or offensive when taken out of their original context.

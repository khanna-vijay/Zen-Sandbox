# Cipher Scene

![Cipher Scene — infographic](img/ISR238.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers hide a secret, audience-suggested behavioral quirk in plain sight while the opposing team tries to decode it.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where one team performs a grounded scene while hiding a secret, audience-suggested behavioral quirk in plain sight. The opposing team acts as decoders who watch the scene and try to guess the exact quirk. It relies heavily on dramatic irony, as the audience knows the secret and watches the performers try to drop clues without breaking the reality of the scene.

## Setup
Two teams of 3 to 4 players. A Referee to host and judge. Noise-canceling headphones for the guessing team, or a designated off-stage isolation area so they cannot hear the audience suggestion.

## How to Play
1. Isolation: The Referee sends Team B (the Decoders) out of earshot using noise-canceling headphones or an off-stage booth.
2. The Suggestion: The Referee asks the audience for a specific, playable behavioral quirk or minor obsession (e.g., secretly believes they are a dog, obsessed with the texture of velvet, always checks their pulse).
3. The Scene: Team A (the Performers) gets 60 to 90 seconds to perform a single, continuous, grounded scene. One designated player is the Cipher who has the quirk, while their teammates support and justify the behavior. There are no scene interruptions, freezes, or gimmick injections.
4. The Guess: At the buzzer, Team B returns. They have 15 seconds to huddle, then must deliver one single, specific guess about what the quirk was.
5. The Reveal and Swap: The Referee reveals the true suggestion, awards points, and the teams swap roles for round two.

## Coaching Notes
- Cooperative scoring: If Team B guesses the quirk correctly, BOTH teams receive 5 points. This forces Team A to make the quirk guessable while challenging Team B to pay close attention.
- Overt Clue foul: If Team A explicitly states the quirk out loud, the Referee deducts 2 points.
- Use the audience: Their laughter serves as a hot/cold meter for the performers, rewarding subtle hints and dramatic irony.
- Keep it grounded: Focus on justifying bizarre behavior in a realistic setting rather than relying on chaotic interruptions.

## Variations
- Contagious Cipher: The quirk starts with one player, but by the end of the 90 seconds, the entire performing team must have caught and justified the behavior.
- Prop Cipher: Instead of a behavioral quirk, the secret endowment is a bizarre emotional attachment or phobia regarding a specific, mundane prop on stage.

## Why It Works
Cooperative scoring aligns the incentives so performers want the guessers to succeed, ensuring the quirk is actually played. The short duration prevents the single joke from overstaying its welcome, and dramatic irony makes subtle choices hilarious.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee must strictly filter suggestions to ensure they are behavioral (e.g., obsessed with lint) rather than identity-based, medical, or related to disabilities and neurodivergence (e.g., rejecting tics or OCD suggestions). Standard short-form fouls apply for any crude or inappropriate content to maintain a family-friendly environment.

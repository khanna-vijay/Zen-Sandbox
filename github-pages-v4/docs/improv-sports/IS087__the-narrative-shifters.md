# The Narrative Shifters

![The Narrative Shifters — infographic](img/IS087.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An external 'Changer' periodically intervenes in a scene, assigning players new, absurd character traits they must instantly embody.

## Overview
2-3 actors collaboratively build a scene and characters, but with a unique twist: an external 'Changer' periodically intervenes, assigning one player a new, often absurd, character trait or motivation. The affected player must instantly and seamlessly embody this radical shift, while their scene partners must organically react, justifying the new reality within the evolving narrative. This dynamic challenges performers to demonstrate extreme adaptability, creative narrative justification, and a joyful embrace of spontaneous, profound character changes.

## Setup
A Host or designated 'Changer' (who can be a judge or pre-selected audience member) oversees the game. Get a setting/location from the audience (e.g., 'A deep-sea research lab'). The improvisers immediately initiate a scene within that setting, establishing their characters, initial relationship, and an early objective or conflict.

## How to Play
1. The improvisers begin the scene, establishing a clear direction based on the audience suggestion.
2. At random intervals, the Changer pauses the scene momentarily (e.g., by signaling with a chime, calling 'SHIFT!', or raising a paddle).
3. The Changer loudly announces a new, specific, and often unexpected character trait or motivation and designates one player to adopt it (e.g., by pointing to them).
4. The designated player must instantly and seamlessly integrate this new trait or motivation into their character's behavior, dialogue, and objective without breaking character or acknowledging the game mechanism.
5. The non-shifting player(s) must immediately observe and react to the shifted player's new behavior organically, treating it as a natural development within the scene and validating the shift.
6. The scene immediately resumes with the new character dynamic. Multiple shifts can occur throughout the scene, affecting different characters or adding layers to an already shifted character.
7. The scene concludes when a logical, albeit sometimes absurd, narrative resolution is reached, or the Host calls 'Scene'.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: Use the 'Shift' to force plot twists. Compel performers to invent new justifications, establish new conflicts, and drive the story in entirely unforeseen directions.
- Embracing Unexpected Turns: Performers must embrace the shift without hesitation or self-doubt. A performer's ability to fearlessly dive into a radically new character truth is central to the game.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: When one player receives a shift, the others must immediately pivot, legitimize the new behavior within the scene's logic, and actively help their partner succeed with the new trait.
- Justification: Performers must cleverly and seamlessly integrate the often absurd 'Shifts' into the scene's evolving context, providing internal consistency and comedic logic.
- Active Listening: Players must listen closely to understand the precise nature of the 'Shift' and how it immediately impacts both the affected player and the scene as a whole.
- Emotional Range & Status Play: Shifts frequently introduce new emotional states or alter character status; performers must quickly adapt their emotional expression and power dynamics.

## Why It Works
The game explicitly promotes narrative development by forcing plot twists and reframing 'failure' (failing to maintain the initial character) as a powerful gift and catalyst for creativity. It tests core improv philosophies like 'Being Changed', 'Yes, And', justification, active listening, and emotional range, requiring players to actively help their partner succeed with the new trait and push the collective story forward.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical and emotional boundaries are respected when assigning or reacting to sudden character shifts, especially those involving intense emotions, phobias, or physical proximity. Players should avoid shifts that force unsafe or non-consensual physical contact.

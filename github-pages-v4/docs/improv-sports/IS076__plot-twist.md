# Plot Twist!

![Plot Twist! — infographic](img/IS076.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An improv game of instantaneous scene transformation where players must instantly justify and embody radical, referee-directed changes.

## Overview
In 'Plot Twist!', two improvisers start a scene based on audience suggestions. At any point, the referee can shout 'PLOT TWIST!' and introduce a radical, immediate change to the scene's genre, a character's hidden truth, the established reality, or the overall objective. The improvisers must instantly justify, embody, and commit to this new reality, creating hilarious and lightning-fast narrative gymnastics.

## Setup
Two improvisers on a standard competitive short-form stage with no props (all mimed). The audience provides an initial suggestion for a scene location and a relationship between the two characters. The referee stands by to deliver specific, impactful twists.

## How to Play
1. The two improvisers begin a scene based on the audience's suggested location and relationship, establishing a clear dynamic.
2. At any moment, the referee shouts 'PLOT TWIST!' and follows it immediately with a directive (e.g., a genre change, hidden truth, shift in reality, or character endowment change).
3. Both improvisers must immediately acknowledge and embody the new reality without hesitation, questioning, or debating the twist.
4. Players re-contextualize their prior dialogue and actions to fit the new paradigm, using extreme 'Yes, And...' to build upon the twist with their partner.
5. The scene continues through 3-5 'PLOT TWIST!' calls until the referee calls time or ends the scene with a final 'PLOT TWIST! And... CUT!'

## Coaching Notes
- Referees should look for creative, challenging, yet playable twists.
- Watch out for the 'Wait, What?' Foul: penalize players who hesitate, question the twist, break character due to confusion, or openly struggle to adapt.
- Watch out for the 'Shrug' Foul: penalize players who accept the twist verbally but make weak, minimal, or non-committal physical or character choices.
- Award points for flawless integration, strong effort, and partner support (making choices that specifically enable a partner to navigate the twist).
- Standard clean-content and groaner fouls still apply.
- Players must listen intently to the referee's twist and their partner's instantaneous reactions to co-create the new reality.
- New genres or situations demand imaginative and specific object work to sell the new reality.

## Why It Works
The instantaneous nature of the game demands lightning-fast decision-making and execution, acting as a supercharged 'Yes, And...' exercise. It forces improvisers to think on their feet, testing spontaneous storytelling skills, character endowments, pacing, and object work under pressure.

## Safety & Inclusion
The humor derives from the abrupt, often absurd, shifts in context and character, not from offensive content. The referee must ensure twists remain family-friendly and appropriate for all ages.

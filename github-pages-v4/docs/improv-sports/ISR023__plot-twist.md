# Plot Twist!

![Plot Twist! — infographic](img/ISR023.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams perform a scene while instantly adapting to sudden, game-changing 'Plot Twists' drawn from a deck, including a secret twist deployed by their opponents.

## Overview
A high-energy, competitive short-form game where teams perform a scene while instantly adapting to sudden, game-changing 'Plot Twists' drawn from a deck by the Referee. To add a layer of sports-like strategy, each team is armed with exactly one secret 'Opponent Twist' card they can deploy to trip up their rivals at the worst possible moment.

## Setup
You need a Referee whistle, a main deck of 50+ pre-written 'Plot Twist' cards (e.g., 'Suddenly, it's a Spaghetti Western', 'Gravity is reversed', 'All dialogue must rhyme'), and two distinct 'Challenge Flags' (or buzzers) for the teams. The Referee shuffles the main deck and deals exactly ONE random card face-down to each team. This is their single 'Opponent Twist'. Teams may look at their card but must keep it secret.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a standard scene suggestion from the audience (e.g., an unusual location or a non-geographical relationship) for Team A.
2. Team A starts their scene based on the suggestion.
3. At their discretion (typically every 30-45 seconds, or when energy dips), the Referee blows their whistle, yells 'PLOT TWIST!', and reads a random card from the main deck. Team A must instantly and seamlessly justify this new reality into their ongoing scene.
4. At any point during Team A's scene, Team B can deploy their single 'Opponent Twist' by throwing their Challenge Flag (or signaling the Referee).
5. To prevent chaos, Team B does not yell the twist themselves. The Referee acknowledges the flag, blows the whistle at the next natural beat, takes Team B's secret card, yells 'OPPONENT PLOT TWIST!', and reads it aloud. Team A must incorporate it.
6. After 2-3 minutes, or a particularly strong comedic resolution to a twist, the Referee calls 'Scene!'
7. The Reversal: Team B now gets a suggestion and performs their scene, while Team A holds the power to deploy their single Opponent Twist flag.
8. Winning: After both scenes, the Referee awards points based on audience applause and technical execution.

## Coaching Notes
- Scoring: The Referee awards up to 5 points per team based on audience applause (using an applause meter or Ref's judgment). Bonus Points (+1 to +3) can be awarded by the Ref for brilliant, instant justifications of difficult twists.
- Fouls (-1 point): Call 'Twist Block' for ignoring or arguing against a twist, 'Content Foul' for crude/inappropriate content, or 'Groaner' for terrible puns.
- Referee Pacing: By routing all twists through the Referee's whistle, the game maintains a crisp, professional rhythm without devolving into a shouting match.
- Audience Interaction: The audience provides the initial suggestions, judges the winner via applause, and enjoys the suspense of watching the opposing team hover over their Challenge Flag.

## Variations
- Audience Twists: Instead of a pre-written deck, have the audience write Plot Twists on slips of paper before the show. The Referee curates them during intermission to ensure safety and quality.
- The Gauntlet (Exhibition Mode): Instead of two teams competing, one all-star team plays a 4-minute scene where the Referee pulls a new Plot Twist every 20 seconds, escalating the chaos until the scene collapses under its own weight.

## Why It Works
It forces improvisers to exercise extreme 'Yes, And' muscles by instantly justifying absurd, game-changing constraints. The single 'Opponent Twist' acts like a coach's challenge flag in professional sports, adding a highly visible tactical layer that audiences love.

## Safety & Inclusion
All cards in the main deck must be pre-vetted by the facilitator to ensure they are all-ages appropriate and physically safe. Accessibility/Safety Mulligan: If a Plot Twist card dictates a physical action or constraint that a player cannot safely or comfortably perform, that player simply crosses their arms in an 'X'. The Referee immediately says 'Scratch that!' and draws a new card without any penalty or break in the scene's momentum.

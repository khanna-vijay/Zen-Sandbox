# Echo Body

![Echo Body — infographic](img/IS188.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A physical "telephone" game where players collectively embody and evolve a secret, non-verbal Impulse around a circle.

## Overview
Participants stand in a circle to collectively embody and evolve a secret, non-verbal "Impulse." An Initiator receives a private abstract quality and slowly, silently sculpts it within their personal space. This physical sculpture then travels around the circle, with each subsequent player observing, intuitively absorbing, and slightly re-interpreting it before passing their evolved version to the next.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle, facing inwards, close enough so they can clearly observe the physical actions of their neighbors but not touch. One player is designated as the "Initiator." The facilitator privately gives the Initiator a single, non-verbal "Impulse" to convey (e.g., "An unbearable weight," "A fragile discovery," "Whispering a deep secret"). The Initiator must not tell anyone the Impulse.

## How to Play
1. The Initiator begins to physically embody the Impulse slowly and clearly, without moving from their spot, making vocal sounds, or using props. Their actions should be contained within their personal space as a 'sculpture' of their body.
2. The player immediately to the right of the Initiator becomes the first "Receiver."
3. The Receiver observes and intuitively absorbs the physical sculpture or gesture being offered by the Initiator.
4. Once the Receiver feels they have truly felt and understood the physical Impulse, they begin to slowly and deliberately echo it, interpreting and slightly evolving the Impulse within their own body to continue the physical narrative.
5. As the Receiver begins their physical embodiment, the Initiator stops and becomes a watcher.
6. This process continues around the circle. Each player in turn becomes the Receiver of the person to their left, absorbs the physical message, translates it through their own body, evolves it slightly, and becomes the "Passer" to the person on their right.
7. Maintain a continuous and fluid movement, with a brief overlap as the receiver begins and the passer ends, creating a continuous current of physical energy.
8. The Impulse travels around the entire circle until the last player embodies their interpretation.
9. The facilitator asks the original Initiator to reveal the original Impulse, and the group briefly discusses what they perceived, what evolved, and how they felt.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (Receiver): 'What is the specific physical quality of the movement/stillness being expressed by the person to my left, and how does my body instinctively respond to and continue this physical narrative?'
- Point of Concentration (Passer): 'Am I embodying this physical Impulse clearly enough for my right-hand neighbor to truly feel it and pass it on?'
- Remind players that this is not mimicry; it is an interpretation and slight evolution of the Impulse within their own body.
- Ensure movements remain slow, deliberate, and visually clear.
- During the discussion, emphasize that there is no right or wrong interpretation, only different shades of understanding.

## Why It Works
It fosters an acute awareness of subtle physical cues and encourages immediate, intuitive bodily responses rather than intellectual analysis. It develops the ability to convey complex ideas solely through physical presence, cultivating a strong sense of group awareness, shared responsibility, and trust in the collective intuition.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players maintain enough personal space so they do not touch or strike each other during their physical embodiments. Emphasize that there are no wrong interpretations to maintain a supportive, low-pressure environment.

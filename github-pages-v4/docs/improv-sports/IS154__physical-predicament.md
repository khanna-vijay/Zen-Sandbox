# Physical Predicament

![Physical Predicament — infographic](img/IS154.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must silently and continuously embody a unique, secretly assigned physical condition throughout a character-driven scene.

## Overview
Physical Predicament challenges improvisers to create a cohesive, character-driven scene while each silently and continuously embodying a unique, assigned physical condition or constraint. The humor arises from their ingenious physical justifications and the audience's attempt to guess these hidden predicaments. Points are awarded for comedic execution and fidelity to the condition, while deductions penalize breaking the physical rule or revealing the secret too soon.

## Setup
Have 3-4 players on stage at a time (e.g., two from the Red Team, two from the Blue Team), with off-stage players acting as the opposing team for the guessing phase. No physical props are used. The referee gets a scene suggestion and a single word for physical inspiration (e.g., 'Magnetic', 'Stuck') from the audience.

## How to Play
1. The referee solicits a general scene suggestion and one word to inspire a physical condition from the audience.
2. The referee secretly assigns a different, actionable physical predicament to each player on stage, derived from the inspiration word (e.g., whispering or using cue cards).
3. Players immediately begin the scene, incorporating their assigned predicament from the very first moment.
4. Players must show their predicament, not tell it, justifying their unusual physical state through their character's actions, reactions, and dialogue without explicitly naming it.
5. Players must 'Yes, And' their scene partners' offers and observed physical states, reacting logically to the unknown behavior.
6. The referee ends the scene after 2-4 minutes or once all players have clearly demonstrated their predicaments.
7. The referee polls the audience for guesses, asks the off-stage opposing team for their official guesses, and finally has each player verbally reveal their actual assigned predicament to tally points.

## Coaching Notes
- Implicit Justification: Players must show their predicament, not tell it. Dialogue should reflect the predicament indirectly.
- Active Listening & Observation: Players must keenly observe their partner's physical behavior and react logically to it.
- Dynamic Pacing & Object Work: The predicament should naturally lead to strong physical choices and dynamic pacing. Object work is key to demonstrating struggles.
- Watch for the Predicament Breach Foul (-2 points) if a player overtly breaks their assigned physical condition.
- Watch for the Giveaway Foul (-3 points) if a player explicitly states or too overtly implies their secret physical predicament.

## Why It Works
It forces players to make strong, consistent physical choices and object work to demonstrate their predicament through mime and movement. Each predicament acts as a fundamental character endowment, shaping how the player moves, speaks, and interacts with their environment, while the competitive guessing element adds a fierce observational challenge.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical choices and mime are executed safely without risking injury to the player or others on stage. The standard clean-content foul applies to keep content family-friendly.

# Narrative Jukebox

![Narrative Jukebox — infographic](img/ISR056.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A continuous scene instantly shifts genre, tone, and physicality whenever a new music track plays.

## Overview
Two improvisers perform a continuous scene that is repeatedly interrupted by the Referee 'hitting the Jukebox.' Whenever a new, highly distinct snippet of music plays, the players must instantly adapt the scene's genre, emotional tone, and physicality to match the vibe of the track, all while maintaining the original narrative and relationship.

## Setup
Format: Competitive Short-Form match. Requires 2 improvisers, 1 Referee, and a Tech Booth. Prepare a pre-loaded playlist of 5-10 highly distinct, royalty-free instrumental tracks (e.g., romantic sax, heavy metal, tense horror synth, bouncy polka). No physical props; all object work is mimed.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a mundane location to ground the scene.
2. The two improvisers begin a grounded, realistic scene, taking 20-30 seconds to establish a clear relationship, character wants, and a basic plot.
3. The Referee blows their whistle and calls 'Next Track!' or points to the tech booth.
4. The tech booth plays 5 to 10 seconds of a distinct musical track. The improvisers must instantly let the music wash over them, physically and vocally transforming to match the genre or emotion of the song.
5. As the music fades out, the players must stay in that newly established style and continue the scene, advancing the original plot and relationship rather than just dancing or making jokes about the music.
6. The Referee cues a 'Next Track' every 30-45 seconds, forcing the players to navigate 4 to 5 wildly different styles over a 3-minute scene.
7. The Referee calls 'Time!' on a high note or after the final style has been successfully integrated.

## Coaching Notes
- Award up to 5 points based on instantaneous commitment to the music, strong physical choices, and how well the core narrative was maintained despite the stylistic chaos.
- Call a 'Stuck Groove Foul' (-3 points) if players abandon the plot to just dance to the music, or if the scene devolves into disconnected gags rather than a continuous story.
- Call a 'Slow Spin Foul' (-3 points) if an improviser is noticeably slow to adapt to the new track or clings to the previous style.
- Call a 'Content Foul' (-5 points) for blue humor, swearing, or inappropriate content.
- Ensure the tech booth and Referee control a lightning-fast pace, eliminating clunky mid-scene audience polling.

## Variations
- Acapella Jukebox (Low Tech): If you don't have a tech booth, the Referee gets a list of 4-5 musical genres from the audience upfront. To trigger a shift, the Referee vocally sings, beatboxes, or hums the new 'track' for 5 seconds.
- Emotional Jukebox: Instead of musical genres, the tech booth plays specific sound effects (a crying baby, a massive explosion, a slide whistle, a ticking clock). The players must justify the sound effect and let it dictate the emotional state of the scene.

## Why It Works
Using actual audio tracks creates a literal 'Jukebox' feel that is highly novel and entertaining. The strict narrative anchor explicitly requires players to maintain the original plot, developing their ability to navigate extreme stylistic and physical shifts without devolving into a generic dance party.

## Safety & Inclusion
Volume Control: Ensure the tech booth fades the music quickly enough so improvisers don't have to strain their voices to be heard. Physical Safety: High-energy tracks can inspire wild physicality; players must maintain spatial awareness to avoid collisions. Content: Strictly enforce the clean-content foul to keep all genre interpretations clean and family-friendly.

# Narrative Navigator

![Narrative Navigator — infographic](img/IS155.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A game of instant story edits where a Director actively controls the scene in real-time.

## Overview
Narrative Navigator is an improv game where 3-5 improvisers collaboratively build a story from an audience suggestion, but with a unique twist: a Director (referee) actively edits the scene in real-time. Players must instantly adapt to commands like REWIND, FAST FORWARD, FLASHBACK, INNER MONOLOGUE, and META-COMMENTARY, seamlessly integrating these structural shifts while maintaining narrative cohesion and delivering comedic or dramatic impact.

## Setup
Requires 3-5 improvisers (typically 2-3 active scene players and others swapping in or supporting) and one Referee/Scorekeeper acting as the Director. A clear stage space is needed with no specific props; all environment and objects are mimed. Solicit an audience suggestion for a Relationship and a Location.

## How to Play
1. The Premise: Solicit a suggestion from the audience for a Relationship and a Location.
2. Initial Scene Establishment: Two improvisers begin the scene, taking 60-90 seconds to quickly establish their characters, the initial situation, and the central conflict or comedic premise.
3. The Director's Commands: Once the scene is underway, the Referee (Director) yells out one of five pre-determined structural commands. Players must immediately and instinctively adapt their performance to this new narrative device.
4. REWIND!: Players must instantly replay the immediately preceding 10-20 seconds of the scene, subtly altering or adding new emphasis to dialogue, physical actions, or reactions to reveal a deeper truth or unspoken thought.
5. FAST FORWARD!: Players must immediately jump ahead in time (1-5 minutes) within the scene, organically picking up the action where it would logically continue and incorporating implied off-screen developments.
6. FLASHBACK!: Players must abruptly transition to a past event or memory relevant to the current scene for 15-30 seconds, then quickly return to the present scene when the Director calls CUT! or issues a new command.
7. INNER MONOLOGUE!: The Director points to one specific player who freezes physically while others pause. The selected player directly addresses the audience to vocalize internal thoughts or hidden motivations for 10-15 seconds, then seamlessly re-enters the scene.
8. META-COMMENTARY!: All players freeze. The Director selects one player to step forward, break the fourth wall, and offer witty analytical commentary on the scene's progression, improv tropes, or their performance for 5-10 seconds before returning to their frozen position to await UNFREEZE!.
9. Scene End: The game continues for approximately 5-7 minutes until the Director calls 'Scene!' at a natural conclusion or after a particularly strong final command, followed by tallying points.

## Coaching Notes
- The Director should allow enough time for the audience to grasp the basic setup before calling the first command.
- Aim to call commands frequently (every 30-60 seconds, or after a significant scene beat) to keep the energy high and the players challenged.
- Competitive match scoring: Award points for Smooth Integration (3 points), Narrative Cohesion (2 points), Comedic/Dramatic Impact (2 points), and Bold Choices (1 point).
- Deduct points for hesitation, confusion, failing to commit to the command, breaking character, or directly acknowledging the game's mechanics within the scene itself.
- The primary challenge and source of humor come from the sudden shifts and the players' ability to make them seem logical or entertainingly absurd within the evolving narrative.

## Variations
- Specific audience members could be designated to yell certain commands on cue.
- The Referee could poll the audience for the next command.

## Why It Works
The rapid, unpredictable commands demand constant adaptation, quick thinking, and full commitment from the improvisers. The five structural commands provide specific, understandable rules that are distinct from typical improv scene-work, leading to unique comedic challenges. By making the audience/referee an active editor and structural director of the scene, it introduces a meta-level of play that transforms a live scene into a dynamic, improvisational director's cut in real-time.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during sudden freezes and rapid transitions. Players should respect physical boundaries and maintain emotional check-ins, especially when exploring deeper truths or hidden motivations during Inner Monologues.

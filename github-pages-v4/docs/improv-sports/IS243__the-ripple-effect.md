# The Ripple Effect

![The Ripple Effect — infographic](img/IS243.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must instantly integrate and accumulate unexpected character alterations imposed by a Ripple Judge throughout the scene.

## Overview
The Ripple Effect is a competitive improv game designed to heighten narrative complexity and force radical adaptation in improvisers. A 'Ripple Judge' periodically introduces 'Consequence Cards' that impose unexpected physical, psychological, or narrative alterations on a character. The targeted player must instantly integrate this new reality while maintaining all previous consequences, accumulating bizarre layers of character complexity.

## Setup
The team receives a basic scene suggestion from the audience (e.g., location, relationship, initial premise). A designated 'Ripple Judge' sits off-stage, equipped with a distinct noisemaker (e.g., a bell or wooden block) and a deck of pre-written 'Consequence Cards' that describe a physical, psychological, or narrative alteration for a single character.

## How to Play
1. Begin the scene using the audience suggestion.
2. The Ripple Judge observes the scene, waiting for a slight hesitation, a surprising statement, a minor gaffe, or an opportunity to inject chaos.
3. The judge sounds the noisemaker to trigger a 'Ripple'.
4. The judge points to a specific player and hands them a random Consequence Card.
5. The targeted player must immediately read the card and integrate this new reality into their character's behavior and performance.
6. Crucially, all previously assigned consequences remain in effect, creating an accumulating layer of complexity.
7. The other players must acknowledge, react to, and build upon the affected player's new reality, using 'Yes, And' to weave it into the narrative.
8. The scene continues with the accumulating consequences until the standard scene timer ends (e.g., 4-6 minutes), with the judge triggering multiple ripples throughout.

## Coaching Notes
- The noisemaker trigger is not a judgment of error, but an invitation for escalation.
- Players do not have to explain the consequence directly; their behavior should reflect it.
- Consequences can dramatically alter a character's status within the scene, forcing players to adjust power dynamics on the fly.
- Many consequences are physical (e.g., invisible limb, uncontrollable tic), demanding strong physical commitment.
- When scoring as a competitive improv game, evaluate teams on adaptation, collaboration, narrative coherence, audience engagement, and risk-taking.

## Variations
- Silent vs. Aloud: The targeted player can read the Consequence Card silently to add mystery for the audience and scene partners, or read it aloud for immediate shared context.

## Why It Works
The game forces narratives into increasingly complex and unforeseen directions, demanding continuous justification and integration. It acts as a direct mechanism for embracing the unexpected, transforming hesitations or missteps into dynamic gifts, and inherently demands extreme collaboration to 'Yes, And' bizarre new traits.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical consequences (like dancing or invisible limbs) are performed safely without risking injury or crossing physical boundaries with scene partners. Psychological consequences should avoid triggering trauma or crossing the cast's established boundaries.

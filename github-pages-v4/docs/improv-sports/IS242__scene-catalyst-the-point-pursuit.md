# Scene Catalyst: The Point Pursuit!

![Scene Catalyst: The Point Pursuit! — infographic](img/IS242.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams strategically activate high-stakes 'Catalyst Challenges' mid-scene for significant bonus points.

## Overview
Scene Catalyst: The Point Pursuit! is a competitive short-form improv game where two teams create dynamic scenes. Teams can strategically activate high-stakes 'Catalyst Challenges' mid-scene, which introduce demanding improvisational constraints. Successfully integrating these challenges earns significant bonus points, while failure results in penalties, adding a layer of strategic risk-taking.

## Setup
Two teams (Red vs. Blue), each with 2-4 players, are on stage or ready to enter. The referee is equipped with a whistle, scorepad, and a stack of 'Catalyst Challenge' cards. A clearly audible buzzer or bell is placed center stage or accessible to the players on the bench for challenge activation.

## How to Play
1. The referee calls for an audience suggestion for a scene (e.g., location, relationship, or activity), assigns the scene to one team, and calls two players from that team to begin.
2. The two players begin the scene, establishing characters, plot, and environment. The referee observes, awarding small points (1-2 points) throughout the scene for particularly strong moves or excellent teamwork.
3. At any point during their scene, the performing team (or a designated player from their bench) can choose to activate a 'Catalyst Challenge' by hitting the buzzer/bell and clearly shouting, 'CATALYST!' Each team gets one Catalyst activation opportunity per match round.
4. Upon 'CATALYST!', the referee immediately freezes the scene with a whistle, dramatically draws a 'Catalyst Challenge' card from the deck, explains the challenge rules, and sets a timer (e.g., 60 or 90 seconds).
5. The scene resumes, and the players must fully integrate and sustain the new, difficult constraint for the set duration, without breaking character or the narrative.
6. Once the challenge time expires, the referee calls 'TIME!' with a whistle and adjudicates whether the challenge was successfully integrated or if the team struggled or broke the rules.
7. If successful, the performing team earns a significant bonus (e.g., +7 bonus points). If they fail, they either lose points (e.g., -5 points) or the opposing team gains a smaller amount of points (e.g., +3 points).

## Coaching Notes
- Referee Game Management: The referee is critical for the strategic aspect, acting as the Catalyst Facilitator who explains rules, sets timers, judges integration, and assigns points.
- Fouls: Uphold clean-content standards by calling a 'content foul' (for blue humor, swearing, or innuendo; results in -1 point and a physical marker like a bag over the head) and the 'Groaner Foul' (for excessively bad puns; results in -1 point).
- Strategic Play: Teams must analyze their scene's strength and their own confidence to choose the optimal moment to activate a challenge.
- Object Work & Emotion: Many physical Catalyst Challenges demand precise object work, while others require rapid emotional shifts or sustained intense emotions.

## Variations
- Audience Object Oracle: A Catalyst Challenge that directly calls for immediate audience input, where the scene must instantly incorporate an object shouted out by the audience.
- The Emotional Echo Chamber: A Catalyst Challenge where, for a set time (e.g., 75 seconds), every line spoken must end with the exact opposite emotion it began with, clearly conveyed in voice and body language.

## Why It Works
The game tests adaptability, quick thinking, and the courage to commit to bold improv choices. It develops 'Yes, And' skills by forcing players to accept sudden constraints, enhances active listening to teammates and referee instructions, and encourages strategic risk assessment.

## Safety & Inclusion
The game is strictly family-friendly. All 'Catalyst Challenge' cards must feature wholesome, creative, and skill-based constraints with no crude, suggestive, or violent content. The referee rigorously enforces the clean-content foul to penalize inappropriate humor, ensuring the competitive aspect emphasizes skill and joy rather than personal attacks.

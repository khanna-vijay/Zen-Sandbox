# Kinetic Echo

![Kinetic Echo — infographic](img/ISR215.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Transform the traditional 'pass the clap' into an exercise in deep physical listening by passing a completely new impulse.

## Overview
A non-verbal ensemble warm-up that transforms the traditional 'pass the clap' chain reaction into an exercise in deep physical listening. Instead of imitating the impulse they receive, players allow the sound or movement to viscerally affect them, and then pass a completely new impulse to the next person based on that internal sensation.

## Setup
Players stand in a loose circle facing inward. No props are needed. The facilitator stands just outside the circle to side-coach and control the tempo.

## How to Play
1. Form a circle. The facilitator explains the Point of Concentration: 'Allow the impulse to hit you, feel its energy, and let your body spontaneously translate it into something entirely new.'
2. Player A initiates by turning to Player B (on their left) and offering a single, simple physical impulse and/or non-linguistic sound (e.g., a sharp hand-clap and a 'Ha!').
3. Player B receives the impulse. Instead of imitating it, they let it resonate and immediately pass a completely different sound/movement to Player C. For example, the sharp 'Ha!' might make Player B flinch, prompting them to pass a slow, soothing hand-wave and a soft 'Shhh' to Player C.
4. Player C receives the 'Shhh', feels its calming energy, and perhaps translates it into a heavy, grounding foot stomp and a low hum, passing it to Player D. The chain continues around the circle.
5. If a player freezes, overthinks, or accidentally imitates the impulse they just received, the facilitator calls 'Wash!' The player immediately shakes their hands out and throws any random, new impulse to the next person to keep the energy moving.
6. The facilitator acts as a conductor for pacing. The game begins slowly ('Let it resonate, take your time'). After a few rounds, the facilitator gradually calls 'Pick up the pace', 'Faster', and eventually 'Lightning speed'. At top speed, players have no time to think and must rely purely on instant, visceral reaction.

## Coaching Notes
- The facilitator side-coaches throughout, calling out tempo changes ('Faster now, don't think!') and gently correcting imitation ('Wash! Shake it off, new impulse!').
- Force players to slow down and focus on internal, somatic responses before reacting during the slow rounds.
- Graduating tempo prevents the exercise from becoming too precious or slow.

## Variations
- Blind Echo: All players close their eyes. Impulses are purely vocal/auditory. Players must translate the feeling of the sound they hear into a new sound for the next person.
- Cross-Circle Echo: Instead of passing to the left, players make strong eye contact with anyone across the circle and 'throw' the impulse to them. This requires heightened visual focus and readiness from the entire group.

## Why It Works
It builds deep ensemble listening and non-verbal communication. It trains actors to bypass intellect, trust their somatic instincts, and react authentically in the moment.

## Safety & Inclusion
Highly inclusive and safe, requiring absolutely no physical contact. The game can easily be played seated or in wheelchairs. Facilitators should remind players that all movements should respect their own physical limitations and boundaries; there is no 'wrong' reaction, only an authentic one.

# The Resonant Current

![The Resonant Current — infographic](img/ISR200.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Let the offer change you, then pass that changed energy forward in this non-verbal game of intuitive impulses.

## Overview
A non-verbal ensemble warm-up where players pass a spontaneous sound and gesture around a circle. Instead of simply copying the impulse, each player lets the offer affect them, then generates a completely new sound and gesture based on how it made them feel. It trains active listening, emotional reactivity, and trusting your gut instincts without overthinking.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle facing inward, ensuring everyone can see each other. No props or prior experience are needed. A facilitator stands just outside or within the circle to side-coach the flow.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator establishes the Point of Concentration: 'Let the offer change you, then pass that changed energy forward.'
2. Player A turns to Player B (on their left) and gives a distinct, non-verbal impulse—a sound combined with a physical gesture (e.g., a sharp gasp and a step back, or a warm sigh and a relaxed posture).
3. Player B receives the impulse, allowing it to land and 'resonate' emotionally or physically for a split second.
4. Player B then turns to Player C and delivers a completely new sound and gesture inspired by how Player A's offer made them feel (e.g., reacting to the gasp with a defensive posture and a low hum).
5. Player C receives this, transforms it, and passes a new impulse to Player D. This continues fluidly around the circle.

## Coaching Notes
- If players freeze, overthink, or get stuck 'resonating' for too long, actively coach the pace.
- Use prompts like: 'Breathe it in, breathe it out!', 'Don't invent, just react!', 'First thought, best thought!', and 'Keep the current moving!'
- The goal is achieving a continuous, unthinking flow where the ensemble acts as a single reactive organism.
- Ensure players are genuinely changed by an offer rather than just mimicking it.

## Variations
- Cross-Circle Current: Instead of passing sequentially to the left, players pass the impulse to anyone in the circle by making strong eye contact. The receiver must lock eyes, absorb the impulse, then find a new person across the circle to pass their transformed impulse to.
- Current to Scene: Two players step into the center and pass the resonant current back and forth three times, allowing the emotion to heighten. On the fourth exchange, they drop the abstract sounds and speak their first line of dialogue, grounding the scene entirely in the emotional state they just built.

## Why It Works
The Point of Concentration clearly isolates the target skill of intuitive responsiveness. It builds deep ensemble trust and non-verbal communication by forcing players to be genuinely changed by an offer rather than just mimicking it.

## Safety & Inclusion
This game can be played fully seated; gestures can be adapted to whatever range of motion is comfortable for each player. Emphasize that impulses must not involve physical contact or aggressive advances toward others. Establish a 'no wrong answers' environment to reduce performance anxiety, ensuring players know that whatever sound or movement comes out of them is the right one.

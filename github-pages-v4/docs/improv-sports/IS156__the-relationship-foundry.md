# The Relationship Foundry

![The Relationship Foundry — infographic](img/IS156.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Forge and continuously transform complex character relationships under pressure by rapidly adapting to externally-imposed dynamic shifts.

## Overview
The Relationship Foundry is an improv game for 3-4 players and a Host, where improvisers must forge and continuously transform complex character relationships under pressure. Players begin a scene in an audience-suggested scenario with initial relationships, then the Host periodically interjects with 'Relational Anvils'—short, impactful directives that immediately alter specific aspects of the characters' connections or reveal new dynamics.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 improvisers, 1 Host to manage flow and keep score, and a standard performance space with minimal props. The Host needs a pre-written list of 10-15 'Relational Anvils' or be prepared to solicit them from the audience. Get audience suggestions for a shared scenario/location and initial neutral-ish relationships for all players.

## How to Play
1. The Host welcomes the audience, explains the game, and assigns the audience-suggested scenario and initial relationships to specific players.
2. Players immediately begin the scene, establishing the scenario and their assigned relationships.
3. Every 30-90 seconds, or when an opportunity arises, the Host loudly interjects with 'FORGE!' and presents a 'Relational Anvil' (e.g., 'Player A just realized Player B has been secretly gaslighting them').
4. Players must immediately and seamlessly integrate this new dynamic into their actions, reactions, and dialogue without explicitly stating the anvil.
5. The Host acts as a referee, awarding points for seamless integration, deep emotional commitment, subtextual play, and compelling conflict/connection.
6. The Host deducts points (fouls) for ignoring the anvil, explicit exposition, blocking the anvil, or wimping out.
7. The scene concludes when 5-7 anvils have been integrated or a satisfying relational turning point is reached, focusing on the journey rather than a neat plot resolution.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus on subtext: show the relational shift through nuanced physical actions, vocal tone, and implied dialogue rather than direct exposition.
- Avoid 'Explicit Exposition' fouls: do not directly state or explain the Relational Anvil to other players or the audience.
- Commit deeply to the emotional consequences or new motivations presented by an anvil.
- Do not wimp out or avoid the natural conflict, discomfort, or comedic potential generated by a relational shift.
- Advance the relationship organically rather than looping on the same emotional beat.

## Why It Works
It forces constant, dynamic shifts in emotional states, power dynamics, and conflict resolutions, leading to profoundly complex and nuanced character work. It rewards players who integrate directives organically through character behavior, fostering a deeper understanding of non-verbal communication and implicit dynamics.

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game involves rapid shifts in power dynamics, conflict, and emotional intensity (e.g., gaslighting, jealousy), players should maintain strong physical and emotional boundaries. Use a tap-out or edit mechanic if a relationship dynamic becomes too uncomfortable or crosses personal boundaries.

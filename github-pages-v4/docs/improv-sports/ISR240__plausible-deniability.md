# Plausible Deniability

![Plausible Deniability — infographic](img/ISR240.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players organically tag in to endow opponents with increasingly absurd quirks that must be instantly accepted and justified with flawless logic.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where players organically tag in to endow their opponents with increasingly absurd quirks, situations, or physicalities. The endowed player must instantly accept the bizarre reality and justify it with flawless, character-driven logic. It tests rapid character commitment, emotional grounding, and the ability to make the ridiculous seem perfectly rational, all while building a single, compounding scene.

## Setup
Two teams (e.g., Red and Blue) line up on opposite sides of the stage. A Referee stands downstage to manage the game, take audience suggestions, and call time or fouls.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a mundane, everyday location (e.g., a post office, a dentist's waiting room) to ground the scene.
2. One player from the Red Team and one from the Blue Team step center stage to begin a grounded, realistic scene based on the suggestion.
3. At any point, a player on the sidelines can tag out their own teammate. The tagging player immediately enters the scene and delivers an absurd, bizarre endowment to the opposing player (e.g., 'Sir, why are you eating the deposit slips?').
4. The endowed player must instantly accept the new reality ('Yes, And'), physically commit to it if necessary, and provide a plausible, character-driven justification with full emotional commitment (e.g., 'Because the ink is rich in iron, and my doctor said I am anemic!').
5. The scene continues to breathe and play out with this new established reality. Players interact normally, exploring the justification, until the next tag-in.
6. The opposing team can then tag in their own player to escalate the scene, endowing the other side with a new complication that builds on the existing chaos.
7. The Referee allows the scene to run continuously for 3 to 4 minutes, watching the absurdities compound, before calling 'Scene!' on a high-energy punchline or a particularly brilliant justification.
8. At the end of the scene, the Referee asks the audience to vote by applause for the team that provided the most seamless, hilarious, and committed justifications. The winning team is awarded 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- Organic tag-ins shift the burden of invention to the players, allowing scenes to breathe and flow naturally.
- Force rapid character commitment and strong, immediate emotional choices.
- Ensure the initial location remains mundane to serve as a vital contrast to the escalating absurdity.
- Granular, per-turn scoring is eliminated to keep the game moving; focus on the compounding, shared absurd reality.

## Variations
- The Interrogation Room: Set the scene strictly in a police interrogation room. Tag-ins are different detectives entering with new, increasingly absurd pieces of 'evidence' that the suspect (who remains in the scene the whole time) must instantly justify.
- Classroom Exercise: Remove teams and points. One player stands in the center of a circle. Others step in one by one to endow them with a quirk. The center player must justify three different endowments before returning to the circle, making it a rapid-fire training drill.

## Why It Works
It isolates and drills the specific improv skill of 'justification'—making the ridiculous seem perfectly rational. It forces rapid character commitment and strong, immediate emotional choices while creating a compounding, shared absurd reality.

## Safety & Inclusion
Endowments must focus on behaviors, situational quirks, or absurd objects, and strictly avoid relying on harmful stereotypes, punching down, or non-consensual physical contact. If an endowment crosses a player's personal boundary, they or the Referee can use a 'Sweep' or 'Bail' mechanic to instantly reset the scene without penalty. The Referee actively enforces clean, all-ages content, utilizing a clean-content foul for any inappropriate language or innuendo.

# The Reality Remixer

![The Reality Remixer — infographic](img/IS247.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Dynamic, overt alterations of scene reality via an external producer.

## Overview
The Reality Remixer is an improv game where 3-4 performers begin a scene based on a simple location, only for an off-stage Remix Producer to dynamically and overtly alter its underlying reality. Based on rapid-fire audience suggestions, the Producer uses a distinct buzzer and a visible dry-erase board to announce new genre shifts, character motivations, or physical constraints, demanding instant and visible adaptation from the improvisers. This continuous, layered remixing creates unpredictable, high-stakes comedy as players grapple with increasingly absurd realities.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 improvisers on a standard stage and 1 Remix Producer (often the MC or an experienced improviser) positioned just off-stage. Props needed include a large dry-erase board or large cue cards with a marker, clearly visible to both the audience and performers, and a distinct, loud sound cue device like a bell, buzzer, or horn for the Producer.

## How to Play
1. The MC solicits a single, straightforward location suggestion from the audience.
2. The 3-4 improvisers immediately begin an everyday, realistic scene in that location, establishing characters and relationships without unusual constraints for 30-45 seconds.
3. The MC introduces the Remix Producer, explaining they will take audience suggestions to fundamentally alter the scene mid-play.
4. The Remix Producer asks the audience for a variety of remix ideas, such as genre shifts, character secrets, physical constraints, or emotional states, and jots down 3-5 distinct suggestions.
5. At any point when the scene needs a jolt, the Producer loudly hits their buzzer or bell, and all players on stage immediately freeze.
6. The Producer selects a suggestion, writes it concisely on the dry-erase board so the audience can see it, verbally announces the new reality or constraint, and says 'Go!'.
7. The players unfreeze and immediately resume the scene, instantly integrating the new Remix into their performance.
8. This cycle of freezing, remixing, and unfreezing repeats 3-5 times per scene, with new Remixes building upon or conflicting with previous ones.
9. The scene concludes when a natural comedic peak is reached, typically within 4-7 minutes, signaled by the MC or Producer.

## Coaching Notes
- Crucially, the Remix is not dialogue for the players to say, but a new underlying reality, constraint, or secret motivation to immediately adopt.
- Players targeted by a specific personal Remix must embody it without explicitly stating what their new condition is.
- Other players on stage must observe and react to the changes they perceive in their scene partner(s), justifying the evolving reality through their own actions and dialogue.
- As Remixes accumulate, the comedy stems from the performers' ingenious or hilariously desperate attempts to reconcile contradictory realities.

## Variations
- Competitive Short-Form Scoring: Award 1-5 points per successful Remix integration based on seamlessness, creative justification, and audience response.

## Why It Works
It directly addresses the critical need for audience clarity and visible comedic tension. The evocative name, visible controller, visual cues, and clear start/stop signals make the premise instantly understandable. The Producer acts as an external force providing visually and audibly obvious sources of tension, pushing the fundamental principle of 'Yes, And...' to its extreme as players actively build upon ever-changing, often contradictory new realities.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical constraints suggested by the audience (e.g., 'the floor is lava') are executed safely without risking injury or pushing players beyond their physical comfort zones. The Remix Producer should filter out inappropriate or unsafe suggestions.

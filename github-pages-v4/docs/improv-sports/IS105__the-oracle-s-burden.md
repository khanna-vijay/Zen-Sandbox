# The Oracle's Burden

![The Oracle's Burden — infographic](img/IS105.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two performers build a scene profoundly impacted by a pre-destined, unchangeable prophecy, curse, or unavoidable condition dictated by the audience.

## Overview
The Oracle's Burden is an improvisational game where two performers collaboratively build a scene profoundly impacted by a pre-destined, unchangeable 'burden'—a prophecy, curse, or unavoidable condition—provided by an unseen Oracle (audience). The core objective is for improvisers to immediately and continuously 'Yes, And' this fundamental constraint, making it the central engine of the narrative. They must explore its deep, ongoing implications for characters, relationships, and conflicts, driving the story towards logical consequences or transformation.

## Setup
The Host solicits a single, concise statement for the 'Oracle's Burden' from the audience or judges (e.g., a concrete, ongoing, character-affecting destiny, curse, or unavoidable reality). The Host then takes a standard scene suggestion, such as a location, relationship, or initial activity.

## How to Play
1. The Host solicits the 'Oracle's Burden' from the audience or judges. It must be an ongoing, unavoidable, impactful, clear, and generative constraint.
2. The Host gets a standard scene suggestion (e.g., location, relationship, or activity).
3. Two improvisers enter the stage and begin the scene, establishing their characters and initial objective.
4. From the very first moment, the Oracle's Burden must be overtly present and actively impacting at least one character or the scene's reality.
5. The improviser(s) embodying the burden must immediately and consistently manifest its effects physically, vocally, emotionally, or perceptually.
6. The scene partner(s) must fully 'Yes, And' the reality of the burden, actively acknowledging its presence and integrating it into the unfolding story without trying to magically dismiss or fix it.
7. The improvisers must make the burden the central engine of the narrative, exploring how it affects their relationship, goals, decision-making, and obstacles.
8. As the scene progresses, the burden's impact should deepen, evolve, or reveal unforeseen consequences, moving towards a narrative turning point or significant character transformation.
9. The scene ends naturally through resolution or character change, or at the host's discretion within the 3-5 minute time limit.

## Coaching Notes
- Ensure the burden suggestion is ongoing, unavoidable, impactful, clear, concrete, and generative. Reject static traits like 'You are sad' or simple tasks like 'You must find a key.'
- The burden is not a sideline gag or a mere character quirk; it must be the central engine of the narrative.
- The scene partner's role is critical: they must actively support the character experiencing the burden by reacting truthfully, acknowledging its weight, and adapting their own behavior.
- Characters must be different, not just say they are different, allowing the core change to dictate actions, emotional states, and interactions.
- Listen intently to the manifestation of the burden (physicality, vocal changes, emotional impact) and react truthfully.
- Many burdens require precise and consistent physical embodiment and object work.
- Embrace the 'mistake' or inconvenience of the burden as a springboard for engaging, risky storytelling.

## Why It Works
This game makes narrative development indispensable by using a unique, pre-destined constraint as a profound narrative catalyst. It forces improvisers to move beyond isolated gags into sustained storytelling, exploring cause-and-effect and character motivation under duress. It tests 'Yes, And' at its deepest level, requiring players to accept a fundamental, restrictive premise and build powerfully and logically from it, while also exploring dynamic status shifts and deep collaborative scene-building.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical choices related to the burden are safe to maintain for the duration of the scene. Players should respect physical boundaries and avoid burdens that force non-consensual physical contact or cross personal boundaries.

# Genre Mashup Mania

![Genre Mashup Mania — infographic](img/IS116.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Rapid-fire Genre Shifting with Relay Teams

## Overview
This game takes the rapid-fire adaptability of a competitive short-form match and pushes players to fluidly transition and commit to completely different tones within a single, evolving scene. It introduces a relay aspect and scores heavily on seamless, committed shifts, capitalizing on the comedic gold found in jarring tonal shifts.

## Setup
Form two teams, each with 2-3 players. Assign a Referee (who acts as the Genre Master with a distinct signal and a diverse list of genres) and a Scorekeeper. Ask the audience for a location and a relationship between the initial two characters.

## How to Play
1. One player from each team steps forward to begin the scene in a neutral, everyday style, establishing the chosen location and initial relationship.
2. The Referee announces the first genre, and players must immediately embody it.
3. At any point (typically every 15-30 seconds), the Referee signals and announces a new, contrasting genre.
4. Players must instantaneously pivot their dialogue, physicality, character motivations, and narrative direction to match the new genre while maintaining the ongoing storyline.
5. After approximately 2-3 minutes, the Referee calls 'SWITCH!' and current players must immediately tag out to a new player from their respective teams.
6. Incoming players step directly into the ongoing scene, embodying the current genre and seamlessly continuing the narrative thread.
7. The game concludes after a set number of rounds or an 8-10 minute time limit.

## Coaching Notes
- Award 3 points for seamless transitions where players commit instantly without hesitation or breaking character.
- Award 2 points for strong genre commitment in dialogue, physicality, and character choices.
- Award 2 points for coherence and continuity, acknowledging players' ability to re-contextualize previous dialogue to fit the new genre.
- Award 1 point for humor or dramatic impact.
- Deduct points for ignoring the genre (5 points), hesitation or breaking character (3 points), or lacking a narrative link (2 points).

## Why It Works
It develops rapid-fire adaptability, deep commitment to genre tropes, and the ability to maintain narrative continuity despite jarring contextual shifts.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during rapid tag-outs and transitions. Maintain standard improv consent and boundaries, especially when rapidly shifting into potentially intense genres.

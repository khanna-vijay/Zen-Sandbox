# Innovation Station

![Innovation Station — infographic](img/IS039.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams compete to invent and dynamically pitch hilariously absurd solutions to an everyday audience problem using energetic object work.

## Overview
Innovation Station is a competitive short-form game where two teams compete to invent and dynamically pitch solutions to an everyday problem suggested by the audience. After a brief huddle, each team presents their unique, often hilariously absurd invention using energetic object work, physical comedy, and enthusiastic demonstrations, aiming to convince the investors of its brilliance. The game rewards creativity, clear physical storytelling, and humor, while penalizing derivative ideas or a lack of physical demonstration, culminating in a fast-paced showcase of improvisational ingenuity.

## Setup
Two teams (Red and Blue) face each other across an open stage with a referee center stage. No physical props are used; all items are created through object work. Each team involves all its active players in the demonstration, with roles dynamically shifting between lead inventor, enthusiastic demonstrator, product model, or satisfied customer.

## How to Play
1. The referee asks the audience for an everyday, relatable problem or need and chooses one clear, family-friendly suggestion.
2. The referee clearly states the chosen problem to both teams, emphasizing that they must invent a unique and distinct solution.
3. Both teams retreat quickly to their respective sidelines for a 20-second rapid-fire huddle to collaboratively brainstorm the name of their invention, its core function, and 2-3 key features they plan to demonstrate.
4. The referee signals Team A to come forward for a 90-second unveiling. They must announce the name of their invention, briefly explain how it solves the problem, and demonstrate its features using energetic and clear object work.
5. Immediately after Team A, Team B steps forward to present their own distinct invention addressing the exact same audience problem, following the same 90-second format.
6. After both teams have presented, the referee awards 1-5 points based on Creativity & Originality, Clarity of Demonstration, Humor & Engagement, and Problem Solved.
7. The game continues with a new audience suggestion for a problem. The team with the most points after a predetermined number of rounds wins the game.

## Coaching Notes
- During the huddle, don't overthink it! The goal is a quick, bold concept, not a perfected blueprint. Simple and clear is better than complex and unfinished.
- Enforce the 'Show, Don't Tell' Foul: If a team describes a feature without actively demonstrating it through object work, deduct a point and demand an immediate physical re-do.
- Enforce the 'Marketing Malarkey' Foul: If a presentation relies too heavily on verbose explanations or abstract claims rather than active physical demonstration, call 'Less Talk, More Show!' and deduct a point.
- Enforce the 'Derivative Design' Foul: If a team's core invention concept is deemed too similar to the opposing team's idea, deduct points and playfully challenge them to differentiate.
- Playfully apply the 'Groaner Foul' for excessively bad puns or jokes in slogans or product names that fail to land humorously.
- The referee's commentary and playful justifications for scoring decisions and foul calls are integral to the game's humor and engagement.

## Why It Works
The game tests 'Yes, And' and active listening during the rapid huddle to build a coherent concept. It heavily develops object work and endowment as players must use clear, specific pantomime to create the invention and show user interaction. The rapid pacing demands fast thinking and immediate execution, while the pitch format builds character and audience management skills.

## Safety & Inclusion
Strictly enforce a clean-content foul for any blue humor, inappropriate innuendo, or swearing in the invention's concept, name, or demonstration to maintain family-friendliness. The competitive aspect should always be playful, emphasizing mutual support and audience enjoyment over cutthroat rivalry.

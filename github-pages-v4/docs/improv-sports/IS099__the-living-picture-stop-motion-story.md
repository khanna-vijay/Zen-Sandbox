# The Living Picture: Stop-Motion Story

![The Living Picture: Stop-Motion Story — infographic](img/IS099.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A stop-and-go theatrical game where players freeze into living pictures and use the implied narrative to propel their next actions.

## Overview
A Narrator-Director calls 'Action!', prompting players to engage in unscripted movement and interaction, then 'Freeze!', requiring them to instantly hold their positions as a 'living picture.' Players intuitively interpret the implied narrative, intentions, and emotional states within each frozen tableau. They use these realizations to spontaneously propel their actions and continuously evolve the scene when 'Action!' is called again.

## Setup
Players (Actors) stand in an open space. One designated player (initially the facilitator, but this role can rotate) is the 'Narrator-Director.'

## How to Play
1. The Narrator-Director calls out 'Action!'
2. Upon 'Action!', all Actors immediately begin to move, initiating simple, unscripted actions or interactions with other players (e.g., setting a table, having a whispered disagreement, reaching for an imagined object). The key is to do something that implies a situation or relationship.
3. At any moment, the Narrator-Director can call out 'Freeze!'
4. Upon 'Freeze!', all Actors must instantly stop in place, holding their exact body position, facial expression, and spatial relationship to others to become a 'living picture' or tableau.
5. The Narrator-Director observes the frozen tableau, allowing a few moments of silent contemplation, and then calls out 'Action!' again.
6. Upon the next 'Action!', Actors immediately pick up from their exact frozen position, continuing or evolving their scene/interaction based on their internal impulse or the new information they perceive in the tableau.
7. The previous action might simply continue, or the internal realization from the 'freeze' might completely shift the scene's direction, emotion, or intention.
8. Continue this cycle. There is no 'out' in this game; everyone remains a part of the unfolding, collective story.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus: Challenge players to immediately identify the implied narrative, unspoken intention, and internal emotional state of their frozen moment, and allow that precise realization to instinctively propel their body and actions into the subsequent 'Action!' phase.
- Encourage Intuition Over Intellect: The strict and immediate nature of the 'Freeze' and 'Action' commands bypasses intellectual deliberation. Players shouldn't have time to 'think' about what they should do; they must react viscerally.
- Remind players to be fully present in their bodies and actively 'listen' to the silent information within the tableau.
- Emphasize that there is no 'right' or 'wrong' way to freeze or act. Any physical posture or emotional state is valid, ensuring a safe environment for playful discovery.

## Variations
- Environmental Parameter: The Narrator-Director introduces a simple suggestion at the beginning (e.g., 'Everyone is at a busy train station,' 'You are all searching for something precious,' 'This is a moment of deep regret') to add a subtle layer of suggestion without dictating specific actions.
- Rotating Director: Players can take turns stepping out of the scene to act as the Narrator-Director.

## Why It Works
This game cultivates spontaneous scene creation, immediate physical and emotional responsiveness, and non-verbal storytelling. It forces players to sustain presence under interruption, heightens their awareness of spatial relationships and ensemble dynamics, and develops their ability to find continuity and transformation in abrupt shifts.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing space is clear of obstacles so players can move and freeze safely. Encourage players to respect physical boundaries and practice enthusiastic consent during unscripted physical interactions.

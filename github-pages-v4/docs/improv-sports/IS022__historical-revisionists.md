# Historical Revisionists

![Historical Revisionists — infographic](img/IS022.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Improvise scenes that progressively rewrite the past as a sudden 'historical revision' retroactively changes everything players thought they knew.

## Overview
Historical Revisionists is an improv game where players begin a scene based on an audience suggestion. At a pivotal moment, a referee calls 'TIME!' and introduces a 'historical revision'—a retroactively applied piece of information that fundamentally alters the established facts of the scene. Players must then immediately resume the scene, acting as if this new, often absurd, truth has always been in place, skillfully re-contextualizing their previous actions and dialogue.

## Setup
Requires 3-5 improvisers and a Referee/Host on a standard open stage with no props. The audience provides an initial simple scene suggestion (e.g., 'Two strangers sharing a park bench' or 'A first day at a new, unusual job').

## How to Play
1. The players begin a short scene (approximately 2-3 minutes), establishing characters, a basic location, and some initial conflict or relationship.
2. The Referee observes closely, taking mental notes of key character details, established facts, and lines of dialogue.
3. At a climactic, revealing, or opportune moment, the Referee calls 'TIME! Revision!' to pause the action.
4. The Referee addresses all players and the audience, stating a new, game-altering piece of 'historical information' or a 'forgotten truth' that retroactively changes the fundamental understanding of the scene.
5. The Referee calls 'CONTINUE!' and the players must immediately resume the scene, picking up at the logical next moment after where they left off.
6. Players must now act as if the historical revision has always been true, skillfully and humorously integrating the new truth and justifying previous actions.
7. This process of Scene, Revision, and Resume is repeated 2-3 times for maximum chaos and comedic build-up.
8. The game concludes after 2-3 revisions, with the Referee optionally awarding points for seamless integration, comedic justification, heightening the stakes, and teamwork.

## Coaching Notes
- The revision must provide new information that re-contextualizes what the characters thought was true, without explicitly contradicting a factual statement if possible (e.g., don't say 'the red shirt was blue' unless the character can plausibly not know what color their shirt is).
- Players are not explicitly 'correcting' the past; they are playing from the 'new truth' and must react to the new information in character.
- The Referee should craft revisions that make the narrative itself a playground, such as 'Actually, the park bench itself is a sentient being and has been manipulating their conversation.'
- Penalties can be deducted for breaking character, ignoring the revision, or directly addressing the game by asking 'Wait, what was the rule again?'

## Why It Works
It demands heightened 'Yes, And...' by challenging players to agree not only with present offers but to retroactively affirm a new reality. The humor stems from the cognitive dissonance of the audience knowing the original timeline while the characters grapple with the newly imposed truth.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the Referee's revisions respect player boundaries and do not force improvisers into uncomfortable or non-consensual character dynamics. Maintain a supportive environment where players feel safe adapting to sudden, bizarre changes.

# The Chrononaut's Compass

![The Chrononaut's Compass — infographic](img/IS228.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Dynamically reshape a scene's established reality by re-calibrating past events into surprising new truths.

## Overview
The Chrononaut's Compass is a two-player improvisation game where performers collaboratively build an evolving narrative by dynamically reshaping their scene's established reality. Periodically, one player echoes a specific phrase or action previously made by their partner. The partner then declares a 'Compass Shift,' offering an entirely new, reality-altering interpretation of that echoed element, forcing both improvisers to immediately embrace the drastically altered past or present.

## Setup
A host or judge who understands the rules to facilitate. Optionally, a simple visual indicator like a 'Rewind' card or a small chime for the host to trigger transitions.

## How to Play
1. The host calls for a scene suggestion (e.g., location, relationship, genre). Two performers begin a conventional improvisational scene, establishing characters, location, and an initial premise for about 1 minute, aiming to include memorable lines or specific actions.
2. After approximately 60-90 seconds, the host calls 'TEMPORAL ECHO!' (or uses the chime/card).
3. Upon the cue, Performer A must immediately identify a single, specific verbal phrase or significant non-verbal action performed by Performer B within the immediately preceding section of the scene, and physically or vocally echo it as precisely as possible.
4. As soon as Performer A echoes the phrase/action, Performer B must declare a 'Compass Shift'—an entirely new, significant, and reality-altering interpretation of that echoed phrase/action that changes a fundamental truth about the scene's past, present, or future.
5. Both performers must immediately and fully commit to this new reality established by the Compass Shift. The scene continues with characters reacting honestly and dynamically, without questioning or contradicting the declared shift.
6. After another 60-90 seconds of playing in the new reality, the host calls 'TEMPORAL ECHO!' again. This time, roles alternate: Performer B identifies and echoes a phrase/action from Performer A, and Performer A declares the Compass Shift.
7. This alternating cycle continues for a set duration (e.g., 3-5 rounds or 5-7 minutes total scene time) until the host calls 'SCENE' to conclude the evolving narrative.

## Coaching Notes
- Crucially, the Compass Shift must be a positive addition or re-contextualization that broadens the narrative, adds a layer of meaning, or introduces new conflict/stakes. It cannot simply negate a previous point without enriching the story.
- Encourage active listening; it is crucial for accurately identifying the echoed phrase or action.
- Performers must build upon the new reality using 'Yes, And' without hesitation. They must not question the new reality.
- Celebrate performers for fearlessly accepting and integrating even the most outlandish shifts.
- Remind players to allow their characters to be fundamentally changed by the new revelations, altering their objectives, relationships, and even their identities based on the shifts.

## Why It Works
This game demands constant narrative evolution and promotes storytelling over simple comedic beats. By forcing players to embrace unexpected turns and radical, unplanned declarations, it highlights collaborative scene-building, making your partner look good, and the core improv skill of allowing yourself to be changed.

## Safety & Inclusion
Standard improv safety and consent apply. Ensure that reality-altering shifts respect the physical and emotional boundaries of the performers, avoiding choices that force a partner into uncomfortable or non-consensual character dynamics.

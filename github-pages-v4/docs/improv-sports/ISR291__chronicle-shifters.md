# Chronicle Shifters

![Chronicle Shifters — infographic](img/ISR291.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must flawlessly 'Yes, And' major contradictions to the scene's physical reality while maintaining their core emotional relationship.

## Overview
A facilitator-led training exercise where one player (the Chronicler) periodically introduces a major contradiction to the established physical reality or history of the scene. The other players (Adapters) must flawlessly 'Yes, And' this new truth without hesitation, while maintaining the scene's core emotional relationship and status dynamic.

## Setup
2 to 4 players. One designated 'Chronicler', the rest are 'Adapters'. A facilitator with a timer and a bell or clap. No props or special staging required. Best for intermediate to advanced improvisers.

## How to Play
1. Get a simple relationship-based suggestion from the room, such as siblings cleaning an attic or co-workers on a lunch break.
2. The players begin the scene, establishing a grounded physical reality, who they are, and their emotional dynamic.
3. The scene plays normally for the first minute to allow the relationship to ground.
4. Exactly once per minute, the facilitator claps or rings a bell.
5. On this cue, the Chronicler must seamlessly introduce one piece of information in their next line of dialogue that directly contradicts an established physical fact, location, or character identity.
6. Crucially, the Chronicler must maintain the established emotional truth and relationship dynamic. For example, if they are bickering siblings in an attic, they might suddenly refer to the attic as a submarine, but the bickering sibling dynamic remains exactly the same.
7. The Adapters must instantly accept the new reality without any hesitation, questioning, or shock. They justify the shift as if it has always been true, adapting their physical space and dialogue immediately.
8. The scene continues to ground itself in the new reality for another minute until the facilitator signals the next shift.
9. Run for 3 to 4 minutes total, then debrief on how it felt to justify the changes while holding onto the emotional core.

## Coaching Notes
- The facilitator side-coaches to ensure shifts aren't too frequent and that the emotional truth stays grounded.
- The 'win' is the ensemble's ability to seamlessly justify the shifts without breaking the emotional reality.

## Variations
- The Wandering Chronicler: Instead of a designated player, the facilitator acts as the Chronicler. The facilitator freezes the scene, taps a player on the shoulder, feeds them a contradiction to say, and unfreezes the scene.
- Genre Shifters: Instead of physical facts, the contradiction shifts the genre of the scene (e.g., from grounded drama to sci-fi), but the emotional relationship must still remain identical.

## Why It Works
It trains extreme listening, radical acceptance, and the separation of physical facts from emotional truth.

## Safety & Inclusion
Remind players that 'contradictions' should focus on the physical environment, objects, or absurd history. They should never be used to cross personal boundaries, force physical contact, or introduce sensitive/triggering content. The strict rule of maintaining emotional consistency helps prevent the scene from devolving into aggressive, mean-spirited, or unsafe conflict.

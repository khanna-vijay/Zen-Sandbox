# The Chronoscapes Gallery

![The Chronoscapes Gallery — infographic](img/IS023.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams compete to create and present the most hilariously innovative, historically improbable, and physically expressive 'living exhibit'.

## Overview
The Chronoscapes Gallery is a competitive short-form improv game where teams compete to create hilariously innovative and historically improbable living exhibits. Curators verbally invent absurd justifications for their Artifact teammates, who physically embody the exhibit through object work and non-verbal actions. A Chief Curator actively challenges the team by asking probing questions and directing physical changes, forcing the Curators to seamlessly 'Yes, And' every development.

## Setup
Divide the stage conceptually into an 'Exhibit Space' for the living exhibits and a downstage area for the 'Curator(s)' to address the audience. The Chief Curator (Referee) oversees the gallery, solicits a single, broad, and unusual audience suggestion for an exhibit concept, and designates which team goes first.

## How to Play
1. The Chief Curator gets an audience suggestion for an exhibit concept (e.g., 'The Evolution of the Stapler').
2. The designated team takes 15-30 seconds to assign 1-2 players as the Curator(s) and the remaining players as the Artifacts.
3. The Artifacts move into the Exhibit Space and immediately begin creating a physical tableau or short, silent, evolving scene based on the concept.
4. The Curator(s) step forward, address the audience as 'esteemed visitors,' and introduce the exhibit, completely justifying and building upon whatever the Artifacts are physically doing.
5. The Curators must 'Yes, And' every physical action, gesture, or object work performed by the Artifacts, weaving it into a coherent narrative.
6. At any point, the Chief Curator may interject with probing questions that the Curator(s) must immediately integrate into their explanation.
7. The Chief Curator may also signal the Artifacts to change their pose, movement, or 'malfunction', which the Curator(s) must instantly 'Yes, And' into their ongoing explanation.
8. After 2-3 minutes, the Chief Curator declares 'Gallery Closed!' and the other team takes their turn.
9. The Chief Curator awards points for Curatorial Eloquence, Artifact Ingenuity, Seamless Integration, and Audience Applause, and deducts points for fouls.

## Coaching Notes
- Curators must embody an enthusiastic, slightly eccentric, and highly knowledgeable expert.
- Artifacts must rely on strong, clear, and creative physical improvisation and object work without speaking.
- Curators must intently observe their Artifacts and listen to the Chief Curator's questions to endow the Artifacts with meaning and context.
- Call an 'Anachronism Alert!' Foul if the Curator's verbal explanation directly contradicts or completely ignores the physical actions of the Artifacts.
- Call a 'Static Display!' Foul if the Artifacts remain completely motionless and offer little or no physical action for their Curators to work with.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' for excessively bad puns or overly simplistic, uninspired explanations.
- The game demands quick thinking and immediate reactions from both roles, especially with Chief Curator interventions.

## Variations
- Same Suggestion: The second team uses the exact same audience suggestion as the first team to allow for diverse interpretations and direct comparison.
- New Suggestion: The Chief Curator solicits a brand new suggestion for the second team to ensure variety.

## Why It Works
It tests absolutely critical 'Yes, And' skills, as Curators must justify Artifacts' actions and Chief Curator interventions, while Artifacts must adapt to directives. It blends verbal dexterity and physical theatre, fostering constant engagement, rapid pacing, and comedic surprises.

## Safety & Inclusion
The museum exhibit premise inherently guides humor towards intellectual absurdity. The theoretical threat of a clean-content call keeps improvisers vigilant against any accidental slip into inappropriate humor, ensuring a purely wholesome and family-friendly competitive short-form experience.

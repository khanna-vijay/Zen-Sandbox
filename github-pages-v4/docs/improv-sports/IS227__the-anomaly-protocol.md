# The Anomaly Protocol

![The Anomaly Protocol — infographic](img/IS227.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers construct a narrative while the fundamental laws of reality are continuously and unpredictably altered by unseen forces.

## Overview
The Anomaly Protocol is an improvisational game where performers collaboratively construct a narrative within a scene where the fundamental laws of reality are continuously altered by unseen forces called Anomalies. At random intervals, a new Anomaly is introduced, requiring performers to instantly adapt their physicality, dialogue, and character objectives. The game challenges players to integrate these radical shifts into a coherent story, embracing 'being changed' rather than letting the Anomalies derail the narrative.

## Setup
The MC (or a designated 'Anomaly Weaver' judge) prepares a deck of 'Anomaly Cards,' each describing a single, unpersonified, reality-altering force (e.g., erratic gravity, stretching time, contagious emotions). The MC gets an audience suggestion for a location and a simple relationship/objective.

## How to Play
1. Before the scene begins, the MC reveals the first Anomaly Card and gives performers a few seconds to absorb it.
2. The scene begins. Performers must immediately integrate the effects of the current Anomaly into their physical actions, dialogue, and interactions.
3. At unpredictable intervals (typically every 30-60 seconds or at a dramatic moment), the MC or judge signals a shift with a 'ding' and reveals a new Anomaly Card.
4. The previous Anomaly immediately ceases. Performers must instantly drop their previous physical and narrative responses and integrate the new Anomaly into the ongoing scene.
5. Performers must maintain narrative cohesion, using the Anomalies as primary obstacles or catalysts for their characters' journeys rather than isolated gags.
6. The scene continues for 3-4 minutes or until the MC calls 'Scene!' after a final Anomaly has been presented and integrated.

## Coaching Notes
- Do not treat the Anomalies as isolated gags; integrate them deeply into the characters' objectives, relationships, and the overarching plot.
- Embrace mistakes and unexpected turns. You have no time to deliberate, so immediately commit to the new reality and treat every change as a gift.
- Collaboratively build the scene by reacting not only to the Anomaly but also to how your partner is reacting to it.
- Commit heavily to physicality. Anomalies often manifest physically (e.g., changes in gravity or density), requiring expressive and consistent body work.
- Remember there is no 'failure' in how you respond to an Anomaly, only in a lack of commitment.

## Variations
- Advanced Play: Instead of the previous Anomaly ceasing when a new one is introduced, layer the new Anomalies on top of the existing ones (note: this can become unwieldy).

## Why It Works
It pushes improvisers to the absolute limits of their adaptability, creative interpretation, and collaborative trust. It is a true test of 'being changed,' demanding an exceptional commitment to the moment and to the ever-mutating world they collectively inhabit.

## Safety & Inclusion
Players should be mindful of physical safety and spatial awareness when acting out extreme physical anomalies (like erratic gravity or viscous air) to avoid accidental collisions or physical strain.

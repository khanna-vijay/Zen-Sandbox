# Reality Warp

![Reality Warp — infographic](img/IS106.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must maintain a coherent scene while a Referee continuously introduces dramatic, physics-altering 'Warps' to their reality.

## Overview
'Reality Warp' challenges improvisers to maintain a coherent scene and character relationships while the very fabric of their reality is continuously and dramatically altered by the Referee or audience. It is a test of adaptability, physical commitment, and narrative resilience against an ever-changing, absurd world.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 players (2-3 active, one potentially rotating or 'on-call') and a standard competitive short-form stage with space to move. One player is designated the 'Reality Anchor'. All props are mimed. The audience provides an initial scene suggestion (location, relationship, or event). The Referee prepares 'Warp Cards' or solicits 'Reality Warp' suggestions directly from the audience.

## How to Play
1. 1. Scene Initiation (The Anchor): 2-3 players begin a scene based on the audience suggestion. The 'Reality Anchor' grounds the scene for the first 30-45 seconds, establishing a normal environment and clear character relationships.
2. 2. The Warp Call: At any point after the initial Anchor phase, the Referee calls out, 'REALITY WARP!'
3. 3. The Warp Effect: The Referee immediately announces a dramatic, environmental, or physics-altering change (e.g., 'Gravity reverses!', 'The air turns to jelly!', 'All dialogue must be sung!').
4. 4. Instantaneous Integration: Players must instantly and fully 'Yes, And...' the Warp. They must physically and verbally reflect the new reality without questioning, ignoring, or trying to undo it.
5. 5. Stacking Warps: The Referee can call 'REALITY WARP!' again at any time to introduce a new effect that layers on top of or replaces previous ones.
6. 6. Character and Narrative: Players must persist with the scene's plot and maintain their character's personality despite the chaos, finding comedy in how their specific character deals with the impossible circumstances.

## Coaching Notes
- Award 1-3 points for seamless integration, creative reactions, narrative persistence, teamwork, and generating big audience laughs.
- Call a 'Reality Check Foul' (-1 point) if a player questions the Warp, fails to commit physically, or tries to logically explain away the absurdity. The core of this game is accepting the impossible.
- Call a 'Lag Foul' (-1 point) for a significant delay in reacting to a 'REALITY WARP!' call.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' (-1 point) for obvious jokes or rejecting a partner's offer, and a 'content foul' (-1 point) for blue humor or swearing.
- Remind players that they must constantly listen and observe both the Referee's Warps and their partners' reactions to integrate them seamlessly.
- The Referee can call 'Warp Cleanse!' to remove a previous effect if the scene becomes too chaotic, allowing for a new direction.

## Variations
- Rotation: With 4 players, one player can rotate in as a new character or take over from a player who 'falls victim' to a warp (e.g., gets stuck in jelly, floats too high).

## Why It Works
It pushes the 'Yes, And...' muscle to the extreme by forcing players to accept completely external, outlandish offers. It demands active listening, intense mimed object work, and strong physical choices while maintaining core character motivations in a world turned upside down.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety when reacting to extreme environmental warps (e.g., bouncing, floating, avoiding lava). Maintain family-friendly boundaries, enforcing the content foul for any inappropriate content.

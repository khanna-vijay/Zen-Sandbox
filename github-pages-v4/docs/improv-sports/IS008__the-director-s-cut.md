# The Director's Cut

![The Director's Cut — infographic](img/IS008.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An active Director pauses a live improvisational scene to provide real-time, targeted feedback that performers must immediately integrate into the evolving narrative.

## Overview
The Director's Cut is an improv game that introduces an active Director who pauses a live improvisational scene to provide real-time, targeted feedback or Director's Notes. Performers must immediately integrate these notes, such as replaying a moment with new subtext or justifying a past choice, into the evolving narrative. This unique mechanism challenges improvisers to continuously refine and deepen their storytelling on the fly, transforming unexpected interruptions into a dynamic engine for enhanced narrative depth.

## Setup
Two or three improvisers are on stage, ready to create a scene. One designated individual (The Director) sits separately with a distinct signal, such as a large hand-bell or a theatrical buzzer. The Director explicitly does not physically or verbally enter the scene as a character. The scene begins with a single, open audience suggestion (e.g., a shared location, a relationship, or a character trait).

## How to Play
1. Initial Scene Establishment: The improvisers begin a regular scene, aiming to quickly establish characters, relationship, and a clear platform (who, what, where), striving to initiate a conflict or narrative hook.
2. Director's Interruptions (The Cut): At any point during the scene, the Director can ring the bell or buzz to immediately pause the action and offer a Director's Note to a specific improviser, a pair, or the scene as a whole.
3. Note Option 1 - Rewind & Replay: The Director identifies a precise moment and instructs performers to re-perform that moment and everything that followed with a new, specified emotional subtext, status dynamic, or character intention.
4. Note Option 2 - Justify That Choice!: The Director points to a specific, ambiguous, or unexpected choice made by an improviser, who must then improvise an immediate, compelling justification in character, which partners must Yes, And.
5. Note Option 3 - Explore the Alternative Path: The Director pinpoints a pivotal decision point, asks improvisers to undo that choice, and re-enter the scene making a different, plausible choice to create a narrative branch.
6. Note Option 4 - Deepen the Stakes: The Director asks performers to immediately articulate or demonstrate the profound underlying stakes of the current situation through heightened action or revealing dialogue.
7. Note Option 5 - Character Monologue: The Director asks what a character is really thinking. The improviser steps out of the scene briefly to deliver an unvarnished monologue, then re-enters, with partners reacting to the energy of the revealed thought.
8. Performers' Response: Improvisers must instantly acknowledge the Director's input, integrate it fully and joyfully, and continue the scene from the specified point, treating the notes as an ultimate form of Yes, And.
9. Scene End: The scene concludes when the Director perceives a natural end point or upon a pre-determined time limit (e.g., 6-8 minutes), signaled by a final Cut! from the Director.

## Coaching Notes
- The Director's primary function is to serve the narrative, challenging performers to explore greater depth, specificity, and dramatic potential rather than dictating plot.
- Aim for 3-5 interruptions within a typical 5-7 minute scene to allow for substantial narrative flow while providing ample opportunity for meta-engagement.
- Collaborative Scene-Building is paramount: when one improviser is given a note, scene partners must wholeheartedly support and Yes, And the new reality or revised history.
- Performers must constantly and willingly allow their character's history, current emotions, or future trajectory to be fundamentally altered by the Director's notes.
- Active listening is critical for parsing the Director's precise instructions and integrating them seamlessly into the scene's fabric.
- Meta-Awareness: Performers operate on dual planes, fully immersed as characters while simultaneously possessing the awareness to adapt to external directorial input.

## Why It Works
This game actively builds and rebuilds the story, forcing performers to consider cause-and-effect, character motivation, and the impact of choices on the evolving plot. It demands cheerful and creative integration of unexpected interruptions, transforming potential failures or unplanned elements into rich narrative opportunities, and explicitly valorizes the craft of revision on the fly.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the Director's notes focus on narrative and character choices rather than personal critiques of the performers. Establish boundaries beforehand regarding the emotional intensity of 'Deepen the Stakes' or 'Character Monologue' prompts to maintain a supportive and consensual playing environment.

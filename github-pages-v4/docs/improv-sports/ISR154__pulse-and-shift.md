# Pulse and Shift

![Pulse and Shift — infographic](img/ISR154.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A non-verbal ensemble warm-up where the group shifts a shared rhythm through silent consensus.

## Overview
A non-verbal ensemble warm-up where the group maintains a shared physical rhythm or sound. Anyone can suggest a change, but the group only adopts it if they collectively, silently agree through eye contact and physical cues. It builds intense group focus and listening without the pressure of being funny.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle where everyone can clearly see each other. No props or physical contact are required. The facilitator or a designated player starts as the 'Catalyst'.

## How to Play
1. Establish the Pulse: The Catalyst starts a simple, repetitive physical action and/or sound (e.g., a slow foot tap, a gentle sway, or a steady hum). Everyone joins in immediately, matching the rhythm and volume exactly to create a unified 'pulse'.
2. Offer a Prompt: At any time, any player can call out a single descriptive word to suggest a change to the pulse (e.g., 'Faster', 'Lighter', 'Angry', 'Underwater', 'Jagged').
3. Look for the Cues: Players must not change their action immediately. Instead, they must look around the circle for concrete physical cues of agreement. Look for sustained eye contact, a sudden collective inhale, or tiny micro-movements leaning toward the new prompt.
4. The Shift: If a player sees the group starting to adopt the change, they join them. The shift should happen organically, rippling through the circle like a flock of birds changing direction.
5. The Rejection: If the group does not physically agree (e.g., people keep doing the original pulse and don't make eye contact), the prompt is dropped. The group simply continues the original pulse. This is a normal part of the game.
6. Ending the Game: The exercise continues for a set time limit of 3 minutes, or until the facilitator calls 'Neutral and breathe' to bring the group to a resting stop.

## Coaching Notes
- The 'win' is achieving a seamless, unified group shift without anyone forcing it.
- Encourage players to comfortably let go of prompts that the group doesn't pick up.
- Remind players to read concrete physical cues rather than forcing their own ideas onto the group.
- Emphasize that there is no pressure to be funny or invent a narrative; the goal is pure focus on listening.

## Variations
- Blind Pulse: Players close their eyes and rely entirely on auditory cues (breathing shifts, volume, tempo) to agree on and execute shifts. Excellent for advanced listening.
- Emotion Only: Instead of physical descriptors (like 'faster'), players only suggest emotional states (like 'joyful' or 'suspicious'), challenging the group to find the physical manifestation together.

## Why It Works
It isolates non-verbal communication and ensemble awareness, teaching players to read concrete physical cues rather than forcing their own ideas onto the group, all without the pressure of being funny or inventing a narrative.

## Safety & Inclusion
Highly accessible as it requires no physical contact and can be played entirely seated. Facilitators should explicitly state that a prompt being 'rejected' by the group is a successful part of the game's mechanics, not a personal rejection, ensuring emotional safety. Players should keep all movements within a comfortable range of motion.

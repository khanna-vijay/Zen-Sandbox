# The Unfolding Canvas

![The Unfolding Canvas — infographic](img/ISR301.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players build a scene while a Host feeds them external clues pointing to a secret Narrative Seed known only to the audience.

## Overview
A narrative long-form game where players build a scene while a Host feeds them external clues pointing to a secret 'Narrative Seed.' Crucially, the audience knows the secret seed beforehand, creating delicious dramatic irony as they watch the players scramble to justify bizarre prompts and organically discover the hidden truth.

## Setup
Format: Competitive improv format (Narrative Long-Form). Requires 2-3 players, a Host/Director, and a bell or buzzer. You will also need a whiteboard or large pad to reveal the secret to the audience. The stage should be mostly bare to allow the players' imaginations to fill the space.

## How to Play
1. The Secret Reveal: The players are sent out of earshot or asked to cover their eyes and hum. The Host asks the audience for a mundane location (e.g., 'A bakery') and a bizarre, hidden 'Narrative Seed' (e.g., 'The baked goods are coming alive'). The Host writes the Seed on a whiteboard and shows it to the audience.
2. The Initiation: The players return. The Host gives them the mundane location, and they begin a grounded, realistic scene, establishing their characters and relationships.
3. The Director's Notes: Every 60-90 seconds, the Host rings a bell and delivers a 'Director's Note'—a verbal stage direction or sensory detail that hints at the Narrative Seed. (e.g., 'Director's Note: A strange ticking sound is coming from the oven.')
4. Instant Justification: The players must instantly 'Yes, And' the note without breaking character. They cannot ask the Host what it means; they must incorporate the new reality into their scene and justify it.
5. Escalation: The Host's notes should start subtle and become increasingly overt. To maintain momentum, the Host speaks these notes clearly over the action without stopping the scene, acting as a live side-coach.
6. The Climax: The scene ends when the players organically discover the truth of the Narrative Seed, or when they arrive at a brilliantly justified alternative conclusion that wraps up the narrative arc.
7. Scoring: Judges score the scene (typically 1-5 points) based on the players' ability to justify the prompts, maintain narrative coherence, and allow their characters to be changed by the new information.

## Coaching Notes
- Lean into Dramatic Irony: The audience knows the secret, making the players' oblivious choices highly entertaining.
- Enforce the Forced 'Yes, And': Undeniable external offers force players to abandon preconceived plots.
- Keep Prompts Streamlined: Using only verbal side-coaching eliminates stage clutter and technical delays.
- Maintain Momentum: The Host should speak notes clearly over the action without stopping the scene.

## Variations
- The Prop Box (Physical Medium): Instead of verbal notes, limit the medium entirely to physical objects. The Host calls 'Freeze!', walks on stage to hand a player a bizarre, unrelated prop, calls 'Unfreeze!', and leaves. The players must justify the object.
- Soundscape Canvas (Audio Medium): The improviser on the sound board plays specific, unannounced sound effects as the only prompts. The players must justify every sound cue as part of the unfolding mystery.

## Why It Works
It trains players to let go of their own ideas and serve the emerging story. Undeniable external offers force players to abandon preconceived plots, while dramatic irony makes their oblivious choices highly entertaining for the audience.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Host must ensure 'Director's Notes' focus on the environment, sensory details, or external events (e.g., 'The room smells like ozone') rather than dictating a player's physical autonomy or internal trauma (avoid 'You suddenly feel enraged and want to hit someone'). Players always retain the right to justify a prompt in a way that maintains their personal boundaries and physical safety.

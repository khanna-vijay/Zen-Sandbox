# Object of Consequence

![Object of Consequence — infographic](img/IS192.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A seemingly ordinary object progressively reveals extraordinary, scene-altering properties at timed intervals to drive an escalating narrative.

## Overview
Object of Consequence is an improvisational game for two performers centered on a seemingly ordinary object that progressively reveals extraordinary, scene-altering properties at timed intervals. Beginning with a mundane prop within a given location and relationship, improvisers collaboratively discover and 'Yes, And' each new characteristic, dramatically escalating the narrative. The game culminates in a resolution phase where all revealed properties must be integrated for a cohesive ending.

## Setup
Two improvisers take the stage. The host elicits a starting location (e.g., a cluttered antique shop) and a relationship between the two characters (e.g., estranged siblings) from the audience. The host then presents a single, ordinary, tangible object. This object must be common, visually present, easy to handle, and generic enough to allow for a wide range of unexpected properties (e.g., a rusty wrench, a single sock, a worn-out book, a plastic spoon). Avoid objects with too many inherent functions or strong pre-existing symbolic associations.

## How to Play
1. Opening (0-1 Minute): The improvisers begin the scene in the given location and relationship. The object is introduced as an entirely mundane prop, integrated naturally into their initial interaction.
2. Revelation 1 (at 1 Minute): A clear, audible chime or host call signals the first revelation point. The object must now collaboratively reveal its first new, significant, and unexpected property or consequence. One performer initiates this through interaction, and the other must immediately 'Yes, And' it.
3. Revelation 2 (at 2 Minutes): The chime sounds again. The object gains another new, significant, and unexpected property. This should build upon, contrast with, or complicate the previous one, further escalating the stakes.
4. Revelation 3 (at 3 Minutes): The final chime sounds. The object gains its most dramatic and scene-altering property, pushing the narrative towards a critical turning point or climax.
5. Resolution Phase (60-90 Seconds): Immediately after the 3-minute chime, the host calls 'Resolution!' (or 'Scene, and finish!'). Performers have a fixed amount of time (e.g., 75 seconds, clearly timed and visible/audible) to bring the narrative to a satisfying conclusion that incorporates all of the object's revealed properties.

## Coaching Notes
- Object Selection: Crucial for setting the stage. Keep it mundane and generic to allow for maximum creative freedom.
- Timed Signals: Use clear, distinct chimes or vocal calls at 1, 2, and 3 minutes, and a clear 'Resolution!' call followed by a ticking timer.
- Subtle Nudge (Optional, Last Resort): If performers struggle to initiate a new property after a 5-10 second pause following a chime, the host may offer a single, non-specific sensory cue (e.g., a faint hum, a flicker of light, or a whispered descriptive word like 'warm' or 'vibrating'). This is a gentle prompt to inspire discovery, not dictate content.
- Embrace Mistakes/Risks: Performers must commit fully to the new realities, however outlandish. There is no 'wrong' idea, only a lack of commitment.
- Collaborative Scene-building: Make your partner look good. Actively 'Yes, And' their initiated consequences, confirming reality and exploring implications.
- Being Changed & Status Play: Characters must visibly react to and be transformed by the object's evolving properties, allowing for dramatic shifts in status and power dynamics.

## Why It Works
The object acts as an undeniable central driver of the story, forcing the narrative to evolve exponentially and creating rising action. It tests essential improv skills including discovery, acceptance ('Yes, And'), object work, building a narrative arc, being changed by circumstances, status play, unwavering commitment, and active listening.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety when interacting with the tangible object, especially as stakes and physical reactions escalate. Maintain standard boundaries and consent regarding physical touch and emotional intensity during high-stakes relationship scenes.

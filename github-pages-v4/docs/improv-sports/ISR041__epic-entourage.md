# Epic Entourage

![Epic Entourage — infographic](img/ISR041.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Four players embody distinct narrative roles to complete an audience-suggested quest in a fast-paced, competitive showdown.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where a team of four completes an audience-suggested quest by embodying four distinct narrative roles: The Narrator, The Hero, The Obstacle, and The Solution. Teams compete back-to-back to see who can tell the most entertaining and cohesive story within a strict time limit.

## Setup
Requires 8 players total (two teams of 4) and a Referee downstage. No props; everything is mimed. Get an epic, mundane, or ridiculous quest suggestion from the audience (e.g., 'Return the stolen Wi-Fi router to the peak of a treacherous mountain').

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a quest suggestion from the audience and sets a timer (usually 2 to 3 minutes per team).
2. Team A takes the stage. The four players quickly assign themselves the four roles: The Narrator, The Hero, The Obstacle, and The Solution.
3. The Referee blows the whistle to begin. The Narrator steps forward to set the opening scene, describe the environment, and introduce the Hero.
4. The Hero steps into the scene, establishing their character and beginning their journey through mime and monologue.
5. The Obstacle steps in as a character, creature, or physical force of nature to block the Hero's path. The Hero and Obstacle interact directly, playing out a short scene of conflict or banter.
6. Once the Hero is sufficiently trapped, outmatched, or the scene peaks, The Solution steps in. They can enter as a helpful ally, a magical item, or a sudden realization, providing exactly what the Hero needs to overcome the Obstacle.
7. The Hero uses the Solution to defeat the Obstacle. The Obstacle and Solution step back.
8. The Narrator immediately steps back in to bridge the story, transitioning the Hero to the next location or escalating the stakes.
9. This organic cycle repeats, escalating in absurdity and danger, until the Hero finally completes the quest and the Referee calls 'Time!'.
10. Team B then takes the stage, gets a new quest suggestion from the audience, and plays their round.
11. After both teams have played, the Referee conducts a cheer-off. The team that receives the loudest applause for their quest wins 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee can call standard fouls: the clean-content foul for inappropriate content (loss of points/turn), the 'Groaner' foul for terrible puns, or a 'Delay of Game' foul if a player stalls or the story grinds to a halt.
- Encourage organic transitions driven by scene flow rather than rigid turn-taking.
- Direct scene-work between the Hero and the Obstacle allows for character banter and physical comedy.
- Maintain high-energy pacing enforced by the ticking clock and the Referee.

## Variations
- Genre Entourage: The audience provides both a quest and a specific movie or literary genre (e.g., Sci-Fi, Western, Noir). The Narrator's vocabulary, the Obstacle's nature, and the Solution's tools must all fit the genre.
- Revolving Entourage: Every time the Narrator speaks to transition the scene, the players physically rotate positions in their lineup. The Hero becomes the Obstacle, the Obstacle becomes the Solution, etc. This forces every player to adapt to every role on the fly.

## Why It Works
Defined narrative roles teach story structure while generating comedy. The clear constraints of the roles allow players to focus on escalating the stakes and driving the narrative forward organically without getting bogged down in plotting.

## Safety & Inclusion
Because the Hero and Obstacle interact directly in conflict, emphasize physical safety: no actual striking, grabbing, or lifting. All combat or physical struggles must be mimed with safe space between the players (stage combat rules apply). The Referee strictly enforces the clean-content foul to ensure all content remains family-friendly and accessible.

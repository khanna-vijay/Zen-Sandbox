# Continuum Cascade

![Continuum Cascade — infographic](img/IS031.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players build a scene that is frequently interrupted by a host imposing a 'cascade'—a new narrative truth that forces them to retroactively justify all previous actions.

## Overview
Continuum Cascade is an improvisational game where players collaboratively build an evolving narrative. The story is frequently interrupted by a host who imposes a 'cascade'—a new, specific narrative truth. Players must immediately stop and retroactively justify all their previous actions and dialogue as if this new truth had been the underlying reality all along.

## Setup
Two or three improvisers (teams of two are ideal) are on stage. A host or judge with a distinct, loud signal (e.g., a bell, a clapper, or simply shouting the game name) is present. The host takes a single suggestion from the audience to establish the initial scene, such as a location, relationship, or opening line.

## How to Play
1. The improvisers begin the scene, establishing characters, environment, and an initial conflict or situation to build a logical, engaging narrative.
2. At semi-regular intervals (e.g., every 45-75 seconds, or at a natural pause), the host/judge interjects by shouting, 'CONTINUUM CASCADE!'
3. Immediately after shouting, the host/judge delivers a new, specific narrative element or reinterpretive frame (e.g., 'one of you is a shapeshifter').
4. Upon hearing the Cascade, players must immediately stop their previous action and dialogue without breaking character.
5. Players must re-frame their immediate past, retroactively justifying how everything that just occurred before the Cascade actually makes perfect sense within this newly imposed reality.
6. The players continue building the narrative with this new reality incorporated, driving the story forward while ensuring their past actions remain consistent with the new context.
7. The game continues with multiple Cascades until the host/judge calls 'Scene!'

## Coaching Notes
- Do not simply start a new scene or act as if the Cascade just happened; the new truth must be accepted as if it has been true all along.
- Listen intently to each other's first attempts at justification and immediately build upon them to co-create the 'why' and 'how' of the new reality together.
- Characters are constantly changed by the revelation of these new truths. Adapt your physicality, vocal tone, and perspective instantly.
- Active listening and observation are crucial for picking up on prior dialogue or actions that can be creatively re-contextualized by the new cascade.
- Embrace the unexpected turn. What might otherwise be a 'mistake' suddenly becomes fertile ground for justification.

## Why It Works
It explicitly demands complex, recursive narrative development and trains performers to see all input as a gift. It challenges players in 'Yes, And', justification/logic, active listening, and emotional range by forcing them to invent a compelling retrospective logic that ties disjointed elements together.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical and emotional boundaries are respected during sudden character shifts, and avoid cascades that force players into unsafe, offensive, or non-consensual subject matter.

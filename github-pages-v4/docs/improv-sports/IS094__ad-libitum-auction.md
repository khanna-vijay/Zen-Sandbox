# Ad-Libitum Auction

![Ad-Libitum Auction — infographic](img/IS094.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players compete as high-stakes bidders for an absurd, audience-suggested item, rapidly improvising increasingly outlandish justifications for why they desperately need it.

## Overview
"Ad-Libitum Auction" is an innovative competitive short-form improv game where players compete as high-stakes bidders for an absurd, audience-suggested item. The core gameplay involves rapidly improvising increasingly outlandish and compelling justifications for why they desperately need the item, using 'Yes, And' to build a shared, ludicrous lore around its significance. Overseen by a comedic Auctioneer, the game blends persuasive rhetoric, rapid escalation, strategic team play, and unique audience interaction.

## Setup
Requires 1 Auctioneer (Referee) and 2 primary Bidders (one from each team). Other team members form a support bench, ready for a strategic tag-in. The Auctioneer asks the audience for something mundane, abstract, or delightfully silly (like a single sock, the sound of a sigh, or a forgotten shopping list). The Auctioneer may gently guide the audience towards suitable suggestions or choose from a pre-vetted list to ensure a comedic premise.

## How to Play
1. Item Introduction: The Auctioneer presents the audience-suggested item plainly and sets a whimsical starting bid (e.g., '$5 and your most embarrassing childhood memory!').
2. Alternating Bids: Players alternate teams, taking turns as the primary bidder.
3. Increase the Bid: Each turn, a primary bidder must offer a higher value (monetary, emotional, or ridiculously abstract).
4. Justify and Escalate: The bidder must provide a unique, improvised justification for why they desperately need this item. This justification must 'Yes, And...' and build upon the established lore or claims made by previous bidders.
5. Support Player Tag-In: If a primary bidder hesitates, they may, once per team per round, briefly tag in a teammate from their bench for one immediate, quick supporting justification. The original primary bidder then immediately tags back in.
6. Auctioneer's Role: The Auctioneer acts as a traditional, theatrical auctioneer ('Going once, going twice...'), actively mediating, encouraging, and judging the creativity and comedic impact of bids.
7. Round End: The round ends when the Auctioneer declares 'Sold!' to a team. This happens either when a player delivers an unbeatable final justification, or an opposing player fails to offer a new bid within the 5-second 'Stalling' rule.
8. Scoring: +3 points for an exceptionally creative winning justification; +2 points for a strong, original justification; +1 point for a competent justification; -1 point for excessive hesitation, repetition, or failing to 'Yes, And'.
9. Fouls: The Referee enforces the clean-content foul (inappropriate content, 3 points to opposing team), Groaner Foul (obvious pun, -1 point), and Stalling Foul (taking longer than 5 seconds, -1 point and item awarded to the other team).

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage 'Yes, And...', active listening, and object work to imbue the mundane item with massive significance.
- The Auctioneer's physical and vocal character choices can significantly elevate the game's energy.
- Remind players that the comedy stems from imaginative escalation and the battle of wits, not blue humor.
- Ensure support players adhere to the 'Yes, And...' principle and directly support the primary bidder's argument when tagging in.
- Maintain dynamic pacing; the 5-second stalling rule should be strictly enforced to keep the energy high.

## Why It Works
The game's inherent mechanism of justifying absurd needs for mundane objects challenges core improv skills. It develops persuasive rhetoric, rapid 'Yes, And' escalation, and strategic team play while building a shared, ludicrous lore.

## Safety & Inclusion
The game relies on a clear foul system (especially the clean-content foul) to ensure all content remains strictly clean, family-friendly, and suitable for all ages, guiding humor away from inappropriate themes.

# The Narrative Conductor

![The Narrative Conductor — infographic](img/IS098.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Seamlessly weave abrupt, audience-dictated narrative or stylistic shifts into an ongoing scene.

## Overview
An MC (the 'Conductor') frequently calls 'SHIFT!' to introduce abrupt, audience-dictated or MC-chosen changes to an ongoing scene. These shifts fundamentally alter genre, character objectives, language style, or time. Improvisers must instantly and seamlessly integrate these new, often conflicting, directives into their evolving narrative.

## Setup
3-4 improvisers on stage. An MC acts as the 'Narrative Conductor' to pace the game and mediate audience input. No props required. Get a location and a relationship between two characters from the audience to begin.

## How to Play
1. Two improvisers begin a scene based on the audience suggestions (location and relationship). A third or fourth player may enter naturally later.
2. At any point, the MC calls out 'SHIFT!' to introduce a new directive for comedic effect or challenge.
3. The MC presents a new directive from a category (Genre, Objective, Secret, Pace & Time, Language Style, Power Dynamic, Physicality), sometimes using a quick audience suggestion.
4. Improvisers must immediately pivot and justify the new directive within the existing context of the scene, without restarting.
5. The scene continues with these new constraints, and the MC can call 'SHIFT!' multiple times to layer new challenges.
6. The MC calls 'SCENE!' to end the game when a suitable narrative point or comedic peak is reached.

## Coaching Notes
- Ensure shifts fundamentally alter a core aspect of the scene's narrative, genre, character motivation, or theatrical style, not just emotion.
- Improvisers must seamlessly integrate the change into what's already happening, making it appear as if the new reality was always bubbling beneath the surface.
- Avoid 'Ignoring a Shift' (failing to acknowledge or integrate), 'Breaking Character' (laughing or commenting on the game), or 'Wimping Out' (not fully committing to the new reality).
- The MC should declare shifts concisely and clearly.

## Variations
- Competitive Scoring: Award points for seamless integration, witty justification, and narrative adaptability. Use an audience applause meter to gauge success. Call fouls for ignoring shifts, breaking character, or wimping out.

## Why It Works
Forces constant 'Yes, And...' to external, unpredictable, and often contradictory commands, demanding peak listening and justification skills. It celebrates the improviser's ability to make sense out of nonsense.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical shifts (like being trapped in a box or moving like animals) are performed safely without risking injury or crossing physical boundaries.

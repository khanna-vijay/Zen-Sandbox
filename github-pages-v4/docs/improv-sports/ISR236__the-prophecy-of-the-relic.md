# The Prophecy of the Relic

![The Prophecy of the Relic — infographic](img/ISR236.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams alternate playing the same two characters to seamlessly continue a narrative about an ordinary object cursed with an absurd prophecy.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two teams alternate playing the same two characters, seamlessly continuing a narrative about an ordinary object cursed with an absurd, audience-suggested prophecy.

## Setup
Two teams (Red and Blue) stand in the wings. The Referee stands downstage center. The Referee explains the game and asks the audience to fill in the blanks of a prophecy: 'Whoever wields the [Ordinary Object] shall [Absurd Consequence].' For example: 'Whoever wields the spatula shall cause everyone to speak in rhyme.'

## How to Play
1. The Initiation: Two players from the Red Team step on stage. They begin a scene where they discover, buy, or interact with the suggested object. They must establish clear characters and use precise pantomime to make the invisible object feel real.
2. The Trigger: The Red Team must naturally trigger the object's power within the first 45 seconds. As soon as the absurd consequence happens (e.g., they start speaking in rhyme), the Referee blows the whistle and yells 'Switch!'
3. The Relay (The Tag-Out): The two Red Team players immediately freeze in place. Two players from the Blue Team run on stage, gently tap the Red players on the shoulder, and assume their exact physical positions.
4. The Continuation: The Blue Team players take over the exact same characters. They must instantly justify their physical positions and continue the exact same story, escalating the chaos of the prophecy.
5. Rapid Fire: The Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Switch!' every 30-60 seconds, forcing the teams to rapidly swap in and out. The narrative must remain continuous, with both teams sharing the responsibility of playing the same two characters.
6. The Climax: After 4 to 6 rotations, the Referee announces, 'Next team to enter must conclude the prophecy!' The final duo must find a way to break the curse, destroy the relic, or fully succumb to it, ending the scene.
7. Scoring: The Referee awards up to 5 points to the team that maintained the most consistent physical object work, and up to 5 points to the team that drove the narrative forward most creatively. The audience then cheers for their favorite team, awarding a 5-point bonus to the loudest cheer.

## Coaching Notes
- Character Relay: Opposing teams must collaborate by sharing and matching the same characters and physicalities.
- Object Work Focus: Force players to maintain the physical reality of the invisible prop (size, weight, and location) across multiple cast changes.
- Prophecy Fill-in-the-Blanks: Ensure the audience suggestion guarantees a unique, chaotic premise every time.
- The Referee should strictly enforce a content foul (loss of 3 points) for any blue humor or innuendo, ensuring the scene remains family-friendly.

## Variations
- Prophecy of the Pants (Classic Mode): Restrict the object to an article of clothing that the characters must physically put on to trigger the curse.
- Genre Relic: The Referee gets a list of movie genres from the audience. Every time they call 'Switch!', they also shout a new genre. The new team must continue the exact same story, but instantly adapt the tone to the new genre (e.g., Western, Sci-Fi, Noir).

## Why It Works
It forces players to maintain the physical reality of an invisible prop across multiple cast changes while collaborating with an opposing team to share and match the same characters and physicalities.

## Safety & Inclusion
Broadening the prompt from 'pants' to 'relic/object' removes the inherent risk of cheap crotch-based humor or unwanted physical proximity during pantomime. Tag-outs must be taught as gentle shoulder taps, never shoves.

# Scene Sculptors

![Scene Sculptors — infographic](img/ISR059.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must instantly justify bizarre, pre-collected audience suggestions injected mid-scene by the Referee without breaking character.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two teams build a scene together, but the Referee periodically yells 'SCULPT!' to inject pre-collected audience suggestions like a new emotion, a bizarre object, or a sudden relationship shift. Players must instantly justify these bizarre changes without breaking character or acknowledging the game mechanics, treating the new reality as if it has always been true.

## Setup
Format: Competitive Short-Form (competitive short-form match style). Players: 4 players (2 from Team A, 2 from Team B) and 1 Referee. Props: None (all objects are mimed). Pre-Game Prep: Before the scene begins, the Referee rapidly collects a list of 4-5 items from the audience in specific categories: Emotions, Objects, Relationships, and Secrets. The Referee writes these down or memorizes them to use mid-scene without stopping the action.

## How to Play
1. 1. Get the Base Suggestion: The Referee asks the audience for a starting Location and a mundane activity to ground the scene.
2. 2. Establish the Baseline: Two players (one from each team) begin the scene. They must play a grounded, realistic scene for at least 45 to 60 seconds. This establishes a strong baseline reality so the upcoming changes have comedic impact.
3. 3. The Sculpt Command: At the 45-60 second mark (and every 45-60 seconds thereafter), the Referee blows the whistle and yells 'SCULPT!' followed by a category and a pre-collected suggestion. (e.g., 'SCULPT OBJECT: The broom is now a live rattlesnake!').
4. 4. Instant Justification: The players must immediately accept the new reality without missing a beat. They cannot explicitly state the change or ask questions about it (e.g., 'Why am I holding a snake?'). They must justify it through dialogue and action (e.g., 'I told you the pest control in this saloon was getting out of hand, keep sweeping!').
5. 5. Tagging Out: To keep energy high, the Referee can blow the whistle and call 'TAG!' The two off-stage players tap in to replace the current players. The incoming players assume the EXACT same characters, maintaining the current sculpted reality, emotions, and objects. The scene does not reset; it continues from the exact second the tag occurred.
6. 6. Escalation: The Referee continues to layer 'Sculpts' on top of each other until the scene reaches a natural comedic peak or time runs out (usually 3-4 minutes).
7. 7. Scoring: The Referee awards +1 point for a seamless, hilarious justification of a Sculpt, and +2 points if players cleverly reincorporate a previously sculpted element. Fouls (-1 point) are called for 'The Explainer' (acknowledging the game mechanics instead of justifying the reality) or 'Sculpt Freeze' (visibly hesitating or dropping the scene's momentum). The audience decides the overall winner via applause at the end.

## Coaching Notes
- Pre-collected suggestions ensure the scene's pacing is never interrupted by crowd work.
- 45-60 second intervals between Sculpts allow the scene to breathe and establish a firm reality before disrupting it.
- Tag-out mechanics force players to actively listen from the sidelines so they can seamlessly inherit a heavily altered reality.
- Watch out for 'The Explainer' (acknowledging the game mechanics instead of justifying the reality) or 'Sculpt Freeze' (visibly hesitating or dropping the scene's momentum).

## Variations
- Genre Sculpt: Instead of objects or emotions, the Referee collects a list of movie/theater genres. When 'SCULPT GENRE!' is called, the players must instantly shift the tone, tropes, and physical style of the scene to match the new genre while keeping the plot intact.
- Solo Sculpt: Only one player is 'sculpted' at a time (e.g., 'SCULPT EMOTION - PLAYER 1: Deeply Paranoid'). The other player must remain the grounded 'straight man,' reacting naturally to their partner's sudden, bizarre shift in behavior.

## Why It Works
Tests advanced 'Yes, And' skills by forcing immediate, unquestioning acceptance of absurd parameters.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical Safety: Because objects and actions change rapidly, players must maintain spatial awareness and avoid aggressive physical contact (e.g., if an object is sculpted into a weapon, it must be mimed safely at a distance). Content Safety: The Referee must filter pre-collected suggestions to ensure they are clean, all-ages, and avoid harmful stereotypes (especially when collecting 'Relationships' or 'Emotions'). Players always have the right to tag out if a sculpted element makes them uncomfortable.

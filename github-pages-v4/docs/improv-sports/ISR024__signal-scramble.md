# Signal Scramble

![Signal Scramble — infographic](img/ISR024.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy competitive short-form game where improvisers play a grounded scene periodically interrupted by bizarre, audience-suggested sensory shifts.

## Overview
Signal Scramble is a high-energy competitive short-form game where improvisers play out a grounded, everyday scene that is periodically interrupted by bizarre, audience-suggested sensory shifts. Instead of just standing and reacting, players must instantly justify and physically commit to these new sensory realities while continuing their original scene, relationship, and objective. The Referee acts as the dial-turner, calling out new signals to test the players' physical commitment and ability to weave absurd environmental changes into a coherent narrative.

## Setup
2 to 4 players (can be played head-to-head with representatives from both teams, or by a single team playing against the clock). No props or setup required. The Referee stands downstage to manage the audience and call the scrambles. Get a mundane location and relationship from the audience to ground the scene. Then, ask the audience for 3 to 4 bizarre sensory signals (e.g., a strange smell, a weird physical sensation, an atmospheric change like fluctuating gravity).

## How to Play
1. The Referee establishes the base scene by getting a mundane location and relationship from the audience.
2. The Referee then collects 3 to 4 abstract sensory signals from the audience, such as a texture, a smell, or a physical sensation.
3. The players begin their grounded scene, focusing on their relationship and objective.
4. After 30 to 45 seconds, allowing the scene to establish, the Referee blows the whistle and calls out 'Signal Scramble!' followed by the first audience suggestion.
5. Players must immediately alter their physicality and dialogue to reflect this new sensory reality, justifying it within the context of their scene without dropping their characters.
6. The Referee continues to call new scrambles every 30 to 45 seconds, forcing the players to adapt to new realities while pushing the scene forward.
7. The game concludes after all signals have been used and the scene reaches a natural button.
8. At the end of the scene, the audience votes by applause for the team that best integrated the signals, and the winner receives 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- Granular mid-scene points are entirely removed to maintain pacing.
- The Referee may call standard fouls during play, such as a Delay of Game if players ignore a signal, or a content foul if the scene becomes too confusing or inappropriate.
- Ensure players balance the absurd sensory work with grounded scene-work and relationship building.

## Variations
- Split Signal: The Referee assigns a different sensory signal to each player simultaneously, forcing them to justify why they are experiencing different realities in the same space.
- Emotional Scramble: Instead of sensory shifts, the audience provides intense, bizarre emotional states that the players must instantly adopt and justify within the scene.

## Why It Works
It forces immediate physical commitment and Yes-And agreement to environmental changes. It balances absurd sensory work with grounded scene-work and relationship building, making it excellent for practicing sensory object work and group mind in a performance setting.

## Safety & Inclusion
Players must avoid dangerous physical choices, such as throwing themselves to the ground if the signal is heavy gravity. Ensure players respect physical boundaries if a sensory shift implies proximity or touching. For accessibility, sensory shifts can be adapted to focus on vocal, emotional, or verbal shifts if physical mobility is limited, ensuring all players can fully participate.

# Narrative Rupture

![Narrative Rupture — infographic](img/ISR300.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A grounded scene instantly shifts genres when a player accidentally triggers a secret action.

## Overview
A scene-work drill where a grounded scene undergoes an instantaneous genre shift, triggered by a secret, pre-determined action or mistake made by a performer. This forces improvisers to instantly re-contextualize their reality and justify the trigger action as the inciting incident of a new world, all while maintaining their core character relationship.

## Setup
Two players on stage. A facilitator and the rest of the workshop/ensemble acting as the audience. A whiteboard or list of genres is helpful but not required.

## How to Play
1. Send the two performing players out of earshot.
2. The facilitator and the observing group agree on a mundane 'Rupture Trigger' (e.g., crossing arms, sighing, picking up a prop, or saying the word 'Well').
3. Bring the players back. Ask the observing group for a mundane location and a grounded relationship (e.g., 'Co-workers in a breakroom').
4. The players begin a grounded, slice-of-life scene, completely unaware of the secret trigger.
5. The observing group watches for the trigger, enjoying the dramatic irony. The moment a player naturally performs the trigger action, the facilitator loudly calls 'RUPTURE!' followed immediately by a drastically different genre (e.g., 'RUPTURE: Space Opera!').
6. Fallback Rule: If the players do not naturally hit the trigger within two minutes, the facilitator artificially forces the rupture at the next optimal narrative moment by calling it anyway, ensuring the drill keeps moving.
7. Upon the call, the players must instantly snap into the new genre. They must justify the exact physical action or word that triggered the rupture as the catalyst for the new world (e.g., the sigh was actually a spacesuit depressurizing, or the crossed arms activated a deflector shield).
8. The players continue the scene for 2-3 minutes. They must fully commit to the new genre's tropes and stakes while keeping their original emotional dynamic and relationship intact.

## Coaching Notes
- The observing ensemble participates by setting the secret trigger, which creates high engagement and dramatic irony as they wait for the players to accidentally step on the landmine.
- Side-coach to ensure the core relationship isn't lost in the genre shift.
- Encourage instant genre commitment and full-body transformation.
- Focus on rapid justification of mistakes or mundane actions.
- Remind players to embrace failure as a narrative gift.

## Variations
- Director's Call: Abandon the secret trigger entirely. The facilitator simply calls 'RUPTURE: [Genre]' at the climax or a moment of high tension in the scene, focusing purely on the genre shift rather than the accidental trigger.
- Genre Gauntlet: The facilitator calls 'RUPTURE' with a new genre every 60 seconds, forcing the players to carry the exact same relationship through 3-4 different realities in rapid succession.

## Why It Works
It forces instant genre commitment and rapid justification of mistakes or mundane actions, training players to maintain relationship dynamics across radical context shifts and embrace failure as a narrative gift.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure 'Rupture Triggers' are accessible and do not require specific physical abilities (e.g., use 'saying a specific word' rather than 'jumping'). When shifting into high-stakes genres (like Action or Horror), players must respect physical boundaries, avoid startling physical contact, and rely on mime and emotional intensity rather than physical aggression. Keep genre suggestions free of harmful stereotypes.

# The Cross-Connection Caper

![The Cross-Connection Caper — infographic](img/ISR263.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams perform separate scenes on a split stage, instantly justifying shared elements thrown at them by a Referee.

## Overview
Two teams perform separate, thematically linked scenes on a split stage, taking turns in the spotlight. A Referee toggles focus between the scenes and periodically yells 'Cross-Connection!' to introduce a shared element—like a prop, a line of dialogue, or a sudden emotion—that both teams must instantly justify in their respective worlds. It is a fast-paced, competitive short-form game that tests adaptability and split-screen comedy.

## Setup
The stage is divided down the middle with an imaginary line. The Red Team (strictly 1-2 players) takes the left side; the Blue Team (strictly 1-2 players) takes the right side. The Referee stands downstage center or off to the side with a whistle or a clear pointing gesture to direct focus.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a 'Common Catalyst' (a broad theme like 'a secret,' 'a misunderstanding,' or 'impending disaster'). This theme will apply to both scenes.
2. The Referee then gets two separate, distinct locations from the audience—one for the Red Team and one for the Blue Team.
3. The Referee starts the clock (typically 3 minutes) and calls 'Red Team, Go!' (or points to them). The Red Team begins their scene. The Blue Team must remain completely frozen and silent.
4. To share focus, the Referee calls 'Blue!' or points to the Blue Team. Red instantly freezes, and Blue springs to life, beginning their scene. The Referee toggles focus back and forth every 15-30 seconds to keep both narratives moving.
5. At unpredictable intervals, the Referee calls 'Cross-Connection!' Both teams immediately freeze.
6. The Referee announces a crossover challenge that both teams must incorporate. Examples include a Verbal Echo ('Someone must say: Is that a banana?'), an Object Crossover ('Red Team, hand your mimed object across the center line to Blue'), or a Shared Sound Effect ('You both hear a loud BOING').
7. The Referee resumes play by calling focus to one team. That team must instantly justify the Cross-Connection. When focus shifts to the other team, they must do the same in their own context.
8. Play continues, toggling focus and adding 3-4 Cross-Connections, until time is called.
9. At the end of the 3-minute round, the Referee asks the audience to applaud for the Red Team's scene, then the Blue Team's scene. The team with the loudest applause (measured by the Referee's applause meter) wins 5 points. The Referee may award 1 Bonus Point for an exceptionally brilliant justification of a Cross-Connection. Standard fouls (like a clean-content foul for inappropriate content) deduct 1 point.

## Coaching Notes
- Enforce the split-focus mechanic strictly, ensuring only one scene is audible and active at a time while the other remains completely frozen.
- Maintain the strict 1-2 player limit per team to minimize stage clutter and cognitive load.
- Referees should aim for 3-4 Cross-Connections per round, providing prompts that force rapid, creative 'Yes, And' justifications.
- Toggle focus frequently (every 15-30 seconds) to keep both narratives moving and maintain high energy.

## Variations
- The Invisible Portal: Instead of abstract prompts, players can physically hand mimed objects across the invisible center line while they have focus, forcing the other team to justify what the object is in their world as soon as focus switches.
- Genre Crossover: The Cross-Connections are sudden genre shifts (e.g., 'Cross-Connection: Western!'). When focus is passed, the active team must play their scene in that genre until the next Cross-Connection is called.

## Why It Works
The game develops adaptability and split-screen comedy by forcing players to rapidly 'Yes, And' unexpected elements into their established narratives. The parallel structure tied together by a single theme highlights the contrast between the two worlds, while the strict freeze mechanic builds discipline in sharing focus.

## Safety & Inclusion
The strict 'freeze' mechanic ensures physical safety by preventing players from colliding across the split stage. The Referee enforces a clean-content foul to immediately penalize and stop any blue humor, swearing, or innuendo, keeping the game family-friendly. Players are instructed to avoid sudden loud noises near each other's ears during 'Shared Sound Effect' prompts.

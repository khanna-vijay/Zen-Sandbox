# Metamorphosis Mashup

![Metamorphosis Mashup — infographic](img/IS109.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive short-form game where players improvise a scene that undergoes constant, absurd physical and narrative transformations.

## Overview
Metamorphosis Mashup is a fast-paced, high-energy competitive short-form game where two teams compete to create an imaginative and cohesive scene despite constant, absurd, and immediate physical transformations. The Referee frequently calls 'Metamorphose!' followed by an audience suggestion (an animal, object, or abstract concept). The players on stage must instantly and physically transform into that entity, continuing the scene's narrative as their new form.

## Setup
3-4 players per team, with one team on stage at a time. Standard open stage, which must be clear and safe for dynamic physical movement. No props are used; everything is mimed. The audience provides the initial scene suggestion (location, relationship, or activity) and the 'Metamorphose!' suggestions.

## How to Play
1. The Referee begins by soliciting a core suggestion from the audience (e.g., location, relationship, activity) to establish the initial context.
2. Players from one team step forward and immediately begin improvising a scene based on the audience suggestions.
3. At unpredictable intervals (generally every 20-45 seconds), the Referee loudly interjects with, 'Metamorphose!' and solicits a specific suggestion from the audience (an animal, an object, or an abstract concept).
4. Upon hearing the suggestion, all players currently on stage must immediately and physically transform into that suggested entity.
5. The scene continues as if this transformation is a natural part of their reality. The characters are now the metamorphosed entities, but their original goals and relationships must persist.
6. Players must verbally justify their new form, incorporating its characteristics into their dialogue and actions, and 'Yes, And' their teammates' metamorphoses.
7. The Referee continues to call 'Metamorphose!' to maintain a rapid pace until calling 'Freeze!' or 'Scene!' after 2-3 minutes.

## Coaching Notes
- Award 5-10 points for seamless transformations, creative justification, strong physicality, active listening, and audience engagement.
- Call a 'Hesitation Foul' (and deduct points) if players take more than 1-2 seconds to initiate their physical transformation.
- Call a content foul if players break character, acknowledge the absurdity directly to the audience, swear, or use innuendo.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' for obvious puns, lazy humor, or a lack of clever integration.
- Call a 'Lazy Metamorphosis Foul' if a player's physical commitment is insufficient, generic, or lacks specificity and imagination.
- Remind players that they are not merely 'playing' the entity; they must become it in every fiber of their being while maintaining their original character's objectives.

## Why It Works
The game forces constant adaptation and prevents stagnation. The comedic value stems from the sheer absurdity of the transformations coupled with the players' unwavering commitment to making it make sense within the scene's established reality, building razor-sharp quick thinking.

## Safety & Inclusion
The playing area must be completely clear of obstacles to allow for dynamic, safe, and often exaggerated physical movement during rapid transformations.

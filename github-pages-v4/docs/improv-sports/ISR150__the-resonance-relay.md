# The Resonance Relay

![The Resonance Relay — infographic](img/ISR150.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass an emotional state or physical sensation down a line like a baton, letting it naturally transform with each player.

## Overview
A non-competitive, facilitator-led ensemble warm-up where players pass an emotional state or physical sensation down a line like a baton. As each player receives the 'resonance,' they embody it, let it naturally transform, and pass it to the next person. It builds deep non-verbal connection, active listening, and physical commitment while getting players out of their heads.

## Setup
Divide the group into lines of 4 to 6 players. Players stand single-file, facing the back of the person in front of them. Leave enough space between players for free movement.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator gives the first player in each line a 'Resonance Seed'—an emotion (e.g., Joy, Panic), a physical sensation (e.g., Freezing, Itchy), or an environmental quality (e.g., Walking through thick mud).
2. The first player takes a few seconds to fully embody this seed physically and energetically, without using words.
3. When ready, they tap the shoulder of the second player (or say 'turn' if playing no-touch), and the second player turns to receive.
4. The first player 'passes' the resonance using a clear, non-verbal action (sustained eye contact, a specific gesture, a shared breath, or a sound).
5. The second player actively receives the resonance, mirroring and absorbing the physical and emotional state of the first player.
6. The second player then turns away, allowing the resonance to subtly transform within them. They don't just copy it; they let it evolve (e.g., 'Panic' might morph into 'Frantic Energy' or 'Paranoia').
7. They signal the third player and pass this newly transformed state. The relay continues down the line at a brisk, fluid pace.
8. Once the final player receives and embodies the resonance, they strike a final pose, signaling the end of the relay.

## Coaching Notes
- Keep a brisk pace to prevent overthinking—encourage players to trust their first physical impulse.
- Remind players that they are not just copying the previous person; the magic happens when they let the resonance organically evolve within their own body.
- If played in front of an observing class, point out the organic, often hilarious or profound evolution of a single feeling as it travels down the line.
- Focus on getting players out of their heads and into their bodies. This requires zero props or prior acting experience, just physical responsiveness.

## Variations
- Scene Launch: Bridge the gap between exercise and performance. When the final player receives the resonance, they immediately step forward with the second-to-last player and begin a spoken, two-person scene grounded entirely in that final emotional state.
- The Sound Machine: Players must include one specific, non-verbal sound (a hum, a click, a sigh) with their pass. The next player must receive that exact sound and let it morph alongside their physical transformation.

## Why It Works
It forces active listening and physical responsiveness without the pressure of words. By relying on brisk pacing and physical embodiment, it gets players out of their heads and cultivates deep ensemble connection and non-verbal communication.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical touch is strictly prohibited during the 'pass' unless explicit, prior consent has been established among all team members. Players should rely on eye contact, energetic proximity, and gesture to transmit the resonance. Facilitators should encourage players to interpret physical sensations in ways that accommodate their own mobility levels.

# The Inner Voice Loudspeaker

![The Inner Voice Loudspeaker — infographic](img/IS095.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players briefly pause their scene to deliver a rapid-fire, direct-address monologue to the audience, revealing their character's secret thoughts.

## Overview
The Inner Voice Loudspeaker is a dynamic competitive short-form game where players performing a scene are prompted, either by the Referee or their own initiative, to briefly pause and step out of character. During this pause, they deliver a direct-address monologue to the audience, revealing their character's secret thoughts, meta-commentary, or witty observations, before seamlessly snapping back into the scene. This mechanic transforms internal monologues into outward comedic bursts, requiring sharp wit and seamless transitions.

## Setup
4-6 players form two teams (Red and Blue). Two players from opposing teams are on stage at a time, performing a scene, while others wait offstage. All props are mimed. The stage is a standard competitive short-form configuration. The audience provides an initial scene suggestion and acts as confidantes for the asides.

## How to Play
1. 1. The Referee obtains a single scene suggestion from the audience (e.g., location, relationship, shared problem).
2. 2. Two players (one from each team) begin the scene.
3. 3. At any point, the Referee can shout, 'LOUDSPEAKER!' The player currently speaking or acting must immediately freeze their scene partner and their own body.
4. 4. The player steps forward slightly, addresses the audience directly, and delivers a brief (5-10 second) 'Inner Voice' monologue (a secret thought, plea, aside about the partner, or meta-commentary).
5. 5. Alternatively, a player may initiate a 'Loudspeaker' moment themselves by making clear eye contact with the audience and breaking their scene's physical framing. Their scene partner must freeze immediately.
6. 6. During the aside, players do not engage in direct conversation with the audience; it is a monologue to them, not with them.
7. 7. After the monologue, the player immediately snaps back into the scene, picking up exactly where they left off. The scene partner resumes their action/dialogue from their frozen state.
8. 8. Players continue to maintain a coherent scene narrative and work towards a comedic outcome.
9. 9. After 2-3 minutes, the Referee calls 'FREEZE!' and tags in new players, potentially continuing the same scene or starting a new one.

## Coaching Notes
- Enforce the strict 5-10 second limit for each 'Loudspeaker' to maintain fast pacing and high energy.
- Award points for: Witty Loudspeaker (strong laugh/clever), Insightful Voice (deepens character/meta-commentary), Seamless Return (smooth transition back), Immaculate Freeze (partner holds freeze), and Initiator's Genius (bonus for self-initiating a successful aside).
- Deduct points for: Groaner Foul (terrible pun), Disconnected Dialogue Foul (irrelevant/confusing aside), Long-Winded Loudspeaker Foul (over 10 seconds), and Breaking Freeze Foul (partner moves during aside).
- Remind players to 'Yes, And' their scene partners before and after their asides, seamlessly integrating back into the established reality.
- The act of freezing, stepping out, and snapping back requires precise physical control and stage awareness.

## Why It Works
The game transforms internal thoughts into external humor, giving the audience a privileged peek into the characters' minds. It pushes players to be exceptionally quick-witted, physically precise, and deeply connected to both their scene and their spectators, acting as a masterclass in revealing character depth and hidden motivations.

## Safety & Inclusion
Maintain a family-friendly environment. Enforce the standard clean-content foul: any inappropriate content (blue humor, swearing, innuendo) results in a 1-point deduction and a stern warning.

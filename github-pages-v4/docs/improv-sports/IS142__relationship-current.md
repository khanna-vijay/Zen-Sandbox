# Relationship Current

![Relationship Current — infographic](img/IS142.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A circle pass-and-catch game where players embody and exchange relational dynamics through an imaginary ball.

## Overview
Relationship Current adapts a simple circle pass-and-catch into a dynamic exercise where players embody and exchange relational dynamics. When passing an imaginary ball, the thrower physically manifests a clear attitude or relationship towards the receiver. The receiver instinctively reacts to this incoming current and transforms it into a new relational attitude before passing it on.

## Setup
Players stand in a large circle. A facilitator starts with an imaginary ball (or a real, soft beanbag/ball for initial focus, to be removed later).

## How to Play
1. The facilitator explains that the 'ball' is not just an object; it carries an invisible thread of relationship.
2. The first player receives the imaginary ball and chooses another player in the circle to 'throw' it to.
3. As the player 'throws' the ball, they must imbue the entire action of the throw (their gaze, posture, energy, and the imaginary trajectory/weight of the ball) with a single, clear, and felt attitude or relationship towards the receiver (e.g., angrily, lovingly, hesitantly, seductively).
4. The receiving player must immediately physically and vocally react to the incoming 'relationship' conveyed by the pass. This reaction should be an authentic, intuitive response to what was just offered.
5. Once the player has received and reacted, they then transform that relationship into a new or modified one.
6. They immediately throw the ball to a different player in the circle, again imbuing their throw with this new relationship.
7. The game continues with the 'current' of relationships flowing and transforming between players. There are no breaks unless the relationship becomes unclear or drops.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Let the relationship you hold for the receiver entirely shape the trajectory, weight, and timing of your throw, and let the incoming relationship entirely shape your reception.
- Observe, encourage, and offer non-judgmental prompts if a player becomes intellectual or unclear.
- Use prompts like: 'What's the relationship now?', 'What's happening between you and them?', or 'Trust your first impulse.'
- Prioritize clear, felt impulse over 'good acting'.
- Maintain a fast pace to prevent overthinking and foster spontaneous, visceral responses.

## Why It Works
It develops emotional responsiveness and spontaneity by forcing players to react intuitively. It trains the physical embodiment of intangible relationships, active listening to non-verbal cues, and ensemble building. By prioritizing intuition over intellect, it embodies a philosophy of presence and play-based discovery.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during fast or intense throws, even with an imaginary ball. Encourage players to respect physical boundaries and emotional limits when projecting intense relationships like anger or fear, maintaining a safe play space.

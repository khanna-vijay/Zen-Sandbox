# Resonant Echoes

![Resonant Echoes — infographic](img/ISR183.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A sensory ensemble warm-up where a blindfolded Seeker navigates the space using only sound and physical intuition.

## Overview
A sensory ensemble warm-up where a 'Seeker' with closed eyes navigates the space using only sound and physical intuition, guided by the group's vocal 'echoes.' It builds deep listening, ensemble trust, and gets players out of their heads and into their bodies.

## Setup
Clear the room of all chairs, bags, and obstacles. The group scatters around the room and plants their feet. One player volunteers to be the 'Seeker' and closes their eyes (or uses a blindfold). Crucially, assign 1-2 players to be 'Spotters'—their only job is to physically shadow the Seeker and gently redirect them to prevent collisions.

## How to Play
1. 1. The Call: The Seeker, with eyes closed, emits a non-verbal sound (e.g., a sustained hum, a sigh, a sharp vowel) and begins moving slowly through the space.
2. 2. The Echo: The stationary players (Responders) immediately echo back a sound inspired by the Seeker's call, acting as a collective sonic beacon.
3. 3. The Navigation: The Seeker moves toward the sounds, focusing on the physical pull and texture of the audio rather than intellectually mapping the room.
4. 4. The Approach: As the Seeker gets closer to a specific Responder, that Responder slightly increases the intensity or shifts the texture of their sound to signal proximity.
5. 5. The Connection (No-Touch Tag): When the Seeker feels they are directly in front of a Responder, they stop, point forward, and say 'Here.' If correct, the Responder says 'Yes.'
6. 6. The Reset: The found Responder becomes the new Seeker, the old Seeker plants their feet, and the game continues with a new call.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach the Point of Concentration (POC): 'Listen with your whole body.'
- 'Let the sound move you, do not plan your steps.'
- 'Responders, let your echo be an immediate impulse, not a planned noise.'

## Variations
- Echo to Scene (Transition to Scene Work): Once the Seeker finds the Responder, both open their eyes. Whatever physical posture and sound they are currently making instantly becomes the emotional endowment and first line of dialogue for a 2-person scene.
- Emotional Echoes: The Seeker's call must convey a specific emotion (e.g., grief, joy, fear). The Responders must echo back a complementary or contrasting emotion, guiding the Seeker through a shifting emotional landscape.

## Why It Works
It provides a strong, explicit Point of Concentration (POC), forcing players out of their heads and into their bodies. It builds ensemble trust, deep whole-body listening, and focuses on non-verbal communication and physical intuition.

## Safety & Inclusion
Safety is paramount: 1-2 dedicated Spotters must shadow the Seeker at all times with hands ready to 'bumper' them away from walls or other people. To respect physical boundaries and consent, the game uses a 'no-touch' tagging system (stopping and pointing) rather than groping. Players with mobility limitations can fully participate as Responders, or as a Seeker with a Spotter offering a guiding elbow.

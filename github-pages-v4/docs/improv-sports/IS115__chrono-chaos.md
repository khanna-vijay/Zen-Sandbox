# Chrono-Chaos

![Chrono-Chaos — infographic](img/IS115.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Construct a single, coherent story by dynamically jumping between past, present, and future while organically integrating unrelated audience plot points.

## Overview
Chrono-Chaos is an improv game where players, guided by a Host, collaboratively construct a single, coherent, and often hilarious story by dynamically jumping between past, present, and future. The Host triggers 'FLASHBACK!' or 'FAST FORWARD!' commands, forcing improvisers to instantly adapt their characters and situations to new points in the timeline. Meanwhile, players must organically integrate unrelated 'plot point' suggestions provided by the audience at the start, earning points for clever integration and seamless time warp transitions.

## Setup
3-5 improvisers on a clear stage, optionally with a designated 'reset line' or zone. A Host/Referee is essential to manage the game, call Time Warps, and award points. You will need a whiteboard or large cue cards clearly visible to players and the audience to list the audience-suggested Plot Points.

## How to Play
1. The Host addresses the audience and asks for three distinct, unrelated, and ideally quirky 'Plot Points' or 'Key Events' (e.g., 'A dog learns to play the ukulele'), writing them clearly on the whiteboard.
2. The improvisers quickly establish an opening scene, creating 2-3 characters, their relationship, and a basic 'present day' scenario.
3. The Host signals the start of the game by announcing 'Chrono-Chaos begins!'
4. At unpredictable intervals, the Host yells 'FLASHBACK!' to immediately shift the scene to an earlier point in time, or 'FAST FORWARD!' to jump to a later point in time.
5. Upon a time warp command, all players on stage must immediately adapt physically and verbally, establishing the new temporal context and revealing relevant information or consequences.
6. Players earn points by organically incorporating one of the audience-suggested plot points into the ongoing scene.
7. Once a plot point is successfully integrated, the player who initiated it declares, 'PLOT POINT [NUMBER/NAME]!' The Host verifies the integration and crosses it off the board.
8. The Host awards points in a competitive short-form match style: +1 for a Seamless Time Warp, +2 for Clever Plot Point Integration, +1 for Narrative Cohesion, and -1 for a 'Chrono-Foul'.
9. The game continues until all three plot points have been integrated into the narrative, or the Host calls 'Time!' after 5-7 minutes.

## Coaching Notes
- The last line spoken or the action being performed before a time warp command should ideally feel like a catalyst for the time jump.
- Ensure the new scene quickly establishes its new temporal context.
- Plot point integration should be surprising, humorous, or dramatically satisfying, making the seemingly disparate event feel earned within the unfolding story.
- Watch out for 'Chrono-Fouls': penalize players for failing to respond immediately to a command, ignoring previous scene context, contradicting established facts (unless justified by something like memory loss), breaking character, or forcing a plot point in clumsily.

## Why It Works
It challenges improvisers to be masters of narrative continuity across discontinuous timelines, creating a wild, unpredictable, yet surprisingly cohesive story.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players maintain physical awareness and safety during rapid, instantaneous transitions between time periods. Establish boundaries for physical choices when portraying much older or younger versions of characters to avoid injury.

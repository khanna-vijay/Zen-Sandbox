# Retcon Roulette

![Retcon Roulette — infographic](img/IS260.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Improvisers must seamlessly integrate unpredictable, game-changing retroactive continuity shifts thrown at them by a Truth Weaver.

## Overview
Retcon Roulette is an improvisational game where two performers collaboratively build a scene based on an audience suggestion. At unpredictable intervals, a Truth Weaver introduces a 'retcon'—a new, often contradictory, immutable truth about the scene or characters. The performers must immediately accept this new truth and seamlessly integrate it as if it has always been part of their reality, retroactively justifying previous actions and dialogue without breaking character.

## Setup
Two performers are on stage. A third person, the Truth Weaver (a judge or host), sits at a small table stage-side with a physical 'Retcon Device' (like a spinning wheel, deck of cards, or button device). Get an audience suggestion (e.g., relationship, location, simple conflict) to begin.

## How to Play
1. The two performers begin an ordinary scene based on the audience suggestion, establishing basic facts, relationships, and the environment.
2. At unpredictable intervals (e.g., every 30-45 seconds, or on a distinct auditory cue), the Truth Weaver activates the Retcon Device.
3. The Truth Weaver clearly and concisely announces a 'retcon'—a new, immutable truth about the scene, its characters, or its history.
4. Upon hearing the retcon, both performers must immediately accept it as absolute, indisputable truth.
5. Performers integrate the retcon into the scene as if it has always been true, without acknowledging it as a 'retcon' or questioning its veracity in character.
6. Performers must retroactively justify previous dialogue, actions, or emotional states in light of this new truth, reinterpreting glances or reframing lines to make sense of the new reality.
7. The scene continues, flowing organically with the newly established reality, until the next retcon, or until the host calls 'Scene'.

## Coaching Notes
- Immediate Acceptance: No hesitation, no argument. The retcon is true.
- No Fourth Wall Break: Do not acknowledge the 'game' or the 'Truth Weaver' in character. Maintain the scene's reality at all costs.
- Active Justification: Don't just ignore the contradiction; actively find an in-character explanation or re-contextualization for how the new truth fits into what just happened.
- Collaborative Integration: Both performers are responsible for helping each other integrate retcons. An integration from one player might offer a path for the other to follow.
- Maintain Character Core: While external facts change, strive to maintain an underlying truth to your character's reaction and internal consistency where possible.

## Why It Works
This game is a furnace for narrative development that elevates disruption to a fundamental building block. It pushes 'Yes, And' into 'Yes, And, This Changes Everything,' forcing characters to be constantly and profoundly changed. It tests advanced levels of acceptance and justification, as performers must demonstrate why the new truth makes sense given everything that has transpired.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Truth Weaver should ensure that retcons remain fun and challenging without crossing the performers' personal boundaries or forcing unsafe physical or emotional situations.

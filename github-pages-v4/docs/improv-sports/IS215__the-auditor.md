# The Auditor

![The Auditor — infographic](img/IS215.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A designated Auditor vigilantly watches for logical inconsistencies, freezing the scene to challenge players to hilariously justify their narrative missteps.

## Overview
The Auditor is an improvisational game where players collaboratively build a cohesive scene, but with a unique twist: one designated 'Auditor' player vigilantly watches for any logical inconsistencies or narrative flaws introduced by the others. Upon spotting a contradiction, the Auditor immediately interjects, freezing the scene and challenging the offending player to hilariously and ingeniously justify their narrative misstep in character. This dynamic turns potential narrative flaws into comedic opportunities.

## Setup
Standard improv stage. You need 3-4 'Scene Players' and 1 designated 'Auditor'. A notepad and pen for the Auditor can add theatricality. The audience provides the initial suggestion(s) for the scene (e.g., location, unusual relationship, first line of dialogue). A referee or scorekeeper manages points in a competitive short-form match style.

## How to Play
1. The audience provides a suggestion to initiate the scene.
2. Two Scene Players enter and begin the scene, establishing characters, relationship, and initial environment based on the suggestion.
3. The Auditor quietly takes their position in a corner of the stage or off to the side, observing intently.
4. As the scene progresses, the Auditor meticulously observes all offers, actions, and dialogue for any logical inconsistency, break in continuity, defiance of physics, character contradiction, or violation of established scene rules.
5. When the Auditor identifies a significant inconsistency, they immediately interject, shouting 'AUDIT!' and pointing directly at the player whose action or line created the inconsistency. The scene immediately freezes.
6. The Auditor clearly and succinctly states the identified inconsistency, referencing the specific action or line.
7. The accused player(s) must now, in character, immediately improvise a plausible and ideally comedic justification for the perceived logical flaw. They cannot deny the inconsistency; they must accept its premise and ingeniously explain it away.
8. After the justification, the Auditor (or referee, possibly with audience cheers/boos) rules on its success. If clever and sufficient, they declare 'AUDIT CLEARED!' and the scene unfreezes and continues. If weak, nonsensical, or denying the premise, they declare 'AUDIT FAILED!' and the scene stops for a potential penalty.
9. The scene continues with new players potentially tagging in, but the Auditor remains vigilant. The game runs for a set time (e.g., 3-5 minutes) or until the scene reaches a natural comedic conclusion.

## Coaching Notes
- The Auditor should look for contradictory statements, impossible physics, character breaks, environmental inconsistencies, and unexplained events.
- Other Scene Players may, if appropriate and quick, subtly assist in the justification, but the primary responsibility lies with the accused.
- Competitive Scoring: If the Auditor identifies a clear, compelling inconsistency, their team may earn 1 point. The Scene Players' team earns 1 point for a successful and comedic justification. The Auditor's team earns 1 point (or the Scene Players lose 1 point) for catching an unfixable inconsistency.
- Penalties for a failed audit can include the offending player having to switch characters, perform with a physical handicap for 30 seconds, or their team losing a point.

## Why It Works
It turns potential narrative flaws into comedic opportunities, compelling players to 'Yes, And' not just each other's offers, but also their own accidental breaches of logic, all under the scrutinizing gaze of the Auditor.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the Auditor's interjections remain playful and focused on narrative logic rather than personal attacks. If using physical handicap penalties for failed audits, ensure they are safe, respectful, and do not mock actual disabilities.

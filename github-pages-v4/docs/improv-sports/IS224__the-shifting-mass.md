# The Shifting Mass

![The Shifting Mass — infographic](img/IS224.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An ensemble collaboratively passes an invisible object whose perceived weight continuously and spontaneously changes.

## Overview
The Shifting Mass is a theater game where an ensemble collaboratively passes an invisible space object whose perceived weight continuously and spontaneously changes. Any player can non-verbally alter the mass's heaviness or lightness, and all others must instantaneously and physically adapt to this new reality, ensuring continuous motion and collective embodiment.

## Setup
Players gather in an open space, forming a flexible, roughly circular configuration that allows for easy eye contact and movement within the group. There are no physical props; all interactions are with a space object. One player (the Initiator) begins by demonstrating through their posture, hands, and full body a carefully imagined, invisible mass roughly the size of a softball cradled or held between their hands. The Initiator imbues this mass with an initial, specific perceived weight (e.g., very light, moderately heavy, surprisingly dense), making this weight clear through their physical effort and somatic experience.

## How to Play
1. The mass is passed from player to player, always maintaining continuous, careful (invisible) physical contact and motion. It should never be allowed to rest or be stationary.
2. At any point, the player currently holding the mass, or any observing player, can unilaterally and non-verbally initiate a subtle, spontaneous change in the mass's perceived weight.
3. If the mass feels heavier to the current holder, they immediately embody this through increased physical strain, slower movements, a lower trajectory, or a greater need to brace themselves.
4. If it feels lighter, they show less strain, allow for higher, more buoyant movements, or a quicker transition.
5. All other players in the ensemble must instantaneously and without verbal discussion acknowledge, accept, and physically attune themselves to this newly perceived weight.
6. A receiving player must be prepared to react to whatever weight is currently being offered, even if it has just shifted. An observing player might also subtly reinforce the new weight through sympathetic physical responses (e.g., lowering their center of gravity in anticipation of a heavier mass approaching them).
7. The collective goal is to keep the mass in continuous motion and contained within the ensemble, ensuring its ever-changing weight is collectively understood and embodied.
8. If a player fails to accurately perceive and respond to a weight shift, causing the mass to lose its collective physical consensus (e.g., it crashing due to a lack of support for its heaviness, or floating away due to an overreaction to lightness), the mass is considered dropped.
9. A drop is not a failure but a natural endpoint for that iteration. The mass dissolves, and a new Initiator begins a fresh mass with a new starting weight.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): The continuously and spontaneously shifting perceived weight of the invisible mass. This requires players to bypass intellectual anticipation or negotiation and instead engage in immediate, intuitive kinesthetic sensing.
- Are you really feeling that weight, or just acting heavy?
- How does its sudden lightness affect your whole body?
- Listen with your muscles; how dense is it right now?
- Is the entire group breathing with its current quality?
- Don't predict the weight; receive it in this present moment.
- What agreement does the group have on its trajectory and speed given its current mass?
- Can you feel the gravitational pull on it, here and now?

## Why It Works
Players develop intuitive kinesthetic empathy and responsiveness, cultivating a deep, non-intellectual following the follower at a somatic level. The game demands constant presence, acute spatial awareness, and the ability to instantly adapt one's physical commitment to a dynamically changing, collectively imagined object, fostering strong ensemble cohesion and a shared flow state.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing space is clear of physical obstacles so players can move freely while focused on the space object. Remind players to respect their own physical limits and avoid actual physical strain when embodying extreme heaviness.

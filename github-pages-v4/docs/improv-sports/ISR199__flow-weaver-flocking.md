# Flow Weaver (Flocking)

![Flow Weaver (Flocking) — infographic](img/ISR199.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A dynamic movement exercise where players mirror a single leader and fluidly pass leadership through subtle proposals.

## Overview
A dynamic variation of the classic movement exercise 'Flocking.' Players move as a unified group, mirroring a single leader's physical gestures and sounds. The twist is fluid leadership: any follower can subtly propose a new movement, and if the current leader adopts it, leadership instantly passes to the proposer.

## Setup
Players stand together in a loose cluster in an open space. No props or predetermined leaders are needed.

## How to Play
1. The Initial Offer: One player spontaneously starts a simple, repeatable physical gesture and sound (e.g., a rhythmic stomp with a 'chug' sound).
2. The Mirror: Everyone else immediately copies the gesture and sound as precisely as possible. The initiator is now the 'Weaver' (the leader).
3. The Flow: The Weaver slowly moves around the space, continuing their gesture and sound. The group follows, maintaining the mimicry.
4. Proposing a Shift: At any point, any follower can introduce a subtle variation to the movement or sound (e.g., changing the stomp to a slide).
5. Passing the Lead: If the current Weaver notices the variation and adopts it, leadership instantly passes to the player who proposed it. The old Weaver becomes a follower, and the entire group now follows the new Weaver. If the Weaver ignores the proposal, the proposer simply goes back to following.
6. The Group Freeze: At any moment, any player can suddenly freeze in place and make a 'shh' sound. The goal is for the entire group to notice and freeze instantly. Once the room is completely still for 3 seconds, the person who initiated the freeze starts a brand new movement, becoming the new Weaver.

## Coaching Notes
- Remind players that this is non-competitive; there are no points or judges.
- Strive for a seamless, hive-mind state where leadership passes so smoothly that an outside observer wouldn't know who is leading.
- Encourage fluid, emergent leadership without any verbal negotiation.
- Maintain a strong focus on physical mirroring and vocal listening.

## Variations
- Silent Flocking: Remove the vocal component to focus entirely on physical awareness and peripheral vision.
- Emotion Flocking: Instead of just gestures, the Weaver leads with a specific emotion (e.g., joyful skipping, angry stomping), and the followers must mirror the emotional intensity.
- Grid Flocking: Players stand in a diamond or grid shape. Whoever is at the front of the group's current facing direction is the automatic leader. When the leader turns 90 or 180 degrees, the person now at the front instantly takes over.

## Why It Works
It builds group mind, peripheral awareness, and ensemble trust by requiring intense physical mirroring and vocal listening.

## Safety & Inclusion
Encourage players to move at their own pace and avoid physical contact. Remind players to offer movements that are accessible to everyone in the room (e.g., avoid high jumps or floor work if someone has mobility limitations). Keep eyes open and be aware of the physical space to avoid collisions.

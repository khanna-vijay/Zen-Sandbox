# Mood Maestro

![Mood Maestro — infographic](img/ISR194.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Instantly embody a randomly assigned emotion to respond to a neutral call and drive a spontaneous micro-scene.

## Overview
A facilitator-led ensemble game where players practice instantaneous emotional commitment and physical embodiment. Players pass a neutral call around a circle, and the receiver must instantly embody a randomly assigned emotion to respond, letting the feeling dictate the progression of a short scene.

## Setup
Players form a single circle facing inward. The facilitator stands just outside or on the edge of the circle. No props or stage setup are required.

## How to Play
1. Player A turns to Player B (the person to their left) and delivers a neutral Call such as a mundane spoken sentence, a physical gesture, or a gibberish phrase.
2. The Facilitator (or the rest of the ensemble acting as the Maestro) immediately shouts out an emotional state or physical quality.
3. Player B must instantly adopt that emotion, letting it wash over their entire body and voice, and respond to Player A's call without overthinking the plot.
4. Player A and Player B play out a short, fast-paced micro-scene of about 3 to 4 lines of dialogue or interaction. Player B's assigned emotion must dictate the stakes and progression, while Player A reacts naturally.
5. The Facilitator calls 'Scene!' or 'Pass!' to end the interaction once the emotional dynamic has been clearly established.
6. Player B now becomes the new Caller, turning to Player C to deliver a brand new neutral Call.
7. The cycle continues around the circle until everyone has played both roles.

## Coaching Notes
- Actively side-coach and shout out the emotional prompts if acting as the Maestro.
- Ensure players let the emotion wash over their entire body and voice, not just their dialogue.
- Encourage players to avoid intellectualizing or plot-planning; the emotion and relationship should drive the scene.
- Use physical or gibberish calls if players are getting too stuck in their heads.

## Variations
- Gibberish Only: All calls and responses are performed entirely in gibberish, forcing players to rely 100% on tone, volume, facial expressions, and physicality.
- Emotion Escalation (Advanced): The scene continues for a full minute. Every 15 seconds, the facilitator shouts a new emotion that Player B must seamlessly transition into, justifying the sudden emotional swing.

## Why It Works
It isolates emotional commitment and physical embodiment as the primary point of concentration, training players to let emotion and relationship drive scene progression rather than relying on witty dialogue.

## Safety & Inclusion
Emotions can run high; remind players to maintain physical boundaries and never use physical aggression. Players can opt out of any triggering emotion by raising a hand for a new prompt. Fully accessible to all mobility levels, as physical embodiment can be expressed through facial expressions, posture, and vocal tone.

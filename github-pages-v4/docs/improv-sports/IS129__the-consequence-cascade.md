# The Consequence Cascade

![The Consequence Cascade — infographic](img/IS129.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Every significant character action or perceived mistake triggers an immediate, surprising external consequence that players must instantly integrate.

## Overview
The Consequence Cascade is an improvisational game where three performers collaboratively build a dynamic narrative, guided by a Narrator of Fate. Every significant character action or perceived mistake triggers an immediate, often surprising, external consequence delivered by the Narrator. Performers must instantly 'Yes, And' these consequences, seamlessly integrating them into the scene and allowing them to unpredictably escalate the plot.

## Setup
You need 3 performers and 1 Narrator of Fate (NofF) who observes from off-stage. Get audience suggestions for a unique location, a core relationship/conflict between the characters, and a single, mundane object present in the scene.

## How to Play
1. Opening Scene (2 minutes): The three performers establish the scene based on the audience suggestions, introducing their characters, relationship, and setting.
2. The Cascade Trigger: The Narrator of Fate watches intently for a 'Consequence' trigger, which happens during a significant character action (a decisive choice that advances the plot) or a perceived 'mistake' (hesitation, tripping, awkward silence).
3. The Consequence Delivery: When a trigger occurs, the NofF calls out 'CONSEQUENCE!' and delivers a new narrative element or constraint (e.g., environmental shift, object transformation, character trait change, or relationship twist).
4. Integration and Escalation: Performers must immediately 'Yes, And' the consequence, justifying its presence and integrating it into the narrative without ignoring or questioning it.
5. Scene End: The scene continues until the NofF calls 'Scene!' after a particularly impactful consequence has been successfully integrated, usually around the 6-8 minute mark.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: Treat each consequence as pure narrative pressure and a forced plot point to prevent stagnation.
- Embracing Mistakes: The NofF should interpret stumbles or awkward silences not as failures, but as opportunities for narrative redirection.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: Performers must intensely listen and agree, not only with each other but with the external consequences, to build a shared reality.
- Being Changed: Encourage players to fully embody radical shifts in their environment, abilities, and relationships.
- Object Work/Environment: Since consequences often alter physical reality, demand strong, specific object work and environmental awareness from the players.
- Status Play: Use consequences to explicitly alter character status, forcing performers to adjust their power dynamics on the fly.

## Why It Works
The game applies pure narrative pressure, transforming mistakes into narrative gold and fostering a fearless approach to improvisation. Every consequence acts as a massive 'Yes, And' exercise, challenging performers to find the logic within the illogical and weave disparate elements into a single, coherent, and escalating narrative thread.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during chaotic environmental shifts or object transformations. Players should respect physical boundaries and use consent-based choices when consequences alter relationships or character status.

# Resonance Web

![Resonance Web — infographic](img/ISR189.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass a repetitive sound and motion around a circle, transforming its expression while keeping its core essence.

## Overview
A focus and ensemble-building warm-up where players pass a repetitive sound and motion around a circle. Instead of copying the action exactly like in a standard pass-the-clap game, each player transforms the action by keeping its core 'essence' (like tempo, volume, or emotional tone) but changing the physical or vocal expression.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle where everyone can clearly see and hear each other. No props are needed. A facilitator stands outside the circle to side-coach.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator asks Player A to start a simple, repetitive physical gesture and sound (e.g., stomping a foot rhythmically while humming).
2. Player B (to their right) observes Player A, then takes the 'essence' of that action and transforms it into something new. For example, if Player A's essence is 'heavy and slow,' Player B might slowly clap their hands while groaning.
3. Player C then observes Player B, takes the essence of Player B's new action, and transforms it again.
4. A strict stopping rule applies: A player continues their repetitive motion only until the person next to them has successfully passed it on. For example, Player A stops moving the exact moment Player C starts. This ensures only two adjacent players are ever moving at the same time.
5. The pattern continues around the circle until it returns to Player A, or until the facilitator calls 'Freeze' to end the exercise.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach to keep the energy moving, using prompts like 'Keep the rhythm, change the body part!' or 'Don't plan it, just react!'
- Enforce the strict stopping rule to prevent chaos and maintain focus.
- Provide concrete examples of keeping the 'essence' for literal-minded players: match the tempo but change the body part (fast blinking becomes fast finger tapping), match the volume but change the sound (a loud 'ha!' becomes a loud 'boom!'), or match the emotional tone but change the action (anxious pacing becomes anxious nail-biting).

## Variations
- Sound Only (Eyes Closed): Players close their eyes and pass only vocal sounds, focusing purely on auditory essence like pitch, rhythm, and volume.
- Speed Web: The facilitator pushes the group to pass the energy as fast as possible, forcing players to rely entirely on gut instinct rather than planning their transformation.
- Cross-Circle Web: Instead of passing to the right, players make eye contact and pass the energy to anyone across the circle, requiring heightened group awareness.

## Why It Works
It isolates non-verbal listening and energetic transfer beautifully. It forces players out of their heads by demanding immediate, intuitive physical responses, creating a safe, low-pressure environment for beginners to practice spontaneity.

## Safety & Inclusion
Highly accessible. Players can remain seated throughout the exercise. Because players choose their own motions, they can easily accommodate their own physical mobility limits. No physical contact is required, making it a safe space for all comfort levels.

# Echo Chamber

![Echo Chamber — infographic](img/IS132.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams competitively reinterpret an audience-suggested scene, with the second team replicating the first under a dramatic, Referee-imposed Lens.

## Overview
Echo Chamber is a fast-paced, competitive game where two teams engage in a creative dialogue with a preceding scene. One team performs an initial scene based on an audience suggestion. The opposing team then immediately performs the same scene, recreating its core narrative and character relationships, but under a dramatic, referee-imposed Lens that twists the reality of the scene.

## Setup
Typically 4-6 improvisers are involved, with 2-3 players from each team (Red and Blue) on stage. Remaining players are on the sidelines ready to rotate in. All props are mimed. Use a standard competitive short-form stage setup with performing teams facing the audience. A Referee officiates, calls fouls, and awards points. A Scorekeeper tracks points. The audience provides the initial scene suggestion and may offer categories for the Lens.

## How to Play
1. The Referee takes a single, simple suggestion from the audience (e.g., an unusual location, an everyday object, a mundane activity).
2. Team Red (the Original Scene Team) performs a 90-second scene based on this suggestion, focusing on clear initiation, strong character choices, active listening, and effective object work.
3. Immediately after Team Red's scene concludes, the Referee steps forward and announces a 'Lens' (a specific, often absurd constraint or filter) to be applied to the same scene by the opposing team.
4. Team Blue (the Echo Team) steps onto the stage and performs the exact same scene as Team Red for 90 seconds, aiming to hit the same general plot points, character dynamics, and setting, but filtered entirely through the announced Lens.
5. The Referee awards points after the Echo Pair concludes: up to 4 points for the Original Scene (initiation, object work, narrative clarity, character choices) and up to 6 points for the Echo Scene (Yes, And-ing the original, Lens integration, quick adaptation, comedic effect).
6. The Referee calls fouls if necessary, applying standard competitive improv fouls (clean-content call, bad pun foul) or game-specific fouls (No Echo, Lens Blur, Mime Mimic).
7. For the next round, the roles switch: Team Blue becomes the Original Scene Team, and Team Red becomes the Echo Team, starting with a new audience suggestion.

## Coaching Notes
- The Echo Team's goal is creative reinterpretation, not mere mimicry. They must creatively adapt the original content.
- The Referee should choose Lenses quickly for comedic potential and challenge (e.g., 'Everyone is secretly a villain', 'It's actually an opera', 'Moving in slow motion').
- Call a 'No Echo' foul (-2 points) if the Echo Team fails to clearly acknowledge or replicate major narrative beats, character relationships, or the core setting established by the Original Scene Team.
- Call a 'Lens Blur' foul (-1 point) if the Lens is not consistently applied throughout the scene, is forgotten, or if the scene deviates significantly from the implications of the Lens.
- Call a 'Mime Mimic' foul (-1 point) if the Echo Team directly copies the exact lines or physical actions without adding their own interpretation or adapting it to the Lens.
- Players must make instant, committed choices; the 90-second limit means there is no time for hesitation.

## Why It Works
It demands quick wit, creative reinterpretation, and fundamental improv skill mastery within a clearly structured framework. It promotes 'Yes, And...' by forcing the Echo Team to accept the original scene's offers and build upon them with the Lens, while also developing active listening, object work, dynamic pacing, and strong physical and character choices.

## Safety & Inclusion
The game is designed to be family-friendly. The humor stems entirely from creative interpretation, character work, and the clever integration of the Lens, ensuring content remains appropriate for all ages. The Referee moderates audience suggestions to maintain this standard, and a clean-content foul (-3 points) is strictly applied for any blue humor, swearing, or inappropriate innuendo.

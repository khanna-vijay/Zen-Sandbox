# The Flux Foundry

![The Flux Foundry — infographic](img/ISR270.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced scene where players must instantly integrate bizarre 'Flux Endowments' and justify a final contradictory 'Paradox Pulse'.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where players build a scene while the Referee injects a series of bizarre, pre-gathered 'Flux Endowments' that instantly change the reality of the scene. The game culminates in the 'Paradox Pulse'—a final endowment that directly contradicts an earlier one, forcing players to use extreme cognitive flexibility to justify how both opposing realities can coexist.

## Setup
Two players step onto the stage (one from Team A, one from Team B). The Referee asks the audience for a mundane location and a relationship to anchor the scene. Before starting the scene, the Referee rapidly gathers 3 to 4 'Flux Endowments' from the audience (e.g., a strange physical rule, a bizarre character secret, a magical property of an object). The Referee notes these down to prevent dead air during gameplay. The Referee keeps the final 'Paradox Pulse' (a direct contradiction) secret, inventing it on the fly based on how the scene unfolds.

## How to Play
1. The Base Scene: The two players begin a grounded scene using the audience-suggested location and relationship.
2. The Flux Injections: The Referee blows the whistle to freeze the scene a maximum of 3 times. At each freeze, the Referee announces one of the pre-gathered Flux Endowments (e.g., 'Flux! Gravity is now reversed!' or 'Flux! Player A, you are secretly a sentient puddle!').
3. Instant Integration: When the whistle blows to unfreeze, the players must instantly accept, justify, and integrate the new reality into the ongoing scene without dropping any of the previous realities.
4. The Paradox Pulse: For the 4th and final interruption, the Referee introduces the 'Paradox Pulse'—an endowment that directly contradicts a previously established Flux (e.g., if a previous Flux was 'The room is freezing cold,' the Paradox Pulse is 'The room is actually an active volcano').
5. The Justification Climax: The players must immediately invent a brilliant, comedic justification for how both contradictory realities are simultaneously true (e.g., 'Ah, the volcano's magma is made of liquid nitrogen!').
6. Resolution: After the Paradox Pulse is successfully justified and the scene reaches a comedic peak, the Referee calls 'Scene!'
7. Scoring: At the end of the scene, the Referee asks the audience to vote by applause for which player/team provided the best justification during the Paradox Pulse, awarding standard match points (e.g., 5 points) to the winner.

## Coaching Notes
- Maintain zero dead air: gather endowments upfront to keep the game's momentum lightning-fast.
- Encourage extreme justification and cognitive flexibility; players must hold onto all previous realities even as new ones are added.
- Remind players to actively listen and retain memory of all injected Flux Endowments.
- The Referee should focus on pacing and scene-building, timing the whistle blows to interrupt at the best comedic moments.

## Variations
- Blind Flux: The players step out of the room or wear noise-canceling headphones while the audience provides the Flux Endowments to the Referee, ensuring genuine surprise when they are announced.
- Tag-Team Flux: Played with 4 players (2 per team). When a Flux is announced, a player on the sidelines can tag in to replace their teammate, taking on the burden of justifying the new reality and keeping the energy fresh.

## Why It Works
The game forces active listening, memory retention, and extreme cognitive flexibility. By gathering endowments upfront, it eliminates dead air and maintains lightning-fast momentum, while the Paradox Pulse creates a built-in comedic climax through justification.

## Safety & Inclusion
Referees must ensure that 'Flux Endowments' do not force players into unsafe physical actions (e.g., 'you are on fire' should be played emotionally/comedically, not with dangerous tumbling). Endowments must remain family-friendly and avoid crossing personal boundaries or relying on harmful stereotypes. The game is highly accessible as it relies entirely on verbal justification and can be played entirely seated or with limited mobility.

# Kinetic Narrative

![Kinetic Narrative — infographic](img/IS152.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players dynamically adjust their physical proximity based on real-time external signals, instantly justifying the shifts in their characters' emotions and relationship.

## Overview
Kinetic Narrative is an improvisation game where two players must dynamically adjust their physical proximity and spatial relationship based on real-time, external signals from a Conductor. The core challenge is for players to immediately and creatively integrate these forced physical changes into their characters' emotions, evolving relationships, and the scene's ongoing story.

## Setup
Clear a designated performance space. A non-playing Conductor or Narrative Engineer stands off-stage, equipped with three distinct, non-verbal signals (like colored cards, audio tones, or hand gestures) representing Reduce Distance, Increase Distance, and Lock Proximity.

## How to Play
1. Get an audience suggestion for a Relationship (e.g., estranged siblings) and a Shared Objective (e.g., to pack a suitcase).
2. Two players start in a neutral standing position, medium distance apart, and begin establishing their characters, environment, and shared objective.
3. At random intervals (every 10-20 seconds), the Conductor issues one of the three signals.
4. Signal 1 (Reduce Distance): Players must find an immediate, justified reason to significantly decrease the physical distance between their characters.
5. Signal 2 (Increase Distance): Players must find an immediate, justified reason to significantly increase the physical distance between their characters.
6. Signal 3 (Lock Proximity): Players must freeze their relative physical distance for a brief period, maintaining that exact distance even if moving around the stage.
7. Immediately upon receiving a signal, players must physically respond and crucially integrate and justify this physical shift into the ongoing narrative, character emotion, and relationship dynamics.
8. The scene plays for approximately 3-5 minutes, culminating in a natural blackout or scene end initiated by the Conductor.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus on narrative development over gags; relationships cannot remain static and must be physically pushed and pulled.
- Embrace mistakes and take risks. An awkward physical position is an imposed external gift that must be woven into the fabric of the scene.
- Demand intense collaboration. If one player makes a bold physical offer, the other must acknowledge and justify their complementary move.
- Every movement, step, and lean must convey meaning and drive the story.
- Allow yourself to be changed. You cannot stick to a predetermined emotional state; the external signal must change your feelings and actions immediately.
- Use proximity as a fundamental indicator of status. Reducing distance can signify intimacy or aggressive challenge, while increasing distance can reflect escape or dismissal.
- Acutely observe your partner's physical reaction to the cue to ensure seamless integration.

## Variations
- Add a third player to act as a non-speaking object of attention or an external force if the scene demands it.

## Why It Works
The constant physical shifts force narrative development and prevent static scenes. By imposing external spatial constraints, players are compelled to actively listen, immediately accept offers, and deeply justify emotional changes, naturally driving character development and status play within the collaborative spirit of improvisation.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the performance space is clear of tripping hazards, as players will be moving frequently while focusing on each other. Respect personal boundaries and physical consent, especially during Reduce Distance or Lock Proximity cues that may lead to close physical proximity or touch.

# The Consequence Coaster

![The Consequence Coaster — infographic](img/ISR264.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must instantly justify bizarre, audience-assigned consequences during sudden scene freezes.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where two players build a scene that is abruptly interrupted by the referee. During these freezes, the audience assigns a bizarre, immediate consequence to a player, who must instantly justify and integrate this new reality into whatever they do next.

## Setup
One player from the Red team and one from the Blue team step forward. The referee gets a standard scene suggestion from the audience, such as a location or relationship. The referee stands downstage with a whistle, ready to pause the action.

## How to Play
1. Scene Start: The two players begin improvising the scene based on the audience suggestion, establishing their characters and the environment.
2. The Freeze: At a moment of heightened action or right before a player is about to do something significant, the referee blows the whistle and calls 'Freeze!'
3. The Consequence: The referee points to one of the frozen players and asks the audience for an immediate consequence. Prompts should be specific, such as 'What bizarre physical sensation suddenly strikes them?' or 'What absurd emotion completely overwhelms them right now?'
4. The Integration: The audience yells out suggestions. The referee selects the best, most family-friendly option, repeats it clearly to the players, and yells 'Play!'
5. Justification: The affected player must instantly apply that consequence to whatever action or dialogue they were about to execute. Their scene partner must 'Yes, And' this new reality, treating it as a completely truthful part of the scene.
6. Pacing Limit: The referee repeats this freeze-and-consequence mechanic a maximum of 2 to 3 times per scene. This strict limit ensures the narrative doesn't grind to a halt and the scene maintains its momentum.
7. End and Score: After the final consequence has been explored and the scene reaches a comedic peak, the referee calls 'Scene!'
8. The referee awards up to 5 points to the team that best justified and physically committed to their consequences, or splits the points if both players collaborated seamlessly. The referee can call a content foul (-5 points) for inappropriate content, or a groaner foul (-3 points) if a player completely ignores their assigned consequence.

## Coaching Notes
- Ensure high audience interaction through frequent, impactful prompts.
- Force immediate physical and emotional commitment from the players the exact moment 'Play!' is called.
- Test players' ability to justify sudden, absurd endowments by choosing moments of heightened action or anticipation for the freezes.
- Keep the narrative fast-paced and driven by unexpected obstacles by strictly adhering to the 2 to 3 freeze limit.

## Variations
- Prop Coaster: Instead of physical or emotional consequences, the referee asks the audience for a bizarre, invisible object that the frozen player suddenly realizes they are holding.
- Genre Coaster: The consequence is a sudden shift in theatrical genre or movie style that both players must adopt for the next 30 seconds of the scene.

## Why It Works
It forces immediate physical and emotional commitment while testing players' ability to justify sudden, absurd endowments, creating a fast-paced narrative driven by unexpected obstacles.

## Safety & Inclusion
The referee acts as a crucial safety filter, immediately rejecting audience suggestions that are inappropriate, unsafe, or mock real-world disabilities. Players are encouraged to interpret physical limitations creatively and safely rather than literally, ensuring no one is forced into uncomfortable or dangerous physical positions.

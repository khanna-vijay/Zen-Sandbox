# Echo Chamber

![Echo Chamber — infographic](img/ISR106.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two players build a scene using a strictly limited, shared vocabulary, adding exactly one new word per line.

## Overview
A competitive short-form game where two players must build a scene using a strictly limited, shared vocabulary. After an initial short seed phrase, players may only speak using words already established in the scene, plus exactly one new word per line. This gamifies active listening and word economy, forcing players to rely heavily on physical and emotional choices to communicate.

## Setup
2 players and 1 Referee equipped with a buzzer or whistle. Get an audience suggestion for a location and a relationship. No props or set pieces required.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a location and relationship from the audience to ground the scene.
2. Player A begins the scene with a short 'seed' sentence (maximum 5 words, e.g., 'I love this little dog'). These words form the initial 'Vocabulary Pool'.
3. Player B must respond. Their line of dialogue must be constructed entirely from words currently in the Vocabulary Pool, but they are allowed to add exactly ONE new word (e.g., 'I love this HOT dog' - adding 'hot').
4. Play alternates. Every time a player speaks, they may reuse any words from the growing pool in any order, plus exactly ONE new word (e.g., Player A replies: 'Hot dog, I EAT this!' - adding 'eat').
5. Words can be repeated and rearranged freely, but changing the tense or plurality of a word (e.g., changing 'run' to 'running' or 'dog' to 'dogs') counts as using your one new word.
6. The Referee listens with intense focus. If a player uses more than one new word in a single line, uses a word not in the pool, or hesitates/stalls for too long, the Referee blows the whistle or hits the buzzer, calling a Foul.
7. When a Foul is called, the audience cheers the failure, the player must immediately justify the mistake or take a physical penalty (like a lap around the stage), and the scene resumes.
8. The scene ends after a set time limit (e.g., 2-3 minutes) or after 3 total Fouls.

## Coaching Notes
- The audience acts as the ultimate judges of the Vocabulary Pool. The Referee encourages the audience to yell 'Buzz!' if they catch a player using an illegal word before the Ref does.
- Points are awarded to the team based on how long they survive without a Foul, or by audience applause for the most coherent/hilarious narrative built despite the restriction.
- Strip away verbal crutches by demanding strong physical and emotional choices from the players.

## Variations
- Audience Seed: Instead of Player A inventing the first sentence, the Referee gets 3-4 random words from the audience to serve as the starting Vocabulary Pool.
- Gibberish Echo: Played entirely in Gibberish. Players must track and reuse specific Gibberish sounds/words, relying entirely on physical translation and tone to make the 'new' Gibberish word clear.

## Why It Works
It forces intense active listening and memorization while stripping away verbal crutches, demanding strong physical and emotional choices to communicate effectively.

## Safety & Inclusion
The cognitive load of this game is intentionally high. The Referee should frame the game so that failure is celebrated—the struggle and the inevitable mistakes are where the comedy lives, reducing performance anxiety. Because players will be highly focused on their words, encourage them to keep physical staging simple and safe to avoid accidental collisions. Content remains clean and all-ages.

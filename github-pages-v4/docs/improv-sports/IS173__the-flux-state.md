# The Flux State

![The Flux State — infographic](img/IS173.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Collaboratively build a compelling narrative while consistently adapting to an evolving, environment-driven constraint that changes periodically throughout the scene.

## Overview
The Flux State is an improv game challenging improvisers to collaboratively build a compelling narrative while continuously adapting to a single, evolving, environment-driven constraint. This constraint changes periodically throughout the scene, escalating or altering, forcing performers to instantly integrate new physical and verbal rules into their character's actions and dialogue. These dynamic setups rigorously test core improv skills, as the shifting constraints become integral plot points that drive the story.

## Setup
The host requests a 'Location' and a 'Relationship' from the audience (e.g., 'A deep-sea diving bell,' 'Estranged siblings'). The host then announces the initial 'Flux State' (the core environmental constraint) which will define the characters' physical and often verbal interactions. A pre-determined timer (e.g., 45-60 seconds) or a visible cue from a judge is established to signal a 'State Shift'.

## How to Play
1. The scene begins with performers establishing the location, relationship, and immediately embodying the initial 'Flux State'.
2. When the 'State Shift' signal occurs, a new, related or escalated environmental constraint is announced by the host (or revealed on a screen/card).
3. Performers must instantly integrate this new rule into their actions, dialogue, and the evolving narrative.
4. The new Flux State might intensify the existing constraint, alter it, introduce a contrasting constraint, or provide a temporary solution/complication.
5. Performers must maintain a coherent narrative arc despite these dramatic and often absurd shifts, treating the constraints as integral plot points and character motivations.
6. Performers must actively 'Yes, And' not only their partners' dialogue but also their immediate physical and verbal adaptations to the new Flux State.
7. If a performer momentarily forgets a rule or makes a physical choice that breaks the current Flux State, their partner should accept and integrate that 'failure' into the scene, making it a new character challenge or plot point.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: The Flux State actively drives the narrative. Each shift in the environment presents a new crisis or opportunity, forcing characters to re-evaluate their goals.
- Embracing Mistakes: The periodic 'State Shifts' inherently create unexpected turns. Any misstep in adhering to the evolving rules becomes an instant 'gift' for the partner to build upon.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: Performers must be hyper-attentive to their partners' physical and verbal adherence to the Flux State. Actively supporting a struggling partner is paramount to making them 'look good'.
- Status Play: The Flux State can instantly flip status. A dominant character might become utterly helpless under a new physical constraint, forcing a re-evaluation of power dynamics.

## Why It Works
It is a rigorous test of 'Being Changed' and physicality. Performers must constantly alter their character's physicality, emotional state, and vocal patterns in response to the dynamic external environment, forcing continuous adaptation while simultaneously requiring strong narrative drive and unwavering collaborative support.

## Safety & Inclusion
Because the game involves rapid physical changes, sudden constraints, and potential physical contact (e.g., touching to lift constraints), players must respect physical boundaries and consent. Ensure the stage is clear of tripping hazards, and avoid dangerous physical choices when embodying extreme constraints like 'floating' or 'freezing'.

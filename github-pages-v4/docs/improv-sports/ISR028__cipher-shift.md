# Cipher Shift

![Cipher Shift — infographic](img/ISR028.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy guessing game where improvisers deduce a secret phrase using audience 'Clue Words' and 'Hot/Cold' crowd reactions.

## Overview
A high-energy guessing game where improvisers must deduce a secret phrase using three audience-provided 'Clue Words' and 'Hot/Cold' crowd reactions. To keep the scene theatrical and prevent it from devolving into a mere word-search, players must seamlessly weave their guesses into grounded dialogue and adapt to sudden emotional or physical 'Shifts' called by the Referee.

## Setup
Format: Competitive Short-Form. Props: A whiteboard and marker for the Referee, plus optional Red/Green flags for accessibility. The Referee gets a 4 to 5 word 'Secret Confession' from the audience while the playing team is out of earshot, then extracts 3 'Clue Words' related to the phrase. The audience is instructed to cheer as players get conceptually closer to the phrase and groan when they move away.

## How to Play
1. Send the playing team out of the room or put them in soundproof headphones.
2. The Referee asks the audience for a specific, action-oriented 4 to 5 word sentence to be the 'Secret Confession' (e.g., 'I glued the dog to the ceiling'). Write this on a whiteboard visible only to the audience.
3. Ask the audience for 3 'Clue Words' that hint at the confession without giving it away completely (e.g., 'Sticky', 'Bark', 'Up').
4. Bring the players back. Announce the 3 Clue Words to them.
5. Explain the feedback loop: As the players perform the scene, the audience will cheer louder as their dialogue and actions get closer to the meaning of the Secret Confession (Hot), and groan if they drift away (Cold).
6. Establish the Golden Rule: Players must establish a base reality (Character, Relationship, Where). They cannot just stand and list words; every guess must be justified as natural dialogue.
7. Start the clock (typically 3 minutes). The players begin the scene, using the Clue Words to probe the audience's reactions.
8. THE SHIFT: Every 45-60 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'SHIFT: [Emotion/Genre/Physicality]!' (e.g., 'SHIFT: Melodrama!' or 'SHIFT: Moving in slow motion!'). Players must immediately adopt this new style while continuing the scene and the guessing.
9. The game ends immediately when a player speaks the exact Secret Confession (or a remarkably close variation approved by the Ref), or when time expires.

## Coaching Notes
- Scoring: Award 5 points for each Clue Word naturally integrated into the scene, 10 points for discovering the core action/concept, and 25 points for uttering the exact Secret Confession.
- Call a 'Listing Foul' (minus 5 points) if players drop the scene to just play 20-questions or list random words.
- Call a 'content foul' (loss of points) for inappropriate content.
- Strictly enforce scene-building. Penalizing word-listing elevates the improv quality above standard parlor games.
- Use the 'Shift' mechanic to force players to focus on acting, emotion, and character, preventing the common guessing-game trap of 'talking heads'.

## Variations
- Reverse Cipher: The players are given the Secret Confession and must hide it seamlessly within a 2-minute scene. A designated audience member (or a player from the opposing team who was out of the room) must guess the phrase at the end based on the scene's clues.
- Gibberish Cipher: The players speak entirely in gibberish. The audience's hot/cold reactions guide their physical actions and emotional tone until they physically act out the secret phrase, forcing a 'show, don't tell' approach.

## Why It Works
The built-in audience feedback loop makes a complex puzzle solvable and highly interactive, while thematic Clue Words provide a springboard for the scene's narrative. The 'Shift' mechanic and strict enforcement of scene-building force players to focus on acting, emotion, and character, preventing the scene from devolving into a static word-search.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee must heavily vet the Secret Confession to ensure it is clean, family-friendly, and free of sensitive or triggering topics (enforced via a content foul). For accessibility, if players have auditory processing difficulties with audience cheering, or if the venue is too loud, the Referee must use clear visual cues (e.g., raising a green flag for 'Hot' and a red flag for 'Cold'). Ensure physical 'Shifts' accommodate the mobility levels of all players on stage.

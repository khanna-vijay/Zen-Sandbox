# Symbiotic Scaffolding

![Symbiotic Scaffolding — infographic](img/ISR124.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A silent, physical ensemble game where players collectively build and maintain an evolving structure through shared balance, gentle touch, and mutual support.

## Overview
A silent, physical ensemble game where players collectively build and maintain an evolving structure through shared balance, gentle touch, and mutual support. By focusing on eye contact, continuous visual consent, and physical listening, players develop hyper-present group mind and intuitive responsiveness without the need for spoken words.

## Setup
Clear a large, open room of all obstacles. The facilitator gathers the ensemble in a circle for a mandatory consent and boundary check-in. Players establish their individual touch preferences (e.g., 'shoulders and arms only', 'no weight-bearing'). The facilitator explains that silence will be maintained during the exercise, except for safety calls like 'Release' or 'Stop'.

## How to Play
1. Consent Check-In: Before starting, the facilitator leads a brief check-in where every player explicitly states their physical boundaries for the day. The facilitator establishes universal 'no-go' zones (e.g., no pressure on joints, necks, or heads).
2. The Anchor: The facilitator asks for a volunteer to be the 'Anchor'. This player steps into the center and takes a strong, stable, comfortable physical stance (e.g., feet wide, arms extended).
3. The Invitation: The Anchor makes eye contact with a player in the circle, offering a visual invitation to join. That player approaches slowly.
4. Visual Consent & Connection: Before making physical contact, the approaching player offers a gesture (like reaching out a hand) and waits for a nod or eye-contact confirmation from the Anchor. Once acknowledged, they connect using gentle, supportive pressure, leaning with the person, not on them, creating a shared counter-balance.
5. Building the Scaffold: One by one, through eye contact and visual consent, other players join the structure. The core rule is: 'Everyone must eventually be supporting someone else, and simultaneously being supported by someone else.'
6. Evolving the Structure: Once the scaffold is built, any player can slowly and gently shift their weight or position. This shift acts as a physical impulse. The rest of the group must intuitively adapt to the changing pressure and balance to maintain the collective structure.
7. Resolution: The game continues until the facilitator calls 'Neutral' or 'Slowly dissolve.' Players gently release their connections and return to a neutral standing position.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Maintaining the structural integrity of the group through shared balance and physical listening.
- Develops hyper-present, non-verbal communication and ensemble trust.
- Forces players out of their heads and into their bodies.
- Emphasizes continuous, dynamic consent and spatial awareness.

## Variations
- Magnetic Scaffolding (No-Touch): Instead of physical contact, players build the structure using proximity. They hold their hands or bodies 2-3 inches away from each other, imagining a strong magnetic tension. If one player moves, the 'magnetized' players must move with them to maintain the exact distance. Excellent for accessibility and low-touch days.
- Fingertip Scaffolding (Low-Touch): Players connect using only the tips of their index fingers. This removes all weight-bearing elements and shifts the focus entirely to micro-adjustments, spatial awareness, and delicate group balance.
- Environmental Shifts: Once the structure is stable, the facilitator side-coaches environmental conditions (e.g., 'A gentle breeze pushes from the left,' 'The gravity in the room suddenly feels very light'). The structure must adapt collectively in silence.

## Why It Works
It forces players out of their heads and into their bodies, developing hyper-present group mind and intuitive responsiveness without the need for spoken words through physical listening and shared balance.

## Safety & Inclusion
Blindfolds have been explicitly removed from this exercise to ensure safety, allow for visual consent, and improve balance. Mandatory consent check-ins occur before play. Anyone can opt-out of weight-bearing or touch entirely by playing the 'Magnetic' variation. Seated players or those with mobility aids can easily participate as the Anchor or by connecting via arms/hands from their seated position. Any player can say 'Release' at any time to safely and immediately dissolve the structure.

# Emotional Current

![Emotional Current — infographic](img/ISR190.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Keep a real balloon in the air while passing a rapid-fire chain of spontaneous emotional sounds and reactions.

## Overview
A fast-paced ensemble warm-up where players work together to keep a real balloon in the air. Every time a player hits the balloon, they must make a spontaneous, non-verbal sound and facial expression. The next player to hit the balloon must visibly react to or echo that previous emotion before making their own, creating a continuous, rapid-fire chain of emotional reactions driven by the physical urgency of the falling balloon.

## Setup
Players stand in a loose circle in a clear room free of tripping hazards. The facilitator holds a real, inflated balloon (do not use an imaginary object, as the actual gravity and float of a real balloon provide the necessary physical urgency).

## How to Play
1. The facilitator tosses the balloon into the center of the group. The physical goal is simple: keep the balloon from touching the floor.
2. Whenever a player strikes the balloon, they must simultaneously emit a loud, non-verbal sound and a clear facial expression or gesture (e.g., a frustrated grunt, a joyous laugh, a terrified gasp, a confused hum).
3. The next player who moves to hit the balloon must visibly 'catch' the previous player's emotion. To make this observable for the facilitator, the player must either briefly echo the sound/expression or physically react to it (e.g., flinching at an angry shout) as they move toward the balloon.
4. As that second player strikes the balloon, they must instantly drop the previous emotion and emit their own completely new, spontaneous sound and expression.
5. No actual words or dialogue are allowed—only breaths, sounds, and physical expressions.
6. If the balloon hits the ground, if a player uses words, or if a player approaches the balloon blank-faced without reacting to the previous emotion, the facilitator calls 'Reset!' and immediately tosses the balloon back in to restart the flow.

## Coaching Notes
- Act as a side-coach, calling out prompts like 'Don't think, just make a sound!', 'Let their sound affect your body!', or 'Keep it up, keep breathing!' to maintain high energy and prevent players from intellectualizing their choices.
- Watch for the concrete 'echo or react' mechanic; it makes the abstract concept of 'listening' physically observable for you as the facilitator.

## Variations
- Seated/Accessible Version: For groups with limited mobility, players sit in a tight circle and pass a lightweight ball or beanbag rapidly from hand to hand (Hot Potato style) instead of chasing a balloon. The emotional sound and reaction rules remain exactly the same.
- Gibberish Current: Instead of just sounds and breaths, players must speak a short phrase of gibberish with strong emotional intent when they strike the balloon.

## Why It Works
The physical urgency of the falling balloon bypasses the intellectual brain, forcing spontaneous choices. It also builds ensemble connection and eye contact through shared physical and emotional energy.

## Safety & Inclusion
Using a real balloon ensures a slow, predictable fall, which reduces the risk of players colliding or diving dangerously. Always clear the playing space of bags, chairs, and other tripping hazards. For players with mobility issues or injuries, immediately default to the Seated/Accessible variation so everyone can participate equally. Remind players that they can step out of the circle at any time if the physical chaos or noise level becomes overwhelming.

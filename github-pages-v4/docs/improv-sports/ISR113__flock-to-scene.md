# Flock to Scene

![Flock to Scene — infographic](img/ISR113.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Move as a leaderless flock, freeze on command, and use your abstract physical positions to launch a grounded scene.

## Overview
Players move together as a single, leaderless flock, communicating only through physical impulses and breath. At the facilitator's cue, the group freezes and must immediately use their abstract physical positions to inspire and launch a concrete, grounded improv scene.

## Setup
6 to 12 players in an open space. No props or chairs needed. The facilitator stands on the periphery.

## How to Play
1. Form the Flock: Players gather in a loose cluster in the center of the room, maintaining at least an arm's length of space between each other.
2. Abstract Movement: On the facilitator's cue, the group begins moving together in silence. There is no designated leader.
3. Offer and Amplify: Players offer small physical impulses, such as a shift in weight, a raised arm, a change in tempo, or a non-verbal sound like a sigh. When a player notices an impulse, they immediately mirror and amplify it. The leadership organically passes around the group.
4. The Freeze: Once the group is moving fluidly as a single unit, the facilitator claps loudly and calls, 'Freeze!' All players freeze instantly in their current physical positions.
5. Justify the Picture: The facilitator calls, 'Scene!' The players observe their own bodies and the tableau of the group. The first person to speak must initiate a concrete scene that justifies their physical positions. For example, if everyone is crouching and looking down, someone might say, 'I told you we shouldn't have opened this manhole.'
6. Play the Scene: The abstract movement dissolves, and the players perform a short 1 to 2 minute scene based on that physical initiation, establishing characters, relationship, and setting.

## Coaching Notes
- If there is an observing class or audience, ask them to provide a collective sound (like a gasp, a groan, or a laugh) or an emotion. The flock must physically embody that sound or emotion before the freeze.
- Ensure players do not pre-plan the scene during the flocking phase; the scene should be a spontaneous justification of the frozen tableau.
- Encourage players to commit fully to the physical impulses and amplify them without hesitation.

## Variations
- Tap Out: During the freeze, the facilitator taps 2 or 3 players on the shoulder. Only those tapped come alive to justify the scene, while the rest of the group quietly melts away to the sidelines.
- Emotion Flock: The facilitator calls out specific emotions during the movement phase. The resulting scene must maintain the emotional undercurrent of the final prompt.

## Why It Works
It forces deep non-verbal listening and group mind, encouraging players to get out of their heads and rely on physical impulses. It effectively bridges the gap between abstract physical warm-ups and concrete scene work.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical Boundaries: Explicitly establish a no-touch rule during the flocking phase. Players must maintain spatial awareness and a small buffer zone to prevent accidental collisions. Accessibility: Flocking does not require walking or running. Players with mobility restrictions can lead and follow using upper body movements, facial expressions, or vocalizations. The group is instructed to match the physical capacity of all members.

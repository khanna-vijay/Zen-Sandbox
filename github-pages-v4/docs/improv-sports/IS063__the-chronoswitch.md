# The Chronoswitch

![The Chronoswitch — infographic](img/IS063.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Dramatically transform a character's core persona and occupation on immediate cue, forcing players to exhibit extreme adaptability within an ongoing scene.

## Overview
"The Chronoswitch" is a competitive improv game where one player, the "Chronoswitcher," must instantly and completely transform their character's persona and occupation upon an external verbal prompt. Meanwhile, the "Anchors" must seamlessly accept this new reality, integrating it into the ongoing scene without questioning the sudden change, as if the Chronoswitcher had always been their new identity. The game tests extreme adaptability and commitment from all participants, scored on the swiftness of transformation and the believability of integration within the unyielding new context.

## Setup
A minimal, open stage space. No special lighting or sound effects beyond a simple "ding" or bell sound, or just the host's voice, for the trigger. No specific props are required, though players may initiate prop work. The audience is integral for supplying the "switch" prompts.

## How to Play
1. The host asks the audience for a single, broad relationship (e.g., "Co-workers") and a single, broad location (e.g., "Elevator"). Players enter the scene and immediately establish this relationship and location.
2. One player is initially designated "The Chronoswitcher." This player's character will be the one undergoing transformations. The other 2-3 players are "Anchors"; their characters remain consistent throughout the round, reacting to the Chronoswitcher's changes.
3. At any point during the scene, the host (or a designated audience member with a bell) will loudly shout, "CHRONOSWITCH!"
4. Immediately after the trigger, the host or audience member will shout out a new, distinct, and strong occupation or core identity for the Chronoswitcher's character (e.g., "Now you're a supervillain!" or "Now you're a gossipy florist!").
5. Upon hearing "CHRONOSWITCH!" and the new identity, the Chronoswitcher must instantly shed their previous persona and fully embody the new character. They transform as if they had always been this new person within the established relationship and location.
6. The Anchors must instantly and seamlessly accept the Chronoswitcher's transformation as the new reality of the scene. They do not question it, act confused, or try to rationalize the change, but immediately adapt their own characters' reactions and dialogue to interact naturally with the new persona.
7. The scene continues with the new character dynamics established by the transformation, developing naturally until the next "CHRONOSWITCH!" (typically 2-4 times) or until the host calls "SCENE!"
8. Judges (or the audience in a competitive short-form match) award points based on swiftness, commitment, integration, and narrative adaptability.

## Coaching Notes
- The Chronoswitcher must shift three key elements instantly: Physicality (posture, movement, gestures), Voice (tone, cadence, accent), and Mindset/Status (attitudes, knowledge).
- Anchors must avoid asking questions like, "Wait, aren't you a barista?" Instead, if the roommate suddenly becomes a brain surgeon, they should immediately ask about a difficult surgery or marvel at their expertise.
- Award high points for the Chronoswitcher's immediate, undeniable commitment and the Anchors' smooth, believable adaptation.
- Deduct points (penalties) for hesitation, breaking character, commenting on the transformation, or failing to fully commit to the new reality.

## Variations
- Multi-Round Rotation: For multi-round games, players can rotate being the Chronoswitcher so everyone gets a turn undergoing transformations.

## Why It Works
The game forces rapid-fire character generation and absolute "Yes, And" acceptance. The Chronoswitcher practices instant commitment to physicality and voice, while the Anchors practice seamless justification and integration without denying or questioning the new reality, keeping the scene flowing despite abrupt shifts.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical transformations are done safely without risking injury to oneself or scene partners. Maintain respectful boundaries when adopting new personas and avoid harmful stereotypes.

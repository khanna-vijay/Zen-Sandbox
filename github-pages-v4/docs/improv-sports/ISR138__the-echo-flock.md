# The Echo Flock

![The Echo Flock — infographic](img/ISR138.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A group moves across the room as a unified flock, seamlessly passing a continuous, precise sound or gesture from player to player.

## Overview
A non-competitive ensemble exercise where a group moves across the room as a unified flock, passing a continuous, precise sound or gesture called the Echo. The focus is on hyper-present listening and seamless hand-offs, quieting the intellect to achieve pure group synchronization.

## Setup
Clear an open room of all obstacles. Designate a Start zone and a Finish zone about 20-30 feet apart. Group players into ensembles of 5 to 8. The facilitator stands on the sidelines where they can clearly see and hear the entire group.

## How to Play
1. The group gathers in the Start zone as a tight, unstructured cluster (a flock or blob).
2. The facilitator assigns a simple, continuous Echo (e.g., a two-clap rhythm, a specific hum, a tongue click, or a repetitive finger snap).
3. One player organically volunteers to be the first Carrier and begins producing the Echo.
4. The Carrier may take up to three steps toward the Finish line while maintaining the Echo. The rest of the flock moves alongside them, staying clustered.
5. Before the Carrier takes their fourth step, another player must catch the Echo by perfectly replicating it. The moment the new player starts the Echo, the previous Carrier must stop.
6. The new Carrier may now take up to three steps, and the process repeats. The group must organically share the Echo without pre-planning the order or using verbal cues.
7. The Facilitator acts as the sole judge of perfect replication. If the Echo mutates, stops, or a Carrier takes a fourth step, the Facilitator calls Drop! The group must pause, take a collective breath, and restart from the Start zone (or their current spot, for a gentler learning curve).
8. The exercise is complete when the entire flock crosses the Finish line with an unbroken, perfectly replicated Echo.

## Coaching Notes
- Act as the sole judge of perfect replication. If the Echo mutates, stops, or a Carrier takes a fourth step, call Drop!
- Side-coach with phrases like 'Listen to the rhythm,' 'Share the focus,' and 'Breathe together'.
- Ensure the group organically shares the Echo without pre-planning the order or using verbal cues.
- Remind the group that the win is the successful, unbroken journey of the flock.

## Variations
- Stationary Echo: The group stands in a circle and passes the Echo using eye contact instead of walking. Ideal for limited space or varying mobility.
- Evolving Echo: Instead of perfect replication, each new Carrier intentionally alters one small aspect of the Echo (pitch, speed, size), which the next person must then replicate exactly before changing it again.

## Why It Works
It develops hyper-present listening, non-verbal communication, and ensemble synchronization by providing a Point of Concentration that quiets the intellect.

## Safety & Inclusion
Clear the floor of all tripping hazards, as players' visual focus will be on each other rather than the ground. Steps can be adapted to wheelchair pushes, weight shifts, or arm swings for players with different mobility needs. Ensure the assigned Echoes are physically comfortable for all participants to perform repeatedly without strain.

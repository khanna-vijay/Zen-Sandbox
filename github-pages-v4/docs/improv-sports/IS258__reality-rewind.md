# Reality Rewind

![Reality Rewind — infographic](img/IS258.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must instantly adapt to sudden, referee-imposed plot twists and new realities without acknowledging the change.

## Overview
Reality Rewind is an improv game where teams of two start a scene based on audience suggestions. A referee periodically intervenes with a REWIND! call, introducing sudden, often contradictory plot twists or changes to the scene's established reality. Players must instantly and seamlessly integrate these new circumstances, acting as if the twist was true from the beginning without acknowledging the rewind itself.

## Setup
Two players from the Red Team take the stage, followed by two players from the Blue Team for their turn. The Referee requests two audience suggestions: a Setting and a Relationship between the two characters. The Referee clearly states these suggestions for the first team.

## How to Play
1. The first team starts a scene based on the audience's Setting and Relationship, establishing their characters, environment, and dynamic.
2. After 20-40 seconds, once the scene is clearly established, the Referee loudly calls out: REWIND!
3. Immediately after REWIND!, the Referee introduces a new, often unexpected or contradictory endowment or plot twist.
4. Players must instantly integrate this new information into the ongoing scene. They must not acknowledge the rewind or question the new reality, playing as if the new twist was true from the very beginning.
5. The Referee can call REWIND! multiple times within the same scene, layering or entirely altering previous twists.
6. After 60-90 seconds (or 2-3 rewinds), the Referee calls FREEZE! and then NEXT SCENE!
7. The other team takes the stage, receiving a new set of audience suggestions for their turn. Teams alternate performing scenes until the round is complete.
8. The Referee awards points based on seamless integration, creative adaptation, and energetic play. The team with the highest cumulative score wins.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee must pay close attention to the scene and deliver twists that are challenging yet playable.
- Call a content foul for any blue humor, swearing, or innuendo, regardless of the twist.
- Call a bad pun foul for excessively bad puns or low-effort jokes.
- Call a denial foul if a player explicitly acknowledges the rewind, denies the new reality, or contradicts a clear, established fact from the new reality.
- Call a stalling foul if players become stuck, freeze, or fail to initiate action after a rewind for too long.
- The less players appear to think when integrating the twist, the better. Reward seamless, natural integration.
- Encourage dynamic object work, as new twists often necessitate new imaginary props or physical interactions.

## Why It Works
It tests absolute Yes, And skills, requiring players to shed previous assumptions and embrace new realities on the fly. It develops active listening to hear the exact nature of the endowment, adaptability, and maintaining clear characters and relationships that fluidly adapt to changing circumstances.

## Safety & Inclusion
All twists and scene content must remain family-friendly. The Referee should guide players toward wholesome hilarity, and any inappropriate content or blue humor should be immediately penalized with a content foul.

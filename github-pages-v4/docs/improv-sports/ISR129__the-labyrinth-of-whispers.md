# The Labyrinth of Whispers

![The Labyrinth of Whispers — infographic](img/ISR129.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A blindfolded Walker navigates a human maze guided only by a continuous whisper.

## Overview
A sensory ensemble-building exercise where a blindfolded 'Walker' navigates a human maze formed by silent 'Architects,' guided only by the continuous sound of a 'Guide Whisperer.' It builds intense focus, trust, and non-verbal spatial awareness.

## Setup
Clear the room of all bags, chairs, and tripping hazards. One player volunteers as the 'Walker' and closes their eyes (or uses a blindfold). One player is chosen as the 'Guide Whisperer.' The remaining players are 'Architects.' The Facilitator remains outside the play area as a dedicated safety spotter.

## How to Play
1. The Walker establishes a 'bumper stance' by raising their hands to chest level, elbows bent, palms facing outward to gently feel for obstacles.
2. The Architects silently take positions around the room to create a maze of bodies. They must plant their feet firmly and may use their arms and torsos to form gentle barriers, but cannot move their feet while the Walker is in motion.
3. The Guide Whisperer stands at the far end of the room and begins a continuous, gentle sound (e.g., a hum, a rhythmic click, or a soft chant). This is the Walker's beacon.
4. The Walker slowly moves toward the sound. If they gently bump into an Architect's hands or arms, they must feel their way around the barrier to find an open path.
5. If the Walker comes to a complete stop, the Architects may silently take 1-2 steps to reshape the maze. The moment the Walker begins moving again, all Architects must instantly freeze their feet.
6. The round ends when the Walker successfully reaches the Guide Whisperer (who gently taps the Walker's shoulder) OR after a strict 2-minute time limit called by the Facilitator.
7. The Walker opens their eyes to see the maze they just navigated. Rotate roles so a new player becomes the Walker.

## Coaching Notes
- Maintain a highly focused, meditative atmosphere in the workshop room.
- Ensure clear, distinct Points of Concentration (POCs) are maintained for all three roles.
- Remind the ensemble that the 'win' is successfully protecting and guiding the Walker through shared focus and spatial awareness.

## Variations
- Moving Target: The Guide Whisperer slowly moves around the perimeter of the space while making their sound, requiring the Walker to constantly adjust their trajectory.
- Dual Whispers: Two Guides make different sounds (e.g., one high pitch, one low pitch). The Walker is instructed to follow one sound while actively avoiding the other, which acts as a 'danger' beacon.

## Why It Works
It isolates auditory and spatial listening skills while building intense ensemble trust and non-verbal connection.

## Safety & Inclusion
Strict safety protocols are required. The Facilitator must act as an active spotter and can call 'Freeze' at any time to stop all movement. The Walker must maintain their 'bumper stance' to protect their face and body. Architects must NEVER move their feet while the Walker is moving to prevent tripping. Touch is limited to gentle hand-to-hand or hand-to-arm contact. Players uncomfortable with having their eyes closed can play with eyes open, moving in extreme slow-motion while focusing solely on the sound.

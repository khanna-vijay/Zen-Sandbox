# The Endowment Extravaganza

![The Endowment Extravaganza — infographic](img/IS147.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams compete to seamlessly integrate a continuous, real-time barrage of bizarre character traits, object properties, and environmental changes shouted out by a Referee.

## Overview
The Endowment Extravaganza is a fast-paced competitive short-form game where teams of players must continuously integrate a barrage of increasingly bizarre and challenging endowments introduced in real-time by a Referee. Players must 'Yes, And' each new endowment, building upon all previously assigned ones to create a cohesive narrative. Points are awarded for seamless integration and deductions are given for neglecting any active endowments.

## Setup
The game is played by two teams (Red vs. Blue), typically with 2-3 players per team on stage. Before the first scene, the Referee asks the audience for quick, diverse suggestions for generic scene locations, character professions/roles, unusual object properties, and specific quirky traits. The Referee jots down 5-10 of each type of suggestion, ensuring they are inherently family-friendly and offer strong comedic potential.

## How to Play
1. The Referee calls one team to the stage and announces a general location chosen from audience suggestions.
2. Players begin the scene, establishing basic characters and interaction within the given location.
3. After approximately 15-20 seconds, the Referee shouts 'ENDOWMENT!' and immediately assigns the first specific endowment to one player. The scene continues without pausing.
4. Players must instantly integrate this new endowment into their performance.
5. After another 20-30 seconds, the Referee shouts 'ENDOWMENT!' again, introducing a second, often conflicting or layering endowment to the other player(s) or the scene environment.
6. This process continues rapidly, with the Referee introducing 3-5 distinct endowments into a single 2-3 minute scene (e.g., new character traits, unusual object properties, bizarre environmental changes, or new relationship dynamics).
7. Crucial rule: All previously assigned endowments must remain active and be maintained throughout the scene, even as new ones are added.
8. After 2-3 minutes of escalating chaos, the Referee shouts 'SCENE!' and awards points based on seamless integration, maintaining all endowments, heightening, and audience laughter.
9. The other team then takes the stage for a new round with a different location and a fresh barrage of endowments, alternating for a set number of rounds.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee is the Chief Architect of Chaos and must clearly call out 'ENDOWMENT!' with a distinct voice to introduce new constraints.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' if a player makes an excessively weak, obvious, or explanatory joke about an endowment, or explains it to their partner instead of simply acting it out.
- Call an 'Endowment Neglect Foul' (point deduction) if a player overtly ignores, drops, or contradicts a previously assigned endowment. Pause the scene, call the foul, briefly remind the player of the forgotten endowment, and resume.
- Call a 'Lack of Yes, And Foul' if a player actively blocks or overtly resists an endowment from their partner or the Referee.
- Players must use intense active listening to hear both their scene partners and the Referee's rapid-fire declarations.
- Keep the pace and energy moving to prevent mental overload from slowing down the comedic timing.

## Why It Works
It rigorously tests fundamental improv skills like 'Yes, And', active listening, object work, and character work. Players must demonstrate ultimate adaptability and flexibility by pivoting instantly with each new endowment, maintaining complex, multi-layered characters under extreme conditions.

## Safety & Inclusion
As with all competitive short-form games, this is strictly family-friendly. The Referee vets audience suggestions to ensure they are appropriate. A clean-content foul is automatically called if players resort to blue humor, swearing, or sexual innuendo due to the scene's increasing complexity. The game thrives on cleverness and physical comedy, not shock value.

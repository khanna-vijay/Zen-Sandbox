# The Local Loop

![The Local Loop — infographic](img/IS183.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A hidden location deduction game where Locals subtly act out a scene while one Tourist tries to bluff their way through and guess the setting.

## Overview
The Local Loop is a comedic improvisation game where a group of 'Locals' subtly act out a scene in a secret, often absurd, location without explicitly naming it. One 'Tourist' player, unaware of the setting, must cleverly bluff their way through the scene, attempting to deduce the location based on the Locals' cues while simultaneously blending in. The audience acts as judges, identifying the Tourist at the end, and points are awarded for clever location reveals, successful blending or deduction, and overall strong improvisational play.

## Setup
Requires 4-6 improvisers and one Host/Referee. Prepare a 'Location Card Deck' with distinct, absurd, or highly specific locations (e.g., 'Underwater Basket Weaving Convention', 'International Space Station Cafeteria'). Prepare one 'Tourist Card'. The Host privately selects one Location Card and prepares enough duplicate Location cards for all players minus one, who gets the Tourist Card. Shuffle and distribute face-down so only one player is the Tourist. The Host may solicit a relationship, shared dilemma, or simple objective from the audience as an initial hook.

## How to Play
1. The Host announces 'The Local Loop begins!' and players initiate the scene organically for the first 30 seconds, loosely integrating the audience hook.
2. Locals (players with the Location Card) collaboratively build the scene to clearly yet subtly define the secret location through environmental details, activities, jargon, or customs, without explicitly naming it.
3. The Tourist (player with the Tourist Card) attempts to deduce the location based on the Locals' cues while bluffing their way through the scene using vague language, generic observations, and mirroring.
4. Locals subtly probe and test other players with location-specific prompts to deduce who the Tourist is, while the Tourist aims to respond vaguely or deflect with calculated evasion.
5. After approximately 4-5 minutes, the Host halts the scene. The Host may ask a suspected Tourist to privately write down or state their final guess for the location.
6. The Host asks the audience to vote via pointing or applause on which player they believe was the Tourist.
7. All players simultaneously reveal their role cards, and the Host reveals the actual secret location.
8. The Host awards points to the Locals (for successful Tourist identification, clever location cues, and strong scene work) and to the Tourist (for remaining unidentified, correctly guessing the location, clever bluffs, and strong scene work). Optional fouls can be called for explicit location naming, obvious Tourist errors, or blocking.

## Coaching Notes
- Locals must 'show, don't tell' and are strictly forbidden from explicitly naming the location.
- Tourists should utilize strong physical choices to convey their understanding (or lack thereof) of the environment, activities, and props.
- Tourists can ask open-ended questions ('What is the general ethos of this gathering?') or carefully phrased leading questions.
- If the Tourist becomes confident, they may start subtly incorporating specific details, but this carries the risk of making an obvious error.
- Ensure good visibility for the entire audience to observe subtle player tells and comedic reactions.
- Watch out for fouls in the competitive short-form spirit: Explicit Location Naming (-2 points to Locals), Obvious Tourist Error (-1 point to Tourist), and Blocking/Negating (-1 point to anyone).

## Why It Works
It develops strong game playing within a scene, emphasizing environmental details, active listening, and Yes, And-ing under the cognitive load of a hidden objective. It forces players to make strong physical choices and rely on subtle cues rather than explicit exposition.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all players feel comfortable with the deduction and bluffing mechanics, avoiding aggressive interrogation tactics that might make the Tourist feel genuinely targeted or overwhelmed. Maintain a supportive environment where making a wrong guess or being caught is celebrated as part of the comedic fun.

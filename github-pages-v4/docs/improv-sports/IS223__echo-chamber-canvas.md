# Echo Chamber Canvas

![Echo Chamber Canvas — infographic](img/IS223.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Build a shared reality through a chain of immediate, intuitive responses using single words, gestures, or sounds.

## Overview
Echo Chamber Canvas is an improvisational exercise designed to build a shared reality through a chain of immediate, intuitive responses. Players in a circle each offer a single word, gesture, or sound that must spontaneously and organically extend or respond to the immediately preceding contribution.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle. The facilitator briefly explains the goal is to collectively build a shared reality (an environment, character, or situation) using only single, immediate contributions.

## How to Play
1. One player volunteers or is chosen to start by offering one distinct contribution, which can be a single word, a single gesture, or a single sound.
2. Moving clockwise, the next player immediately offers their own single distinct contribution without hesitation or discussion.
3. The new contribution must feel like a spontaneous next beat that directly resonates with, responds to, or organically expands upon the immediately preceding contribution.
4. Players continue contributing, allowing the shared reality to organically emerge and evolve.
5. The game continues for a set time, or until the facilitator senses a natural completion or calls Freeze.
6. Optionally, players can then describe what they saw or felt was created, reflecting on the shared experience without analyzing individual contributions.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration: To instantaneously receive the immediate past and offer a next beat that feels like a consequence or spontaneous extension of it.
- Encourage players to be utterly present and receptive to the last contribution.
- Remind players to bypass their intellect and respond from deep intuition, asking what must happen next rather than what they should do.
- Focus on seamless, organic connection rather than individual brilliance or clever choices.
- Treat the exercise like building a mosaic where each tile is placed in direct response to the one just laid.

## Variations
- Popcorn Style: If the group is experienced and highly connected, allow players to contribute freely instead of moving strictly clockwise.

## Why It Works
It develops hyper-responsiveness and intuitive ensemble building by forcing players to listen and observe deeply, then respond with unfiltered, authentic spontaneity directly from intuition.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical gestures remain safe and respectful of others' personal space. Encourage a supportive environment where all intuitive offers are accepted without judgment.

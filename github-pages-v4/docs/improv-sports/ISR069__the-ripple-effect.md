# The Ripple Effect

![The Ripple Effect — infographic](img/ISR069.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two simultaneous scenes unfold, where the vocal scene must organically justify the physical actions of the silent miming scene.

## Overview
Two separate scenes unfold simultaneously on stage, but only one has vocal focus at a time while the other mimes. When the miming scene makes a strong physical choice (a 'ripple'), the vocal scene must organically notice and justify that action into their own narrative. It is a workshop exercise that gamifies 'yes, and' by forcing players to accept external, abstract physical offers.

## Setup
Divide 4 to 6 players into two groups (Scene A and Scene B). Establish two distinct playing areas on stage. A facilitator is needed to guide focus and lead the debrief.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator gets two completely unrelated suggestions for Scene A and Scene B.
2. Both scenes begin simultaneously. The facilitator points to Scene A to give them vocal focus. Scene A speaks and acts normally, while Scene B mimes their scene silently.
3. Players in the vocal scene must maintain peripheral awareness of the miming scene.
4. A player in the miming scene performs a clear, significant physical action (the ripple) such as tripping, shivering, or pantomiming a heavy lift.
5. The vocal scene must instantly notice this physical offer and organically justify it into their own environment without breaking reality. For example, if the miming scene drops a heavy object, the vocal scene might react to a sudden earthquake or turbulence.
6. The facilitator periodically calls 'Focus Scene B!'. Scene A immediately drops to silent mime, and Scene B takes over vocal dialogue, now tasked with justifying Scene A's physical ripples.
7. The exercise flows continuously without stopping for the ripples, ending with a facilitator debrief on the most successful justifications.

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage players to maintain high-level peripheral awareness and split focus.
- Remind the miming scene to provide clear, actionable triggers for physical justification.
- Ensure strict adherence to the vocal focus rule to eliminate the wall of noise; only one scene speaks at a time.
- The goal is the group's success in seamlessly integrating the physical offers, not competing.

## Variations
- Emotional Ripples: Instead of physical actions, the miming scene adopts a sudden, intense emotion, which the vocal scene must organically adopt or justify.
- Three-Ring Circus: Add a third scene for advanced players, requiring the vocal scene to monitor two miming scenes simultaneously.

## Why It Works
It gamifies 'yes, and' by forcing players to accept external, abstract physical offers. This develops high-level peripheral awareness, split focus, and the ability to organically justify unexpected inputs without breaking the reality of the scene.

## Safety & Inclusion
Players must ensure physical ripples (like falling or tripping) are done safely using proper stage combat or mime techniques. The facilitator should ensure that the physical offers remain abstract and do not force the receiving scene into non-consensual or inappropriate narratives. All physical choices must be safe for the players' bodies and mobility levels.

# Momentum Shift

![Momentum Shift — infographic](img/ISR248.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must instantly justify rapid-fire, pre-planned narrative shifts called out by a Referee without losing the scene's core objective.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two players build a scene while a Referee rapidly calls out pre-planned, narrative-altering shifts. Players must instantly justify and integrate these sudden changes without breaking character or losing the scene's core objective.

## Setup
Two players from the active team take the stage. The Referee gets a baseline suggestion from the audience (e.g., a mundane location and a relationship) and has a pre-written list of 5 to 7 specific shifts ready to go (e.g., 'Gravity is reversed,' 'This is a Shakespearean tragedy'). No mid-scene audience suggestions are taken to ensure absolute momentum.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a location and relationship from the audience to start the scene.
2. The two players begin the scene, establishing their characters, relationship, and a clear objective within the first 15 seconds.
3. The Referee blows a whistle or yells 'SHIFT!' followed immediately by a pre-planned complication (e.g., 'SHIFT: You are suddenly in a Western!').
4. Players must instantly 'Yes, And' the shift, justifying it within the reality of the scene rather than just playing it as a random gimmick. The core relationship and objective must continue, now filtered through this new lens.
5. The Referee calls 3 to 5 shifts over the course of a 2-to-3 minute scene, pacing them to escalate the narrative tension.
6. If a player hesitates, breaks character, or drops the core narrative to just play the gimmick, the Referee can call a 'Hesitation Foul' and deduct a point.
7. The scene concludes on a high-energy peak. The opposing team then plays their round with a new starting suggestion and a fresh list of shifts.
8. At the end of each team's scene, the Referee evaluates the scene as a whole, awarding up to 5 points based on narrative continuity, instant justification of shifts, and audience reaction. Standard short-form fouls (content foul, groaner) apply.

## Coaching Notes
- Pre-planned shifts maintain high-speed momentum without stopping for audience input.
- Encourage aggressive 'Yes, And' and immediate justification of absurd elements.
- Remind players of narrative endurance: they must keep the story moving despite constant disruptions.
- Ensure players do not drop the core narrative just to play the gimmick.
- Zero props or setup required, making it highly accessible and fast to stage.

## Variations
- Emotional Shift: Instead of genres or physical changes, all shifts are sudden emotional states or relationship reveals (e.g., 'SHIFT: You are deeply suspicious of them'). Great for practicing character focus.
- The Gauntlet (Solo Shift): One player stays on stage while different teammates tag in, each bringing a new, bizarre shift that the solo player must instantly justify and adapt to.

## Why It Works
It forces aggressive agreement and immediate justification of absurd elements, testing narrative endurance by making players keep the story moving despite constant disruptions.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee acts as a strict content filter, ensuring all pre-planned shifts are safe, inclusive, and all-ages appropriate. Physical shifts (e.g., 'Gravity is heavy') must be played safely without risking injury or forcing unwanted physical contact. The content foul instantly stops and corrects any inappropriate content.

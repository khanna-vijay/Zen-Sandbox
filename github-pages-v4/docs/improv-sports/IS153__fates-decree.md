# Fates' Decree

![Fates' Decree — infographic](img/IS153.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players collaboratively construct a dynamic story while seamlessly integrating sudden, pre-ordained plot twists revealed by the Moderator.

## Overview
Fates' Decree is an improvisational game where two teams collaboratively construct a dynamic story, guided by a Moderator who periodically reveals Decree Cards containing sudden, impactful plot twists. Players on stage must immediately and seamlessly integrate each new Decree into their ongoing narrative. The game emphasizes real-time adaptation, collaborative scene-building, and embracing unexpected turns to build a coherent arc despite radical shifts.

## Setup
You need two teams of 3-5 players, a Moderator (the 'Bearer of Fates'), a timer, and a deck of 5-7 pre-written Decree Cards containing open-ended plot points (e.g., 'A natural disaster strikes' or 'They learn of a prophecy'). Get a genre and an initial setting or premise from the audience to begin.

## How to Play
1. Two improvisers from the first team take the stage and begin a scene based on the audience suggestions.
2. At intervals of about 90-120 seconds, the Moderator publicly reveals one Decree Card so both the improvisers and the audience can see it.
3. The improvisers on stage must immediately and visibly integrate this new plot point into the ongoing scene without pausing or starting a new conversation.
4. One improviser initiates the integration, and their partner must actively 'Yes, And' it to confirm the new reality and build upon its implications.
5. After successfully integrating the Decree and showing its immediate consequences, the team tags out the current players, and at least one new improviser enters to pick up the story exactly from that new point.
6. Continue revealing Decree Cards and tagging players until all cards are used or the time limit expires, then bring the story to a natural conclusion.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: Focus on continuous story progression, challenging players to construct a cohesive narrative under constant duress.
- Embracing Mistakes: Transform potential inconsistencies into narrative opportunities. If a Decree contradicts an earlier fact, brilliantly justify it rather than ignoring it.
- Collaborative Scene-building: The improviser who initiates the integration depends entirely on their partner to legitimize the new plot point. Make your partner look good.
- Being Changed: Each Decree should fundamentally alter character objectives, relationships, or stakes. Allow your characters to be changed by these new circumstances.
- Listening and Presence: Performers must be acutely present to both their scene partner and the unexpected external input of the Decree Card.

## Variations
- Competitive Scoring: Play as a judged competitive round where teams are scored 0-5 on Decree Integration, Narrative Cohesion, Risk-Taking, Collaborative Play, and Audience Engagement.

## Why It Works
It forces continuous, dynamic story progression and tests primary improvisational skills like adaptation and flexibility. Performers must instantaneously adapt their understanding of the scene, character motivations, and narrative direction based on the Decree, taking 'Yes, And' to an intensified level.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the pre-written Decree Cards avoid triggering or overly sensitive topics unless previously agreed upon by all players. Players should maintain physical safety and respect boundaries during sudden dramatic shifts.

# Context Quake!

![Context Quake! — infographic](img/IS217.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two to three improvisers must hilariously and inventively justify radical, audience-imposed shifts in their ongoing scene's reality.

## Overview
Context Quake! is a high-energy improv game where two to three improvisers begin a grounded scene, only for a host to periodically declare a "Context Quake!" This forces players to instantly and hilariously justify radical, audience-imposed shifts in their scene's genre, character relationships, or location. The improvisers must seamlessly integrate these bewildering new realities into their ongoing narrative, proving their agility in adapting and "yes, and-ing" extreme changes, leading to progressively absurd and comedic scenarios.

## Setup
3 improvisers on stage for the scene. A Host/Referee is essential for calling the "Context Quake!" and soliciting audience suggestions. Props/Set are minimal to none. Use three large whiteboards, flip charts, or index cards held by the host where distinct categories for audience suggestions are clearly visible: 1. Genre/Style Shift, 2. Fundamental Relationship Shift, 3. Location/Environmental Shift.

## How to Play
1. The host asks the audience for a mundane scene suggestion (e.g., a first date, two people buying a car). Players immediately start a grounded, realistic scene based on this suggestion, establishing characters and a situation.
2. At any point the host deems fit (typically after 30-60 seconds of initial scene play), they will dramatically yell, "CONTEXT QUAKE!"
3. Players must immediately freeze (or acknowledge the seismic shift physically/verbally by briefly gasping or showing disorientation), but must remain in character.
4. The host quickly turns to the audience, pointing to one of the three category boards (Genre, Relationship, or Location). They solicit a specific, unique suggestion from the audience within that chosen category.
5. The moment the host announces the new suggestion, the players must immediately, in character, justify why this radical shift has just occurred within the reality of their ongoing scene. They cannot simply start a new scene or ignore what happened previously.
6. Their dialogue, actions, and character choices must instantly adapt to the new context. The connection to the original scene must remain, but now framed through the new context.
7. The scene continues in the new, bizarre context until the host calls another "CONTEXT QUAKE!" This cycle repeats typically 3-4 times within a single game, layering new realities onto the old.
8. Scoring (Competitive Match Style): A referee or judge makes final scoring decisions. Points are awarded for a clear initial scene, a great audience suggestion, creative justification (5 Points Max), seamless adaptation (5 Points Max), and heightening the humor (3 Points Max).

## Coaching Notes
- The goal of the initial scene is clarity and listen-ability to set up for the coming chaos.
- The best justifications are surprising, illogical yet somehow internally consistent, and immediately comedic.
- Players score higher for making strong, specific choices that build on both the previous scene and the new quake.
- Look for opportunities for specific character details, physical comedy, or callbacks that heighten the absurdity of the quakes.

## Why It Works
It draws inspiration from the quick-witted adaptability of rapid-fire replacement games and character-guessing games, the genre-shifting chaos of style-switching games, and the "yes, and" principle amplified to an extreme. It directly targets the improviser's ability to not just accept new information, but to instantly justify it within their current scene's narrative, no matter how outlandish. It rigorously tests an improviser's ability to "yes, and," justify, and pivot dramatically, rewarding quick thinking and imaginative leaps.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during sudden freezes or disorientation reactions. Players should respect physical boundaries and avoid aggressive or non-consensual physical contact when rapidly shifting relationships or genres.

# Vibe Check

![Vibe Check — infographic](img/ISR081.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two opposing genres or emotional tones battle for dominance as audience cheers dictate instant, wild tonal shifts.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where two opposing genres or emotional tones battle for dominance. At the Referee's whistle, the audience votes by cheering to dictate which 'vibe' the improvisers must instantly adopt, forcing players to seamlessly justify wild tonal shifts while keeping the scene's narrative alive.

## Setup
Two to four players and one Referee. No tech required. The Referee gets a base scene suggestion (location or relationship) from the audience, plus two highly contrasting 'Vibes' (e.g., Film Noir vs. Muppet Movie, or Deeply Tragic vs. Slapstick Comedy).

## How to Play
1. The Referee introduces the scene's base suggestion and the two opposing Vibes.
2. The players begin the scene in a completely neutral, grounded tone.
3. After 30 to 45 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Vibe Check!'. The scene instantly freezes.
4. The Referee points to the audience and asks them to cheer for Vibe A, then Vibe B. The Referee judges which cheer was louder.
5. The Referee announces the winning Vibe (e.g., 'The audience demands Slapstick!'). The Referee blows the whistle to unfreeze the scene.
6. The players must instantly resume the scene, completely committing to the new Vibe while justifying the sudden transition within the existing narrative.
7. The Referee repeats this process 3 to 4 times, decreasing the time between Vibe Checks to escalate the pacing.
8. The scene ends on a high-energy climax or a final, rapid-fire Vibe Check.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee awards 1 to 3 points for seamless justifications, strong physical and vocal commitment to the new vibe, and maintaining the core story.
- Fouls (like the 'Waffling Foul' or 'Delay of Game') can be called if players hesitate, drop the narrative, or fail to commit to the winning vibe.
- Ensure clear, discrete shifts that allow players to focus entirely on their scene partners.
- Build escalation as the intervals between Vibe Checks get shorter.

## Variations
- Emotion Check: Instead of genres, use two opposing core emotions (e.g., Uncontrollable Rage vs. Profound Apathy).
- The Vibe Dial: For advanced players, the Referee acts as a human dial, holding out an arm. As the Ref moves their arm left or right, the players must blend the two vibes on a spectrum (e.g., 70 percent Noir, 30 percent Muppet) without stopping the scene.

## Why It Works
It tests players' adaptability with high-stakes, instant tonal pivots. The zero-tech audience polling via classic cheer-offs keeps the audience highly engaged, while the discrete shifts force players to seamlessly justify wild transitions and maintain the core story.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure suggested vibes are genres or emotions, strictly avoiding accents, cultural stereotypes, or physical conditions. Remind players that the 'intensity' of a vibe does not mean physical danger; players must maintain physical boundaries and safe stage combat practices during sudden, high-energy transitions.

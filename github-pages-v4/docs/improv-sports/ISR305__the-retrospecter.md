# The Retrospecter

![The Retrospecter — infographic](img/ISR305.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players replay a grounded scene from the exact same starting point, but a newly revealed audience secret completely alters the subtext and narrative.

## Overview
Players perform a short, grounded scene. The host then stops the action and gets a secret or hidden truth from the audience. The players restart the scene from their exact starting physical positions and opening line, but let the new secret completely alter the subtext, relationship, and narrative direction of the scene.

## Setup
Two to three players and a host/facilitator. No props or set pieces are required. Best played in a competitive short-form match or narrative long-form format with a panel of judges.

## How to Play
1. The host gets a basic suggestion from the audience (e.g., a location or relationship) and the players perform a grounded, realistic scene for exactly 1.5 minutes.
2. The host calls 'Freeze!' The players immediately stop and hold their physical positions.
3. The host asks the audience for a secret or hidden truth that one or both characters have been hiding during that exact scene (e.g., 'Player A is secretly an undercover cop,' or 'Player B is in love with Player A').
4. The host gives the players a 10-second 'Processing Pause' to internalize how this new truth changes their character's internal motivation and perspective.
5. The players reset to their exact starting physical positions and repeat the exact first line of the original scene.
6. From the second line onward, the scene is free to diverge entirely. Players do not need to hit the same plot beats; instead, they let the new subtext drive a completely new 2-minute scene.

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage players to integrate the secret into their subtext, physical choices, and emotional reactions, rewarding subtle character work over just blurting out the secret.
- In a competitive short-form format, judges should score (1-5 points) based on this subtle integration of the new truth.
- Treat the audience as 'narrative detectives,' engaging them in watching how the exact same starting point blossoms into a totally different story based on their suggestion.
- Remind players that they do not need to hit the same plot beats in the replay; the new subtext should drive a completely new narrative trajectory.

## Variations
- The Silent Secret: The host whispers the audience's secret to only one player. The other player (and the judges) must figure out what the secret is based purely on the first player's new subtext and behavioral changes.
- Genre Shift: Instead of a secret, the audience suggests a specific genre (e.g., Film Noir, Horror) for the replay. The players restart the scene, changing the stylistic subtext and tone rather than the narrative truth.

## Why It Works
It trains players to play the 'underneath' of a scene rather than just the surface plot, demonstrating how a single piece of hidden information can completely change a narrative trajectory using zero props and relying entirely on character work, emotional intelligence, and relationship dynamics.

## Safety & Inclusion
When soliciting secrets from the audience, the host must actively filter out suggestions that rely on harmful stereotypes, trauma, or non-consensual dynamics. Because the scene restarts with heightened emotional stakes, players should rely on eye contact, vocal tone, and emotional intensity rather than uninvited physical escalation, respecting all pre-established physical boundaries.

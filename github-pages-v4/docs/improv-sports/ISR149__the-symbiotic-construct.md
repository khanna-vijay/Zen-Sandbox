# The Symbiotic Construct

![The Symbiotic Construct — infographic](img/ISR149.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players close their eyes and work in complete silence to collaboratively build a tactile sculpture out of diverse objects.

## Overview
A seated, non-verbal, eyes-closed exercise where small pods of 2-3 players collaborate to build a tactile sculpture out of diverse objects. By removing sight and speech, participants must rely entirely on physical listening, shared touch, and kinesthetic empathy to create a unified structure, turning a quiet sensory exercise into an engaging, joyful puzzle.

## Setup
Divide the group into pods of 2-3 players. Seat them around a small table (preferred for accessibility) or in a tight circle on the floor. Place a pile of safe, highly tactile objects (wooden blocks, large building blocks, fabric scraps, smooth stones, sponges) in the center. The facilitator leads a strict boundary and consent check-in regarding hand and arm touching.

## How to Play
1. Consent & Comfort Check: Players state their boundaries for hand/arm contact. Facilitators ensure the seating arrangement works for all mobility needs.
2. Sensory Shift: Players close their eyes (or look down to avoid visual input) and commit to complete silence.
3. The Prompt: The facilitator gives a specific, fun building prompt (e.g., 'Build the tallest stable tower,' 'Build a bridge between your two sides,' or 'Build a creature').
4. Establishing Contact & Building: Players slowly reach into the center. They may maintain gentle physical contact with partners (e.g., the back of a hand resting on a wrist) to sense movement, or simply feel the objects. They collaboratively build the prompt without speaking.
5. Physical Listening: If one player initiates placing an object, the others support and respond to that physical impulse. Players must 'listen' to the structure's balance and their partners' intentions without forcing their own agenda.
6. The Reveal: After 3-5 minutes, the facilitator calls time. Players open their eyes for a 'Ta-Da!' moment to observe their emergent (and often hilarious) sculpture, followed by a brief debrief on how they communicated.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach the energy: 'Breathe together. Feel the weight. If you feel someone pulling away, let them lead.'
- If the structure falls, celebrate the crash and rebuild!
- Maintain a clear point of concentration isolating the tactile senses.
- Lead a debrief focusing on yielding, leading, and following without words.

## Variations
- The Assembly Line (No-Touch): Players are not allowed to touch each other, only the objects. They must sense what their partners are doing purely by feeling the structure itself as it grows, making it a great option for touch-averse groups.
- The Secret Blueprint: One player keeps their eyes open as the 'Architect' but cannot touch the objects or speak words. They guide the blind 'Builders' using only abstract sounds (hums, clicks, gasps) to create a specific shape or structure.

## Why It Works
By removing sight and speech, participants must rely entirely on physical listening, shared touch, and kinesthetic empathy. It isolates the tactile senses, teaching players to yield, lead, and follow without words while solving an engaging puzzle.

## Safety & Inclusion
Highly accessible: best played at a table to accommodate wheelchairs and avoid floor-sitting issues. Use larger objects (pillows, big blocks, pool noodles) for players with limited fine motor skills. Mandatory consent check-in before play for hand/arm touching; players uncomfortable with touch can use the 'No-Touch' variation or act as the Architect. Blindfolds are strictly optional; simply closing eyes is trauma-informed and safer.

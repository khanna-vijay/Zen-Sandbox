# Momentum Mosaic

![Momentum Mosaic — infographic](img/ISR165.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass abstract physical and vocal energy around a circle to train full-body listening and radical presentness.

## Overview
Momentum Mosaic is a closed-workshop ensemble exercise that isolates pure non-verbal impulse and response. Players pass abstract physical and vocal energy around a circle, focusing on fully receiving an offer, letting it physically change them, and spontaneously sending a new impulse to someone else. It trains full-body listening and radical presentness without the pressure of narrative or dialogue.

## Setup
Players stand in a wide circle in a closed workshop or rehearsal space. No props or stage setup are required. The facilitator explains that the goal is to pass energy around the circle using only sound and movement, with absolutely no physical contact. The Point of Concentration is to let the received impulse physically affect your body before you send a new one.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator starts the game by establishing a clear time limit, typically 3 to 5 minutes, to keep the energy focused.
2. Player A makes strong eye contact with any other player in the circle (Player B) and sends a non-verbal impulse across the space. For example, Player A might make a loud 'Whoosh' sound while throwing their arms forward.
3. Player B receives the impulse and must let it physically affect them. If the impulse was a 'Whoosh', Player B might rock backward on their heels as if hit by a gust of wind.
4. Without overthinking, Player B immediately transforms that energy into a completely new physical and vocal impulse, makes eye contact with Player C, and sends it across the circle.
5. Players must maintain their physical distance. All focus and energy are passed using eye contact, voice, and body language from across the room, ensuring no personal space is violated.
6. When the time limit approaches, the facilitator calls for a 'Lightning Round' where impulses are passed as quickly as possible for 30 seconds, followed by a final 'Freeze!' to end the game.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach to encourage full-body reactions and prevent players from pre-planning their moves.
- Use phrases like 'Let it land!' or 'Don't think, just react!' to keep players in the moment.
- Focus on the ensemble's connection, speed of response, and commitment to the physical impulses.

## Variations
- Mirror Mosaic: Instead of immediately transforming the impulse, the receiving player must first exactly mirror the sound and movement they received, and then morph it into a new impulse to pass along. This ensures players are truly observing the details of the offer.
- Emotion Mosaic: The facilitator calls out specific emotions (e.g., joy, panic, exhaustion) every 60 seconds. The physical and vocal impulses passed must be fueled by that specific emotional state, helping players practice emotional agility.

## Why It Works
By isolating pure non-verbal impulse and response, the game forces players to rely on full-body listening and allows them to be genuinely changed by an offer. This builds ensemble trust and radical presentness without the pressure of narrative or dialogue.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical contact is strictly prohibited; all energy is passed across the empty space of the circle. Players are encouraged to use whatever range of motion is comfortable and accessible to them. A subtle shift in posture or a quiet vocalization is just as valid as a large, athletic movement, making the game fully accessible to players of all physical abilities.

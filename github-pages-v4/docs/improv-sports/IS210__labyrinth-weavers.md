# Labyrinth Weavers

![Labyrinth Weavers — infographic](img/IS210.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two Weavers collaboratively construct a narrative that is constantly and arbitrarily reshaped by an omniscient Arbiter.

## Overview
Labyrinth Weavers is an improvisational game where two Weavers collaboratively construct a narrative that is constantly and arbitrarily reshaped by an omniscient Arbiter. The Arbiter intervenes at strategic moments to rectify perceived narrative stumbles, heighten emotional stakes, or introduce sudden environmental shifts by declaring new, unchallengeable truths. The Weavers' core challenge is to immediately accept and seamlessly integrate these often contradictory realities into their developing story.

## Setup
A clear, open performance space. The Arbiter stands visibly but discreetly off to the side of the stage, perhaps with a small bell or buzzer, and a collection of random, simple props nearby (e.g., a feather, a toy car, a book, a hat).

## How to Play
1. The MC receives a single audience suggestion (e.g., a location, a relationship, or an emotional state).
2. The two Weavers immediately begin a scene, establishing a platform (characters, relationship, initial environment) and initiating a clear conflict or objective early on.
3. The Arbiter listens intensely and intervenes at opportune moments by ringing their bell/buzzer and delivering a concise, declarative statement (typically 3-8 words) that acts as an absolute, unchallengeable truth.
4. If a Weaver makes a 'mistake' (hesitates, contradicts a fact, or creates an awkward silence), the Arbiter interjects to resolve it by making it a new, absurd truth or twisting it into a plot development.
5. If the scene stalls or relies on simple gags, the Arbiter intervenes to force 'Horizontal Heightening' (e.g., surprising backstories, external ripple effects) or 'Vertical Heightening' (e.g., escalating emotional stakes, extreme physical actions).
6. At any time, the Arbiter can introduce dynamic environmental shifts, such as placing a physical prop into the scene, adding a sound/light cue, or declaring a global reality change.
7. Upon any Arbiter intervention, the Weavers must immediately accept and integrate the statement as fundamental, undisputed truth without questioning or breaking character.
8. The Weavers must justify the new reality through dialogue, physicality, and emotional responses, finding the internal logic within the shifting reality.
9. After a set time (e.g., 4-6 minutes), the MC calls 'Time!' to end the game.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development over Simple Gags: The Arbiter's continuous interventions force narrative turns and depth. Performers cannot rely on static premises; they are compelled to continuously invent and justify complex story threads.
- Embracing Mistakes: This game explicitly redefines 'mistakes' (hesitation, contradiction) not as failures, but as direct catalysts for narrative innovation. Use the Arbiter to train improvisers to view unexpected occurrences as exciting new offers.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: The rapid shifts necessitate constant 'Yes, And' between the Weavers. They must keenly observe each other's reactions and build on both the Arbiter's and their partner's immediate interpretations.
- Being Changed: Performers are constantly forced to adapt their characters' internal states, physical realities, objectives, and relationships based on external, arbitrary dictates.
- Physicality and Status Play: Environmental shifts, new character traits, or introduced props directly demand creative physical responses and immediate status negotiation.

## Why It Works
The game forces extreme adaptability and tight collaborative 'Yes, And'-ing under immense pressure. By using an Arbiter to inject arbitrary reality shifts and reframe mistakes as narrative catalysts, players learn to abandon static premises, embrace being changed, and continuously justify complex, evolving story threads.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Arbiter should ensure their interventions, while challenging, do not force players into physically unsafe situations or cross personal boundaries. Players must maintain physical awareness and consent during sudden environmental or physical shifts.

# The Weave

![The Weave — infographic](img/ISR193.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A silent, timed ensemble-building exercise where players collaboratively build an abstract structure and immediately inhabit it.

## Overview
A silent, timed ensemble-building exercise where players use a single type of object to collaboratively build an abstract structure. They then immediately inhabit it as the set for a silent scene.

## Setup
Clear the room of all obstacles. Gather a large quantity of a single type of everyday object (e.g., 15-20 chairs, or 30 empty cardboard boxes). Place them in a pile at the edge of the space. The facilitator needs a timer.

## How to Play
1. Phase 1: The Build (3 Minutes). The facilitator starts the timer. In complete silence, players move into the space and begin arranging the objects.
2. Players must build upon, connect to, or adapt what others are doing. The goal is to create one unified, abstract structure or landscape, letting go of individual plans and responding to the group's emerging pattern.
3. Phase 2: The Scene (2 Minutes). When the 3-minute timer sounds, building immediately stops. The facilitator calls 'Inhabit!'
4. Without speaking or planning, the ensemble must step into the structure and use it as a set for a silent scene. They must intuitively agree on what the environment is (e.g., a spaceship, a trench, a busy office) based purely on how they physically interact with the objects and each other.
5. Phase 3: The Debrief. After 2 minutes, the facilitator calls 'Scene.' The group briefly discusses what the environment became, how they discovered it together, and how it felt to build without speaking.

## Coaching Notes
- Remind players that this is non-competitive with no points, judges, or audiences; the win is the shared discovery and ego-free collaboration.
- Enforce the strict time limits to force quicker intuitive choices and prevent the exercise from dragging.
- Encourage players to let go of their individual plans and respond to the group's emerging pattern.
- Ensure the single-object constraint is maintained to reduce setup friction and complexity.

## Variations
- Soundscape Weave: During the 'Scene' phase, players may use non-verbal sound effects (humming, clicking, whooshing) to help define the environment, though words are still forbidden.
- Turn-Based Build: Instead of a free-for-all, players take turns placing exactly one object at a time in a rapid-fire sequence until the 3 minutes are up, increasing focus on individual contributions.
- Micro Weave: Use small objects (like shoes, books, or markers) on a table. The 'Scene' phase involves players using their hands/fingers as characters navigating the miniature set.

## Why It Works
It fosters deep non-verbal listening and ensemble awareness while completely removing performance pressure and ego. The strict time limits force quicker intuitive choices, and the single-object constraint drastically reduces setup friction.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the objects used are safe, lightweight, and clean. Explicitly instruct players not to build structures higher than shoulder height to prevent collapse injuries. The game is highly inclusive, as it requires no spoken language, and players can contribute to the build and scene at whatever physical level or pace is comfortable for their mobility.

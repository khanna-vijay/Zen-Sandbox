# The Rival Director

![The Rival Director — infographic](img/ISR012.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An opposing director calls out sudden narrative shifts to challenge improvisers in a collaborative scene.

## Overview
Two teams collaborate on a single narrative scene, but a Director from the opposing team calls out sudden narrative shifts like Genre, Time Jump, Plot Twist, or Character Focus. The improvisers must instantly justify the new reality while keeping the story alive.

## Setup
Requires 4 to 6 players (2 to 3 per team), 1 Host, and a panel of Judges. Set up a standard stage with a designated Director's Chair in the downstage corner. Get a relationship and a mundane location from the audience to start.

## How to Play
1. The Host gets a relationship and a location from the audience to ground the scene.
2. Two players from Team A start the scene. They must establish the base reality, characters, and relationship clearly.
3. A player from Team B stands downstage as the Rival Director. At any point, they loudly call out one of four specific shifts: Genre, Time Jump, Plot Twist, or Focus.
4. The active players must immediately Yes, And the call, altering their behavior, status, or the narrative without dropping the core story.
5. After 2 to 3 successful shifts, the Host calls Switch! The Team A actors freeze and exit.
6. Two Team B actors immediately step into their exact physical positions and take over the exact same characters, keeping names, relationships, and current stakes.
7. A player from Team A now becomes the new Rival Director, throwing shifts at Team B.
8. After another 2 to 3 shifts, the Host calls Scene! to end the game. Judges award points based on narrative commitment and seamless justification.

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage aggressive Yes-Anding; actors must justify the curveballs seamlessly without breaking character.
- Remind the incoming team during the switch to maintain the exact physical positions, character names, relationships, and stakes.
- The Rival Director should aim to challenge the actors but still allow the core story to progress.
- Keep both teams actively engaged and listening even when they are offstage.

## Variations
- Internal Director: A non-competitive version where a single team's own director tries to help them build the best story possible.
- Audience Director: Instead of an opposing team member, the Host takes suggestions for shifts from the audience and calls them out during the scene.

## Why It Works
It forces rapid adaptation and aggressive Yes-Anding, testing narrative resilience and character consistency across team switches while keeping both teams actively engaged.

## Safety & Inclusion
Rival Directors must respect physical and content boundaries; no calls that force intimate contact, physical danger, or violate the show's rating. Players can adapt physical calls to their own mobility levels. If a call crosses a line, the Host can immediately veto it with a verbal 'Try again'.

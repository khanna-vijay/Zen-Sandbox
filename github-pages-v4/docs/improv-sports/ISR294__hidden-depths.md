# Hidden Depths

![Hidden Depths — infographic](img/ISR294.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers build a scene while integrating secret character traits drawn from their pockets on a sound cue.

## Overview
Hidden Depths is a facilitator-led training game where performers build a scene while integrating secret character traits drawn from their pockets on a sound cue. Players must instantly absorb a 1-4 word secret and let it alter their behavior without explicitly stating it. Meanwhile, the audience or class is shown the secret, creating delicious dramatic irony as the scene partners work to observe, justify, and Yes-And the sudden shifts in each other's demeanor.

## Setup
Prepare a deck of Depth Cards with extremely brief, 1-4 word secrets (e.g., Secretly a ghost, Desperately need validation, Stole their wallet, Holding in pee). Create a way for the audience or class to see the secrets simultaneously, such as a slide deck, a whiteboard, or the facilitator holding up duplicate oversized cards. Give each player 3-4 random cards to place in their pockets before the scene begins. Designate a clear sound cue, like a bell or a clap, that signals a player to draw and read a card.

## How to Play
1. Two players take the stage. The facilitator gets an audience suggestion for a grounded relationship and location to anchor the scene.
2. The players begin a standard, relationship-focused scene, establishing their baseline dynamic.
3. After 30 to 45 seconds, the facilitator triggers a sound cue (e.g., a bell) and points to one player.
4. The designated player immediately pulls one card from their pocket, reads the 1-4 word prompt in a split second, and puts it away. Simultaneously, the facilitator reveals the exact same prompt to the audience or class.
5. The player must instantly let this new secret alter their subtext, physicality, and emotional state. They must NOT explicitly state the secret, but rather let it drive their choices and reactions.
6. The scene partner, who does not know the secret, must keenly observe this behavioral shift and Yes-And it, justifying the new behavior within the reality of the scene.
7. The facilitator repeats the cue 2-3 times throughout the 3-minute scene, alternating players or layering multiple secrets onto one player, escalating the complexity until calling Scene!
8. At the end of the scene, the facilitator asks the scene partner to guess what the exact cards were, serving as a debrief on how clearly the subtext was played and observed.

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage players to play subtext and internal motivations rather than relying on superficial gags.
- Remind the scene partner to actively listen, intensely observe, and justify the new behavior within the reality of the scene (e.g., You seem awfully twitchy all of a sudden, are you worried about the audit?).
- Ensure players do not explicitly state their secret; the goal is to let it alter their behavior and be changed by the new information.

## Variations
- Double Blind: The facilitator rings the bell and BOTH players draw a card simultaneously, forcing them to navigate their own sudden shift while simultaneously dealing with their partner's change.
- Emotion Only: Instead of secrets, the cards contain only a single strong emotion (e.g., Furious, Devastated, Overjoyed). This isolates the skill of emotional transformation and justification.

## Why It Works
It trains active listening and intense observation of scene partners. It forces players to play subtext and internal motivations, teaching the vital improv skill of being changed by new information. It also creates instant dramatic irony, making it highly engaging for the observing class or audience.

## Safety & Inclusion
Keep card prompts clean, all-ages, and free from trauma triggers (avoid themes of assault, self-harm, or phobias that might genuinely distress players). Implement a Discard Rule: if a player pulls a card they are uncomfortable playing, they can instantly drop it and pull another without penalty or explanation. For players without pockets or with mobility needs, cards can be placed face-down on a small table within easy reach.

# Chrono-Flux

![Chrono-Flux — infographic](img/IS032.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers collaboratively build a scene until a player yells FLUX! to rewind a crucial narrative moment and declare a radical alteration that everyone must immediately integrate.

## Overview
Chrono-Flux is an improvisational game where performers collaboratively build a scene until a player can FLUX! a crucial narrative moment, rewinding it to just before that point. The caller then declares a specific, radical alteration to an element like character intent, an action's meaning, or new information. All players must immediately accept and integrate this new truth into the resumed scene.

## Setup
Typically played with 2-3 improvisers on stage. A neutral stage setup, possibly with a few movable blocks or a simple prop box. A judge or MC on the side, holding a Flux Card (or a small gong or bell) indicating when the flux mechanism can be activated.

## How to Play
1. Scene Start: The scene begins as normal, with audience suggestions for a location, relationship, and/or a trigger word or first line.
2. The Build-Up: Performers develop the scene, characters, and narrative arc naturally, focusing on honest connection, objectives, and building story.
3. The Flux Point (Player Activation): At any point, a performer who feels a crucial, high-stakes moment has just occurred (a significant line, a key action, a pivotal decision, an emotional outburst) can yell FLUX! The judge signals 'active' (e.g., holding the Flux Card up) during the initial call.
4. The Rewind: Upon hearing FLUX!, all players immediately rewind their physicality and dialogue back to just before the significant line/action that triggered the call. This rewind should be physical, audible (e.g., playback sounds), and clear for the audience.
5. The Alteration Imperative: The performer who called FLUX! must then declare one specific, crucial element that must now change about that moment or its immediate consequence (e.g., character intention, meaning of an action, or new information).
6. Scene Resumption (Altered Reality): The scene proceeds from this altered moment, with all players immediately accepting the new truth imposed by the alteration. They must Yes, And not just the offer, but the change itself, letting it ripple through their character's objectives and relationships.
7. Flux Card Limit: A team can only call FLUX! a maximum of three times per scene, and only when the judge/MC signals the Flux Card is available (e.g., 30-second cooldown between calls to prevent excessive use).
8. Scene End: The scene continues until the MC calls time, or the improvisers naturally bring it to a compelling conclusion.

## Coaching Notes
- Embrace Mistakes and Unexpected Turns: The specified alteration acts as a controlled, high-stakes mistake or gift that must be accepted. Performers must release their attachment to previous narrative choices and immediately pivot.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: When one player introduces an alteration, the other players must instantly comprehend and build upon this new reality. It demands hyper-attentive listening, immediate acceptance, and empathetic understanding.
- Being Changed: Performers must allow their characters and their own improvisational choices to be radically altered by the FLUX! and its imperative. Their status, objectives, and emotional state must shift authentically.
- Listening and Status Play: Critical for identifying the Flux Point and internalizing the specific Alteration Imperative. An alteration can instantly invert or shift status dynamics, requiring quick and believable adjustments.
- Objective Work: Characters' objectives can be completely re-defined by a FLUX!, requiring performers to adapt their focus and drive instantly.

## Why It Works
This game is a narrative crucible. Each FLUX! actively forces the story into a new, often deeper or more complex, direction. It explores 'what if' scenarios within a single, unfolding story, pushing beyond linear plot to examine cause, effect, and alternative outcomes. It promotes complex character arcs by suddenly re-contextualizing past actions or intentions.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical rewinds are done safely without risking injury, especially if the Flux Point occurred during a moment of high physical action. Players should respect physical boundaries when altering relationship dynamics.

# The Relic Ripple

![The Relic Ripple — infographic](img/IS204.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players collectively build an evolving, non-verbal emotional narrative around a single, unremarkable object.

## Overview
The Relic Ripple is a novel improv game where players collectively build an evolving, non-verbal emotional narrative around a single, unremarkable object. Inspired by Hot Potato and object transformation, participants sit in a circle, passing a Relic. Each player non-verbally imbues the object with an emotion or implied history through their physical interaction, then conveys that accumulated weight through the manner of the pass.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle. A single, common, and unremarkable object is chosen (e.g., a smooth stone, a simple key, a small piece of wood, a plastic bottle cap). This object is designated 'The Relic'.

## How to Play
1. One player volunteers to start. They take The Relic and physically imbue it with an unspoken emotion, a memory, or the resonance of a significant past event entirely non-verbally through subtle physical posture, facial expression, and delicate manipulation.
2. The initiating player passes The Relic to the person immediately to their left or right. They attempt to convey the emotional weight and implied history through the manner of the pass itself (speed, tension, delicacy, reluctance, or urgency).
3. The receiving player intuitively perceives and accepts the emotional charge, physically reflecting their gut understanding of its current state or history as they receive it.
4. The new player internalizes the perceived history and adds their own layer to The Relic's accumulating emotional journey through their unique physical interaction, still entirely non-verbally.
5. This continuous flow proceeds around the circle. Each player receives, interprets, internalizes, amplifies or transforms, and passes The Relic.
6. Absolutely no verbal communication about the story, emotion, or interpretation is allowed at any point.
7. After several rounds, or when a distinct narrative arc emerges, stop the game for an optional debrief where players share what they felt The Relic represented or the story they imagined.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Intuitively receive the unspoken emotional/historical weight of The Relic from the manner of its pass, and authentically contribute a new, layered chapter solely through physical interaction and the energy of the subsequent pass.
- Focus on how the 'relic' is experiencing the transfer, not just handing over an object.
- Bypass intellectual analysis and self-censorship; rely purely on gut feelings and physical impulses to enable spontaneous, authentic behavior.
- Remember that each contribution, regardless of interpretation, becomes a valid part of the ongoing ripple, removing the pressure of getting it wrong.

## Why It Works
It develops intuitive and embodied storytelling, empathic resonance, and the ability to read subtext. By strictly banning verbalization, it forces players to bypass intellect and rely on physical impulses, deepening object relationship and ensemble listening while proving that everyone can act without traditional performance pressure.

## Safety & Inclusion
The clear focus on The Relic and the playful structure removes the pressure of personal performance, fostering a safe space for experimentation. Ensure physical boundaries are respected during the pass, and maintain a supportive environment where all interpretations are valid.

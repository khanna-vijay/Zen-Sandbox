# Reality Warp

![Reality Warp — infographic](img/ISR285.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Actors must instantly embody and justify sudden, bizarre physical reality shifts injected by an external Warp Weaver.

## Overview
A narrative long-form scene where an external 'Warp Weaver' injects sudden, physical reality shifts via prompt cards. Actors must instantly embody these physical changes and justify them within the ongoing story without breaking the scene's emotional truth. It tests an improviser's ability to maintain narrative stakes and character relationships while dealing with bizarre, physical disadvantages.

## Setup
Requires 2-3 actors (Scene Weavers) and 1 external player (the Warp Weaver). Prepare a deck of 10-15 'Warp Cards' with short, punchy physical directives (e.g., 'All objects are incredibly heavy', 'The floor is increasingly sticky', 'You are magnetically repelled by your scene partner'). Get a location, a relationship, and a mundane objective from the audience.

## How to Play
1. The Scene Weavers begin a grounded, realistic scene based on the audience's suggestions, focusing on relationship and their mundane objective.
2. The Warp Weaver watches from the side. At a moment of routine or slight narrative stagnation, the Warp Weaver yells 'REALITY WARP!' and reads a single Warp Card aloud.
3. The Scene Weavers must instantly physically embody this new reality constraint. If the card says 'The air is thick as molasses,' their movements must immediately reflect that.
4. Crucially, the actors must narratively justify the physical change without breaking character or talking about the card itself. They treat the bizarre physical shift as a real, urgent problem within the reality of their scene.
5. To prevent cognitive overload and physical chaos, there is a strict limit of 1 or 2 warps per scene.
6. If a second warp is called, it completely supersedes the first one. The physical constraint of the first warp ends immediately, replaced by the new one. The narrative consequences of what happened during the first warp remain, but the physical burden shifts.
7. The scene concludes when the narrative arc reaches a satisfying resolution, usually shortly after the consequences of the final warp have played out.

## Coaching Notes
- Physical embodiment of narrative constraints forces actors out of their heads and into their bodies.
- Simplified, one-sentence prompts ensure instant comprehension for both players and the audience.
- Superseding rules (new warps replace old ones) prevent cognitive overload and keep the scene focused.
- Strict limit of 1-2 warps per scene ensures the narrative remains the primary focus, rather than devolving into a gimmick.
- If playing in a competitive improv format, judges score on a scale of 1-5 based on three criteria: the physical commitment to the warp, the cleverness of the narrative justification, and the ability to maintain emotional stakes despite the absurdity.

## Variations
- Audience Weaver: Instead of pre-written cards, the host pauses the scene and asks the audience for a physical constraint (e.g., 'What is suddenly wrong with the gravity?'), increasing audience investment.
- Internal Warp: For advanced ensembles, remove the Warp Weaver. A bell is rung at random intervals, and the actors must spontaneously invent, mutually agree upon non-verbally, and embody a physical warp together.

## Why It Works
Physical embodiment of narrative constraints forces actors out of their heads and into their bodies. It tests an improviser's ability to maintain narrative stakes and character relationships while dealing with bizarre, physical disadvantages.

## Safety & Inclusion
Explicitly brief players that physical warps must not force unsafe acrobatics or unwanted physical contact. Warp cards should focus on environmental or solo physical changes (e.g., altered gravity, sticky floors) rather than forcing players to grab or manipulate each other. Players are always empowered to modify physical choices to accommodate their own mobility needs and physical boundaries; the justification is more important than athletic perfection.

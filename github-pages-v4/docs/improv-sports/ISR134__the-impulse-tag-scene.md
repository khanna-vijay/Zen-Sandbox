# The Impulse Tag Scene

![The Impulse Tag Scene — infographic](img/ISR134.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced ensemble exercise where an ongoing core scene is continuously injected with single, spontaneous impulses from a perimeter of participating players.

## Overview
A fast-paced, facilitator-led ensemble exercise where an ongoing core scene is continuously injected with single, spontaneous impulses from a perimeter of participating players. Instead of inventing clever ideas, players on the perimeter are 'tagged' in via a verbal and visual cue, stepping in to deliver one immediate physical or verbal reaction before tagging someone else.

## Setup
A clear stage or workshop space. 2 to 3 'Anchor Players' start in the center of the space. The rest of the ensemble (the 'Impulse Pool') forms a loose semi-circle around the back and sides of the playing space. A Facilitator stands downstage or in the house to side-coach and call time. All players are pre-consenting workshop or ensemble members.

## How to Play
1. The Facilitator gives the Anchor Players a location and they begin a grounded, physical scene.
2. Once the scene is established, an Anchor Player makes direct eye contact with someone in the Impulse Pool, points at them, and says 'Tag!'
3. The tagged player must immediately step into the scene and execute ONE spontaneous physical or verbal impulse reacting to the exact moment they entered (e.g., a dramatic gasp, dropping an imaginary object, or a single line like 'It's too late!').
4. The Anchor Players must instantly Yes-And and justify this impulse, weaving it seamlessly into the reality of the scene.
5. Immediately after delivering their impulse, the tagged player makes eye contact with another player in the perimeter pool, points, says 'Tag!', and then returns to their spot on the perimeter.
6. The newly tagged player immediately steps in to deliver their impulse, and the cycle continues rapidly.
7. The Facilitator side-coaches to maintain the pace and calls 'Scene!' to end the exercise.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach to maintain the pace with prompts like 'Don't think, just react!' and 'Anchor players, justify that!'
- Focus heavily on raw, unedited impulse and immediate reaction, forcing anchor players to aggressively Yes-And and justify random, disjointed offers.
- Remind players of the Point of Concentration: 'Instantly identify the single clearest impulse that responds to what just occurred, deliver it, and pass the focus.'
- Remind the Impulse Pool that they only need to provide a single, brief action, which eliminates the 'deer in headlights' feeling of having to invent a clever idea.

## Variations
- Silent Impulse: Tagged players may only contribute physical impulses (a gesture, a movement, a soundless reaction), forcing the Anchor Players to interpret and justify purely physical offers.
- Rotating Anchors: When a player steps in to give an impulse, they can optionally tap an Anchor Player on the shoulder (with prior consent) to take their place in the core scene, while the displaced Anchor joins the perimeter pool. This keeps the core scene fresh.

## Why It Works
It trains raw responsiveness, aggressive Yes-Anding, and eliminates the time for self-judgment by forcing players to react instantly with a single, brief action rather than inventing clever ideas.

## Safety & Inclusion
All participants must explicitly opt-in to the exercise and remain in the designated stage area (no running through audience aisles or the house). Tagging is done strictly via eye contact, pointing, and a verbal cue ('Tag!')—no physical contact is required to pass the turn, respecting physical boundaries and preventing collisions. Players with mobility needs can participate fully from a stationary position in the perimeter, delivering verbal impulses or upper-body gestures.

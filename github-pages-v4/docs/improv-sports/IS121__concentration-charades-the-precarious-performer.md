# Concentration Charades: The Precarious Performer

![Concentration Charades: The Precarious Performer — infographic](img/IS121.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Act out charades prompts while simultaneously balancing a challenging, unstable object on your body.

## Overview
Concentration Charades: The Precarious Performer is a unique take on traditional charades where players must act out prompts while simultaneously balancing a challenging, unstable object on a designated body part. This strict physical constraint, the 'Point of Concentration,' forces performers to prioritize maintaining balance, thereby overriding self-consciousness and intellectual 'acting' choices. If the object falls, the player must pause their performance to re-balance it, losing precious time, ultimately encouraging intuitive, integrated physical presence and authentic, non-verbal communication under pressure.

## Setup
Divide participants into two or more teams (3-5 players per team). Prepare a list of charades prompts (e.g., movie titles, common phrases, actions, objects, book titles) written on separate slips of paper. Gather small, lightweight, and slightly unstable objects for each team (e.g., a beanbag, a plastic cup, a small stuffed animal, a slightly deflated balloon, a small book, or a feather). Designate a playing area and prepare a timer set to 90 seconds per round.

## How to Play
1. One player from Team A steps forward to be the 'Precarious Performer'.
2. Before receiving their prompt, the Performer must select one of the designated objects and place it on a part of their body where it will be challenging to balance (e.g., on their head, on the back of their hand, on their shoulder, on a knee while standing, or on their forehead).
3. Once the object is balanced, the Performer receives their charades prompt and the 90-second timer begins.
4. The Performer acts out the charades prompt silently, using only gestures and physical actions, while continuously maintaining the balance of the object.
5. Crucial Rule: If the object falls, the Performer must immediately freeze their charades actions, retrieve the object, re-balance it in the exact same spot, and only then can they continue acting out the prompt. The time continues to run during this process.
6. Teammates shout out guesses. If the team guesses correctly within the time limit, they earn a point.
7. If time runs out or the Performer gives up, no point is awarded for that round.
8. Teams take turns sending up a new Performer for subsequent rounds. Play for a set number of rounds, and the team with the most points wins.

## Coaching Notes
- The strict and unwavering Point of Concentration (POC) is to continuously maintain the balance of the chosen object on the designated body part throughout the entire performance.
- Remind players that their intellect should be entirely consumed by the physical task of balancing. This forces the body to find intuitive and spontaneous ways to communicate the charades prompt as a secondary, almost subconscious, activity.
- Strictly enforce the freeze rule when an object falls. The loss of time is the pressure that drives the game's focus.
- Encourage players to choose genuinely challenging balance points rather than taking the easy way out.

## Why It Works
This game develops integrated physical presence and intuitive communication under constraint. The constant demand of balancing forces performers out of their heads and into their physical reality, overriding self-consciousness and intellectual 'acting' choices. It cultivates physical awareness, resourcefulness, and a unique form of non-verbal clarity, embodying the 'playing without acting' principle by demonstrating that perceived limitations can paradoxically unlock new, authentic pathways for theatrical expression.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing area is completely clear of tripping hazards, furniture, or obstacles, as the Performer's visual and mental focus will be entirely on balancing the object rather than their surroundings. Use only lightweight, soft objects to prevent injury if they fall.

# The Multiverse Mixer

![The Multiverse Mixer — infographic](img/IS062.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Witness how one small, pivotal moment can shatter a scene into hilariously disparate realities, with performers adapting to an ever-changing 'truth'.

## Overview
The Multiverse Mixer is an improvisational game where 3-4 players begin a scene based on audience suggestions, establishing characters and stakes. At a crucial moment, an MC interjects with a 'Multiverse Shift,' introducing a dramatic and often absurd new condition that recontextualizes everything that has just occurred. Players must then immediately restart the scene from a designated point, adapting their previous dialogue and actions to this new reality.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 improvisers, a dedicated MC/Referee, and a clear performance space. No props are needed; players rely on strong object work. Audience interaction is integral for initial suggestions and scoring.

## How to Play
1. The MC approaches the audience for two suggestions: a Location and a Relationship between two characters.
2. The MC selects 2-3 players to begin a 1-2 minute scene based on these suggestions, establishing characters, stakes, and a developing narrative.
3. At a moment of established reality or a key interaction, the MC interjects loudly with: 'HOLD! Multiverse Shift!' and the players freeze in position.
4. The MC announces a new, unexpected condition or revelation that dramatically recontextualizes everything that has just happened (e.g., they are spy rivals, or the money is an antique potato).
5. The MC commands: 'RESET! From [Player A]'s last line, incorporating the shift!' or points to a clear moment just before the shift.
6. The players immediately pick up from the designated reset point, performing the same scene but now fully integrating the new Multiverse Shift condition.
7. The MC observes the new reality and, at a fresh compelling beat, triggers another Multiverse Shift with a new condition. This happens 2-3 times per game.
8. After 2-3 shifts, or when a strong comedic point is made in the final reality, the MC yells 'Scene!' and the audience applauds.

## Coaching Notes
- Players must remember the current active reality, not revert to previous ones.
- Performers should exaggerate the implications of the shift. If arguing over money that is now an antique potato, maintain the intensity of the argument but apply it absurdly to the potato.
- Crucially, players should aim for a moment in the initial scene that could be interpreted in multiple ways, or a statement that feels like a 'truth'.
- The MC must choose conditions that fundamentally alter a character's core truth or dramatically recontextualize the physical reality.

## Variations
- Competitive Match Scoring: The MC acts as a Referee and awards points: +3 for smoothest adaptation, +5 for biggest laugh from a re-interpretation, +4 for heightening the absurdity, and +2 for clear callbacks to previous realities.
- Audience Applause-O-Meter: At the end, the MC has the audience applaud to determine which Multiverse Shift or which performer they found most impressive or funny.

## Why It Works
This game develops rapid scene construction and quick thinking by forcing players to re-evaluate what is happening based on a new parameter. It thrives on juxtaposition and heightening, challenging improvisers to seamlessly adapt to ever-changing truths and create comedy from the clash of realities.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players maintain physical safety during sudden freezes and rapid resets. As realities shift quickly, players should respect personal boundaries and avoid forcing non-consensual physical or emotional choices on their scene partners.

# Reality Remix

![Reality Remix — infographic](img/ISR224.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams alternate playing the exact same characters in a single scene while instantly adapting to bizarre physical anomalies triggered by a Referee.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two teams alternate playing the exact same characters in a single scene. A Referee periodically triggers 'Anomaly Cards' that instantly alter the physical reality of the environment. Teams must seamlessly adopt these new physical realities without dropping the narrative, testing their physical commitment, object work, and rapid adaptation.

## Setup
Requires a Referee (the 'Reality Remixer') center stage with a whistle and a pre-written deck of 15-20 'Anomaly Cards.' Two teams (Red and Blue) stand on opposite sidelines. The Referee gets a mundane location and a simple relationship/objective from the audience to start the scene.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a mundane location (e.g., 'Library') and a relationship/objective (e.g., 'Librarian enforcing silence on a loud patron') from the audience.
2. Team Red starts. Two players step forward and begin a grounded, naturalistic scene to establish the characters, objective, and base reality. This lasts about 30 seconds.
3. The Referee blows the whistle, yells 'REMIX!', and reads an Anomaly Card aloud (e.g., 'The floor is now made of ice!').
4. Team Red must instantly and physically integrate this new reality into their scene without breaking character or dropping the narrative objective. They play this anomaly for 30-45 seconds.
5. The Referee blows the whistle and yells 'NEW DIMENSION - TEAM BLUE!'. Team Red immediately steps back.
6. Two players from Team Blue step in, taking over the EXACT same characters, physical positions, and storyline that Team Red just left.
7. The Referee immediately reads a new Anomaly Card. By default, the new anomaly REPLACES the old one to prevent cognitive overload.
8. Team Blue adapts to the new anomaly while continuing the story.
9. The Referee alternates teams 4-6 times, keeping the pace fast and the anomalies escalating, before calling 'Scene!' at a high-energy climax.
10. Scoring: Award 1 point immediately each time a team successfully and instantly physically adapts to a new anomaly. Deduct 1 point for a 'Reality Denial Foul' if a team ignores the physical constraint. Deduct 5 points for a 'content foul' (inappropriate content). At the end of the game, the Referee asks the audience to applaud for the team that handled the chaos best, awarding a 3-point bonus to the winner.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee must maintain excellent pacing control, ensuring the scene never drags and anomalies escalate.
- Watch for 'Reality Denial Fouls' where players ignore the physical constraint, and deduct points accordingly.
- Ensure incoming players flawlessly mimic the physical positions and characters of the outgoing team.
- Keep the focus on seamlessly adopting new physical realities without dropping the narrative objective.

## Variations
- Layered Remix (Advanced): Instead of new anomalies replacing old ones, they stack. By the fourth swap, players might be dealing with a sticky floor, backwards talking, AND extreme heaviness all at once.
- Audience's Choice: Instead of drawing randomly, the Referee holds up two Anomaly Cards during a swap. The audience cheers for the one they want to see inflicted on the incoming team.

## Why It Works
It forces players to prioritize physical commitment and object work over purely verbal wit, while testing intense active listening and observation as the incoming team must flawlessly mimic and inherit the outgoing team's characters and narrative.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical Safety is paramount. The Referee must enforce the 'Illusion of Danger, Safety of the Actor' rule. Players must mime slipping, struggling, or falling without actually throwing their bodies to the floor. Running, jumping, and actual physical wrestling are strictly prohibited. Accessibility: Anomaly Cards should be curated to the physical abilities of the cast. Avoid cards that demand specific mobility (e.g., 'Everyone must do jumping jacks') if it excludes a player; focus instead on environmental changes (e.g., 'The room smells terrible') or object work.

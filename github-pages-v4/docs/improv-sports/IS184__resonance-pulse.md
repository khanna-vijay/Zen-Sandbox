# Resonance Pulse

![Resonance Pulse — infographic](img/IS184.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Cultivate profound presence and intuitive responsiveness through a continuous chain of non-verbal emotional sounds and involuntary physical reactions.

## Overview
Deeply rooted in foundational improv philosophy, this game cultivates profound presence, intuitive responsiveness, and authentic emotional expression by rigorously bypassing intellect. Participants form a continuous chain where one person's non-verbal emotional sound triggers an immediate, involuntary physical impulse in the next, who then vocalizes a new emotional sound born directly from that bodily reaction.

## Setup
Participants stand or sit comfortably in a circle, facing inwards.

## How to Play
1. One participant begins by making a single, authentic, non-verbal emotional sound (e.g., a pure groan of frustration, a sharp gasp of surprise, a soft sigh of longing). This must be immediate, unplanned, and avoid words or complex melodies.
2. The next participant receives that sound. Their Point of Concentration (POC) is to allow the previous sound to generate an immediate, involuntary physical impulse or shift in their own body.
3. This physical reaction should not be a pantomime or an intellectual choice, but a genuine, spontaneous bodily reaction (e.g., a slight recoil, a leaning forward, a tensing of shoulders). The focus is on receiving the impulse, not creating a reaction.
4. Immediately following that involuntary physical impulse, the participant emits their own non-verbal emotional sound. This sound must arise directly from and be a consequence of that physical impulse, creating a new emotional expression.
5. The chain continues around the circle, building a complex, intuitive emotional-physical narrative that is always in motion, reflecting the authenticity of each participant's immediate response.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Strictly receive the previous sound with your entire body, allow it to generate an immediate, involuntary physical impulse, and express the first authentic, non-verbal emotional sound that arises directly from it.
- Remind players: 'Let it hit you!' and 'Where do you feel that sound in your body? Let it resonate, deep inside.'
- Encourage bypassing the intellect: 'Don't think about the sound, let your body react first! Get out of your head!'
- Prompt for immediacy: 'What's the first sound that comes from that feeling, before you can name it, before you can filter it?'
- Validate responses: 'Trust your impulse - it's already there! It's happening inside you right now.'
- Keep them grounded: 'Stay with the pure impulse, not a character, not a concept - just the raw you.'

## Why It Works
The rigorous chain of Sound to Involuntary Physical Impulse to New Sound completely bypasses intellectual thought, forcing gut-level, intuitive reactions. By aligning with foundational improv philosophy, it demands authentic human response rather than trained performance, training actors to connect emotional impulses directly to authentic physical expression rooted in genuine bodily sensation.

## Safety & Inclusion
Emphasize that there is no 'correct' impulse or sound to remove performance pressure and validate all honest responses. The required physical impulse doesn't need to be large or dramatic; it is often an internal shift, which ensures comfort and participation for all physical abilities and respects individual boundaries.

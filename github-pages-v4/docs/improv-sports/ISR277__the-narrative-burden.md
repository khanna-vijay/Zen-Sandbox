# The Narrative Burden

![The Narrative Burden — infographic](img/ISR277.jpg){ .game-hero }

> One player physicalizes a secret internal burden while their partner reacts truthfully to the subtle cues.

## Overview
A facilitator-led scene exercise where one player is given a secret, internal burden or strong subtext. They must translate this internal state into a subtle, consistent physical behavior. Their partner observes and reacts truthfully to this physical reality, allowing the scene's tension and narrative to escalate organically without the burden ever being explicitly named.

## Setup
Two players take the stage. The facilitator needs a list of playable, relatable burdens (internal secrets or emotional states). The rest of the workshop serves as the audience and provides a mundane location to ground the scene.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator asks the observing group for a mundane location (e.g., a laundromat, a breakroom, a bus stop).
2. The facilitator secretly assigns a Narrative Burden to Player A (e.g., 'Guilt over a recent petty lie,' 'Intense fear of being fired,' 'Secretly in love with the other person'). Player B does not know the prompt.
3. Player A must immediately choose a subtle physical manifestation of this burden (e.g., avoiding eye contact, fidgeting with a hem, a stiffened neck, shallow breathing).
4. The scene begins. Player A plays the scene while maintaining and slowly escalating their physical manifestation based on the interaction.
5. Player B observes and reacts truthfully to Player A's physical behavior and tone. Player B should not try to guess or name the secret out loud, but rather deal with how Player A is behaving in the moment.
6. Players let the scene evolve organically. As Player B reacts, Player A should allow their physical burden to be affected—intensifying, shifting, or softening based purely on Player B's treatment of them.
7. The scene concludes when a natural emotional shift occurs. This might be the tension resolving, the relationship changing, or the emotional weight organically transferring to Player B.

## Coaching Notes
- The facilitator may gently side-coach (e.g., 'Heighten the physicality,' 'Let their words affect your posture') to keep players focused on the physical subtext rather than inventing verbal plot twists.
- Afterward, lead a brief discussion on what physical cues were noticed and how they drove the scene's subtext.
- Remind Player B not to guess the secret, but to react to the behavior itself.

## Variations
- Mutual Burdens: Both players receive different secret burdens from the facilitator, creating a complex, highly physical web of subtext.
- The Relief: The scene's implicit goal is for Player A to find a way to organically drop their physical burden based purely on Player B's comforting or changing behavior, without ever discussing the secret.

## Why It Works
Trains players to observe and react to their partner's physical cues rather than just waiting for dialogue. Encourages organic scene discovery and emotional shifts over forced plot twists, and practices being changed by a partner's reactions in real-time.

## Safety & Inclusion
Psychological safety is paramount: facilitators must explicitly avoid prompts involving trauma, phobias, or heavy psychological distress. Use playable, relatable stakes. Physical manifestations must be accessible; players should choose physicalities that do not strain their bodies or mimic disabilities. Side-coaching should focus on improv technique rather than pushing players into genuine emotional distress.

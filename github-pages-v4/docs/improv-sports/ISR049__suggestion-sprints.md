# Suggestion Sprints

![Suggestion Sprints — infographic](img/ISR049.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams perform scenes periodically interrupted by random, pre-collected audience suggestions that must be seamlessly woven into the narrative.

## Overview
Suggestion Sprints is a fast-paced, competitive short-form game where teams perform scenes that are periodically interrupted by the referee injecting pre-collected audience suggestions. Instead of stopping the scene to poll the crowd, the referee reads from a bucket of audience slips. Players must organically and seamlessly weave these random objects, phrases, or emotions into their established base reality.

## Setup
Requires a bucket or hat filled with pre-collected audience suggestions (objects, phrases, sounds) gathered before the show or during intermission. Needs an active referee to host, hold the bucket, award points, and call fouls. Played in a competitive short-form match format with two teams.

## How to Play
1. Before the game begins, the referee gathers the bucket of pre-written audience suggestions and quickly vets them for family-friendly content.
2. The referee gets a base suggestion (e.g., a location or relationship) from the live audience and starts Team A's scene.
3. Team A is given 30 to 45 seconds to establish a strong 'who, what, and where' without any interruptions, grounding the scene.
4. The referee blows a whistle, pulls a slip from the bucket, and reads it aloud instantly (e.g., 'A flaming sword!'). The scene does not stop; players continue acting.
5. Players must accept and justify the suggestion into the scene's narrative. There is no strict countdown timer; the goal is smooth, logical, or hilariously justified incorporation.
6. The referee injects 3 to 4 suggestions total over a 2 to 3 minute scene, allowing the narrative to breathe and develop between interruptions.
7. Once Team A's scene concludes, Team B takes the stage for their scene with a new base suggestion and fresh slips from the bucket.
8. The referee awards 1 to 3 points for 'Seamless Integration', calls a 2-point 'Shoehorn Foul' if players awkwardly force the suggestion in, or a 3-point 'Drop Foul' if the suggestion is ignored entirely.
9. At the end of both scenes, the audience applauds for their favorite team, and the referee awards a 5-point bonus to the winner.

## Coaching Notes
- Pre-collected suggestions eliminate dead time and keep the game's momentum high.
- Removal of the countdown timer encourages organic, theatrical justification rather than panicked gagging.
- Delayed interruptions allow players to build a solid base reality, making the absurd additions much funnier.
- Clear referee mechanics make it easy to run and highly engaging for a competitive short-form crowd.
- Ensure the referee is actively listening and watching to award points for 'Seamless Integration' or call 'Shoehorn' and 'Drop' fouls accurately.

## Variations
- Blind Sprints: Instead of the referee reading the slips, players draw the slips themselves from their pockets, read them aloud, and immediately justify them.
- Emotion Sprints: All pre-collected slips are specific emotional shifts or physical ailments rather than objects or phrases, testing the players' acting range.

## Why It Works
Pre-collected suggestions eliminate dead time and keep momentum high. Removing a countdown timer encourages organic, theatrical justification rather than panicked gagging, and delaying the first interruption allows players to build a solid base reality, making absurd additions much funnier.

## Safety & Inclusion
The referee pre-vets all written suggestions to ensure content remains clean, all-ages, and free from offensive material. Because there is no strict countdown timer, players are not pressured into making panicked, potentially unsafe physical choices. The focus on organic integration promotes supportive scene work over aggressive scene-stealing.

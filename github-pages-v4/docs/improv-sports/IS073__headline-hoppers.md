# Headline Hoppers

![Headline Hoppers — infographic](img/IS073.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players collaboratively build an unfolding scene, instantly justifying and incorporating rapidly changing, unrelated newspaper headlines thrown at them by the Referee.

## Overview
Headline Hoppers is an improv game where players collaboratively build an unfolding scene, initially inspired by an outlandish, audience-provided newspaper headline. Periodically, a Referee introduces a completely new and unrelated headline, forcing the players to instantly incorporate and comically justify this fresh absurdity into their ongoing narrative. The game challenges improvisers to swiftly 'yes, and' disparate information, maintaining character and scene momentum through rapid, often nonsensical, shifts to create a continuously evolving and humorous story.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 players (typically 2 from each team, or 2 players from one team with a third acting as a facilitator) on a standard open stage with no physical props. The audience provides the initial outlandish, family-friendly headline suggestion and subsequent 'hopping' headlines. The Referee is crucial: they take the initial headline, keep time, select and introduce new headlines at intervals, award points, and call fouls. The Referee might hold up a mimed newspaper with the new headline written on it for visual effect.

## How to Play
1. The Referee solicits an outlandish, family-friendly headline from the audience (e.g., 'Local Squirrel Discovers Cure for Mondays!').
2. 2-3 players immediately launch into a scene that attempts to explain the circumstances leading to this headline, establishing characters, a setting, and a basic premise.
3. At intervals of 30-45 seconds (signaled by a whistle, bell, or Referee's announcement), the Referee presents a new, completely unrelated, and equally bizarre headline.
4. The players must immediately incorporate and justify the new headline into their ongoing scene. They cannot ignore it or dismiss it as irrelevant.
5. Players can justify the headline by having a character read it, suddenly embodying it, discovering it as a consequence of the existing scene, reacting to it as breaking news, or having it physically manifest.
6. Players must 'Yes, And' the new headline information, integrating it as seamlessly and comically as possible to further the narrative, even if it becomes increasingly nonsensical.
7. The game continues for 3-5 headlines (determined by the Referee based on time and scene energy), at which point the Referee calls 'Time!'.

## Coaching Notes
- Fast and Fierce: The quick turnover of headlines demands instant 'Yes, And' responses. Challenge players to be bold in their justifications, committing fully to absurd premises and transforming the scene on a dime without hesitation.
- Scoring: Award +3 points for exceptionally clever, immediate, and hilarious justification; +2 for solid, clear, and quick justification; +1 for successful but slightly delayed incorporation.
- Watch for the 'Headline Dodge' Foul (-3 points) if a player overtly attempts to ignore, dismiss, or backtrack from a new headline, failing to accept it as the new reality.
- Call a 'Deer in Headlights' Foul (-2 points) if players visibly freeze, struggle, or show a clear lack of initiative in incorporating a new headline for more than a few seconds.
- Call a 'Dead Air' Foul (-1 point) for prolonged silence or unnecessary pauses, especially after a headline reveal, and a 'Groaner Foul' (-1 point) for lazy puns.
- Encourage strong physical choices and object work (miming newspapers, roller skates, pizzas, etc.) to enhance the visual comedy.
- Remind players to maintain consistent character reactions while adapting to an increasingly bizarre reality.

## Why It Works
The game's core mechanic forces the ultimate 'Yes, And...' by demanding every new headline be accepted and built upon instantly. It develops active listening to track the evolving narrative, dynamic pacing through rapid shifts dictated by the Referee, and strong character endowments as players maintain consistency amidst bizarre, changing realities.

## Safety & Inclusion
The humor must remain universally accessible and family-friendly. The comedy stems from outrageous situations and clever adaptations, not from blue material. The Referee should moderate audience suggestions and apply the standard clean-content foul (-5 points) for any inappropriate content.

# Resonance Web

![Resonance Web — infographic](img/ISR137.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players pass and transform a repetitive sound or motion around a circle, changing the action but keeping its energetic essence.

## Overview
An ensemble warm-up where players pass a repetitive sound or motion around a circle. Instead of copying the action exactly, each player transforms it, changing the body part or sound while keeping the 'essence' (the rhythm, tempo, or energy). It builds deep non-verbal listening, focus, and group intuition.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a circle where everyone can see and hear each other clearly. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator asks for a volunteer to start. This first player initiates a simple, repetitive physical gesture or sound (e.g., tapping their thigh at a steady, upbeat tempo).
2. The player to their right observes the action and identifies its 'essence' (is it fast, slow, heavy, light, sharp, or smooth?).
3. The second player immediately begins a NEW action or sound that shares that same essence. For example, if Player 1 is tapping their thigh quickly, Player 2 might start blinking their eyes quickly, or making a rapid 'ch-ch-ch' sound.
4. The first player continues their original action while the second player starts theirs. They overlap for a few seconds so the energetic connection is clear.
5. The third player then receives the essence from the second player and transforms it into a new action. CRITICAL TIMING: As soon as the third player begins their action, the FIRST player stops. This ensures only two adjacent players are active at any time, preventing a muddy cacophony.
6. The impulse travels around the circle, organically morphing from one action to the next until it returns to the starting player.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach to maintain flow with prompts like: 'Don't plan ahead, just react!', 'Keep the tempo, change the body part!', or 'Let the first player drop out as the third begins!'
- Ensure players overlap for a few seconds so the energetic connection is clear.
- Enforce the rule that only two adjacent players are active at any time to prevent the exercise from turning into a muddy cacophony.

## Variations
- Cross-Circle: Instead of passing to the right, players pass the impulse to anyone in the circle by making strong eye contact. The 'two active players at a time' rule still applies.
- Emotion Web: Players focus on passing the emotional essence rather than just the physical rhythm (e.g., a 'nervous' foot tap transforms into a 'nervous' hum).

## Why It Works
It deepens non-verbal listening and ensemble connection while forcing players out of their heads by prioritizing intuitive, gut-level responses over intellectual planning.

## Safety & Inclusion
This game is highly accessible and safe for all physical abilities. Players can remain seated, and since the goal is transformation, any movement or sound (no matter how small or localized) is valid. There is no physical contact required. Facilitators should encourage players to only make movements that are physically comfortable for them.

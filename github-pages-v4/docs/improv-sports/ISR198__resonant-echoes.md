# Resonant Echoes

![Resonant Echoes — infographic](img/ISR198.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players physically explore a space based on evocative prompts to create spontaneous, non-verbal group tableaus.

## Overview
A non-competitive ensemble warm-up where players physically explore a space based on evocative prompts, creating spontaneous, non-verbal group tableaus. It trains deep physical listening, spatial awareness, and gets players out of their heads and into their bodies by focusing on how environments feel rather than just how they look.

## Setup
Clear the room of tripping hazards, leaving a few neutral chairs or blocks if available. The group forms a circle. The facilitator prepares a list of 'Resonance Prompts' that range from concrete to abstract (e.g., 'A Place to Hide', 'The Center of Gravity', 'A Forgotten Memory').

## How to Play
1. Phase 1: The Anchor (30 seconds). The facilitator selects one player and gives them a prompt (e.g., 'The Hottest Spot in the Room'). The player silently explores the space, finds a location that intuitively matches the prompt, and strikes a sustained, non-verbal physical pose to embody it.
2. Phase 2: The Echo (60-90 seconds). The facilitator announces the same prompt to the rest of the group. The ensemble silently moves into the space to join the Anchor. They do not simply mimic the Anchor; instead, they add to the physical picture by harmonizing, contrasting, or expanding the tableau.
3. Phase 3: The Release. Once the group picture feels complete and settled, the facilitator calls 'Neutral' or rings a soft chime. Players immediately drop their poses and return to the starting circle.
4. Phase 4: Structured Debrief (1-2 minutes). The facilitator asks 1-2 targeted questions to connect the exercise to scene work. Examples: 'Anchor, what drew you to that specific corner?' 'Ensemble, how did you decide where to place yourselves?' 'How can we use this physical awareness to establish the environment in our scenes?'
5. Repeat: Select a new Anchor and a new prompt. Play 4-5 rounds, gradually increasing the abstraction of the prompts as the group gets comfortable.

## Coaching Notes
- Success is measured by the ensemble's silent focus, physical commitment, and ability to build a cohesive group picture.
- Remind the ensemble not to simply mimic the Anchor, but to harmonize, contrast, or expand the tableau.
- Encourage players to bridge the gap between abstract feeling and concrete stage pictures.
- Maintain a non-competitive atmosphere; there are no points, judges, or audience suggestions.

## Variations
- Scaffolded Prompts: To help beginners, start with highly concrete prompts ('A crowded bus', 'A tightrope') for the first two rounds, move to evocative prompts ('A secret meeting place', 'A machine waiting to start') for the middle rounds, and finish with abstract prompts ('The persistent hum', 'The weight of a secret').
- Soundscape Echo: Once players are comfortable with the physical tableau, allow them to add continuous, non-verbal vocalizations (hums, clicks, breaths) that match the physical energy of the prompt, creating a unified room soundscape.

## Why It Works
It trains deep physical listening and ensemble awareness, forcing players out of their heads and into their bodies by focusing on how environments feel rather than just how they look.

## Safety & Inclusion
Players can participate fully from seated positions or mobility devices; the 'echo' is about spatial relationship and intention, not specific physical acrobatics. Explicitly remind players that physical touch requires prior consent; players can create powerful spatial relationships and harmonize with each other without making physical contact. Ensure the room is free of tripping hazards.

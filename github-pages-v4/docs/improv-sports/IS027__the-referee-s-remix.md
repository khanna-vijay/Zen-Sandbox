# The Referee's Remix

![The Referee's Remix — infographic](img/IS027.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Adapt instantly to the referee's spontaneous, mid-scene interventions that drastically alter the reality of the scene.

## Overview
The Referee's Remix is a competitive improv game where two players start a scene based on audience suggestions. At unpredictable intervals, an active Referee shouts 'REMIIIIX!' and introduces spontaneous, dramatic changes—such as altering character traits, swapping roles, or shifting the genre. Players must instantly 'Yes, And' these interventions, seamlessly integrating the absurd new realities into their narrative while maintaining a strict family-friendly tone.

## Setup
Two players from one team step forward. The referee asks the audience for a location, a relationship between the two players, and a starting endowment for one or both characters (e.g., 'one of you is secretly a robot').

## How to Play
1. 1. Scene Initiation: The two players start their scene, establishing their characters, relationship, and the environment based on the audience suggestions.
2. 2. The 'Remix!' Call: At unpredictable intervals (typically every 30-60 seconds, or when the scene settles into a predictable rhythm), the Referee shouts 'REMIIIIX!' with a dramatic gesture.
3. 3. Referee's Command: Immediately after shouting, the Referee issues a specific command that drastically alters the scene. Examples include 'Change Endowment!', 'Swap Roles!', 'New Scene Element!', 'Mini-Challenge!', 'Genre Shift!', or 'Audience Inject!'.
4. 4. Player Adaptation: Players must immediately 'Yes, And' the Referee's command, integrating it seamlessly and humorously into the ongoing scene without breaking character or denying the new reality.
5. 5. Scene Continuation: The scene continues, repeating the Remix cycle, until the Referee calls 'Freeze!' or 'Scene!'.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee is an active, unpredictable element who controls the pace and intensity of the game. Aim for variety, escalating absurdity, and challenging the players in novel ways.
- Scoring: Award 2-5 points for each successful, creative adaptation to a Remix command. Award 1-3 bonus points for exceptional 'Yes, And' moments, strong character work under pressure, or outstanding teamwork.
- Use an 'Audience Applause-o-meter' after a particularly strong adaptation to help gauge point value and reinforce audience involvement.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' (-2 points) if a player delivers an excessively bad pun or joke that detracts from the scene.
- Call a 'Denial Foul' (-2 points) if a player denies a previous offer from their scene partner or fails to 'Yes, And' a Referee's Remix command.
- Explain point assignments briefly to the audience for transparency.

## Why It Works
It forces absolute adherence to 'Yes, And' and active listening under intense pressure. Players must demonstrate extreme adaptability and flexibility to pivot their characters, narrative, and physical space instantly while maintaining a coherent scene build.

## Safety & Inclusion
The game strictly adheres to a family-friendly ethos. The Referee must immediately call a 'Clean-Content Foul' (-5 points) and dramatically present a penalty marker if any player uses blue humor, swearing, or inappropriate innuendo.

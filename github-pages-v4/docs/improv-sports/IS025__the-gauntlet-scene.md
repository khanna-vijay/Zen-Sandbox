# The Gauntlet Scene

![The Gauntlet Scene — infographic](img/IS025.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams compete by performing scenes that are constantly interrupted by rapid-fire, mandatory challenges from a referee and audience.

## Overview
Two teams compete by performing scenes that are constantly interrupted and challenged by the referee and audience. The goal is to successfully integrate these high-stakes, real-time challenges, maintain a cohesive and humorous narrative, and demonstrate exceptional improv skills. Teams earn points for meeting challenges and delivering an engaging scene, with the referee acting as the ultimate arbiter of success and difficulty.

## Setup
Two teams (Red and Blue), typically with 2-3 players per team on stage for each round. A dedicated referee, equipped with a whistle, score-keeping device, and a list of varied 'Gauntlet Challenges.' A clear performance area. An engaged audience ready to provide suggestions and cheer.

## How to Play
1. The referee asks the audience for a core 'Who,' 'What,' and 'Where' for the scene.
2. The active team sends its 2-3 designated players onto the stage, and the referee blows the whistle to signal the start of the scene.
3. Players begin the scene, establishing characters and initial conflict based on the audience suggestions.
4. At unpredictable points (typically every 20-45 seconds), the referee blows the whistle and announces a 'Gauntlet Challenge!' (e.g., Emotional Shift, Gibberish Dialogue, Object Integration, Physical Constraint, Genre Warp).
5. Players must visibly and audibly accept the challenge and implement it immediately (within 1-2 seconds) into the ongoing scene.
6. The referee observes closely, blowing the whistle again to signify the end of a time-bound challenge period or to announce the next challenge.
7. The referee lets the scene run for approximately 2-3 minutes, incorporating 3-5 challenges, then blows the whistle to end the scene for the active team.
8. The referee awards points for successful challenge completion (+3 points), exceptional execution (+5 points), or partial integration (+1 point), and deducts points for fouls (Content Foul: -5, Groaner: -2, Challenge Dodge: -7, Scene Stall: -3, No, But: -2).
9. The other team then takes the stage for their round with new suggestions and challenges. The team with the highest total score wins.

## Coaching Notes
- Active Players: The 2-3 players on stage must work collaboratively, using 'Yes, And' to accept and build upon both the scene's premise and the sudden challenges.
- Off-Stage Players: Players from the active team not currently on stage can offer silent encouragement but cannot interfere unless explicitly allowed by a challenge.
- Referee Interaction: The referee is the primary source of challenges, picking from a list or improvising based on the scene's flow to maximize comedic effect, difficulty, or to highlight a player's strengths/weaknesses.
- Judging Challenges: The referee is the sole and final judge of whether a challenge has been successfully met. This judgment is immediate and often accompanied by a quick explanation for points awarded or denied.
- Audience Appreciation Bonus: At the end of each team's scene, the referee can solicit audience applause. The team with the loudest applause might receive a subjective bonus of 1-3 points.

## Why It Works
It pushes improvisers to their creative limits by demanding absolute 'Yes, And' skills. Players must accept not only their scene partner's offers but also the referee's sudden, often absurd, challenges, and build upon them. It tests active listening, adaptability, flexibility, character work under immense pressure, object work, narrative cohesion, ensemble play, and pacing.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Gauntlet Scene is meticulously designed to be 100% family-friendly. All challenges are crafted to encourage creative, physical, and verbal humor without any reliance on suggestive themes, blue humor, or inappropriate language. The referee's strict and immediate enforcement of the 'Content Foul' (-5 points) ensures that any attempt at off-color humor is swiftly penalized, maintaining a safe and enjoyable atmosphere for audience members of all ages.

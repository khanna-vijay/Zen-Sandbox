# Morphing Prop

![Morphing Prop — infographic](img/ISR061.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must continuously justify a single physical prop magically transforming into different audience-suggested objects mid-scene.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive scene where players must continuously justify a single physical prop magically transforming into different objects. As the scene progresses, the Referee repeatedly freezes the action, gets a new object suggestion from the audience, and forces the players to instantly explain why the item has changed without breaking the reality of their ongoing narrative.

## Setup
You need one versatile, safe, and ambiguous physical prop like a foam cylinder, a strange piece of hardware, or a silicone kitchen tool. Two teams of two players take the stage. A Referee manages the time, takes audience suggestions, and calls fouls.

## How to Play
1. The Referee brings Team A forward, hands them the prop, and gets an audience suggestion for a location and what the prop initially is.
2. Team A begins a scene in that location, using the prop as suggested.
3. After 15 to 20 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle and the players freeze in place.
4. The Referee immediately asks the audience, 'What is the prop now?' and selects a clear, family-friendly noun.
5. The Referee blows the whistle again to unfreeze the scene.
6. Team A must instantly resume the exact same scene, justifying why the object has transformed into the new item. They cannot ignore the change or start a new scene; they must weave the transformation into the existing narrative.
7. This freeze-and-transform process repeats 3 to 4 times over a 2-minute scene.
8. Team A's scene ends. Team B takes the stage with the same prop, gets a new location and starting object, and performs their own 2-minute scene with 3 to 4 audience-driven transformations.
9. After both teams have performed, the Referee calls for audience applause to determine the winner, awarding 5 points to the team with the loudest cheers.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee should not award points mid-scene; this preserves the rapid momentum of the game.
- The audience should be highly active, shouting out new nouns every time the scene freezes.
- The Referee must strictly filter for family-friendly suggestions and can call a content foul for inappropriate content, or a Delay Foul if players hesitate too long to justify the new object.
- Encourage players to use strong physicality to anchor the scene, demanding clear object work with the versatile prop.

## Variations
- Rapid-Fire Props: Instead of a continuous scene, both teams line up. Players alternate stepping forward to deliver a quick, 5-second disconnected sight gag using the prop. If a player hesitates or repeats a gag, they are eliminated.
- Genre Morph: Instead of the prop changing, the genre of the scene changes at each freeze (e.g., Western, Sci-Fi, Soap Opera), while the prop remains the same object but is used in a genre-appropriate way.

## Why It Works
It creates continuous narrative pressure that forces players to practice immediate justification of absurd premises. It also generates high audience engagement as the crowd dictates the twists in real-time, while the single physical prop demands clear and grounded object work.

## Safety & Inclusion
The prop must be physically safe, meaning lightweight, soft, and free of sharp edges. Players must maintain physical boundaries and never use the prop to strike another player. The Referee must swiftly reject any audience suggestions that are inappropriate, unsafe, or rely on harmful stereotypes, ensuring a welcoming environment for all ages.

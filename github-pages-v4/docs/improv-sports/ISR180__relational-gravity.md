# Relational Gravity

![Relational Gravity — infographic](img/ISR180.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players explore the tangible push and pull of human relationships through instant physical and energetic dynamics.

## Overview
A fast-paced, facilitator-led warm-up where players explore the tangible 'push and pull' of human relationships. By initiating strong physical and energetic dynamics directly with one another, players practice instantly adopting complementary status and emotional states without overthinking.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle or scattered loosely in an open space. A facilitator stands on the perimeter to side-coach. No props, chairs, or specialized space are required.

## How to Play
1. Initiation (The Offer): Player A makes strong eye contact with Player B across the space. Player A adopts a distinct physical posture, facial expression, and energetic state (e.g., pleading, dominating, suspicious, adoring) and directs it entirely at Player B.
2. The Clarifier: To help clarify highly ambiguous physical offers, Player A may use exactly ONE word or a single gibberish sound as they initiate (e.g., a sharp 'Hey!', a pleading 'Please?', or a dismissive 'Pfft').
3. The Complement (The Reaction): Player B must instantly, without thinking, adopt the complementary side of that relationship. If Player A is a demanding authority figure, Player B instantly physically cowers or rebels. If Player A is a fawning fan, Player B becomes the aloof celebrity.
4. The Hold: Both players lock into this shared dynamic, holding the physical tension and eye contact for 2 to 3 seconds to solidify the relationship.
5. The Shift: Player B then physically 'shakes off' the energy, turns to a new person (Player C), and immediately initiates a completely NEW and different relational dynamic. The chain continues.

## Coaching Notes
- Actively side-coach to prevent players from getting stuck in their heads.
- Call out prompts like: 'Don't think, react!', 'Just one word!', 'Hold it... now shift!', 'Breathe into the posture!', and 'Speed it up!'
- Remind the ensemble to actively observe the shifting dynamics and learn from the physical choices being made.

## Variations
- Gibberish Only: Remove the single-word option entirely; players may only use gibberish sounds to accompany their physical offers, forcing total reliance on tone and body language.
- All-Skate Orbit: Instead of a single chain, the facilitator pairs everyone up. On the facilitator's clap, all pairs establish a dynamic. On the next clap, they find a new partner and establish a new dynamic instantly, creating a room full of shifting gravity.

## Why It Works
It forces players to rely on physicality and non-verbal cues to establish relationships. It is excellent for training status, emotional responsiveness, and being changed by an offer, bypassing the intellect by demanding instantaneous, gut-level reactions.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical contact is strictly prohibited; all 'pushes' and 'pulls' are energetic and communicated across space. Players should maintain a safe distance. For players with limited mobility, the game can easily be played seated, with the focus shifting to upper body posture, facial expressions, breath, and vocal tone. The facilitator should monitor intensity to ensure the energetic offers remain playful and do not cross into genuine aggression.

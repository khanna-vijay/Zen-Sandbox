# Context Cascade

![Context Cascade — infographic](img/ISR039.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy competitive short-form challenge where improvisers build a scene around a central mimed object or location that is repeatedly transformed by unexpected audience suggestions.

## Overview
A high-energy competitive short-form challenge where improvisers build a scene around a central mimed object or location that is repeatedly transformed by unexpected audience suggestions. Players must instantly justify these sudden shifts in reality using sharp 'Yes, And' skills and precise physical object work.

## Setup
Played head-to-head. 2-4 players per team. A Referee manages the game, a whistle, and the audience. All props are strictly mimed. The Referee asks the audience for a mundane object or location to serve as the 'Focal Point' for each team's scene.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a mundane Focal Point from the audience (e.g., 'a coffee mug' or 'a bus stop') for Team A.
2. Team A begins a 2-to-3 minute scene, clearly establishing the Focal Point through mimed object work, environment building, and dialogue.
3. After 30-45 seconds of solid scene building, the Referee blows the whistle, freezing the action.
4. The Referee asks the audience for a new Adjective (e.g., 'haunted', 'sticky') or a new Function (e.g., 'time-travels', 'makes julienne fries') to apply to the Focal Point.
5. The Referee calls 'Unfreeze!' and the players must instantly integrate and justify this new reality into the ongoing scene, altering their physicality and emotional reactions without breaking the narrative.
6. The Referee repeats this interruption exactly 2 or 3 times per scene, ensuring players have enough time between whistles to actually explore the new reality rather than just reacting to a barrage of prompts.
7. Team B then takes the stage, gets a new mundane Focal Point, and plays their own scene with 2-3 audience-suggested transformations.
8. At the end of both scenes, the Referee calls for an audience applause vote. The team with the loudest applause wins 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- Force immediate justification and 'Yes, And' under pressure.
- Place a strong emphasis on physical object work and environmental adaptation.
- Maintain a streamlined pacing that balances rapid reactions with actual scene-building.
- The Referee strictly enforces a clean-content foul (loss of points/warning) for any crude or inappropriate audience suggestions, immediately asking for a clean alternative.

## Variations
- Shared Cascade: Both teams play in a single continuous scene. When the whistle blows, the Referee gets a new adjective/function, and players from the sidelines tag out the active players to take over the newly transformed scene.
- Genre Cascade: Instead of changing the object's adjective or function, the Referee asks the audience for a new film or theatrical genre (e.g., 'Western', 'Sci-Fi') that the scene must instantly adopt.

## Why It Works
It forces immediate justification and 'Yes, And' under pressure, while developing strong physical object work and environmental adaptation skills.

## Safety & Inclusion
All props must be mimed to prevent physical hazards during rapid scene shifts. The Referee acts as a strict gatekeeper for audience suggestions, using a clean-content foul to maintain a clean, all-ages environment. Players should respect physical boundaries and avoid aggressive physical choices when 'justifying' a dangerous new object trait.

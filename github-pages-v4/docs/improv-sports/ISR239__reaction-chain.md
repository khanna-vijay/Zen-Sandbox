# Reaction Chain

![Reaction Chain — infographic](img/ISR239.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams share a single scene, passing physical and emotional 'triggers' to each other like a baton.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two teams share a single scene, passing physical and emotional 'triggers' to each other like a baton. As players enter, they are endowed with a specific, scene-grounded trigger by the player before them. To prevent chaos, only three players are allowed on stage at once; as new players enter, veterans must justify their exits. The goal is to build a hilarious, cohesive narrative while instantly committing to escalating, interconnected endowments.

## Setup
Two teams (e.g., Red and Blue). One Referee. A suggestion of an everyday location and a mundane activity. Maximum 3 players on stage at any time. No props or set pieces required.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets an everyday location and a mundane opening activity from the audience.
2. Player 1 (Team Red) enters and begins the scene, establishing the base reality and character.
3. The Referee blows the whistle, pauses the scene, and gives Player 1 their First Trigger. This trigger MUST be grounded in the scene's reality (e.g., in a bakery: 'Every time you hear the oven ding, you panic that the bread is burning').
4. Player 1 resumes the scene, playing naturally until the trigger condition occurs, at which point they must react fully and physically.
5. Player 2 (Team Blue) enters. Player 1 must organically endow Player 2 with a NEW, scene-related trigger through dialogue or action (e.g., 'Careful, the flour in here makes you sneeze uncontrollably!').
6. Player 2 accepts the endowment, reacts to their new trigger while advancing the scene, and prepares to endow Player 3 (Team Red) when they enter.
7. The Rule of Three: There is a strict maximum of three players on stage. When a fourth player enters, the player who has been on stage the longest must immediately justify their exit (e.g., 'I need to go check the stockroom!').
8. The game continues alternating teams for 3 to 4 minutes, creating a continuous chain of scene-grounded triggers, reactions, and justified exits.
9. The Referee awards 1 point for every successfully passed and executed scene-grounded trigger. The Referee deducts 1 point for 'Random Gag' fouls or for failing to justify an exit.
10. The Referee blows the whistle to call 'Scene' when the narrative reaches a satisfying comedic peak or time expires. At the end of the scene, the audience cheers for the team that best committed to their triggers and the narrative, awarding a 3-point bonus to the winner.

## Coaching Notes
- Force immediate physical and emotional commitment to endowments.
- Maintain narrative integrity by enforcing scene-grounded triggers over random, disconnected gags.
- Watch out for 'Random Gag' fouls (triggers that ignore the scene's reality, like suddenly acting like a crab in a post office).
- Prevent stage clutter and chaos by strictly enforcing the 3-person cap and mandatory justified exits.

## Variations
- Secret Triggers: Instead of speaking the trigger out loud, players write it on a slip of paper and hand it to the entering player as a 'note' or 'receipt', who must read it, announce it in character, and instantly justify it.
- Emotion Chain: A simpler warm-up or novice version where players only pass strong emotional points of view rather than complex conditional triggers (e.g., 'You are absolutely furious about the price of stamps').

## Why It Works
It forces immediate physical and emotional commitment to endowments while maintaining narrative integrity by enforcing scene-grounded triggers over random, disconnected gags. The strict stage limit prevents clutter and keeps the audience engaged with constant new elements.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical safety is paramount; triggers must not require dangerous acrobatics, falling, or inappropriate physical contact. The Referee will immediately call a clean-content foul for any blue, unsafe, or non-family-friendly content. Triggers can and should be adapted to the physical abilities of the players, utilizing vocal or emotional reactions if physical ones are inaccessible.

# The Unreliable Architect

![The Unreliable Architect — infographic](img/IS127.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An unseen Architect periodically interjects with unassailable narrative truths that a Character must instantly accept, justify, and embody.

## Overview
The Unreliable Architect is an improv game where a Character navigates a scene while an unseen Architect periodically interjects with unassailable narrative truths that redefine the Character's reality. The Character's objective is to instantly accept, justify, and embody these new, often contradictory, realities. This forces them to constantly 'Be Changed' and integrate unexpected twists into the ongoing story.

## Setup
Two improvisers participate: one plays the Character, the other plays the Architect. The Character begins alone on stage, typically having received an audience suggestion for their initial situation. The Architect stands off-stage or in a designated non-playing area, visible to the audience but not interacting directly with the Character's physical scene.

## How to Play
1. Phase 1 (Character Establishes): The Character begins the scene, establishing the location, an initial action, and perhaps a clear objective or emotional state.
2. Phase 2 (Architect Intervenes): At any point, the Architect interjects with a short, descriptive narrative statement that introduces a new, unassailable truth about the Character's internal world, past, current circumstances, or a hidden aspect of the scene's reality.
3. Phase 3 (Character Embodies and Justifies): The Character instantly 'Yes, Ands' the Architect's statement, immediately incorporating the new truth into their performance, dialogue, physical actions, and emotional state.
4. If the Architect's statement contradicts something the Character just said or did, the Character must find a justification for the discrepancy.
5. Phase 4 (Continuous Play): The Character continues to drive the scene forward, providing new material. The Architect continues to interject periodically (e.g., every 15-30 seconds, or when a strong beat is established), layering on new, sometimes contradictory, narrative details.
6. Ending: The game concludes after a set time (e.g., 3-5 minutes) or when the host calls 'Scene!'

## Coaching Notes
- The Architect's statements are essentially pre-packaged mistakes or unexpected curveballs; the Character must treat these as absolute truths, regardless of how they destabilize the established reality.
- The Architect should listen to the Character's scene and build upon it or twist it in a way that provides new dramatic or comedic opportunities, taking risks by introducing bold, sometimes outlandish, ideas.
- The Character must take every gift from the Architect and elevate it, making the Architect's bold choices look brilliant.
- The Character must constantly re-evaluate their identity, motivations, and the reality of the scene based on external narrative input, practicing the skill of 'Being Changed'.
- The Architect holds the narrative 'truth' status; the Character, while driving the physical scene, must defer to this higher narrative authority.
- Architect statements are not direct commands but declarations of 'what is' (e.g., 'What they didn't know was that this seemingly innocent object held the key to a deeply buried family secret.').

## Why It Works
This collaborative exercise rigorously tests improvisers' narrative agility, risk-taking, and their ability to build compelling, surprising, and humorous narratives by embracing radical acceptance and justification of meta-narrative interventions. It highlights narrative development, embracing mistakes, collaborative scene-building, and the profound art of 'Being Changed'.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the Architect's interventions respect the physical and emotional boundaries of the Character. Avoid dictating actions that force the Character into unsafe or non-consensual physical situations, focusing instead on internal narrative and situational truths.

# Emotional Circuit

![Emotional Circuit — infographic](img/ISR195.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass a sound and movement around a circle, completely transforming the emotional impulse with every person.

## Overview
Emotional Circuit is a fast-paced ensemble warm-up where players pass a sound and movement around a circle, but with a major twist: the impulse must completely change with every person. Instead of just passing a static zap or a clap, players must let the energy they receive affect them, then instantly transform it into a brand new emotion, sound, and gesture to pass to the next person. It trains actors to get out of their heads, stop planning, and allow themselves to be changed by their scene partners.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle facing inward. The facilitator establishes whether the group will use the Touch or Non-Touch version. No props or stage space are required.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator starts by turning to the person on their left and giving them a strong, distinct sound and physical gesture (for example, a heavy sigh with slumped shoulders, or a sharp gasp while throwing hands up).
2. The receiving player must take in that energy, make eye contact, and let the emotion affect them physically.
3. Without pausing to think, the receiver immediately transforms that energy into a completely new sound and gesture, and passes it to the next person on their left.
4. The next person receives, transforms, and passes it on, creating a continuous, unbroken flow of changing energy around the circle.
5. If a player hesitates, plans ahead, or just copies what they received, the facilitator gently calls 'Reset' and starts a new impulse to keep the pace fast and intuitive.

## Coaching Notes
- Keep the energy high by calling out encouragements like 'Don't think, just react!', 'Let it change you!', or 'Faster!' to prevent players from intellectualizing their choices.
- Watch for players planning their reactions in advance; encourage them to wait until they receive the impulse before deciding what to do.
- If the flow stops or someone just copies the previous gesture, gently call 'Reset' and throw a new impulse to keep the pace fast and intuitive.

## Variations
- Non-Touch (The Default): Instead of physical contact, players pass the impulse using strong, direct eye contact and leaning their energy toward the receiver. This is the safest and most accessible way to play.
- Volume Knob: The facilitator calls out 'Turn it up!' or 'Turn it down!' while the circuit is moving, forcing the players to make their emotional transformations increasingly massive and theatrical, or incredibly subtle and internal.

## Why It Works
It forces players out of their heads and into their bodies, directly training the vital improv skill of allowing oneself to be changed by an offer.

## Safety & Inclusion
To ensure total accessibility and safety, use the Non-Touch variation (eye contact and proximity) as the standard default for new or mixed-experience groups. If playing a version that involves physical contact (like a gentle tap on the shoulder to pass the impulse), the facilitator MUST conduct a clear consent check first. Players must be able to opt out of being touched without explanation, and boundaries must be strictly respected.

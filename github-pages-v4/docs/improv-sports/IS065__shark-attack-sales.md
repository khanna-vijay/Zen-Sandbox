# Shark Attack Sales

![Shark Attack Sales — infographic](img/IS065.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two entrepreneurial teams collaboratively pitch an outlandish, audience-suggested product or service to a notoriously cynical 'Shark Investor'.

## Overview
Two teams of 'entrepreneurs' face off in a high-stakes improv pitch, vying for an 'investment' from the notoriously cynical and quick-witted Referee, who embodies a formidable 'Shark Investor.' They must collaboratively pitch an outlandish, audience-suggested product or service, demonstrating strong creativity and commitment to secure a point. The goal is to survive the 'Shark's' brutal interrogation and win the 'investment' (point) for that round.

## Setup
The two competing teams (Red and Blue) line up on either side of the stage. The Referee stands prominently in the center, embodying the 'Shark Investor' persona from the outset - perhaps with a stern expression, folded arms, or pacing.

## How to Play
1. The Referee (as the Shark) dramatically requests an audience suggestion for a new, revolutionary product or service, emphasizing the need for something truly unique, utterly absurd, or hilariously unworkable.
2. The Shark proactively guides and filters audience suggestions with comedic flair, ensuring novelty and strict family-friendliness, then announces the chosen suggestion.
3. The Shark points to Team Red. All active players on the Red Team step forward, with 2-3 players primarily foregrounding the pitch.
4. Team Red is given a short, strict time limit (e.g., 90-120 seconds), enforced by a visible timer or the Shark's interruptions.
5. Players must immediately launch into their pitch, collaboratively building and selling the concept, actively 'Yes, And-ing' each other's ideas, adding features, benefits, and justifications.
6. Players must demonstrate the product through clear, imaginative object work, showing how it works, its unique features, and its impact.
7. The Shark actively paces, scowls, interrupts, and throws constant curveballs, demanding demonstrations or asking tough questions. Players must quickly and cleverly 'Yes, And' the Shark's challenges, justifying absurdities on the fly.
8. Immediately after Team Red's time expires or is cut short, Team Blue steps forward for an equal timed turn to present their unique pitch for the exact same audience-suggested product.
9. Team Blue cannot simply repeat Red's ideas but must find a fresh angle or set of features, demonstrating their unique entrepreneurial vision, while facing the same Shark interrogation.
10. After both teams have pitched, the Shark dramatically assesses their performance and awards one point to the team whose pitch was superior for that round, based on creativity, collaboration, problem-solving, and object work.
11. The game typically consists of 3-5 distinct 'product rounds'. The team with the most points ('investments') at the end is declared the winner, and the final score is added to the overall match score.

## Coaching Notes
- The Shark must proactively vet audience suggestions to ensure they are clean and accessible.
- Players must use clear and creative object work to demonstrate the product's features and functionality, even if it's just air.
- Rapidly create logical (within the game's context) explanations for absurd products and answer the Shark's tough questions.
- Groaner Foul: Administered by the Shark for fumbling, rambling, ignoring the Ref's direct questions/demands, repeating a previous joke from the other team, or breaking character/commitment. The Shark dramatically 'financially penalizes' them, potentially costing them the round's point.
- Clean-content foul: Administered immediately and severely if any player attempts blue humor, swearing, or inappropriate innuendo. The Shark instantly shuts down the pitch, declares it a catastrophic failure, and awards a significant penalty (forfeiture of the round and possible point deduction).

## Why It Works
It tests 'Yes, And' for collaborative team pitching, active listening to teammates and the Shark, primary object work for demonstrating features, rapid justification/problem solving, endowing imaginary products, and strong character work as earnest entrepreneurs and the formidable Shark. The relentless competitive pressure keeps the pace high and players on their toes.

## Safety & Inclusion
Strict family-friendliness is enforced through the Referee's proactive filtering of audience suggestions and the immediate, severe application of the clean-content foul for any blue humor, swearing, or inappropriate innuendo.

# Resonance Relay

![Resonance Relay — infographic](img/ISR186.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A hyper-focused ensemble warm-up where players pass a micro-movement across a circle using only eye contact.

## Overview
A hyper-focused ensemble warm-up where players pass a micro-movement or subtle physical impulse across a circle using only eye contact. By breaking away from a predictable sequential pass and allowing the impulse to zip randomly across the space, the game trains hyper-vigilance and group connection. The facilitator can dynamically scale the energy by calling out modifiers, turning tiny physical shifts into a highly engaging, unpredictable web of shared movement.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a loose circle, at least arm's length apart, in a quiet space. The facilitator stands just outside the circle to observe, side-coach, and call out dynamic modifiers.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator designates one player to be the Originator for the first round.
2. The Originator chooses a specific, subtle internal state and gives it a micro-movement (e.g., a sharp intake of breath, a weight shift, a jaw clench, or a single finger twitch).
3. The Originator broadcasts this impulse to ANY player across the circle by making strong eye contact and performing the micro-movement.
4. The Receiver must observe closely, internalize the impulse, and immediately mirror that exact micro-movement back to acknowledge receipt.
5. Without pausing, the Receiver turns to a new person across the circle, makes eye contact, and passes the impulse on.
6. The impulse bounces randomly across the group. Players must maintain soft focus on the entire circle so they are always ready to receive.
7. The facilitator can call out modifiers: 'Magnify' (the next receiver makes the movement slightly larger), 'Micro' (shrink it back down to barely perceptible), or 'Mutate' (intentionally shift the movement to a new body part while keeping the same rhythm).
8. If the impulse is dropped or missed, the group collectively claps once to celebrate the failure, and the player who missed it immediately starts a new micro-movement.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach with prompts like 'Keep soft focus,' 'Don't plan your pass,' and 'Let the movement evolve naturally' to maintain a brisk, highly engaged pace.
- Ensure players maintain cross-circle focus; zipping the impulse randomly demands absolute, continuous attention from every player.
- Use dynamic scaling calls like 'Magnify' and 'Micro' to keep the exercise novel and prevent it from becoming visually stagnant.
- Ground abstract energy exercises into concrete, observable physical reality by ensuring the micro-movements are physically verifiable.
- Enforce the group clap on a dropped impulse to remove performance anxiety and reinforce ensemble support.

## Variations
- Contagion: The Originator passes the impulse to two people simultaneously (using eye contact and a directional micro-movement). They each pass it to two more, multiplying the impulse until the entire group is performing the movement in unison.
- Room Scale: Instead of a circle, players walk randomly through the space at a neutral pace. They must pass the micro-movement to someone passing by them without breaking their walking stride.
- Emotional Resonance: The Originator attaches a subtle micro-emotion to the movement (e.g., a suppressed giggle, a hidden wince, a suspicious squint). Receivers pass both the physical movement and the emotional intention.

## Why It Works
Cross-circle focus demands absolute, continuous attention from every player, training hyper-vigilance and group connection. It grounds abstract energy exercises into concrete, observable physical reality, while dynamic scaling prevents visual stagnation.

## Safety & Inclusion
Completely safe and requires zero physical contact. Fully accessible for players with mobility limitations, as it can be played entirely seated and impulses can be adapted to whatever movements are comfortable. The 'celebrate failure' mechanic actively removes the pressure to perform perfectly, making it a psychologically safe space for all experience levels.

# Expert Express

![Expert Express — infographic](img/IS066.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams race against a 90-second clock to deliver an expert presentation on an absurd topic using an unexpected prop.

## Overview
Expert Express is a fast-paced, competitive improv game where two teams race against a 90-second clock to deliver an 'expert' presentation on an absurd, audience-suggested 'Impossible Topic.' Each team must collaboratively explain their ridiculous subject in a specified 'Presentation Style' while ingeniously integrating an 'Essential Prop' into their demonstration. The game rewards clear, collaborative communication of outlandish ideas, creative object work, and unwavering commitment to the comedic style.

## Setup
Form two teams (Red vs. Blue), typically 2-3 players per team. A Referee stands centrally with a whistle and scoreboard. Place a 'prop box' with random, innocuous objects (e.g., rubber chicken, traffic cone, feather duster) on stage, or ask the audience for a small object.

## How to Play
1. The Referee prompts the audience for three distinct suggestions (The 'Triple Threat'): an 'Impossible Topic', a 'Presentation Style', and an 'Essential Prop'.
2. Each team gets a loud, timed 15-second 'Eureka! Huddle' to quickly brainstorm how they will collaboratively tackle the prompt.
3. One team steps forward. The Referee blows the whistle, starting a 90-second timer.
4. The team launches into their presentation, jointly explaining the Impossible Topic in the given Presentation Style, actively incorporating the Essential Prop throughout.
5. Players must 'Yes, And' each other's points to build a coherent explanation, and the prop must be used as an illustrative tool, not just held.
6. After 90 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle to stop the team.
7. The second team then takes the stage and performs their presentation using the exact same Triple Threat.
8. The Referee awards points based on clarity, comedic impact, object work, style commitment, and team collaboration, deducting points for any fouls called.

## Coaching Notes
- Call a 'Muddled Message Foul' if the team's explanation becomes genuinely unclear, contradictory, excessively rambling, or completely abandons the central Impossible Topic.
- Call an 'Object Amnesia Foul' if the Essential Prop is introduced but then forgotten, neglected, or not actively integrated into the explanation.
- Call a 'Style Slip Foul' if the chosen Presentation Style is dropped, inconsistent, or not fully committed to by the players.
- Remind players that object work is crucial; the prop must be a living, breathing part of the presentation.
- Encourage active listening so individual contributions flow cohesively with the overall 'expert' presentation.

## Variations
- New Prompts per Team: Instead of both teams using the same Triple Threat, get a completely new set of suggestions for the second team.

## Why It Works
The game forces fast-paced, high-energy collaboration under a strict time limit. It tests fundamental 'Yes, And' skills, active listening, strong endowment of character, and inventive object work, all while requiring players to maintain focus on delivering a clear (even if ridiculous) explanation.

## Safety & Inclusion
The game inherently champions family-friendly humor through absurd topics and wholesome styles. The Referee maintains strict oversight with a 'content foul' ready for any inappropriate language or gestures, ensuring the content remains clean and accessible.

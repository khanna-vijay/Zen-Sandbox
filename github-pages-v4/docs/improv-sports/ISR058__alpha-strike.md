# Alpha Strike

![Alpha Strike — infographic](img/ISR058.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams face off in a high-speed shared scene where every spoken line must begin with a random letter revealed on a flashcard.

## Overview
Alpha Strike is a high-speed, competitive short-form game where two teams face off in a single shared scene. Every spoken line must begin with a random letter revealed on a flashcard by the Referee just seconds before they speak. By using unpredictable Strike Cards, the game eliminates pre-planning, demanding lightning-fast vocabulary access, fierce justification, and seamless Yes, And skills.

## Setup
4 players (2 per team) and a Referee to run the game, track fouls, and pace the scene. A deck of 15-20 large, high-contrast Strike Cards (each featuring a single random letter), pre-shuffled. The audience provides a suggestion for a high-stakes relationship or location to ground the scene.

## How to Play
1. One player from the Red Team and one from the Blue Team step center stage to begin the scene based on the audience suggestion.
2. The Referee stands downstage center (visible to players but not blocking the audience) and holds up the first Strike Card.
3. The first speaker must instantly deliver a line of dialogue where the very first word begins with the letter on the card.
4. As soon as the line is delivered, the Referee drops the card and immediately holds up the next random letter for the responding player.
5. Play alternates back and forth. Players must instantly justify the letter they are given while maintaining the narrative, emotional, and physical reality of the scene.
6. The Referee watches for three specific fouls: Stutter-Step (hesitating for more than two seconds), Typo (using the wrong starting letter), or Block (saying a word that fits the letter but completely ignores or destroys the scene's reality).
7. If a foul occurs, the Referee blows the whistle. The offending team loses a point, and the offending player is instantly tagged out by their teammate.
8. The incoming teammate must seamlessly take over the same character, adopt their physical posture, and successfully deliver a line starting with the exact same letter that caused the foul.
9. As the scene progresses, the Referee speeds up the card reveals, forcing faster reactions and heightening the energy until the deck is depleted or a natural scene conclusion is reached.
10. The game starts with a 0-0 tie. The Referee awards 1 point to the opposing team every time a player commits a foul. If a teammate makes a brilliant, seamless tag-in save, the Referee can award 2 Bonus Points. At the end of the deck, the team with the most points wins.

## Coaching Notes
- The Strike Card mechanic keeps players' heads up and completely prevents pre-planning, forcing true spontaneity.
- The Penalty Tag-In mechanic preserves the scene's narrative momentum rather than stopping the game for a reset.
- Rapid-fire pacing naturally escalates the physical and vocal energy of the scene.
- The audience acts as the ultimate judge of Blocks, groaning or cheering to influence the Referee's whistle.

## Variations
- Whiteboard Countdown: Write 15 random letters on a whiteboard upfront. Players can see the dreaded X or Q approaching, creating comedic anticipation.
- Elimination All-Skate: Played with 6 to 8 players in a single line. The Referee holds up cards rapidly; if a player hesitates or misses, they are eliminated. The last player standing wins 5 points for their team.

## Why It Works
The game eliminates pre-planning, demanding lightning-fast vocabulary access, fierce justification, and seamless Yes, And skills as players fight to keep the narrative alive.

## Safety & Inclusion
To support players with dyslexia, ESL backgrounds, or processing differences, Referees must accept phonetic matches (e.g., F for Phantom or C for Kite). The goal is speed and narrative flow, not a strict spelling bee. High-contrast, large-print cards ensure visibility for players with low vision. The Referee enforces standard clean-content rules, blowing the whistle and calling a foul for any inappropriate or unsafe content, ensuring the game remains family-friendly.

# The Symbiotic Construct

![The Symbiotic Construct — infographic](img/ISR202.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A non-verbal, eyes-closed ensemble exercise where players maintain continuous physical contact to form a single organism.

## Overview
A non-verbal, eyes-closed ensemble exercise where players maintain continuous physical contact to form a single 'organism.' Relying entirely on kinesthetic empathy and shared impulses, the group must move, breathe, and collectively morph their bodies into abstract shapes and concepts without speaking or seeing.

## Setup
Clear the room entirely of tripping hazards. Gather 6 to 12 players and 1 facilitator. No props or blindfolds are needed. Before starting, the facilitator must conduct a brief consent check to establish safe touch boundaries (e.g., 'hands, arms, and shoulders only').

## How to Play
1. Consent & Connection: Players stand in a cluster, close their eyes, and reach out to establish physical contact with each other. Everyone must be touching at least one other person at all times to form a single, unbroken chain or web.
2. Calibration (Minute 1): The facilitator instructs the 'organism' to simply breathe together. Once breathing synchronizes, the facilitator asks the group to slowly shift their collective weight (e.g., leaning slightly left, then right) without breaking contact, letting the impulse ripple through the group.
3. The Morph (Minutes 2-4): The facilitator calls out an abstract prompt (e.g., 'A blooming flower,' 'A thunderstorm,' 'A giant machine,' 'Sorrow').
4. Shared Impulse: Without speaking or opening their eyes, the group must intuitively shift, lower, rise, or extend their bodies to represent the prompt. Players must practice 'kinesthetic empathy'—listening to the physical pushes and pulls of their neighbors rather than trying to individually direct the shape.
5. The Reveal (Minute 5): Once the group's movement naturally settles into a final tableau, the facilitator calls, 'Freeze, and open your eyes!'
6. Debrief: The group briefly looks at the 'Symbiotic Construct' they have formed with their bodies, shakes it out, and discusses how impulses traveled through the group before trying a new prompt.

## Coaching Notes
- The 'win' is the ensemble's ability to surrender individual control, trust the group's physical impulses, and experience the surprise of the final tableau.
- Encourage players to 'listen' with their bodies rather than their ears, relying on kinesthetic empathy.
- Remind players that no single person can lead; the shape must emerge from collective compromise.
- Keep the exercise short and focused (around 5 minutes) to prevent physical fatigue while maximizing concentration.

## Variations
- Seated Symbiosis: For a lower-energy or highly accessible variation, the group sits in a tight circle of chairs. They connect via hands and arms, and build the 'construct' using only their upper bodies. Excellent for focusing purely on micro-impulses.
- The Amoeba (Travel Variation): Instead of forming a stationary shape, the group must travel from one side of the room to the other as a single mass. To ensure safety, the 'outer ring' of players keeps their eyes open to steer, while the 'inner core' of players keeps their eyes closed.

## Why It Works
Closing eyes removes visual planning and forces players out of their heads. It builds an emergent group mind by relying entirely on kinesthetic empathy, ensuring no single player can lead and that every movement is a collective compromise.

## Safety & Inclusion
Crucial: Floor objects must be entirely removed to eliminate severe tripping hazards. Blindfolds are replaced by simply closing eyes, allowing any player to instantly open their eyes if they feel dizzy, unsafe, or off-balance. Explicit consent protocols must be established before play: players dictate where they are comfortable being touched (defaulting to hands/arms/shoulders). A 'Tap Out' mechanic is included: if a player needs to break contact or step out, they simply squeeze their neighbor's hand twice and step back.

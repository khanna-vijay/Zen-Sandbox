# Narrative Resonance

![Narrative Resonance — infographic](img/ISR281.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers build a dramatic scene and occasionally step out to deliver an in-character aside revealing a hidden backstory or secret emotion.

## Overview
A dramatic, narrative-focused game where performers build a scene and occasionally step out of the action to deliver an in-character aside—a 'resonant echo' revealing a hidden backstory or secret emotion. The host triggers these asides a maximum of one or two times per scene, forcing the players to immediately weave this new, high-stakes information into the ongoing narrative, instantly changing the characters' relationship and the scene's trajectory.

## Setup
Requires 2 to 3 players and a host/director. No props or whiteboards are needed. The audience provides a unique setting (e.g., 'A lighthouse during a storm') and a core relationship dynamic or unspoken conflict (e.g., 'One owes the other a life debt').

## How to Play
1. 1. Initiation: Players begin a grounded scene based on the audience suggestions, establishing the environment and hinting at the underlying tension without giving away the whole story.
2. 2. The First Echo: After 1 to 2 minutes, when the scene has a solid foundation but needs a narrative push, the host calls 'Echo!' (or rings a bell) and points to one player.
3. 3. The Aside: The scene freezes. The chosen player steps forward and delivers a brief, 1 to 2 sentence in-character aside or inner monologue revealing a crucial piece of backstory or a hidden motivation (e.g., 'I never told him I was the one who cut the brakes.').
4. 4. Integration: The host briefly repeats the core of the echo to cement it for the audience and players (e.g., 'You cut the brakes. Action!'). The scene immediately resumes.
5. 5. Being Changed: Both players must now 'Yes, And' this new reality. The character who spoke the aside might act with more guilt or sudden boldness, while the other character remains unaware but reacts to the shift in energy, or the secret is immediately confessed.
6. 6. The Second Echo (Optional): After another 1 to 2 minutes of escalating tension, the host may call one final 'Echo!' for the other player, adding a final layer of complexity or a twist.
7. 7. Resolution: The host allows the scene to breathe. The players use the remaining 1 to 2 minutes to drive the scene to a satisfying, emotionally resonant climax based on the revealed secrets, ending the scene organically.

## Coaching Notes
- Mechanics explicitly force players to be changed by new information in real-time.
- Uses in-character asides to maintain theatrical momentum without breaking the fourth wall.
- Limits interruptions to let the scene breathe and escalate naturally.
- In a competitive improv format, a panel of judges scores the scene from 1 to 5 based on narrative structure, emotional commitment, and how seamlessly the actors integrated the 'echoes' to change their characters.

## Variations
- Audience Echoes: For a more chaotic or comedic variant, the host collects 3 to 4 secrets from the audience beforehand on slips of paper. When the host calls 'Echo!', they read one aloud, and the chosen player must justify it as their own internal monologue or backstory.
- The Monologue Snap: Instead of the host calling it, a player can voluntarily step out and snap their fingers to deliver an aside whenever they feel the narrative needs a jolt, giving advanced players full control of the pacing.

## Why It Works
Provides a structured way to practice building deep, dramatic narratives and high-stakes relationships.

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game often deals with dramatic tension and secrets, establish a clear boundary beforehand regarding triggering content (e.g., no non-consensual violence or abuse). Players should use standard safety tools, like tapping out or a 'bow out' gesture, if a revealed 'echo' pushes the scene into uncomfortable territory. Keep the drama focused on the relationship dynamics rather than trauma.

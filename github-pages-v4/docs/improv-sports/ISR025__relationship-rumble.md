# Relationship Rumble

![Relationship Rumble — infographic](img/ISR025.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players use an audience-suggested physical trigger to reveal their relationship baggage without using verbal exposition.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two players from opposing teams share a scene based on an audience-suggested relationship and location. To prevent boring exposition, players are forbidden from talking about their past. Instead, an audience-suggested 'Physical Trigger' forces their underlying relationship baggage to instantly boil over into a massive emotional or physical reaction.

## Setup
Requires 2 players (one from the Red team, one from the Blue team) and a Referee. No props or special tech are needed; all objects and environments are mimed. The stage is set with two optional chairs.

## How to Play
1. The Referee calls one player from each team to the stage.
2. The Referee asks the audience for a 'Relationship' (e.g., Rival bakers) and a 'Location' (e.g., A submarine).
3. The Referee asks the audience for a mundane 'Physical Trigger' (e.g., 'Whenever someone sighs' or 'Whenever a player sits down').
4. The players begin a 2-3 minute scene, pursuing the scene's objective while embodying the relationship dynamics.
5. Players cannot use 'talking-head exposition' (e.g., 'Remember ten years ago when you...'); they must establish shared history entirely through subtext, tone, and physical behavior.
6. Whenever the Physical Trigger occurs organically, the players must instantly exhibit a heightened emotional or physical reaction driven by their shared history, justifying it using only present circumstances.
7. If a player starts verbally dumping backstory, the Referee blows the whistle and calls an 'Exposition Foul' (-1 point).
8. The scene escalates as triggers happen more frequently, and the Referee calls 'Time!' on a high point of comedic tension.
9. The Referee asks the audience to applaud for each player; the winner earns 5 points for their team. Standard fouls (content foul, groaner) also apply.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus on justifying absurd situations through grounded, character-driven relationship dynamics.
- Eliminate talking-head exposition by relying on the physical/emotional trigger to reveal history.
- Keep the setup brisk with just two prompts to prevent the show from dragging before the scene begins.
- Ensure players justify their sudden outbursts or shifts using only the present circumstances of the scene.

## Variations
- Emotional Trigger: Instead of a physical action, the audience suggests a specific emotional trigger (e.g., 'Whenever someone gets defensive'). This forces players to actively listen for tone shifts.
- Rehearsal Mode (The 'Baggage' Drill): Played in workshops without the trigger or points. A pure training exercise challenging improvisers to establish deep shared history entirely through subtext, status, and physical distance. The facilitator stops the scene the exact moment someone says 'Remember when...' to build the habit of showing rather than telling.

## Why It Works
It forces players to rely on subtext, tone, and physical behavior to establish shared history, breaking the habit of using 'talking-head exposition' while generating high-stakes comedic tension through the trigger mechanism.

## Safety & Inclusion
When soliciting the Physical Trigger, the Referee must ensure it is safe and consensual. Reject triggers that force unwanted physical contact (e.g., 'whenever they kiss' or 'whenever they hit each other') and guide the audience toward safe, self-contained actions like 'crossing arms', 'adjusting glasses', or 'sitting down'. The standard content foul ensures the content remains clean, all-ages, and respectful.

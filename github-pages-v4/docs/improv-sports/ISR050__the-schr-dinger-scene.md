# The Schrödinger Scene

![The Schrödinger Scene — infographic](img/ISR050.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two players perform a scene while a Narrator occasionally interjects to instantly rewrite one specific fact of their reality.

## Overview
Two players perform a scene while a third player (the Narrator) occasionally interjects to instantly rewrite ONE specific fact of their reality. The scene players must immediately justify, physically embody, and emotionally commit to this new truth while keeping the core narrative moving forward.

## Setup
Three players from one team (two Scene Players, one Narrator) and a Referee to oversee, score, and call fouls. No props or set pieces required. The Narrator stands off to the side with a microphone. The Referee gets a location and a relationship from the audience to start the scene.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a location and a character relationship from the audience, then blows the whistle to begin.
2. The two Scene Players initiate the scene, establishing their characters, the environment, and the base reality. The Narrator must let them play uninterrupted for at least 30-45 seconds to build a foundation.
3. The Narrator interjects by saying 'Correction:' or 'Actually...' and changes EXACTLY ONE established fact in the scene. This must be a single, specific alteration to an object, a physical state, an emotion, or a relationship dynamic.
4. The intervention MUST build on the existing narrative rather than destroying it. The Narrator cannot teleport the players to a new location or introduce random non-sequiturs; they must heighten what is already there.
5. Upon hearing the correction, the Scene Players must instantly accept the new reality without hesitation or argument. They must physically and emotionally embody the change and justify it within the ongoing story.
6. The Narrator must wait for the players to fully explore the new reality (at least 4-5 lines of dialogue) before interjecting again. A standard 3-minute scene should have no more than 3 to 4 interventions total.
7. The scene concludes when the Referee blows the whistle, usually after a successful justification of the final intervention.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee awards 5 points for every seamless, immediate physical and emotional transformation by the Scene Players.
- The audience provides the initial suggestion, and their laughter/applause dictates the success of the players' justifications.
- The Referee should call a 'Laundry List Foul' (-5 points) if the Narrator gives more than one change at a time.
- The Referee should call a 'Bulldozer Foul' (-5 points) if the Narrator destroys the narrative instead of heightening it.
- Encourage immediate physical and emotional commitment from the scene players.

## Variations
- Audience Omniscience: Before the game, the Referee collects 4-5 specific 'corrections' from the audience on slips of paper. The Narrator must draw and read these blindly at appropriate moments in the scene.
- Genre Schrödinger: Instead of changing physical facts, the Narrator changes the cinematic style or genre of the scene (e.g., 'Correction: This is actually a gritty Noir film').

## Why It Works
It practices extreme 'Yes, And' by demanding players accept reality shifts as absolute truth, and provides high audience appeal in watching improvisers struggle with and justify sudden constraints.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Narrator must never dictate a physical change that forces players into unsafe positions, non-consensual physical contact, or inappropriate/blue content. If a prompt violates safety or boundaries, the Referee must immediately blow the whistle, call a 'content foul,' deduct points, and force the Narrator to provide a clean, safe alternative.

# The Echo Chamber

![The Echo Chamber — infographic](img/IS004.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams repeatedly re-enact a short, original scene through wildly different audience-suggested genres and stylistic filters.

## Overview
The Echo Chamber challenges two teams to re-enact a short, previously performed scene multiple times. With each re-enactment, the audience provides a new, dramatically different genre or filter through which the scene must be played. The comedy comes from seeing the same core events twisted through wildly different stylistic lenses, showcasing the players' lightning-fast adaptation skills.

## Setup
Requires 4-6 players total (2-3 per team), one Referee, and one Scorekeeper. Played on a standard competitive short-form stage with no props (all objects and environments are mimed). The audience is crucial for providing genre/filter suggestions.

## How to Play
1. The Referee establishes a 20-30 second 'original' scene, either recalling a memorable moment from a previous game in the match or taking a simple audience suggestion for a location and two characters.
2. Two players perform this 'original' scene quickly, establishing a clear action, interaction, or conflict.
3. The Referee calls 'FREEZE!' at the end of the initial scene and solicits a genre or filter suggestion from the audience (e.g., Film Noir, Romantic Comedy, 8-bit Video Game).
4. The Referee designates one team (e.g., 'Team Red!') and announces they must re-enact the scene in the style of the audience suggestion.
5. The designated players immediately replay the events and core dialogue points of the original scene for 30-45 seconds, applying the stylistic, physical, vocal, and character choices of the new genre.
6. The Referee calls 'FREEZE!' again, gets a new genre suggestion from the audience, and points to the other team to re-enact the scene in the new style.
7. This rapid rotation continues for 3-5 rounds, alternating between teams, ensuring the core actions and basic plot points remain recognizably consistent across all transformations.

## Coaching Notes
- Award 5-10 points per round based on Genre Fidelity (commitment to tropes/physicality), Scene Coherence (recognizability of the original plot), Humor & Audience Reaction, and Quick Adaptation & Creativity.
- Call a 'Lost in Translation' Foul (5-point deduction) if a team completely loses the plot or core events of the original scene, making the narrative unrecognizable.
- Call a 'Genre Drift' Foul (3-point deduction) if a team starts strongly in one genre but inadvertently drifts into another style without comedic intent.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' (3-5 point deduction) for obvious, uninspired, or overly forced puns that cause the audience to groan.
- Remind players that the crucial element is maintaining the actions and basic plot points of the original 20-second scene; the comedy arises from the dissonance between the familiar plot and the wildly new style.
- Allow other team members to support or play minor roles if the genre allows (e.g., background extras in a reality show).

## Variations
- Meta-Echo: Instead of generating a new scene on the spot, the Referee chooses a short, memorable scene (20-30 seconds) that just occurred in a previous game within the current match to add a meta-comedic layer and reward audience recall.

## Why It Works
It requires intense active listening to both the original scene's plot and the audience's genre suggestion, lightning-quick 'Yes, And' to the scene's premise and the new genre's demands, strong and instantaneous character endowments, imaginative object work, and dynamic physical choices.

## Safety & Inclusion
Enforce a clean-content foul for any inappropriate or offensive content, resulting in an automatic 10-point deduction and immediate Referee intervention. Ensure the game remains family-friendly by deriving humor from clever genre parody rather than inappropriate content.

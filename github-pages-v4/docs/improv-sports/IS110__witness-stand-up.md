# Witness Stand-Up

![Witness Stand-Up — infographic](img/IS110.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Collaboratively build a chaotic narrative from multiple, biased perspectives as each witness reveals new, contradictory details through their unique character quirk.

## Overview
Witness Stand-Up is an improvisational game where players collaboratively build a chaotic narrative from multiple, biased perspectives. Based on an audience-suggested mildly dramatic event, each player embodies an assigned character quirk and takes a turn as a witness recounting their version of what happened. Inspired by competitive short-form matches, the game features teams, a referee, and scoring for strong character work, inventive contradictions, and escalating absurdity.

## Setup
Requires 4-6 improvisers split into two teams of 2-3 players. Set up two designated witness stands or marked spots on opposite sides of a central event zone, optionally using simple chairs. A Ref/Host is essential for scoring, pacing, and suggestions, along with a bell or buzzer to signal scene changes.

## How to Play
1. The Ref asks the audience for a suggestion of a recent, mildly dramatic, or slightly embarrassing public event to serve as the central focus.
2. Teams huddle briefly while the Ref elicits unique character quirks or personality filters from the audience, assigning one distinct quirk to each player. Players briefly embody their quirk for their team to strategize, ensuring the opposing team is also aware of each assigned quirk.
3. Round 1: The Ref calls the first witness from Team A to the stand. The player embodies their quirk, establishes initial facts colored by their bias, and mimes in the central event zone for 60-90 seconds before the Ref calls time.
4. Round 2: The Ref calls a witness from Team B to their stand. This player must offer new details and contradict, challenge, or significantly reframe at least one piece of information from the previous testimony, justifying their truth through their quirk for 60-90 seconds.
5. Subsequent Rounds: Play alternates between teams until all players have testified. Each new witness must embody their quirk, introduce new information, contradict a previous fact, and justify their version.
6. Optional Rebuttal: The Ref may choose a pair of witnesses (one from each team) for a 30-45 second rapid-fire closing argument, directly challenging the opposing testimony.
7. The Ref awards points for strong comedic choices, character work, inventive contradictions, and collaborative storytelling to determine the winning team.

## Coaching Notes
- Award points for strong character work, clear and humorous contributions, inventive details derived from the quirk, and creative justification.
- Issue Foul calls if a witness simply agrees with previous testimony without adding a new, conflicting angle, or if they drop their character.
- Encourage active listening; players must listen to previous testimonies to find opportunities for contradiction and ingeniously justify their alternative facts.
- Look for opportunities for comedic callbacks and running gags as details get warped and repeated.
- Playfully poll the audience on which witness was most convincing, though the Ref retains final scoring power.

## Why It Works
The constant re-interpretation of a single event ensures the story never stagnates, always moving into funnier, more absurd territory. It provides an excellent platform for players to create and sustain distinct characters, requiring active listening to find opportunities for contradiction and justification.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the mildly dramatic or slightly embarrassing audience suggestions remain lighthearted and avoid crossing into genuine trauma or sensitive real-world events. Maintain a playful, collaborative spirit even during confrontational rebuttals.

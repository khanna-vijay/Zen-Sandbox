# The Improv Archeologist

![The Improv Archeologist — infographic](img/IS015.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams race to excavate and interpret a 'Forgotten Civilization' based on audience-suggested mundane 'artifacts.'

## Overview
Two teams of 'Improv Archeologists' race to uncover, interpret, and reanimate the lost history of a mysterious 'Forgotten Civilization.' Guided by random, mundane 'artifacts' suggested by the audience, they must use quick wit, vivid object work, and seamless 'Yes, And' to build a humorous, coherent, and utterly unique narrative of a past world.

## Setup
Divide 4-6 players into two teams (e.g., 'Excavation Crew A' and 'Excavation Crew B'). Establish a designated 'dig site' at center stage where all 'unearthing' occurs. The game is exclusively mimed; no physical props are used. The audience will provide the initial suggestion for the civilization and individual mundane artifacts throughout the game.

## How to Play
1. 1. Establishing the World (Host): The host starts by asking the audience for a name for a unique, fictional, and slightly absurd 'Forgotten Civilization' (e.g., 'The Sock Puppet Empire').
2. 2. Initial Excavation (Team A): Two players from Team A step forward to the dig site. The host asks the audience for a mundane, everyday object (e.g., 'a rubber duck').
3. 3. Player 1 (Archeologist A) begins to physically 'excavate' (mime digging, brushing away dust) and unearths the suggested object, immediately interpreting it within the context of the Forgotten Civilization.
4. 4. Player 2 (Archeologist B) immediately 'Yes, Ands' by elaborating on the discovery, revealing its cultural significance, or demonstrating its use through physical action and dialogue.
5. 5. Team A has 90 seconds for their turn to establish an initial scene and interaction related to their discovery.
6. 6. Relay Discovery (Team B): After Team A's 90 seconds, Team B sends two players. The host asks the audience for a new mundane object.
7. 7. Team B excavates this new artifact, interpreting it in the context of the same Forgotten Civilization. They must build on any previously established information from Team A where it makes sense.
8. 8. 'Unexpected Find!' (Dynamic Pacing): Throughout each team's turn, the host may shout 'Unexpected Find!' The audience immediately yells out another mundane object, and the performing team must instantly integrate it into their ongoing scene.
9. 9. Scoring: Award 1 point for vivid object work, clever interpretation, seamless 'Yes, And', dynamic character endowment, and fast integration. Award 2 bonus points for a 'Golden Artifact' (brilliant interpretation) or a 'Rosetta Stone Moment' (connecting disparate elements from both teams).
10. 10. Fouls: Deduct points for a 'Dust Bunny' foul (-2 for ignoring established elements or new finds), 'Anachronism Alert' (-3 for contradicting the world without witty justification), Groaner foul (-1 for cheap puns), and content foul (-5 for inappropriate content).

## Coaching Notes
- Focus on vivid object work: clear, creative miming of the 'artifact' is central to the game.
- Listen actively: it is crucial for building a coherent world, remembering previous discoveries, and weaving them into the ongoing narrative.
- Commit to dynamic character endowment: create memorable, distinct characters (as either archeologists or civilization members) through voice, physicality, and attitude.
- Watch out for the 'Dust Bunny' Foul: failing to incorporate an 'Unexpected Find!' or ignoring established elements.
- Watch out for the 'Anachronism Alert' Foul: introducing something that contradicts the civilization without a witty justification.
- Referees: look for 'Crossover Moments' where one team brilliantly connects to a previous discovery from the other team to award bonus points.

## Why It Works
The game relies on foundational 'Yes, And' mechanics and active listening to build a coherent world. The constant demand for quick, creative interpretations of mundane objects ensures dynamic, high-energy play, while shifting suggestions force rapid character creation and narrative agility.

## Safety & Inclusion
Enforce the standard content foul (-5 points and potential expulsion) for anything offensive, non-family-friendly, or inappropriate. The game's focus on absurd civilizations naturally avoids blue humor, keeping content safe for all ages.

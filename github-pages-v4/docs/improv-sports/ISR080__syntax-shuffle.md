# Syntax Shuffle

![Syntax Shuffle — infographic](img/ISR080.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-stress, fast-paced competitive game where two improvisers navigate a hilarious linguistic minefield of swapping verbal constraints.

## Overview
A competitive short-form game for exactly two master-level improvisers. They perform a scene while adhering to individual verbal constraints. A referee blows a whistle to swap their constraints on the fly or call fouls, creating a hilarious linguistic minefield without breaking the scene's narrative.

## Setup
Requires exactly 2 players and 1 Referee with a whistle. No props, tech, or stage setup needed. The Referee comes prepared with a 'Constraint Bank' of 8-10 pre-written verbal constraints on a clipboard (e.g., 'Must rhyme,' 'Questions only,' 'Three-word sentences,' 'Start every sentence with the last letter of the previous word').

## How to Play
1. Get the Scene Suggestion: The Referee asks the audience for a location or relationship to ground the scene (e.g., 'Two astronauts fixing a satellite').
2. Top Up the Bank: To keep pacing fast, the Referee asks the audience for just 2 or 3 distinct verbal constraints to add to their pre-written Constraint Bank.
3. Assign Initial Constraints: The Referee assigns one unique constraint to Player A and a different one to Player B.
4. Play the Scene: The players begin the scene. They must strictly adhere to their assigned constraints while actively building the narrative and relationship.
5. The Switch: At any point, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'SWITCH!' followed by a new constraint for one of the players. The player must instantly adapt, even mid-sentence.
6. Calling Fouls: If a player breaks their constraint, hesitates too long, or completely abandons the scene's reality, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'FOUL!' The offending player loses a point, is immediately given a new constraint, and the scene resumes.
7. Ending the Game: The game ends after 3 minutes, a predetermined number of fouls, or on a massive laugh. Players start with 5 points and lose 1 point per foul. The Referee tallies the fouls to determine the winner.

## Coaching Notes
- Strictly limit the game to 2 players to ensure the audience can easily track the constraints and the scene doesn't devolve into chaos.
- The Referee drives the pacing with sharp whistle blows for Switches and Fouls.
- Zero mid-scene positive scoring: the Referee ONLY interrupts to call a 'SWITCH' or a 'FOUL'. Announcing positive points steps on laughs and ruins the scene's flow.
- Use a pre-set constraint bank supplemented by only 2-3 audience suggestions to keep the setup under 45 seconds.

## Variations
- Syntax Swap: Instead of pulling a new constraint from the bank, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'SWAP!', forcing the two players to instantly trade their current constraints with each other.
- Physical Syntax: Mix physical constraints into the bank (e.g., 'Must always be touching the floor,' 'Cannot bend elbows,' 'Must maintain unbroken eye contact') alongside the verbal ones for an added layer of challenge.

## Why It Works
It develops extreme linguistic agility, rapid adaptation, and the ability to maintain narrative focus and relationship under high cognitive load.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee must strictly vet the pre-set bank and audience suggestions to ensure they are accessible and clean. Veto any constraints that demand offensive accents, cultural stereotypes, or physical actions that could be unsafe for the performers. The focus is on linguistic agility, not punching down.

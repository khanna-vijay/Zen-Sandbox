# Paradigm Shift

![Paradigm Shift — infographic](img/IS090.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players must seamlessly integrate dramatic, overarching rule changes introduced mid-scene while sustaining a coherent story.

## Overview
Paradigm Shift is a scene game where a designated 'Shifter' periodically calls out new, overarching rules that instantly alter the reality of the scene. Players must immediately accept and justify these changes, weaving them into the ongoing narrative without breaking character. The challenge lies in maintaining a coherent story and relationship dynamics despite the constantly shifting circumstances.

## Setup
Designate one person as the 'Shifter' (a host or non-playing improvisor). Get an audience suggestion (e.g., location, relationship, or object) for the playing team of 2-3 improvisors.

## How to Play
1. Players begin an improvised scene based on the suggestion, establishing characters, an environment, and a core objective or conflict.
2. At intervals (e.g., every 45-60 seconds) or at their discretion, the Shifter calls out 'SHIFT!' followed by a new, overarching rule that instantly alters the scene's reality.
3. Upon hearing the new rule, improvisors must immediately accept it and seamlessly integrate it into their ongoing performance without breaking character.
4. Characters should implicitly (or occasionally explicitly) justify why this new rule has suddenly come into being within their world.
5. All subsequent dialogue and physical actions must conform to and build upon the new rule, showing its practical implications.
6. The most recent 'SHIFT!' defines the current reality and takes precedence over previous rules.
7. The scene continues for 3-5 minutes, incorporating multiple shifts, until the host calls 'Scene!'

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: Players cannot simply acknowledge the rule; they must weave it into the ongoing story, advancing plot and character relationships.
- Embracing Unexpected Turns: Performers must cheerfully accept these shifts and transform them into exciting new narrative opportunities.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: Actively observe and build on your partner's immediate reactions and justifications for the shift, supporting their interpretation.
- Being Changed: Instantly and fully embody the new reality dictated by the rule change, both physically and emotionally.
- Justification: Find a plausible reason within the scene's new context for why the reality has shifted, making it organic rather than arbitrary.
- Status Play: Consider how the rule changes affect character hierarchies and power dynamics within the scene.

## Why It Works
It forces improvisors to be truly present and deeply collaborative, challenging core skills like 'Being Changed', 'Yes, And', and 'Justification' through instantaneous mental and physical pivoting.

## Safety & Inclusion
When introducing rules that affect physics, sensory input, or physical contact (e.g., 'touching swaps items'), ensure players maintain physical safety and respect personal boundaries.

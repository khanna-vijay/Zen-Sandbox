# The Narrative Contagion

![The Narrative Contagion — infographic](img/IS125.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players collaboratively build an evolving narrative driven by a mutating 'contagion' that infects and transforms characters.

## Overview
The Narrative Contagion is an improvisation game where players collaboratively build a story around a mutating internal trait or compulsion. As the contagion organically spreads between characters, it must mutate into a new, often more extreme or inverted form. This challenges improvisers to deeply engage with being changed, justify new behaviors, and collectively drive a dynamic narrative.

## Setup
Use a relatively bare stage. The host introduces a basic scene context or location and announces a unique, abstract 'Narrative Contagion' (e.g., 'Compulsive Truth-Telling' or 'An Inability to Feel Shame'). The host then designates one improviser as 'Patient Zero' to be immediately infected.

## How to Play
1. The designated Patient Zero immediately begins to manifest the initial contagion through their dialogue, actions, and character choices.
2. The contagion must spread organically from character to character within the scene as a natural consequence of their interaction, not a forced handover.
3. When the contagion passes to a new character, or when a character has been embodying it for a significant period, it must mutate.
4. Each new character who contracts it must interpret and manifest it in a slightly different, evolved way reflecting their own character's logic, the narrative, and the previous manifestation.
5. Players evolve the contagion using conceptual vectors like Amplification, Inversion, Diversion/Specificity, Internalization/Externalization, or Transference.
6. The host monitors the spread and can interject with 'Contagion Check!' to clarify the current form, or 'Mutation Vector Prompt!' to suggest a specific evolution.
7. The scene concludes when the host determines a satisfying narrative arc has been explored, typically after 5 to 7 minutes.

## Coaching Notes
- Performers must take significant risks in interpreting the contagion, especially its mutations; unexpected interpretations become narrative gold.
- Players must actively accept the contagion from their partner, then creatively evolve it to provide rich, new material for the next player.
- Focus on 'Being Changed': authentically and visibly transform your character's behavior, beliefs, or motivations as you become infected.
- Use 'Yes, And' to accept the contagion's previous form and build upon it with a new mutation.
- Constantly justify why your character is embodying this new, mutated trait and how it makes sense within the evolving reality of the scene.

## Why It Works
The contagion's spread and mutation act as the narrative engine, forcing characters into evolving arcs and creating escalating dramatic tension. It pushes for deeper character development as personalities and objectives develop organically in response to the contagion, strongly emphasizing making your partner look good.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the 'contagions' chosen by the host do not mimic real-world traumatic illnesses or phobias in a way that could be triggering; keep the traits abstract, behavioral, or absurd. Players should respect physical boundaries when 'infecting' others.

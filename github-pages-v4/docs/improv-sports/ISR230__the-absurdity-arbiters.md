# The Absurdity Arbiters

![The Absurdity Arbiters — infographic](img/ISR230.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams battle to explain a bizarre mystery using random props, specific genres, and rapid-fire cross-examinations.

## Overview
A competitive short-form game where two teams improvise scenes to explain an absurd, audience-suggested mystery. To keep players on their toes, each team must incorporate a random audience-suggested prop as key evidence and present their theory in a specific genre. After both scenes, teams face off in a rapid-fire cross-examination round to debunk each other's theories before the referee awards points for creativity and hilarious leaps of logic.

## Setup
The Referee gets an 'Unsolved Bizarre Mystery' from the audience (e.g., 'Why do all the neighborhood dogs bark at the mail carrier's left shoe?'). The Referee then gets two random, unrelated objects from the audience (e.g., a spatula, a disco ball) and assigns one to each team to use as crucial evidence. Finally, the audience suggests a distinct genre or format for each team's scene (e.g., Film Noir, Nature Documentary).

## How to Play
1. The Referee announces the mystery, assigns the mandatory evidence props, and gives each team their specific genre.
2. Team Red performs a 2-minute scene in their assigned genre, explaining the mystery and seamlessly integrating their assigned prop as the definitive proof of their theory.
3. Team Blue performs a 2-minute scene in their assigned genre, offering a completely different explanation for the same mystery, using their specific prop.
4. The Rebuttal Round: Both teams step forward. Team Red has 15 seconds to cross-examine Team Blue, pointing out a logical flaw in Blue's scene.
5. Team Blue has 15 seconds to improvise a defense.
6. The teams switch roles: Team Blue has 15 seconds to attack Team Red, and Team Red has 15 seconds to defend.
7. The Referee delivers a rapid, one-sentence verdict on which theory was more delightfully absurd and awards points (1-5) to each team based on genre commitment, seamless use of the random prop, and how well they defended their theory in the rebuttal, heavily influenced by audience applause.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee must watch for the 'Too Plausible Foul' during scenes, blowing the whistle and deducting a point if a team's explanation becomes too mundane or logical, forcing them to instantly heighten the absurdity.
- Encourage the audience to cheer loudly for the best takedowns and defenses during the Rebuttal Round.
- Ensure teams seamlessly integrate the random prop as definitive proof rather than just mentioning it in passing.

## Variations
- Expert Witnesses: Instead of a full scene, teams present their theory as an expert panel, taking rapid-fire questions from the Referee and the opposing team.
- Silent Evidence: The assigned evidence prop cannot be spoken about directly; its importance must be entirely demonstrated through physical object work and reactions.

## Why It Works
The game forces players to balance multiple constraints (genre, prop integration, and narrative explanation) while maintaining a fast-paced, competitive energy. The rapid-fire cross-examination builds skills in active listening, spotting logical flaws, and instant justification.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee strictly enforces a clean, all-ages environment by calling a content foul for any inappropriate content. The game relies on intellectual absurdity rather than physical exertion, making it highly accessible. When soliciting mysteries, the Referee should steer the audience away from real-world tragedies or sensitive topics, focusing on whimsical, everyday surrealism.

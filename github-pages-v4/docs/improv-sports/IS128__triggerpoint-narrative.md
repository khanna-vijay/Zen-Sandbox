# Triggerpoint Narrative

![Triggerpoint Narrative — infographic](img/IS128.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Improvisers build a story that is constantly reshaped by unexpected environmental and character changes triggered by their own words and actions.

## Overview
Triggerpoint Narrative is a short-form game where improvisers collaboratively build a story that is constantly reshaped by external interventions. A Trigger Master observes the scene for specific Triggerpoints which, when activated, immediately introduce a pre-determined, surprising Catalytic Effect. Performers must instantly and seamlessly integrate these unexpected new realities to maintain narrative coherence.

## Setup
A host or designated judge acts as the Trigger Master. Before the scene begins, the Trigger Master secretly prepares a list of 3-5 Triggerpoint conditions (specific words, physical actions, or emotional expressions) paired with Catalytic Effects (environmental shifts, character changes, or genre twists). The audience provides a suggestion for the initial scene premise, such as a location and relationship.

## How to Play
1. The improvisers begin the scene based on the audience suggestion, establishing their characters and initial premise.
2. The Trigger Master closely monitors the performance for any of the pre-set hidden Triggerpoints.
3. When a performer utters a trigger word/phrase, performs a trigger action, or clearly expresses a trigger emotion, the Trigger Master immediately shouts TRIGGERPOINT! and announces the corresponding Catalytic Effect clearly and audibly.
4. Performers must immediately and seamlessly integrate this new reality into the scene, justifying the change within the narrative and continuing the story as if this new element was always part of their world.
5. The scene continues, potentially encountering multiple Triggerpoints and their associated Catalytic Effects, increasing the challenge with each new layer of reality.
6. The scene concludes either after a pre-determined time limit (e.g., 3-5 minutes) or after a specific number of Triggerpoints have been activated (e.g., 3 or 4).

## Coaching Notes
- Immediate Acceptance: Performers must accept and embrace the Catalytic Effect instantly and without hesitation; resistance or questioning the change is against the spirit of the game.
- Narrative Integration: The goal is not merely to acknowledge the change but to actively weave it into the ongoing story, using the new element to develop new plot points, character motivations, conflicts, or comedic situations.
- No Deliberate Triggering: Performers should not overtly try to guess or deliberately trigger effects; the magic lies in the spontaneous nature of the triggers.
- Trigger Master's Discretion: The Trigger Master's decision on whether a Triggerpoint has been met is final, and they should aim for clear, unambiguous triggers.
- Audience Clarity: The Trigger Master's announcement of the Catalytic Effect must be clear and audible for the entire audience to ensure their engagement.
- Status Play & Physicality: Catalytic Effects can dramatically alter character status or demand immediate physical adjustments; performers must instantly adapt their status, interactions, and physicality.

## Why It Works
The Catalytic Effects act as narrative accelerants, forcing the story into new directions and preventing stagnation. It challenges the paramount skill of 'being changed' by forcing performers to constantly shed their previous reality and embody a new one. It also demands a heightened level of 'Yes, And' and mutual support to navigate rapidly shifting scene realities, transforming potential chaos into creative opportunities.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical changes (like sudden gravity shifts or aging) are played safely without risking injury. Players should respect physical boundaries and consent when adapting to sudden relationship or character shifts.

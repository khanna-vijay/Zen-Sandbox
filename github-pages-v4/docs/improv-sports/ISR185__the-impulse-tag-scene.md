# The Impulse Tag Scene

![The Impulse Tag Scene — infographic](img/ISR185.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced circle game where players are non-physically tagged to instantly step in and contribute a single, authentic impulse to an ongoing scene.

## Overview
A fast-paced, facilitator-led workshop exercise where participants stand in a circle around an active playing space. A few Anchor Players maintain an ongoing, physical scene in the center. Players on the perimeter are non-physically tagged to instantly step in, contribute a single impulse, and immediately tag someone else.

## Setup
All participants stand in a large, unified circle. The empty space in the middle serves as the stage. 2 to 3 experienced improvisers (or the facilitator and volunteers) step into the center to act as the Anchor Players. No chairs or audience seating are used.

## How to Play
1. The Anchor Players begin a simple, open-ended scene in the center of the circle, focusing on clear physical environment/location work with minimal dialogue.
2. Once the scene is established, an Anchor Player makes strong eye contact with anyone standing in the circle, points clearly at them, and says 'Tag!' (or 'You!').
3. The tagged player immediately steps into the center. Without planning or overthinking, they must offer ONE single physical or verbal reaction that furthers the scene based on what they just witnessed.
4. The Anchor Players instantly accept, justify, and integrate this new offer into the ongoing scene, treating it as a brilliant addition.
5. As soon as the tagged player has delivered their single impulse, they must immediately make eye contact with a new person in the circle, point at them, say 'Tag!', and step back into their spot on the perimeter.
6. The newly tagged player steps in, offers their impulse, tags the next person, and leaves. The game continues in a rapid, continuous flow.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Instantly identify and deliver a single authentic impulse to the active scene, then immediately pass the focus to another player.
- The facilitator keeps the energy high by calling out reminders: 'Don't think, just step in!', 'Support that offer!', 'Keep the tags moving!', and 'React with your body first!'
- Emphasize physical impulses and authentic reactions over witty dialogue or plot-building.
- Strong focus on ensemble support, as Anchor Players must instantly validate and justify rapid-fire offers.

## Variations
- Sound & Motion Tag (Warm-up): Remove the scene context entirely. Anchor players start a repetitive sound and motion. Tagged players step in, mirror the motion, naturally transform it into something new, tag the next person, and step out.
- Genre Tag (Advanced): The facilitator calls out different genres or emotional states (e.g., 'Sci-Fi!', 'Melodrama!', 'Panic!') every 60 seconds. The Anchor Players adapt the base scene, and all incoming impulses must immediately match the new style.

## Why It Works
The game demands immediate, non-intellectual reactions, fostering intuitive presence and collaborative storytelling without time for self-judgment. It eliminates the time needed for intellectual planning.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical tagging is entirely replaced with strong eye contact, pointing, and a verbal cue ('Tag!') to prevent physical boundary violations and accidental collisions. If a player in the circle needs to opt out or take a break, they can simply cross their arms over their chest, signaling others not to tag them.

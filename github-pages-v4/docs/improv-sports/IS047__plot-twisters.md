# Plot Twisters!

![Plot Twisters! — infographic](img/IS047.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two improvisers perform a scene while their teammates frequently interrupt with mandatory, unpredictable plot changes and genre shifts.

## Overview
Plot Twisters! is an improv game where two 'Twistees' perform a scene, while their 'Plot Twister' teammates frequently interrupt with mandatory, unpredictable plot changes, challenges, or genre shifts. The Twistees must immediately 'Yes, And' and seamlessly integrate each twist into their ongoing narrative. Meanwhile, the Plot Twisters aim to deliver concise, challenging commands that heighten absurdity and compel innovative improvisational responses.

## Setup
Divide 4-6 players into two teams: two 'Twistees' playing the scene and 2-4 'Plot Twisters' on the sidelines. A Referee manages the game. Use an open, neutral performance space with no props. Get an audience suggestion for a specific location, a relationship between the two main scene players, and a shared goal or objective.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a Location, Relationship, and Objective from the audience.
2. Two Twistees take the stage and begin their scene, establishing their characters and the situation.
3. At random, frequent intervals (every 15-30 seconds), the Referee yells 'Twist!' and points to one of the Plot Twisters on the side of the stage.
4. The designated Plot Twister immediately shouts a clear, brief command or statement that drastically alters an element of the scene (e.g., a genre shift, a new phobia, reversed gravity).
5. The Twistees must immediately and unconditionally accept the Twist and integrate it into the scene physically, verbally, and emotionally.
6. The game continues for 2-3 minutes, with Plot Twisters constantly injecting new challenges to escalate the scene's stakes or absurdity.

## Coaching Notes
- Plot Twisters should strive for variety in their commands, escalating the scene's stakes or absurdity rather than repeating similar twists.
- Plot Twisters must listen to the scene and aim for twists that enhance rather than derail, unless the derailment is the point comically.
- Twistees must justify the twist and weave it into their characters' objective, finding the humor in the adaptation.
- Call a negation foul on Twistees for negating, questioning, or breaking character to acknowledge the absurdity.
- Call a clarity foul on Plot Twisters for commands that are too long, unclear, impossible to act out, or repetitive.
- Call a cheap-joke foul for obvious or cheap jokes, especially puns that do not advance the scene.
- Encourage strong physical choices and object work, especially for twists like changing gravity or sentient objects, to prevent talking heads.

## Variations
- Wild Card Twists: Audience members can provide twists by writing ideas on cards beforehand, which the Referee reads at their discretion.

## Why It Works
This game is an absolute masterclass in 'Yes, And!', forcing players to accept every bizarre plot development as truth and build upon it. It demands active listening from both sides and dynamic pacing, as every twist inherently alters the scene's emotional intensity or stakes, revealing character resilience and quirky coping mechanisms.

## Safety & Inclusion
Enforce a clean-content foul strictly for any adult humor or suggestive content to keep the game family-friendly. Ensure physical safety when acting out extreme twists like reversed gravity or slapstick elements.

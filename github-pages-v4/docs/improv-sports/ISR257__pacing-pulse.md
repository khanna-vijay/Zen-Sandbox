# Pacing Pulse

![Pacing Pulse — infographic](img/ISR257.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy, competitive short-form game where teams perform a scene while instantly adapting their physical and vocal speed to a Referee's live commands.

## Overview
Teams perform a scene while instantly adapting their physical and vocal speed to a Referee's live commands. From agonizingly slow-motion to chaotic fast-forward, players must maintain the narrative and their characters' integrity through constant, abrupt shifts in momentum.

## Setup
Two players from the first team and two players from the second team. The Referee gets a separate 'Who, What, Where' suggestion for each team. No props or set pieces are required.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a suggestion for the first team and starts their 90-second clock, calling 'Pace: Normal!' to begin the scene.
2. As the scene progresses, the Referee blows a whistle and shouts a new Pace Call. The players must instantly snap to the new speed, adjusting both their dialogue rate and physical movement.
3. The standard Pace Calls are: SLOW-MOTION (exaggeratedly slow speech and movement, resisting gravity), NORMAL (standard conversational pace), FAST-FORWARD (rapid, clipped dialogue and quick, jerky movements), FRENZIED (maximum safe speed, chaotic energy, high-pitched delivery), and PAUSE (players freeze completely in silence).
4. Players must continue to advance the story and 'Yes, And' their partner's offers regardless of the current speed.
5. After 90 seconds, the Referee calls 'Scene!' and resets with a new suggestion for the second team, who then perform their 90-second scene under the same rules.
6. At the end of both scenes, the Referee asks the audience to applaud for the first team, then the second team. The team that receives the loudest applause for their pacing control and storytelling wins a flat 5 points.
7. Standard fouls (a clean-content call for inappropriate content, a bad pun foul for bad puns, or a delay foul for failing to instantly match a Pace Call) result in a 1-point deduction called live by the Referee.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee should aim for 5 to 7 Pace Calls per scene, ideally interrupting players mid-action or mid-sentence for maximum comedic effect.
- The pacing should affect how players do things, not what they are doing. Ensure players do not drop the narrative.
- Demand instantaneous physical and vocal transitions. If players lag, call a delay foul to enforce the rule.
- Remind players that this is a high-energy crowd-pleaser that tests active listening and physical control under pressure.

## Variations
- Rewind Pulse: Add 'REWIND' to the Pace Calls, forcing players to walk and talk backward through the exact actions they just performed until the Referee calls a forward speed.
- Emotional Pulse: Instead of speed, the Referee calls out escalating levels of a specific emotion (e.g., 'Anger Level 1', 'Anger Level 10') while the players maintain the scene.

## Why It Works
It tests active listening and physical control under pressure, forcing players to commit fully to physical and vocal choices while maintaining the narrative integrity of the scene.

## Safety & Inclusion
Frenzied and Fast-Forward paces can lead to accidental collisions. Players must maintain spatial awareness and avoid running or swinging their arms wildly. 'Frenzied' should mean high energy and rapid delivery, not reckless movement. For players with mobility limitations, 'Pace' can be interpreted purely vocally and through facial expressions rather than full-body movement.

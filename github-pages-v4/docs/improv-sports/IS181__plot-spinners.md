# Plot Spinners

![Plot Spinners — infographic](img/IS181.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Weave a wildly contradictory tale as you constantly rewrite each other's realities on the fly!

## Overview
Plot Spinners is a competitive improv game where players aim to weave a wildly contradictory tale by constantly rewriting each other's realities on the fly. One or two active players begin a scene from an audience suggestion, but at any moment, an off-stage Spinner can interject, freezing the action to narrate a new, often contradictory or bizarre fact about the characters or environment. The active players must then immediately accept and integrate this twist, justifying the altered reality and continuing the scene seamlessly.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 improvisers (3 is ideal for faster rotation) and one dedicated scorekeeper, referee, or MC. Use an open playing space; basic stage blocks or chairs can be used for set dressing. Get a simple scene premise from the audience, such as a location, relationship, or activity.

## How to Play
1. The MC asks the audience for a simple location, relationship, or activity to kick off the scene.
2. Two (or three) improvisers step forward to begin the scene based on the suggestion, while the remaining player(s) stand off to the side, ready to spin.
3. The active players start the scene, establishing characters and initial context, treating everything said as true for their current reality.
4. At any point, an off-stage player can shout 'Spin It!' or step forward to the edge of the playing space. The active scene freezes immediately.
5. The Spinner steps slightly into the scene and narrates a new, contradictory, or unexpected fact about the active players or surroundings that overrides something previously established.
6. As soon as the Spinner finishes their narration, they step back to their off-stage position.
7. The active players must immediately accept this new fact as absolute truth, seamlessly integrating and justifying how it is now true, and continuing the scene.
8. The scene continues with the active players incorporating the twist until another off-stage player feels inspired to 'Spin It!' again.
9. The referee or MC awards +1 to +3 points for the creativity of the Spin, and +1 to +3 points for how seamlessly and humorously the active players integrate the new reality.
10. The game runs for a set amount of time (e.g., 5-7 minutes) or until a predetermined number of spins have occurred. The team or player with the most points wins.

## Coaching Notes
- Reward quick thinking, strong justification, and imaginative storytelling under pressure.
- Apply a 1-point 'No Spin' penalty if a Spinner narrates something that isn't truly a twist or doesn't contradict the established reality significantly.
- Apply a 1-point 'No Integrate' penalty if active players ignore the twist, fail to justify it, or question its validity.
- Apply a 1-point 'Walk Through' penalty if the Spinner steps too far into the scene or interacts directly as a character during their narration.

## Variations
- Team vs. Team: Play competitively with two teams (e.g., Red vs. Blue). Players rotate as Spinners and Integrators within their team's turn or across teams.

## Why It Works
It draws heavily from rapid-fire competitive match energy and a focus on adapting to unexpected narrative shifts. It forces players to practice the 'yes, and' principle under pressure, creating a wonderfully chaotic scene that celebrates the improviser's ability to create order from glorious disarray.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during sudden freezes. Players should respect personal boundaries when narrating new realities for other players' characters, avoiding content that crosses established lines or forces players into uncomfortable physical or emotional situations.

# The Labyrinth of Whispers

![The Labyrinth of Whispers — infographic](img/ISR182.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A blindfolded Walker navigates a shifting human maze guided solely by the modulated sounds of a Guide Whisperer.

## Overview
An ensemble-building sensory exercise where a blindfolded 'Walker' navigates a shifting human maze. They are guided to a specific destination solely by the modulated sounds of a 'Guide Whisperer', while the rest of the ensemble forms safe, dynamic walls.

## Setup
Clear the room entirely of all trip hazards, chairs, and bags. Form a group of 8-15 players. Ask for a volunteer 'Walker' (who explicitly consents to being blindfolded or closing their eyes) and a 'Guide Whisperer'. The remaining players are 'Architects'. Designate a clear 'Destination' point at the opposite end of the room where the Guide will stand or lead the Walker.

## How to Play
1. The Facilitator sets a strict 2-minute time limit for the Walker to reach the Destination, giving the exercise a clear arc and preventing aimless wandering.
2. The Architects scatter between the Walker and the Destination, creating a human maze. They must hold up 'Bumper Hands' (palms facing outward at chest level, elbows bent) to act as soft, safe physical boundaries.
3. The Guide Whisperer begins their sound. They must use concrete vocal modulations to steer the Walker. Examples: A smooth, continuous hum means 'walk straight forward'. Staccato clicks or pops mean 'turn'. Increasing volume means 'you are getting closer'. A sudden drop in pitch or a sharp hiss means 'stop, obstacle ahead'.
4. The Walker moves slowly through the space, following the Guide's sound. If they veer off course, they will gently make contact with the Architects' 'Bumper Hands', which act as physical walls.
5. The Architects slowly and silently shift their positions to dynamically change the maze, forcing the Walker to rely entirely on the Guide's changing sounds to find the new open path.
6. The Facilitator side-coaches the time ('One minute left!', 'Thirty seconds!') to build gentle urgency.
7. If the Walker reaches the Guide within the time limit, the ensemble celebrates. If time runs out, the Facilitator calls 'Time', and the Walker removes their blindfold to see their progress. Rotate roles and repeat.

## Coaching Notes
- The Facilitator leads the room, side-coaching the Guide to vary their sounds ('Change your pitch to help them turn!').
- Ensure the Architects maintain safe, gentle boundaries with their Bumper Hands at all times.

## Variations
- Siren's Call: Add a second Guide making a different sound (a 'decoy') at a different destination. The Walker must focus only on their designated Guide's sound to reach the correct destination, enhancing selective listening.
- Environmental Maze: Architects can make soft, continuous environmental sounds (like wind, ocean waves, or forest rustling) to create a sensory landscape. The Walker must filter out this background noise to hear the Guide Whisperer.

## Why It Works
Deepens auditory listening and spatial awareness by removing visual cues. Forces entirely non-verbal communication and collaboration between the Guide and the Walker. Builds significant physical trust and sensitivity within an ensemble. Teaches players how to modulate vocal qualities (pitch, volume, tempo) to convey specific meaning.

## Safety & Inclusion
Strict Safety Protocols: 1) The Facilitator or ANY player can yell 'Hold!' at any time to instantly freeze all movement if a collision or trip is imminent. 2) Architects MUST use 'Bumper Hands' (palms out, elbows bent) to gently block the Walker - no grabbing, pulling, or pushing. 3) Blindfolds are strictly opt-in; players may simply close their eyes or look softly at the floor if they prefer not to have their vision completely obscured.

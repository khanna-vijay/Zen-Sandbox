# Synapse Scramble

![Synapse Scramble — infographic](img/ISR053.jpg){ .game-hero }

> The audience acts as a collective director, voting on massive plot twists that improvisers must instantly justify.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where the Referee pauses the scene exactly twice to let the audience vote on a massive plot twist. Using low-tech voting like cheering or colored cards, the audience acts as a collective director. The improvisers must instantly and seamlessly justify the winning twist, no matter how absurd, earning points for their agility and narrative justification.

## Setup
Two teams of 2 to 3 players. A Referee equipped with a whistle and two distinct visual voting tools (like a Red card and a Blue card, or simply using their hands to measure cheers). No special tech required. The Referee prepares a list of wild, contrasting 'Synapse Prompts' (plot twists, genre shifts, or character reveals) ahead of time.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a standard suggestion from the audience (e.g., a location or relationship) to start the scene.
2. The active team begins the scene, establishing the base reality for about 45 to 60 seconds.
3. The Referee blows the whistle and yells 'SYNAPSE!' to freeze the action.
4. The Referee presents two drastically different options for what happens next (e.g., 'Does the boss reveal they are an alien, or does the building suddenly catch fire?').
5. The audience votes by cheering for Option A or Option B. The Referee quickly declares the winner.
6. The players unfreeze and must immediately incorporate the winning twist, justifying it as a natural progression of the scene.
7. After another 45 to 60 seconds of play, the Referee interrupts for the second and final 'SYNAPSE!', offering another audience vote.
8. The players integrate this final twist and drive the scene to a rapid, satisfying conclusion over the last 30 to 45 seconds.
9. The Referee awards points based on how smoothly and creatively the team justified the twists, and calls fouls for hesitation or denial.

## Coaching Notes
- Award 5 points for completing the scene, plus up to 3 bonus points for brilliant, instant justification of the twists (guided heavily by audience reaction).
- Call standard fouls: -1 point for 'Delay of Game' (hesitating to integrate the twist) or 'Denial' (ignoring the twist), and -5 points for a 'content foul' (inappropriate content).
- Encourage aggressive justification: players must make sense of completely unexpected, often absurd shifts.
- Maintain structured pacing: exactly two interruptions ensure a clear beginning, middle, and end without derailing the scene work.

## Variations
- Actor's Choice: Instead of plot twists, the Referee offers two wildly different emotional states or physical endowments for a specific character.
- The Gauntlet: A rapid-fire 1-minute version used as a tie-breaker, featuring three rapid twists to test the absolute limits of a single player's justification skills.

## Why It Works
It provides high audience agency by letting them directly control narrative pivots in real-time, while forcing improvisers to practice aggressive justification to make sense of absurd shifts within a structured beginning, middle, and end.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee strictly curates the 'Synapse Prompts' to ensure they are family-friendly and physically safe to perform, preventing harmful, offensive, or overly physical tropes. Players are encouraged to use emotional and narrative justification rather than dangerous physical stunts.

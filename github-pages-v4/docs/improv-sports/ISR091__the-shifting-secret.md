# The Shifting Secret

![The Shifting Secret — infographic](img/ISR091.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive short-form game where two mundane props are assigned secret character quirks known only to the players.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where two mundane props are assigned secret character quirks known only to the players. Whenever a player holds a prop, they must instantly adopt its quirk and justify the sudden behavioral shift. As the props change hands, the scene becomes a hilarious juggling act of shifting personalities, culminating in the audience guessing the secrets for points.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 players and a Referee. Place 2 distinct, mundane, and safe-to-handle props on stage (e.g., a rubber chicken, a clipboard, a bright yellow traffic cone). The Referee needs a list of pre-written 'quirks' or secret motivations (e.g., 'Desperately needs to pee', 'Thinks they are in a film noir', 'Deeply suspicious of everyone').

## How to Play
1. 1. Get a Suggestion: The Referee asks the audience for a mundane location or relationship to ground the scene (e.g., 'A parent-teacher conference' or 'Working at a bakery').
2. 2. Assign the Secrets: The Referee selects 2 distinct props on stage. The Referee then shows the players (but NOT the audience) two secret quirks from their pre-written list. Quirk 1 is assigned to Prop A; Quirk 2 is assigned to Prop B.
3. 3. Start the Scene: The players begin the scene normally. They start in a 'neutral' baseline character state.
4. 4. The Shift: Whenever a player physically touches or holds Prop A, they must instantly exhibit Quirk 1. If they hold Prop B, they exhibit Quirk 2.
5. 5. Justification: The core challenge is that players must justify these sudden emotional or physical shifts within the reality of the scene.
6. 6. Passing the Baton: If a prop is passed to another player, the new holder instantly takes on the quirk, and the previous holder reverts to their baseline character. A player can only have one quirk at a time.
7. 7. Subtlety: Players must never explicitly state their secret out loud. They must show it through action, tone, and dialogue.
8. 8. The Climax: The Referee calls 'Time!' after 3 to 4 minutes, or when the scene reaches a natural comedic peak.
9. 9. The Guess: The Referee turns to the audience and asks them to guess the secret assigned to Prop A, and then Prop B.
10. 10. Scoring: The team earns 5 points for a well-played scene. They earn 2 bonus points for each secret the audience correctly guesses at the end.

## Coaching Notes
- Players cannot just act crazy; they must find a reason why their character suddenly became deeply suspicious when they picked up the clipboard.
- The Referee should call a content foul if a player explicitly states their secret out loud, deducting 1 point.
- Encourage physical interaction with the environment and dynamic object work to keep the props moving.

## Variations
- Blind Shifting Secret: The players are sent out of the room. The audience provides the quirks. The Referee writes the quirks on masking tape and sticks them to the bottom of the props. When players return, they must discreetly read the tape mid-scene when they pick up the prop, adopting the trait on the fly. In this version, the audience knows the secrets, and the fun is watching the players discover and justify them.
- Escalation (3+ Props): For advanced players who have mastered the 2-prop version, the Referee can introduce a 3rd or 4th prop halfway through the scene to increase the cognitive load and onstage chaos.

## Why It Works
Forces players to actively justify sudden behavioral and emotional shifts, building strong 'Yes, And' muscles. Encourages physical interaction with the environment and dynamic object work. The audience guessing mechanic at the end provides a highly interactive and satisfying payoff.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all props are safe to pass, toss, or drop (avoid glass, sharp edges, or heavy items). Players must respect physical boundaries when passing or grabbing props from one another - no aggressive snatching. The Referee must ensure all pre-written quirks are clean, all-ages appropriate, and avoid trauma-based or offensive stereotypes.

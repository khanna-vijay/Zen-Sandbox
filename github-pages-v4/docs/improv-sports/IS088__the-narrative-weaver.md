# The Narrative Weaver

![The Narrative Weaver — infographic](img/IS088.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An evolving scene is repeatedly interrupted by an off-stage MC who delivers radical reframing prompts that fundamentally alter the established reality.

## Overview
The Narrative Weaver is an improvisational game where performers collaboratively build an evolving story, punctuated by an off-stage MC. At unpredictable intervals, the MC delivers radical Narrative Reframing Prompts that fundamentally alter the scene's established reality. Performers must instantly accept these prompts, seamlessly integrating the new information and justifying how previous events now fit the revised context.

## Setup
Two to three improvisers take the stage. An off-stage MC (the 'Narrative Weaver') is equipped with a distinct audible cue like a bell or buzzer. Get a simple, open-ended suggestion for a scene's starting point from the audience.

## How to Play
1. The improvisers begin the scene, establishing characters, basic relationships, setting, and initial conflict.
2. At unpredictable intervals (e.g., every 45-75 seconds), the Narrative Weaver rings the bell or buzzer.
3. The Weaver immediately delivers a Narrative Reframing Prompt that introduces a radically different context to the scene (e.g., 'This is actually a flashback,' or 'They are auditioning for a commercial').
4. The improvisers must immediately 'Yes, And' the prompt without denial.
5. Performers instantly incorporate the new information, justifying how all previous events and dialogue make sense within this new reality.
6. The scene continues with the adapted reality, layering multiple Reframing Prompts until the story reaches a climax or time is called.

## Coaching Notes
- Embrace the unexpected: There is no blocking a prompt. Performers must cheerfully integrate even the most challenging shifts.
- Collaborative scene-building: When reality changes, players must actively support each other in justifying the new context, often taking on roles that make their partner's shifted reality believable.
- Justification is key: Flex the muscle of rationalizing outlandish reframes so they seem like they were always meant to be that way.
- Emotional agility: Characters must rapidly shift their emotional states to match the new genre, motivation, or revelation.

## Why It Works
It forces stories to evolve in unexpected directions, fostering sophisticated plot twists and character arcs. It acts as an ultimate test of 'Yes, And' by requiring players to accept a complete rewrite of the scene's foundation and build upon it instantly.

## Safety & Inclusion
The MC should ensure reframing prompts respect player boundaries and avoid forcing improvisers into non-consensual or emotionally triggering situations. Always read the room and the players' comfort levels when delivering radical shifts.

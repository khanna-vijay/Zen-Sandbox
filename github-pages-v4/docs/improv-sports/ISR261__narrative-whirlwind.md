# Narrative Whirlwind

![Narrative Whirlwind — infographic](img/ISR261.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two opposing players collaborate to build a scene while instantly adapting to unpredictable, game-changing 'Whirlwind' prompts thrown by the Referee.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two opposing players collaborate to build a scene, but must instantly adapt to unpredictable, game-changing 'Whirlwind' prompts thrown at them by the Referee. It tests rapid-fire 'Yes, And' skills under pressure as players justify sudden new realities without breaking the story.

## Setup
Format is a competitive short-form match. Place 1 Red Team player and 1 Blue Team player on stage, with the rest of their teams on the sidelines ready to tag in. A Referee stands downstage or to the side with a whistle and a prepared list of 'Whirlwind' prompts (e.g., sudden genre changes, bizarre objects, physical constraints, or emotional shifts). Get a starting suggestion from the audience.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a starting suggestion from the audience and starts the scene with one Red player and one Blue player on stage.
2. The two players begin building a grounded, collaborative scene based on the suggestion.
3. At unpredictable intervals (every 20-40 seconds), the Referee blows the whistle, shouts 'WHIRLWIND!', and delivers a prompt.
4. The players on stage must instantly accept, justify, and integrate the prompt into the reality of their scene without dropping the existing narrative.
5. At any point, a player on the sideline can tag out their own teammate. The incoming player takes over the exact physical position and character of the departing player.
6. The Referee awards points for brilliant integrations and calls fouls for hesitation or blocking.
7. The scene ends after 3 to 5 Whirlwind prompts, culminating in a final, high-energy resolution.

## Coaching Notes
- Award 1-3 points to a team when their player makes a particularly clever, seamless, or hilarious choice to integrate a Whirlwind prompt.
- Call a 'Brake Pedal' Foul (-1 point) for hesitating, stalling, or failing to immediately integrate the prompt.
- Call a 'Bulldozer' Foul (-1 point) for ignoring the opponent's offers to force your own idea.
- Call a clean-content foul (-1 point) for inappropriate, non-family-friendly content.
- Encourage sideline players to use the tag-out mechanic to rescue a struggling teammate or inject new energy.
- To increase audience interaction, the Referee can collect 3-4 'Whirlwind' ideas from the audience before the game begins.

## Variations
- Prop Whirlwind: Instead of verbal prompts, the Referee throws safe, bizarre physical props onto the stage while shouting 'WHIRLWIND!' The players must instantly make the prop vital to the scene.
- Genre Whirlwind: All prompts are strictly film, television, or theatrical genres (e.g., 'WHIRLWIND! Film Noir!', 'WHIRLWIND! Children's Educational Show!').

## Why It Works
It tests rapid-fire 'Yes, And' skills under pressure, forcing players to justify sudden new realities without breaking the story. The head-to-head collaboration and Referee-driven pacing keep energy high and unpredictable.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical safety is paramount; Referees must never give prompts that require unsafe physical stunts. The clean-content foul ensures all content remains clean and accessible for all ages. The teammate tag-out mechanic acts as a built-in safety valve, allowing a player who feels overwhelmed or stuck to be seamlessly rescued by a teammate without stopping the game.

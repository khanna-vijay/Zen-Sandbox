# The Scene Scramble

![The Scene Scramble — infographic](img/IS043.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two players must maintain a coherent scene while a Referee rapidly introduces chaotic changes to their physical environment or the identity of a key prop.

## Overview
The Scene Scramble champions rapid-fire adaptation, ingenious justification, and unwavering commitment to character amidst chaotic, referee-driven environmental shifts. It is a high-energy test of a team's collective improvisational agility, often keeping the audience on the edge of their seats. Players are challenged to instantly 'Yes, And' these 'Scrambles,' creatively justifying the sudden shifts in reality and seamlessly integrating them into the ongoing narrative.

## Setup
Two players step onto the playing area while a Referee stands nearby. Get a single Relationship and a Starting Location from the audience (e.g., 'Two estranged siblings at a dusty antique shop').

## How to Play
1. The Referee yells 'SCRAMBLE!' to begin, and the players immediately initiate a scene based on the audience suggestions.
2. At any point, the Referee loudly interjects with an 'ENVIRONMENT SCRAMBLE! [New Location]!' (e.g., 'A SUBMARINE!').
3. Players must instantly accept the new location, physically reacting and vocally justifying how or why they got there without breaking character.
4. The Referee can also interject with an 'OBJECT SCRAMBLE! [Existing Object] IS NOW A [New Object]!' (e.g., 'THAT HAT IS NOW A FRISBEE!').
5. Players must instantly accept the object's new identity and integrate it into the scene, justifying the change.
6. The scene continues with the new reality. The Referee calls Scrambles every 10-20 seconds to keep the pace fierce.
7. After 2-3 minutes, the Referee calls 'TIME!' to conclude the scene.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee is the primary engine of the game and should vary between Environment and Object Scrambles to keep players on their toes.
- Watch out for the 'Lag Scramble': penalize players if they hesitate for more than 2 seconds, break character due to confusion, or ignore the Scramble.
- Ensure players maintain their established characters, relationship, and the scene's underlying narrative goals despite the wild shifts.
- Call 'Groaner Fouls' for excessively bad puns or justifications that are simply too weak.
- Demand strong object work; players must convincingly transform imaginary props when an object scramble is called.

## Why It Works
The game forces absolute reliance on 'Yes, And' and active listening. It builds quick thinking and adaptation by requiring players to process new information and react instantly, all while maintaining character commitment and narrative flow through increasingly absurd circumstances.

## Safety & Inclusion
Maintain a family-friendly atmosphere by avoiding blue humor, swearing, or innuendo (enforced via content fouls). Ensure physical safety during rapid environmental shifts and chaotic object work.

# Emotional Current

![Emotional Current — infographic](img/ISR214.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players pass an imaginary object, transforming both its physical properties and its emotional state before passing it on.

## Overview
Emotional Current is a non-competitive, unified-circle warm-up where players pass an imaginary object imbued with a distinct emotion. A player conjures a space object filled with an emotion, visibly embodying both, and passes it across the circle. The receiver must immediately catch and embody that exact object and emotion, then physically and emotionally transform both before passing it to someone else.

## Setup
Players form a single, unified circle facing inward. The facilitator stands either in the center or just outside the circle to provide continuous side-coaching. No props, chairs, or teams are required.

## How to Play
1. Form a single circle. The facilitator explains that the group will be passing an imaginary object that carries both a physical weight/texture and a strong emotional state.
2. Player A begins by molding an imaginary object in their hands. They must clearly establish its physical properties (heavy, light, sticky, sharp) AND imbue it with a clear emotion (joy, anger, sorrow, disgust, panic).
3. Player A makes eye contact with another player across the circle and 'passes' the object to them (tossing, sliding, floating, or pushing it, depending on its weight and emotion).
4. The receiving player (Player B) must catch the object and immediately mirror the exact physical weight and emotion that Player A established. They let the emotion 'infect' their face and posture for a brief moment.
5. Player B then transforms BOTH the object and the emotion into something entirely new. For example, the 'furious, heavy brick' melts into a 'serene, floating feather'.
6. Once the transformation is complete and clear in their body, Player B makes eye contact with a new player and passes the new object.
7. The game continues continuously. There are no 'fouls' or stops.

## Coaching Notes
- If a player drops the energy or the transformation is unclear, actively side-coach to keep the flow alive (e.g., 'Show us the weight!', 'Let the emotion hit your whole body!', 'Transform it completely before you pass!').
- Act as a side-coach rather than a referee, guiding the group to deeper physical and emotional commitment without stopping the flow.
- Encourage intense eye contact, active listening, and reading of body language to maintain the non-verbal ensemble focus.
- Remind players to bypass intellectual overthinking by letting go of their previous state and immediately adopting the new one.

## Variations
- Vocal Current: Players add a non-verbal sound or gibberish phrase that matches the emotion and physical effort of the object as they hold and pass it.
- Group Contagion: When the receiver catches the object and feels the emotion, the entire circle must instantly mirror that emotion. The circle holds the emotion until the receiver transforms the object, at which point the circle returns to neutral, waiting for the next catch.

## Why It Works
It trains intense non-verbal listening, physical embodiment, and emotional fluidity. By forcing players to simultaneously embody an object's physical properties and an emotional state, it bypasses intellectual overthinking and builds spontaneous transformation skills.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical Safety: Remind players not to 'throw' aggressive or dangerous imaginary objects directly at people's faces; passes should be clear but safe. Emotional Safety: Instruct players to stick to theatrical, playable emotions (joy, frustration, awe, sadness) and avoid drawing on genuine personal trauma. Accessibility: This game can be played entirely seated; players can adapt the 'passing' and 'catching' motions to their own mobility levels, focusing on facial expressions, posture, and arm movements.

# The Symbiotic Scaffolding

![The Symbiotic Scaffolding — infographic](img/ISR177.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A silent, eyes-closed ensemble exercise where players build an interconnected physical structure through gentle touch and shared weight.

## Overview
A silent, eyes-closed ensemble exercise where players build an interconnected physical structure through gentle touch and shared weight. Guided by a facilitator, the group relies entirely on kinesthetic listening to maintain balance as the structure slowly breathes, adapts, and eventually moves as a single living organism.

## Setup
Clear a large, open room (use mats if available). Divide the group: two-thirds are Builders (eyes closed), one-third are Spotters (eyes open). Before playing, conduct a mandatory Boundary Check: players explicitly state their touch boundaries (e.g., 'shoulders and arms only') and weight-bearing limits. Establish a universal safety word like 'Drop' to immediately and safely dissolve the structure.

## How to Play
1. Boundary Check: The facilitator leads a strict consent check where players state touch preferences and physical limitations to the group.
2. Spotter Positioning: Sighted spotters surround the play area. They hover hands near Builders to prevent falls, and provide a gentle, continuous ambient hum to give Builders an auditory sense of the room's boundaries.
3. The Anchor: The facilitator places one Builder in the center in a strong, stable stance to act as the foundation.
4. Building the Scaffold: One by one, Builders slowly approach with hands out. Upon finding the Anchor or another Builder, they establish a point of contact (respecting stated boundaries) and apply gentle, mutual pressure. The rule is to lean WITH someone, not ON them.
5. The Breathing Structure: Once everyone is connected, the facilitator instructs the group to breathe together. Any player can initiate a micro-shift in weight. The rest of the structure must intuitively adapt to maintain balance without speaking.
6. The Evolution: To increase engagement, the facilitator cues 'Evolve.' The entire structure must slowly change its overall elevation (e.g., from standing to crouching) or travel one foot in any direction without breaking connection or speaking.
7. Resolution: The facilitator calls 'Slow Dissolve.' Players gradually release weight, open their eyes, and thank their partners.

## Coaching Notes
- Focus entirely on kinesthetic listening and non-verbal communication.
- Remove visual and verbal crutches to build deep ensemble trust.
- Maintain a clear Point of Concentration (POC): maintaining shared dynamic tension and collective balance.
- Ensure spotters act as an active, supportive audience, remaining vigilant to ensure physical safety.

## Variations
- The Soundscape Scaffold: As the structure shifts, players emit sustained vocal tones that rise in pitch with physical tension and lower with relaxation, creating a living, breathing choir.
- The Seated Scaffold: Players sit in a tight circle on chairs. They build the structure using only hands, arms, and gentle upper-body tension. Perfect for non-weight-bearing needs or mobility limitations.
- The Environmental Scaffold: The facilitator calls out weather conditions (e.g., 'A gentle breeze from the left', 'Heavy gravity'). The structure must collectively adapt its tension and shape to survive the element.

## Why It Works
It removes visual and verbal crutches to build deep ensemble trust, focusing entirely on kinesthetic listening and non-verbal communication to maintain shared dynamic tension and collective balance.

## Safety & Inclusion
Mandatory pre-game consent check for touch boundaries. Sighted spotters are strictly required to prevent falls. The 'lean with, not on' rule prevents crushing. The Seated Scaffold variation ensures players with mobility or balance limitations can fully participate. A universal safety word immediately pauses the exercise.

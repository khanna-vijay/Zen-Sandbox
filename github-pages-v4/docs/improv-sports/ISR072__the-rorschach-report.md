# The Rorschach Report

![The Rorschach Report — infographic](img/ISR072.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A panel of experts 'Yes-And' each other's insane theories to explain an ambiguous audience suggestion through a highly physical, shared flashback.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where a panel of 'Experts' must explain a completely ambiguous audience suggestion, such as a sound, gesture, or nonsense phrase. Instead of arguing, players must 'Yes-And' each other's increasingly insane theories into one cohesive, monstrous explanation, physically acting out the bizarre events in a shared flashback.

## Setup
Set up a row of chairs on one side of the stage for the 'Panel,' leaving the center stage wide open as the 'Reenactment Zone.' A stopwatch or timer is required.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a completely ambiguous stimulus: a vague sound (e.g., 'boing'), a random gesture, or a cryptic three-word phrase.
2. The Referee sets a strict 3-minute timer, introduces the players as a panel of top-tier experts gathered to explain this mysterious phenomenon, and starts the clock.
3. Player 1 steps into the Reenactment Zone, states their expert theory of what the stimulus was, and physically acts it out (e.g., 'That boing was clearly a subterranean mole-rhino,' while miming the creature).
4. Player 2 immediately jumps up, joins the Reenactment Zone, and must 'Yes-And' Player 1's theory by adding a new, wild layer that connects to it (e.g., 'Yes, and the mole-rhino was operating a geothermal pizza oven!'). Player 2 physically interacts with Player 1's mime.
5. Players 3 and 4 follow suit, each required to validate all previous theories and weave them into a single, unified, highly physical flashback scene.
6. The Referee actively moderates, calling fouls for any denial, arguing, or 'Static Play' (remaining seated instead of joining the physical reenactment).
7. At the 3-minute buzzer, the Referee calls 'Time!' The players must instantly freeze in their final, monstrously complex physical tableau.
8. The audience votes for the winning team by applause at the end. The Referee awards points for seamless 'Yes-And' justifications, outstanding physical commitment, and successfully tying all absurd elements together.

## Coaching Notes
- Actively moderate and call fouls for Denial (negating another expert), Static Play (refusing to act it out), or Delay of Game (waffling or stalling).
- Encourage players to transform a traditionally static panel game into a highly physical, scene-based flashback.
- Ensure the strict time limits are maintained to keep high energy and prevent scenes from dragging.
- This game is a perfect fit for competitive short-form with clear referee interventions and fouls.

## Variations
- Silent Film Rorschach: The stimulus is a 5-second silent physical movement demonstrated by an audience volunteer, which the experts must replicate and explain.
- The Alibi: Instead of experts, the players are suspects in an interrogation room, Yes-Anding a single, ridiculously complex alibi for where they were last night.

## Why It Works
It forces extreme 'Yes-And' justification instead of conflict, requiring players to weave absurd elements together while committing to a highly physical, shared reality.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical reenactments respect personal boundaries; players should mime interacting with objects rather than forcing physical contact with each other. The Referee must strictly enforce clean, family-friendly content, immediately blowing the whistle on any inappropriate or unsafe escalations.

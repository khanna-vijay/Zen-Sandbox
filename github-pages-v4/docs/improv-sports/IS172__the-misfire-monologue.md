# The Misfire Monologue

![The Misfire Monologue — infographic](img/IS172.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A solo performer's monologue is repeatedly derailed by a host shouting 'Misfire!' prompts that force instant character flaws, culminating in a high-stakes scene shift.

## Overview
The Misfire Monologue is an improvisational game where a solo performer delivers a monologue while a 'Narrative Impeller' (host) periodically shouts a 'Misfire!' prompt like 'Hubris' or 'Betrayal'. The performer must instantly integrate this word as a significant character flaw or setback into their ongoing story. After a few misfires, the Impeller can initiate a 'Scene Shift' and enter the stage as a new character to transform the monologue into a dynamic dialogue.

## Setup
One performer takes the stage. A designated 'Narrative Impeller' (Host/Judge) stands by. Get an audience suggestion (e.g., location, character type, or specific object) to inspire the initial monologue.

## How to Play
1. The performer begins delivering a solo monologue based on the audience suggestion, building a character and narrative.
2. At various points, the Narrative Impeller shouts: 'Misfire! [One-Word Theme/Descriptor]!' (e.g., Oversight, Hubris, Betrayal, Temptation).
3. The performer must immediately accept this 'misfire' as a literal failure or critical setback their character just made or realized, seamlessly integrating it into the ongoing monologue.
4. The Narrative Impeller can trigger up to three 'Misfire!' prompts during the monologue phase.
5. After the third 'Misfire!', the Impeller shouts 'Scene Shift! [Character Name, enter!]' and physically enters the scene as that newly named character.
6. The Impeller's first action or line must directly reference one of the preceding misfires, establishing their connection to the story.
7. The original performer must 'Yes, And' this new character, transforming the monologue into a high-stakes, two-person dialogue.
8. The scene concludes when the Impeller or another judge calls time, typically 2-3 minutes into the dialogue phase.

## Coaching Notes
- Misfire prompts should relate to a character flaw, emotional state, environmental challenge, or twist of fate (e.g., Illusion, Pressure, Obsession, Recklessness, Cowardice, Greed).
- The misfire shouldn't just be a random plot twist; it must be a personal character failing or an immediate obstacle caused by an earlier misjudgment.
- Performers should focus on 'Being Changed' by showing a genuine emotional response and realization when a misfire is called.
- When the Impeller enters during the Scene Shift, they must drive the narrative forward by directly connecting their character to the established flaws.
- Judge on 'Narrative Weaving': the integration of the misfire should feel organic and raise the stakes, rather than feeling tacked on or clumsy.

## Variations
- Continue Monologue: Instead of initiating a Scene Shift after the third misfire, the Impeller can choose to call another 'Misfire!' prompt, escalating the solo narrative further without ever entering the scene.

## Why It Works
It explicitly uses forced character failure as the primary narrative driver. By demanding instant creative justification for dictated 'failures', it develops narrative agility, deepens character portrayal, and forces performers to fully embrace the improv principle of 'Being Changed'.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the emotional intensity and themes introduced by the 'Misfire' prompts remain within the performers' comfort zones. Players should respect physical and emotional boundaries during the high-stakes Scene Shift.

# The Anomaly Consensus

![The Anomaly Consensus — infographic](img/ISR178.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players collaboratively build a shared reality by intuitively and non-verbally justifying bizarre, abstract stimuli.

## Overview
An advanced ensemble exercise where players collaboratively build a shared reality by intuitively justifying abstract, bizarre stimuli (anomalies) introduced by a facilitator. Without verbal discussion, players must immediately embody and act as if each strange sound, object, or visual cue perfectly belongs in their world.

## Setup
A clear, open room. One facilitator to lead and side-coach. Gather a collection of non-representational, abstract items (e.g., oddly shaped foam blocks, textured fabric scraps, driftwood) and a way to make abstract sounds (simple percussion, mouth noises, or a basic phone soundboard).

## How to Play
1. Establish the Base Reality: Players begin a simple, non-verbal scene based on a mundane physical activity (e.g., waiting at a bus stop, cleaning a laboratory, organizing a library) to establish a shared physical environment.
2. Introduce the Anomaly: At a random interval (e.g., after 30 seconds), the facilitator introduces a completely uncontextualized, abstract stimulus, like tossing a weirdly shaped object into the room or making a sudden, unusual sound.
3. Immediate Justification: Players must instantly integrate the anomaly into their reality without speaking or breaking character, treating it as if it perfectly belongs in their world.
4. Non-Verbal Consensus: Players must observe each other's physical reactions and align with them. If one player treats a sound as a bird call or an object as burning hot, everyone must physically agree and support that reality.
5. Layering: The facilitator introduces new anomalies every 30-60 seconds. The ensemble must continuously adapt their shared reality to incorporate the fresh input, building a complex world purely through physical agreement.

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach with phrases like: 'Accept it immediately.'
- 'What does your partner think it is? Agree with them physically.'
- 'Let your body react before your brain tries to explain it.'

## Variations
- Human Anomaly (Zero-Tech): Instead of props or sounds, the facilitator taps an off-stage player to become the 'Anomaly.' That player enters the space doing a repetitive, abstract physical movement and sound, which the rest of the group must justify and integrate.
- High-Tech (If Accessible): If the venue allows, use a projector for abstract visuals (fractals, random color fields) and a sound system for complex audioscapes, provided all safety checks are cleared.
- Gradual Dialogue: Start completely non-verbal for the first three anomalies. On the fourth anomaly, allow sparse, essential dialogue that supports the established physical reality, ensuring the words serve the physical choices rather than explaining them away.

## Why It Works
It intensely works the muscle of immediate, unquestioning justification and deep non-verbal ensemble connection. The abstract nature of the prompts prevents cliché responses, forcing genuine discovery and relying on instinct rather than intellect.

## Safety & Inclusion
Crucial: Before playing, conduct a sensory consent check regarding sensitivities to loud noises, sudden movements, or specific textures (and photosensitivity if using lights). Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards. Players can step out or cover their ears/eyes at any time if overwhelmed, which should be treated as a valid character choice by the rest of the ensemble.

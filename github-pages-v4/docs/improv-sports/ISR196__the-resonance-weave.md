# The Resonance Weave

![The Resonance Weave — infographic](img/ISR196.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass an energetic state around the room, with each new person adding a single physical or vocal layer before it resets.

## Overview
The Resonance Weave is a physical and vocal warm-up where players pass an energetic state around the room, with each new person adding a single layer to the movement or sound. To prevent the game from becoming an untrackable mess, the state resets after three layers. It is a highly active, non-verbal exercise that forces players out of their heads and into their bodies, building deep ensemble listening and intuitive responsiveness.

## Setup
Players scatter loosely around a spacious room. No props or chairs are needed. The facilitator stands near the edge of the space to side-coach and guide the flow.

## How to Play
1. Establish the Pass: Agree on how states will be passed. The default is a gentle, consensual tap on the shoulder. (For a no-touch option, use strong eye contact and a clear pointing gesture).
2. Layer 1 (The Base): The facilitator points to Player A. Player A begins a simple, repeatable physical movement or vocalization (e.g., hunching their shoulders and making a low clicking sound).
3. Layer 2 (The Addition): Player A walks up to Player B and taps their shoulder. Player B immediately takes on Player A's exact state (hunched shoulders, clicking). Then, Player B intuitively adds ONE new layer of their own (e.g., adding a sweeping arm motion).
4. Layer 3 (The Final Addition): Player B walks to Player C and taps their shoulder. Player C takes on the composite state (hunch, clicking, sweeping arm) and adds ONE final layer (e.g., a bouncy walk).
5. The Reset: The state has now reached its maximum of three layers. Player C walks to Player D and taps their shoulder. Because the state is 'full,' Player D drops all previous layers completely. Player D invents a brand new, single base layer (e.g., standing tall and whistling) and the cycle begins again.
6. The Weave Effect: After a player passes a state, they KEEP DOING that state until someone else taps them with a new one. This ensures the entire room remains in motion, creating a 'weave' of different physicalities.
7. Multiple Strands: For groups larger than 8, the facilitator can start 2 or 3 different 'Layer 1' seeds simultaneously to keep the energy high and the room active.

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage deep physical listening and non-verbal communication.
- Prompt players to move away from intellectual planning and rely on their bodies' intuition.
- Remind players to maintain their current state after passing it to keep the 'weave' alive.
- Watch for cognitive overload; ensure the reset happens strictly after the third layer.

## Variations
- Alternating Channels: Force players to alternate between adding a physical movement and adding a vocal sound. If Layer 1 is physical, Layer 2 must be vocal, and Layer 3 must be physical.
- Single Strand Freeze: Instead of players continuing their movement after passing it, they freeze in a neutral position. This focuses the entire room's attention on the single active state moving through the group, which is great for beginners learning the mechanics.

## Why It Works
It forces players out of their heads and into their bodies, building deep ensemble listening and intuitive responsiveness. The built-in reset mechanic prevents cognitive overload and keeps the game moving fluidly.

## Safety & Inclusion
Consent and Touch: Always establish boundaries before playing. If players are uncomfortable with a shoulder tap, use the 'eye contact and point' passing method. Physical Accessibility: Remind players to only initiate and copy movements that are within their physical abilities. If a player cannot safely replicate a movement (e.g., a jump), they should capture the essence or rhythm of it using a part of their body they can safely move (e.g., a sharp hand gesture). Vocal Safety: Discourage screaming or straining vocal cords; sounds should be sustainable.

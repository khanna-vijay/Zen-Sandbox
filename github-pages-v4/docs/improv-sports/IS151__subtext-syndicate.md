# Subtext Syndicate

![Subtext Syndicate — infographic](img/IS151.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players secretly receive unique meta-agendas that they must subtly weave into a shared scene without explicitly breaking character or the established reality.

## Overview
Subtext Syndicate is an improvisational game where players secretly receive unique 'meta-agendas' that they must subtly weave into a shared scene, without explicitly breaking character or the established reality. The objective is to influence the narrative and their partners, demonstrating strategic thinking, deep listening, and collaborative subtlety. Success is judged by how seamlessly these hidden objectives are integrated to enhance the story's depth and direction.

## Setup
Played by two teams, typically with 2-3 players per scene. Prepare 'Subtext Syndicate Agenda Cards' containing meta-narrative directives (e.g., 'Convince your partner that time is repeating', 'End the scene with an important item unexpectedly broken or lost', 'Initiate an unprompted physical action from your partner', 'Subtly suggest the scene is a dream', 'Introduce a supernatural element', 'Shift the power dynamic', 'Make your partner question their identity', 'Inspire your partner to express a deep secret'). The MC gets a Location and Relationship suggestion from the audience. Out of sight of teammates and the audience, each player secretly receives one Agenda Card.

## How to Play
1. The MC starts the scene based on the audience suggestions for Location and Relationship.
2. Players begin improvising, establishing their characters and the initial scene reality.
3. Throughout the scene, each player subtly maneuvers their dialogue, actions, and reactions to fulfill their secret meta-agenda.
4. Players must use 'Yes, And' and active listening, integrating their agenda by building on their partners' offers rather than forcing it illogically.
5. Players attempt, initiate, or drive towards their agenda within the narrative flow; it does not need to be explicitly 'solved' or 'completed'.
6. The MC calls 'Scene' after the predetermined time limit (typically 3-5 minutes).
7. After the scene, each player publicly reveals their 'Subtext Syndicate Agenda Card' for the judges and audience to evaluate the performance.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: Think structurally about how your goal fits into and advances the overall plot.
- Embracing Mistakes: If an attempt isn't picked up, adapt immediately, find a new path, or repurpose the 'failure' to further the goal.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: Make your partner look good. Subtly invite them into your agenda's reality so their choices seem inspired as they unknowingly advance your plot point.
- Yes, And: Never deny what has been established just to force your agenda.
- Listening & Subtext: Listen deeply to identify openings where an agenda can be gently inserted by interpreting a partner's casual line or action.
- Being Changed: Be open to your own character and the scene being altered by the unknown agendas of your partners.

## Why It Works
This game uniquely challenges players to support their partners while simultaneously pursuing a hidden agenda. It cultivates deep narrative play, strategic thinking, and collaborative subtlety, forcing improvisers to be both architects and performers shaping stories from the inside out.

## Safety & Inclusion
When agendas involve physical actions (e.g., breaking an item, unprompted physical gestures) or emotional manipulation (e.g., questioning identity, revealing secrets), ensure players respect each other's physical boundaries and emotional consent.

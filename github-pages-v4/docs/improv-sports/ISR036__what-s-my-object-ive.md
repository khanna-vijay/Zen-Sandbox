# What's My Object-ive?

![What's My Object-ive? — infographic](img/ISR036.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Improvisers must seamlessly integrate audience-suggested mimed objects into their scene while fulfilling bizarre, hidden objectives.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where improvisers must seamlessly integrate a list of audience-suggested mimed objects into their scene. The comedic hook: for each object, the Referee flashes a pre-written 'Objective' card to the players and the audience. The players must instantly justify and execute this bizarre action with the object without breaking the reality of the scene, while the audience enjoys the dramatic irony of anticipating how they will pull it off.

## Setup
Format: Competitive Short-Form. Personnel: 2 improvisers from the same team and 1 Referee. Materials: A stack of pre-written 'Objective' cards printed large enough for the audience to read (or a whiteboard/marker). The Referee gets a scene suggestion (e.g., relationship and location) and a list of 3 to 4 random, unrelated nouns (objects) from the audience BEFORE the scene begins.

## How to Play
1. The Setup: The Referee gets a location and relationship from the audience to ground the scene. Then, to maintain pacing during the game, the Referee asks the audience for 3 to 4 random nouns (e.g., a toaster, a pool noodle, a fabergé egg) and writes them down or memorizes them.
2. The Scene Begins: The two improvisers start a grounded, organic scene based on the initial location and relationship.
3. Introducing the Object: About 30-45 seconds in, the Referee blows their whistle or calls out 'OBJECT!' and announces the first item from the audience's list (e.g., 'A pool noodle!').
4. The Objective Reveal: Simultaneously, the Referee holds up a pre-written Objective card so both the audience and the players can read it (e.g., 'Treat it like a sacred artifact' or 'Try to secretly eat it').
5. Rapid Justification: The players must immediately mime the object, incorporate it into the scene, and fulfill the objective through their actions and dialogue, all while justifying it within the established reality. They cannot explicitly state the objective.
6. Continuous Play: The Referee does NOT stop the scene to score. Once the players have successfully justified the first object and objective, the Referee waits for a natural beat, calls out the next object on the list, and holds up a new Objective card.
7. The Climax & Call: The scene builds in absurdity as 3-4 objects are introduced. The Referee calls 'SCENE!' on a high energy moment or a strong joke after the final objective is fulfilled.
8. Scoring: The Referee recaps the objects and objectives, awarding up to 5 points total: 1 point for clear mime/object work, 2 points for maintaining the scene's reality without breaking, and 2 points for the cleverness of the objective justifications. Standard short-form fouls (clean-content foul for inappropriate content, groaner for bad puns, delay of game for ignoring the objective) apply.

## Coaching Notes
- Rapid Justification: Force players to instantly make sense of bizarre constraints within an established reality.
- Dramatic Irony: The audience is in on the joke the moment the card is revealed, creating built-in laughs before the players even speak.
- Uninterrupted Pacing: Front-loading the object suggestions prevents momentum-killing stops during the scene.
- Detailed Object Work: Demand precise mime skills to make the invisible objects read clearly to the back of the house.
- Remind players they cannot explicitly state the objective; they must show it through action and dialogue.

## Variations
- Blind Partner (Advanced): The Referee only shows the Objective card to ONE of the two players (while still showing the audience). The player who sees it must initiate the objective, and their partner must 'Yes, And' the bizarre behavior without knowing the actual prompt, increasing the dramatic irony.
- Prop-ective (Physical Variation): Instead of miming objects, the Referee pulls actual, random physical props from a box and hands them to the players along with the Objective card.

## Why It Works
It develops rapid justification and precise mime skills, forcing players to instantly make sense of bizarre constraints within an established reality while leveraging dramatic irony for built-in laughs.

## Safety & Inclusion
Objective cards must be pre-vetted to ensure they are safe, all-ages appropriate, and do not require non-consensual physical contact between players (e.g., use 'Treat the object like it is burning hot' rather than 'Use the object to hit your partner'). The Referee should immediately veto any audience object suggestions that are inappropriate or inherently unsafe to mime. Players must maintain spatial awareness during heightened physical comedy.

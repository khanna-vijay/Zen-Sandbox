# Plot Twist Triathlon

![Plot Twist Triathlon — infographic](img/ISR022.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced narrative relay where players tag in to instantly justify bizarre, audience-driven plot twists.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two teams co-create a single, continuous scene. Two players (one from each team) are on stage at all times. The Referee frequently pauses the action to get a bizarre 'Plot Twist' from the audience. An off-stage player must immediately tag out their teammate, assume their exact character, and instantly justify the new twist.

## Setup
Two teams (e.g., Red and Blue) line up on opposite sides of the stage. A Referee stands downstage with a whistle. No props are required. The Referee asks the audience for a location and a relationship to start the scene.

## How to Play
1. 1. The Referee gets a base suggestion (e.g., 'A bakery' and 'Two rival bakers') and starts the scene with Player A (Team Red) and Player B (Team Blue).
2. 2. The two players begin the scene, establishing the base reality, their relationship, and the dialogue.
3. 3. After 20-30 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle, freezing the scene. The Referee immediately points to the audience and asks for a random plot twist (e.g., 'What suddenly falls from the ceiling?', 'What shocking secret is revealed?').
4. 4. The Referee selects the best/loudest twist. An off-stage player from Team Red tags out Player A.
5. 5. TAG MECHANIC: The incoming Red player must take the exact physical position of Player A and inherit their character. Player A leaves the stage.
6. 6. The Referee blows the whistle to unfreeze the scene. The new Red player must immediately deliver a line of dialogue that justifies the twist while maintaining the established character and relationship with Player B.
7. 7. The scene continues for another 20-30 seconds. The Referee blows the whistle again, gets a new twist, and this time an off-stage player from Team Blue tags out Player B, inheriting their character.
8. 8. This alternating relay continues for 4-6 twists. The final pair on stage must bring the absurd narrative to a definitive, satisfying conclusion.
9. 9. Scoring: 5 points are awarded to the team that receives the loudest audience applause at the end of the game. The Referee may award a +1 point 'Twist Bonus' for an exceptionally brilliant or instant justification. Standard fouls apply (e.g., content foul for inappropriate content, Delay of Game for stalling on a tag).

## Coaching Notes
- Maintain strong relationship dynamics and continuous dialogue; the scene should not collapse when a twist is introduced.
- Off-stage players must pay strict attention so they can seamlessly inherit the character and physical position during a tag.
- Rely entirely on mime and object work, as no props are used.
- Commit to immediate, high-stakes justification of the absurd audience offers without hesitation.

## Variations
- Pre-loaded Twists (High Speed): To avoid stopping the action for crowd work, the Referee gets 5-6 twists from the audience before the scene starts, writes them down, and simply reads them out sequentially when blowing the whistle.
- New Character Tag: Instead of inheriting the character, the incoming player enters as a brand new character introduced by the twist (e.g., the sudden appearance of a monster, or a long-lost mother). The departing player simply exits, shifting the scene's dynamic.

## Why It Works
It is a high-energy test of justification, listening, and narrative survival that keeps the audience highly engaged by forcing players to instantly adapt to chaotic inputs.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical tags (high-fives or shoulder taps) should be gentle. For accessibility, players can use a verbal 'Tag!' or a specific sound instead of physical contact. Players with mobility restrictions can enter at their own pace; the Referee holds the freeze until they are safely in position. The Referee must strictly enforce the content foul to keep audience suggestions and player reactions family-friendly and emotionally safe.

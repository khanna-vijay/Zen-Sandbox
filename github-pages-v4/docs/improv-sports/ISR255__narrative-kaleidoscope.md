# Narrative Kaleidoscope

![Narrative Kaleidoscope — infographic](img/ISR255.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive short-form game where players perform a shared scene that abruptly shifts genres.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where players perform a shared scene that abruptly shifts genres. A Referee controls the pacing, calling out audience-suggested genres just as the current one gets established, forcing players to instantly adapt their acting style, tropes, and dialogue while keeping the core narrative moving forward.

## Setup
2 to 4 players (1 or 2 from each team). A Referee with a whistle or horn. The Referee gets a mundane location or activity from the audience, plus a list of 5 to 6 distinct movie, theater, or TV genres written on a clipboard.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a mundane location or everyday activity to ground the scene, followed by 5 to 6 distinct genres (e.g., Film Noir, Soap Opera, Sci-Fi, Western).
2. The players begin acting out a full, grounded scene based on the location or activity, using their normal improvisational voices and natural physicalities.
3. After 3 to 4 exchanges of dialogue (or about 30 seconds), once the base reality is established, the Referee blows the whistle and calls out the first genre from the list.
4. Players must immediately shift their physicalities, vocal tones, and dialogue to match the new genre, seamlessly justifying the transition within the reality of the scene.
5. The Referee lets the scene play in this new genre for another 4 to 6 beats, allowing the players enough time to fully establish the style, play with the tropes, and advance the narrative.
6. The Referee continues to blow the whistle and call out new genres every 30 to 45 seconds, ensuring each genre gets enough time to breathe before the next pivot.
7. The game concludes when all genres have been used or the narrative reaches a hilarious, natural climax, at which point the Referee calls 'Scene!'
8. At the end of the scene, the Referee asks the audience to cheer for the Red Team and the Blue Team based on who adapted best. The team with the loudest applause is awarded 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- During play, the Referee may award a 1-point Style Bonus for an exceptionally clever genre trope, or call a Groaner Foul (minus 1 point) for a terrible pun or lack of commitment.
- Encourage full scene play rather than single-sentence storytelling, allowing for character relationships and object work to develop.
- Ensure paced genre shifts every 30 to 45 seconds, giving players time to actually establish and play with the tropes.
- Use the simplified, applause-based scoring to remove complex math for the Referee and keep the energy high.

## Variations
- Emotion Kaleidoscope: Instead of genres, the Referee calls out extreme emotions or physical states (e.g., Paranoid, Zero Gravity, Dangerously in Love) that the players must instantly adopt.
- Director's Cut: Instead of a shared scene, teams play separate 2-minute scenes. The Referee pauses the scene and forces the active team to replay their exact same 30-second beat in a brand new genre.

## Why It Works
It is a high-energy, highly adaptable competitive format that tests both narrative skills and genre awareness, forcing players to maintain character relationships and object work while instantly adopting new tropes.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee strictly vets the audience genres, rejecting anything inappropriate, offensive, or inherently unsafe. The standard clean-content foul applies: any player crossing into blue humor, swearing, or unsafe physical contact is immediately whistled, penalized minus 1 point, and corrected. Players must adapt genres without resorting to physical aggression; for example, in an Action Movie genre, combat must be mimed at a safe distance using slow-motion or exaggerated reactions.

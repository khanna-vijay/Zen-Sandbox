# The Sympathetic String

![The Sympathetic String — infographic](img/ISR197.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass a pure physical impulse across a circle using only eye contact and spontaneous bodily reactions.

## Overview
A facilitator-led ensemble warm-up focusing on non-verbal listening and the core improv skill of allowing yourself to be physically changed by a partner. Players pass a pure physical impulse across a circle using only eye contact and spontaneous bodily reactions, creating a continuous chain of movement without intellectualizing or miming.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle with enough space to move freely. No props or specific stage setup are needed. The facilitator stands just outside the circle to observe and side-coach.

## How to Play
1. Initiation: Player A creates a single, minimal, pure physical impulse. This is a raw movement, not a mimed action.
2. The Connection: Player A immediately makes deliberate eye contact with Player B across the circle, sending the energy of that impulse.
3. The Echo: Player B receives the eye contact and lets that energy physically change them. Without planning, Player B translates that feeling into a new spontaneous physical movement (e.g., if Player A twitched, Player B might organically recoil or stretch).
4. Pass It On: As soon as Player B's movement happens, they make eye contact with Player C, sending their new impulse forward.
5. Continuous Flow: The chain continues indefinitely. Players must drop their impulse once they pass it, returning to a neutral, receptive state.
6. Handling Broken Chains: If focus drops, someone misses a cue, or two players lock eyes simultaneously, the facilitator simply calls, 'Reset, breathe. [Player Name], send a new impulse.' Keep the momentum alive without making the break a failure.

## Coaching Notes
- Concrete Examples: A 'pure impulse' is a sudden chest expansion, a heavy sigh dropping the chin, or a shift in weight. An 'acting choice' (what to avoid) is miming drinking coffee, pretending to be a dog, or making a theatrical sad face.
- Facilitator Side-Coaching: Use prompts like 'Don't plan your move!', 'Let the eye contact surprise you!', 'Breathe and let your body react, not your brain!', and 'It is not charades, just move!'
- As a non-competitive Theater Game, there are no points, judges, or audience suggestions. The focus is entirely on the ensemble's process, deep listening, and group mind.

## Variations
- Amplification (The Wave): Each time the impulse is passed, the receiver must make their physical reaction 10 percent larger than the one they received, escalating until the movements are massive and full-body, then gradually shrinking them back down.
- Sound and String: Once the physical flow is established, allow players to attach a single, non-linguistic sound (a grunt, a sigh, a trill) to their physical impulse. The sound must be an organic extension of the movement, not a spoken word.

## Why It Works
It isolates the specific skill of being physically and emotionally changed by a partner, bypassing the intellect to build deep non-verbal listening and group mind.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical movements should be self-directed and require no physical contact between players, making it safe and accessible. Facilitators should encourage players to move within their own mobility limits; an impulse can be as small as a blink or a shift in breathing. Ensure players know they cannot fail at their reaction.

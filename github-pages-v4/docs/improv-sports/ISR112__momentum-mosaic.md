# Momentum Mosaic

![Momentum Mosaic — infographic](img/ISR112.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced, non-verbal ensemble exercise where players pass and evolve physical and vocal impulses around a circle.

## Overview
A fast-paced, non-verbal ensemble exercise where players pass and evolve physical and vocal impulses around a circle. By focusing purely on immediate, unthinking reactions, it trains radical presentness, full-body listening, and spontaneous reaction without intellectual processing.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle in a closed workshop or rehearsal space. A facilitator stands on the perimeter to side-coach and manage time. No props or set pieces are required.

## How to Play
1. Form the Circle: The ensemble forms a circle. The facilitator asks everyone to ground themselves, clear their minds, and focus their attention on the group.
2. The First Impulse: Player A makes strong eye contact with Player B (anyone else in the circle) and sends a clear, non-verbal 'impulse'—a simultaneous abstract sound and full-body movement directed at them.
3. The Reaction: Player B must instantly let that impulse 'hit' them. Without thinking, judging, or planning, Player B reacts physically and vocally to the energy, volume, and scale of Player A's movement.
4. The Pass: Player B's spontaneous reaction seamlessly becomes the start of a new impulse. Player B immediately turns, makes eye contact with Player C, and sends this newly transformed sound and movement toward them.
5. Continuous Flow: The momentum bounces continuously around the circle. The goal is fluid, unbroken energy transfer. No words are used—only abstract sounds and movements.
6. Ending Mechanic: To prevent the exercise from dragging, the facilitator runs it for a strict 3 to 5 minutes. For the final 30 seconds, the facilitator calls 'Double speed!' to force pure instinct, ending with a sharp 'Freeze! And shake it out' to cleanly conclude the game.

## Coaching Notes
- Actively side-coach to maintain flow: 'Stay out of your head!', 'Let your body lead!', 'React to the energy you were given, not what you planned!', and 'Breathe!'
- Remind players there is no audience, scoring, or judging; the focus is entirely on the ensemble's internal process, mutual support, and skill development.

## Variations
- Emotion Mosaic: The facilitator calls out specific emotions (e.g., 'Joy!', 'Panic!', 'Suspicion!'). The physical and vocal impulses must immediately take on the physical characteristics of that emotion while maintaining the rapid passing mechanic.
- Machine Mosaic: Instead of passing the impulse across the circle, players step into the center one by one. Each player attaches their own repeating sound and movement to the previous player's impulse, eventually building a giant, interconnected, kinetic ensemble 'machine'.

## Why It Works
It isolates non-verbal communication and physical listening, forcing players out of their heads and into their bodies. It builds ensemble trust through shared vulnerability and silliness without requiring prior scene-work experience.

## Safety & Inclusion
Play in a closed workshop environment to ensure explicit consent and psychological safety, removing any unpredictable 'public stranger' element. No physical contact is required; impulses are passed through space and eye contact. Players with mobility limitations can participate fully by adapting the physical impulses to their own comfortable range of motion (e.g., focusing on facial expressions, upper body gestures, and vocalizations).

# Scenario Shift

![Scenario Shift — infographic](img/IS146.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two players must continuously 'Yes, And' sudden, referee-imposed changes to their scene's object, environment, or character endowments.

## Overview
Scenario Shift is a fast-paced, high-energy improvisational game designed for competitive short-form matches. It challenges players to demonstrate quick thinking, adaptability, and fundamental improv skills by continuously 'Yes, And-ing' sudden and unexpected changes ('Shifts') imposed by the referee. The game blends audience suggestions, detailed object work, and dynamic character transformations into a single, cohesive, family-friendly competitive format.

## Setup
A team sends two players onto the playing area, with remaining teammates off-stage ready to tag in. The opposing team observes. The Referee asks the audience for an everyday, mundane location and one completely ordinary object that you would never expect to find there.

## How to Play
1. The two active players take the stage and immediately initiate a realistic, grounded scene within the suggested mundane location, featuring the out-of-place object as a prominent element.
2. At any unpredictable moment, the Referee dramatically calls out 'SHIFT!' to impose an instantaneous change.
3. The Referee imposes one of three changes: Object Warp (the object becomes wildly different), Environment Warp (the location becomes completely contrasting), or Character Warp (a player is endowed with a new character type or trait).
4. Players must instantly integrate the 'Shift!' without breaking character or visibly hesitating, adjusting their object work, dialogue, and surroundings accordingly.
5. Any player on the active team can tag in a teammate at any point to inject new energy; the tagged-out player immediately exits.
6. The Referee calls 'SHIFT!' multiple times within a round, ensuring rapid changes, and ends the round by calling 'SCENE!'.
7. The Referee awards positive points for Scene Start Genius (+1), Warp Mastery (+2 per shift), Object Work Excellence (+1), Pace & Energy (+1), and Audience Cheer Points (+1).
8. The Referee deducts points (-1) for a Content Foul (inappropriate content), Groaner Foul (weak pun), or Freeze Foul (hesitation or failing to 'Yes, And').
9. Teams alternate turns, completing a full round before the other team begins. The team with the highest cumulative score after 3-5 rounds wins.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee is central to the gameplay and acts as the ultimate chaos agent, controlling the pacing mechanism.
- Players must demonstrate 'Yes, And' to the absurd under extreme pressure, maintaining comedic momentum.
- Enforce the Freeze Foul if a player visibly freezes or hesitates for more than a few seconds to encourage fierce, fast-paced play.
- The nature of the Warp suggestions (e.g., explosive crystal orb, secret lair) should encourage whimsical and innocent humor.

## Why It Works
It rigorously tests core improv skills under pressure, specifically 'Yes, And', active listening, object work, character work, adaptability, and spontaneity. By continuously layering new constraints and empowering the referee as a primary instigator of chaos, it ensures every round is fresh, demanding, and hilariously unpredictable.

## Safety & Inclusion
The game strictly enforces family-friendly humor through the 'Content Foul', which penalizes blue humor, swearing, or inappropriate innuendo (optionally placing a paper bag over the offending player's head). This serves as a vital guardrail for appropriate humor.

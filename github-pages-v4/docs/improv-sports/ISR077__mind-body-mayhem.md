# Mind-Body Mayhem

![Mind-Body Mayhem — infographic](img/ISR077.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams perform a scene while juggling an escalating linguistic constraint and a demanding physical constraint.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where teams perform a scene while juggling exactly two escalating constraints: one strictly linguistic and one strictly physical. The comedy comes from the friction of maintaining a rigid vocal rule while navigating a demanding physical environment.

## Setup
Requires two teams of 2 to 3 players each and one Referee. No props or chairs are needed; the game relies entirely on mime and space work. The Referee prepares a list of Linguistic Constraints (e.g., speak only in questions) and Physical Constraints (e.g., the floor is covered in sticky glue).

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a location suggestion from the audience and explains that the improvisers will be hit with two surprise rules during the scene.
2. Team A begins their scene. They have 30 seconds to establish a strong base reality (who, what, where) with no constraints.
3. The Referee blows the whistle and announces the Linguistic Constraint. Players must immediately adopt this vocal rule while continuing the narrative.
4. After another 30 to 45 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle again and layers on the Physical Constraint.
5. For the final minute, players must maintain BOTH the linguistic and physical constraints simultaneously. The Referee blows the whistle to call a foul if a player drops either rule.
6. The Referee calls 'Scene!' at the 2-minute mark. Team B then takes the stage for a new scene with a new audience suggestion and a fresh pair of constraints.
7. After both teams have played, the Referee asks the audience to applaud for the team that best handled the mayhem. The winning team receives 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- Use the whistle to call fouls if a player breaks a constraint; this adds comedic pressure.
- Ensure players establish a strong base reality in the first 30 seconds before constraints are added.
- Keep the strict two-constraint limit to prevent cognitive overload and scene collapse, keeping the game fun rather than frustrating.
- Encourage players to fully commit to the mime and space work to avoid talking-head scenes.

## Variations
- Audience's Revenge: Before the match, the audience writes down physical and linguistic constraints on slips of paper. The Referee draws them randomly from two separate buckets during the scene.
- Tag-Out Mayhem: If a player drops a constraint, the Referee blows the whistle and calls a foul. That player is immediately tagged out and replaced by a teammate on the sidelines, who must seamlessly enter the scene and adopt both constraints.

## Why It Works
It forces players out of talking-head scenes by mandating physical and environmental interaction, while the strict two-constraint limit prevents cognitive overload and scene collapse.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical constraints must be safe and rely entirely on mime, requiring no actual acrobatics or dangerous stunts. The game is highly adaptable for players with mobility limitations; physical constraints can be translated to upper-body movements, vocal strain, or facial expressions. Keep constraints family-friendly and clean.

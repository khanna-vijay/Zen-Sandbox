# Story Sprint

![Story Sprint — infographic](img/ISR256.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams frantically weave a single continuous narrative by tagging out and taking over the exact same characters every 30 seconds.

## Overview
Story Sprint is a high-speed, competitive short-form game where two teams collaboratively weave a single continuous narrative. Every 20 to 30 seconds, the Referee calls 'SWITCH!', forcing the opposing team to tag out the frozen players, assume their exact physical positions, and seamlessly continue the story as the exact same characters. It demands intense active listening, rapid physical mimicry, and flawless narrative continuity.

## Setup
Requires a Referee, two competing teams (e.g., Red and Blue) with at least 2 to 3 players each, and a whistle or buzzer. The Referee stands downstage center or off to the side with clear visibility. Two players from the starting team take the stage.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a single suggestion from the audience (e.g., a mundane location or a specific event) to ground the scene.
2. The Referee points to the starting team and yells 'GO!' Two players from that team begin the scene, establishing characters, relationships, and the environment.
3. After 20 to 30 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle and yells 'SWITCH!'
4. The players currently on stage must instantly freeze in their exact physical positions and stop speaking.
5. Two players from the opposing team sprint on stage, carefully tap out the frozen players, and assume their exact physical poses. They are now playing the exact same characters that were just established.
6. As soon as the new players are in position, the scene immediately resumes. The incoming players must 'Yes, And' the previous dialogue, justify their physical positions, and drive the narrative forward without breaking character.
7. The Referee continues to call 'SWITCH!' every 20 to 30 seconds, alternating teams for about 3 to 4 minutes until the story reaches a natural, or hilariously chaotic, conclusion.
8. At the end of the game, the Referee recaps the story and asks the audience to applaud for the team that had the most seamless transitions, best physical matches, and strongest narrative justifications. The winning team receives 5 points.
9. Standard fouls apply during play: a 'Delay of Game' foul (-1 point) can be called if a team hesitates during a transition, and a 'clean-content' foul (-15 points) ensures content remains family-friendly.

## Coaching Notes
- Emphasize narrative continuity over isolated gags.
- Remind players that a confident, simple justification is always better than a frantic, complex one.
- Encourage intense active listening so incoming players can seamlessly pick up the exact thought or sentence.
- Watch for 'Delay of Game' fouls if a team hesitates during a transition.

## Variations
- New Faces, Same Place: Instead of taking over the exact characters, the incoming team enters as entirely new characters in the exact same location, reacting to the mess the previous characters just left behind.
- Genre Sprint: The Referee calls out a new movie or literary genre with every 'SWITCH!' (e.g., 'SWITCH! Western!'), forcing the incoming team to adopt the physical poses but instantly filter the ongoing story through the new genre.

## Why It Works
The strict 20-30 second time limit creates high energy and urgency, forcing intense active listening and rapid justification. Physical matching and character sharing build instant ensemble trust while demanding flawless narrative continuity.

## Safety & Inclusion
Players must practice safe tag-outs; no shoving or aggressive physical contact when replacing a frozen player. Teams should establish boundaries beforehand regarding physical proximity and levels (e.g., avoiding lifting or unsafe balancing). The fast pace can induce panic, so facilitators should monitor the physical intensity and remind players to stay grounded.

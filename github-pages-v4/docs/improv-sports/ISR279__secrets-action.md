# Secrets & Action

![Secrets & Action — infographic](img/ISR279.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A dynamic narrative game where improvisers instantly toggle between revealing intimate backstory and escalating high-stakes physical chaos.

## Overview
A dynamic, director-called narrative game where improvisers must instantly toggle between two extreme storytelling modes: 'Deep Secrets' (revealing intimate backstory, relationship history, and hidden motives) and 'Action Movie' (rapid-fire plot advancement, physical escalation, and immediate cause-and-effect). By switching back and forth, players weave rich emotional history into high-stakes chaos.

## Setup
2 to 3 players on stage. 1 Director (facilitator, referee, or host) standing downstage or in the wings with a microphone. Ask the audience for a mundane location and a relationship between the characters. No props or special tech required.

## How to Play
1. The players begin a grounded scene based on the audience suggestion, establishing their characters and the environment.
2. At any point, the Director calls out 'Deep Secrets!' The players must immediately pause the physical action and shift into intense, emotional exposition. They must reveal hidden truths, past grievances, or deep personal feelings about each other, focusing entirely on backstory and character depth.
3. When the Director calls 'Action Movie!', the players instantly snap out of the emotional exposition and into high-stakes, rapid-fire plot progression. Every line or physical action must trigger an immediate, escalating consequence (cause-and-effect).
4. The Director toggles back and forth between 'Deep Secrets' and 'Action Movie' every 15 to 30 seconds.
5. The players must justify the sudden shifts, using the secrets they just revealed to fuel the action, and using the chaotic action to trigger new emotional revelations.
6. The scene continues for about 3 minutes, building to a climax where the emotional backstory and the physical plot resolve simultaneously, at which point the Director calls 'Scene!'

## Coaching Notes
- In a judged competitive format, judges award points (1-5) based on the players' ability to justify the sudden transitions, their emotional commitment during the 'Secrets' phases, and their physical commitment and pacing during the 'Action' phases.
- To increase audience interaction, the Director can hand out two large colored signs (e.g., a heart for Secrets, a lightning bolt for Action) to audience members, letting them hold up the signs to dictate when the modes switch.
- Dynamic Pacing: Rapidly alternating between slow/emotional and fast/physical keeps the audience highly engaged.
- Director-Controlled Rhythm: The facilitator can save a stalling scene by instantly shifting the energy.

## Variations
- Genre Swap: Instead of Secrets and Action, use two contrasting genres suggested by the audience (e.g., 'Soap Opera' and 'Sci-Fi'), forcing players to toggle the narrative style and tropes.
- Internal Monologue: During 'Deep Secrets', instead of characters talking to each other, the action freezes and one character steps forward to deliver a dramatic inner monologue to the audience, before snapping back into 'Action Movie'.

## Why It Works
It isolates specific skills, forcing players to practice the distinct mechanics of backstory generation versus plot advancement, while relying entirely on the performers' physicality and emotional commitment.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical safety is paramount during 'Action Movie' phases; players must mime all violence, weapons, or stunts safely without making aggressive physical contact. For 'Deep Secrets', players should keep revelations grounded in the reality of the scene (e.g., 'I stole your prized recipe') rather than introducing real-world trauma or crossing personal boundaries. Ensure all physical escalations respect the mobility levels of the performers on stage.

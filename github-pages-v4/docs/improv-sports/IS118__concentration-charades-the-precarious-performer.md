# Concentration Charades: The Precarious Performer

![Concentration Charades: The Precarious Performer — infographic](img/IS118.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Act out charades prompts while simultaneously balancing a challenging, unstable object on a designated body part.

## Overview
This game is a unique take on traditional charades where players must act out prompts while simultaneously balancing a challenging, unstable object on a designated body part. This strict physical constraint, the 'Point of Concentration,' forces performers to prioritize maintaining balance, thereby overriding self-consciousness and intellectual 'acting' choices.

## Setup
Divide participants into two or more teams (3-5 players per team). Prepare a list of charades prompts (movie titles, common phrases, actions, objects, book titles) on separate slips of paper. Gather small, lightweight, and slightly unstable objects (e.g., a beanbag, plastic cup, small stuffed animal, slightly deflated balloon, small book, or feather). Designate a playing area and set a timer for 90 seconds per round.

## How to Play
1. One player from Team A steps forward to be the 'Precarious Performer.'
2. Before receiving their prompt, the Performer selects one of the designated objects and places it on a part of their body where it will be challenging to balance (e.g., head, back of hand, shoulder, knee while standing, forehead).
3. Once the object is balanced, the Performer receives their charades prompt and the 90-second timer begins.
4. The Performer acts out the charades prompt silently, using only gestures and physical actions, while continuously maintaining the balance of the object.
5. Crucial Rule: If the object falls, the Performer must immediately freeze their charades actions, retrieve the object, re-balance it in the exact same spot, and only then can they continue acting out the prompt. The timer continues to run during this process.
6. Teammates shout out guesses. If the team guesses correctly within the time limit, they earn a point.
7. Teams take turns sending up a new Performer for subsequent rounds. The team with the most points at the end wins.

## Coaching Notes
- The strict and unwavering Point of Concentration (POC) is to continuously maintain the balance of the chosen object on the designated body part throughout the entire performance.
- Remind players that the intellect should be entirely consumed by the physical task of balancing, forcing the body to find intuitive and spontaneous ways to communicate the charades prompt as a secondary, almost subconscious, activity.
- Enforce the 'Crucial Rule' strictly: absolutely no acting or communicating can occur while the object is dropped or being re-balanced.

## Why It Works
It develops integrated physical presence and intuitive communication under constraint. The constant demand of balancing forces performers out of their heads and into their physical reality, overriding self-consciousness and intellectual 'acting' choices, embodying the 'playing without acting' principle.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing area is completely clear of tripping hazards, as performers will be highly distracted by balancing. Use only lightweight, soft, or safe objects that will not cause injury or break when dropped.

# The Offer Chain

![The Offer Chain — infographic](img/ISR027.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive scene where players must explicitly accept and build upon the last offer made, or face a 'Broken Link' foul and rewind.

## Overview
The Offer Chain is a fast-paced competitive scene where players must explicitly accept and build upon the last offer made. If a player ignores or blocks an offer, the Referee calls a 'Broken Link' foul. The offending player must rewind, accept the ignored offer, and justify their response to maintain scene integrity.

## Setup
2 players total (one from Team A, one from Team B). No props needed. Standard competitive setup with a Referee downstage. The audience provides an initial suggestion (e.g., a relationship and a location) and acts as secondary judges.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a suggestion from the audience, such as a relationship and a location, and starts the clock (typically 3 minutes).
2. The two players begin a scene. Every time a player speaks or acts, they must clearly accept and incorporate the previous player's specific verbal or physical offer before adding their own new information.
3. If a player blocks, ignores, or steamrolls an offer, the Referee immediately blows the whistle and calls 'Broken Link!'
4. The offending player's team loses 1 point, and the player must 'rewind' to the exact moment before their mistake.
5. The player must now explicitly accept the missed offer and justify their reaction within the reality of the scene, maintaining character and narrative integrity rather than relying on random comedic gimmicks.
6. Play continues seamlessly from the repaired moment. The scene ends when time is called, followed by audience applause to determine the overall scene winner.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee deducts 1 point for every 'Broken Link' foul called. At the end of the scene, the audience votes by applause to award 5 points to the team that drove the best narrative.
- Encourage the audience to actively listen and yell 'Ooh!' if they catch a dropped offer before the Referee blows the whistle, making them active participants in the listening exercise.
- Ensure players maintain scene integrity by forcing narrative justification rather than relying on random comedic gimmicks during the rewind.

## Variations
- Three-Way Chain: Play with 3 players (mixed teams). This increases the difficulty of tracking offers and is great for advanced listeners.
- Physical Chain: Focus entirely on object work and physical offers. Verbal offers are allowed, but the Referee only calls 'Broken Link' for ignored physical endowments or mimed objects.

## Why It Works
This game directly targets the core improv skill of listening and accepting, using a 'rewind and repair' mechanic that is both a highly effective teaching tool and a hilarious spectator event.

## Safety & Inclusion
A standard content foul applies for any non-family-friendly content or language. Ensure physical boundaries are respected; players should not force physical contact during 'rewinds'. For accessibility, the definition of an 'offer' can be calibrated to the players' mobility, and the Referee should adjust the strictness of 'Broken Link' calls to accommodate different cognitive processing speeds.

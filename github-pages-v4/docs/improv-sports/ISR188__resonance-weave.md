# Resonance Weave

![Resonance Weave — infographic](img/ISR188.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass and organically morph a combined physical movement and vocal sound across the room.

## Overview
A focused ensemble warm-up where players pass a combined physical movement and vocal sound across the room. As the 'resonance' is passed from player to player, it organically morphs and evolves. It trains deep listening, eye contact, and physical commitment while keeping players out of their heads and reacting on instinct.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle or comfortably scattered around the room with enough space to move freely. The room should be quiet to allow for focus. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. One player (the Initiator) makes clear, undeniable eye contact with another player across the space.
2. The Initiator sends a 'Resonance' by performing a distinct, repeatable physical movement paired with a vocal sound (e.g., a deep breath, a hum, a sharp click, or a sustained tone).
3. The receiving player accepts the pass by immediately mirroring the exact movement and sound back to the Initiator, establishing a clear connection.
4. The receiver then allows the movement and sound to organically morph into something entirely new (e.g., a sweeping arm motion with a hum becomes a sharp shoulder shrug with a click).
5. Once transformed, the player makes eye contact with a new person and passes their new Resonance.
6. Players have a maximum of three seconds to morph the resonance and pass it on, adhering to the '3-Second Rule'.

## Coaching Notes
- Enforce the '3-Second Rule' strictly to prevent overthinking or self-indulgence.
- Escalate the pacing by calling out 'Double Time!' to force rapid-fire, reflex-driven passing.
- Call out 'Slow Motion' to challenge the players' physical control.
- Encourage deep focus, sustained eye contact, and physical commitment throughout the exercise.
- Ensure players are grounding their abstract movements with concrete, perceptible vocal sounds.
- Remind the group that the 'win' is a continuous, unbroken flow of sound and movement that keeps the entire ensemble engaged.

## Variations
- Speed Weave: The mirroring and morphing steps are removed. Players simply receive the sound/movement and instantly pass a completely new one as fast as possible, relying purely on reflex.
- Emotion Weave: The facilitator calls out an emotion (e.g., 'Joy', 'Panic', 'Curiosity'). The sound and movement must be driven by that emotion as it morphs around the room.
- Blind Weave: Players stand in a tight circle and close their eyes. The pass is done purely by directing sound toward the person next to them, who must pick up the sound, morph it, and pass it along.

## Why It Works
It builds ensemble awareness and non-verbal communication skills by grounding abstract movement with concrete vocal sounds, forcing players to rely on instinct and deep listening rather than overthinking.

## Safety & Inclusion
This game is completely non-contact and physically safe. It is highly accessible, as the movement can be adapted to any physical mobility level; players can participate while seated, and movements can be as small as a facial expression or a hand gesture. Remind players to respect their own physical boundaries and avoid straining their voices.

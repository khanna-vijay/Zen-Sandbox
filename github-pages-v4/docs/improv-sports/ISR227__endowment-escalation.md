# Endowment Escalation

![Endowment Escalation — infographic](img/ISR227.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams alternate scenes using a single neutral prop that gains compounding physical and sensory endowments from the audience.

## Overview
A fast-paced, competitive short-form game where two-person teams alternate scenes centered around a single neutral prop. As the game progresses, the Referee asks the audience to add up to three compounding physical or sensory endowments to the object. Players must juggle these physical realities while maintaining a coherent relationship-driven scene, with the Referee awarding points for physical commitment and calling fouls for dropped endowments.

## Setup
Requires two teams (e.g., Red and Blue), a Referee equipped with a whistle, and a single neutral, versatile prop placed center stage (like a foam cylinder, a wooden block, or a rolled-up towel). The prop should not have a strong pre-existing identity.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a base identity for the neutral prop (e.g., a toaster, a steering wheel) and its first physical or sensory endowment (e.g., it is incredibly sticky, it is boiling hot).
2. Two players from Team A step forward and begin a scene using the prop, clearly demonstrating its identity and the first physical endowment while establishing a relationship and dialogue.
3. After 15 to 20 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Switch!' Two players from Team B immediately tag in, replacing Team A.
4. Team B must start a completely new scene, but they MUST use the same prop and honor its established physical endowment.
5. After a few alternating tags, the Referee blows the whistle, freezes the action, and asks the audience for a second physical or sensory endowment (e.g., it also vibrates violently). The next pair must now play with both endowments simultaneously.
6. The game continues with alternating tags. The Referee eventually adds a third and final physical endowment (e.g., it smells like rotten eggs).
7. The maximum number of simultaneous endowments is strictly capped at three to prevent scene collapse and cognitive overload.
8. The round ends after both teams have had a chance to play scenes with all three endowments active. The Referee then awards points.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee awards up to 5 points per team based on physical commitment to the endowments, relationship dynamics between the two players, and narrative justification.
- Call fouls (like a content foul for inappropriate content or a 'Block' for ignoring an endowment) to deduct points.
- Encourage the audience to cheer to influence the Referee's subjective scoring.
- Ensure endowments are strictly physical or sensory to ground object work.
- Focus on two-player scenes to ensure relationship dynamics and dialogue do not get lost in the physical gimmick.

## Variations
- Narrative Escalation: Instead of starting new scenes on a tag, the incoming pair must continue the exact narrative and characters of the outgoing pair, seamlessly picking up the dialogue.
- Drop and Swap: When a fourth endowment is added by the audience, the first one is dropped, creating a rolling window of three physical traits to test memory and adaptability.

## Why It Works
It forces players to ground their object work and physical commitment while simultaneously managing relationship dynamics and dialogue, testing their ability to juggle multiple realities without dropping the core scene.

## Safety & Inclusion
Endowments must be strictly physical or sensory, avoiding emotional or trauma-based triggers. The Referee strictly filters audience suggestions to ensure they are family-friendly and physically safe to mime without risking injury (e.g., rejecting 'it is covered in razor blades' in favor of 'it is covered in wet slime'). Players should respect physical boundaries when interacting with the shared prop, ensuring no tug-of-war dynamics lead to accidental physical harm.

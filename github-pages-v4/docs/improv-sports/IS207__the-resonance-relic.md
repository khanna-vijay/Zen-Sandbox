# The Resonance Relic

![The Resonance Relic — infographic](img/IS207.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Collaboratively build an evolving narrative around a single object whose identity dynamically shifts on a cue, while preserving all past consequences.

## Overview
Two players collaboratively build a narrative around a single, ordinary object whose perceived identity and purpose dynamically shift throughout the scene. Triggered by an audio cue, players must instantly assign the object a completely new identity. However, all narrative consequences and implications from its previous forms must remain absolutely true and active within the scene.

## Setup
Two players on stage. Place a single, common, unassuming object (e.g., a stick, a rock, a shoe, a piece of fabric) center stage to act as the 'Resonance Relic'. A judge or host needs a clear audio cue (e.g., a bell, a specific phrase) to signal a 'Resonance Shift'.

## How to Play
1. Phase 1 (Initial Identity): The scene begins. Players immediately establish a narrative context, agree upon the first identity and purpose of the Resonance Relic, and develop a mini-narrative arc around it for about 45-60 seconds.
2. Phase 2 (The Resonance Shift): The host triggers the audio cue. Instantly, the previous definition of the object ceases to be valid.
3. Players must collaboratively and instantly establish a completely new and different identity and purpose for the same physical object within the unfolding scene.
4. Apply the Crucial Rule: While the object's identity changes, all narrative consequences and implications of its previous forms and uses remain absolutely true and active (e.g., an injury from a 'sword' remains even if the object is now a 'loaf of bread').
5. Phase 3 (Subsequent Shifts): The game continues through 3-4 shifts over a 4-6 minute scene. Players must actively justify how the object transformed and why it holds its new identity, building upon the increasingly complex narrative history.

## Coaching Notes
- The physical object never leaves the stage or changes form; only its perceived identity and function change.
- Upon the cue, players must immediately cease their previous interaction. Hesitation or a lapse in agreement should be avoided.
- Ensure the new identity is clearly distinct from the immediate previous one (e.g., a sword cannot immediately become a dagger).
- All plot points, emotional states, injuries, or benefits from past identities are irrevocably canon and must be reincorporated.
- Encourage players to integrate a logical (or fantastical) justification for why the object transformed within the scene's emerging reality.
- Focus on narrative coherence and weaving a cohesive story, rather than going for isolated gags.
- Maintain emotional truth and character objectives amidst the radical shifts.

## Why It Works
It forces instantaneous 'Yes, And' on a meta-level, demanding intense listening and shared reality-building under pressure. Players practice complex narrative weaving, justification, reincorporation, and adaptability by turning mandated unexpected shifts into a cohesive story.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety when interacting with the object, especially if its previous identity involved physical conflict or high-energy movement. Maintain standard scene consent and physical boundaries.

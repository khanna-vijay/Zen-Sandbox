# Reality Shift

![Reality Shift — infographic](img/IS089.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Performers build an evolving narrative that is continually reshaped by significant, undeniable twists introduced by an external Architect.

## Overview
Reality Shift is an improvisational game where performers collaboratively construct an evolving narrative that is continually reshaped by significant, often absurd, external interventions. At regular intervals, a designated 'Architect' pauses the scene to introduce a major, undeniable twist, which the performers must instantly 'Yes, And' and integrate into their ongoing story. This forces deep adaptability, collaborative storytelling, and the creation of highly dynamic, unpredictable narratives.

## Setup
Two or three performers take the stage. A designated 'Architect' (typically the host, a judge, or a highly skilled director) stands by with a clear signaling device like a loud bell, buzzer, or light. Get a maximum of three audience suggestions to start the scene (e.g., a specific location, a defined relationship, and an immediate objective or problem).

## How to Play
1. The performers begin a normal, unscripted scene based on the audience suggestions.
2. At regular, predetermined intervals (e.g., every 90 seconds), the Architect clearly cues a freeze using their bell, buzzer, or light signal.
3. The Architect steps forward and introduces a major, undeniable narrative twist or revelation.
4. The Architect gives a clear 'Unfreeze!' cue.
5. The performers unfreeze and must immediately, verbally and physically, 'Yes, And' the twist. They must integrate it demonstrably and without hesitation into the ongoing scene.
6. The game continues for a set number of twists (e.g., 3-4 twists) or a total scene duration (e.g., 5-7 minutes), at which point the Architect or host ends the scene.

## Coaching Notes
- The Architect is a storyteller-catalyst. Each twist should ideally build upon previous ones, propelling the narrative forward or revealing deeper layers, rather than introducing disconnected, random elements.
- Architects should aim to raise the stakes, reveal hidden truths, alter the core objective/relationship, or introduce a MacGuffin/new element.
- Performers must bravely 'Yes, And' radical changes. A 'failure' in this game is ignoring a twist, trying to explain it away logically, or resisting its implications.
- Players must actively help each other incorporate the twist. If a partner's reality changes, your job is to authentically engage with and help define their new role, demonstrating shared reality.
- Do not question the twist; it is the new absolute truth of the universe.

## Why It Works
The game rigorously tests the essential improv skills of 'Being Changed' and 'Yes, And.' By forcing constant narrative shifts, performers cannot rely on simple setups or comfortable patterns, which drives dynamic, complex storytelling and intense collaborative scene-building.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Architect must ensure that their twists respect the performers' physical and emotional boundaries, avoiding mandates that force players into non-consensual physical contact or overly triggering subject matter.

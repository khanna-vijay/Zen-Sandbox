# Thought Bubbles

![Thought Bubbles — infographic](img/ISR034.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players alternate revealing their true internal thoughts to the audience before delivering contrasting external dialogue to their scene partner.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where players alternate revealing their true internal thoughts to the audience before delivering their contrasting external dialogue to their scene partner. The comedy comes from dramatic irony: the audience knows the characters' secret motives, while the characters maintain a polite, deceptive, or clueless facade.

## Setup
Two players, one from each team. The Referee solicits a Relationship and a Location from the audience. Players must establish a clear physical gesture, such as placing a hand on their chin and looking directly at the audience, to indicate when they are speaking an internal thought.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets the audience suggestions, sets a timer for 3 minutes, and blows the whistle to begin.
2. Player A starts by making the 'Thought Bubble' gesture (e.g., hand on chin). They speak their character's unfiltered internal thought directly to the audience. Player B's character cannot hear this thought.
3. Player A drops the gesture, turns to Player B, and delivers their external dialogue. This dialogue should heavily contrast with, mask, or awkwardly attempt to execute the internal thought.
4. Player B listens to Player A's external dialogue. Before responding, Player B makes the Thought Bubble gesture and speaks their own internal thought to the audience, reacting to Player A's spoken words or revealing their own secret agenda.
5. Player B drops the gesture and delivers their external dialogue to Player A.
6. The scene continues back and forth. Every single turn must consist of an Internal Thought followed by External Dialogue.
7. The Referee awards +1 point for a strong contrast between thought and speech, and +2 points for a major character reveal.
8. The Referee MUST call a 'Telepathy Foul' (-1 point) if a player accidentally responds to their partner's internal thought instead of their external dialogue, ensuring the reality of the scene stays intact.
9. The audience votes for the winning team at the end of the game based on who had the funniest contrasts.

## Coaching Notes
- Ensure the physical toggle is clear so the audience and scene partner know when it's a thought vs. spoken dialogue.
- Focus on dramatic irony, character duality, and active listening.
- Avoid the 'Telepathy Foul': players must only react to external dialogue, not the internal thoughts they just heard.
- The Referee can call a clean-content foul for inappropriate content.
- Example: Player A thinks 'I am terrified of dogs' but says 'What a cute pitbull!' Player B thinks 'This guy is sweating profusely' but says 'Would you like to pet him?'

## Variations
- Director's Cut: Instead of every turn, players speak normally until the Referee blows a whistle and points at a player, forcing them to immediately freeze and reveal their current internal thought before resuming the scene.
- Three-Way Bubble: Add a third player to the scene. The web of secrets and dramatic irony becomes exponentially more complex as players must track who knows what.

## Why It Works
It develops dramatic irony, clear physical toggles, character duality, and active listening. The game forces players to hold two realities at once and heightens the comedic tension between what is felt and what is said.

## Safety & Inclusion
Emphasize that 'internal thoughts' should never be used as an excuse for mean-spirited, bigoted, or unsafe content; keep the secrets petty, absurd, or character-driven. The physical gesture ensures accessibility for neurodivergent players and audience members by making the subtext and emotional reality of the scene explicitly clear.

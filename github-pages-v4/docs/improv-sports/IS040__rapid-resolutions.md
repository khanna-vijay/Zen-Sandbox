# Rapid Resolutions

![Rapid Resolutions — infographic](img/IS040.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A Game of Ingenious Solutions to Outlandish Problems

## Overview
Rapid Resolutions is an energetic improv game where two teams compete to invent and collaboratively demonstrate the most ingenious and hilarious step-by-step solutions to bizarre problems suggested by the audience, all within a strict time limit. Players must use quick thinking, 'Yes, And' teamwork, and strong object work to physically perform their proposed 'resolutions', while a Referee manages the game, scores based on creativity, collaboration, and physicality, and enforces family-friendly content.

## Setup
The two competing teams (Red and Blue) line up on opposite sides of the stage. The Referee stands center stage, ready to present challenges and manage the game. A timekeeper is essential for the strict time limits.

## How to Play
1. The Referee turns to the audience and asks for the most outlandish, silly, or seemingly impossible problem that needs solving.
2. The Referee selects the most promising and family-friendly suggestion and presents the challenge to one team (e.g., 'Red Team, your challenge is... You have 60 seconds to show us your Rapid Resolution!').
3. The active team has a maximum of 5 seconds for a quick huddle or for one player to step forward and begin.
4. The team has 60-90 seconds to collaboratively perform their step-by-step solution. This isn't just telling; it's showing. Players must use 'Yes, And' to build on each other's ideas, employing strong object work to demonstrate the tools, techniques, and stages of their solution.
5. The performance should have a clear comedic beginning (acknowledging the problem) and a satisfying (or comically failed) end (the problem is 'resolved').
6. The Referee shouts 'Time!' or 'Resolution Complete!' when the time limit is reached or when a particularly strong comedic button is hit.
7. The Referee offers immediate, playful feedback and awards up to 5 points based on Creativity & Absurd Logic (up to 2 points), Collaborative Ingenuity (up to 1 point), Physicality & Object Work (up to 1 point), and Energy & Pacing (up to 1 point).
8. The opposing team then receives a new, distinct problem from the audience, and the process repeats.
9. The game typically runs for 2-3 problems per team. The team with the highest cumulative score at the end of the game is declared the winner.

## Coaching Notes
- Referee Game Management: The Referee acts as the master of ceremonies, introducing problems, starting/stopping rounds, keeping the energy high, and engaging in playful banter with the players.
- Content Foul: Strictly enforced. Any suggestion or resolution step that is not family-friendly, contains blue humor, swearing, or innuendo results in an immediate foul and a significant point deduction (e.g., -3 points) or disqualification for the round.
- Groaner Foul: Deduct 1 point for excessively obvious, uncreative, or genuinely bad pun-based 'solutions' that halt the comedic momentum.
- Silence Foul: Deduct 1 point if a team fails to initiate any part of their resolution within 10 seconds of receiving the problem, or if there are prolonged silences.
- Solo Show Foul: Deduct 1 point if one player monopolizes the entire resolution without allowing teammates to contribute meaningfully.
- Object Work & Character: Encourage players to physically demonstrate the often-absurd steps and spontaneously adopt characters to explain or enact parts of their solution, making the abstract concrete and hilarious.

## Why It Works
The strict time limit demands immediate choices, quick reactions, and sustained high energy. It tests foundational 'Yes, And' skills, active listening for seamless collaboration, and collaborative storytelling by weaving individual contributions into a shared, dynamic, and humorous narrative of problem-solving.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee actively screens all audience problem suggestions to ensure they are age-appropriate and wholesome. The strict Content Foul is a zero-tolerance policy for inappropriate humor, acting as a constant safeguard to ensure all content remains suitable for all ages.

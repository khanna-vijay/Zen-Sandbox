# Echo Chamber

![Echo Chamber — infographic](img/ISR035.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive scene where players are explicitly rewarded with a bell for active listening and callbacks.

## Overview
Echo Chamber is a fast-paced competitive scene where players are explicitly rewarded for active listening and callbacks. A referee tracks 3 to 4 major details (Echoes) established early in the scene. Whenever a player successfully reincorporates one of these Echoes later on, the referee rings a bell, racking up points silently without stopping the action. Vague or unhelpful offers are penalized with a foul, forcing players to be highly specific and pay fierce attention to each other's contributions.

## Setup
Requires 4 players (2 per team) and a Referee. The Referee needs a clear-sounding bell (like a desk bell) and a whistle. No props or set pieces are required. The audience provides a starting suggestion, such as an unusual object, a strange location, or a bizarre secret.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a single suggestion from the audience to inspire the scene.
2. The scene begins with players from both teams interacting and building the narrative.
3. During the first minute, the Referee listens for the first 3 to 4 highly specific, usable details introduced by the players (e.g., a fear of mayonnaise, a squeaky left shoe, a sentient teacup). The Referee mentally locks these in as the Active Echoes.
4. As the scene progresses, whenever a player organically brings back, heightens, or cleverly references one of those Active Echoes, the Referee rings the bell (DING!).
5. The players do not stop or break character when the bell rings; they use the energy of the bell to fuel the scene's momentum.
6. If a player makes a frustratingly vague offer (e.g., 'Look at that thing over there!' instead of 'Look at that three-legged hamster!'), the Referee blows the whistle, stops play momentarily, and calls a Whisper Echo Foul.
7. If a player blatantly ignores a major established detail that was just handed to them, the Referee can call a Deaf Ear Foul.
8. At the end of the 3 to 4 minute scene, the Referee calls 'Scene!' and tallies the points out loud for the audience. Teams earn 2 points for every successful bell ring and lose 1 point for every foul.

## Coaching Notes
- The bell instantly rewards players and trains the audience without interrupting the dialogue.
- Explicitly penalize vague, unhelpful offers via the Whisper Echo Foul to force specificity.
- Scoring is announced as a lump sum at the end of the scene to preserve narrative flow.
- The audience naturally learns to cheer every time the bell rings, creating a gamified, high-energy atmosphere.

## Variations
- Audience Echoes: Instead of the players generating the details, the Referee asks the audience for 4 highly specific, unrelated details before the scene begins. The players must weave them into the scene, getting a bell ring each time they successfully integrate one.
- Blind Echoes (Rehearsal/Warm-up): Players perform the scene back-to-back or blindfolded. This removes visual cues and forces them to rely entirely on auditory active listening to catch and reuse the Echoes.

## Why It Works
Mechanically forces players to actively listen and use each other's ideas. It requires zero props or setup, relying entirely on player skill and memory to build a cohesive narrative through specific callbacks.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the bell and whistle are not sounded directly next to players' ears. To maintain a safe and supportive environment, the Referee should establish that Echoes must be character choices or scene details, never physical traits or personal attributes of the actors themselves. Standard content boundaries apply; inappropriate material receives a standard foul.

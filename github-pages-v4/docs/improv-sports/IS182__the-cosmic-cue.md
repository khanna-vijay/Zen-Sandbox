# The Cosmic Cue

![The Cosmic Cue — infographic](img/IS182.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Improvisers must adapt their scene, instantly and hilariously, to sudden, reality-bending rules dictated by the audience.

## Overview
The Cosmic Cue is an improv game where 'Cosmonauts' begin a scene based on a basic audience suggestion. Periodically, the Host calls 'FREEZE!' and solicits 'Cosmic Cues' from the audience—reality-bending rules that alter physical laws, character traits, sensory experiences, or narrative twists. The improvisers must instantly and seamlessly integrate each new cue into the ongoing scene, while also remembering and maintaining all previously introduced rules.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 improvisers ('Cosmonauts') and one Host/Referee on a standard improv stage with minimal or no props. The Host is crucial for guiding audience suggestions, delivering cues, and potentially scoring.

## How to Play
1. Scene Initiation (The Launch): The Host solicits a single, simple scene suggestion from the audience (e.g., a location, relationship, or object). The Cosmonauts immediately begin an improvised scene based on this suggestion.
2. The Cosmic Interruption (Anomaly Detected!): After 45-90 seconds, or when a clear scene beat is established, the Host shouts, 'FREEZE! COSMIC CUE!' The Cosmonauts must freeze in their exact positions and silence.
3. Audience's Command (The Universal Directive): The Host asks the audience for a 'Cosmic Cue' to alter the scene's reality. The Host prompts with categories (physical laws, character quirks, sensory distortions, or narrative twists) and chooses one concise, actionable cue.
4. Reality Shift (Integrating the Anomaly): The Host clearly states the chosen cue to the Cosmonauts. The scene unfreezes, and the Cosmonauts must immediately integrate this new rule without question or hesitation, treating it as undeniable reality.
5. Continuous Play and Escalation (The Event Horizon): Repeat the interruption and reality shift 3-4 more times. Players must integrate the newest cue while remembering and maintaining all previously introduced cues.
6. Scene End (Return to Base): The Host calls 'Scene!' after 3-5 Cosmic Cues have been introduced and the chaotic narrative reaches a comedic conclusion.

## Coaching Notes
- Host Prompting: Guide the audience by asking for specific categories of cues (e.g., 'Give me a new physical law!' or 'Give me a sensory distortion!') to ensure playable, fun ideas.
- Scoring/Judging: If playing competitively, award points for seamless integration, full commitment to all overlapping realities, and finding creative humor in the constraints.
- Penalties: Penalize players for ignoring a cue, breaking character to question or clarify how a cue works, or 'wimping out' in frustration.
- The cue is reality: No explanation is needed within the scene for why the reality shifted; players must simply 'yes, and' the new state of the universe.

## Why It Works
It combines rapid-fire adaptability with the immediate acceptance of offers and high-stakes transformation. It challenges improvisers to embody the core principle of 'accepting gifts' at a significant level, acting as a 'New Choice' applied to the very fabric of the scene's reality.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical cues (like altered gravity or invisible lava) are played safely without risking trips, falls, or collisions. Players should respect physical boundaries and consent even when adopting new character quirks or sensory distortions.

# Arc-itects

![Arc-itects — infographic](img/IS100.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams collaboratively construct a compelling narrative arc for a central character, brick by emotional brick, using audience-suggested momentum points.

## Overview
Two improv teams take turns, scene by scene, building upon a narrative for a central character. They integrate audience-suggested 'Momentum Points' while maintaining continuity, culminating in the character achieving their goal. A referee oversees the game, awarding points for narrative integration, continuity, and humor.

## Setup
Two teams (Red and Blue) stand on opposite sides of the stage. One improviser from each team will typically be the 'Lead Arc-itect' for their respective scene segments, with other teammates playing supporting roles. A Referee stands centrally to oversee the game, collect audience suggestions, and announce 'Momentum Points'.

## How to Play
1. The Referee asks the audience for a Central Character (e.g., 'A grumpy librarian').
2. The Referee asks for an Ultimate Goal/Outcome for that character (e.g., 'Learns to fly').
3. The Referee solicits 3-5 'Momentum Points' - abstract emotional states, significant challenges, or major tonal shifts the character experiences on their journey (e.g., 'A moment of profound self-doubt').
4. The Referee writes these down clearly visible to the teams and audience.
5. Team Red begins. The Referee announces the first Momentum Point.
6. Team Red's Lead Arc-itect and supporting teammates start a 60-90 second scene, establishing the character and integrating the Momentum Point into the action and dialogue.
7. The Referee calls 'TIME!' to end Team Red's scene.
8. Team Blue steps forward. The Referee announces the second Momentum Point.
9. Team Blue's Lead Arc-itect continues the narrative arc from where Team Red left off in a 60-90 second scene, integrating their Momentum Point.
10. This alternation continues for all Momentum Points, with teams building upon the preceding scene's developments to ensure narrative continuity.
11. After the final Momentum Point scene, the Referee announces 'Final Scene: The Ultimate Goal!'
12. The team performing the last Momentum Point scene performs a final 60-90 second scene demonstrating the character achieving their Ultimate Goal, drawing upon the journey and lessons learned.

## Coaching Notes
- Lead Arc-itects must make strong character choices and react to the scene's progression.
- Supporting Arc-itects should jump in to play secondary characters, objects, or environmental elements, actively listening and 'Yes, And'-ing their Lead.
- Teams can strategically rotate their Lead Arc-itect for each segment.
- The Referee can call an 'Arc Disconnect' foul if a team completely disregards the narrative continuity established by the previous scene, or fails to meaningfully integrate their assigned Momentum Point. This results in a point deduction.
- The Referee can call a groaner penalty for excessively bad puns or jokes that halt momentum, resulting in a point deduction.
- Focus on active listening to accurately track and respond to the narrative established by the opposing team.
- Use object work to create believable environments and props without relying on physical items.

## Why It Works
It develops strong 'Yes, And' skills, active listening, and narrative building by forcing players to construct a coherent and engaging story from disparate prompts while respecting the established reality of the opposing team.

## Safety & Inclusion
Maintain a family-friendly tone. A content foul is immediately called for any blue humor, swearing, or innuendo, resulting in a point deduction and potentially a re-do of the moment.

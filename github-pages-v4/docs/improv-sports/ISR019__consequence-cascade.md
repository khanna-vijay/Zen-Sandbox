# Consequence Cascade

![Consequence Cascade — infographic](img/ISR019.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams battle in a rapid-fire relay of object work, physically morphing mimed items to launch entirely new realities.

## Overview
Consequence Cascade is a high-energy, competitive short-form game where two teams battle in a rapid-fire relay of object work and pantomime. A scene revolves around a mimed object, and on the Referee's whistle, the scene freezes. The opposing team steps in, takes the exact physical shape of the frozen object, and physically morphs it into something entirely new to launch a completely different reality.

## Setup
Format: Competitive Short-Form. Personnel: 2 teams (2-3 players each) and 1 Referee with a whistle. Props: None. The Referee gets an audience suggestion for a simple, handheld object (e.g., a plunger, a whisk, a bowling ball) to start the game.

## How to Play
1. 1. Initiation: Team A starts a scene using the audience-suggested object. They must establish clear physical dimensions, weight, and use for the item.
2. 2. The Freeze: After 20 to 30 seconds, or immediately following a strong comedic beat, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Cascade!' Team A freezes instantly.
3. 3. The Steal: A player from Team B steps up to the frozen Team A player holding the object. The Team B player must carefully 'take' the invisible object, maintaining its exact physical dimensions and position in space. Team A then clears the stage.
4. 4. The Physical Morph: Team B may NOT verbally deny the object (e.g., they cannot say 'This isn't a banana peel!'). Instead, they must use purely physical pantomime to manipulate, twist, stretch, or reorient the object until it becomes something entirely new.
5. 5. The Reality Wipe: Once the object is visually transformed, Team B speaks, initiating a brand-new scene. This is a complete reality wipe; the old scene's characters, setting, and universe are gone. Only the newly morphed object exists to anchor the new universe.
6. 6. Alternating Play: The Referee blows the whistle again after 20 to 30 seconds, and Team A steals the new object to morph it. This rapid back-and-forth continues for a set time limit (usually 3 to 4 minutes).
7. 7. Scoring: At the end of the round, the Referee calls time and asks the audience to applaud for Team A's scenes, and then Team B's scenes. The team with the loudest applause wins 5 points.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee can call a 'Denial Foul' (minus 1 point) if a player uses words to change the object instead of physical pantomime.
- The Referee can call a 'Ghost Foul' if a player drops or loses the physical dimensions of the object during the transfer.
- Enforce complete reality wipes between scenes to guarantee maximum variety and high novelty.
- Keep the pace fast and alternating to keep energy peaking.

## Variations
- Silent Cascade: The entire game is played in gibberish or complete silence, forcing 100 percent reliance on physical storytelling and pantomime.
- Genre Cascade: The Referee calls out a new movie or theatre genre with every 'Cascade!' whistle, forcing the new reality to fit the style (e.g., Western, Sci-Fi, Soap Opera).

## Why It Works
It is a thrilling test of object permanence, physical transformation, and rapid scene initiation. By banning lazy verbal denial, it forces players to rely entirely on their pantomime skills to anchor a new universe.

## Safety & Inclusion
Physical safety: Ensure the stage is clear of tripping hazards since players will be rushing on and off stage to tag in. Consent and Boundaries: When 'stealing' the object from the frozen player, players must take the object from the negative space, avoiding inappropriate physical contact with the frozen player's body. Content: Standard family-friendly rules apply; the Referee will blow the whistle for any blue or inappropriate content (Standard clean-content foul).

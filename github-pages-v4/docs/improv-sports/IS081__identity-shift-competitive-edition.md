# Identity Shift: Competitive Edition

![Identity Shift: Competitive Edition — infographic](img/IS081.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Teams instantly tag out and inherit all character endowments, physical positions, and narrative constraints on the referee's command.

## Overview
Identity Shift is a fast-paced improv game where two teams take turns advancing a scene with audience-suggested characters, location, and relationship. The core mechanic, 'SHIFT!', demands that players instantly tag out, transferring all their character endowments, emotional states, physical positions, and the ongoing narrative to the incoming opposing team. A secondary 'TRAIT!' mechanic adds individual character quirks that also persist.

## Setup
Two players from Team Red start on stage. Two players from Team Blue wait offstage, attentive and ready to jump in. The stage should be relatively clear, perhaps with a few generic objects for object work. Get audience suggestions for a Location, two distinct Character Roles/Occupations, and a Relationship between them.

## How to Play
1. Team Red takes the stage and begins a scene based on the initial endowments, establishing location, characters, relationship, physical positions, and emotional states.
2. At any point, the Referee yells 'SHIFT!'. The two active players must immediately tag the two waiting players from the opposing team.
3. All current character endowments, emotional states, physical positions, the established relationship, and the ongoing narrative instantly transfer to the new players, who continue the scene without a break.
4. The tagged-out players return to their team's side to observe keenly.
5. During any team's turn, the Referee can yell 'TRAIT!' and point to one active player, providing a brand new, specific character endowment or constraint.
6. That player must instantly integrate this new trait, which also transfers to the next player embodying that character during a 'SHIFT!'.
7. The game continues with alternating 'SHIFT!' calls and new 'TRAIT!' calls until the Referee calls 'Game!' after a predetermined number of shifts or set time.

## Coaching Notes
- Call a 'Dropped Endowment' Foul (-1 point) if a player fails to maintain an established character endowment, relationship, emotional state, or storyline element passed to them.
- Call a 'Frozen Scene' Foul (-1 point) if players hesitate for more than 3 seconds to react to a 'SHIFT!' or 'TRAIT!' call.
- Call a 'Groaner Foul' (-1 point) for excessively bad puns.
- Award points for seamless shifts (+2), creative trait integration (+1), exceptional 'Yes, And' scene building (+1), and audience laughter (+1).
- Players must instantly and fully embody a complex character, including prior actions, emotional states, and new traits.
- Encourage strong object work to enhance the scene and ground the characters in the physical reality of the changing environment.

## Variations
- Audience Traits: The referee may open up 'TRAIT!' suggestions to the audience for variety, rather than providing them themselves.

## Why It Works
It rigorously tests 'Yes, And' skills, active listening, and justification. Players are challenged to invent immediate, humorous justifications for rapidly changing endowments within the scene's context, forcing them to stay completely present and hyper-focused on their scene partners.

## Safety & Inclusion
Standard competitive short-form rules apply: use a clean-content foul to penalize blue humor, swearing, or innuendo. The focus should remain on quick thinking, character work, and collaborative storytelling in a family-friendly environment.

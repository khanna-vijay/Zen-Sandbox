# Piston Patter

![Piston Patter — infographic](img/ISR029.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams build a grounded scene in rapid-fire, 7-second relay bursts.

## Overview
Piston Patter is a high-energy, competitive short-form game where two teams build a grounded scene in rapid-fire, 7-second bursts. Acting as a relay, each team controls a single character in a two-person scene. Players must step up, make a meaningful character contribution, and hit a buzzer to pass the turn before their 7 seconds expire, then rotate to the back of their line.

## Setup
Two teams of 2 to 4 players stand in single-file lines on opposite sides of the stage. Place a sturdy stool or small table downstage center with two large tap-bells, game-show buzzers, or a physical chess clock. A Referee stands downstage to monitor the time, award points, and call fouls. Both teams start with 5 points. The audience provides a suggestion for a location and a relationship between two characters.

## How to Play
1. 1. The Referee gets a suggestion for a location and a relationship (e.g., a dentist and a nervous patient).
2. 2. Team A is assigned Character 1, and Team B is assigned Character 2. The first player in each team's line steps up to the center table.
3. 3. The Referee blows the whistle and starts the clock. Team A's active player has exactly 7 seconds to deliver a line or action in character, hit their buzzer/clock, and immediately rotate to the back of their team's line.
4. 4. Hitting the buzzer instantly starts Team B's 7-second timer. Team B's active player must immediately accept the offer (Yes-And), respond in character, hit their buzzer, and rotate.
5. 5. The next player in line must immediately step into the 'action zone' at the table, adopting the exact physical posture and emotional state their teammate just left.
6. 6. Players must stay grounded in the scene. There is no narration; they are acting out a real-time scene, simply alternating which player is speaking for the character.
7. 7. If a player fails to hit the buzzer within 7 seconds, denies an offer, or stalls without adding new information, the Referee blows the whistle and calls a foul.
8. 8. The Referee deducts 1 point for every foul: 'Piston Jam' for running out of time, 'Idle Gear' for stalling/waffling, and 'Misfire' for denying an offer.
9. 9. After a foul is called and a point is deducted, the Referee resets the clock, gives a brief prompt to restart the momentum (e.g., 'Team A, you just found a cavity, go!'), and play continues until the 3-minute round is over. The team with the most points wins.

## Coaching Notes
- Encourage the audience to vocalize the countdown ('3, 2, 1, BUZZ!') if a player is taking too long, adding to the pressure.
- Remind players to hyper-focus on their teammates' physical and emotional choices to maintain consistency when stepping into the action zone.
- Use the physical buzzer/clock mechanic to preserve scene reality while enforcing strict pacing.
- Enforce the specific, themed fouls actively to ensure Yes-And and scene progression.

## Variations
- Shrinking Pistons: To build natural escalation, the Referee sets the time limit to 10 seconds per turn for the first minute, 7 seconds for the second minute, and a frantic 3 seconds for the final minute.
- Four-Cylinder Engine: For advanced groups, each team controls TWO distinct characters (e.g., Team A is a husband and the family dog; Team B is the wife and the nosy neighbor). The active player must instantly adopt the physicality of whichever character is currently being addressed.

## Why It Works
The strict 7-second time limit and relay-style rotation demand active listening, physical consistency, and relentless forward momentum, forcing players to make immediate, grounded choices without overthinking.

## Safety & Inclusion
Players must be explicitly instructed to 'walk, don't run' when rotating to avoid collisions, and to hit the buzzers gently to avoid injuring hands. For accessibility, players with limited upper-body mobility or visual impairments can shout 'Pass!' instead of physically striking a buzzer, with the Referee managing the timer. The Referee strictly enforces a clean-content foul to keep all content family-friendly and safe.

# Morphing Momentum Relay

![Morphing Momentum Relay — infographic](img/IS145.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players non-verbally pass an imaginary object, changing exactly one of its physical characteristics with each pass.

## Overview
Morphing Momentum Relay is a theater game adapted from relay races, where players non-verbally pass an imaginary object around a group. The focus is entirely on the object's constantly evolving physical characteristics. The goal is not speed or competition, but to cultivate collective awareness, embodied listening, and a shared, fluid imaginative reality through responsive physical agreement.

## Setup
Players form a large circle or a loose, irregular formation, standing equidistant from one another. The facilitator explains that the goal is to pass an invisible object around the group, focusing on its physical properties.

## How to Play
1. One player begins by 'serving' the imaginary object, clearly defining its initial physical characteristics (e.g., size, weight, texture, temperature, sound) through their physical embodiment and action.
2. The server 'passes' this defined object to another player.
3. The receiving player's body must react authentically to the received properties of the object.
4. Before passing it on, the receiving player must change exactly one of its physical characteristics (e.g., making it bigger/smaller, heavier/lighter, hotter/colder, rougher/smoother, or introducing a new quality like a vibration).
5. The player then passes the newly transformed object to another player in the group.
6. Continue passing the object in a fluid, non-linear way (any player can pass to any other player). All communication must be non-verbal and entirely physical.
7. If a player misinterprets an offer, they must respond to what they perceived and make their own clear offer based on that; there is no 'dropping' or 'missing' the object.
8. End the game when a strong ensemble connection is reached, after a set time, or by having one player physically dissolve or integrate the object back into the space.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Focus on the constantly transforming physical reality of the invisible object as offered by the previous player, and your subsequent physical act of receiving, responding to, and imparting its next singular, unambiguous change.
- Encourage players to actively 'see' and 'feel' the imaginary object based solely on the previous player's physical offers.
- Players should respond spontaneously and truthfully from a place of intuition, letting the physical change dictate their reaction.
- Remind players to make clear, distinct, and committed physical offers (the 'singular change') that invite precise reception from the next player.
- Emphasize that 'errors' in interpretation become part of the shared, evolving reality.

## Why It Works
It develops Embodied Listening, Shared Creation, and Responsive Non-Verbal Communication. The game deeply trains players to build an immediate, shared imaginative reality that evolves collectively and relies on deep ensemble trust and agreement.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players have enough physical space to safely embody extreme changes in the object's size or weight. Encourage players to respect their own physical limits when miming heavy or awkward objects.

# The Director's Whisper

![The Director's Whisper — infographic](img/ISR100.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players instantly adopt secret internal motivations shown on cards held behind their partner's back.

## Overview
A fast-paced competitive short-form game where the Referee stands behind one player and holds up a secret internal motivation card for the other player and the audience to see. The targeted player must instantly adopt this new subtext without saying it out loud, while their partner must seamlessly justify the sudden behavioral shift.

## Setup
Requires 2 players from the same team, 1 Referee, and a stack of large, highly legible cards or a whiteboard with internal motivations written on them (e.g., 'Desperately needs to pee,' 'Is secretly a vampire,' 'Thinks the other person is a robot'). The stage should be set for a relatively stationary scene, such as two chairs or a standing counter, to help the Referee manage sightlines.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a location and relationship suggestion from the audience.
2. The players begin the scene. The Referee positions themselves directly behind Player A, facing Player B and the audience.
3. The Referee holds up a motivation card. The audience and Player B can clearly see it, but Player A cannot.
4. Player B must immediately adopt the motivation on the card as their internal subtext, altering their physicality, tone, and choices without explicitly stating what the card says.
5. Player A, who cannot see the card, must react naturally and justify Player B's sudden bizarre behavior within the reality of the scene.
6. After 30 to 45 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle, moves behind Player B, and holds up a new card for Player A and the audience to see, seamlessly swapping the dynamic.
7. The scene runs for a strict 2 to 3 minutes to keep energy high and prevent the gimmick from dragging, ending on a high-energy justification or a final whistle blow.

## Coaching Notes
- The audience provides the initial suggestions and enjoys the dramatic irony of knowing the secret while the non-targeted player struggles.
- The Referee awards points (e.g., +2 points) for brilliant justifications and subtle, accurate subtext play.
- The Referee calls fouls (e.g., -1 point) if a player explicitly reads or states their secret motivation out loud.
- Forces players to make strong physical and emotional choices rather than relying on dialogue.
- The Referee manages the cards behind players' backs, ensuring smooth execution without relying on audience members.

## Variations
- Head-to-Head: One player from Team A and one from Team B. The Referee only gives cards to Team A's player, testing Team B's ability to justify. Then swap roles for round two.
- Audience Whiteboard: Instead of pre-written cards, the Referee takes a whiteboard into the audience and has an audience member quickly scribble the next motivation during the scene.

## Why It Works
The game creates dramatic irony, maximizing laughs as the audience is in on the joke while the non-targeted player struggles to figure out what is happening. It also provides excellent subtext training, forcing players to make strong physical and emotional choices rather than relying on dialogue.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all pre-written cards are clean, all-ages appropriate, and avoid phobias or sensitive trauma triggers. The Referee must be mindful of player mobility and physical boundaries when moving closely behind them. Players should maintain a relatively fixed stage picture to avoid accidental collisions with the Referee.

# Impulse Circuit

![Impulse Circuit — infographic](img/ISR143.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass a sound and motion around a circle, instantly transforming it into a completely new emotion.

## Overview
Impulse Circuit is a fast-paced ensemble warm-up where players pass a sound and motion around a circle. Instead of just repeating what they received, players must let the energy affect them and instantly transform it into a completely new emotion, sound, and movement before passing it on.

## Setup
Players form a circle facing inward. No props are needed. The facilitator explains that energy will be passed using strong eye contact, sound, and full-body motion, without requiring physical touch.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator starts by turning to the player on their left, making strong eye contact, and delivering a clear sound and motion with a specific emotional weight (for example, a heavy, tired sigh while slumping the shoulders).
2. The receiving player must genuinely absorb this energy and react to it physically, rather than ignoring it or blocking it.
3. Without pausing to think, the receiver immediately transforms that energy into a completely new sound, motion, and emotion. For example, the tired sigh might transform into a sudden, sharp gasp with a rigid, fearful posture.
4. The player then turns to the person on their left, makes strong eye contact, and passes this new, transformed impulse to them.
5. The cycle continues rapidly around the circle. The goal is a continuous, evolving circuit of energy with no pauses for planning.

## Coaching Notes
- If a player freezes, overthinks, or just copies the previous motion, the facilitator gently coaches them with concrete prompts like 'React to what they gave you!' or 'Change the emotion!' to keep the flow moving.
- Emphasize the 'receive and be changed' aspect of improv.
- Push players out of their heads and into their bodies.

## Variations
- Cross-Circle Bounce: Instead of passing to the left, players make eye contact and pass the impulse to anyone across the circle, requiring everyone to stay highly focused.
- The Escalator: Each transformation must be bigger and louder than the last until the group reaches maximum energy, then the next player resets it to a tiny whisper.

## Why It Works
It trains the core improv skill of letting yourself be changed by your partner. It develops active listening, emotional agility, and spontaneous physical reaction.

## Safety & Inclusion
Mandatory physical touch has been completely removed; energy is passed via eye contact, sound, and proximity. If a facilitator wishes to include physical touch (like a shoulder tap), they must establish explicit boundaries and get verbal opt-in consent from all players beforehand. The game is fully accessible to seated players or those with limited mobility, as impulses can be adapted to any physical capacity.

# The Goal Gambit

![The Goal Gambit — infographic](img/ISR101.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two players pursue secret, actionable objectives in a shared scene, trying to be subtle enough to maintain reality but clear enough for the audience to guess.

## Overview
In this competitive short-form game, two players are each assigned one secret, actionable objective to pursue organically within a shared scene. The challenge is to achieve the goal subtly enough to maintain the scene's reality, but clearly enough that the audience can guess the secret objective at the end. It is a hilarious test of justifying unusual behavior and playing strong, clear actions.

## Setup
2 players (one from Team A, one from Team B). A Referee. A deck of pre-written Objective Cards (or objectives gathered from the audience before the show). A clear stage with minimal props.

## How to Play
1. The Referee brings two players to the stage (one from Team A, one from Team B) and asks the audience for a location and a mundane relationship (e.g., 'Co-workers at a hardware store').
2. The Referee secretly shows each player exactly ONE Objective Card. Players keep their objectives hidden from each other and the audience.
3. The players begin a 2-3 minute scene. They must actively pursue their secret objective while maintaining character and justifying their actions within the scene's reality.
4. Players must integrate their goals organically—they cannot explicitly state what they are trying to do or break the scene's logic to force the objective.
5. The Referee calls 'Scene!' after 2-3 minutes, or when both players have made strong, clear attempts at their goals.
6. Instead of having players explain their actions, the Referee immediately turns to the audience and asks them to shout out their guesses for Player A's secret objective.
7. Player A reveals their card to see if the audience was right. The Referee awards a single overall score of 5 points to a player's team if the audience correctly guesses their objective. The process is then repeated for Player B.

## Coaching Notes
- Ensure objectives are actionable and concrete (e.g., 'Get the other player to compliment your intelligence,' 'Subtly recruit them into a cult,' 'Get them to hand you an object').
- Focus on organic integration: players must justify their weird behavior within the scene rather than forcing it.
- Keep the audience guessing fast-paced to replace tedious post-scene explanations and keep the energy high.
- Strictly limit the game to 2 players with 1 objective each to prevent narrative chaos and keep the scene focused.
- Reward players who play their goals clearly and organically rather than confusing the audience or being too blunt.

## Variations
- Workshop Mode: Strip away the points and teams entirely. Use this as an ensemble exercise where the facilitator stops the scene, and the class guesses the objectives to practice playing clear, organic actions.
- Emotion Gambit: Instead of action-based objectives, players are given secret emotional transitions they must organically reach by the end of the scene (e.g., 'Go from ecstatic to deeply suspicious').

## Why It Works
It tests a player's ability to justify unusual behavior and play strong, clear actions without breaking the reality of the scene, rewarding organic integration over bluntness.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all Objective Cards are safe, all-ages appropriate, and respect physical boundaries. Avoid objectives that require non-consensual physical contact (e.g., no 'kiss the other player' or 'tackle them'). Focus on verbal admissions, emotional shifts, or safe physical actions (e.g., 'get them to sit on the floor').

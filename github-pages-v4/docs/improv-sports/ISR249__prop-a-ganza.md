# Prop-a-Ganza!

![Prop-a-Ganza! — infographic](img/ISR249.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A rapid-fire Freeze Tag game where players must instantly initiate new scenes by endowing random, mundane props.

## Overview
A high-energy, competitive short-form game where players play a rapid-fire series of unrelated scenes similar to Freeze Tag. Every time a new player enters, they must grab a random, mundane prop from a table, tag out an existing player, and instantly initiate a brand new scene by endowing their new prop. It tests lightning-fast object work, physical justification, and quick thinking.

## Setup
Set up a 'Prop Table' upstage center with 10-15 diverse, mundane, and safe props (e.g., feather duster, colander, rubber chicken, plunger, pool noodle). Two teams (Red and Blue) stand ready on the wings. A Referee stands downstage to run the game, call fouls, and manage time.

## How to Play
1. The Referee gets a single suggestion (e.g., a location or relationship) from the audience to inspire the very first scene.
2. The Referee blows the whistle and yells 'Go!' One player from the Red Team and one from the Blue Team rush the Prop Table, grab one prop each, and immediately step downstage to begin a 15-20 second scene.
3. Players must instantly 'endow' their props, using them as something other than what they actually are (e.g., a plunger becomes a metal detector).
4. After 15-20 seconds, the Referee blows the whistle. Both active players freeze instantly in their exact physical positions.
5. A new player from the sidelines grabs a new prop from the table, runs in, and tags one of the frozen players on the shoulder.
6. The tagged-out player exits the stage and takes their prop with them. This ensures there are always exactly two players and two props on stage, preventing overcrowding.
7. The entering player assumes the exact physical position of the departing player.
8. The entering player speaks first, initiating a brand new, completely unrelated scene that justifies their new prop and their physical position. The remaining player must instantly drop the old scene's context and justify their existing prop and body position in this new reality.
9. Play continues in this rapid-fire Freeze Tag format for 2 to 3 minutes, with players alternating teams as they tag in.
10. At the end of the 3-minute game, the Referee conducts a 'Cheer-Off' (audience applause) to award a flat 5 points to the winning team.

## Coaching Notes
- The Referee should blow the whistle to deduct 1 point for a 'Delay of Game' (taking more than 3 seconds to grab a prop and start).
- Deduct 1 point for a 'Groaner' (lazy puns).
- Deduct 1 point for a clean-content call (inappropriate/crude use of a prop, keeping the game all-ages).
- Encourage rapid-fire object work and high-energy, fast-paced transitions.
- Remind players to fully justify existing physical positions in completely new contexts.

## Variations
- Prop Swap: Instead of grabbing a new prop from the table, the entering player takes the departing player's prop, but must endow it as something completely different in the new scene.
- Blind Bag: Props are hidden inside a large duffel bag. Entering players must reach in and pull out a prop blindly on their way into the scene, forcing entirely spontaneous endowments.

## Why It Works
It forces players to practice lightning-fast object work, physical justification, and quick thinking by combining Freeze Tag mechanics with physical props.

## Safety & Inclusion
Prop Safety: Ensure all props on the table are soft, lightweight, and free of sharp edges (no real glass, heavy metal, or actual weapons). Physical Boundaries: When tagging a frozen player, players should use a gentle tap on the shoulder. When assuming a frozen player's position, players must respect personal space and avoid forcing the remaining player into non-consensual physical contact. Content: The strict clean-content call ensures all prop endowments remain family-friendly and safe for all ages.

# The Whispering Prop

![The Whispering Prop — infographic](img/ISR241.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A fast-paced competitive guessing game where a mundane prop is endowed with a highly specific, family-friendly scandalous backstory by the audience.

## Overview
One player leaves the room while the audience gives a mundane prop a bizarre, G-rated secret backstory. When the player returns, their teammates play a scene using the prop, dropping physical and verbal hints until the guesser can deduce the object's true identity. The audience enjoys the dramatic irony of watching the guesser piece together the clues.

## Setup
Played in a Competitive Short-Form (refereed) format. Two teams compete in alternating rounds. Each team selects 3 players: 1 Guesser and 2 Hint-Givers. You need a Referee to facilitate, a soundproof headset (or a designated spot outside the room) for the Guesser, and a table with 4-5 mundane, everyday props (e.g., a rubber chicken, a watering can, a feather duster, a garden gnome).

## How to Play
1. The Guesser from the active team is sent out of the room or put in soundproof headphones.
2. The Referee selects one mundane prop from the table and asks the audience for a G-rated, slightly scandalous or absurd secret backstory for it.
3. The Referee confirms the exact secret with the audience and the Hint-Givers, ensuring everyone in the room knows the dramatic irony.
4. The Guesser returns to the stage. The Referee calls 'Scene!' and starts a 3-minute timer.
5. The Hint-Givers initiate a scene where the prop is central to the action. They must drop verbal and physical clues about the prop's secret identity without explicitly stating it.
6. The Guesser must actively participate in the scene, interacting with the prop and their teammates, trying to justify the bizarre behavior while piecing together the clues.
7. To guess, the Guesser must weave the answer naturally into the scene's dialogue (e.g., 'No wonder you're treating this watering can with such respect, it's a retired mob boss!').
8. If the guess is correct, the Referee blows the whistle and awards points. If incorrect, the scene continues until the timer runs out.

## Coaching Notes
- The team earns 5 points for correctly guessing the secret within the 3-minute time limit.
- The Referee can award up to 5 additional points for exceptional object work, scene cohesion, and creative clue-giving.
- Focus on object work to force players to physically interact with and elevate a mundane prop.
- Maintain clean comedy constraints to breed creativity through limitation.
- Ensure the Guesser actively participates in the scene rather than just standing and guessing.

## Variations
- Interrogation Room: Instead of an open scene, the Guesser plays a detective interrogating the Hint-Givers about a 'crime' committed by the prop. The Hint-Givers act as the prop's accomplices or victims.
- Prop Line-Up (Advanced): The Guesser must figure out the secrets of THREE different props in 4 minutes, moving rapidly from one object to the next.

## Why It Works
The audience knows the secret, making the Guesser's confusion and discovery highly entertaining through dramatic irony. The guessing game engine provides a clear, driving objective for the scene, while the object work focus forces players to physically interact with and elevate a mundane prop.

## Safety & Inclusion
The Referee strictly filters audience suggestions to ensure the 'scandalous' backstory remains entirely family-friendly. Standard clean-content fouls apply: if any player uses blue humor or inappropriate innuendo to hint at the secret, the Referee blows the whistle, stops the scene, deducts 5 points, and the team forfeits the round. Physical safety must be maintained; props should not be thrown or used to strike other players.

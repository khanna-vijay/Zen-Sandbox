# Scene Sculptors

![Scene Sculptors — infographic](img/IS218.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Collaboratively build a scene that constantly shifts its narrative focus, perspective, and underlying meaning based on live, audience-called 'sculpting tools.'

## Overview
Scene Sculptors is an innovative improv game where 3-4 players collaboratively build a scene that continuously shifts its narrative focus, perspective, and meaning based on live, audience-called 'sculpting tools.' These tools allow the audience to instantly manipulate elements like character's internal thoughts, underlying subtext, character relationships, and time. The game challenges improvisers to quickly adapt their characters, dialogue, and story with seamless integration and commitment, while an essential host facilitates audience interaction, manages the pace of calls, and awards points for clever and effective responses.

## Setup
Requires 3-4 improvisers and an essential Host/MC to explain rules, facilitate audience interaction, manage the pace of calls, and award points or call fouls. No props or stage sets are strictly necessary; a clear, open performance space is ideal, though chairs can be used. The audience is integral and will be given specific 'sculpting tools' to call out, which the host explains clearly before the game begins.

## How to Play
1. Scene Suggestion: The Host/MC begins by soliciting a suggestion from the audience for a simple scene premise or setting.
2. Scene Initiation: The 3-4 players immediately begin the scene based on the audience suggestion, establishing characters, a basic relationship, and an initial conflict or situation.
3. The Sculpting Calls: At any point, the audience (or Host/MC) can call out a pre-defined 'sculpting tool'. Players must immediately and seamlessly integrate the requested shift into the scene. The call applies to the character(s) currently speaking or the central focus of the immediate interaction.
4. 'Inner Monologue!': The speaking character pauses external dialogue to voice their immediate, internal thought out loud for 1-2 short sentences, then instantly resumes external dialogue as if nothing happened.
5. 'Subtext Amplify!': For the next 2-3 lines of dialogue, all characters involved in the immediate exchange must speak the underlying meaning or unspoken truth behind what they would ordinarily say, then revert to normal dialogue.
6. 'Relationship Flip!': The audience or Host points to two specific characters. Their established relationship instantly reverses or dramatically shifts to its opposite. Players must immediately adapt their status, physical behavior, and dialogue.
7. 'Time Warp!': The audience or Host calls 'Time Warp!' and specifies 'Flashback' or 'Fast Forward', optionally adding a brief detail. Players instantly transport the scene to that new time for a brief beat (15-30 seconds) before returning to the original timeline.
8. Flow and Pacing: The scene continues with players reacting to the calls. The Host/MC manages the rhythm, ensuring calls are frequent enough to keep energy high but not so rapid as to overwhelm the players.
9. Ending the Scene: The scene concludes when the Host/MC calls 'Scene!', typically after several 'sculpting' challenges have been navigated or a natural narrative beat is reached.

## Coaching Notes
- Host/MC should encourage clear, decisive calls from the audience.
- Award 3 points for an exceptionally seamless, witty, surprising, or emotionally resonant integration of a 'sculpting' call.
- Award 2 points for a strong, clear, and funny integration of a call, demonstrating excellent listening and commitment.
- Award 1 point for a competent and effective integration of a call, maintaining the scene's flow.
- Call a 'Delay of Game' foul for hesitating too long or appearing confused before responding to a call.
- Call an 'Ignoring the Sculpt' foul for not fully committing to or improperly executing the requested shift.
- Call a 'Breaking the Fourth Wall' foul for acknowledging the game's mechanics within the scene itself (e.g., 'Oh, looks like we have to do an inner monologue now!').
- Call a 'Lack of Collaboration' foul for not supporting other players' shifts or dominating the scene.
- Call a 'Boring' foul (a classic competitive improv foul) if the scene isn't moving forward, is low energy, or becomes stale between calls.

## Why It Works
It challenges players' quick thinking, active listening, character commitment, and ability to navigate rapid shifts while maintaining a coherent, albeit dynamically evolving, narrative. It encourages improvisers to delve deeper into character and narrative possibilities.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical safety during rapid transitions or 'Relationship Flips' (e.g., shifting power dynamics). Players should maintain boundaries and respect consent, especially when exploring amplified subtext or intense emotional shifts.

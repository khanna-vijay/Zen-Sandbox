# The Chronos Relic

![The Chronos Relic — infographic](img/IS070.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Construct a multi-generational narrative centered on a single, evolving object to show how events and emotions ripple through time.

## Overview
The Chronos Relic is a collaborative improv game where teams construct a multi-generational narrative centered on a single, evolving object (the 'Chronos Relic'), suggested by the audience along with an initial emotional tone. Players begin in a past era, establishing the relic's origin, then endure abrupt 'Chronos Shifts' that instantly transport them to significantly future eras. In each new era, performers must embody new characters and settings, with the relic (or its legacy) present, demonstrating how past events and emotions echo and transform across vast stretches of time, ultimately building to a cohesive and satisfying thematic arc.

## Setup
The MC asks the audience for two suggestions: a mundane, tangible object (e.g., a rusty key, a tarnished locket, a chipped ceramic mug) to serve as the 'Chronos Relic', and a single, strong emotional tone associated with the initial significant use of that object (e.g., desperation, fierce ambition, profound regret). Two teams of 2-3 players participate, with one team performing at a time.

## How to Play
1. The scene begins in an unspecified past era (performers define it implicitly or explicitly).
2. Players establish characters, setting, and their initial interaction with the Chronos Relic, clearly embodying the suggested emotional tone related to its use. This establishes the relic's origin story and initial legacy. The scene plays out for approximately 90-120 seconds.
3. At a signal from the referee (e.g., a buzzer, a light flash, or the MC shouting 'Chronos Shift!'), the scene instantly and abruptly jumps forward in time. The jump should be significant and dramatic (e.g., 50 years, 100 years, several centuries, or even millennia into the future).
4. Upon the signal, players must immediately embody new characters (or vastly aged/descendant versions of previous ones) and a new, distinct setting appropriate to the new time period.
5. The Chronos Relic (or a clear symbolic representation, or its story/legend) must be present or significantly referenced in the new scene to connect the eras.
6. The new scene must organically build upon the themes, consequences, or emotional legacy of the previous scene, showing how past events, feelings, or choices echo and manifest in the future.
7. Each subsequent era plays for approximately 90-120 seconds before another 'Chronos Shift' occurs.
8. The game typically involves 3-4 time jumps, creating a total of 4-5 distinct eras. The final scene should aim to provide a sense of resolution or significant thematic culmination for the Relic's journey and the inherited emotional legacy.

## Coaching Notes
- Narrative Development: Focus on creating a broad, multi-layered story rather than disconnected moments. Consider the long-term impact of earlier decisions and how a narrative can evolve across generations, ensuring depth over simple gags.
- Embracing Mistakes & Risks: The abrupt time jump is the ultimate risk. If a previous scene was ambiguous or went 'wrong,' 'Yes, And' that ambiguity, allowing future characters to grapple with an unknown past, transforming potential 'failure' into rich narrative fodder.
- Collaborative Scene-Building: Rapidly establish new characters, relationships, and settings, and collectively agree on how the Chronos Relic and its inherited legacy manifest in the new era. This requires extremely active listening, selfless offering, and immediate acceptance.
- Contextual Awareness & Reincorporation: Recall the essence of previous scenes and weave those elements organically into the present narrative.
- Character Embodiment & Status Play: Rapidly create distinct, believable characters with clear objectives and relationships in an instant. Status shifts naturally occur as new characters and eras are introduced.
- Referee Role: The referee controls the timing of the 'Chronos Shift' signals and ensures basic adherence to rules (e.g., the Relic is present, new characters are formed).

## Why It Works
It forces performers to create a broad, multi-layered story rather than disconnected moments, pushing them to consider the long-term impact of earlier decisions. It challenges improvisers to be fundamentally changed by each time jump, rapidly adopting entirely new personas and circumstances while maintaining contextual awareness and reincorporating the essence of previous scenes.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure time jumps and historical eras are portrayed respectfully, avoiding harmful stereotypes or offensive caricatures of different cultures or time periods. Maintain physical safety during abrupt transitions.

# The Intuition Weave

![The Intuition Weave — infographic](img/IS120.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass an evolving non-verbal impulse around a circle by transforming its underlying energy rather than imitating its form.

## Overview
The Intuition Weave is a sequential, non-verbal theater game designed to cultivate intuitive responsiveness, deep non-verbal listening, and ensemble cohesion. Players pass an evolving impulse around a circle, responding to the underlying energy of the previous action with their own distinct transformation. This continuous flow encourages participants to bypass intellect for spontaneous, honest reactions, collectively creating an evolving 'weave' of shared energetic states.

## Setup
Participants stand in a loose circle or scattered throughout an open space. The facilitator explains the game and its Point of Concentration.

## How to Play
1. One player begins by offering a single, clear, spontaneous non-verbal impulse, such as a gesture, movement, vocal sound, or combination (e.g., a sudden twist, a sharp inhale, a soft hum). This initial offering should be brief (1-3 seconds) and un-choreographed.
2. The player immediately to the left (or right, as pre-determined) becomes the 'receiver'.
3. The receiver's task is not to imitate the action, but to deeply feel and perceive its underlying energy, quality, or impulse (e.g., quick, slow, tense, relaxed, urgent, playful, heavy, light).
4. Based solely on that perceived energy, the receiver immediately offers their own distinct, intuitively connected non-verbal action that feels like a natural continuation or transformation of the previous energy.
5. This process continues, with each subsequent player receiving the most recent offering and immediately responding with their intuitive addition, passing the evolving energy around the circle.
6. The game concludes when the facilitator senses a natural completion, after a set number of rounds, or when a palpable collective energy has been achieved.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (POC): Receive the underlying energy and impulse (not merely the literal form) of the preceding non-verbal offering, and immediately respond with a new, distinct, and intuitively connected physical or vocal transformation.
- Encourage players to bypass intellect and react without conscious thought or planning. The speed of the weave should actively prevent overthinking.
- If a player freezes or 'doesn't know what to do,' prompt them to make any immediate, honest sound or movement, no matter how small, to keep the flow going.
- Remind players there is no 'right' or 'wrong' response; only honest, spontaneous reaction to the perceived impulse.
- Example transformation: If player 1 offers a sudden, sharp, percussive hand clap, player 2 might respond with a quick, high-pitched vocal pop and a dart of the eyes, echoing the sharpness without physically clapping. If player 1 offers a slow, heavy drag of the foot and a sigh, player 2 might slump their shoulders and slowly drop their head.

## Why It Works
It trains participants to bypass intellectual analysis and react instantaneously and authentically to the felt qualities and underlying impulses communicated by others. It sharpens the ability to 'read' nuanced non-verbal communication and to connect on a deeply embodied, empathetic level, fostering genuine give-and-take.

## Safety & Inclusion
Maintain a play-based, safe environment where there are no 'mistakes' to remove the pressure of performance. Ensure players respect physical boundaries and adapt movements to their own physical abilities so that everyone can safely participate.

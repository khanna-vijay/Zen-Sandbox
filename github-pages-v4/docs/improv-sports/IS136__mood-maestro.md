# Mood Maestro

![Mood Maestro — infographic](img/IS136.jpg){ .game-hero }

> An Audience-Driven Emotional Call-and-Response Game

## Overview
Mood Maestro is a theater game that reinvents the call-and-response format by empowering the audience to dictate the emotional tone. Players deliver neutral calls, an 'Audience-Maestro' shouts an emotion, and the next player responds by fully embodying that emotion. This process fosters instantaneous emotional fluidity and transforms the audience into active co-directors.

## Setup
Players form a semi-circle or line, facing an 'Audience' (which can be a subset of players, a workshop group, or a real audience). The facilitator introduces the game and briefly explains the audience's role in shaping the emotional landscape.

## How to Play
1. The Call: One player (or the facilitator to start) steps forward and delivers a simple, neutral 'call.' This is a short statement or question intentionally devoid of inherent emotional content (e.g., 'I need to go to the store' or 'The sky is blue').
2. The Maestro's Prompt: Immediately after the call, a designated 'Audience-Maestro' (an audience member chosen by the facilitator or volunteering) shouts out a single, clear emotional state or quality (e.g., 'Suspiciously!', 'Joyfully!', 'Exhausted!').
3. The Response: The next player in the line/semi-circle must immediately respond to the original call while fully embodying the audience-dictated emotional state. The verbal content can be anything that logically follows the call, but the voice, body language, and facial expression must be driven purely by the Maestro's Prompt.
4. Rotation: After the response, the original caller delivers a new, neutral call, and a different Audience-Maestro gives a new emotional prompt to the next player in line. The game continues in a dynamic, continuous flow.

## Coaching Notes
- Point of Concentration (Performer): Instantly and intuitively filter your entire response through the audience-dictated emotion. Let the emotion completely possess your expression without intellectualizing or 'acting' it.
- Point of Concentration (Audience-Maestro): Listen acutely to the call and spontaneously select a compelling and contrasting quality that challenges the performer and illuminates the scene.
- Encourage players to bypass self-censorship and trust their first, intuitive impulses in expressing emotions.
- Remind the group that there is no 'right' or 'wrong' way to feel or portray an emotion, fostering a safe, non-judgmental environment.

## Why It Works
It develops instantaneous emotional fluidity and authentic embodiment by forcing players to transition rapidly between diverse emotional states. It also enhances active listening, responsiveness, and elevates the audience from passive observers to active co-directors.

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure emotional prompts remain respectful and avoid trauma-triggering states. Players should modulate physical choices to avoid injury or crossing physical boundaries during high-intensity emotional responses.

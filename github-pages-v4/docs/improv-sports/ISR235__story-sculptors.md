# Story Sculptors

![Story Sculptors — infographic](img/ISR235.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Two teams collaborate and compete to tell a single, cohesive story while simultaneously building a precarious, freestanding sculpture out of random props.

## Overview
As the Referee switches control between two teams, players must seamlessly continue a shared scene, grab a new prop, and physically balance or attach it to a growing central sculpture. Every addition must be justified in the story, and if the sculpture falls, the offending team loses points! This fast-paced competitive short-form game tests narrative teamwork, quick object work, and physical dexterity.

## Setup
Place exactly 7 to 10 random, safe, everyday props in a pile downstage center (e.g., a tennis racket, a bucket, a scarf, a cardboard tube, a rubber chicken). Position the two teams (2 to 3 players each) on opposite sides of the stage. The Referee stands downstage or at a desk with a whistle, ready to call fouls and switch focus.

## How to Play
1. 1. The Prompt: The Referee asks the audience for an event that requires building, assembling, or creating something (e.g., 'A science fair', 'Preparing a Thanksgiving feast', 'Defusing a bomb').
2. 2. The First Turn: The Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Team A, go!' Team A's players enter the scene. They must grab exactly ONE prop from the pile, establish the scene's characters and setting, and place the prop center stage to serve as the base of the sculpture. They must justify the prop in the reality of the scene.
3. 3. The Switch: After 20 to 40 seconds of scene work, the Referee blows the whistle and calls 'Team B!' Team A immediately freezes and returns to their line. Team B steps in and seamlessly continues the exact same scene from the exact moment it left off.
4. 4. The Build: Upon entering, Team B must grab ONE NEW prop from the pile. They must physically balance, hook, or drape this new prop onto the existing sculpture. They cannot hold the sculpture up; it must remain freestanding. They must also verbally justify this new addition in the ongoing story.
5. 5. Continuing Play: The Referee continues to alternate between teams with the whistle. Every time a team takes the stage, they must add one more prop to the central sculpture and justify it narratively.
6. 6. The Collapse (Timber Foul): If the sculpture falls over while a team is adding a prop (or before the Ref switches to the next team), the Referee blows the whistle and calls a 'Timber Foul' (-3 points). The offending team must quickly restack it while staying in character, then continue the scene.
7. 7. The Climax: The game concludes when the final prop is added. The team that places the last prop must bring the scene to a satisfying narrative conclusion.
8. 8. Scoring: The Ref awards up to 5 base points per team for narrative flow, character commitment, and creative prop justification. Fouls include Timber Foul (-3 points), Content Foul (-5 points for inappropriate content), and Delay of Game (-2 points for stalling).
9. 9. Audience Voting: At the end, the Referee points to the completed sculpture and asks the audience to applaud for Team A's contributions, then Team B's. The team with the loudest applause gets a +5 point bonus and wins the game.

## Coaching Notes
- Single Shared Narrative: Ensure players maintain one cohesive, escalating scene rather than confusing parallel stories.
- Dynamic Object Work: Encourage players to make quick, creative choices to justify random objects as part of a growing central structure.
- High Stakes & Slapstick: Lean into the physical tension of the balancing sculpture; a collapse is a fun, theatrical moment rather than a game-breaker.
- Pacing: The Referee should keep turns tight (20 to 40 seconds) to maintain momentum and force quick prop justifications.

## Variations
- Blind Build: The props are kept in a closed box or opaque bag. Players must reach in and pull out a prop blindly when it's their turn, forcing immediate justification.
- The Rival Sculptors (Independent Scenes): Instead of a shared story, teams get 2 minutes each to perform completely independent scenes using the exact same set of 5 props. They must build a sculpture and tell a complete story. The audience votes on who used the props better.

## Why It Works
The physical tension of the balancing sculpture adds natural comedic stakes, while forcing players to make quick, creative choices to justify random objects. It tests narrative teamwork, quick object work, and physical dexterity by combining a shared story with a physical challenge.

## Safety & Inclusion
Prop Selection: Ensure all props are lightweight, soft, or blunt. Avoid heavy items, glass, or anything with sharp edges that could cause injury if the sculpture collapses. Physical Boundaries: Players should be coached not to build the sculpture taller than head-height to prevent items from falling on faces. Accessibility: For players with mobility limitations, the sculpture can be built on a sturdy table rather than the floor, making it easier to reach and balance items without bending down.

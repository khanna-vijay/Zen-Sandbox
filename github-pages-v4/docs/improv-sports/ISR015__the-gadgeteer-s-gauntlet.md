# The Gadgeteer's Gauntlet

![The Gadgeteer's Gauntlet — infographic](img/ISR015.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Players pass a continuously evolving mimed super-gadget around a circle, discovering and demonstrating new fantastical functions.

## Overview
The Gadgeteer's Gauntlet is a collaborative ensemble warm-up where players stand in a circle and pass a single, continuously evolving mimed super-gadget. Each player receives the object, honors its established physical weight and shape, uses its current function in a brief mini-scene, and then discovers a brand-new function before passing it on.

## Setup
Players form a single circle with a clear space in the center. A facilitator leads from the edge of the circle. No props are used; the gadget is entirely mimed.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator asks the group for a simple, everyday object to serve as the base gadget, such as a toaster or a stapler.
2. Player 1 steps into the center, establishes the object's physical dimensions and weight through mime, and uses it for its normal purpose.
3. Player 1 then discovers a hidden, fantastical function (e.g., pressing a button that turns it into a laser pointer), demonstrates this new function in a brief 30-second solo scene, and passes the object to Player 2.
4. Player 2 must physically receive the object, meticulously maintaining its established size, shape, and weight (the blueprint).
5. Player 2 begins their turn by using the gadget's last established function (the laser pointer) to show physical agreement, then discovers a third, entirely new function (e.g., it now translates dog barks), demonstrates it, and passes it to Player 3.
6. The cycle continues around the circle.
7. If a player dramatically changes the object's shape, they must physically justify it (e.g., miming the unfolding of a new antenna or the expansion of a handle).

## Coaching Notes
- Side-coach players to let their mini-scenes breathe, ensuring they show rather than tell the new functions.
- Blueprint Continuity: Players practice physical listening by maintaining the exact spatial dimensions and weight of the object passed to them.
- Show, Don't Tell: Players are encouraged to demonstrate the new functions through action and object work rather than verbally explaining them like a user manual.
- Continuous Flow: By having players invent the new functions organically during their scenes, the exercise maintains a steady, immersive rhythm without stopping for external suggestions.

## Variations
- Silent Gadget: No speaking is allowed. All new functions must be communicated entirely through mime, physical reactions, and gibberish or sound effects.
- Duo Gadget: Players enter the center in pairs, discovering the new function together in a 2-person scene before passing the gadget to the next pair.

## Why It Works
It trains space object work, physical listening, and Yes And in a continuous, non-competitive flow.

## Safety & Inclusion
Facilitators should remind players to keep physical choices safe and avoid miming weapons that target others in the circle. Ensure the imagined weight or vibration of the object does not force players into uncomfortable physical strains. The exercise is low-pressure and all-ages friendly.

# The Seven Improv Principles

These seven principles are the **mindset** of improv. Each one connects to a concrete **skill or technique** you can practise — that's the principle → skill loop at the heart of [this site](../index.md).

Every page has a **playful infographic** explaining the principle's mechanics, plus a crisp writeup and a 2-minute drill.

| # | Principle | In one line | The skill it builds |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **[Yes, And](yes-and.md)** | Accept your partner's reality, then add to it. | Agreement & Building |
| 2 | **[Listening & Presence](listening-and-presence.md)** | Be here, be changed by what you hear. | Active Listening & Reincorporation |
| 3 | **[Make Your Partner Look Good](make-your-partner-look-good.md)** | Play to lift the other person. | Support & Endowment |
| 4 | **[Accept & Build on Offers](accept-and-build-offers.md)** | Treat everything offered as a gift. | Offer Recognition & Gift-Giving |
| 5 | **[Be Specific & Bold](be-specific-and-bold.md)** | Vivid, committed choices beat vague ones. | Specificity & Object Work |
| 6 | **[Embrace Failure](embrace-failure.md)** | Mistakes are fuel; recover with a smile. | Risk-Taking & Recovery |
| 7 | **[Group Mind & Ensemble](group-mind-ensemble.md)** | Play as one organism. | Unison, Awareness & Edits |

---

*New here? Read them in order — but **[Yes, And](yes-and.md)** is the one to start with.*

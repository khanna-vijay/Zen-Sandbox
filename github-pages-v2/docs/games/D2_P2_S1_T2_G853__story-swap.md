# Story Swap

> Co-create a single, seamless narrative by swapping storytellers instantly at the sound of a clap.

## Overview
Two players collaborate to tell a single, cohesive story. At any moment, the facilitator signals a swap, forcing the other partner to immediately pick up the narrative mid-sentence without missing a beat. This forces players to abandon personal agendas and listen intensely to their partner's exact words.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Serve the Story; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Narrative Architecture; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Last Word Response; Yes, And… sentence games; Story Spine; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening and radical acceptance by requiring players to build directly on their partner's immediate last words, fostering unfiltered spontaneity and narrative cohesion.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other. The facilitator stands where they can be heard by all pairs. No props or special staging required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have them stand or sit facing one another.
2. Assign one person in each pair to be Player A and the other to be Player B.
3. Explain that they will collectively tell a single, continuous story from a simple prompt provided by the facilitator.
4. Player A begins telling the story, speaking at a normal, steady pace.
5. At random intervals, the facilitator claps their hands or calls out 'Swap!'
6. Upon hearing the signal, Player A must stop speaking immediately, even mid-word or mid-sentence.
7. Player B must instantly pick up the story, continuing the exact sentence or thought where Player A left off, without pausing or using filler words.
8. The facilitator continues to swap the storytellers back and forth, gradually varying the intervals to keep players on their toes.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Listen to the very last word!' Remind players that planning ahead prevents them from hearing how their partner's sentence actually ends.
- Pitfall: Players pausing to think or saying 'uh' or 'um' when they take over. Fix: Encourage them to speak immediately, prioritizing momentum and flow over narrative perfection.
- Coaching cue: 'Accept the grammar!' If Player A ends on 'and then the giant...', Player B must start with a word that grammatically fits that setup.
- Pitfall: Hijacking the story. One player tries to steer the plot to their pre-planned idea regardless of what their partner just said. Fix: Remind them that they do not own the story; they are merely stewards of the current sentence.

## Variations
- Word-by-Word: Instead of swapping on a clap, players alternate telling the story one single word at a time.
- Emotional Shift: When the swap occurs, the incoming player must also adopt a new emotional state or genre suggested by the facilitator.
- Physical Pass: Instead of a facilitator clapping, the active speaker passes a physical object to signal the swap at their own whim.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your planned direction cut off mid-sentence?
- What strategies helped you pick up the story instantly without a lag?
- How does letting go of your own 'perfect' idea serve the overall narrative?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players speak at a moderate pace so partners with processing differences have time to absorb the words. If physical clapping is startling or inaccessible, use a visual cue like a hand raise or a soft chime.

## Why It Works
By interrupting the speaker mid-thought, the game makes planning ahead impossible. Players must practice absolute presence, receiving the partner's last word as a complete gift and immediately treating it as the only foundation for their next contribution.

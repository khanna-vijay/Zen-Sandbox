# Simon Says

> Sharpen your focus and reaction speed by filtering commands with absolute presence and precision.

## Overview
A high-energy focus game where players must execute physical actions only when preceded by a specific verbal cue. It challenges players to listen to the exact words spoken rather than reacting on autopilot or copying their peers. The experience is fast-paced, playful, and demands total concentration.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Peripheral Awareness
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening, resist physical impulse and autopilot, and build peripheral awareness of the group's collective movement.

## Setup
Players stand in a semi-circle or spread out across the room, all facing the facilitator who starts as the leader. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator explains that they are the leader and will give physical commands, such as touching a body part or making a movement.
2. Players must only execute the command if it begins with the exact phrase 'Simon says.'
3. If the leader gives a command without saying 'Simon says' first, players must remain completely still.
4. The leader announces that the game officially begins only when they say 'Simon says the game has started.'
5. The leader uses tricks, such as asking 'Do you understand?' to catch people nodding, or performing the action themselves while giving a non-Simon-says command to test visual versus auditory processing.
6. When a player makes a mistake, they step to the side and become active observers, helping the leader spot other errors.
7. The game continues at a rapid pace until only one or two players remain, or a set time limit is reached.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Listen to the words, don't just copy my body!'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Commit 100% to your stillness or your movement.'
- Pitfall: Players getting discouraged when eliminated. Fix: Keep the rounds incredibly fast (1-2 minutes) so eliminated players aren't sitting out for long, or have eliminated players immediately become 'judges' to keep them engaged.
- Pitfall: The leader moving too slowly. Fix: Speed up the tempo of commands to force players into intuitive, split-second decisions.

## Variations
- Peer Leader: Have a student take over the leader role to practice commanding a room and reading group energy.
- Reverse Simon: Players must do the opposite of the command when 'Simon says' is used, and do nothing when it is omitted.
- Emotional Simon: Commands must be executed with a specific emotional state, such as 'Simon says jump joyfully' or 'Simon says cry while touching your knees.'

## Debrief
- How did it feel to balance watching the leader's physical actions versus listening to their actual words?
- What strategies did you use to override your automatic physical impulses?
- How does this level of hyper-focused listening translate to supporting a scene partner on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure commands are physically accessible to all participants. Offer alternative movements (e.g., 'raise a hand' instead of 'jump') and encourage players to adapt any physical command to their own comfort and mobility level.

## Why It Works
It forces players out of intellectual planning and into the present moment. By creating a high-stakes listening environment, it trains the brain to process auditory information before reacting physically, breaking the habit of anticipating or assuming what a partner will do next.

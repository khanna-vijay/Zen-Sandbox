# Persona Swap

> Step into your partner's shoes instantly by inheriting their physical, vocal, and emotional choices.

## Overview
Three players initiate a scene with highly distinct, exaggerated physical and vocal choices. At the facilitator's cue, players rotate positions, instantly adopting the exact posture, voice, and emotional state of the character they have inherited. This fast-paced drill demands intense observation, physical empathy, and seamless commitment to your partner's creations.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Active Listening; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Heightening & Exploration
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise; Last Word Response; Character Walks/Centers; Vocal characterization; The 'ladder' (escalating beats)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep physical empathy, active listening, and instant mirroring skills. Players learn to let go of their own ideas and fully inhabit the physical, vocal, and emotional choices made by their partners, heightening those choices with 100% commitment.

## Setup
An open performance space. Three players stand in a line or a clear triangle facing the audience/facilitator. The remaining group members watch as active observers. The facilitator acts as the host/caller.

## How to Play
1. Position three players on stage and ask them to establish a simple, everyday relationship and setting.
2. Instruct each player to immediately adopt a highly distinct physical posture, vocal pattern, and emotional attitude that contrasts sharply with the other two.
3. Begin the scene, allowing the players to interact while strongly committing to their unique character traits and physical setups.
4. At any moment, the facilitator calls out 'Switch!' or blows a whistle.
5. Upon hearing the cue, players physically rotate clockwise (Player A moves to Player B's spot, B to C's, and C to A's).
6. The player rotating into the spot of the character who was just speaking must repeat that character's last line of dialogue to anchor the transition.
7. All players must instantly adopt the physical stance, facial expression, vocal tone, and emotional viewpoint of the character whose spot they just took.
8. Continue the scene from this new configuration, with players heightening and escalating the established character traits until the next 'Switch!' is called.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to make bold, easily readable physical and vocal choices at the start; subtle characters are incredibly difficult to mirror accurately.
- If players forget to repeat the last line, pause the scene and have them repeat it to build the habit of active listening and seamless continuity.
- Watch out for 'caricature dilution'—where characters lose their specific details with each rotation. Remind players to match the exact physical tension and vocal pitch of the original creator.
- Vary the timing of the 'Switch!' calls. Sometimes call it mid-sentence to force instant physical adaptation, and other times let a beat play out so characters can develop.

## Variations
- Emotional Escalation: Each time a character is inherited, the new player must increase the intensity of that character's core emotion by 10%.
- Silent Swap: Run the entire exercise as a silent, physical-only scene, focusing purely on body language, object work, and spatial relationships.
- Two-Player Swap: Run the game with only two players, swapping back and forth, requiring them to jump rapidly between two contrasting perspectives.

## Debrief
- What physical or vocal cues were the easiest to inherit, and what made them so accessible?
- How did it feel to let go of your original character and fully commit to your partner's creation?
- In what ways did repeating the last spoken line help you lock into the emotional state of the new character?
- How does this exercise train us to 'make our partner a genius' during a standard, unscripted scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical rotation is done at a safe, controlled pace to prevent collisions. Encourage players to adapt physical choices to their own mobility levels; if a player cannot match a deep squat or high-energy movement, they should mirror the energy and tension of the posture rather than the exact physical strain.

## Why It Works
This game acts as a dynamic mirror exercise. By forcing players to physically and vocally inhabit a character created by someone else, it bypasses intellectual planning and taps directly into somatic empathy. Repeating the partner's last line ensures deep active listening, while the physical swap demands a literal 'yes-and' to the partner's physical reality, reinforcing the principle of making your partner's choices important.

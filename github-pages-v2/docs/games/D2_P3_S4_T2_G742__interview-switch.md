# The Expert Exchange

> Rotate roles rapidly, transforming your partner into an instant authority on any bizarre topic.

## Overview
Two players sit downstage in an interviewer-and-expert dynamic while the rest of the ensemble waits upstage. At any moment, an off-stage player calls 'Switch!', triggering a rotation where the interviewer becomes the new expert, a fresh player becomes the interviewer, and a new topic is introduced. The game relies on rapid-fire character endowment and immediate, joyful acceptance of absurd expertise.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light)
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To practice seamless endowment-acceptance and make your partner look like a genius by instantly adopting the high-status authority, name, and background gifted to you by the interviewer.

## Setup
Place two chairs side-by-side facing the audience downstage. The remaining players (3 to 8) stand in a line upstage, acting as the queue and the 'directors' who will call for transitions. No physical props are needed other than the chairs.

## How to Play
1. Designate the player in the left chair as the Interviewer and the player in the right chair as the Expert. The remaining players stand in a line upstage.
2. The Interviewer asks the group for a random, mundane topic (such as 'paperclips' or 'puddles').
3. The Interviewer immediately starts a talk-show style segment, introducing the show, naming the Expert, and endowing them with a specific, highly-specialized background related to the topic.
4. The Expert must instantly accept this endowment, adopting the name, attitude, and specific expertise gifted to them, answering the Interviewer's questions with absolute confidence.
5. At any point during the interview, any player standing in the upstage line can call out 'SWITCH!'
6. Upon hearing 'SWITCH!', the current Expert leaves their chair and joins the back of the upstage line. The Interviewer slides over into the Expert chair. The player at the front of the upstage line sits in the vacant Interviewer chair.
7. The new Interviewer immediately calls for a brand-new topic, introduces their show, and endows the former Interviewer (now the Expert) with a new identity and specialty.
8. Continue this cycle, ensuring every player gets multiple opportunities to both gift elaborate endowments and enthusiastically accept them.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Gift them a name and a specific passion!' Encourage interviewers to avoid generic questions and instead make bold statements that the expert can agree with.
- Coaching Cue: 'Yes, and your expertise!' Remind the new experts to immediately agree with whatever wild claim the interviewer makes about their past or achievements.
- Pitfall: The rotation happens too slowly or players hesitate. Fix: Encourage the upstage line to call 'SWITCH!' at high-energy peaks or right after a funny revelation, keeping the pacing brisk.
- Pitfall: The interviewer asks open-ended, difficult questions like 'So, tell me about your life?' instead of endowing. Fix: Side-coach the interviewer to make active statements, e.g., 'You've written three books on the emotional life of paperclips, correct?'

## Variations
- Character Carryover: Instead of getting a new topic on a switch, the new interviewer must interview the same character but about a completely different, unrelated topic, forcing the expert to justify how their character is an expert in both.
- Emotional Shift: The player calling 'SWITCH!' also calls out an emotion (e.g., 'SWITCH! Jealousy!'). The new pair must conduct the interview under the influence of that specific emotional dynamic.
- Tag-Team Experts: Two experts sit in the hot seat, and the interviewer treats them as bitter rivals in the same niche field.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your name, status, and history decided for you by your partner?
- What strategies did you use to make your partner look like an absolute genius when you were the interviewer?
- How does immediate, enthusiastic agreement ('Yes, and') relieve the pressure of having to be clever on the spot?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical transition between chairs is clear and unobstructed to prevent tripping. Players can call 'Switch' at a moderate pace if rapid physical movement is a barrier for any participant.

## Why It Works
This game works because it strips away the fear of invention by dividing the labor of character creation. The interviewer does the heavy lifting of defining the character's identity, while the expert simply has to agree and expand. By focusing entirely on making the partner look brilliant, players bypass their internal editors and tap into effortless, spontaneous comedy.

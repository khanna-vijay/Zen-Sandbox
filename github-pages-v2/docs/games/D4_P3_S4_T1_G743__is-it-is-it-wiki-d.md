# Hyperlink Roulette

> A fast-paced, tech-assisted scene game driven by random online articles and audience-triggered edits.

## Overview
Players perform scenes inspired by randomly generated online encyclopedia articles projected onto a screen. Off-stage players or the audience control the pacing and direction by calling out commands to either click deeper into hyperlinks or jump to entirely new random pages, demanding rapid edits and high-energy adaptation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Piece; Base Reality First; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Pacing & Rhythm; Suggestion Deconstruction (A-to-C); World-Building; Room Reading
- **Technique(s):** Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light); What's interesting about this? mining; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Reading the suggestion's intent
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** Develops rapid editing skills, pacing, and the ability to quickly establish a base reality from a random text prompt while serving the overall rhythm of the show.

## Setup
Set up a projector and computer with internet access, displaying a collaborative online encyclopedia. Project the screen onto the back wall of the stage or a screen visible to both players and the audience. Position a designated Tech Operator at the computer.

## How to Play
1. Ask the audience for a number between 1 and 10.
2. The Tech Operator clicks the Random Article button on the encyclopedia homepage that many times to land on the starting prompt.
3. The players on stage quickly read the title and the first sentence or two of the article, avoiding getting bogged down in reading.
4. Two or more players immediately step forward to initiate a scene inspired by the article's title, theme, or a specific detail.
5. At any point during the scene, an off-stage player or audience member can shout Dig Deeper! to edit the scene.
6. Upon hearing Dig Deeper!, the Tech Operator clicks a random blue hyperlink within the current article, and the players must immediately edit the current scene and start a new scene inspired by the new article.
7. Alternatively, an off-stage player can shout Random Page! to trigger a hard edit. The Tech Operator clicks the Random Article button, and a completely new scene in a new world begins.
8. Continue this cycle of rapid edits, using Dig Deeper for thematic or connected edits and Random Page for hard sweeps, keeping the energy high and the pacing tight.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Don't read the whole page! Grab the first word or vibe that hits you and jump in!
- Pitfall: Players spending too much time reading the screen instead of acting. Fix: Have the Tech Operator scroll down or hide the text after 5 seconds, or coach players to initiate on the very first noun they see.
- Coaching cue: Use Dig Deeper to explore a sub-world or a character's backstory, and Random Page when a beat has reached its natural peak.
- Pitfall: The Tech Operator being too slow. Fix: Ensure the operator is highly attentive and has a fast internet connection; they are a key performer in this game.

## Variations
- A-to-C Association: Instead of clicking a link, when Dig Deeper is called, the players must verbally shout out a word associated with the current page, and the Tech Operator searches for that word.
- Tag-Out Integration: Players can physically tag out actors on stage to start the new hyperlink scene, keeping the physical momentum high.
- Blind Run: The actors on stage cannot see the screen; only the audience and the off-stage players can see it. The off-stage players must shout out the new title or concept to initiate the edit.

## Debrief
- How did the visual edits (clicking links) change your approach to scenic pacing compared to traditional sweep edits?
- What strategies did you use to instantly establish a base reality when you had almost no information about the topic?
- How did we balance serving the individual scene versus serving the overall rhythm of the entire run?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the projector light does not shine directly into players' eyes for extended periods. If a random page contains sensitive, offensive, or triggering topics, the Tech Operator should immediately click Random Article again without hesitation or explanation to maintain a safe environment.

## Why It Works
It gamifies the edit. By externalizing the edit mechanism through technology and audience or off-stage cues, it forces players to let go of preciousness. It trains the ensemble to read the room's energy, using Dig Deeper for micro-explorations (similar to tag-outs) and Random Page for macro-clears (similar to sweep edits), directly teaching pacing and rhythm.

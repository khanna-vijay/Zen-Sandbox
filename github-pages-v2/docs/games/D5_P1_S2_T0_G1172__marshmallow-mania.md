# The Gravity Game

> Perform a serious scene where every audience laugh forces you to stuff your face.

## Overview
A high-energy comedy game where players attempt to perform a grounded, serious scene under a strict constraint: they must not make the audience laugh. Every time the audience chuckles, the player responsible must add a marshmallow to their mouth, forcing them to justify their changing voice and physical state.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D5 — The Audience
- **Principle(s):** The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner; Fail Joyfully; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Audience-Energy Management; Vocal Craft; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Vocal characterization; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop acute awareness of audience energy, practice vocal articulation under physical constraints, and master the art of justifying absurd physical realities on stage.

## Setup
A stage area for two to three active players, with the remaining group acting as the live audience. A bowl of standard-sized marshmallows is placed on a small table downstage.

## How to Play
1. Select two to three players to begin a scene on stage, and establish a simple, grounded base reality.
2. Instruct the players that their goal is to keep the scene completely serious and avoid making the audience laugh.
3. Instruct the audience that they should not actively suppress genuine amusement; if something is funny, they should laugh naturally.
4. When an audience member laughs, the facilitator pauses the action and identifies which player's line or action triggered the laughter.
5. The triggered player must immediately take one marshmallow from the bowl and place it in their mouth.
6. The player is allowed to chew the marshmallow to manage space, but they are strictly forbidden from swallowing it during the scene.
7. The scene resumes immediately, with the player now forced to speak through the marshmallow, using vocal craft to remain intelligible and justifying their muffled speech within the context of the scene.
8. Continue the scene, adding more marshmallows as more laughs are triggered, until a player can no longer speak clearly or the scene reaches a natural comedic climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to focus on a grounded base reality first; trying to be funny on purpose ruins the tension. The comedy comes from trying not to be funny.
- If the audience is holding back laughs to help the players, remind them that their honest reaction is the engine of the game.
- Encourage immediate justification. If a player has three marshmallows in their mouth, they should justify it within the scene's reality.
- Monitor safety closely. If a player is struggling to breathe or looks distressed, call scene immediately.

## Variations
- Grapes or Berries: Substitute marshmallows with grapes or cherry tomatoes for a juicier, less sticky alternative.
- The Tag-Out: When a player's mouth becomes too full to speak, a player from the audience tags them out, inheriting the marshmallows or starting fresh.
- The Confession: Players must deliver a dramatic monologue instead of a multi-person scene, trying to keep a straight face while the audience reacts.

## Debrief
- How did the threat of a penalty change how you listened to the audience's reactions?
- What strategies did you use to maintain vocal clarity while your mouth was full?
- How did you justify the physical restriction so it fit the reality of the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
This game involves food and presents a potential choking hazard. Ensure all participants are comfortable consuming the chosen prop. Always have a trash can nearby for quick disposal. Players with dietary restrictions, dysphagia, or sensory sensitivities can participate by holding physical objects like beanbags or cups instead of putting food in their mouths.

## Why It Works
This game highlights the audience as an active scene partner. By penalizing laughter, it creates a delicious tension where the players' struggle to remain serious becomes the primary source of comedy. It forces players to listen deeply to the room's energy while practicing high-level justification and vocal control under pressure.

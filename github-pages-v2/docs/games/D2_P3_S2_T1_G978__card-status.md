# Card Status

> Discover your social standing solely through how your scene partners treat you.

## Overview
Players improvise a scene with playing cards secured to their foreheads, visible to everyone except themselves. The card's value dictates their character's social status, forcing players to discover their own rank by reading and reacting to the subtle status cues gifted by their partners.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Status Modulation; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Meisner Repetition; Status Seesaw; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To master status modulation and the status seesaw technique by actively gifting status to partners, allowing them to accurately deduce and embody their own relative social standing.

## Setup
Gather a standard deck of playing cards and elastic headbands (or string) for each player. Have 4 players step forward. Place a random card on each player's forehead facing outward, ensuring they do not see their own card but can clearly see everyone else's. Establish a high-stakes hierarchical setting, such as a royal court or a prestigious kitchen.

## How to Play
1. Assign each of the four active players a random playing card secured to their forehead, facing outward, using an elastic band.
2. Explain the status scale: Aces or Kings are the highest status (royalty/CEOs), while Twos are the lowest status (servants/interns).
3. Establish a clear, hierarchical base reality and location where social rank heavily influences interactions.
4. Begin the scene. Players must treat their scene partners strictly according to the card values they see on their partners' foreheads.
5. Players must not explicitly state the number or rank of anyone's card; instead, they must show it through physical posture, tone, deference, or dominance.
6. As a player, pay close attention to how others treat you (the 'status seesaw') and modulate your own behavior to match the level they are gifting you.
7. Continue the scene until a natural conclusion is reached or the facilitator calls freeze once everyone has settled into their correct relative status.
8. Before removing the cards, have the players line up in order of what they believe their status is, from lowest to highest, to see if their deductions match reality.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Show, don't tell.' Remind players not to say 'Hello, Number Two.' Instead, bow deeply to a King or ignore a Two.
- Pitfall: Players playing their guessed status too early. Fix: Encourage them to start neutral and let the environment shape them. They must earn their status through their partners' reactions.
- Coaching cue: 'Make your partner a genius.' If you see a high card, treat them like royalty immediately so they can easily play high status.
- Pitfall: The scene becomes a shouting match or a flat line. Fix: Remind players of the status seesaw—to make someone look high status, you must actively lower your own status in relation to them.

## Variations
- Blind Self-Status: Players only know their own card value but do not know anyone else's, forcing them to assert their own status while discovering where others stand.
- Dynamic Shift: Mid-scene, the facilitator calls 'Swap!' and players must blindly exchange cards with a neighbor, instantly modulating their behavior to adapt to their new, unknown rank.
- Silent Status: Run the scene entirely in gibberish or silently, relying purely on physical status seesaw, eye contact, and spatial relationships.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your status entirely dependent on how others treated you?
- What specific physical or verbal offers made it easiest for you to guess your rank?
- How does lowering your own status help make your partner look like a genius?
- What did you notice about the physical distance and eye contact between high and low-status characters?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure elastic bands are comfortable and players can opt to hold the card to their forehead or use a hat clip if they have sensory sensitivities. Remind players that low status does not mean abusive behavior; keep the status play focused on social deference, space, and posture rather than personal cruelty.

## Why It Works
This game works because it operationalizes the core improv principle of making your partner a genius. A player cannot successfully play their own status without their partner actively gifting it to them. By forcing players to focus entirely outward on their partners' cues, it builds deep active listening and physical status modulation, demonstrating that status is a transaction rather than an individual choice.

# The Turning Circle

> Synchronize movement and shift directions instantly as a single, unified organism.

## Overview
A high-energy, physical warm-up where players run in a circle and instantly reverse direction upon a shared cue. It transitions from a vocal command to a silent, shared impulse, demanding intense focus and spatial awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Timing exercises
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop peripheral awareness, group rhythm, and the ability to 'follow the follower' by sensing and reacting to collective movement shifts without a designated leader.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players stand in a wide, evenly spaced circle facing inward. No props needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a large, spacious circle facing inward with clear space in the center and around the perimeter.
2. Instruct the group to begin jogging slowly in a clockwise direction, maintaining equal distance between each other.
3. Introduce the first phase: any player in the circle can shout 'Turn!' at any moment.
4. Upon hearing the cue, every player must instantly pivot and run in the opposite direction (counter-clockwise) without colliding.
5. Emphasize that the cue can come from anyone, at any time, requiring constant vocal and physical readiness.
6. Transition to the second phase: remove the vocal cue. Now, players must initiate and match the direction change purely through physical impulse and peripheral vision.
7. If one player decides to pivot, the rest of the circle must instantly sense the shift and pivot with them, aiming for a seamless, simultaneous group transition.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes soft. Don't just stare at the back of the head in front of you; use your peripheral vision to see the whole circle.'
- Pitfall: Players running too fast or crowding the center. Fix: Remind them to maintain the circle's shape and keep a safe, controlled pace.
- Coaching cue: 'Feel the rhythm of the group. Anticipate the turn rather than just reacting to it.'
- Pitfall: Hesitation during the silent phase where multiple people turn at different times. Fix: Encourage committing 100% to any movement seen, even if it is a false start.

## Variations
- Tempo Shift: Introduce changes in speed (walking, jogging, sprinting, slow-motion) initiated silently by any member of the group.
- Level Changes: Incorporate high/low movement (e.g., ducking, jumping) that the circle must mirror instantly.
- The Wave: The turn doesn't happen simultaneously but cascades around the circle like a wave, requiring precise timing.

## Debrief
- How did your focus shift when we moved from the vocal cue to the silent impulse?
- What did it feel like to 'follow the follower' when there was no designated leader?
- How did you use your peripheral vision to keep the group synchronized?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is non-slippery. Remind players to adjust their speed to accommodate all physical abilities in the room. Offer a walking-pace option to keep the exercise accessible and low-impact while maintaining the same mental focus.

## Why It Works
By removing a single leader, the game forces players to distribute their attention across the entire group. The physical commitment of changing direction instantly builds trust and 'group mind,' as players must rely on mutual responsiveness rather than explicit instructions.

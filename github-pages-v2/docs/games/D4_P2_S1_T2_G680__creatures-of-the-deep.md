# Deep Sea Currents

> Pass physical, morphing creature movements around a circle to build collective focus and physical empathy.

## Overview
In this physical passing game, players stand in a circle and transmit abstract, creature-like movements and sounds to their neighbors. Instead of trying to preserve the original form perfectly, players must mirror exactly what they just received, allowing the movement to naturally mutate as it travels. As multiple distinct physical currents are introduced, the group must track multiple threads simultaneously, building deep ensemble awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Follow the Follower; Group Mind; Fail Joyfully; Yes, And

- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Mirror exercise

**Objective:** Develops peripheral awareness, physical mirroring, and 'follow the follower' dynamics by training players to accept physical offers exactly as received and track multiple physical threads.

## Setup
Players stand in a shoulder-to-shoulder circle with plenty of space to move their arms and bodies. No props or materials are needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle facing inward, ensuring everyone has enough physical space to move dynamically.
2. The facilitator initiates the game by turning to their neighbor and presenting a distinct, physical, and vocal creature movement.
3. The receiving player must immediately mirror and absorb this movement and sound as accurately as possible, then turn to their other neighbor to pass it on.
4. Each player must copy only what their immediate neighbor just offered them, rather than trying to recreate the original version started by the facilitator.
5. Allow the physical offer to naturally mutate, escalate, or de-escalate as it travels around the circle, embracing any accidental changes as the new reality.
6. Once the first creature has traveled a few players down, the facilitator introduces a second, completely different creature going in the same direction.
7. Continue introducing new physical threads so players must track multiple incoming and outgoing movements, maintaining high peripheral awareness.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach: 'Don't try to fix it! Copy the exact mutation you just received, even if it changed completely.'
- Side-coach: 'Keep your eyes soft. Use your peripheral vision to track the other movements moving around the circle.'
- If players try to 'correct' the movement back to its original form, remind them that there is no correct version; the joy of the game is in the evolution of the movement.
- If the circle stalls when multiple threads collide, coach players to 'Yes-And' the collision by merging them briefly or letting one pass through the other.

## Variations
- Bidirectional Currents: Send creatures in opposite directions, forcing players to manage the chaotic intersection when two movements cross paths.
- Silent Depths: Play the entire game in complete silence, relying purely on physical mirroring and eye contact.
- Emotional Currents: Instead of physical creatures, pass physicalized emotional states that mutate as they travel.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to let go of the 'original' movement and accept the mutated version your neighbor gave you?
- What strategies did you use to keep track of multiple physical threads at the same time?
- How does this game illustrate the concept of 'following the follower' in an improv scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries and physical abilities; movements can be adapted to any level of mobility, such as using facial expressions or smaller hand gestures. Encourage non-contact physical play.

## Why It Works
By removing the pressure of verbal invention, players focus entirely on physical empathy and somatic listening. The inevitable errors in transmission demonstrate how organic escalation and heightening occur naturally when a group collectively commits to following the follower without a rigid script.

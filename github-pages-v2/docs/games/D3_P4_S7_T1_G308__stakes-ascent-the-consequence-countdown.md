# Consequence Countdown

> Escalate narrative tension under rapid-fire time constraints and high-stakes external consequences.

## Overview
A high-energy narrative exercise where two active players establish a scene with clear, simple objectives, only to have a facilitator repeatedly freeze the action to inject increasingly severe, time-limited consequences. As the countdown shrinks and the stakes skyrocket, players must rapidly adapt their characters' motivations and justify the escalating chaos. It is a thrilling, fast-paced drill that teaches players how to embrace urgency and use rising stakes to drive a scene's narrative arc.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Commit 100%; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Raising the Stakes; Stakes / The 'Want'; Justification; Narrative Architecture; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Stakes-escalation reps; Make it matter more beats; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop the ability to rapidly raise stakes, adapt character objectives under pressure, and construct a compelling narrative arc using external constraints (Stakes-escalation reps).

## Setup
An open performance space with no props. The group gathers as an audience, with two active players taking the stage. The facilitator stands nearby, ready to actively side-coach and manage the timer.

## How to Play
1. Ask the audience or group for a simple location, a relationship between two characters, and an initial minor conflict or problem to launch the scene.
2. Instruct the two active players to begin the scene, establishing their characters, environment, and relationship immediately.
3. Within the first minute of play, both players must clearly voice or demonstrate their character's immediate objective (their 'want') and a minor consequence if they fail to achieve it.
4. Let the scene play naturally for about two minutes to establish a solid baseline of reality and emotional connection.
5. Freeze the scene. As the facilitator, directly address one or both characters and introduce a new, highly specific external consequence tied to their objective, along with a strict 45-second countdown.
6. Unfreeze the scene. The players must immediately play with heightened urgency, integrating and justifying this new threat while fiercely pursuing their goals.
7. Freeze the scene again when the timer expires. Introduce an even more severe, high-stakes consequence that builds logically or comically on the previous one, and set a shorter 30-second countdown.
8. Repeat this cycle for a third and final round with an extreme, near-impossible consequence and a frantic 15-to-20-second countdown, pushing the characters to their absolute limits.
9. End the scene at the final buzzer, focusing on the peak of dramatic or comedic tension rather than forcing a neat resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- As the facilitator, act as the 'God of Consequences.' Your prompts must be highly specific, tangible, and directly tied to what the characters already care about, rather than random, disconnected events.
- Ensure the escalation builds progressively. Start with personal or professional setbacks (e.g., losing a job) before moving to absurd or catastrophic scales (e.g., global security threats).
- If players freeze or ignore the new stakes, call out side-coaching cues like 'Let it land!' or 'How does this change your immediate plan?' to force active justification.
- Keep the transitions between the frozen state and active play extremely fast to maintain the high-energy momentum of the exercise.

## Variations
- Internal Escalation: Instead of external events, the facilitator injects escalating emotional or psychological stakes (e.g., 'If you don't convince them in 30 seconds, you will have to confess your deepest, darkest secret').
- Tag-Team Stakes: Allow audience members to call 'Freeze!' and supply the next escalating consequence instead of the facilitator.
- Silent Pressure: Run the game where the escalating consequences must be communicated physically or through environmental changes rather than verbal facilitator prompts.

## Debrief
- How did the introduction of a ticking clock change your physical energy and decision-making on stage?
- What strategies did you use to justify the increasingly absurd or extreme consequences within the reality of your scene?
- How does raising the stakes help move a story forward when a scene feels stuck or repetitive?
- In what ways did your character's core 'want' evolve as the external pressure increased?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because the game introduces high-pressure scenarios and escalating consequences, players should establish boundaries during setup regarding sensitive topics (e.g., physical danger, family trauma). Facilitators should ensure the consequences remain playful, dramatic, or comically absurd rather than genuinely distressing.

## Why It Works
By stripping away the time to overthink, the countdown mechanism forces players to rely on their immediate instincts. The structured, external escalation models the narrative principle of 'if true, what else is true,' showing players how to expand a simple premise into a high-stakes climax. It teaches that narrative momentum is sustained not by inventing new plots, but by deepening the importance of what is already established.

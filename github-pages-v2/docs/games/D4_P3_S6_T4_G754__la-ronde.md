# La Ronde

> Connect a chain of character-driven duets to map a shared, living world.

## Overview
A classic longform structure where players explore a shared universe through a series of overlapping two-person scenes. Each performer plays a single, consistent character across exactly two consecutive scenes, passing the narrative baton to create a closed loop of relationships.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Piece; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Format Literacy; Thematic Synthesis; Active Listening; Narrative Architecture; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Longform vs. shortform mechanics; Weave the threads; Story Spine; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop format literacy, thematic synthesis, and world-building by sustaining a character across different relational contexts without relying on heavy plot-driving.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in a semi-circle facing the performance space. No props are needed, though two chairs can be placed in the playing area.

## How to Play
1. Establish a shared location or overarching theme for the entire run to ground the universe.
2. Player A and Player B step into the performance space to initiate the first two-person scene, establishing their characters and relationship.
3. After a clear beat or when the scene reaches a natural peak, the facilitator calls for a transition.
4. Player A exits the stage, while Player B remains in character, maintaining their established physical and emotional traits.
5. Player C enters the space as a brand-new character and initiates a new scene with Player B, exploring a different relationship or dynamic.
6. Repeat this pattern: after Player B and Player C's scene, Player B exits, Player C remains, and Player D enters as a new character.
7. Continue this sequence until every player in the group has performed in exactly two consecutive scenes.
8. For the final scene, the last player to enter performs with Player A, who returns as their original character from the very first scene to close the loop.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your character, change the relationship.' Remind players that while their character remains consistent, how they interact with a new person should shift based on the new dynamic.
- Pitfall: Players trying to force a linear plot or gossiping about previous scenes. Fix: Encourage players to focus on the immediate, present relationship rather than recapping what happened in the last scene.
- Coaching cue: 'Build the world, don't just talk about it.' Encourage physical object work and environmental awareness to ground the shared location.
- Pitfall: Rushing the transitions. Fix: Allow the remaining player a moment to physically transition or adjust their posture to signal they are ready for the next partner.

## Variations
- Thematic Threads: Instead of a physical location, use an abstract theme to connect the scenes across different times and places.
- Time Jump: Each transition represents a jump forward or backward in time, showing how the remaining character has changed between the two scenes.
- Blind Entrances: The entering player does not know who the remaining character is beforehand, forcing them to discover the relationship entirely in the moment.

## Debrief
- How did your character change when interacting with a different partner compared to the first?
- How did the world-building expand organically without a pre-planned plot?
- What thematic patterns or motifs did you notice emerging across the entire loop?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players respect physical boundaries during transitions, especially when entering a scene with an established character. Encourage players to establish clear physical boundaries if their character choices involve close proximity.

## Why It Works
By limiting each player to two consecutive scenes, the format forces players to focus on character consistency and relational shifts. It teaches 'serving the piece' because players must hold the memory of the world while allowing new dynamics to breathe, resulting in a rich, interconnected tapestry rather than a single linear storyline.

# Party Quirks

> A hilarious guessing game of bizarre party guests, physical comedy, and joyful, justified mistakes.

## Overview
One player hosts a social gathering while several guests arrive one by one, each embodying a highly specific, physically-driven quirk or secret identity assigned by the audience. The host must interact with the guests, maintain the social fiction of the party, and deduce each guest's unique quirk through active observation and playful guessing. The joy of the game lies in the physical commitment of the guests and the host's hilarious, incorrect assumptions.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self

- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Active Gifting; Justification; Stage Presence & Clarity

**Objective:** To develop physical characterization using distinct physical centers and walks, practice joyful failure through rapid-fire guessing, and master the art of justification in a high-energy comedic setup.

## Setup
Clear a performance space to represent a living room or party venue. One player (the Host) leaves the room or plugs their ears. The remaining players (the Guests) stand offstage. The facilitator gathers distinct, highly physical quirks, historical figures, or bizarre habits from the audience for each guest.

## How to Play
1. Designate one player as the Host and send them out of earshot of the stage.
2. Gather distinct, highly physical quirks from the audience for three to four Guest players, ensuring each quirk has a strong physical center or movement pattern.
3. Bring the Host back to the stage to begin the scene by preparing the party space using pantomimed object work to set up food, music, or decorations.
4. The first Guest 'rings the doorbell,' enters the party, and immediately establishes their quirk through their walk, posture, and physical interaction with the environment.
5. The Host greets the Guest and engages them in conversation, using active listening and observation to figure out the quirk while keeping the party atmosphere alive.
6. The Host must make bold, verbal guesses integrated naturally into the dialogue rather than breaking character to guess.
7. If a guess is incorrect, the Guest must joyfully justify the mistake and keep playing, while the Host celebrates the wrong guess and tries a new angle.
8. Subsequent Guests enter one by one at staggered intervals, increasing the chaotic energy of the party and forcing the Host to juggle multiple interactions.
9. Once the Host correctly identifies a Guest's quirk, that Guest finds a polite, character-consistent reason to exit the party, ending when all quirks are solved.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Show, don't tell! Let your physical center lead you before you speak.'
- Coaching cue: 'Host, guess early and guess often! Don't wait for certainty; make your mistakes part of the fun.'
- Pitfall: Guests explaining their quirk verbally. Fix: Remind guests to express their quirk through physical walks, posture, and spatial relationships rather than just stating clues.
- Pitfall: The Host freezing up out of fear of guessing wrong. Fix: Encourage the Host to treat incorrect guesses as hilarious gifts that build the scene's reality.

## Variations
- Emotional Centers: Instead of physical quirks, guests are assigned extreme emotional states or physical centers (e.g., leading with the chin, knees, or forehead) that dictate their entire personality.
- Chain Reaction: A guest's quirk is triggered only when another specific action occurs in the room, such as barking like a dog whenever someone touches their own elbow.
- Virtual Adaptation: Play in a gallery view where guests 'enter' by turning their cameras on, using exaggerated facial expressions and upper-body physicality to convey their quirks.

## Debrief
- How did it feel as the Host to guess incorrectly, and how did justifying those mistakes keep the scene alive?
- For the Guests, how did committing to a physical center or walk help you sustain your character without relying on verbal explanations?
- How does treating the audience's suggestions as physical gifts make the comedy feel more collaborative?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical quirks are accessible to all players' physical abilities. Avoid quirks that mock real-world physical disabilities, mental health conditions, or marginalized groups. Encourage players to choose physical centers (like leading with the chest or nose) that are safe and comfortable for their bodies.

## Why It Works
This game thrives on the tension between physical commitment and verbal justification. By forcing the Host to guess constantly, it strips away the fear of being 'wrong,' transforming mistakes into comedic fuel. The physical constraints placed on the guests bypass intellectual overthinking, forcing them to rely on immediate, embodied choices.

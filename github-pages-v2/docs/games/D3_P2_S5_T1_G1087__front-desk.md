# The Grand Lobby

> Co-create a bustling, physically consistent hotel lobby to ground characters, relationships, and objectives.

## Overview
In this scene-building exercise, players collaboratively construct and inhabit a busy hotel lobby. By interacting with a central reception desk and maintaining a shared physical environment, players practice establishing a solid base reality. The game emphasizes physical consistency and immediate character dynamics as players enter and exit the space.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Show, Don't Tell; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Physicality & Space Work; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Object work; Playing architecture/objects
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop strong world-building, physical space awareness, and immediate C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where) establishment within a shared base reality.

## Setup
Clear a moderate performance space. Place two chairs to define the boundaries of an imaginary reception desk, leaving the rest of the stage empty for physical exploration. No actual props are used.

## How to Play
1. Assign one or two players to start behind the imaginary reception desk as the lobby staff.
2. Form a line with the remaining players offstage, ready to enter one by one as guests, delivery workers, or other hotel personnel.
3. The staff members begin the scene by establishing their characters and performing clear, silent object work, such as typing on a computer or polishing the counter.
4. The first offstage player enters the lobby, immediately establishing the physical 'Where' by interacting with an existing element (like a heavy door) and adding one new physical object to the space.
5. Upon reaching the desk, the entering player and the staff establish their Relationship and Objectives through active dialogue and character choices.
6. Subsequent players enter the scene, ensuring they respect all previously established physical objects (e.g., walking around an invisible couch) while introducing their own characters and goals.
7. Players who finish their primary interaction can either exit the space naturally or remain in the background performing supportive, silent object work to maintain the environment.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach physical consistency by calling out forgotten objects: 'Remember the height of that counter!' or 'Don't walk through the luggage.'
- Encourage immediate C.R.O.W. development by prompting: 'How do you feel about this receptionist?' or 'What is your urgent need today?'
- If the scene becomes too chaotic, instruct background players to freeze or exit to keep the focus on the primary interaction.
- If players rely too much on verbal exposition, challenge them to show their character's status and occupation through physical actions first.

## Variations
- Shift Change: At the sound of a bell, the current receptionist must find a quick, justified reason to leave, and a guest must take over the desk.
- Genre Lobby: Apply a specific genre (e.g., sci-fi space station, gothic mansion, or spy thriller) to influence the physical world-building and character archetypes.
- Silent Start: The first two minutes of the lobby's operation must be conducted entirely without dialogue, relying solely on physical storytelling.

## Debrief
- How did respecting the physical environment help you discover your character's objective?
- What non-verbal cues did your partners use that made their C.R.O.W. elements immediately clear?
- How does a highly detailed physical space make it easier to initiate a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical playing area is free of actual obstacles. Encourage players to adapt their physical choices to accommodate all mobility levels, ensuring no one is forced to perform strenuous physical mime.

## Why It Works
By anchoring the scene to a fixed physical location and requiring collective agreement on the environment, players are forced to slow down and establish a solid base reality. This physical discipline naturally grounds their characters, relationships, and objectives, preventing the scene from dissolving into abstract talking heads.

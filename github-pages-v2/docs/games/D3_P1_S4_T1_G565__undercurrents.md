# Undercurrents

> Drive compelling narrative tension by playing powerful, unspoken desires beneath mundane everyday interactions.

## Overview
Two or three players perform a scene in an ordinary, low-stakes setting, but each carries a secret, unstated motivation assigned privately by the facilitator. Players must never explicitly state their hidden agenda, instead conveying it through physical behavior, subtext, and loaded choices. The narrative progresses organically as players detect and react to each other's unspoken undercurrents.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Show, Don't Tell; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Stakes / The 'Want'; Narrative Architecture; World-Building; Active Listening; Justification
- **Technique(s):** What do they stand to lose? reps; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Platform/Tilt; Meisner Repetition; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master the art of subtextual communication, training players to establish high stakes and clear character wants ('What do they stand to lose?') without relying on explicit verbal exposition.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with two chairs. The facilitator prepares a list of mundane, low-conflict scenarios (such as folding laundry, waiting for a bus, or preparing breakfast) and a set of secret, high-stakes motivations that directly clash with the setting or the other character's assumed goals.

## How to Play
1. Select two players to take the stage and ask the remaining group to act as observers.
2. Assign a mundane, low-stakes scenario to establish the physical environment and surface-level activity.
3. Privately assign each player a secret, high-stakes motivation or urgent desire that they must keep hidden from their partner.
4. Instruct the players that they are strictly forbidden from explicitly stating their secret motivation or directly naming their hidden agenda.
5. Begin the scene with players engaging in the mundane activity, using physical object work to ground the reality.
6. Players must actively project their secret desire through non-verbal cues, tone of voice, loaded word choices, and deliberate pacing.
7. Simultaneously, players must listen deeply to their partner's subtext, treating every unusual pause or physical choice as a clue to their partner's hidden reality.
8. Players must react to their partner's perceived subtext as if it is true, adjusting their own behavior to raise the stakes without breaking the unspoken boundary.
9. The facilitator calls 'scene' once the unspoken tension reaches a natural climax or when the underlying relationship has fundamentally shifted.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid 'withholding' or playing passive; they must actively try to achieve their secret goal, not just hide it.
- If a player becomes too explicit, gently side-coach: 'Show us, don't tell us. Let your body do the talking.'
- Pitfall: Players ignoring their partner's cues because they are too focused on their own secret. Fix: Remind them that making their partner a genius means treating every sigh or glance as a gift.
- Encourage heavy use of physical object work; scrubbing a plate or folding a shirt aggressively is a highly effective way to manifest internal stakes.

## Variations
- Monologue Cutaways: The facilitator can freeze the scene and tap a player to share their internal monologue directly with the audience, revealing their current state of panic or desire.
- Audience-Generated Secrets: Have the audience write down secret motivations on slips of paper before the show, which players draw blindly before the scene starts.
- The Third Wheel: Introduce a third player who has no secret motivation but is trying to accomplish a completely normal task, forcing the other two to navigate their subtext around an oblivious outsider.

## Debrief
- What did you guess your partner's secret motivation was, and what specific physical or verbal cues gave it away?
- How did having a secret change the stakes of a simple, mundane activity like folding laundry?
- What did your character stand to lose if their secret was openly exposed during the scene?
- How did reacting to the unspoken subtext create a more compelling narrative than if you had just argued out loud?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure secret motivations do not involve non-consensual physical contact, trauma, or sensitive personal topics unless explicitly negotiated beforehand. Remind players they can always adjust the physical proximity of the scene to maintain personal comfort boundaries.

## Why It Works
By stripping away the ability to speak directly about their problems, players are forced to rely on physical behavior and subtext, embodying the core principle of 'Show, Don't Tell.' The clash of hidden agendas naturally generates a narrative engine driven by high stakes—specifically, what the characters stand to lose if their secrets are revealed—which builds compelling, organic tension without relying on forced plot points.

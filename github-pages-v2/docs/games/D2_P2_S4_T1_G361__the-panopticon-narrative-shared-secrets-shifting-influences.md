# Secret Currents

> Weave a collaborative digital story guided by shared visuals and secret, shifting narrative directives.

## Overview
A virtual, turn-based storytelling game where players collaboratively build a cohesive narrative one sentence at a time. Guided by a sequence of shared visual backdrops, players must also secretly integrate private directives sent by the facilitator. The result is a layered, unpredictable story where players must balance visible narrative offers with invisible, shifting influences.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Narrative Architecture; World-Building; Thematic Synthesis
- **Technique(s):** Yes, And… sentence games; Story Spine; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop advanced offer reception and 'Yes, And' skills by requiring players to simultaneously synthesize multiple layers of input—the previous line, a shared visual environment, and a private, secret prompt—into a single, seamless narrative contribution.

## Setup
A virtual video conferencing platform with gallery view, screen-sharing capabilities, and private chat functionality. The facilitator prepares a sequence of 5 to 7 evocative, open-ended images. The facilitator also pre-writes three distinct prompt banks of 6 to 8 prompts each: Bank 1 (Internal Quirks), Bank 2 (Sensory Details), and Bank 3 (Action Imperatives). At the start, the facilitator sends these three banks to all players via chat.

## How to Play
1. Distribute the Prompt Banks: At the start of the session, the facilitator sends the three pre-written prompt banks to all players via private chat so everyone has a copy of the options.
2. Establish the Turn Order: Set a clear, fixed speaking order for the 6 to 10 players to ensure smooth transitions without verbal overlap.
3. Launch the First Scene: The facilitator shares their screen to display the first evocative image, establishing the initial setting for the story.
4. Assign Secret Directives: The facilitator privately messages each player a single number (1, 2, or 3) designating which prompt bank that player must draw from for the current round.
5. Deliver the Sentences: In the established order, each player contributes exactly one sentence to the story that builds on the previous line, reacts to the shared image, and subtly integrates one self-selected prompt from their assigned bank.
6. Maintain Subtlety: Players must not explicitly state their secret prompt; instead, they must let the prompt color their character's perspective, physical behavior, or dialogue naturally.
7. Shift the Landscape: Once every player has contributed one sentence, the facilitator displays the next image in the sequence and privately sends everyone a new bank number for the next round.
8. Introduce Wildcard Twists: Once or twice during the game, the facilitator can privately send a highly specific 'Twist Card' directive to a single player to inject a sudden, dramatic plot point.
9. Conclude the Chronicle: Continue this cycle through 3 to 5 images, wrapping up the narrative on the final image with a collaborative resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- Manage Host Workload: Pre-format your private chat messages in a separate text document so you can rapidly copy, paste, and send them between rounds without stalling the game.
- Coaching Subtlety: If a player says their prompt out loud, gently side-coach them to show, not tell, by letting the secret influence manifest in their tone or descriptive choices instead.
- Pacing and Latency: Remind players to take a breath and fully absorb the previous line before speaking, using the virtual format's natural pause to synthesize their three inputs.
- Pitfall - Disconnected Sentences: If players focus too hard on their secret prompt and ignore the previous speaker, remind them that the 'Yes, And' connection to the prior line is the absolute priority.

## Variations
- Exhaustion Mode: Once a player uses a specific prompt from their assigned bank, they must cross it off and cannot use it again for the rest of the game.
- The Detective Variant: At the end of each round, players have 30 seconds to guess which prompt bank their peers were assigned based on their spoken sentences.
- Silent Echoes: Non-speaking players can use the chat to add silent, atmospheric sound effects or environmental descriptions that match the current image.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to balance three distinct sources of inspiration (the previous line, the visual, and your secret prompt) at the same time?
- What strategies did you use to make your secret prompt feel like a natural extension of the story rather than a forced interruption?
- When a partner's sentence took an unexpected turn due to their secret prompt, how did you justify and accept that shift in your own turn?
- How does this game mirror the real-world improv challenge of balancing your own internal ideas with the external offers of your partner?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since players receive secret prompts that dictate behavior, ensure the prompt banks do not contain themes of physical violation, extreme phobias, or non-consensual touch. Establish a 'skip' protocol where any player can instantly request a different bank number via private chat if their assigned bank feels uncomfortable or inaccessible.

## Why It Works
This game works because it gamifies the cognitive multitasking of high-level improvisation. By using the private chat as an asynchronous delivery system, it introduces hidden variables that prevent the story from becoming predictable. Players cannot plan ahead because they must wait for the immediate 'Yes, And' offer of the preceding sentence, forcing deep active listening. Synthesizing a visual cue, a verbal offer, and an internal constraint trains the brain to find unexpected, creative connections and build highly textured narrative worlds.

# Bedtime Story

> Co-create a whimsical, physicalized fairy tale through a dynamic handoff between narrator and actors.

## Overview
One player acts as a gentle narrator reading an improvised bedtime story to the audience, while the remaining players physically and verbally bring the narrative to life on stage. The magic of the game lies in the seamless handoff of focus, where the narrator's words inspire the actors, and the actors' spontaneous choices feed back into the narrator's unfolding plot.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Group Mind; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Support Work; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Story Spine; Playing architecture/objects
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop narrative architecture and active listening by practicing the 'Story Spine' structure and sharing focus dynamically between narration and scenic action.

## Setup
An open performance space. One player (the Narrator) stands to one side of the stage, holding an imaginary storybook. The other players (the Actors) stand ready in the upstage or offstage area, prepared to enter the playing space. Obtain a fictional children's book title suggestion from the group.

## How to Play
1. The Narrator addresses the audience in a warm, soothing, bedtime-story voice, introducing the suggested title and opening their imaginary book.
2. The Narrator begins the tale using the classic 'Once upon a time...' prompt, introducing a protagonist and their ordinary world.
3. As soon as the Narrator describes a character, object, or setting, the Actors immediately step into the playing area to physically embody those elements.
4. Once the scene is established, the Narrator pauses, handing over the focus. The Actors improvise dialogue and actions that build upon the Narrator's setup, adding their own creative details.
5. The Narrator actively listens to the Actors' additions, then steps back in with narration to advance the plot, using the next step of the Story Spine (e.g., 'Until one day...').
6. Play continues to alternate back and forth: the Narrator guides the overarching narrative arc, while the Actors flesh out the moment-to-moment relationships and physical comedy.
7. The Narrator guides the story to a satisfying resolution ('Until finally...' and 'Ever since that day...'), closing the book to end the game.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Pass the torch!' Remind the narrator to stop talking once the actors start playing, and remind actors to freeze or yield when the narrator speaks.
- Pitfall: The narrator over-narrates, leaving no room for the actors to make choices. Fix: Coach the narrator to describe only the setup, then pause and let the actors discover the scene's details.
- Pitfall: Actors merely pantomime exactly what the narrator says without adding new offers. Fix: Encourage actors to speak, express emotions, and introduce unexpected character quirks that the narrator must then accept.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Use the Story Spine!' If the narrative gets muddy, call out the next structural prompt (e.g., 'Because of that...', 'Until finally...') to help the narrator drive the plot forward.

## Variations
- The Sleepy Listener: One player sits on stage acting as a tired child who occasionally interrupts the story with questions or demands, forcing the narrator and actors to instantly adapt.
- Genre Flip: Instead of a sweet bedtime story, tell the story in the style of a gritty noir, a sci-fi epic, or a gothic horror, adjusting the narrator's tone and the actors' physical choices accordingly.
- Silent Actors: The actors must remain completely silent, using only physical theater, mime, and gibberish to bring the narrator's spoken words to life.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to share the responsibility of storytelling between the narrator and the actors?
- What strategies did the narrator use to incorporate the actors' spontaneous choices into the larger plot?
- How did the 'Story Spine' structure help keep the narrative on track without stifling creativity?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles, as actors will be rapidly forming physical environments and objects. Encourage players to be mindful of physical contact when forming group objects, opting for proximity and gesture over forced touch unless explicit consent is established.

## Why It Works
This game functions as a narrative engine because it balances macro-level plot control with micro-level character play. By separating the structural architect (the narrator) from the emotional heart (the actors), players learn to trust the narrative flow, practice active listening, and experience how 'serving the story' is a collaborative, push-and-pull process.

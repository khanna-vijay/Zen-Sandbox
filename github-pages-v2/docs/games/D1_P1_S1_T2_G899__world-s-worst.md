# World's Worst

> Leap forward instantly to showcase the absolute worst examples of any profession or role.

## Overview
A high-energy, fast-paced line-up game where players stand facing away from the audience, turning and leaping forward to deliver rapid-fire, comedic examples of the worst version of a suggested profession. It emphasizes immediate physical commitment and trusting the very first instinct that pops into your head.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; The First Thought Is a Gift; Play for the Back Row
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Character Walks/Centers; Make the choice readable
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To bypass the internal editor, practice unfiltered spontaneity, and build physical commitment by launching onto the stage before a joke is fully formed.

## Setup
Players form a line at the back of the stage, facing away from the audience with their backs turned. The facilitator stands near the audience to gather suggestions.

## How to Play
1. Players stand in a line along the back wall of the stage, facing away from the audience to prevent planning or overthinking.
2. The facilitator asks the audience for a profession, job, or social role, such as a surgeon, teacher, or lifeguard.
3. Once the suggestion is announced, players must immediately spin around, step forward into the playing area, and act out a brief, high-commitment example of the world's worst version of that role.
4. Each contribution must be short, punchy, and highly physical, lasting no more than five to ten seconds.
5. After delivering their line or action, the player immediately steps back to the line and turns their back again, clearing the space for others.
6. Multiple players can step forward one after another to offer different takes on the same suggestion, keeping the pace rapid and relentless.
7. Once the energy for a suggestion peaks, the facilitator calls out a new profession to start the cycle again.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Move your body first, think second! Encourage players to physically step forward before they even know what they are going to say.
- Pitfall: Players lingering on stage trying to save a joke. Fix: Remind them to deliver one clear choice and immediately retreat. A bad joke delivered with 100 percent commitment is still a win.
- Coaching cue: Lead with your body! Encourage physicalizing the character immediately upon turning around to spark the verbal idea.
- Pitfall: Players facing the audience the whole time and planning their jokes. Fix: Enforce the backs-to-the-audience rule strictly to keep the mind blank and ready for spontaneous impulses.

## Variations
- World's Best: Flip the prompt to showcase the overly enthusiastic, perfect, or absurdly idealized version of the role.
- Tag-Team Worst: Players can step forward in pairs to do a quick, two-person micro-scene of the world's worst behavior.
- Category Slide: Instead of professions, use broader categories like World's Worst First Date or World's Worst Infomercial.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to step forward before you had a complete idea of what you were going to say?
- What happened to the energy of the room when a player committed 100 percent to a silly or simple idea?
- How does physical movement help quiet the internal critic?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards, as players will be spinning and stepping forward quickly. Encourage players to be mindful of physical boundaries when stepping forward simultaneously.

## Why It Works
By physically turning away and forcing a sudden, rapid pivot into the space, the game disrupts the brain's analytical planning phase. It forces the player to rely on their immediate physical impulse and first thought, proving that high commitment and clear stage presence can make even the simplest offer hilarious and successful.

# Pattern and Pivot

> Establish a predictable behavioral loop, then break it with a justified, story-altering choice.

## Overview
A narrative-focused scene-work game where players establish a clear, repetitive behavioral pattern and then execute a sudden, justified shift. This exercise trains improvisers to recognize the structural engine of their scene and transition it into a dynamic story arc rather than getting stuck in endless repetition.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Show, Don't Tell; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes; World-Building; Game Identification; Justification; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Stakes-escalation reps; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Finding & Playing the Game; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops the ability to establish a stable platform, identify the scene's behavioral engine, and execute a justified narrative tilt to advance the story.

## Setup
An open performance space. Two active players stand on stage, while the remaining participants sit as an active observing audience. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Two players step onto the stage and receive a simple suggestion of a location or relationship to establish their initial platform.
2. The players begin the scene with the goal of establishing a clear, repetitive behavioral pattern or relational dynamic within the first three exchanges.
3. Players must repeat and heighten this established pattern at least two or three times, ensuring the partner and audience clearly understand the rules of their interaction.
4. Once the pattern is firmly established, the facilitator calls out 'Pivot!' to signal the transition.
5. Upon hearing the cue, the active player currently speaking or acting must immediately introduce a new piece of information, a secret, or a dramatic choice that disrupts the pattern.
6. This disruptive choice must be fully justified by the preceding scene work, drawing on established character motivations rather than introducing random external chaos.
7. The partner must immediately accept this new reality, abandoning the old repetitive loop to explore the emotional and narrative consequences of the pivot.
8. The players continue the scene under this new dynamic, driving the narrative toward a natural resolution or climax based on the altered stakes.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'What is the predictable loop here? Lean into it!' Use this if players are struggling to find a clear pattern in the first few lines.
- Common Pitfall: The 'Deus Ex Machina' Pivot. Players often introduce random, unrelated events (like a sudden alien invasion) instead of a justified shift. Fix: Remind them that the pivot should feel like an inevitable truth finally coming to light.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Play the consequence, not the novelty.' Ensure players don't just laugh at the pivot, but immediately treat it as their new, high-stakes reality.
- If the pattern is too subtle, ask the players to over-express their character's core desire or habit so the baseline is undeniable before it is broken.

## Variations
- Self-Pivoting: Remove the facilitator's verbal cue entirely. Players must independently sense when the pattern has been fully explored and execute the pivot organically.
- The Double Pivot: For advanced players, introduce a second 'Pivot!' cue later in the scene, forcing them to adapt to a secondary shift that builds on the first.
- Silent Platform: Establish the initial pattern entirely through physical action and object work before the verbal pivot occurs.

## Debrief
- How did establishing a clear, repetitive pattern make the eventual pivot feel more satisfying or dramatic?
- What is the difference between a random plot twist and a justified narrative tilt?
- How did your character's internal motivation guide the choice of when and how to pivot?
- How did you balance maintaining the reality of the scene while executing a sudden structural shift?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that pivots do not rely on sudden, unconsented physical contact or sensitive personal disclosures that cross boundaries. Encourage players to keep the stakes high through emotional and situational truths rather than shock value.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to first build a predictable loop and then break it, the game externalizes the invisible architecture of storytelling. It teaches improvisers that narrative progression relies on the tension between routine and disruption, ensuring scenes have a clear beginning, middle, and end.

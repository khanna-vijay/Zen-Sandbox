# The Narrative Calibrator

> Pause active scenes to consciously analyze, articulate, and instantly integrate critical story-building choices.

## Overview
A dynamic training exercise where a facilitator periodically pauses a live scene to ask individual players targeted questions about their character's hidden motives, relationship shifts, or environmental realities. Players must instantly verbalize their narrative choices and immediately resume the scene, weaving their articulated answers directly into the unfolding story. This creates a powerful bridge between spontaneous play and deliberate narrative design.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Base Reality First; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; Justification; World-Building; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); If this is true, what else is true?; Story Spine; Platform/Tilt; Justify the absurd; What do they stand to lose? reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop a conscious, real-time awareness of narrative structure, helping players move from passive reactivity to active, intentional story progression by mastering stakes, justification, and relationship dynamics.

## Setup
Designed for virtual platforms. Two to three active players turn their cameras on, while the rest of the group keeps their cameras off to act as the active audience. The facilitator acts as the Calibrator, ready to unmute and intervene. No physical props or special space required.

## How to Play
1. Select two or three players to initiate a scene and have them turn their cameras on.
2. Obtain a simple suggestion from the off-camera players to establish the base reality.
3. Instruct the active players to begin the scene naturally, focusing on establishing their characters, environment, and initial dynamic.
4. At a strategic narrative moment, typically every 30 to 60 seconds, the facilitator calls out Calibrate!
5. The players immediately freeze in place on screen, maintaining their physical positions.
6. The facilitator addresses one specific player by name and asks a highly targeted question about their character's internal state, relationship shift, or environmental justification.
7. The addressed player must answer the question concisely in 5 to 10 seconds, speaking from a meta-perspective to define their narrative intent.
8. The facilitator immediately calls Resume! and the players unfreeze.
9. The players must instantly integrate the newly articulated narrative truth into their physical actions, dialogue, and emotional stakes.
10. Repeat this calibration process two to three times throughout the scene, targeting different players and narrative elements, before calling Scene! to conclude.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: Keep questions highly specific. Instead of asking 'What is happening?', ask 'How did your partner's last line change your character's status?'
- Pitfall: Players might overthink and stall the scene's momentum. Fix: Remind them that there are no wrong answers; the first instinctual answer is the absolute truth of the scene.
- Coaching Cue: Ensure non-queried players are actively listening. They must treat their partner's articulated answer as absolute gospel and adjust their own play accordingly.
- Pitfall: The scene becomes a dry Q&A session. Fix: Keep the calibration pauses brief (under 15 seconds) and ensure the energy remains high upon resumption.

## Variations
- Peer Calibration: Instead of the facilitator, a designated off-camera player acts as the Calibrator, practicing their own directorial and analytical skills.
- The Silent Calibration: The facilitator private-messages the question to the player in the chat, and the player must integrate the answer without saying it out loud, forcing the partner to read their physical and subtextual changes.
- Chain Calibration: Each calibration question must build directly on the answer of the previous calibration, creating a highly logical, cause-and-effect narrative chain.

## Debrief
- How did verbalizing your character's hidden motivation change the choices you made immediately after the freeze?
- For the non-queried player, how did hearing your partner's explicit intent make it easier to make them a genius?
- What is the difference between playing a scene purely on impulse versus playing with conscious narrative intent?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game involves sudden freezes and direct questioning, ensure players know they can pass or adjust any prompt that touches on personal discomfort. In a virtual space, players can use a simple hand gesture to request a different question without breaking the exercise's flow.

## Why It Works
By interrupting the subconscious flow of a scene, this game forces players to step into a director's mindset. Articulating a narrative choice aloud solidifies it as a concrete reality, making it impossible to ignore. This builds the cognitive muscle of 'if true, what else is true,' transforming random offers into a cohesive, structured story.

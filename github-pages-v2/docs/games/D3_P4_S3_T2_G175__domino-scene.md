# The Domino Chain

> Build an escalating narrative arc where every action triggers an immediate, unavoidable consequence for your partner.

## Overview
The Domino Chain is an active improv game where two players collaboratively construct a scene using a strict cause-and-effect structure. Every major action or declaration by one character immediately imposes a direct, escalating consequence on the other, forcing a continuous cycle of acceptance, justification, and new action. This rapid-fire narrative engine keeps players highly focused on active listening and logical progression.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Justification; Stakes / The 'Want'; Game Identification; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Justify the absurd; If this is true, what else is true?; Yes, And… sentence games
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops narrative architecture and the platform/tilt technique by training players to link every plot point directly through cause and effect, while practicing rapid justification and active offer reception.

## Setup
Two active players stand in the performance space. The remaining group acts as an active audience. The facilitator establishes a neutral starting platform with a clear relationship and location to give the scene a grounded beginning.

## How to Play
1. Establish a neutral starting platform with a clear relationship and location, such as two siblings sorting through old boxes in an attic, or two coworkers organizing a retail display.
2. Player A initiates the scene by making a significant declaration or taking a clear, plot-forwarding action (the first domino).
3. Player B immediately declares a direct, undeniable consequence that Player A's action has caused specifically for Player B, stating it as an absolute fact rather than a question.
4. Player A must fully accept this consequence, then briefly justify why their character took the initial action in light of this new reality, revealing their character's underlying motivation or want.
5. In the same turn, Player A must then launch a new significant action or declaration that sets up a new consequence for Player B.
6. Player B repeats the cycle: accepts the consequence, justifies their previous behavior, and launches a new triggering action back at Player A.
7. Continue this alternating cycle for three to four complete rounds (about three to five minutes), consciously escalating the stakes with each turn.
8. Conclude the scene once a natural narrative climax is reached or when the final domino falls, resolving the core tension.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Declare, don't ask!' Remind players to state consequences as absolute, unshakeable facts rather than asking for permission or validation.
- Pitfall: Players make consequences purely negative or catastrophic. Fix: Remind them that consequences can be absurdly positive or comically mundane, which often opens up richer comedic avenues.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Justify the choice!' Ensure players don't ignore the consequence; they must explain why their character's internal logic made sense of the initial action.
- Reset Mechanism: If players lose the cause-and-effect thread and start making random, disconnected offers, freeze the scene and ask: 'What was the direct result of that last action?' Have them re-state the consequence before proceeding.

## Variations
- Silent Dominos: Play the entire sequence using only physical actions and object work, where physical movements trigger physical consequences and reactions.
- The Ripple Effect: Expand to three players, where Player A's action triggers a consequence for Player B, Player B's reaction triggers a consequence for Player C, and Player C's action loops back to Player A.

## Debrief
- How did the strict requirement of cause-and-effect change how you listened to your partner's offers?
- What strategies did you use to justify your character's actions when confronted with an unexpected consequence?
- How did escalating the consequences naturally reveal your character's core wants and stakes?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that physical consequences (such as a character reacting to an injury or physical change) are played with respect to physical boundaries. Players should always use mime or verbal description rather than making physical contact to represent consequences.

## Why It Works
By formalizing the 'If this is true, what else is true?' principle into a strict turn-based mechanic, this game prevents players from introducing unrelated narrative elements. It forces them to build vertically rather than horizontally, ensuring that every tilt is directly justified by the platform, which builds a highly cohesive and satisfying narrative arc.

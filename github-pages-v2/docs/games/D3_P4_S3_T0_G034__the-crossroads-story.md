# Crossroads of Choice

> Steer scenes through high-stakes narrative forks by collaboratively choosing the path of greatest consequence.

## Overview
This virtual narrative game challenges players to navigate a scene that is periodically frozen by a facilitator who presents two distinct, high-stakes plot directions. Players must instantly align on one path, unfreeze, and seamlessly justify the new reality. The experience is a thrilling exercise in shared narrative control, forcing players to balance character desires with macro-level story architecture.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; Justification; Game Identification; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** If this is true, what else is true?; What do they stand to lose? reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop advanced narrative architecture skills by training players to recognize pivotal story junctures, evaluate competing narrative engines, and collaboratively commit to high-stakes plot developments.

## Setup
Played in a virtual video-conferencing space. Two active players turn their cameras on to perform, while the remaining participants keep their cameras off to act as the audience. One facilitator acts as the Narrative Guide, ready to pause the action and offer structural choices. No physical props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Two players turn on their cameras to begin an open scene, establishing their characters, a clear relationship, and an initial want or problem within the first minute.
2. Once the initial platform and conflict are established, the facilitator calls Freeze to pause the action.
3. The facilitator presents two distinct, high-stakes, and conflicting narrative directions or consequences that could immediately stem from the current situation.
4. The active players have five seconds to collaboratively select one of the two paths using quick, non-verbal cues like a nod, a thumbs-up, or a single-word agreement.
5. The facilitator calls Unfreeze and the players immediately resume the scene, integrating the chosen narrative path.
6. Players must justify this new direction through their characters emotional reactions, dialogue, and immediate actions, raising the stakes of the scene.
7. After another minute of play, the facilitator freezes the scene again and offers a second, escalating set of choices that build directly on the previous decision.
8. The players select their second path, unfreeze, and drive the scene toward its climax and eventual resolution based on this final narrative engine.

## Facilitation Notes
- Facilitator Tip: Ensure the two choices offered are balanced in drama and potential. Avoid offering one boring option and one exciting option; both should present compelling dilemmas.
- Side-Coaching Cue: If players take too long to choose during the freeze, coach them with: First instinct, nod and go! to maintain momentum.
- Common Pitfall: Players ignoring the chosen path or reverting to their original plan. Fix: Remind them of if true, what else is true? and push them to immediately voice the consequences of the choice.
- Facilitator Tip: Tailor the choices to the characters established wants. If a character wants fame, offer one choice that grants it at a terrible cost, and another that destroys the chance of fame entirely.

## Variations
- Audience Choice: Instead of the facilitator generating the options, the off-camera audience uses the chat feature to suggest two conflicting paths, and the players must choose one.
- Multi-Player Crossroads: Run the scene with three or four active players, requiring a larger group mind to align on the chosen path instantly through physical consensus.
- The Silent Fork: The facilitator freezes the scene and types the two options in the chat. Players must choose and resume without speaking out loud during the freeze.

## Debrief
- How did making an explicit, macro-level plot choice feel different from your usual moment-to-moment reactive improv?
- How did the chosen narrative paths affect your character original want or motivation?
- What strategies did you use to instantly align with your partner on which path to take without talking it out?
- How did justifying an external plot twist help you discover new depth in your character relationship?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game is played virtually, ensure players are comfortable with rapid visual and audio transitions. Facilitators should avoid offering narrative choices that introduce sensitive, triggering, or non-consensual themes unless explicit boundaries have been established beforehand.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to step out of the scene and select a macro-level narrative engine, the game breaks the habit of passive, circular play. It teaches players to view their scenes structurally, understanding that every major choice must have immediate, escalating consequences. The rapid consensus mechanic strengthens group mind and active listening, proving that narrative is a shared responsibility.

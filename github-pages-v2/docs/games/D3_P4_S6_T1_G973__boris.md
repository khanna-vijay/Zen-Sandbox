# The Invisible Enforcer

> Justify absurd accusations and react to an invisible brute to survive a high-stakes interrogation.

## Overview
This high-energy, two-player scene features an interrogator accusing a suspect of increasingly bizarre crimes, backed up by an invisible, silent brute. The suspect must enthusiastically accept all accusations, justify the absurd details, and physically mime the comical physical persuasion of the unseen henchman.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Raising the Stakes; Offer Reception; Physicality & Space Work; Status Modulation
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Stakes-escalation reps; Endowment-acceptance; Object work; Status Seesaw
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** Develops rapid justification of absurd offers, high-stakes narrative commitment, and physical space work by reacting to an invisible scene partner.

## Setup
Set up two chairs in the performance space. One chair is for the suspect, while the interrogator can stand or sit opposite them. Ensure the surrounding area is clear of obstacles to allow for safe physical movement.

## How to Play
1. Assign one player as the Interrogator and the other as the Suspect.
2. Establish that an invisible, silent, and incredibly strong henchman named Bruno is standing directly behind the Suspect.
3. The Interrogator begins the scene by making a highly specific, absurd accusation without pre-determining the crime (e.g., 'We know you smuggled the radioactive cheese across the border').
4. The Suspect must immediately accept the accusation using 'yes-and' and justify why they did it, adding details that make them look even more guilty.
5. If the Suspect hesitates, denies an accusation, or fails to incriminate themselves further, the Interrogator commands the invisible henchman to 'persuade' them (e.g., 'Bruno, stretch his ears').
6. The Suspect must instantly react with high physical commitment, miming the comical, over-the-top physical punishment inflicted by the invisible henchman.
7. The Interrogator can direct the specific type of physical punishment or leave the choice of physical reaction entirely up to the Suspect's imagination.
8. The scene escalates as the Suspect confesses to wilder crimes to avoid further punishment, culminating in a dramatic, self-incriminating climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't deny! Lean into the guilt. Make yourself look worse!'
- Coaching cue: 'Sell the physical contact. If Bruno grabs your collar, show the tension in your neck and shoulders.'
- Pitfall: Suspects sometimes play the physical pain too realistically, which dampens the comedy. Fix: Remind them to play it as a cartoonish melodrama where the physical reactions are highly stylized.
- Coaching cue: 'Interrogators, don't just ask questions. Throw out wild, specific accusations for the suspect to justify.'

## Variations
- The Accomplice Twist: The suspect attempts to shift the blame onto the invisible henchman, accusing them of being the true mastermind, forcing the interrogator to negotiate with their own muscle.
- The Double Threat: If the suspect is proving too tough to crack, the interrogator calls in the henchman's even more terrifying sibling for double the invisible physical comedy.
- Good Cop, Bad Cop, Invisible Cop: Add a third player as a sympathetic co-interrogator who tries to protect the suspect from the invisible brute, creating a classic status dynamic.

## Debrief
- How did accepting the absurd accusations immediately help build the narrative momentum?
- What did you notice about the relationship between physical commitment (miming the henchman) and your vocal delivery?
- How does making yourself look more guilty serve the story better than trying to defend yourself?

## Safety & Inclusion
Although the game involves mimed physical violence, players must maintain complete control over their own bodies. No actual physical contact should occur between the two active players. Ensure the playing area is clear of tripping hazards, and encourage players to scale the physical comedy to their own comfort and mobility levels.

## Why It Works
By removing the option to deny, the game forces players to practice radical justification. The physical comedy of the invisible henchman acts as an instant feedback loop: any hesitation is met with a physical challenge, keeping the energy high and bypassing the analytical brain to access pure, committed instinct.

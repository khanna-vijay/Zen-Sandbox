# Character Swap

> Step directly into your partner's physical and vocal shoes mid-scene to master deep physical empathy.

## Overview
Two players initiate a scene with bold, distinct physical and vocal choices. At the facilitator's signal, they instantly swap roles, adopting each other's posture, voice, and perspective to continue the scene seamlessly. This high-focus exercise demands intense observation and celebrates the creative choices of your partner.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Active Listening; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise; Character Walks/Centers; Vocal characterization
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep physical empathy, active listening, and precise mirroring skills by embodying a partner's unique character choices mid-scene.

## Setup
An open performance space. Two players stand in the playing area, while the rest of the group observes as active audience members. No props or chairs are needed, though one or two chairs can be used if the scene demands it.

## How to Play
1. Pair up two players and have them stand in the performance space.
2. Instruct the players to begin a standard two-person scene, immediately establishing distinct, bold physical postures, vocal cadences, and emotional viewpoints.
3. Allow the players to interact for about 60 to 90 seconds, ensuring both characters become fully defined and recognizable to the audience and each other.
4. At a high point of character clarity, call out 'Swap!' or clap your hands to pause the action.
5. Upon hearing the cue, both players must instantly freeze, observe their partner, and adopt the exact physical posture, facial expression, and spatial position of their partner.
6. Resume the scene immediately with the players now speaking with the other's voice, holding their physical tension, and defending their character's point of view.
7. Let the swapped scene run for another 60 seconds to allow players to settle into the new physicality and vocal patterns before calling 'Scene' to end the round.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Observe the details! Look at their hands, their spine, where they hold their weight, and listen to their vocal rhythm before the swap.'
- Common Pitfall: Players caricature or mock their partner's choices during the swap. Fix: Remind them of 'Make Your Partner a Genius'—the goal is to honor and elevate their partner's creation, not parody it.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't just swap voices; swap the emotional engine. Why does this character stand this way?'
- Common Pitfall: Players lose the narrative thread or relationship during the swap. Fix: Encourage them to continue the exact conversation they were having, just from the opposite side of the table.

## Variations
- Continuous Swaps: The facilitator calls 'Swap!' multiple times throughout a single scene, forcing players to pass the characters back and forth rapidly.
- The Silent Swap: Instead of a verbal cue, the players must mutually decide when to swap based on a physical trigger or a shared moment of silence.
- Group Swap: Run a three- or four-person scene where players rotate characters clockwise on the facilitator's cue.

## Debrief
- What did you notice about your partner's character only after you had to physically embody it?
- How did it feel to see your own character choices mirrored back to you by your partner?
- How does paying this level of attention to your partner's physical and vocal choices change how you support them in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical safety and personal boundaries when mirroring postures. If a player has physical limitations, their partner should mirror the essence or energy of the choice rather than attempting movements that cause discomfort or risk injury.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to step into their partner's physical and vocal patterns, the game operationalizes 'Make Your Partner a Genius.' It transforms passive listening into active physical empathy. Mirroring your partner's physical state naturally triggers the corresponding emotional state, proving that physical choices drive internal character depth.

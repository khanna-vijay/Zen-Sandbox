# The Monarch's Favor

> Win the approval of a fickle ruler through high-status gifting and dramatic, joyful failures.

## Overview
One player sits as a highly demanding Monarch, while the rest of the group acts as subjects entering the court to win their favor with gifts, news, or services. The Monarch responds with instant, arbitrary commands—'Proceed', 'Freeze', or 'Banish'—forcing players to adapt their physical and verbal offers on the fly. The game ends when a subject successfully navigates the court's whims to physically tap the Monarch's shoulder.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Status Modulation; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Status Seesaw; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To practice active gifting and endowment under pressure, learning to treat every rejection as a gift that defines the scene's reality and elevates your partner's status.

## Setup
Set up a single chair at one end of the playing space to serve as the throne. The player designated as the Monarch sits on the throne. The remaining players line up at the opposite end of the room, acting as the queue of hopeful courtiers.

## How to Play
1. Designate one player to sit on the throne as the Monarch, establishing an attitude of extreme high status and specific, unspoken preferences.
2. The remaining players line up at the back of the room. One at a time, a courtier steps forward and enters the court, immediately presenting a physical or verbal offer (e.g., bringing a tray of imaginary food, delivering urgent news, or performing a dance).
3. The Monarch must instantly react to the courtier's entrance using one of three commands: 'Proceed' (continue moving forward), 'Freeze' (stop in place immediately), or 'Banish' (the offer is rejected, and the player must dramatically exit or collapse).
4. When issuing a 'Banish' command, the Monarch must endow the courtier with a specific reason that reveals the Monarch's character or preferences (e.g., 'I despise the smell of lavender, banish them!').
5. Courtiers who are told to 'Freeze' must hold their physical posture perfectly until the Monarch addresses them again or commands them to 'Proceed'.
6. Courtiers who are told to 'Proceed' must continue their approach, adapting their offer based on any feedback or endowments the Monarch has dropped.
7. The Monarch can manage multiple courtiers in the space at once, freezing some while allowing others to advance or banishing them in quick succession.
8. The round ends when a courtier successfully reaches the throne and gently taps the Monarch on the shoulder, at which point that courtier becomes the new Monarch for the next round.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage the Monarch to make rapid, decisive judgments. Hesitation slows down the momentum; arbitrary cruelty is funnier and keeps the energy high.
- Side-coach the courtiers to 'Fail Joyfully' when banished. A dramatic, theatrical exit or collapse keeps the energy positive and celebrates the rejection.
- Remind the Monarch to use specific endowments. Instead of just saying 'Banish,' say 'Banish! Your boots squeak too loudly!' This gives the next player a clue about what the Monarch hates or loves.
- Watch out for players rushing the throne. Ensure they respect the 'Freeze' command instantly to maintain the physical safety and theatrical tension of the game.

## Variations
- Silent Court: The entire game is played in gibberish or complete silence, relying purely on physical offers, pantomime, and physicalized commands.
- The Cooperative Court: Courtiers can enter in pairs or trios, sharing the risk. If one is banished, both must exit, forcing them to support each other's physical offers.
- Status Reverse: The Monarch is incredibly low-status and insecure, and the courtiers must enter with high-status demands, trying to make the Monarch comfortable enough to let them approach.

## Debrief
- How did the Monarch's specific reasons for banishment help the next players in line shape their offers?
- What did it feel like to be banished? How can we bring that sense of 'joyful failure' into our regular scenic work?
- How did physicalizing the 'Freeze' command help build the tension and status dynamic in the room?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since the objective is to touch the Monarch's shoulder, establish a clear boundary before playing: all physical contact must be a gentle, respectful tap on the shoulder. Players can also opt for a 'virtual touch' (pointing from a safe distance of two feet and saying 'Touch') if physical contact is uncomfortable.

## Why It Works
This game works because it gamifies rejection, turning a 'No' into a highly specific endowment that builds the world. By forcing the Monarch to give reasons for banishment, it trains active gifting and listening. The courtiers learn to treat the Monarch's arbitrary whims as absolute truth, instantly making their partner look like a genius ruler while modulating their own status to match.

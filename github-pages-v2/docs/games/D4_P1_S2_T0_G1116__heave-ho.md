# The Canopy Lift

> A high-trust physical exercise in total group support and collective physical coordination.

## Overview
Players form a double-line physical zipper with their arms, creating a secure human platform. One by one, active players lie back and are gently passed down the line from hand to hand, experiencing absolute support while the group practices intense physical focus and collective responsibility.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Consent & Boundaries
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Support Work; Boundary Navigation
- **Technique(s):** Negotiating physical contact
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To build deep physical trust, non-verbal group coordination, and a literal experience of supporting and being supported by the ensemble.

## Setup
A large, open room with clear floors. All players must remove shoes, jewelry, watches, and empty their pockets to prevent injury or snagging.

## How to Play
1. Form two parallel lines facing each other, standing shoulder-to-shoulder, with about two feet of space between the lines.
2. Have players extend their arms forward, palms up, alternating arms with the person opposite them to create a dense, interlocking zipper-like platform of forearms.
3. Ensure everyone bends their knees slightly, establishing a strong, athletic stance to distribute weight safely.
4. Designate one player at the end of the line to be the traveler and two spotters at the far end to assist with a safe landing.
5. The traveler stands at the start of the line, turns their back to the line, stiffens their body like a plank with arms crossed over their chest, and verbally asks, 'Ready to support?'
6. The line responds in unison, 'Ready to support!', after which the traveler gently leans backward onto the first set of hands.
7. The group uses a smooth, coordinated hand-to-hand passing motion to slide the traveler down the canopy of arms, keeping them horizontal and level.
8. Once the traveler reaches the end, the spotters help them stand upright onto their feet safely.
9. The traveler then joins the end of the line, and the next person at the front takes a turn.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your knees bent and use your legs, not just your back, to support the weight.'
- Coaching cue: 'Keep the traveler level—do not let their head dip lower than their feet.'
- Pitfall: Players might try to throw or toss the traveler. Fix: Emphasize a smooth, rolling, conveyor-belt motion rather than a throwing motion to maintain control.
- Pitfall: The traveler bending at the waist. Fix: Remind the traveler to keep their core tight and body straight like a wooden plank.

## Variations
- Silent Conveyor: Run the entire exercise in complete silence, relying purely on breath and physical connection to coordinate the movement.
- The Slow-Motion Glide: Challenge the group to pass the traveler as slowly and gently as possible, testing endurance and micro-adjustments.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to completely surrender control and trust the group to hold you up?
- What physical cues did you have to pay attention to in order to keep the traveler safe and balanced?
- How does this literal physical support translate to how we support each other's choices on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
This is a highly physical, contact-heavy exercise. Participation must be entirely voluntary. Anyone who prefers not to be lifted or to lift can serve as a dedicated spotter, safety caller, or anchor. Ensure proper lifting techniques (bend knees, straight back) are taught beforehand. Respect personal boundaries regarding physical touch.

## Why It Works
It makes the abstract concept of support entirely literal. By physically holding up a teammate, players experience the immediate, high-stakes consequence of breaking focus. It forces the group to act as a single organism where every individual's physical engagement is crucial to the safety of the whole.

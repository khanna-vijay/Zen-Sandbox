# The Duck Chorus

> A rhythmic, cumulative counting game that demands intense focus, active listening, and collective recovery.

## Overview
Players stand in a circle and collaboratively build a rhythmic sequence about ducks, their legs, and their quacks. Each player contributes exactly one word or sound in turn, with the numbers multiplying and accumulating as the round progresses. It is a high-focus, fast-paced warm-up that highlights group mind and celebrates mistakes.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle facing inward. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle with all players facing inward.
2. Explain the basic pattern sequence: '[Number] Ducks', '[Double that Number] Legs', and '[Number] Quacks'.
3. Clarify that each player in the circle contributes exactly one word or one sound at a time, moving clockwise.
4. Walk through the first cycle slowly: Player 1 says 'One', Player 2 says 'Duck', Player 3 says 'Two', Player 4 says 'Legs', Player 5 says 'Quack'.
5. Move immediately into the second cycle: Player 6 says 'Two', Player 7 says 'Ducks', Player 8 says 'Four', Player 9 says 'Legs', Player 10 says 'Quack', Player 11 says 'Quack'.
6. Continue to the third cycle: 'Three' 'Ducks' 'Six' 'Legs' 'Quack' 'Quack' 'Quack' (each word/quack spoken by a single player in sequence).
7. If anyone hesitates, says the wrong number, misses a 'quack', or speaks out of turn, the entire group celebrates the mistake with a collective cheer, and the next player starts over at cycle one ('One').
8. Challenge the group to see how high they can collectively count without breaking the rhythm or the pattern.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Keep the beat steady! Don't let the math slow down the rhythm.'
- Coaching cue: 'Support your neighbors. Anticipate your turn by tracking the math ahead of time.'
- Pitfall: Players get frustrated when a mistake is made. Fix: Encourage a 'Fail Joyfully' response—raise hands in the air, cheer, and immediately start the next round with high energy.
- Pitfall: The rhythm drags during the 'Quack' sequences. Fix: Encourage players to treat the quacks like a rapid-fire drumbeat, keeping the tempo crisp.

## Variations
- The Spider Spin: Replace ducks with spiders (8 legs) and 'Spin' sounds (e.g., 'One Spider, Eight Legs, Spin' -> 'Two Spiders, Sixteen Legs, Spin Spin').
- Reverse Direction: Change the direction of the circle (counter-clockwise) every time a mistake is made and the count resets.
- Silent Pulse: Play the game without a spoken beat, relying entirely on eye contact and physical nods to pass the turn instead of going in strict circle order.

## Debrief
- How did the group's energy change when we celebrated mistakes instead of mourning them?
- What strategies did you use to keep track of the math while maintaining the rhythm?
- How does peripheral awareness help you anticipate your turn before it actually arrives?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players who struggle with rapid mental math or auditory processing feel supported; emphasize that mistakes are the fun part of the game, not a failure. Allow players to pass or assist each other non-verbally if needed.

## Why It Works
This game forces players to balance individual cognitive load (doing the math) with collective awareness (maintaining the rhythm). Because the pattern is highly structured, any deviation is immediately obvious, making it a perfect tool for practicing 'failing joyfully' and building a unified group mind.

# Attunement Waves

> Progress from silent physical mirroring to emotional echoes and dynamic status-shifting scene play.

## Overview
A three-phase partner exercise that systematically builds deep interpersonal connection. Players transition from silent physical synchronization to structured emotional validation, culminating in a spoken scene where they consciously manipulate and balance status dynamics.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Assume Competence; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Status Modulation; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Emotional Fluidity
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise; Emotional-echo drills; Meisner Repetition; Yes, And… sentence games; Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment-acceptance; Status Seesaw; Give them the answer
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep partner empathy, active listening, and responsive status modulation by layering physical, emotional, and verbal attunement.

## Setup
An open, quiet room with enough space for pairs to stand facing each other at arm's length without crowding. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have partners stand facing each other, maintaining soft, steady eye contact in silence.
2. Phase 1 (Silent Mirroring): Instruct pairs to begin moving together without a designated leader or follower, aiming for a fluid, shared physical state where every movement is mutually initiated.
3. Phase 2 (Emotional Echo): Transition to verbal play where Partner A shares a brief, honest statement about their current internal state or an observation of their partner.
4. Partner B must receive the statement, echo it back exactly, and then add their own present-moment emotional statement (for example: 'You feel a bit tense. I feel a sense of calm expectation').
5. Continue this structured emotional exchange back and forth for several minutes, maintaining the physical connection and eye contact established in Phase 1.
6. Phase 3 (Status Weave): Introduce a simple, low-stakes scene context and have partners begin a spoken scene.
7. In the scene, players must use their partner's last words to start their own line, while consciously choosing to either accept, elevate, or lower the status dynamic.
8. Players must actively gift status to their partner (making them the expert or authority) or gracefully accept gifted status to keep the power dynamic fluid and collaborative.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: During Phase 1, if one person is clearly leading, call out: 'Dissolve the leader. Let the movement live in the space between you.'
- Pitfall: In Phase 2, players often overthink or invent dramatic emotions. Fix: Remind them to report simple, honest physical or emotional sensations like 'My shoulders feel heavy.'
- Coaching Cue: In Phase 3, encourage physical status work. Remind players that status is expressed through posture, eye contact, and physical space, not just verbal sparring.
- Pitfall: Players treating status as a competition to win. Fix: Remind them that the goal is to make their partner look brilliant by accepting their status offers and playing into them.

## Variations
- Speed Attunement: Run each phase for only 60 seconds to practice rapid, high-intensity connection.
- Status Seesaw: In Phase 3, require players to alternate status roles with every single line exchange (high to low, low to high).

## Debrief
- How did the physical attunement in Phase 1 change how you listened to your partner's words in Phase 2?
- What did it feel like to have your emotional state echoed back to you before your partner responded?
- How did gifting status to your partner make them look like a genius, and how did it affect the flow of the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they can share simple, low-stakes physical sensations (like 'My feet feel warm') if sharing deep emotional states feels too vulnerable. Respect physical boundaries during the mirroring phase.

## Why It Works
By scaffolding the interaction from non-verbal to emotional, and finally to narrative status, the game bypasses intellectual overthinking. It forces players to ground their scene work in the actual, present-moment reality of their partner, making 'Yes, And' a physical and emotional truth rather than just a cognitive rule.

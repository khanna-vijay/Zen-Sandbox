# The Pecking Order

> Establish a non-verbal, physical hierarchy of geese through status walks and active listening.

## Overview
Players embody a flock of geese, using physical status walks, posture, and non-verbal vocalizations to negotiate a complete social hierarchy. Without speaking, the group must dynamically discover who is dominant, who is submissive, and where every single player fits in the line from highest to lowest status.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Status Modulation; Active Listening; Physicality & Space Work; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** High/low-status walks; Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** Develops status modulation, physical characterization, and peripheral awareness by requiring players to read and adapt to subtle physical cues to establish a collective hierarchy.

## Setup
An open room with enough space for all players to walk around freely. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to disperse throughout the space and begin moving around as geese, using only non-verbal sounds and physical movements.
2. Explain that the flock has a strict, linear hierarchy from the absolute highest-status goose to the lowest-status goose.
3. Players must use high-status and low-status walks, eye contact, posture, and spatial relationships to negotiate their relative positions in the hierarchy.
4. No English words or explicit numbering gestures are allowed; players must rely entirely on physical status modulation and active listening to the flock's dynamics.
5. As players interact, they must dynamically adjust their status relative to each individual they encounter, finding their specific place where they are higher than some and lower than others.
6. Once the flock reaches a collective, intuitive consensus, the facilitator calls for them to form a single file line from highest status to lowest status.
7. Once lined up, players reveal their perceived positions, and the group discusses how successfully they communicated and read the non-verbal hierarchy.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to exaggerate their physical status: use height, eye contact, and the speed of movement to show dominance or submission.
- Watch out for players trying to use fingers to show numbers or mouthing words. Remind them that status is felt and seen through posture, space, and attitude.
- If two players are clashing for the same spot, encourage one to yield. Emphasize making your partner look great by accepting their high status or elevating their low status.
- Remind the group that playing a low-status goose is just as vital and fun as playing the leader; a hierarchy needs all levels to exist.

## Variations
- Silent Flock: Remove all vocalizations entirely, forcing players to rely solely on physical walks, eye contact, and spatial distance.
- Secret Numbers: Assign each player a secret number from 1 to N beforehand. They must act out that exact status level physically and find their order without revealing their number.
- Corporate Hierarchy: Shift the setting from geese to an office environment, using corporate archetypes from CEO to unpaid intern with subtle physical status walks.

## Debrief
- How did you know when someone was higher or lower status than you without them speaking?
- What physical adjustments did you make to make your partner look more dominant or submissive?
- How did peripheral awareness help you understand the overall structure of the flock beyond your immediate partner?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries while moving through the space. Since geese can be aggressive, emphasize that pecking is strictly physical theater and no actual physical contact or crowding should occur.

## Why It Works
By stripping away verbal communication, players are forced to use physical status modulation to communicate. It embodies the principle of making your partner a genius because a player cannot establish high status unless their partner actively chooses to play low status, creating a collaborative, physical dialogue.

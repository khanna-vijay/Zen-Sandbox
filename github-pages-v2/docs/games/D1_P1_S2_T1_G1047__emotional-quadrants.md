# Emotional Quadrants

> Navigate a physically mapped stage where shifting locations instantly triggers extreme emotional transformations.

## Overview
Players perform a scene on a stage divided into four invisible quadrants, each assigned a distinct emotion. As players move across these physical boundaries, they must instantly adopt the corresponding emotion, justifying the sudden shift within the narrative. This high-energy drill trains actors to commit fully to rapid emotional transitions without losing narrative coherence.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Justification
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Character Walks/Centers; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop emotional fluidity and physical commitment by forcing players to instantly shift their emotional state based on their physical location, while practicing the art of logical justification.

## Setup
Divide the playing space into four equal quadrants (front-left, front-right, back-left, back-right) using tape, chairs, or imaginary lines. Assign one distinct, high-contrast emotion to each quadrant (e.g., Joy, Anger, Fear, Sadness). Ensure the players know which quadrant represents which emotion before starting.

## How to Play
1. Position two to four players on the stage, with each player starting in a different quadrant.
2. Get a simple scene suggestion from the group (e.g., a location or a relationship) to initiate the scene.
3. Begin the scene with players adopting the emotion of the quadrant they currently occupy.
4. As the scene progresses, players must physically move around the stage, crossing the boundaries into different quadrants.
5. The moment a player crosses a boundary, they must instantly dial up the new quadrant's emotion to a high intensity (aiming for an 8 to 10 on the emotional dial).
6. Players must immediately justify why their emotion changed so suddenly, integrating the shift seamlessly into the scene's reality.
7. Encourage players to cross boundaries frequently, forcing rapid-fire emotional handoffs and physical adjustments.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to 'dial up' the emotion immediately upon crossing the line, avoiding a slow fade-in. The transition should be sharp and committed.
- Pitfall: Players staying static to avoid changing emotions. Fix: Side-coach with 'Find a reason to move!' or physically nudge them across a line.
- Pitfall: Losing the scene's plot in favor of random emotional outbursts. Fix: Remind players to justify the shift (e.g., 'I'm suddenly angry because of how you just looked at me').
- Encourage physicalization: ask players how anger lives in the chest versus how sadness affects the shoulders and posture.

## Variations
- The Dial Up: Start the scene with emotions at level 2, and increase the intensity of all quadrants by 2 points every minute until they reach level 10.
- Secret Quadrants: The audience knows the emotions assigned to each quadrant, but the players do not and must discover them through side-coaching or audience reaction.
- Status Quadrants: Instead of emotions, assign different social status levels (1 to 10) to each quadrant.

## Debrief
- How did physical movement help trigger a genuine emotional response?
- What strategies did you use to justify a sudden, extreme shift in emotion without breaking the reality of the scene?
- How does committing 100% to a sudden emotion make the scene more compelling for the audience?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since players are moving dynamically while experiencing high-intensity emotions, ensure the physical boundaries are clear of tripping hazards. Remind players to maintain physical safety and respect personal boundaries, especially when portraying high-energy emotions like anger or fear.

## Why It Works
By tying emotional states to physical locations, the game bypasses intellectual overthinking. The physical act of crossing a line acts as an external trigger, forcing the player to commit 100% to a new state. Justifying these sudden shifts on the fly builds strong narrative muscles, proving that any emotional choice can make sense if fully embraced.

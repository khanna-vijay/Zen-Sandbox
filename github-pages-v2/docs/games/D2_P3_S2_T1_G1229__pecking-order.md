# Secret Status Scale

> Discover your place in a hidden social hierarchy through dynamic, real-time status adjustments.

## Overview
In this scene-based exercise, players secretly select a status rank from one to the number of active participants. As the scene unfolds, players must physically and verbally negotiate their relative power dynamics without explicitly revealing their numbers, creating a fluid status seesaw. The comedy and drama emerge from the natural friction of misaligned social expectations.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Status Modulation; Active Listening; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Status Seesaw; High/low-status walks; Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To master status modulation and active listening by dynamically adjusting one's own status in response to others, practicing the status seesaw technique to make scene partners look brilliant.

## Setup
Clear a moderate performance space. Have 3 to 5 players stand in the playing area while the remaining participants act as observers. No props are required, though chairs can be used to establish physical levels.

## How to Play
1. Determine the number of active players in the scene (for example, four players).
2. Each player secretly chooses a whole number from 1 to the total number of players, where 1 represents the absolute highest status (most dominant) and the highest number represents the lowest status (most deferential).
3. Duplicate numbers are allowed and highly encouraged, but players must keep their chosen number strictly secret from one another.
4. Get a simple location or relationship suggestion from the group to initiate a realistic, grounded scene.
5. Begin the scene, with each player embodying their chosen status through posture, vocal tone, eye contact, and spatial relationships.
6. As the scene progresses, players must actively observe their partners to deduce where others stand on the scale, adjusting their own behavior to maintain the status seesaw.
7. If a player realizes their partner is playing a similar status, they must negotiate that tension theatrically rather than breaking character.
8. After a few minutes of play, the facilitator freezes the scene and asks the players to line up in order of what they believe their status rank was, from highest to lowest, before revealing their actual secret numbers.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Show, don't tell. Use your physical space, eye contact, and response times to establish your rank rather than just talking about power.'
- Pitfall: Players playing too extreme or cartoonish. Fix: Remind them that high status can be quiet and low status can be talkative; focus on subtle shifts in posture and eye contact.
- Coaching cue: 'If you notice two people have chosen the same number, play the tension of that tie. How do two dominant personalities negotiate sharing the top spot?'
- Pitfall: Ignoring the partner's status choices. Fix: Side-coach: 'Make your partner's status choice correct. If they treat you like a servant, accept it and find your status within that constraint.'

## Variations
- Physical Only: Run the scene entirely in gibberish or silently to emphasize body language, spatial distance, and eye contact over dialogue.
- Dynamic Shifts: Halfway through the scene, the facilitator calls out 'Shift!', and players must secretly increment or decrement their status number by one.
- The Underdog: One player is assigned a low status but must try to covertly usurp the high-status player through passive-aggressive tactics.

## Debrief
- How did you determine where your partner sat on the status scale? What physical or verbal cues gave it away?
- What happened when two players held the same status number? How did you resolve or play that tension?
- How does elevating or lowering your own status help make your partner look like a genius?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players understand that high status does not mean physical intimidation or violating personal boundaries. Encourage status to be played through spatial distance, eye contact, and vocal tone rather than physical crowding or aggressive posturing.

## Why It Works
This game forces players to abandon pre-planned lines and focus entirely on the relationship. By using the status seesaw, players learn that status is co-created; you cannot be high status unless your partner plays low, and vice versa. This directly trains active listening and the principle of making your partner look good.

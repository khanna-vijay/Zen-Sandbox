## 2. 🎲 Today's games

#### Warm-up — Dynamic Greetings

> Transform simple hellos into high-stakes relationships through instant physical and emotional endowments.

![Dynamic Greetings — infographic](../../games/img/D2_P3_S5_T1_G1101__greetings.jpg){ .infographic }

`Players 4+` · `~10 min` · `Complexity 1/5` · `Energy medium` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Active Gifting · _connection_

**How to play**

1. Instruct all players to walk around the room at a moderate pace, filling the empty spaces and maintaining soft eye contact with others.
2. Begin with a neutral baseline: players must greet whoever they cross paths with using a simple, polite nod or a brief, standard handshake.
3. Introduce the first relationship prompt, such as: Greet each other like long-lost best friends who haven't seen each other in ten years.
4. Instruct players to immediately commit to this dynamic with the very next person they meet, using their whole body, facial expressions, and a brief verbal exchange.
5. Emphasize that players must gift their partner by treating them as the exact person described in the prompt, making their partner's reaction easy and natural.
6. After 30 to 45 seconds of interaction, call out 'Keep moving!' to have players resume walking neutrally until the next prompt is delivered.
7. Cycle through a series of contrasting prompts that vary in emotional temperature, status, and history, such as: someone you deeply distrust, someone you have a massive secret crush on, or your absolute hero.
8. End the exercise by returning to a neutral walk, allowing players to shake off the high-intensity emotions and ground themselves.

#### Core game — Retroactive Brilliance

> Transform your partner's baffling choices into strokes of absolute, intentional genius.

![Retroactive Brilliance — infographic](../../games/img/D2_P3_S5_T2_G497__the-alchemist-s-justification.jpg){ .infographic }

`Players 2+` · `~10 min` · `Complexity 2/5` · `Energy medium` · `Props: none`

**Trains:** Active Gifting · _mixed_

**How to play**

1. Divide the group into pairs, with one pair stepping forward to perform a scene while others observe.
2. Designate Player A as the 'Oracle' and Player B as the 'Alchemist'.
3. Player A begins the scene by making a single, highly specific, and uncontextualized physical or verbal offer, such as placing a shoe on a table and saluting it.
4. Player B must immediately accept this offer without hesitation, treating it not as a mistake or a joke, but as a deeply intelligent, strategic, or necessary action.
5. Player B then delivers a justification that 'gives them the answer'—explaining the brilliant context behind Player A's action, such as declaring the shoe is a calibrated sensor blocking a satellite.
6. Player A enthusiastically accepts this high-status endowment, fully embodying the genius, expertise, or strategic foresight that Player B has gifted them.
7. The players continue the scene for one to two minutes, exploring the implications of this newly established reality and maintaining the elevated status of Player A.
8. After the scene concludes, players swap roles and repeat the exercise with a new initial offer.

## 3. 💭 Self-reflection

**Deepen your improv**
1. How did your partner's physical posture and facial expression make it easier for you to know how to react?
2. What did it feel like to receive a high-status or high-affection greeting? How did that make you feel like a genius?

**Beyond the stage**
3. Making your partner a genius means setting others up to shine. Whose work could you actively gift this week — a name, a credit, an easier next step?

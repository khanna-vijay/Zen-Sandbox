# Rapid-Fire Goalie

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Defend the net by instantly accepting and returning high-speed character endowments.

## Overview
One player stands alone as the Goalie while a line of teammates bombards them with rapid-fire opening lines. The Goalie must instantly accept every character endowment, snap into a complementary persona, and deliver a sharp response before facing the next shot. It is a high-energy drill designed to bypass overthinking and build immediate commitment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop rapid offer reception and instant endowment-acceptance, training players to trust their first instinct and immediately commit to a relationship and character dynamic.

## Setup
Players stand in a semi-circle or a straight line facing a single player (the Goalie) who stands about six to eight feet away. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. Designate one player to step forward and act as the Goalie, facing the rest of the group.
2. Instruct the remaining players to each quietly prepare a single, strong opening line of dialogue that clearly endows the Goalie with a specific identity, relationship, or emotional state.
3. The first player in line steps forward slightly and delivers their opening line directly to the Goalie with high energy.
4. The Goalie must instantly accept the endowment, physically and vocally snap into the implied character, and deliver a single, committed response that saves the offer.
5. As soon as the Goalie delivers their response, the next player in line immediately fires their opening line, giving the Goalie no time to reset or overthink.
6. The Goalie continues to defend the goal by responding to each player in succession until they have faced everyone in the line.
7. Rotate a new player into the Goalie position and repeat the process with fresh opening lines.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the Goalie to react physically before speaking; changing posture or facial expression helps bypass cognitive blocks.
- Keep the tempo extremely fast. If a Goalie hesitates, gently call out 'Shoot!' to prompt the next offer and keep the momentum moving.
- Pitfall: Goalies trying to be clever or funny, which causes lag. Fix: Remind them that the most obvious, immediate response is always the best one.
- Pitfall: Line players offering vague lines like 'Hey, what's up?'. Fix: Coach the line to make specific, high-stakes endowments that give the Goalie something clear to react to.

## Variations
- Physical Endowments: Instead of spoken lines, the line players approach the Goalie with a distinct physical gesture or posture, and the Goalie must instantly mirror or complement the physical offer.
- Emotional Tag-Team: The line players deliver their lines with a heavy emotional state, and the Goalie must instantly match or contrast that emotional intensity.
- Two-Line Exchanges: Allow a brief, rapid three-beat exchange (Offer, Response, Counter-response) before moving to the next player to practice sustaining the character for an extra beat.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have no time to plan your responses? Did your body or your brain react first?
- What made some offers easier to accept and respond to than others?
- How does physicalizing a character immediately help you find their voice?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players maintain physical safety by keeping a respectful distance during high-energy deliveries. Encourage players to use vocal and facial expressions rather than sudden physical lunges toward the Goalie.

## Why It Works
By bombarding the player with rapid-fire offers, the game forces the analytical mind to shut down, leaving only the intuitive, spontaneous self. It reinforces 'Yes, And' by making immediate acceptance the only path to survival, proving that any committed choice is a good choice.

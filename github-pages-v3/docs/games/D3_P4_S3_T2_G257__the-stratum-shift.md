# The Stratum Shift

> Escalate scene stakes and master narrative structure through timed, layered character secrets.

## Overview
A structured scene-work exercise where two players begin with hidden, asymmetrical information. As the facilitator calls out specific narrative layers, players must systematically reveal their secrets, forcing immediate justification, dramatic shifts in stakes, and a powerful demonstration of platform and tilt.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; Justification; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; If this is true, what else is true?; Justify the absurd; Stakes-escalation reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop narrative architecture skills by practicing the Platform/Tilt technique, teaching players how to introduce disruptive information (tilts) and immediately justify them to serve the broader story.

## Setup
An in-person performance space. The facilitator needs a quiet way to whisper or privately brief two players before the scene starts. No props or physical materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Select two players to perform a scene and ask the group for a location and a pre-existing relationship to establish the platform.
2. Privately brief Player A with a minor, personal secret that influences their behavior but isn't immediately obvious (e.g., they lost a shared item).
3. Privately brief Player B with a more significant, escalating truth related to Player A's situation (e.g., they know Player A lost it because they stole it back).
4. Instruct both players to begin the scene, establishing their relationship and environment while letting their hidden information subtly color their choices.
5. After a solid platform is established (approx. 1-2 minutes), call out 'Stratum One!' to signal Player A to organically reveal their secret.
6. Direct Player B to react to this first reveal, using their own hidden knowledge to heighten the tension without yet revealing their own secret.
7. Once the first reveal is integrated, call out 'Stratum Two!' to signal Player B to reveal their escalating truth, completely re-contextualizing the scene.
8. Have Player A react honestly, immediately justifying their character's past actions and adapting to this new, high-stakes reality.
9. Allow the scene to play out for another minute to explore the fallout of both revelations before calling 'Scene!'

## Facilitation Notes
- Ensure the secrets provided are logically connected; Player B's truth must directly escalate or re-contextualize Player A's secret rather than being a random, unrelated shock.
- Side-coach players to avoid rushing the reveals. The initial phase must establish a normal 'platform' so the 'tilts' have maximum dramatic impact.
- If a player struggles to reveal their secret organically upon the cue, side-coach them with: 'Find a way to confess this right now.'
- Watch out for denial. If Player A reacts to Stratum Two by saying 'That's not true!', pause the scene and remind them of 'Yes, And'—they must accept the new reality as absolute truth.

## Variations
- Blind Stratum: The facilitator does not pre-brief the players. Instead, when 'Stratum One' is called, Player A must invent a secret on the spot, and when 'Stratum Two' is called, Player B must invent an escalating truth that perfectly connects to it.
- Multi-Player Stratum: Run the scene with three players, adding a 'Stratum Three' where the third player reveals a secret that connects and escalates both previous reveals.

## Debrief
- How did having a secret affect your physical and verbal choices before the reveal actually happened?
- For Player A, how did it feel to have your confession immediately overshadowed by a much larger truth?
- How did the structured 'tilts' help or hinder your ability to build a cohesive narrative arc?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because secrets can easily veer into heavy or sensitive personal territory, the facilitator should establish boundaries beforehand (e.g., 'keep secrets PG-13 and avoid themes of severe trauma or abuse') to maintain a safe, collaborative creative space.

## Why It Works
By separating the 'platform' (the baseline setup) from the 'tilts' (the structured reveals), this game physically maps out narrative architecture. It forces players to practice active listening and immediate justification, proving that any sudden plot twist can serve the story if players commit to 'Yes, And' and explore the logical consequences of 'if this is true, what else is true?'

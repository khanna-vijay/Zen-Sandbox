# Undercurrents

![Undercurrents — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T1_G522__undercurrents-confirmation.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Navigate hidden agendas and adapt to shifting emotional subtext in real time.

## Overview
In this exercise, two players improvise a scene while holding secret, non-verbal agendas. A third player acts as an active facilitator, signaling players to silently swap their hidden motives once a subtextual connection is successfully made and received.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Assume Competence; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Status Modulation; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring
- **Technique(s):** Meisner Repetition; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Give them the answer; Status Seesaw
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops active listening, subtextual offer reception, and Meisner-style hyper-observation by training players to respond to physical and emotional undercurrents rather than literal dialogue.

## Setup
Divide players into groups of three (triads). Prepare a set of index cards containing simple, non-obvious social agendas (e.g., 'subtly seek approval', 'quietly assert dominance', 'avoid physical proximity'). Each active player receives one initial card, while the facilitator holds the remaining stack.

## How to Play
1. Assign roles within each triad: Player A, Player B, and the Facilitator.
2. The Facilitator establishes a neutral, everyday scenario and relationship, such as two neighbors discussing a shared fence.
3. Player A and Player B each read their initial secret agenda card, keeping their objectives hidden from each other.
4. The players begin the scene, focusing on embodying their agendas through body language, vocal tone, and subtext rather than explicit statements.
5. Both players practice hyper-vigilant observation, treating every physical shift, pause, and vocal inflection from their partner as a meaningful offer.
6. The Facilitator closely monitors the interaction, looking for a moment where one player projects their subtext and the other clearly registers and adapts to it.
7. Once this subtextual connection is made, the Facilitator gives a subtle, pre-arranged visual cue, such as a silent nod or a double-tap on the shoulder, to the player who successfully communicated their agenda.
8. Upon receiving the cue, that player discreetly takes a new agenda card from the Facilitator and immediately shifts their behavior to embody the new objective.
9. The scene continues seamlessly, requiring the partner to dynamically adapt to the newly shifted emotional landscape without breaking the reality of the scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Remind players that the goal is not to guess the literal words on their partner's card, but to feel and react to the behavioral shift.
- If players become too explicit or start verbally hinting at their agenda, side-coach them to 'show, don't tell' and ground their objective in physical posture.
- Encourage the Facilitator to look for physical mirroring or sudden shifts in status as indicators of successful subtextual reception.
- If the Facilitator waits too long for a perfect exchange, remind them to signal as soon as there is any noticeable behavioral reaction to keep the momentum high.

## Variations
- Status Seesaw: Include explicit status-based agendas to practice dynamic status modulation.
- Silent Echo: Before responding verbally to a perceived shift, players must briefly mirror their partner's physical posture to ground their empathy.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to respond to what your partner was doing rather than what they were saying?
- What physical or vocal cues made you realize your partner's emotional agenda had shifted?
- How did assuming your partner's competence change how closely you observed their minor physical adjustments?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure agenda cards do not contain triggering or highly sensitive personal scenarios. Remind players to maintain physical boundaries unless explicitly negotiated beforehand.

## Why It Works
By removing explicit verbal communication of goals, the game forces players to apply Meisner-style hyper-observation. The real-time feedback loop of the facilitator's signal rewards active gifting and reception, training players to treat subtext as a tangible, actionable offer.

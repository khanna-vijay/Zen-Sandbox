# Boundary Braille

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Pause, check in, and negotiate physical and emotional boundaries in real-time during active scenes.

## Overview
This structured exercise trains improvisers to navigate physical and emotional intimacy safely without losing narrative momentum. By pausing right before high-stakes contact, players practice explicit, low-pressure verbal negotiation. The experience builds a shared safety net, proving that setting boundaries can inspire creative storytelling rather than stall it.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Consent & Boundaries; Truth Over Pandering
- **Skill(s):** Boundary Navigation; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Check-ins; Cut calls; Negotiating physical contact
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop proactive boundary navigation, active listening, and honest offer reception by practicing structured, in-scene check-ins and prioritizing personal safety over pleasing the audience.

## Setup
An open playing space with no props. Two players stand in the center to perform a scene, while one to three players stand on the perimeter as active observers and facilitators.

## How to Play
1. Step 1: Two players begin a standard character-driven scene, establishing a clear relationship and setting.
2. Step 2: One player initiates a 'Proximity Probe' by starting a physical or highly emotional action—such as leaning in for a hug, sitting very close, or preparing to share a deep secret—but freezes their movement just before making contact.
3. Step 3: An off-stage observer or the facilitator immediately calls out 'Boundary Tap!' to gently pause the action, freezing both players in their current physical positions.
4. Step 4: The initiating player performs a 'Braille Read' by breaking character slightly to state their physical or emotional intent and explicitly asking the partner for consent or preference.
5. Step 5: The receiving player responds with one of three structured choices: 'Yes, And' to fully accept, 'Yes, But' to accept with a specific modification, or 'No, Because' to decline the action and establish a firm boundary.
6. Step 6: The players unfreeze and immediately resume the scene, integrating the negotiated boundary into the narrative without breaking the reality of the story.
7. Step 7: At any point during play, any participant can call 'Cut!' to instantly stop the scene for personal safety or comfort, which immediately ends the round with zero questions asked.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: Remind players that the 'Proximity Probe' must stop short of contact; the physical gap is where the safety and negotiation live.
- Pitfall: Players saying 'Yes' to physical contact when they actually feel uncomfortable, just to keep the scene moving. Fix: Actively praise 'No, Because' choices during side-coaching to normalize boundary-setting as a creative gift.
- Coaching Cue: Keep the verbal check-ins brief and clinical. The goal is a quick, clear agreement, not an extended out-of-character discussion.
- Pitfall: Observers missing the moment to call the pause. Fix: Encourage the active players to call their own 'Boundary Tap' if the off-stage observers are too slow to react.

## Variations
- Silent Signals: Perform the check-in and negotiation using only eye contact and subtle, pre-agreed non-verbal gestures instead of spoken words.
- Emotional Probes: Focus the game entirely on high-stakes emotional intimacy or intense verbal conflict rather than physical touch.
- Internalized Tap: Remove the external facilitator; players must self-initiate the pause and check-in seamlessly whenever they feel a boundary approaching.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to explicitly state your physical intentions before executing them?
- Did you feel any pressure to agree to an offer you didn't want, and how did the structured options help you resist that pressure?
- How did a 'No, Because' response actually help move the story forward in an unexpected way?
- What did you learn about the difference between your character's boundaries and your personal boundaries as a player?

## Safety & Inclusion
Establish a clear, non-negotiable 'Cut' signal before starting. Ensure all players understand that setting a boundary is a successful play, and no player is ever required to justify their personal comfort levels to the group.

## Why It Works
By formalizing the pause before physical or emotional contact, this game removes the social pressure to comply. It replaces the fear of rejection with a structured vocabulary, showing players that boundaries actually provide the safety container needed for bold, uninhibited acting.

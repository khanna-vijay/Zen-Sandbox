# The Millipede

![The Millipede — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T0_G1179__millipede.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Transform a linked group into a single, high-speed rolling organism to build instant physical connection.

## Overview
In this high-energy physical warm-up, players link arms to form a tight circle, compress themselves against a wall into a double-layered line, and wheel along the perimeter of the room. The experience is fast-paced and exhilarating, requiring absolute physical synchronization and trust to keep the collective structure intact.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Physicality & Space Work; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Timing exercises
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop group mind, peripheral awareness, and physical coordination by forcing players to move as a single, connected physical unit.

## Setup
A large, open room with completely clear walls. Ensure all chairs, bags, and hazards are moved away from the perimeter. Players begin by standing in a circle in the center of the space.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to stand in a circle and tightly link arms at the elbows with the neighbors on both sides.
2. Guide the entire linked circle to move together toward one of the flat walls of the room.
3. Compress the circle against the wall so it flattens into two parallel lines: one line of players with their backs near the wall, and another line facing them, with everyone remaining tightly linked.
4. Explain that the entire double-layered structure must now move sideways along the perimeter of the room, maintaining its shape.
5. As the group moves, the players at the leading edge must guide the direction, while the trailing players follow, creating a continuous wheeling or rolling motion along the wall.
6. Instruct players that as they reach the end of the line, they must transition smoothly from the front line to the back line (and vice versa) to keep the tread-like movement going.
7. Gradually increase the speed of the movement, encouraging the group to find a shared physical rhythm and velocity.
8. Call a halt once the group has successfully navigated a portion of the room's perimeter at a high, synchronized speed.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach safety constantly: Remind players to keep their hands open and relaxed (no closed fists) to prevent accidental impacts.
- If the chain breaks, pause the game immediately, have the group reform the structure, and remind them to 'follow the follower' rather than rushing ahead.
- Act as a physical spotter near corners or obstacles, calling out warnings to help the leading edge navigate safely.
- Encourage players to use their peripheral vision to match the stride and pace of the people opposite and next to them.

## Variations
- Vocal Engine: Have the group generate a collective sound effect (like a mechanical hum or a rushing wind) that rises in pitch and volume as their speed increases.
- Instant Reverse: On a single clap from the facilitator, the group must instantly reverse their direction of travel along the wall without breaking the link.
- Blind Navigation: The players in the middle of the lines close their eyes, relying entirely on the physical guidance of the players at the outer edges to move safely.

## Debrief
- What physical cues did you have to pay attention to in order to keep the group from breaking apart?
- How did it feel to transition between leading the movement at the edge and being swept along in the middle?
- How does the level of focus required for this game relate to supporting your scene partners on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
This game involves close physical contact and rapid movement. Ensure all participants are comfortable linking arms. For accessibility or comfort boundaries, players can hold onto a shared thick rope or place hands on shoulders instead of linking elbows. Ensure the floor is completely dry and free of slipping hazards.

## Why It Works
By physically locking the players together, individual autonomy is surrendered to the group. Players cannot move successfully on their own; they must feel the tension, weight, and momentum of the entire ensemble. This instantly builds peripheral awareness and a shared physical rhythm.

# The Sovereign and the Servant

> Weave a chain of logical justifications that turn simple requests into an absolute catastrophe.

## Overview
Two players take on high and low status roles—a demanding master and an apologetic servant. As the master makes simple, everyday requests, the servant must politely deny them, justifying each failure by building upon a single, rapidly escalating household disaster. The scene ends when the full, hilarious scope of the tragedy is revealed through impeccable narrative logic.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Raising the Stakes; Status Modulation; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Reincorporation-as-justification; Stakes-escalation reps; Endowment-acceptance
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To practice narrative reincorporation and justification, training players to accept offers, raise stakes logically, and connect disparate details into a single cohesive storyline.

## Setup
Two players stand or sit facing each other in a performance space. No props or special staging are required. One player is designated as the high-status Master, and the other as the low-status Servant.

## How to Play
1. Establish the roles: Player A is the high-status Master (demanding, comfortable, oblivious) and Player B is the low-status Servant (deferential, stressed, eager to please).
2. The Master begins the scene by making a simple, mundane request (e.g., 'Bring me a cup of tea' or 'Fetch my slippers').
3. The Servant must politely decline the request, offering a specific, seemingly minor excuse (e.g., 'I cannot, sir, the kettle is broken').
4. The Master accepts this excuse without anger, immediately pivoting to an alternative request (e.g., 'Very well, bring me a glass of water instead').
5. The Servant must decline this new request as well, but must justify the refusal by linking it directly to the previous excuse, escalating the underlying problem (e.g., 'The water is shut off, sir, because the plumber is trying to fix the exploded kettle').
6. The Master continues to make new, logical requests based on the current situation, while the Servant continues to receive the offers and justify their impossibility by expanding the same disaster.
7. The players continue this cycle, raising the stakes with each exchange, until the Servant reveals that the entire estate is in ruins due to a single, interconnected chain of events.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the Servant to avoid random, disconnected excuses. Every new denial must be a direct consequence of the previous justification (reincorporation).
- Encourage the Master to maintain high status and remain surprisingly calm or oblivious, which allows the stakes to rise naturally without the scene devolving into a shouting match.
- Pitfall: The Servant simply says 'No' without explaining why. Fix: Remind the Servant to use the 'Yes, but...' or 'Yes, and here is the terrible reason why...' framework to keep the narrative moving.
- Side-coach: 'Connect the dots! How does the missing tea lead to the burning barn?'

## Variations
- Modern Office: Adapt the setting to a demanding corporate boss and an overworked assistant dealing with a collapsing company merger or office system failure.
- Sci-Fi Spaceship: Play as a spaceship captain and a chief engineer dealing with a cascading system failure during an alien encounter.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to build a disaster step-by-step rather than inventing a massive catastrophe all at once?
- For the Servant, how did reincorporating previous details make it easier to justify the next refusal?
- How did the Master's high-status composure affect the comedy and tension of the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the escalating disaster remains comedic and cartoonish. Players should check in or establish boundaries beforehand if they wish to avoid specific real-world triggers (such as family emergencies or severe physical harm).

## Why It Works
This game works because it utilizes the narrative engine of cause-and-effect. By forcing the servant to justify every refusal using the previous excuse, it teaches players the power of reincorporation. Instead of inventing new, random obstacles, players discover that the most satisfying narrative payoff comes from deepening and connecting the details already established on stage.

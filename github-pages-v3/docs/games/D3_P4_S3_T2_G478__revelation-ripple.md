# Unspoken Revelations

> Navigate sudden narrative disruptions by integrating secret twists and justifying their profound consequences.

## Overview
Two players establish a grounded base reality before one is secretly handed a disruptive narrative twist. The player with the secret must subtly embody this new truth, while their partner must actively listen, accept the behavioral shift, and collaboratively build a new reality. The resulting scene explores the ripple effects of this disruption, driving toward a satisfying, co-created climax.

- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; If this is true, what else is true?; Platform/Tilt; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master the 'Platform and Tilt' technique by establishing a stable base reality, introducing a sudden narrative disruption, and using justification to rebuild a cohesive story arc.

## Setup
The facilitator prepares several index cards ('Revelation Cards'), each containing a high-impact, narrative-shifting secret (e.g., 'You are an undercover agent investigating your partner,' 'You are experiencing a time loop,' 'You are an artificial intelligence'). Two players stand in the performance space, while the remaining participants observe as active listeners. No special props are needed other than the cards and a writing utensil.

## How to Play
1. Establish the Platform: Two players step forward and receive a simple, grounded suggestion (e.g., an everyday location or relationship) to begin a standard scene.
2. Build the Base Reality: For the first 60 to 90 seconds, the players focus on establishing a clear platform: who they are, where they are, and their relationship (CROW), keeping the stakes grounded.
3. Introduce the Tilt: Once the platform is stable, the facilitator quietly steps up to one player and shows them a pre-written 'Revelation Card' containing a secret twist, keeping it hidden from the other player.
4. Embody the Secret: The player who received the card must immediately accept this new truth and let it alter their behavior, subtext, or physical choices, without directly reading or announcing the card's text.
5. Receive and Justify: The unaware partner must notice this sudden shift in behavior or dialogue, treat it as an absolute truth, and immediately justify why their partner is acting this way.
6. Apply 'If True, What Else is True?': Both players collaboratively explore the logical and emotional consequences of this shift, escalating the stakes based on their newly discovered reality.
7. Drive to Resolution: The players use this heightened tension to steer the narrative toward an impactful climax or resolution, transforming their initial relationship or objective.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching the Secret Holder: Remind the player receiving the card to show, not tell. They should avoid blabbing the twist immediately (e.g., saying 'I am a robot!'). Instead, they should exhibit robotic precision, sensory glitches, or literal interpretations of language.
- Coaching the Unaware Partner: Encourage the unaware player to avoid denial or playing 'crazy.' If their partner starts acting strangely, they must not say 'You're acting weird, stop it.' Instead, they should justify it: 'Your memory is slipping again; is the medication wearing off?'
- Timing the Intervention: The facilitator must wait until a solid platform is built. If the tilt is introduced too early, the scene collapses because there is no established reality to disrupt.
- Pitfall - Over-complicating the Twist: Ensure the cards contain clear, actionable relationships or physical realities rather than complex plot points that require heavy exposition. Keep the prompts simple and active.

## Variations
- Double Blind: Both players are secretly handed different, conflicting Revelation Cards at the same time, forcing them to find a middle ground that justifies both realities.
- Audience-Sourced Twists: The audience writes down the secrets on slips of paper before the show, and the facilitator selects one mid-scene to hand to a player.
- The Slow Burn: The player with the card must delay the physical or verbal manifestation of the twist, letting it leak out in tiny, almost imperceptible increments over several minutes.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your established reality suddenly disrupted, and how did you fight the urge to deny the change?
- For the unaware player, what clues did you pick up on, and how did your justification shape the actual twist?
- How does establishing a strong, boring 'platform' first make the 'tilt' more satisfying and easier to play?

## Safety & Inclusion
While this game does not require physical contact, the sudden introduction of secrets can sometimes touch on sensitive themes. Facilitators should ensure the Revelation Cards avoid triggering topics (e.g., sudden terminal illness, severe trauma) and focus instead on playful, high-stakes sci-fi, fantasy, or relational shifts. Players always have the right to pass on a card if it makes them uncomfortable.

## Why It Works
This game works because it operationalizes the 'Platform and Tilt' narrative structure. By forcing an asymmetric information dynamic, it prevents players from planning the scene's trajectory. The unaware player is forced into hyper-vigilant active listening, while the secret-holder must practice subtle offer-delivery, resulting in organic, highly justified narrative escalation.

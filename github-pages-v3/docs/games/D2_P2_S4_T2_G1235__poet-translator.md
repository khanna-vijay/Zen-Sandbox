# The Verse Translator

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Translate passionate gibberish poetry into profound English verse, matching the emotional and rhythmic intensity.

## Overview
In this game, one player acts as an acclaimed international poet reciting their work in an expressive, fictional language, while another player acts as their dedicated translator. Together, they co-create a cohesive, emotionally charged poem line-by-line, transforming nonsense sounds into deep, justified meaning.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Vocal Craft; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Gibberish; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To practice deep offer reception and endowment-acceptance by treating vocal tone, rhythm, and physical gestures as concrete gifts that must be justified and translated into spoken narrative.

## Setup
Two players stand side-by-side facing the group. No props are needed. One player is designated as the Poet, and the other is the Translator. A third player can optionally act as an interviewer or host to introduce them.

## How to Play
1. The host or facilitator asks the group for a suggestion of a non-existent language and a mundane topic for a poem.
2. The Poet begins by reciting the first line or stanza of their poem entirely in gibberish, using strong vocal dynamics, rhythm, and physical gestures to convey a specific emotion.
3. The Translator listens intently, matching the Poet's physical stance and absorbing the emotional subtext of the gibberish delivery.
4. The Translator then delivers the English translation of that line, matching the length, rhythm, and emotional intensity of the Poet's delivery while justifying the sounds heard.
5. The Poet listens to the translation, accepting it as the absolute truth of their poem, and uses that meaning to inspire the next line of gibberish.
6. The players alternate back and forth for four to six lines, building a logical narrative arc that reaches a poetic and emotional climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Match the length! If the poet speaks for three seconds, the translation should be brief; do not over-explain a short gibberish phrase.
- Coaching cue: Translate the emotion, not just the sounds. Encourage translators to look at the poet's body language and facial expressions to find the true meaning.
- Pitfall: The poet speaks for too long before letting the translator speak. Fix: Remind the poet to deliver only one or two lines of verse at a time to keep the rhythm tight.
- Pitfall: The translator treats the game as a joke, undermining the poet's emotional sincerity. Fix: Coach the translator to treat the poet's work with absolute reverence and high artistic status.

## Variations
- Three-Player Press Conference: A third player acts as a talk-show host who interviews the poet, with the translator translating both the questions and the poet's elaborate, emotional answers.
- Rhyming Translation: The translator must make the English translation rhyme, matching any rhythmic or rhyming patterns established by the poet's gibberish.

## Debrief
- How did paying attention to vocal tone and body language help you translate something with no literal meaning?
- Poets, how did hearing your work translated change or inspire your next line of gibberish?
- How does treating nonsense sounds as valuable gifts mirror the way we accept offers in standard scene work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that the fictional language created by the poet does not mimic or caricature real-world languages, cultures, or accents. Focus on abstract, playful vocalizations rather than cultural stereotypes.

## Why It Works
This game works because it strips away literal language, forcing players to rely entirely on non-verbal communication, vocal variety, and emotional commitment. By treating gibberish as a high-status gift, the translator practices radical endowment-acceptance, proving that any offer can be justified and made brilliant.

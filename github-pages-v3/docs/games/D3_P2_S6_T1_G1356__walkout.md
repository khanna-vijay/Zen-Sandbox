# Trigger Exits

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Justify sudden exits and entrances triggered by secret verbal cues spoken by your partners.

## Overview
A scene-work drill where players must immediately leave or enter the performance space whenever their assigned trigger word is spoken. The core challenge is to seamlessly justify these abrupt transitions within the scene's base reality, keeping the narrative coherent despite the chaotic physical interruptions.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Make Your Partner a Genius; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Active Listening; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Walk-ons
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** Develops rapid justification skills, active listening, and the ability to maintain a strong base reality even when absurd or sudden physical actions are forced upon the players.

## Setup
Three to six players stand in a line-up. The facilitator secretly assigns each player a common but not overly frequent noun or verb (e.g., 'coffee', 'key', 'shadow', 'window') as their personal trigger word. Two players step forward to begin a scene, while the remaining players wait off-stage in the wings.

## How to Play
1. Assign each player a unique trigger word that they must keep in mind throughout the game.
2. Two players step into the playing area to initiate a grounded, relationship-focused scene, establishing a clear base reality.
3. The off-stage players listen closely from the wings for their specific trigger words.
4. If an active player says an off-stage player's trigger word, that off-stage player must immediately enter the scene.
5. If an active player's own trigger word is spoken by their scene partner, that active player must immediately exit the scene.
6. Every single entrance and exit must be instantly justified by the characters within the context of the scene's reality.
7. If a player who has exited hears their trigger word spoken again, they must immediately re-enter the scene, again justifying their return.
8. The scene continues with players constantly cycling in and out as their trigger words are naturally or accidentally spoken, maintaining the narrative thread throughout.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't delay! The moment you hear your word, move first, then justify why you are moving.'
- Pitfall: Players trying to avoid saying the trigger words to keep the scene stable. Fix: Encourage players to speak naturally and even intentionally use their partners' words to play with the mechanic.
- Coaching cue: 'Keep the base reality strong. If someone leaves to check the mail, remember that mail when they return or when you talk about them.'
- Pitfall: Absurd justifications that break the reality of the scene (e.g., teleportation). Fix: Guide them to find mundane, high-stakes, or character-consistent reasons for their sudden movements.

## Variations
- Physical Triggers: Instead of words, players are assigned physical actions (like touching an elbow, sitting down, or crossing legs) that trigger exits and entrances.
- Shared Triggers: Multiple players share the same trigger word, causing mass exits or entrances that require collective justification.

## Debrief
- How did the physical restriction of having to leave or enter suddenly affect your character's commitment to the scene?
- What strategies did you use to make an abrupt exit feel completely natural to the story?
- How did active listening change when you had to listen for specific trigger words while simultaneously staying in character?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles, as players may need to exit or enter quickly. Remind players to move safely without tripping or colliding.

## Why It Works
It forces players to practice justifying the absurd in real-time. By making the physical movement mandatory and immediate, it bypasses the analytical brain, forcing the player to find a creative, logical reason after the action has already begun, which is the heart of strong support work and base reality maintenance.

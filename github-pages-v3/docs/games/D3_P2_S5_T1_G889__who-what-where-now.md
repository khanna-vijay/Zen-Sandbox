# Base Reality Barometers

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build a rock-solid scene foundation with the help of live, physical progress meters.

## Overview
Two players improvise a scene while four classmates act as physical 'barometers' representing Character, Relationship, Objective, Where (C.R.O.W.), and the present moment. As the actors establish these foundational elements through dialogue and action, the barometers raise their hands to track clarity, aiming to reach full satisfaction.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Endowment-gifting drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To rapidly establish a clear, shared base reality at the top of a scene through active gifting, physical world-building, and immediate, present-moment stakes.

## Setup
An open performance space with two chairs for the active players. Four players stand to the side or in the front row of the audience, acting as the Barometers. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Assign two players to take the stage to begin a scene.
2. Designate four off-stage players to serve as the Barometers, each assigned to one element: Character/Relationship (Who), Objective/What (What), Where (Location), and Now (Immediacy/Present Stakes).
3. Instruct the Barometers to start with their hands at their waist, representing zero clarity.
4. Have the two active players begin an unprompted scene, focusing on establishing their base reality through physical action, emotional offers, and dialogue.
5. As the scene progresses, each Barometer incrementally raises their hand as their assigned element becomes clearer and more specific.
6. If an element becomes muddy, contradictory, or ignored, the corresponding Barometer lowers their hand accordingly.
7. When a Barometer's hand reaches fully overhead, they call out 'Satisfied!' to signal that their element is fully established.
8. The scene continues until all four Barometers have reached the top, or the facilitator calls 'Scene' once a stable base reality is locked in.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Show, don't just tell!' Remind players to use object work to establish the 'Where' rather than just naming the location.
- Pitfall: Players info-dumping in the first two lines to satisfy the barometers. Fix: Encourage organic discovery and emotional connection. A slow, deep relationship is better than a rushed list of facts.
- Coaching cue for Barometers: 'Be honest, not polite.' If a player says 'We are in a kitchen' but does no kitchen-related actions, the 'Where' barometer should only raise their hand slightly. True clarity comes from commitment, not just labels.
- Pitfall: The 'Now' barometer stays low because players talk about past events or future plans. Fix: Side-coach the actors to 'Make it about this exact moment. What is happening right here, right now?'

## Variations
- Somatic Start: Before the scene begins, the two actors move around the space. When the facilitator says 'Freeze,' they stop, make eye contact, and read each other's physical posture and facial expression to discover their 'Who' and 'What' before speaking.
- Trio Tag-Outs: Play in groups of three. Two players start a scene. As soon as the third player (the observer) can clearly identify the Who, What, and Where, they yell 'Freeze!', state the established elements, and swap in for one of the players to start a new scene.
- Blind Barometers: The actors do not know which barometer represents which element. They must play a balanced scene and watch the hands rise to deduce what they are doing well and what is missing.

## Debrief
- For the actors: How did having live, physical feedback change how quickly or slowly you established the scene's foundation?
- For the barometers: What was the difference between a scene that 'told' you the details versus one that 'showed' you through behavior and object work?
- How does focusing on 'Now' (the present moment) instantly elevate the stakes of the 'Who' and 'Where'?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical hand-raising is accessible; barometers can use verbal cues (e.g., calling out percentages like '25%', '50%', '100%') or use a thumbs-up scale if holding an arm up is tiring or physically restrictive.

## Why It Works
By externalizing the cognitive checklist of scene-building (C.R.O.W.) into physical barometers, players receive real-time, non-disruptive feedback. This trains active listening and offer reception, forcing players to build on what is actually happening rather than executing a pre-planned script. It emphasizes that a base reality is a collaborative, organic discovery rather than an intellectual exercise.

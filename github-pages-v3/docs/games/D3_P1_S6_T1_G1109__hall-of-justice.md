# Super Justifiers

![Super Justifiers — infographic](img/D3_P1_S6_T1_G1109__hall-of-justice.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Defeat absurd villains by fully committing to and justifying your bizarre, object-inspired superpowers.

## Overview
In this high-energy, comedic game, players take on the roles of superheroes, sidekicks, villains, and victims based on random audience suggestions. The comedy and narrative drive come from how creatively and physically the performers justify their absurd attributes and powers during a dramatic confrontation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Show, Don't Tell; Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Heightening & Exploration; Physicality & Space Work; Active Gifting; Room Reading
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Character Walks/Centers; Endowment-gifting drills; Reading the suggestion's intent
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop physical commitment, active gifting, and the ability to justify absurd offers on the spot, transforming random suggestions into coherent character traits and stage actions.

## Setup
An open performance space with an audience. No props are needed. The facilitator prepares to gather four distinct, unrelated suggestions from the audience (such as household objects, daily routines, minor ailments, or office supplies).

## How to Play
1. Ask the audience for four distinct suggestions: two everyday objects, one physical sensation or ailment, and one mundane office item.
2. Assign the roles: Player A is the Lead Hero (inspired by suggestion 1), Player B is their Sidekick (inspired by suggestion 2), Player C is the Arch-Villain (inspired by suggestion 3), and Player D is the Henchman (inspired by suggestion 4). The remaining players act as endangered citizens.
3. Begin the scene with the endangered citizens in peril, physically and vocally establishing a high-stakes crisis caused by the Arch-Villain and Henchman.
4. The Villains enter, demonstrating their powers. They must physically embody their assigned suggestions (e.g., if the suggestion is 'influenza,' they might move sluggishly, sneeze weaponized germs, or induce feverish confusion) and justify why these traits make them dangerous.
5. The Heroes make a grand entrance to save the day, immediately showcasing their own powers derived from their suggestions (e.g., 'Hammerman' using rigid, heavy physical strikes, and 'Coffee Sidekick' moving at hyper-speed).
6. Play out the confrontation. Every action, attack, and defense must be justified through the lens of the characters' specific themes, with players 'yes-and-ing' the physical reality of their opponents' absurd powers.
7. The scene culminates in a dramatic climax where the heroes either defeat the villains or find a hilarious compromise, driven entirely by the physical and logical justification of their absurd traits.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to 'show, don't tell.' Instead of explaining their powers verbally, they should initiate with a physical action or sound effect first, then let the dialogue justify it.
- Pitfall: Players get stuck in intellectual debates about their powers. Fix: Side-coach them to immediately throw a physical 'attack' or take a physical action to force active play.
- Encourage the victims to make the villains look incredibly dangerous. If a villain attacks with a 'briefcase beam,' the victim must react with high-stakes physical comedy to sell the threat.
- Remind sidekicks and henchmen to actively gift their partners by setting up opportunities for them to use their signature powers.

## Variations
- Origin Story: Before the big battle, play two brief split-scenes showing how the hero duo and the villain duo acquired their absurd powers.
- Secret Identities: The heroes start as regular citizens in a mundane setting (like a grocery store) and must transform into their alter-egos when the villains attack, using nearby items to justify their costumes.

## Debrief
- How did committing to physical movement help you discover the logic behind your absurd superpower?
- What strategies did you use to make your partner's bizarre power look incredibly effective or dangerous?
- How does justifying an absurd premise immediately build a clear comedic game for the audience?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles before starting, as high-energy superhero battles can lead to rapid movement. Remind players to maintain safe physical boundaries and use non-contact stage combat principles when executing physical attacks.

## Why It Works
This game functions as a comedy engine by pairing high-stakes dramatic commitment with low-stakes, absurd suggestions. By forcing players to justify ridiculous concepts as genuine threats or heroic assets, it trains the brain to find instant logic in the unexpected. The physical embodiment of the suggestions ensures that players show their offers rather than just talking about them, keeping the energy high and the storytelling visual.

# Internal Mantra

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Transform your scenes from the inside out by repeating a secret emotional phrase.

## Overview
Two players perform a simple scene, then replay the exact same scenario while silently repeating a specific emotional phrase in their heads. This internal mantra naturally shifts their physical, vocal, and emotional expression without requiring conscious intellectual effort.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Vulnerability; Commit 100%; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Emotional Fluidity; Heightening & Exploration
- **Technique(s):** Emotional substitution; The Emotional Dial (1→10)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** Develops emotional fluidity and vulnerability by using internal subtext (emotional substitution) to automatically drive physical and vocal choices, bypassing intellectual planning.

## Setup
Two players stand in the performance space. The rest of the group acts as observers. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. Select two players to step forward and establish a simple, mundane relationship and activity (e.g., folding laundry, waiting for a bus).
2. Instruct the players to perform a brief, neutral scene for about 60 to 90 seconds, focusing on realistic, low-stakes dialogue.
3. Pause the scene. Assign a specific, emotionally charged 'mantra' to one or both players (e.g., 'I am so happy' or 'I don't trust you'). This mantra must be repeated silently and continuously in their heads.
4. Direct the players to replay the exact same scene, using the same basic dialogue and actions, but fully committing to repeating their assigned internal mantra.
5. Encourage the players to let the silent mantra dictate their physical posture, vocal tone, and eye contact, rather than forcing a caricature of the emotion.
6. Stop the scene after a minute and have the players reflect on how the internal repetition altered their physical presence and emotional connection.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't act the emotion; just say the words in your head and let your body do the rest.'
- Pitfall: Players try to shoehorn the literal words of the mantra into their spoken dialogue. Fix: Remind them that the mantra must remain strictly silent and internal.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Let the mantra affect your breathing and tempo. Slow down or speed up based on its rhythm.'
- Pitfall: Players over-intellectualize or 'indicate' the emotion facially. Fix: Coach them to focus 100% on the internal repetition, allowing the vulnerability of the true feeling to leak out naturally.

## Variations
- Asymmetrical Mantras: Give each player a different, conflicting mantra (e.g., Player A repeats 'I need your approval' while Player B repeats 'You are wasting my time') without telling each other what they are.
- Secret Mantras: Have the audience whisper a mantra to each player individually so neither player knows the other's internal state until the scene begins.
- Mantra Shift: During a continuous scene, the facilitator calls out a new mantra mid-action, forcing the players to instantly shift their internal state.

## Debrief
- How did repeating the silent mantra change your physical tension, breathing, or eye contact without you consciously planning it?
- For the observers, how did the subtext of the second scene compare to the first, even when the spoken words remained similar?
- How does having a strong internal focus make it easier to be vulnerable and present on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that assigned mantras do not trigger personal trauma. Avoid highly charged personal themes unless established beforehand, and allow players to pass or request a different mantra if they feel uncomfortable with the emotional state requested.

## Why It Works
By giving the analytical mind a repetitive task (the mantra), the performer's conscious filter is bypassed. This allows genuine physical and emotional impulses to surface. It teaches emotional substitution by showing that feeling an emotion internally is far more powerful and vulnerable than 'acting' it externally.

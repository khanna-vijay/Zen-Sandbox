# Fingertip Guide

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Navigate the physical space blindly, guided solely by the gentle contact of your partner's fingertips.

## Overview
A quiet, trust-building exercise where partners connect fingertip-to-fingertip. One player closes their eyes while the other guides them safely through the room using only the subtle pressure of their touch. It shifts focus from visual cues to deep physical empathy and shared spatial awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Consent & Boundaries
- **Skill(s):** Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Boundary Navigation; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise; Negotiating physical contact
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep physical empathy, non-verbal communication, and active listening through touch, embodying the principle of making your partner look and feel secure.

## Setup
An open room with a few safe obstacles (like chairs or tables) scattered around. Players pair up and stand facing each other with enough space to move freely.

## How to Play
1. Form pairs and stand facing your partner, maintaining comfortable posture and eye contact.
2. Raise your hands to chest height and touch all ten fingertips to your partner's corresponding fingertips, establishing a light, steady contact.
3. Designate one partner as the 'Guide' and the other as the 'Explorer'.
4. The Explorer gently closes their eyes, committing to keep them closed throughout the round.
5. The Guide begins to move slowly around the room, leading the Explorer using only the physical connection of their fingertips.
6. The Guide must keep the Explorer safe, navigating around other pairs and physical obstacles in the room without losing fingertip contact.
7. Maintain a slow, deliberate pace, ensuring the Explorer feels secure and supported at all times.
8. After a few minutes, pause, have the Explorer open their eyes, swap roles, and repeat the process.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Move at a speed where your partner feels completely safe. If they tense up, slow down.'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep the touch light—do not grab or hold fingers; rely on mutual pressure.'
- Pitfall: Moving too fast or trying to trick the partner. Fix: Remind players that the goal is to make their partner look and feel like a genius, not to test them.
- Pitfall: Losing contact and leaving the partner stranded. Fix: If contact is broken, both players should freeze immediately, re-establish fingertip touch, and then resume.

## Variations
- Single-Finger Connection: Reduce the contact point to just the index fingers of each hand, increasing the sensitivity required.
- Silent Symphony: Introduce low-volume ambient music and encourage pairs to let the music influence the tempo and flow of their movement.
- Double Blind: Both players close their eyes, attempting to move together in a shared space based on mutual, reciprocal leading.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to completely surrender visual control and rely entirely on your partner's touch?
- As the Guide, what adjustments did you make to ensure your partner felt safe and successful?
- How does this level of physical sensitivity translate to active listening in a spoken scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game involves physical touch and closed eyes, always ask for enthusiastic consent before beginning. If a participant is uncomfortable touching fingers, they can hold a short prop (like a pen or a wooden dowel) between them to maintain connection without direct skin contact. Ensure the floor is clear of tripping hazards.

## Why It Works
By removing sight, the exercise forces players to channel all their attention into tactile feedback. The Guide must anticipate the Explorer's physical limits, while the Explorer must trust the Guide's intentions. This builds a profound, non-verbal feedback loop that directly trains physical empathy and partner support.

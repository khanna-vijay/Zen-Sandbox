# The Ripple Effect

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Escalate mundane details into high-stakes narrative climaxes using the logic of cause and effect.

## Overview
A scene-based exercise where players build narrative momentum by treating every statement as a stone dropped in water. By exploring the logical and emotional consequences of each preceding line, players transform a minor, everyday observation into a high-stakes dramatic or comedic climax.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Base Reality First; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Game Identification; Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes
- **Technique(s):** If this is true, what else is true?; Stakes-escalation reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master the principle of 'If this is true, what else is true?', training players to identify narrative games, raise stakes organically, and serve the story through tight, justified cause-and-effect progression.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle or semi-circle facing the performance space. No props or special staging are required. The facilitator prompts the first pair of players with a mundane, low-stakes location.

## How to Play
1. Two players enter the playing space and establish a quiet, low-stakes base reality.
2. Player A initiates the scene by making a single, minor, and unremarkable observation about their environment or situation.
3. Player B accepts this observation and responds by introducing a direct, logical consequence of that fact.
4. Player A then responds to Player B's consequence with a further, heightened consequence.
5. Players continue to alternate lines, with each player asking themselves: 'If what my partner just said is true, what else must now be true because of it?'
6. Every new offer must be a direct consequence of the immediate preceding line, escalating the emotional stakes and personal significance for the characters.
7. The scene continues this upward trajectory of justification and escalation until it reaches a natural, high-stakes climax or the facilitator calls 'Scene!'

## Facilitation Notes
- Watch out for 'lateral additions' where players add random new details instead of building on the existing ones. If a player introduces an unrelated fact, pause and ask: 'How does that directly result from what was just said?'
- Use the side-coaching cue 'Is that true?' if a player makes a massive, unjustified leap in logic. Have them pause, find the missing step in the chain, and rephrase.
- Use the cue 'What else is true?' to push players who are stalling or playing safe to find a deeper, more impactful consequence.
- Encourage emotional commitment. As the logical stakes rise, the characters' emotional reactions must scale proportionally to keep the scene grounded and satisfying.

## Variations
- The Domino Hand-off: Run this as a line-of-three exercise. After a pair establishes a consequence, a third player steps in, states the meta-consequence ('Because of that, now this is true!'), and takes over the scene with one of the original players.
- Silent Ripples: Play the game entirely through physical action and object work, where each physical choice is a direct, escalated reaction to the partner's previous physical choice.
- Internal Ripples: Focus the consequences entirely on character relationships and internal emotions rather than external plot events.

## Debrief
- How did starting with a very small, mundane detail make the eventual high-stakes climax feel more earned and justified?
- What was the difference between a 'lateral' offer (adding random info) and a 'consequential' offer (rippling forward)?
- How did your character's emotional state naturally shift as the logical consequences piled up?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game rapidly escalates stakes, remind players that high stakes do not require resorting to physical violence, self-harm, or trauma-inducing scenarios. Encourage them to find high stakes in professional, social, or absurdly comedic arenas.

## Why It Works
This game works because it operationalizes the 'If this is true, what else is true?' technique into a strict structural engine. By forcing players to treat every line as a direct cause of the next effect, it prevents narrative stalling and random plot jumps. It teaches players that compelling stories are not built from disconnected events, but from a tightly woven chain of inevitable, justified consequences.

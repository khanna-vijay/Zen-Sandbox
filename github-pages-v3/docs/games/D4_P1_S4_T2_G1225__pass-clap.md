# Pass the Pulse

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Send a single, shared beat around the circle to build collective focus and rhythm.

## Overview
A foundational ensemble warm-up where players stand in a circle and pass a physical clap from person to person. It starts as a simple, unidirectional flow and evolves into a dynamic, unpredictable game of focus, reaction, and shared rhythm.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Timing exercises
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop group mind, active listening, and precise timing by aligning the ensemble's physical and auditory focus.

## Setup
Players stand in a comfortable circle facing inward, with enough space to turn slightly toward their neighbors. No props or materials are needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle facing inward, ensuring everyone has clear sightlines to all other players.
2. The facilitator or a designated player initiates the pulse by turning to the player on their right, making eye contact, and clapping once toward them.
3. The receiving player immediately turns to their right, makes eye contact with the next player, and claps to pass the pulse along.
4. Continue this single-direction pass around the circle until a steady, unbroken rhythm is established.
5. Once the rhythm is fluid, introduce the reverse mechanic: any player receiving the clap can choose to clap directly back to the sender, reversing the direction of the pulse.
6. Encourage players to maintain a consistent tempo, treating the entire circle as a single instrument rather than isolated individuals.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes on the sender, then immediately lock eyes with the receiver.'
- Pitfall: Players anticipating their turn and clapping before eye contact is made. Fix: Remind them that the clap is a physical hand-off; you cannot pass it until you have received it.
- Coaching cue: 'Listen to the collective beat. Is it speeding up, slowing down, or staying steady?'
- Pitfall: Hesitation or overthinking when reversing. Fix: Encourage unfiltered spontaneity—commit to the choice instantly without second-guessing.

## Variations
- Double Tap: A double clap reverses the direction, while a single clap continues it.
- Height Shift: Pass the clap high (above the head) or low (near the knees) to add physical dynamics that the receiver must match.
- Multi-Pulse: Introduce a second or third clap pulse into the circle simultaneously, requiring players to track multiple rhythms at once.

## Debrief
- How did the energy in the room change when we shifted from a single direction to allowing reversals?
- What did it feel like when the group achieved a perfect, uninterrupted rhythm?
- How does 'following the follower' apply when the pulse changes direction unexpectedly?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players with limited mobility or hearing can participate by allowing alternative physical cues (like a nod, wave, or verbal 'pass') instead of a clap, and adjusting the circle size for comfort.

## Why It Works
It strips away verbal complexity, forcing players to rely entirely on non-verbal cues, peripheral awareness, and shared timing. By focusing on a single, physical point of contact, the group enters a state of flow where individual ego yields to collective rhythm.

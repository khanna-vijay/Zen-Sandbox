# Instant Support Rush

![Instant Support Rush — infographic](img/D4_P2_S2_T1_G622__5-rush-in.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Flood the stage with immediate, enthusiastic physical and verbal agreement to back up any initiation.

## Overview
A high-energy training drill where a single player initiates a scene's location or situation, and the entire remaining group instantly rushes the stage to physically and verbally support it. Instead of building a long narrative, the focus is on rapid, enthusiastic agreement and immediate world-building. The scene concludes as soon as the premise is fully established and supported by all players.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Follow the Follower; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Support Work; Offer Reception; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Walk-ons; Yes, And… sentence games; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop rapid offer reception, immediate physical and verbal support work (walk-ons), and the 'follow the follower' mindset by eliminating hesitation and instantly validating a teammate's initiation.

## Setup
An open playing space with a clear 'stage' area and an 'offstage' area where the rest of the group stands. No props or chairs are needed. Group size of 5 to 10 players is ideal.

## How to Play
1. Divide the players so that one person stands ready to enter the stage, while the remaining players wait offstage in the wings.
2. The first player steps onto the stage and delivers a clear, high-stakes initiation (verbal and physical) that establishes a specific location, event, or crisis.
3. Without a moment's hesitation, all offstage players must rush onto the stage to physically and verbally support the initiator's premise.
4. Entering players must immediately adopt roles, mime objects, or add details that make the initiator's premise look absolutely correct and brilliant.
5. Keep the interaction brief: the goal is not to perform a full five-minute scene, but to achieve a peak moment of collective agreement and shared reality.
6. The facilitator calls 'Scene!' or 'Cut!' once the entire group has successfully committed to the premise and established the environment.
7. Rotate players so that a different person gets to be the initiator, repeating the drill until everyone has led an initiation.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't think, just run!' Encourage players to move their bodies onto the stage before they even know what they will say.
- Common Pitfall: Entering players try to introduce a conflict or twist. Fix: Remind them that this drill is 100% pure agreement; their only job is to make the initiator's idea work perfectly.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Match the energy!' If the initiator is whispering in fear, the rushing players should rush in whispering and fearful, not loud and boisterous.
- Common Pitfall: Crowding the stage without physical definition. Fix: Encourage players to use the physical space—mime objects, hide behind imaginary furniture, or spread out to define the boundaries of the room.

## Variations
- Silent Rush: The initiator must establish the scene entirely through silent physical action, and the rushing players must join in silently, matching the activity and environment before anyone speaks.
- Varying Group Sizes: Run the drill with different numbers of rushing players (from 2 to 8) to feel how the energy and spatial dynamics shift with larger ensembles.
- The Slow-Motion Rush: To focus on precise physical world-building, players must rush in and support the scene in exaggerated slow motion, paying deep attention to spatial relationships.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your initial idea instantly backed up by four other people without any hesitation?
- What did you notice about your physical choices when you forced yourself to rush in before planning your line?
- How does immediate, enthusiastic agreement change the momentum of a scene compared to cautious, step-by-step building?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game involves physical rushing, ensure the playing space is clear of tripping hazards. Remind players to maintain spatial awareness to avoid physical collisions when entering the stage quickly. Offer a low-impact option where players can 'rush' with high vocal and emotional energy while walking safely.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to move physically before they can overthink, the game bypasses the analytical brain and taps directly into intuitive support. It embodies 'following the follower' because the entire ensemble instantly surrenders their own agendas to elevate the initiator's concept. This builds a deep sense of trust and teaches that any initiation, no matter how simple, can succeed if backed by total group commitment.

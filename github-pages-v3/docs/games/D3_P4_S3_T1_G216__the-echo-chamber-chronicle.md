# The Narrative Relay

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Co-create structured, dynamic stories over video chat by claiming specialized narrative roles.

## Overview
The Narrative Relay is a virtual-first storytelling game where players use platform renaming and chat triggers to build a cohesive story sentence-by-sentence. By adopting specific narrative lenses—such as action, emotion, detail, or twist—players learn to balance story elements while navigating the latency of online spaces. The result is a highly collaborative, low-pressure environment that transforms digital communication challenges into playful mechanics.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Story Spine; Yes, And… sentence games; Timing exercises
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops narrative architecture, active listening, and offer reception by training players to recognize what a story needs next and deliver it collaboratively under structural constraints.

## Setup
A virtual meeting platform with gallery view, a text chat panel, and participant renaming capabilities enabled. No physical props or space required. Best suited for 6 to 12 players.

## How to Play
1. Introduce the four narrative roles: The Anchor (adds concrete facts or setting descriptions), The Heart (expresses internal feelings or character moods), The Engine (initiates physical actions or plot progression), and The Wildcard (introduces unexpected twists or obstacles).
2. Instruct each player to rename themselves in the virtual meeting platform to display their first name followed by their assigned role, such as 'Alex | The Heart'.
3. Have all players switch to gallery view so everyone can see each other's facial expressions and non-verbal reactions.
4. The facilitator provides a simple, open-ended starting sentence to establish the initial setting, such as 'Deep in the archives of the forgotten library, a single candle flickered...'
5. The facilitator speaks the prompt aloud, then immediately types 'PASS!' into the meeting chat.
6. The first player to verbally say 'Yes, and...' after seeing 'PASS!' in the chat claims the floor, bypassing audio latency and preventing talking over one another.
7. The claiming player delivers exactly one sentence that advances the story, strictly adhering to their assigned narrative role.
8. Immediately after speaking their sentence, the active player types 'PASS!' in the chat to open the floor for the next contributor.
9. At any point, a player holding The Engine role can use their turn to transition the scene by stating their action and adding, 'And as that happens, we transition to...' to establish a new location before typing 'PASS!'.
10. After 15 to 20 minutes, or when the story reaches a natural climax, the facilitator or a designated Wildcard player delivers a final resolving sentence and types 'END!' to close the chronicle.

## Facilitation Notes
- If two players speak 'Yes, and...' at the exact same time due to lag, the facilitator should quickly and warmly arbitrate by assigning the turn to one and queuing the other for the next pass.
- Watch for players who drift outside their assigned roles, such as an Anchor driving action, and gently side-coach them to ground their next offer in their specific role.
- Encourage players to type 'PASS!' immediately after speaking, as the momentum of the game relies on a rapid-fire handoff.
- Remind players that they cannot pre-plan their sentences; they must listen intently to the immediate predecessor to ensure their contribution makes logical sense in the narrative arc.

## Variations
- Mid-way through the story, the facilitator calls out 'Rotate!' and players must quickly rename themselves to the next role in the sequence.
- Non-active players use platform reactions or emojis to visually support the emotional beats of the story in real-time, enhancing the group mind.
- The facilitator types a new genre, such as Sci-Fi or Noir, into the chat, forcing players to immediately adapt their role-based contributions to the new stylistic tone.

## Debrief
- How did having a specific narrative role change how you listened to the story compared to a free-form scene?
- What did you notice about the balance of details, emotions, actions, and twists? Did the story feel complete?
- How did the chat-based turn-taking affect the pacing and your sense of connection with the group?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that players who may have difficulty typing quickly or monitoring the chat due to visual or motor processing differences are accommodated. The facilitator can act as a dedicated gatekeeper or establish a fixed speaking order instead of the competitive chat-trigger mechanic to ensure an inclusive environment.

## Why It Works
This game works because it gamifies the technical limitations of virtual platforms into a structured turn-taking mechanic. By dividing narrative construction into explicit, visible roles, it lowers the cognitive load on individual players, allowing them to focus on one specific storytelling element while relying on the group mind to build the larger architecture.

# The Mastermind's Briefing

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Equip your clueless boss with heist details by treating them like an absolute genius.

## Overview
One player steps out of the room while the group establishes three secret heist details: a target, an unusual tool, and a bizarre quirk. When the player returns as the 'Mastermind,' the accomplices must feed them these details through high-status endowment and physical play, making the Mastermind look brilliant as they gradually deduce the plan.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Active Listening; Offer Reception; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment chains
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To practice active gifting and endowment, training players to deliver clear, specific physical and verbal clues that elevate their partner rather than testing them.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with a designated 'offstage' area. One player (the Mastermind) leaves the room. The remaining players (the Accomplices) gather suggestions from the group or audience for: 1) A highly specific target to steal, 2) An unusual tool or weapon, and 3) A bizarre code word, disguise, or physical quirk.

## How to Play
1. Select one player to be the 'Mastermind' and have them step out of earshot.
2. The remaining players (Accomplices) establish three secret details: a specific target (e.g., the Mona Lisa's eyebrows), an unusual tool (e.g., a leaf blower), and a wild-card quirk or code word (e.g., speaking only in whispers, or wearing invisible clown shoes).
3. Bring the Mastermind back into the room. The scene begins immediately in their secret headquarters, with the Accomplices treating the Mastermind as a brilliant, high-status leader who already knows every detail of the plan.
4. Accomplices must use 'active gifting'—using physical object work, status play, and loaded dialogue—to feed the Mastermind the secret details without explicitly naming them.
5. The Mastermind must accept these endowments, acting as if they are fully in control, while actively listening and observing to deduce the three secrets.
6. As the Mastermind deduces a detail, they must confidently integrate it into their orders (e.g., 'And make sure the leaf blower is fully fueled!'), which the Accomplices enthusiastically validate.
7. The game concludes once the Mastermind has successfully identified all three details and officially initiated the heist.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach Accomplices to avoid 'charades' or testing the Mastermind. The goal is to make the Mastermind look like a genius, not to stump them.
- Encourage physical object work. Instead of saying 'Here is the tool,' mime handing over a heavy, vibrating leaf blower and react to its weight and sound.
- If the Mastermind guesses incorrectly, Accomplices should 'yes-and' the guess but gently steer it back (e.g., 'Yes, it is like a vacuum, boss, but it blows instead of sucks!').
- Remind the Mastermind to maintain their high-status authority. They shouldn't ask questions; they should make declarations based on the clues they receive.

## Variations
- Press Conference: The Mastermind is a politician at a podium, and the Accomplices are journalists asking loaded questions to reveal a bizarre scandal, location, and scapegoat.
- Time Limit: Add a ticking clock (e.g., 3 minutes) to increase the comedic urgency of the briefing.
- Physical Endowment: The wild card must be a physical tic or accent that the Mastermind must unconsciously adopt through mimicry before they realize what it is.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to feed information while maintaining the illusion that the Mastermind already knew everything?
- What types of gifts (verbal, physical, emotional) were the easiest for the Mastermind to interpret?
- How does treating your partner as a 'genius' change the dynamic of a guessing game?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the 'crime' themes remain lighthearted, cartoonish, or absurd (e.g., stealing the world's largest ball of twine) to keep the play space inclusive and comfortable for all participants.

## Why It Works
By framing the guessing mechanic as a high-status briefing, the game removes the pressure of 'getting it right' and focuses on collaborative world-building. Accomplices learn that specific, generous physical and verbal endowments are far more helpful than vague hints, directly reinforcing the principle of making your partner look good.

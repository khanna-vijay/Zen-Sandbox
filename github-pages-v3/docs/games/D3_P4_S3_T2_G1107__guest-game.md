# The Unwitting Guest

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> An innocent visitor navigates a strange environment where the hosts harbor a shared, unspoken secret.

## Overview
One player enters a scene as an ordinary visitor with a simple, clear objective, while the remaining players portray local characters who share a hidden, suspicious secret. Through coded dialogue, physical clues, and escalating tension, the hosts drop hints until the truth is fully brought to light. It is a masterclass in narrative tension, subtext, and collaborative storytelling.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Show, Don't Tell; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Heightening & Exploration; Active Gifting; Thematic Synthesis
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Endowment-gifting drills; Weave the threads
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop narrative architecture through the Platform and Tilt technique, teaching players how to establish a baseline reality and gradually introduce a disruptive, unified secret without rushing the reveal.

## Setup
A performance space with a clear entrance (such as a door). One player is designated as the Guest and steps out of earshot. The remaining two to five players (the Hosts) quickly agree on a shared secret (e.g., 'we accidentally shrank the dog' or 'we are hiding a fugitive'). The Guest returns, unaware of the secret, and prepares to enter.

## How to Play
1. Assign Roles: Designate one player as the Guest and the remaining players as the Hosts.
2. Establish the Secret: While the Guest is out of earshot, the Hosts agree on a specific, active secret that directly impacts the location or the Guest's reason for visiting.
3. Set the Platform: The Guest enters the space with a mundane, realistic objective (e.g., dropping off mail, arriving for a dinner party, or fixing a sink) and establishes a normal, grounded reality.
4. Introduce the Tilt: The Hosts receive the Guest warmly but immediately exhibit slightly odd, evasive, or suspicious behavior that hints at their secret without stating it outright.
5. Gift Clues: The Hosts use physical object work and loaded dialogue to drop progressive clues, ensuring they show rather than tell the mystery to the Guest.
6. React and Probe: The Guest reacts to the strange behavior, asking logical questions and trying to accomplish their original task, which forces the Hosts to make excuses and escalate their cover-up.
7. Escalate the Tension: The Hosts heighten the stakes by making their behavior increasingly difficult to ignore, building the narrative architecture toward a breaking point.
8. The Reveal: The scene concludes when the secret is fully exposed, either through the Guest deducing it, a Host cracking under pressure, or a physical event that makes the secret undeniable.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Show, don't tell! Use your physical environment and body language to hint at the secret rather than just talking about it.'
- Pitfall - Rushing the Reveal: Hosts sometimes blurt out the secret too early, killing the narrative tension. Fix: Instruct Hosts to hold back the truth as long as possible, letting the Guest's natural curiosity drive the discovery.
- Pitfall - Guest Guessing Game: The Guest might treat the scene like a trivia game, guessing random secrets. Fix: Remind the Guest to stay grounded in their character's objective; they should react to the weirdness, not try to win a guessing game.
- Coaching Cue: 'Make your partner a genius. Hosts, build on each other's clues so you present a united front.'

## Variations
- The Silent Secret: The Hosts cannot use spoken words to hint at the secret; they must rely entirely on physical object work, eye contact, and spatial relationships.
- The Reverse Guest: The Guest is the one with the secret, and the Hosts must figure out why this bizarre visitor has arrived at their perfectly normal home.
- Genre Shift: Play the scene under specific genre constraints, such as Gothic Horror, Film Noir, or Sitcom, which dictates how the secret is held and revealed.

## Debrief
- How did withholding the secret affect the pacing and tension of the scene?
- For the Guest, what was the difference between trying to guess the secret versus reacting naturally to the weird behavior?
- How did the Hosts use physical object work to drop clues without using explicit dialogue?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the secret chosen by the Hosts does not involve real-world trauma, non-consensual themes, or sensitive personal topics. Keep the tone playful, mysterious, or comedic.

## Why It Works
This game relies on the Platform/Tilt dynamic. The Guest establishes a stable platform (a normal visit), while the Hosts introduce a tilt (suspicious behavior). By withholding the direct truth, players are forced to use subtext and active gifting, which naturally builds narrative architecture and keeps the audience engaged in the unfolding mystery.

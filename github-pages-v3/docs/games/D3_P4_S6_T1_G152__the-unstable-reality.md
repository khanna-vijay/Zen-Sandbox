# Shifting Foundations

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Adapt to sudden, fundamental changes in your scene's reality by retroactively justifying everything before.

## Overview
A high-agility scene-work game where players must instantly adapt to sudden, fundamental alterations to their established reality. When the facilitator calls a 'Reality Shift,' the players must immediately accept the new truth, retroactively justify all prior actions under this new lens, and seamlessly advance the story. It is a thrilling, high-focus exercise in narrative flexibility and radical agreement.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Base Reality First; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Game Identification; Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes; Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; If this is true, what else is true?; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop advanced justification skills, specifically justifying the absurd, and to train narrative agility. Players learn to treat unexpected disruptions not as mistakes, but as gifts that instantly raise the stakes and deepen the story's architecture.

## Setup
An open playing space for an in-person workshop. Two to three players stand in the performance area, while the remaining participants sit as active observers. No props or special materials are required. The facilitator stands nearby, ready to side-coach and deliver shifts.

## How to Play
1. Invite two or three players to step into the performance space to initiate a standard scene.
2. Obtain a simple, grounded suggestion from the group to establish a clear base reality.
3. Instruct the players to begin the scene, focusing heavily on establishing their characters, relationships, objectives, and environment within the first 30 to 45 seconds.
4. Once a stable platform is built, the facilitator calls out 'Reality Shift!' followed by a single, disruptive new truth.
5. The players must immediately accept this new premise without breaking character, pausing, or showing visible confusion.
6. Players must retroactively justify all prior dialogue and physical actions so they make perfect sense under the new reality.
7. Continue the scene, applying the principle of 'if this is true, what else is true?' to explore the immediate consequences of the new reality.
8. The facilitator may introduce one or two additional shifts as the scene progresses, compounding the complexity and forcing deeper narrative integration.
9. End the scene naturally once the players have successfully integrated the final shift and reached a satisfying narrative resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep shifts concise and impactful: Focus on altering one specific element of C.R.O.W. rather than delivering a complex paragraph of exposition.
- Watch for 'denial by omission': Ensure players don't just acknowledge the shift and then ignore it. They must actively change their behavior and objectives to match the new truth.
- Side-coach retroactive justification: If players freeze after a shift, prompt them with questions like, 'How does your last line of dialogue secretly prove this new truth?'
- Avoid arbitrary chaos: As a facilitator, design shifts that build upon or twist existing elements rather than completely erasing the emotional connection between characters.

## Variations
- Audience-Driven Shifts: Instead of the facilitator, the audience or off-stage players call out the 'Reality Shift' and provide the new truth.
- Internal Shifts: Players themselves can initiate a shift by revealing a shocking, retroactive secret about themselves or the environment, taking over the facilitator's role.
- Silent Shifts: The facilitator hands a written shift to only one player, forcing them to play the new reality while the other player must figure it out and justify it on the fly.

## Debrief
- How did retroactively justifying your past actions change your character's motivation or emotional stakes?
- What strategies helped you accept the shift instantly without freezing or breaking character?
- How does this exercise change your perspective on 'mistakes' or unexpected offers in a standard scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because shifts can suddenly alter relationships and personal histories, establish a boundary beforehand: shifts should never introduce non-consensual physical touch, trauma, or sensitive personal topics. Players can use a designated safety word or gesture to instantly veto a shift if it crosses a personal boundary.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to retroactively justify sudden changes, the game bypasses the analytical mind's tendency to reject unexpected offers. It exercises the Narrative Engine by demonstrating that any plot point, no matter how absurd, can become a logical and compelling part of the story if the players commit to finding the connective tissue. It transforms disruption into inspiration.

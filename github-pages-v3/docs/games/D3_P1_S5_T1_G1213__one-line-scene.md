# The Single-Line Scene

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build a rich, physical world and deep relationship using only one spoken line of dialogue.

## Overview
Two players perform a fully realized two-minute scene where only a single line of spoken dialogue is permitted. The rest of the scene relies entirely on physical world-building, silent interaction, and emotional subtext to establish the characters, their relationship, and the environment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Show, Don't Tell; Yes, And; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Physicality & Space Work; Silence & Stillness; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Object work; Do nothing exercises
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To master non-verbal world-building and establish C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where) through physical action, silence, and deliberate offer reception rather than relying on explanatory dialogue.

## Setup
An open performance space with two chairs available if needed. No props. Two players stand or sit in the space, ready to begin. The facilitator acts as the timekeeper.

## How to Play
1. Two players step into the performance space and establish a physical starting position.
2. The facilitator prompts the players to begin a two-minute scene, reminding them that only one single line of spoken dialogue is allowed across the entire scene.
3. Players immediately begin with physical action, establishing the 'Where' (environment) through detailed object work and the 'Character' through posture and movement.
4. Players interact silently to discover their 'Relationship' and 'Objective', using eye contact, physical proximity, and shared activities.
5. At any point during the scene, either player may deliver the single allowed line of dialogue; this line should be high-impact, reacting to the established physical reality rather than explaining it.
6. Once the single line is spoken, no further verbal dialogue is permitted by either player for the remainder of the scene.
7. The facilitator calls out a '20 seconds remaining' warning to help players find a physical resolution or button for their scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Show us where you are before you speak. Let the environment do the talking.'
- Common pitfall: Players rush to speak the single line immediately. Fix: Encourage them to hold back the line until at least the halfway mark, letting the physical tension build.
- Side-coaching cue: 'React physically to the silence. What does their breathing or posture tell you?'
- Common pitfall: The single line is used to explain the plot (e.g., 'I am your father and we are in a space station'). Fix: Coach players to make the line an emotional reaction or a shift in relationship rather than exposition.

## Variations
- The Silent Setup: The first 60 seconds must be completely silent, followed by the single line, followed by a silent resolution.
- The Shared Line: Each player gets exactly one word, which they must say at different times, or they must speak a single line in unison.

## Debrief
- How did the restriction on dialogue change how closely you had to watch your partner's physical offers?
- How did you establish the 'Where' and 'Relationship' without using words?
- What did it feel like to hold back the spoken line, and how did that affect the tension in the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players establish physical boundaries before starting, especially since non-verbal scenes often lead to closer physical proximity or touch. Remind players they can communicate boundaries silently or pause the exercise if needed.

## Why It Works
By stripping away the crutch of verbal exposition, players are forced to use their bodies, facial expressions, and environment to communicate. This directly trains the C.R.O.W. framework, as characters and relationships must be shown through physical status and spatial relationships, making the eventual single line of dialogue carry immense dramatic weight.

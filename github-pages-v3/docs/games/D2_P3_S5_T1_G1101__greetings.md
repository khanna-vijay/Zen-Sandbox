# Dynamic Greetings

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Transform simple hellos into high-stakes relationships through instant physical and emotional endowments.

## Overview
Players move freely through an open space, engaging in brief, improvised greetings based on shifting relationship prompts called out by the facilitator. Each prompt establishes a specific history, status, or emotional dynamic, requiring players to instantly gift their partner with a clear point of view and physical commitment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Commit 100%; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; The Emotional Dial (1→10); Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops active gifting, rapid endowment, and emotional fluidity, training players to make their partner look brilliant by instantly accepting and heightening physical and emotional offers.

## Setup
An open, clear room where players can safely walk around and make eye contact. No props or chairs are required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to walk around the room at a moderate pace, filling the empty spaces and maintaining soft eye contact with others.
2. Begin with a neutral baseline: players must greet whoever they cross paths with using a simple, polite nod or a brief, standard handshake.
3. Introduce the first relationship prompt, such as: Greet each other like long-lost best friends who haven't seen each other in ten years.
4. Instruct players to immediately commit to this dynamic with the very next person they meet, using their whole body, facial expressions, and a brief verbal exchange.
5. Emphasize that players must gift their partner by treating them as the exact person described in the prompt, making their partner's reaction easy and natural.
6. After 30 to 45 seconds of interaction, call out 'Keep moving!' to have players resume walking neutrally until the next prompt is delivered.
7. Cycle through a series of contrasting prompts that vary in emotional temperature, status, and history, such as: someone you deeply distrust, someone you have a massive secret crush on, or your absolute hero.
8. End the exercise by returning to a neutral walk, allowing players to shake off the high-intensity emotions and ground themselves.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Gift your partner with your eyes and posture before you even say a word!'
- Coaching cue: 'Make your partner look great by fully accepting the status and emotion they are giving you.'
- Pitfall: Players might default to talking about the relationship rather than feeling it. Fix: Encourage them to show the relationship physically and emotionally rather than explaining the backstory out loud.
- Pitfall: Hesitation or waiting for the other person to initiate. Fix: Remind them to commit 100% immediately; if both initiate, find the middle ground and play.
- Keep the pace brisk. Do not let any single greeting linger too long; the magic lies in the rapid adaptation to new partners and prompts.

## Variations
- Silent Endowments: Run the entire exercise in complete silence, relying purely on body language, eye contact, and physical distance to convey the relationship.
- Asymmetrical Roles: Assign different prompts to different halves of the room, such as Group A being a demanding boss and Group B being an employee trying to ask for a raise, to practice high and low status dynamics.
- The Secret Gift: Instead of the facilitator calling out the prompt, players whisper a secret relationship endowment to their partner upon meeting, and the partner must instantly play into it.

## Debrief
- How did your partner's physical posture and facial expression make it easier for you to know how to react?
- What did it feel like to receive a high-status or high-affection greeting? How did that make you feel like a genius?
- How does committing 100% to an emotional state right away prevent overthinking in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Establish clear boundaries regarding physical touch before starting. Remind players that greetings do not require physical contact to be emotionally intense; eye contact, gestures, and vocal tone are fully sufficient. Encourage players to use a hand-on-heart gesture or a simple wave if they prefer no physical contact.

## Why It Works
By forcing rapid, high-commitment transitions between different relationship dynamics, this game bypasses the analytical brain. It teaches players that a relationship is not built through exposition, but through the immediate gift of how we look at, stand near, and react to our partner.

# Alien, Tiger, Cow

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A high-energy group alignment game where players strive to make the same choice without planning.

## Overview
This is a fast-paced, physical warm-up where the entire group attempts to achieve instant consensus. On a collective count, every player simultaneously adopts one of three distinct physical and vocal personas, repeating the process until the entire circle is in perfect, unspoken harmony.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops group mind, peripheral awareness, and the ability to fail joyfully while building rapid, non-verbal connection.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle facing inward, with enough space to move their arms and bend forward safely. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Introduce the three distinct characters, each with its own physical gesture and vocalization: Alien, Tiger, and Cow.
2. Demonstrate the Alien: hold index fingers up at the temples like antennae, lean slightly forward, and say 'Bleep bleep!'
3. Demonstrate the Tiger: push one hand forward in a clawing motion, step slightly forward, and roar 'Roar!'
4. Demonstrate the Cow: place one hand flat on the stomach, bend forward at the waist, and say a deep 'Mooo!'
5. Have the entire group practice each character together to establish the physical and vocal baselines.
6. Explain the goal: the entire group must simultaneously choose and perform the exact same character without any verbal agreement, signaling, or pre-planning.
7. Lead a collective countdown: 'One, two, three, jump!' On the final beat, every player instantly commits to one of the three options.
8. Pause to observe the chaotic mix of characters in the circle, encouraging the group to laugh at the mismatch.
9. Immediately initiate another round with the same countdown, encouraging players to use their peripheral vision and intuition to find alignment.
10. Repeat the rounds rapidly until the entire group achieves 100% consensus, then celebrate the victory.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't think, just leap! Let your body choose in the moment.'
- Pitfall: Players looking at each other and changing their choice mid-pose. Fix: Emphasize committing to the choice the exact second they land, holding the pose frozen so everyone can see the actual distribution.
- Coaching cue: 'Feel the room. Don't try to outsmart the group; try to blend with it.'
- Pitfall: Over-analyzing or trying to establish a pattern (e.g., 'we did alien last time, so now we do tiger'). Fix: Remind players to stay in the present moment and let go of strategic thinking.

## Variations
- Majority Rules: Players who choose the minority character(s) step out of the circle to become cheering spectators, narrowing the circle until a final group of matching players remains.
- New Characters: Let the group invent three entirely new characters with unique sounds and gestures (e.g., Robot, Pirate, Chicken) to play a second round.
- Silent Mode: Play the game with only the physical gestures, removing all vocalizations to heighten visual peripheral awareness.

## Debrief
- How did it feel when we finally achieved perfect alignment?
- What strategies did you find yourself using, and did they help or hinder the group mind?
- How does this game show the difference between 'trying to win' and 'trying to connect'?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of personal space when gesturing (especially the tiger claw). Offer low-impact physical alternatives for players with mobility limitations, such as smaller gestures or playing from a seated position.

## Why It Works
It bypasses the analytical brain by demanding instant, high-energy physical commitment. Because players cannot plan, they must rely on a shared, intuitive rhythm and acute peripheral awareness to find synchronization, turning failure into a shared laugh.

# Inside Out Amplification

> Transform your genuine, present-moment feelings into bold, physically expressive characters through gradual amplification.

## Overview
Inside Out Amplification is a somatic character-generation exercise that invites players to start with their actual, present-moment internal state rather than inventing a persona. By tuning into their real physical and emotional feelings and gradually dialing them up, players bypass intellectual planning to discover deeply grounded, vulnerable characters. The experience transitions from quiet self-reflection into a dynamic, expressive group environment where players interact from a place of absolute truth.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Vulnerability; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops emotional fluidity, vulnerability, and physical commitment by teaching players to use their authentic internal state as a foundation for character work, utilizing the Emotional Dial technique to scale expression from a subtle internal spark to a fully realized physical and vocal performance.

## Setup
An open, comfortable room with enough space for all participants to stand, sit, or lie down without crowding each other. No props or materials are required. The facilitator should prepare a calm, focused atmosphere.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to find a personal space in the room, either standing, sitting, or lying down, and close their eyes.
2. Guide players to take a few deep breaths and tune into their actual, present-moment physical and emotional state without judgment (e.g., fatigue, excitement, anxiety, calm).
3. Ask players to let go of any social mask or 'professional face' and allow their physical posture to subtly shift to match this internal feeling (e.g., slumping if tired, fidgeting if anxious).
4. Introduce the Emotional Dial technique: ask players to mentally set their current feeling at a level 1 or 2, and then slowly dial it up to a 4 or 5, letting the physical expression expand naturally.
5. Direct players to dial the emotion up to an 8 or 9, allowing the feeling to take over their entire body, encouraging them to make non-verbal sounds or sighs that match this heightened state.
6. Instruct players to open their eyes while maintaining this amplified physical and vocal state, keeping their focus on their own internal truth rather than looking for approval.
7. Have players begin moving slowly through the space, letting their amplified physical and vocal impulses guide their movement.
8. Encourage players to make brief, passing contact with others in the room—sharing a sound, a gesture, or a moment of eye contact—without dropping their amplified state or trying to 'fix' the interaction.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't invent a feeling. Whatever is actually there—even boredom or distraction—is your goldmine. Dial that up.'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Let the sound come from your gut, not your head. If you are tired, let out a heavy sigh. If you are restless, let out a jittery hum.'
- Pitfall: Players trying to be funny or choosing a stereotypical 'improv character' instead of working from their real state. Fix: Gently remind them to close their eyes for a moment, check back in with their actual body, and dial up the real sensation.
- Pitfall: Players dropping the physicalization as soon as they make eye contact with someone else. Fix: Coach them to 'hold the dial' and let the interaction happen through the physical state, not in spite of it.

## Variations
- The Dialogue Dial: Once players are comfortable interacting non-verbally, allow them to speak in short, simple sentences, keeping their dialogue entirely dictated by their dialed-up emotional state.
- Two-Person Scenes: Pair players up. Have them start a scene at a level 2 on their emotional dial, and periodically call out higher numbers (e.g., 'Dial to 5!', 'Dial to 9!') to watch how the relationship and stakes instantly escalate.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to start a character from your actual, real-time feelings rather than inventing one intellectually?
- What did you notice about the transition from a quiet, internal state to a highly expressive, externalized character?
- How does dialing up a real feeling make your stage presence feel more vulnerable and compelling to watch?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this exercise asks players to connect with their genuine, sometimes difficult emotions, explicitly state beforehand that all feelings are welcome and valid. Players have full agency over how intensely they dial up their emotions; they can choose to keep their dial at a manageable level if a feeling feels too overwhelming. Ensure a supportive, non-judgmental group agreement is established before beginning.

## Why It Works
This game works because it removes the cognitive load of 'inventing' a character, which often leads to superficial or stereotypical choices. By anchoring the character in the player's actual somatic reality and using the Emotional Dial to scale it, the performance remains deeply truthful and vulnerable. The physical and vocal amplification bypasses the analytical mind, allowing for spontaneous, emotionally fluid choices that feel incredibly authentic to both the player and the audience.

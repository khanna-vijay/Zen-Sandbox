# Echo Tag

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Step in, repeat a line, and launch a brand-new scene from that exact moment.

## Overview
A dynamic scene-work exercise where players on the sideline actively listen for inspiring dialogue. By stepping in and repeating a line verbatim, they swap places with the original speaker and instantly initiate a new scene using that line as the opening prompt.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Yes, And; Start in the Middle
- **Skill(s):** Support Work; Pacing & Rhythm; Active Listening; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Tap-ins; Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light); C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** Develops active listening, rapid scene initiation, and seamless ensemble support through physical and verbal tap-ins.

## Setup
A performance space with two players starting a scene in the center, and the remaining players standing on the sideline ready to edit. No props or materials are needed.

## How to Play
1. Two players step into the performance space and begin a standard, relationship-driven scene.
2. The remaining players stand on the sideline, listening intently to every line of dialogue.
3. At any point, a sideline player who hears an inspiring or evocative line steps forward into the space.
4. Upon stepping in, the sideline player repeats the chosen line verbatim, matching or re-interpreting the emotional delivery.
5. The player who originally spoke that line immediately exits the stage to join the sideline.
6. The remaining original player and the new player instantly begin a completely new scene, using the repeated line as the first line of dialogue.
7. The cycle continues with new sideline players tapping in, keeping the pacing brisk and the transitions seamless.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Listen for the line that makes you want to play, not just any line!'
- Pitfall: Players hesitating or waiting too long to tap in. Fix: Encourage rapid-fire edits; the transition should feel like a musical beat.
- Coaching cue: 'Match the physical position of the person you are replacing, or start fresh in the space.'
- Pitfall: The remaining player trying to continue the old scene. Fix: Remind them that a repeated line must launch a new reality, characters, and relationship.

## Variations
- Emotional Shift: The incoming player must repeat the line but with a completely different emotional subtext or attitude than the original delivery.
- Full Sweep: The incoming player repeats the line, but both original players exit, and a second sideline player joins to start a fresh two-person scene.
- Continuity Echo: Instead of starting a new scene, the incoming player uses the repeated line to heighten or pivot the existing scene, keeping the same characters.

## Debrief
- How did listening for a specific line to repeat change how closely you paid attention to the scene?
- What made a line ripe for an echo edit?
- How did it feel to instantly adapt to a new character and relationship when your partner was swapped out?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure clear physical paths for stepping in and out to avoid collisions. Players should make eye contact or use a clear vocal cue to signal the swap safely.

## Why It Works
It forces players to listen to the exact words spoken rather than planning their next move. By using the repeated line as a launchpad, it bypasses overthinking and instantly establishes a 'Start in the Middle' dynamic.

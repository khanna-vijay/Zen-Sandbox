# Name Volley

![Name Volley — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G1200__name-volley.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Keep an imaginary volleyball in play by calling out names and tracking the group's focus.

## Overview
A high-energy, physical warm-up where players work in teams to keep an invisible volleyball in the air. By calling out a teammate's name right before passing, players practice rapid focus-shifting, physical commitment, and collective spatial awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Object work
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To build peripheral awareness, active listening, and group mind by tracking a single physical focus point while dynamically gifting focus to others.

## Setup
Divide players into two equal teams of 3 to 6 players. Have the teams face each other across an imaginary net in a moderate-sized room with clear boundaries. No physical props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into two teams standing on opposite sides of an imaginary volleyball net.
2. Introduce a single, imaginary volleyball into the space, establishing its size, weight, and speed through physical demonstration.
3. To initiate play, one player serves the imaginary ball across the net by miming the hit and loudly calling out the name of a player on the opposing team.
4. The named player must track the ball's trajectory, physically catch or bump it, and immediately call out another player's name to pass it.
5. Every player must maintain physical commitment to the ball's imaginary flight path, reacting to its height, speed, and position even when they are not the target.
6. If the ball is dropped due to a missed name or slow reaction, the facilitator calls a brief pause, and the team that dropped it serves to restart.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Keep your eyes on the ball, even when your name isn't called!
- Coaching cue: Make your pass generous! Give your partner time to react by calling their name before or as you hit it.
- Pitfall: Players calling names too late, causing the receiver to have no reaction time. Fix: Remind players that the name is the wind-up and the hit is the release.
- Pitfall: The ball loses its physical reality, shrinking or moving instantly. Fix: Side-coach the physical weight and trajectory of the ball by calling out its height and speed.

## Variations
- Multi-Ball: Introduce a second imaginary ball of a different color or weight to increase the cognitive load and thread-tracking difficulty.
- No-Net Free-for-All: Remove the teams and net; players stand in a loose circle and volley the ball to anyone in the circle, requiring 360-degree peripheral awareness.
- Emotional Volley: The person passing passes the ball with a specific emotion, and the receiver must catch it and adopt that emotion.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to track the ball when you weren't the one being called?
- What physical cues helped you anticipate where the ball was going before a name was even finished?
- How does this game illustrate the concept of making your partner look good?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards. Players with mobility limitations can participate by adjusting the physical intensity of their hits and catches, focusing instead on vocal projection and eye contact.

## Why It Works
It forces players to balance internal focus with external focus. This dual-focus builds the Group Mind by aligning everyone's physical and mental attention to a single, shared, imaginary object.

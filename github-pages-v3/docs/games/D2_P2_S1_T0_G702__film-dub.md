# Cinematic Dubbing

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Improvise the dialogue and sound effects for a muted movie clip in real time.

## Overview
In this high-energy comedy game, players stand alongside a projection screen and provide the voices for characters in a muted video clip. The performers must match the lip movements, physical actions, and emotional shifts of the actors on screen. The result is a hilarious, fast-paced exercise in spontaneous justification and tight synchronization.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Vocal Craft; Active Listening; Offer Reception; World-Building; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Vocal characterization; Endowment-acceptance; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops active listening, rapid offer reception, and vocal craft by requiring players to instantly justify external visual cues and integrate them with their partner's verbal choices.

## Setup
Set up a projector and screen visible to both the audience and the players. Prepare a selection of short, muted video clips (1-2 minutes long) with clear character interactions. Position two to four players at the side of the screen with microphones (optional) so they can see the footage clearly without blocking the audience's view.

## How to Play
1. Assign each player to a specific character visible in the upcoming video clip.
2. Start the muted video clip, ensuring the players and audience can see it clearly.
3. Speak only when your assigned character's mouth is moving on screen, stopping immediately when their mouth closes.
4. Listen closely to your scene partners, accepting their verbal offers and building a cohesive narrative together.
5. Justify the physical actions on screen by explaining why your character is moving, gesturing, or reacting in that specific way.
6. Incorporate vocal sound effects for environmental elements like doors slamming, footsteps, or objects dropping to build the world.
7. Continue dubbing the scene dynamically until the clip ends or the facilitator calls edit.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to watch the screen constantly: 'Eyes on the screen, match the mouth shapes!'
- Encourage immediate justification: 'If they run, tell us why they are running right now!'
- Pitfall: Players ignore the video and perform a pre-planned scene. Fix: Remind them that the screen is the director; every movement is an offer that must be accepted.
- Pitfall: Overlapping dialogue where characters speak over each other. Fix: Coach players to respect the natural turn-taking of the visual edit.

## Variations
- Genre Flip: Dub a serious dramatic scene as a specific comedic genre, such as a soap opera, sci-fi epic, or film noir.
- Foreign Translation: Play a clip in an unfamiliar foreign language at a very low volume, and have players 'translate' it into English while matching the original vocal cadence.
- Foley Split: Two players provide the dialogue for the characters, while two other players provide only the physical sound effects and environmental noises.

## Debrief
- How did having your physical actions dictated by the screen affect your ability to listen to your partner?
- What did you discover about the relationship between physical movement and vocal emotion?
- How did you handle moments where the character on screen did something completely unexpected?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure video clips do not contain flashing lights, intense violence, or triggering content. For players with visual impairments, a partner can act as a 'spotter' to describe the physical actions in real-time, or the player can focus entirely on providing environmental sound effects based on the dialogue cues.

## Why It Works
By removing control over physical movement, the game forces players into a state of pure reaction. They must treat the visual actions on screen as absolute truths (Yes) and use their dialogue to explain why those actions are happening (And), perfectly demonstrating the core mechanics of justification and base reality.

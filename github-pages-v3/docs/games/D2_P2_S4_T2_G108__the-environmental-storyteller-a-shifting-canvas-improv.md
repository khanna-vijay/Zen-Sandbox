# The Shifting Canvas

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Co-create a seamless narrative by dynamically adapting to your partner's changing virtual environments.

## Overview
A high-energy virtual game where players use pre-prepared digital backgrounds as dynamic scenic offers. By rapidly changing their virtual backdrops, players instantly shift the narrative's location, forcing their partners to immediately accept and integrate the new environment. The result is a fast-paced, visually rich story where the setting itself acts as an active character and plot driver.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Narrative Architecture; World-Building; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Meisner Repetition; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Story Spine; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop rapid offer reception and endowment-acceptance by treating visual, environmental changes as immediate narrative truths that must be validated and built upon.

## Setup
Designed for 6 to 12 players in a virtual meeting space. Before the session, instruct all participants to prepare 5 to 7 distinct, high-contrast virtual backgrounds (e.g., a desert, an underwater trench, a spaceship, a cozy library). Players must know how to quickly toggle these backgrounds. Ensure the platform is set to Gallery View, and the facilitator is ready to use spotlighting and monitor the chat.

## How to Play
1. 1. Begin in Gallery View with all players using a neutral or simple starting virtual background.
2. 2. Run a 30-second warm-up where everyone rapidly cycles through their prepared backgrounds to test technical setup and spark visual inspiration.
3. 3. The facilitator spotlights a starting player, who initiates the scene by establishing their character and immediate situation based on their current background.
4. 4. The starting player calls out another participant by name, and the facilitator immediately spotlights this second player to join the scene.
5. 5. The second player must immediately 'Yes, And' the first player's premise, verbally and physically justifying how their own current background connects to the established scene.
6. 6. At any point, the active speaker can shift the narrative's location by typing 'SHIFT' in the chat, instantly changing their virtual background, and immediately justifying the transition in their dialogue.
7. 7. Other players use the chat to signal their desire to jump in next, allowing the facilitator to dynamically update the spotlighted players and maintain a smooth narrative flow without audio overlap.
8. 8. After 15 minutes of continuous play, the facilitator calls out 'Two more shifts!' to guide the final two spotlighted players to bring the sprawling story to a satisfying conclusion.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Play the environment, not just the script!' Encourage players to physically interact with their virtual backgrounds, such as ducking under a branch or reacting to the temperature.
- Pitfall: Technical lag when changing backgrounds. Fix: Instruct players to lean into the lag and narrate it as a plot point, such as a malfunctioning teleporter or a glitch in the matrix.
- Pitfall: Ignoring the visual offers. Fix: If players get caught up in verbal dialogue and ignore their partner's background, side-coach with: 'Look at their screen! Where are they right now? How does that affect you?'
- Coaching Cue: 'Make your partner's background a gift.' If your partner switches to a volcano, immediately react to the heat or the glow on your own screen.

## Variations
- The Silent Shift: Players are not allowed to verbally explain their own background change; their partner must entirely explain and justify why the location suddenly transformed.
- The Prop Hunt: Players must find a physical household object within arm's reach that matches or hilariously contrasts with their new virtual background and bring it into the frame when they switch.
- Blind Shifting: Players close their eyes, randomly select a virtual background from their list, open their eyes, and must instantly justify whatever environment they landed on.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have the physical reality of your scene constantly rewritten by your partner's background changes?
- What strategies did you use to instantly accept and justify a completely unexpected environment?
- How did physicalizing your reaction to the virtual background (e.g., shivering in a snowy background) help make the scene feel real?
- How can we apply this level of rapid 'Yes, And' acceptance to unexpected changes in our daily collaborative work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all participants are comfortable with having their cameras on. If a participant cannot use virtual backgrounds due to hardware limitations, allow them to use physical drawings, hand-held signs, or descriptive verbal 'green-screening' to represent their changing environments. Ensure background choices are kept PG and respectful of all participants.

## Why It Works
This game translates the physical 'where' of traditional improv into a digital mechanic. By making the environment highly visual and volatile, it forces players off their scripted ideas and demands absolute presence. It gamifies 'Yes, And' by turning every background change into an undeniable physical reality that cannot be ignored, training players to treat every unexpected change as a creative gift rather than an obstacle.

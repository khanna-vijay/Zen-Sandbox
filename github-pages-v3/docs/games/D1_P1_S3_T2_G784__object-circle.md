# Mimed Object Relay

> Pass invisible objects around a circle while preserving their exact physical properties through committed mime.

## Overview
Players sit in a circle and silently pass several imaginary objects of varying sizes, weights, and textures to their neighbors. The challenge is to maintain the physical integrity of each object as it travels, requiring deep focus, precise physical commitment, and active listening. It is a quiet, highly focused exercise that builds spatial awareness and physical consistency.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind

**Objective:** To develop physical commitment and precise object work by practicing the preservation of weight, shape, and texture without verbal communication, while fostering group mind and peripheral awareness.

## Setup
Have all players sit in a circle close enough to easily pass items to their neighbors. No physical props are needed. The facilitator should designate three to four starting players spaced relatively evenly around the circle.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to sit in a circle and remain completely silent throughout the exercise.
2. Designate three or four players spaced evenly around the circle to be the creators of the initial objects.
3. On the facilitator's cue, each creator silently brings an imaginary object into existence, establishing its weight, size, shape, and texture through deliberate physical interaction.
4. Once the creator has clearly defined their object, they carefully hand it to the person sitting to their left.
5. The receiving player must actively observe the hand-off, accept the object while matching its established physical properties, and interact with it briefly.
6. The receiving player then passes the object to the next person to their left, maintaining its physical integrity.
7. This passing pattern continues around the circle, meaning multiple invisible objects will be in motion simultaneously, requiring players to stay highly alert.
8. The round ends when each object successfully makes its way back to its original creator, who takes it back and silently sets it down.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to focus on the transition points: the moment of release and the moment of capture are where object properties are most easily lost.
- If an object's size or weight changes drastically, gently pause the pass and ask the receiver to re-weight it based on how the sender held it.
- Encourage players to use their whole bodies, not just their hands, to show weight by engaging the core, shoulders, and facial expressions.
- Watch out for rushing; remind players to take a moment to fully experience and define the object before passing it.

## Variations
- Directional Cross: Have some objects travel clockwise and others counter-clockwise, forcing players to manage intersections where two objects arrive at the same person simultaneously.
- Emotional Objects: Assign an emotional quality to each object that players must adopt and pass along.
- Blind Pass: Players must keep their eyes closed until the person next to them taps them on the shoulder to hand over the object, forcing them to read the object's properties purely through physical touch and hand placement.

## Debrief
- What physical cues made it easiest to understand the weight and shape of an object before you even touched it?
- How did it feel when an object changed properties along the way, and how did you work to restore its original form?
- How does committing 100% to the physical reality of an invisible object make it easier for your scene partner to play along?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries during the hand-off. Since this involves close physical proximity and simulated touch, players should establish clear, comfortable spacing and can mime the hand-off with a slight gap to avoid unwanted physical contact.

## Why It Works
This game works because it strips away verbal communication, forcing players to rely entirely on physical offer reception and active listening. By requiring the preservation of physical properties, it teaches that object work is a collaborative agreement; when players commit 100% to the weight and volume of the space, the illusion becomes a shared reality that supports group mind.

# Instant Backup Dancers

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Perform a freshly witnessed dance routine with absolute confidence, celebrating every glorious mistake along the way.

## Overview
Players form small teams to create and perform a short, sung-and-danced routine, which another team must immediately attempt to replicate from memory. The magic lies not in perfect replication, but in the joyful, high-energy commitment to faking it when the choreography is forgotten. It shifts the focus from perfection to playful recovery and mutual support.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Self-Recovery; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Peripheral Awareness; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Reframe-the-flub reps; Mirror exercise; Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To build resilience against mistakes (reframe-the-flub reps) by practicing high-commitment physical performance under pressure, turning memory lapses into confident, shared physical choices.

## Setup
An open room with plenty of space for movement. Divide the group of 10 to 25 players into smaller teams of 4 to 5 players. No music players or props are needed; teams will provide their own vocals.

## How to Play
1. Divide the players into teams of 4 to 5 and spread them out across the space.
2. Give the teams 3 to 5 minutes to select a universally known song, agree on a simple 30-second vocal and physical routine, and practice it together.
3. Gather all teams back to form an audience. Designate Team A to perform first, and Team B to be their designated 'shadow' team.
4. Team A performs their routine, singing their song and executing their choreography with high energy.
5. Team B watches closely, adopting the mindset of world-class professional dancers who can instantly absorb any choreography.
6. Immediately upon Team A finishing, Team B takes the stage and attempts to perform Team A's exact routine, singing the same song and mimicking the movements as closely as possible.
7. When Team B forgets a move (which they inevitably will), they must not freeze or apologize; instead, they must confidently invent a new movement, which their teammates must instantly mirror and support.
8. Rotate roles so every team gets to perform their original routine and copy another team's routine.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'No apologies! If you mess up, make it bigger and do it twice!'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Look at each other! If someone makes a mistake, copy them and make it the new choreography!'
- Pitfall: Teams trying to make their original choreography too complex. Fix: Remind them during the prep phase that simple, repetitive movements are easier to copy and more fun to watch.
- Pitfall: Players freezing or laughing self-consciously when they forget a step. Fix: Encourage them to 'fake it till you make it' with high-status posture and intense eye contact.

## Variations
- Silent Echo: The original team performs their dance in complete silence (no singing), forcing the copying team to rely purely on visual cues and rhythm.
- The Telephone Line: Team A performs for Team B. Team B copies it for Team C (who didn't see Team A). Team C copies Team B for Team D, observing how the dance mutates.
- Slow-Motion Recovery: If a player makes a mistake, the entire copying team must transition into slow motion for three seconds to highlight and celebrate the 'flub' before returning to normal speed.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to perform a dance you didn't actually know with absolute confidence?
- What happened when someone made a mistake during the copy phase? How did the group support that mistake?
- How can we apply the 'fake it with commitment' mindset to verbal scenes when we lose our train of thought?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical movements chosen are accessible to all team members. Encourage teams to design choreography that respects everyone's physical boundaries and mobility levels. Remind players that high energy does not require high-impact acrobatics.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to replicate physical movement under time pressure, the analytical brain is bypassed. When memory fails, the player must rely on physical commitment and group mind. Because everyone is in the same boat, the fear of failure evaporates, reframing mistakes as hilarious, shared discoveries rather than individual errors.

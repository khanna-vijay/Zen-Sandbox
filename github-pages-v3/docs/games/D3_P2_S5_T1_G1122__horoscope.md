# Cosmic Forecast

![Cosmic Forecast — infographic](img/D3_P2_S5_T1_G1122__horoscope.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Transform abstract astrological predictions into a grounded, character-driven scene using C.R.O.W. principles.

## Overview
In this performance-oriented game, players use a real horoscope read from a magazine as their suggestion. Instead of playing the literal text, players deconstruct the reading to establish a rich, grounded base reality. The resulting scene explores the emotional subtext and relationships implied by the cosmic advice.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Serve the Story; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Narrative Architecture; Suggestion Deconstruction (A-to-C); Room Reading
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); What's interesting about this? mining; Reading the suggestion's intent
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop skills in suggestion deconstruction, world-building, and establishing a strong base reality (C.R.O.W.) from abstract prompts.

## Setup
Two players stand on stage. The facilitator holds a magazine, newspaper, or printout containing horoscopes. An audience is present to provide a birth date or zodiac sign.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator asks an audience member for their zodiac sign or birth date.
2. The facilitator reads the corresponding horoscope aloud to the players and the audience.
3. The performing players take a brief silent moment to deconstruct the reading, identifying key themes, emotional tones, or warnings.
4. Player A initiates the scene by establishing a physical environment (Where) and a clear activity, grounding the abstract horoscope in a physical reality.
5. Player B enters, immediately establishing a relationship (Relationship) and character dynamic (Character) that reflects the emotional subtext of the horoscope.
6. Both players discover their characters' immediate objectives (Objective) based on the horoscope's advice, integrating it naturally into their behavior.
7. The players play out a grounded scene, prioritizing their established C.R.O.W. elements over literal references to astrology.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid literal mentions of the horoscope; the prediction should be the subtext, not the dialogue.
- If the scene becomes too abstract, call out: 'Find the physical object work! Where are you right now?'
- Encourage players to focus on one specific line or phrase from the reading rather than trying to incorporate the entire paragraph.
- Pitfall: Players playing astrologers or psychics. Fix: Remind them to play ordinary people in an everyday situation affected by the horoscope's themes.

## Variations
- Day in the Life: Use the horoscope to inspire a series of short, connected scenes showing how the prediction manifests throughout an audience member's typical day.
- The Contrarian: Players must actively defy the advice of the horoscope, creating comedic tension as they fight their cosmic destiny.
- Dual Signs: Get signs from two different audience members; Player A plays under the influence of Sign A, while Player B plays under Sign B.

## Debrief
- How did we translate abstract astrological advice into concrete physical actions and relationships?
- What was the difference between playing the literal words of the horoscope versus playing the emotional subtext?
- How did establishing a strong base reality first help us navigate the specificity of the suggestion?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the horoscope read is lighthearted and free of sensitive topics like health crises or tragedy. If an audience member seems hesitant to share their sign, move to another volunteer warmly and quickly.

## Why It Works
This game forces players to practice A-to-C suggestion deconstruction. By taking an abstract concept like a horoscope and filtering it through C.R.O.W., players learn to build robust, believable worlds that feel connected to the suggestion without being literal or repetitive.

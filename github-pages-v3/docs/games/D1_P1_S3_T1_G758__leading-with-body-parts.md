# Physical Centers

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Transform your movement and discover instant characters by leading your walk with different body parts.

## Overview
Players walk around a shared space, shifting their physical center of gravity to different parts of their body as prompted by the facilitator. By exaggerating these physical shifts, players naturally discover distinct character voices, attitudes, and tempos, moving from abstract movement to fully realized characters.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self

**Objective:** To develop physical commitment and character generation through body centers, bypassing intellectual planning to let physical posture dictate character choices.

## Setup
A large, open room cleared of obstacles so players can walk freely without colliding. No props needed.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to walk around the room at a normal, neutral pace, filling the empty spaces and maintaining soft eye contact with others.
2. Call out a specific body part, such as the nose, the chest, the pelvis, or the knees, and instruct players to imagine an invisible string pulling them forward from that exact point.
3. Direct players to gradually exaggerate this physical lead, letting it alter their posture, stride, and speed.
4. Ask players to notice how this physical shift affects their internal emotional state or attitude, observing how leading with the chest might feel proud, while leading with the forehead might feel intellectual or anxious.
5. Prompt players to add a simple vocalization or a repetitive phrase that matches the physical energy of their current walk.
6. Instruct players to briefly interact with one another in character as they pass, using their physical center to guide how they greet or avoid others.
7. Call out 'neutral' to have players return to their normal, everyday walk before introducing a new physical center.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Let the body lead, not the brain. If you lead with your chin, where do your eyes naturally go?'
- Pitfall: Players might intellectualize the character first and then try to force the walk. Fix: Remind them to start purely with the physical point of pull and let the character emerge as a surprise.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Commit 100% to the exaggeration. Make it 10 times bigger, then find a sustainable version that still keeps that core energy.'
- Pitfall: Players walking in a repetitive circle. Fix: Encourage them to cut across the space, change directions, and actively fill the gaps.

## Variations
- The Dial: The facilitator calls out numbers from 1 to 10 to control the intensity of the physical lead, with 1 being barely noticeable and 10 being extremely cartoonish.
- Status Shift: Assign different body parts to represent high status versus low status and have players interact based on those physical dynamics.
- Pass the Lead: In pairs, one player leads with a body part, and the other must mirror it, then they swap who chooses the leading body part.

## Debrief
- How did changing your physical center instantly change your mood or status without you having to think about it?
- Which body parts felt the most comfortable or natural to lead with, and which felt the most challenging?
- How does committing fully to a physical choice help quiet the inner critic that tries to plan a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of their physical boundaries and any pre-existing injuries; encourage them to adapt the prompts to their own mobility levels without strain.

## Why It Works
Physicality bypasses the analytical mind. By focusing on a simple mechanical constraint, players tap into spontaneous inspiration and discover complex characters organically through muscle memory and posture rather than intellectual invention.

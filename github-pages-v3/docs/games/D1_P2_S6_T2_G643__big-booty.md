# Big Booty

![Big Booty — infographic](img/D1_P2_S6_T2_G643__big-booty.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy rhythm game where making mistakes is celebrated with a collective, joyful reset.

## Overview
A fast-paced, rhythmic circle game where players pass a verbal pulse using assigned numbers and a central title. When a player slips up, the entire group celebrates the mistake, the player moves to the end of the line, and the numbers shift, challenging everyone's focus and adaptability.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle. One player is designated as the leader ('Big Booty'). Moving clockwise from the leader, the remaining players are numbered sequentially starting from 'Number One' up to the total number of players minus one.

## How to Play
1. Establish a steady, collective rhythm by having everyone clap their hands and slap their thighs in unison.
2. Initiate the opening chant together in rhythm: 'Ah, yeah! Big Booty, ah yeah! Big Booty, Big Booty, Big Booty!'
3. The leader starts the passing sequence on beat by calling out their own title followed by another player's number (e.g., 'Big Booty, Number Four!').
4. The player whose number was called must immediately respond on the next beat by stating their own number, followed by another target number or the leader's title (e.g., 'Number Four, Number Two!').
5. The sequence continues seamlessly in rhythm: the called player states their own number first, then passes it to someone else.
6. If a player hesitates, loses the rhythm, calls a non-existent number, or fails to recognize their own number, they flub.
7. When a flub occurs, the entire group immediately stops, cheers enthusiastically to celebrate the mistake, and restarts the introductory chant.
8. The player who made the mistake moves to the highest-numbered position (immediately to the leader's right), causing everyone between their old spot and new spot to shift up one number.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Leap into the mistake! Don't apologize, just celebrate and slide to your new spot.'
- Pitfall: Players slowing down the tempo to avoid mistakes. Fix: Keep the physical beat brisk and unyielding; speed is the catalyst for joyful failure.
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes moving. Your number can change at any second, so stay active in the space.'
- Pitfall: Shaming or frustration when someone breaks the chain. Fix: Model extreme enthusiasm during the reset chant so the reset feels like a reward.

## Variations
- Name Game: Instead of numbers, use players' actual names, which eliminates the shifting-number complexity but increases the speed of personal connection.
- Sound Effects: Replace numbers with distinct physical gestures and sound effects assigned to each position in the circle.
- Double Time: Gradually increase the tempo of the slap-clap beat after every successful round-trip back to the leader.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to make a mistake in front of the group when the response was a joyful cheer?
- What strategies did you use to keep track of your changing identity as people shifted?
- How does celebrating a flub change your willingness to take risks in an improv scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical movement of shifting spots in the circle is accessible to all players; players can swap places at a comfortable walking pace, and the rhythm can pause briefly to accommodate physical transitions.

## Why It Works
By pairing a cognitive slip-up with an immediate, high-energy group celebration, the game re-wires the brain's fear of failure. The shifting numbers force players to let go of past mistakes instantly, practicing rapid self-recovery and staying anchored in the present moment.

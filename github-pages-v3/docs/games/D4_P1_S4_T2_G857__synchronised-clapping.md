# Synchronized Pulse

![Synchronized Pulse — infographic](img/D4_P1_S4_T2_G857__synchronised-clapping.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pass a single, simultaneous clap around the circle using pure eye contact and shared timing.

## Overview
A high-focus circle game where players pass a clap from person to person. Instead of one person clapping at another, both partners must clap together at the exact same millisecond, requiring deep eye contact and mutual anticipation. The pulse can travel around the perimeter, reverse direction, or leap across the circle.

## What It Trains

**Objective:** Develops group mind, precise timing, and active listening by shifting focus from individual action to shared, synchronized moments.

## Setup
Players stand in a clear circle with enough space to turn and face their neighbors. No props are needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle facing inward.
2. Player A turns to face Player B, their neighbor to the left.
3. Player A and Player B make eye contact, establish a shared rhythm, and clap their hands together at the exact same moment.
4. Player B then turns to face Player C, makes eye contact, and they execute a simultaneous clap.
5. This synchronized clap continues sequentially around the circle in one direction.
6. To change direction, the receiving player can execute a double-clap with their partner, signaling that the pulse must reverse.
7. To pass across the circle, a player can make eye contact with someone opposite them and execute a synchronized clap across the distance, transferring the pulse.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Don't clap at them, clap with them. Look for the shared breath.
- Pitfall: Players rushing and clapping out of sync. Fix: Slow down the rotation and emphasize eye contact before the hands move.
- Coaching cue: Follow the follower. Both of you are responsible for the timing, not just the sender.
- Pitfall: Hesitation or overthinking the cross-passes. Fix: Encourage players to commit to their target with clear physical orientation.

## Variations
- Sound and Motion: Replace the clap with a synchronized vocal sound and physical gesture.
- Multi-Pulse: Introduce a second pulse moving in the opposite direction, requiring players to manage two simultaneous points of focus.
- Silent Pulse: Play the game entirely in silence, using only a synchronized nod or silent gesture instead of a clap.

## Debrief
- How did it feel when a clap was perfectly synchronized versus slightly off? What made the difference?
- How did you anticipate your partner's timing without verbal cues?
- How does this level of shared focus translate to supporting a scene partner on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players with physical limitations regarding clapping or standing can participate by using eye contact, nods, or vocalizations instead of hand claps, and offer chairs if standing is difficult.

## Why It Works
It strips away verbal communication and forces players to rely entirely on non-verbal cues, breath, and micro-movements. By requiring both players to be responsible for the exact moment of the clap, it embodies follow the follower and builds a tangible sense of Group Mind.

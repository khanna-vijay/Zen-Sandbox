# Human Puppets

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Speak the words while your partner moves your body, or move your partner to match their words.

## Overview
Two actors perform a scene but are physically paralyzed, relying entirely on two other actors to physically manipulate their bodies like puppets. The speaking actors must justify every physical movement forced upon them, while the moving actors must physically support and reflect the spoken dialogue. This creates a hilarious, highly collaborative dynamic where physical action and verbal narrative are split between partners.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Physicality & Space Work; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Mirror exercise; Character Walks/Centers; Justify the absurd

**Objective:** Develops physical endowment-acceptance, justification of external offers, and deep partner connection by forcing players to 'yes-and' physical choices they did not initiate.

## Setup
Four players split into two pairs. Each pair consists of one 'Puppet' and one 'Puppeteer'. The two Puppets stand center stage facing each other, while their respective Puppeteers stand closely behind or beside them. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the four players into two pairs, designating one player in each pair as the Puppet (the speaker) and the other as the Puppeteer (the mover).
2. Position the two Puppets in the center of the playing space, and have the Puppeteers stand directly behind them, ready to physically guide their movements.
3. Establish the core rule: Puppets are completely limp and cannot move any part of their body unless physically guided by their Puppeteer, but they are the only ones who can speak.
4. Get a simple suggestion from the group, such as a location or a relationship, to inspire the scene.
5. Begin the scene with the Puppets speaking dialogue to initiate the interaction.
6. As the Puppets speak, the Puppeteers must physically move their Puppets' limbs, head, and torso to perform actions, handle imaginary objects, or express physical emotions.
7. The Puppets must immediately justify whatever physical position or movement their Puppeteer puts them in, integrating the physical state into their dialogue and character choices.
8. The Puppeteers must listen closely to the dialogue and move their Puppets in ways that support, enhance, or humorously complicate what is being said.
9. Continue the scene, focusing on the symbiotic relationship where movement inspires dialogue and dialogue inspires movement, until a natural conclusion is reached.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Puppets, keep your muscles relaxed! Let your partner do one hundred percent of the physical work.'
- Coaching cue: 'Puppeteers, listen to the words. If your puppet says they are holding a cup, shape their hand into a cup.'
- Pitfall: Puppets moving themselves out of habit. Fix: Remind them to freeze instantly if their puppeteer lets go, or gently side-coach them to 'stay limp.'
- Pitfall: Puppeteers being too passive or too aggressive. Fix: Encourage gentle, deliberate movements that clearly communicate an action or posture rather than chaotic shaking.

## Variations
- Remote Puppeteering: Puppeteers stand at a distance and mimic the movements they want their puppets to make, requiring the puppets to watch them out of the corner of their eye without physical touch.
- Emotional Puppets: Puppeteers manipulate the facial expressions and posture of the puppets to dictate their emotional state, which the puppets must immediately adopt and justify.
- Audience Puppeteers: Invite two audience members to act as the puppeteers, putting the improvisers in the puppet roles to justify unpredictable movements.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your physical choices completely controlled by someone else?
- How did the physical movements help you discover new lines of dialogue or character traits you wouldn't have thought of otherwise?
- What strategies did the Puppeteers use to make their Puppets look good and support the story?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game involves physical contact, establish clear boundaries before starting. Puppeteers must ask their Puppets where they are comfortable being touched (e.g., shoulders, elbows, wrists only) and must use gentle, respectful physical guidance. If physical touch is uncomfortable for any player, use the Remote Puppeteering variation instead.

## Why It Works
It forces a literal division of labor between physical action and verbal choice, highlighting how physical endowment shapes narrative. By separating these elements, players learn the power of justification—making sense of an unexpected physical state—and experience the pure collaborative joy of physical 'Yes, And'.

# The Legend of So-and-So

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build a vivid offstage character through gossip, then watch a partner bring them to life.

## Overview
In this exercise, a small group of players establishes a rich, detailed backstory for an absent character through natural conversation. Once the character's traits, quirks, and history are firmly established, a final player enters the scene embodying that exact persona, instantly validating every endowment. The game highlights the power of active listening, collaborative world-building, and making your partner look like a genius.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Base Reality First; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Active Gifting; World-Building; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Endowment chains
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To practice high-level endowment-acceptance and active gifting. Players learn to listen closely to offstage setup details and immediately physicalize and vocalize those traits upon entry, while the setup players learn to support and celebrate their partner's choices.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with 4 to 6 players. One player is designated as the 'Guest' and steps slightly aside (or turns their back, but remains close enough to hear the conversation clearly). The remaining 3 to 5 players stand in a semi-circle on stage, establishing a realistic, grounded environment (e.g., a retirement party, a faculty lounge, or a family reunion).

## How to Play
1. Select one player to be the 'Guest' who will enter later; this player stands nearby, listening intently to every detail mentioned.
2. The remaining players on stage begin a grounded scene, establishing a shared environment and their relationship to an absent, highly specific individual (e.g., 'Aunt Beatrice' or 'Captain Sterling').
3. The onstage players organically seed specific details about the absent character, including physical quirks, catchphrases, emotional temperaments, and shared history (e.g., 'I hope she doesn't wear that squeaky leather patch again' or 'He always clears his throat three times before speaking').
4. Keep the tone realistic and conversational rather than a rapid-fire list of absurdities, building a cohesive 'base reality' first.
5. Once 4 to 5 distinct details have been established, the 'Guest' player makes a physical entrance into the scene.
6. The entering player must immediately embody as many of the established traits as possible (e.g., squeaking, throat-clearing, wearing a specific attitude) without hesitation.
7. The onstage players immediately receive the Guest, treating them with the exact status and relationship previously described, making the Guest's choices look brilliant and intentional.
8. The scene continues for a brief moment to let the group play in the newly completed reality, then edits.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the onstage players to avoid 'piling on' too many contradictory or physically impossible traits. Keep the endowments grounded so the entering player can actually play them.
- If the entering player misses a detail, the onstage players should not correct them. Instead, adapt and justify: 'Oh, you got rid of the squeaky patch! It looks great without it!'
- Encourage the entering player to commit 100% to the physical and vocal choices right from their first step onto the stage.
- Pitfall: Onstage players listing traits like a checklist. Fix: Remind them to weave the details into emotional, relational dialogue (e.g., 'I'm worried about him' rather than 'He has a limp and a blue hat').

## Variations
- Blind Entrance: The entering player wears headphones or steps out of the room so they cannot hear the gossip. Upon entering, they must make bold choices, and the onstage players must instantly justify how those choices match the gossip they just whispered.
- The High-Status Arrival: Focus specifically on status dynamics, where the offstage character is of extremely high or low status, requiring the entering player to adjust their physical posture and vocal power accordingly.

## Debrief
- For the entering player: How did it feel to have your character pre-defined for you? Did it make your entrance easier or harder?
- For the onstage players: How did you balance giving fun details without overwhelming your partner?
- How does this exercise demonstrate the principle of 'making your partner look like a genius'?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that physical endowments (such as limps, stutters, or sensory differences) are treated with respect and avoid punching down or relying on offensive stereotypes. Focus instead on quirky habits, specific clothing, catchphrases, or emotional temperaments.

## Why It Works
This game works because it removes the pressure of invention for the entering player; their entire character concept is gifted to them by their teammates. By accepting these endowments instantly, the entering player validates the group's collective world-building, creating a powerful sense of group mind and mutual support.

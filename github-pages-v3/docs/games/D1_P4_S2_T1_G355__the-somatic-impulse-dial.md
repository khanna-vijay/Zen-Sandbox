# The Somatic Impulse Dial

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Master emotional range and physical commitment by scaling raw, instinctive impulses from one to ten.

## Overview
A high-commitment physical and vocal drill where players translate abstract prompts into immediate somatic states. By scaling their physical and emotional expression up and down a numerical dial, players build deep control over their performance intensity. The essence of the state is then passed silently around a circle, challenging the next player to receive and transform the impulse.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Vulnerability; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Silence & Stillness; Self-Recovery; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Character Walks/Centers; Projection & breath support; Gibberish; Do nothing exercises; Hold-the-beat reps; Emotional-echo drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop emotional fluidity and somatic control by practicing the 'Emotional Dial' technique, training players to trust their first instinctive physical impulses and scale their expressive intensity dynamically.

## Setup
An open, comfortable room with enough space for players to move and vocalize freely. Players stand in a wide circle facing inward. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator initiates the game by offering a non-verbal or sensory prompt (such as a single evocative word, a sensory image like 'biting a lemon', or a simple physical gesture) to the first player.
2. The receiving player takes a brief moment to let the prompt land in their body, immediately committing to their very first physical, vocal, and emotional impulse without overthinking.
3. The player expresses this impulse as a 'Level 5' baseline, combining a distinct physical posture, a non-verbal vocalization (like a hum, sigh, or gibberish), and a clear emotional state.
4. Once the baseline is established, the facilitator calls out different intensity levels on a scale of 1 to 10 (e.g., 'Dial up to 8!', 'Drop to 2!').
5. The player must instantly adjust their physical and vocal expression to match the called number, keeping the core essence of the original impulse but altering its scale and energy.
6. After three or four rapid dial shifts, the facilitator calls 'Freeze', and the player holds their final physical and emotional state.
7. The active player then silently transfers the essence of their frozen state to the player next to them using only eye contact, a subtle gesture, or a directed breath.
8. The receiving player absorbs this non-verbal cue, instantly finds their own unique somatic response, and establishes their new 'Level 5' baseline to repeat the cycle.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Don't plan your sound or movement—let your body react before your brain can edit it.' This reinforces the 'First Thought Is a Gift' principle.
- Pitfall: Players changing the emotion entirely when shifting the dial (e.g., turning sadness into anger when going from 5 to 9). Fix: Remind them to keep the exact same emotional flavor, just changing the volume, size, and internal pressure.
- Coaching Cue: 'Find the micro-movements at Level 1 and the explosive, full-body commitment at Level 10.' Encourage players to explore the extreme ends of the spectrum.
- Pitfall: Players hesitating during the transfer phase or trying to pantomime a story. Fix: Coach them to pass a pure energetic vibration or feeling, not a literal action.

## Variations
- Rapid-Fire Shifts: The facilitator calls out dial numbers in rapid succession (every 2 seconds) to push players past intellectual processing and force pure, instinctive physical adjustments.
- Emotional Blending: The facilitator introduces a secondary emotion mid-drill (e.g., 'Keep that Level 6 grief, but inject a Level 3 curiosity into it'), challenging players to hold complex, layered states.
- Vocal-Only or Physical-Only: Restrict the expression to only vocal sounds (no movement) or only physical movement (complete silence) to isolate and strengthen specific expressive channels.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to instantly jump to a Level 10 intensity versus holding a quiet Level 1 or 2?
- What did you notice about your brain's tendency to plan, and how did you bypass that to trust your first physical impulse?
- How did receiving the non-verbal transfer from your neighbor differ from receiving a verbal prompt?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are aware of their physical boundaries and surroundings to avoid collisions during high-intensity movements. Encourage vocal safety—players should not strain their vocal cords at Level 10; they can increase intensity through breath support and resonance rather than raw volume.

## Why It Works
By using a numerical scale, the game bypasses the analytical mind and gamifies emotional expression. It teaches players that emotions are physical states that can be dialed up or down like volume, removing the fear of 'getting into' an emotion. The non-verbal transfer builds deep somatic empathy, forcing players to read and mirror energy rather than intellectualizing narrative cues.

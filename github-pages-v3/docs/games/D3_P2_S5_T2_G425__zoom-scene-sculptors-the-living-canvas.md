# Digital Space Sculptors

![Digital Space Sculptors — infographic](img/D3_P2_S5_T2_G425__zoom-scene-sculptors-the-living-canvas.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Collaboratively build a rich virtual environment and character web, one digital layer at a time.

## Overview
Digital Space Sculptors is a fast-paced virtual game where players use video platform features to construct a detailed, shared base reality. Taking rapid turns, players add environmental details, characters, or objects to build an interconnected chain of physical and situational details. The result is a highly collaborative, visually rich environment built entirely from the ground up.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Base Reality First; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; World-Building; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Endowment chains
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** Develops the ability to establish a strong base reality and construct endowment chains in a virtual space, training players in active listening, offer reception, and visual world-building.

## Setup
Set up a virtual meeting room with Gallery View enabled. Ensure all players know how to rename themselves and change their virtual backgrounds. Open the chat window to manage the turn-taking queue.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator establishes a broad, evocative location suggestion, such as an abandoned research station or a dusty museum archive.
2. Players queue up to contribute by typing a designated keyword like ADD into the chat window to establish a clear, lag-free order.
3. The facilitator calls on the first player in the queue and spotlights their video feed to focus the group's attention.
4. The active player has up to 45 seconds to make one specific Yes-And addition to the environment using a virtual background, a character name change, or a pantomimed object.
5. Each subsequent player called from the queue must explicitly connect their addition to the immediately preceding player's contribution, creating an unbroken endowment chain.
6. Players not currently spotlighted keep their cameras on in Gallery View, actively listening and observing the visual and textual changes to prepare their next connection.
7. The facilitator maintains a brisk but manageable pace, ensuring players focus on grounding the physical space rather than rushing into a complex plot.
8. Once a rich, multi-layered environment with established characters and objects is fully sculpted, the facilitator calls Scene to conclude the round.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Build on the last brick! Remind players to directly reference the previous player's specific contribution to keep the endowment chain intact.
- To prevent technical overwhelm, encourage players to use simple, pre-saved abstract colors or basic stock images, focusing on how they verbally endow the background rather than visual perfection.
- Coaching cue: Name the relationship. When renaming, players should include their character's name and their connection to another character or object already in the space.
- If technical lag occurs, have the facilitator actively manage the queue in the chat, calling out who is on deck so the next player is ready to unmute and act immediately.

## Variations
- Silent Sculpting: Players build the entire environment and character web using only virtual backgrounds, name changes, and physical pantomime, with absolutely no spoken dialogue.
- The Object Chain: Every single contribution must be a physical object that is passed through the screen from one player to the next, requiring precise spatial awareness.
- Status Shift: Each new character introduced must explicitly establish a high or low status relative to the characters already present, shifting the power dynamic of the space.

## Debrief
- How did focusing on the physical environment and object endowment help you understand the characters' relationships before any plot started?
- What did it feel like to have your contribution immediately built upon by the next player?
- How did we manage the cognitive load of tracking visual changes alongside verbal dialogue?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all players are comfortable with the technical requirements of renaming and background changes before starting. Offer a verbal-only modification where a player can describe their background or character name if their device does not support these features.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to use distinct digital modalities like visual backgrounds, text-based names, and physical pantomime, the game breaks down the habit of relying solely on verbal dialogue to build a scene. The strict requirement of building on the immediate predecessor's offer creates a tight endowment chain, ensuring that a solid, shared base reality is established collaboratively before any narrative conflict is introduced.

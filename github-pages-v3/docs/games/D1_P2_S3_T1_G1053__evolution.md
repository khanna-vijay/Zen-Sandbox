# Evolution

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A high-energy physical tournament where players joyfully evolve and devolve through distinct creature stages.

## Overview
Evolution is a fast-paced, highly physical warm-up game where players navigate a chaotic space as different creatures. By playing quick rounds of Rock-Paper-Scissors with matching peers, players either ascend to a higher evolutionary tier or humorously devolve to a lower one.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops physical commitment, character centers, peripheral awareness, and a joyful, resilient attitude toward failure and sudden setbacks.

## Setup
An open room cleared of chairs and obstacles. Players stand scattered throughout the space, ready to move.

## How to Play
1. Introduce the four evolutionary stages: Stage 1 is the Egg (hands together overhead, whispering 'egg, egg'), Stage 2 is the Chicken (flapping elbows, clucking), Stage 3 is the Dinosaur (claws out, roaring), and Stage 4 is the Superhero (one arm raised, making a soaring sound).
2. Instruct all players to begin the game at Stage 1 as Eggs.
3. Players must move around the space using only the physical walk, posture, and sound of their current evolutionary stage.
4. Players search the room to find another player who is currently at the exact same evolutionary stage.
5. Once two matching players meet, they play a single, rapid round of Rock-Paper-Scissors.
6. The winner of the round immediately evolves to the next stage, adopting its physical posture and sound.
7. The loser of the round devolves to the previous stage (except for Eggs, who remain Eggs), immediately shifting their physicality to match.
8. Players immediately set off to find a new partner who matches their newly acquired stage.
9. When a player wins a round as a Superhero, they remain at this peak stage and cheer on the remaining players until everyone has evolved.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to embrace the demotions: 'Lose with joy! Instantly shrink into your new character with 100 percent commitment!'
- Watch out for players challenging different stages; remind them they can only play Rock-Paper-Scissors with someone of their own species.
- Encourage players to exaggerate their physical centers, such as leading with the hips for the dinosaur or the head for the chicken.
- If some players get stuck as Eggs, encourage the group to celebrate them, turning the 'eternal egg' status into a fun comedic bit.

## Variations
- Custom Ecosystem: Let the group invent their own four physical stages with unique movements and sounds before starting.
- Silent Evolution: Play the entire game in complete silence, relying entirely on eye contact and exaggerated physical postures to find matches.
- Infinite Loop: Instead of staying at the top, winning as a Superhero devolves you back into an Egg, keeping the high-energy movement going indefinitely.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to lose a round and immediately have to physically shrink or devolve?
- How did changing your physical posture and sound affect your energy level and spontaneity?
- How did you use your peripheral awareness to navigate the chaos and find matching partners?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries and avoid running or colliding in the space. Offer alternative physical gestures for players with mobility limitations, such as using hand gestures or facial expressions instead of deep squats for the Egg stage.

## Why It Works
By tying failure to a dramatic, funny physical transformation, the game removes the stigma of losing. Players learn to fail joyfully because playing a chicken or dinosaur is just as fun as being a superhero. The physical commitment required to embody each stage bypasses intellectual overthinking, fostering unfiltered spontaneity.

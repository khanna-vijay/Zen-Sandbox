# The Teleprompter Tapestry

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Weave a seamless, real-time digital narrative using dynamic renaming, private prompts, and visual turn-taking.

## Overview
A virtual narrative game where players collaborate in a sequential video grid to build a continuous story. Guided by a facilitator who dynamically renames players and sends private emotional prompts, participants must instantly accept and embody their shifting roles. A physical 'Beat Baton' is used to manage audio latency, ensuring a fluid, rapid-fire storytelling experience.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Narrative Architecture; World-Building; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Story Spine; Endowment chains; Timing exercises
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master rapid offer reception and instant endowment-acceptance in a virtual environment, training players to immediately integrate unexpected external directives into an ongoing narrative.

## Setup
Conducted on a video conferencing platform in gallery view. The facilitator configures the video order so all players see the same sequential grid. Each player must have a 'Beat Baton'—a small, highly visible physical object like a colorful pen, block, or toy within arm's reach.

## How to Play
1. Establish the visual sequence by arranging all players in a designated gallery view order, creating a predictable storyboard flow from the first player to the last.
2. Introduce the 'Beat Baton' mechanic: a player must hold their physical object clearly in the camera frame while speaking, and drop it out of frame to signal they are finished.
3. The next player in the visual sequence immediately raises their own Beat Baton to claim their turn, preventing overlapping audio and lag.
4. The facilitator initiates the story by renaming the first player to a specific character or object, such as 'The Eccentric Clockmaker'.
5. As the story progresses, the facilitator dynamically renames upcoming players just before their turn to introduce new elements, such as 'The Time Machine' or 'A Suspicious Shadow'.
6. Players must instantly accept their new name as an endowment, embodying the character, object, or concept physically and verbally during their turn.
7. The facilitator periodically sends private chat messages containing emotional directives or secret motives that the active player must immediately weave into their performance.
8. Non-active players use the platform's digital reaction emojis to provide silent, real-time audience feedback without interrupting the speaker.
9. The narrative loops through the established grid order for three to five rounds until the facilitator calls 'Scene!' at a satisfying narrative climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- To manage the high cognitive load of renaming and messaging, the facilitator can prepare a list of character archetypes, objects, and emotional prompts beforehand.
- Stagger the renaming by changing a player's name one or two turns before their actual turn to give them a brief moment to process their new identity.
- If a player misses a private chat prompt, do not stop the game; let them focus on their renamed identity and keep the momentum going.
- Side-coach players to keep their verbal contributions short and punchy, ideally one to three lines, to maintain a brisk, energetic pace.
- Ensure players are physically engaging with their camera frame, using posture and facial expressions to represent their assigned roles.

## Variations
- Co-Facilitated Tapestry: Use a co-facilitator to handle the technical renaming and private messaging, allowing the main facilitator to focus entirely on side-coaching and narrative pacing.
- Abstract Only: Restrict all renamed identities to abstract concepts, forces of nature, or inanimate objects to push physical and metaphorical embodiment.
- Genre Tapestry: Apply a specific narrative genre, such as Film Noir, Sci-Fi, or Gothic Horror, to guide the naming conventions and emotional prompts.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your identity suddenly changed right before your turn, and how did you practice 'Yes, And' to accept that endowment?
- How did the physical 'Beat Baton' affect your listening and presence compared to typical virtual conversations?
- What strategies helped you integrate the private emotional prompts without breaking the narrative flow?
- How did the non-verbal emoji reactions from your peers support your performance?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure all players have accessible physical objects for the Beat Baton. If a player has limited mobility, they can use a simple verbal cue or a head nod instead of lifting a physical object. Remind players that emotional prompts should remain within safe, non-triggering boundaries.

## Why It Works
This game works because it gamifies the technical constraints of virtual platforms. The 'Beat Baton' solves audio latency by replacing auditory cues with clear visual hand-offs. Dynamic renaming forces instant endowment-acceptance, bypassing the analytical mind and requiring immediate physical and verbal commitment. The private chat prompts add a layer of subtext, training players to balance multiple narrative inputs simultaneously.

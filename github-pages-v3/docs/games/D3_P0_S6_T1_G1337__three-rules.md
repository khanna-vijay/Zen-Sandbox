# The Triple Constraint

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Weave three absurd, audience-imposed limitations into a perfectly logical, grounded scene.

## Overview
In this scene-work game, players must perform a standard scene while strictly adhering to three arbitrary rules provided by the audience. The comedy and skill lie not just in remembering the rules, but in justifying why these bizarre behaviors are happening within their base reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Room Reading
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Object work; Vocal characterization; Reading the suggestion's intent
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops justification skills (specifically justifying the absurd), physical and vocal agility, and the ability to maintain a grounded base reality under extreme constraints.

## Setup
Two to four players stand on stage, with the rest of the group acting as the audience. No props are needed. The facilitator prepares to solicit three distinct types of rules from the audience.

## How to Play
1. Ask two to four players to step onto the stage and establish a simple, everyday suggestion for their location or relationship.
2. Solicit three distinct rules from the audience: one physical constraint (e.g., 'one arm must always touch your knee'), one vocal/linguistic constraint (e.g., 'no words containing the letter S'), and one behavioral trigger (e.g., 'every time a question is asked, you must shout Hooray!').
3. Write these rules down on a board or have the audience keep track of them to ensure accountability.
4. Begin the scene in a grounded base reality, establishing who the characters are and what they are doing.
5. Immediately integrate the three rules into the action, treating them not as meta-jokes, but as real, physical, or psychological realities for the characters.
6. Actively justify why these rules exist in the world of the scene (e.g., if a player must shout 'Hooray!' after a question, they might justify it as a nervous tic or a corporate mandate).
7. Continue the scene, building the relationship and platform while maintaining the constraints, until a natural comedic climax or resolution is reached.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't just do the rule—explain it!' Encourage players to find a logical reason within the scene's reality for their bizarre behavior.
- Coaching cue: 'Keep the base reality strong.' If they are in a kitchen, they are still cooking, even with one arm tied to their knee.
- Pitfall: Players treat the rules as a meta-gag, breaking character to laugh at the difficulty. Fix: Remind them that the character doesn't know they are in an improv game; to the character, this behavior is normal or has a very real, urgent reason.
- Pitfall: Forgetting the rules. Fix: Have the audience gently call out or make a soft buzz sound if a rule is broken, keeping the room reading active.

## Variations
- The Escalation: Start with one rule, and have the facilitator freeze the scene to add the second and third rules as the scene progresses.
- Secret Rules: The players don't know the rules; the audience knows them, and players must discover them through trial and error.
- Tag-Team Rules: When a player tags out, they must inherit the physical or vocal rule of the player they replaced.

## Debrief
- How did justifying the absurd rules actually help build the characters' relationship and the scene's reality?
- What happened to your physical and vocal choices when you were forced to work around these constraints?
- How did the audience's reaction help you gauge which justifications were landing?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical constraints are accessible to all players. If a player has mobility limitations, adapt the physical rule to match their comfortable range of motion (e.g., a facial expression or a specific hand gesture instead of a full-body posture).

## Why It Works
Constraints breed creativity. By forcing players to operate under arbitrary limits, it bypasses their analytical minds. The magic happens when the brain tries to make sense of the nonsense (justification), turning a cheap gimmick into a rich, character-driven game engine.

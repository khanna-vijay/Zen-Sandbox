# Perspective Shift

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Replay a single scene through the biased, subjective lenses of its different characters.

## Overview
Players improvise a brief, high-stakes scene with clear relationships. Afterward, they replay the exact same sequence of events, but with one character stepping forward to narrate their internal, highly biased perspective of what really happened.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Justification; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Reincorporation-as-justification
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops narrative architecture, active listening, and justification by forcing players to track physical actions while re-interpreting emotional subtext and motivations.

## Setup
A performance space with room for two to three active players and a small audience or waiting area. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Select two or three players to perform an initial, objective scene lasting approximately two minutes, focusing on a relationship with clear emotional stakes.
2. Instruct the players to keep the physical actions and dialogue simple, memorable, and easy to track.
3. Once the initial scene concludes, pause the action and select one character to be the first narrator.
4. Begin the replay: the chosen narrator steps slightly forward to address the audience, sharing their internal thoughts, biases, and interpretations of the events as they unfold.
5. The other players recreate the physical actions and dialogue of the original scene, but adjust their performances slightly to match the narrator's subjective memory.
6. Repeat the replay process with a second character narrating, showing how their unique bias completely changes the meaning of the same physical events.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage players to keep the initial scene grounded and physically distinct; complex plots are too difficult to track and replay accurately.
- Side-coach the narrator to express strong, biased opinions about the other characters' motives rather than just describing what is happening.
- Pitfall: Players trying to change the actual sequence of events. Fix: Remind them that the physical facts remain the same, but the emotional interpretation and justification are what shift.
- Remind non-narrating players to make their partner a genius by leaning into the narrator's version of reality during the replay.

## Variations
- Monologue Interruptions: Instead of replaying the whole scene, the facilitator can freeze the live scene at key moments and tap a player to deliver a quick, subjective inner monologue before resuming.
- The Silent Observer: Have a third player play a silent character, such as a waiter or bystander, who then narrates their highly skewed perspective of the interaction.

## Debrief
- How did changing the perspective alter your understanding of the characters' motivations?
- What did you have to track in the original scene to make the replay successful?
- How does knowing your partner's internal thoughts help you justify their onstage choices?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that subjective interpretations do not rely on harmful stereotypes. Encourage players to establish clear boundaries regarding physical touch during the initial scene, as those physical boundaries must be respected and maintained exactly during the replays.

## Why It Works
By keeping the physical structure of the scene constant while shifting the internal narrative, players learn that story is not just a sequence of events, but a negotiation of perspective. It trains active listening because players must remember their partner's physical offers to re-contextualize them, and it builds justification skills by finding new, logical reasons for existing behaviors.

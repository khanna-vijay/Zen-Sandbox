# The Triple-Channel Dial

> Calibrate your voice, body, and emotion instantly to a single, dynamic intensity scale.

## Overview
Two players initiate a scene, each embodying a distinct, chosen emotion. Throughout their interaction, a facilitator calls out numbers from 1 to 10, prompting the players to instantly scale their emotional intensity, physical movement, and vocal delivery to that exact level. The challenge lies in maintaining narrative truth while executing extreme, synchronized shifts in expressive energy.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Vulnerability; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Silence & Stillness; Self-Recovery; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Projection & breath support; Character Walks/Centers; Do nothing exercises
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop precise, simultaneous control over emotional, physical, and vocal expression, training players to commit fully to extreme shifts in intensity while maintaining narrative integrity.

## Setup
An open performance space with no props. Two players stand in the playing area, while the remaining participants observe. One person (usually the facilitator) acts as the Dial Master, positioned where they can clearly see and be heard by the players.

## How to Play
1. Two players step into the performance space and establish a simple relationship and setting.
2. Each player secretly selects or is assigned a distinct, underlying emotion and a corresponding physical center or posture.
3. The facilitator starts the scene by calling out a baseline number, typically 'Dial 5', and the players begin their scene at this moderate intensity.
4. As the scene progresses, the facilitator periodically calls out a new number from 1 to 10.
5. Upon hearing a new number, players must instantly adjust three channels of their performance to match that level: emotional intensity, physical scale/movement, and vocal volume/articulation.
6. At low numbers (1-3), players must explore extreme restraint, minimal movement, and quiet or whispered dialogue without losing their core emotion.
7. At high numbers (8-10), players must expand their physical presence, use high kinetic energy, and project their voices powerfully.
8. Players must integrate these shifts seamlessly into the scene's reality, justifying the sudden changes internally without ever explicitly mentioning the dial or numbers in their dialogue.
9. The scene continues for 3 to 5 minutes, with the facilitator shifting the dial every 15 to 30 seconds to challenge the players' adaptability.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't just get louder; let the physical and emotional tension rise to match the volume.' This prevents players from only adjusting their voices.
- Common Pitfall: Players explaining the shift (e.g., 'Suddenly I feel so angry!'). Fix: Remind them to show, not tell. The shift is an internal and physical reality, not a topic of conversation.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Find the stillness at 1.' Encourage players to discover how powerful a quiet, motionless '1' can be, rather than treating low numbers as dead space.
- Ensure the Dial Master varies the pacing. Rapid jumps (e.g., from 2 to 10) test explosive commitment, while gradual steps (e.g., 5 to 6 to 7) test fine-tuned calibration.

## Variations
- Targeted Channels: The facilitator calls out changes for only one channel at a time (e.g., 'Physicality 8!' or 'Voice 2!') to help players isolate and master each expressive element.
- Emotional Swaps: Along with the number, the facilitator calls out a brand-new emotion (e.g., 'Fear 8!'), forcing an immediate emotional pivot alongside the intensity shift.
- Blind Dial: Only one player can hear or see the dial changes (using hand signals), forcing the other player to dynamically adapt and match their partner's shifting energy.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to justify a sudden leap to a 10 or a drop to a 1 without breaking the reality of the scene?
- Which of the three channels (vocal, physical, emotional) was the hardest to scale in unison, and why?
- How did playing at the extreme ends of the dial (1 and 10) affect your listening and responsiveness to your partner?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because high dial settings (8-10) encourage high physical and vocal energy, establish clear physical boundaries before starting to prevent accidental collisions. Remind players that high vocal intensity does not require vocal strain; encourage proper breath support and projection rather than raw screaming.

## Why It Works
By linking emotional, physical, and vocal expression to a single numerical scale, this game bypasses the analytical mind and forces immediate, holistic embodiment. It teaches players that emotion is not just a mental state, but a physical and vocal commitment. The constraint of justifying these shifts on the fly builds rapid self-recovery and deepens the truthfulness of high-stakes choices.

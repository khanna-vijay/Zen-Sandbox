# Clandestine Script

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Weave a silent digital tapestry punctuated by secret, game-changing narrative whispers.

## Overview
In this virtual improv game, players collaborate on a scene performed primarily through heightened physical expression and silence. Meanwhile, the facilitator acts as an unseen director, sending private chat messages containing secret motivations, sudden revelations, or specific lines of dialogue to individual players. The magic emerges as players instantly justify and integrate these hidden prompts, transforming a silent baseline into a dynamic, unpredictable story.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Show, Don't Tell; Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Justification; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Object work; Endowment-acceptance; Justify the absurd; Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master the Platform and Tilt technique by establishing a clear, non-verbal baseline (the platform) and seamlessly integrating sudden, disruptive narrative offers (the tilts) delivered via private text, training deep physical commitment and rapid justification.

## Setup
An online video conferencing platform with gallery view enabled. All players must have their cameras on, well-lit, and their private chat window open and easily visible without blocking their view of the other participants. Microphones are muted by default, to be unmuted only when a player is prompted to speak.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator establishes a simple, non-verbal starting scenario (such as waiting in a tense security line or attending a high-stakes silent art auction) and posts it in the public chat.
2. Players begin the scene in gallery view with microphones muted, communicating entirely through heightened physical actions, facial expressions, eye contact, and object work to build the platform.
3. The facilitator monitors the physical scene and selects a player to receive a tilt via a private chat message (for example: 'You have a stolen diamond in your pocket' or 'The person on your left is your long-lost rival').
4. The receiving player must immediately read the private message, accept the offer, and instantly shift their physical behavior or object work to justify this new internal reality without breaking character.
5. The facilitator may privately message a specific line of dialogue to a player (for example: 'Unmute and say: "I know what you did last summer!"').
6. Upon receiving a dialogue prompt, the player unmutes, delivers the line with high emotional commitment, and immediately remutes, returning to the physical play.
7. The other players must instantly react to these sudden verbal and physical shifts, treating them as absolute truth and adapting their own physical storytelling to support the evolving narrative.
8. The facilitator continues to weave the story by sending complementary or contrasting private prompts to different players, building the narrative architecture toward a natural climax.
9. The scene concludes when the web of secrets is fully exposed and the narrative reaches a satisfying, high-stakes resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep your private prompts short and highly actionable; long paragraphs of text will break the player's flow and increase cognitive load.
- Side-coach players to avoid 'waiting' for a text; they must remain fully active in the physical scene, treating the private messages as sudden impulses rather than homework.
- If a player struggles to integrate a prompt, send a follow-up whisper to a different player to help justify the moment (e.g., 'React as if they just insulted your family').
- Ensure players do not narrate their actions or explain how they got the information; the integration must feel organic to the character's reality.

## Variations
- Subtext Only: The facilitator only sends emotional states or hidden desires (e.g., 'You are deeply jealous of the person drinking water') rather than explicit plot points or dialogue, keeping the entire scene silent.
- The Double Agent: Two players receive the exact same secret prompt privately, but neither knows the other has it, leading to a dramatic double-reveal.
- Audience Director: In larger groups, non-performing participants can private-message the active players with tilts, taking over the facilitator's role.

## Debrief
- How did the physical-first baseline (the platform) make the sudden verbal lines (the tilts) more impactful?
- What strategies did you use to read the private chat messages while maintaining your physical presence on camera?
- How did you justify a sudden shift in your character's motivation without explicitly telling the audience what your prompt was?
- In what ways did this exercise force you to 'show' your internal state rather than relying on dialogue to explain it?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are comfortable with close-up facial expressions and physical movement on camera. Remind participants that they can adjust their camera framing if they have physical space limitations, and that emotional prompts should remain within agreed-upon boundaries.

## Why It Works
By stripping away continuous dialogue, players are forced to rely on physical storytelling, embodying the 'Show, Don't Tell' principle. The private chat mechanic introduces a literal 'tilt' directly to an individual's awareness, mimicking sudden real-world changes. Because the other players do not know what the prompt was, they must practice radical active listening and offer reception, instantly justifying the visible changes in their partner's behavior to keep the narrative architecture intact.

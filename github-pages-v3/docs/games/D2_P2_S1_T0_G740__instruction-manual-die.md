# Instruction Manual Die

> Co-create a hilarious, high-stakes user guide where hesitation leads to a dramatic demise.

## Overview
In this fast-paced narrative game, players stand in a line to collectively improvise an instruction manual for an absurd, non-mechanical object. Guided by a conductor who points to switch speakers mid-sentence, players must maintain a seamless flow of thought. Any stutter, pause, or repetition triggers a collective cry of 'Die!' from the audience, eliminating the player and prompting a new chapter.

## Setup
Players stand in a straight line facing the audience. One facilitator stands in front of the line, acting as the conductor. The conductor asks the audience for an everyday object or concept that would never normally have an instruction manual.

## How to Play
1. The conductor obtains a suggestion of an absurd object and announces the first chapter of its manual.
2. The conductor points to a player in the line, who must immediately begin explaining the manual's contents in an authoritative, instructional tone.
3. At any moment, even mid-word or mid-sentence, the conductor points to a different player. The new player must instantly continue the exact sentence without a single moment of hesitation, repetition, or grammatical stutter.
4. If a player hesitates, repeats a word, stumbles, or fails to connect their sentence logically, the audience and remaining players shout 'DIE!'
5. The eliminated player dramatically 'dies' by stepping back out of the line with high energy and good humor.
6. The conductor then announces the next chapter and points to a remaining player to resume the story.
7. To increase difficulty, the conductor can introduce stylistic constraints for new chapters, such as requiring the chapter to be delivered in rhyme or with a specific emotional tone.
8. Play continues until only one player remains, who must deliver the final safety warning of the manual to win.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep the pacing brisk. Conductors should switch players rapidly—sometimes after just two or three words—to force players out of their heads and into pure spontaneity.
- Encourage players to accept their 'deaths' with theatrical flair. The 'DIE!' shout should feel like a celebration of a fun mistake, not a punishment.
- Side-coach players to avoid starting their turn with filler words like 'And', 'So', or 'But' unless it perfectly completes the previous speaker's sentence fragment.
- If the conductor points to a player and they freeze, prompt the audience immediately to keep the energy high.

## Variations
- Non-Elimination Mode: Instead of stepping out, players who stumble simply take a quick bow, the audience shouts 'LIVE!', and the conductor immediately starts a new chapter with them to keep everyone playing.
- Emotional Manuals: Each chapter must be delivered in a different extreme emotion.
- Gibberish Translation: The conductor points to one player to speak in gibberish, and then quickly points to another player to translate what was just said into the manual's instructions.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have to speak mid-sentence without knowing where the thought was going?
- What strategies did you use to ensure you were ready to speak the exact millisecond you were pointed at?
- How did the high-energy 'DIE!' shout affect your fear of making mistakes?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles so players can safely step back or 'die' dramatically without tripping. If players have mobility limitations, they can sit or stand, and 'dying' can be represented by a dramatic vocalization or a simple hand gesture rather than physical falling.

## Why It Works
This game forces players into a state of deep active listening because they cannot plan their next line; they must be entirely present to catch the exact syllable dropped by their teammate. By gamifying mistakes with a loud, collective 'DIE!', it reframes failure as a moment of shared comedy, releasing tension and encouraging unfiltered spontaneity.

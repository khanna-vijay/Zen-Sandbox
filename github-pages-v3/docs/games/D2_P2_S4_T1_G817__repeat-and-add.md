# Echo and Expand

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Listen closely, repeat your partner's exact words, and add the next logical step.

## Overview
A rapid-fire pairing exercise where players must repeat their partner's last statement verbatim before adding a new detail of their own. This creates an unbroken chain of shared narrative where every single word is validated and built upon. It is a foundational drill that strips away the anxiety of invention by forcing deep, active listening.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Active Listening; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Yes, And… sentence games; Last Word Response; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop precise offer reception and active listening by requiring players to hear, retain, and repeat their partner's exact words before contributing their own unfiltered spontaneous addition.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other. No props or special staging are required; the exercise can be run simultaneously across the entire room.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have partners stand or sit facing one another.
2. Player A initiates the conversation with a simple, single-sentence statement, such as 'I am going to the grocery store.'
3. Player B must repeat Player A's statement exactly as spoken, and then immediately add a new, single-sentence detail using 'and...', for example: 'You are going to the grocery store, and we need to buy some fresh apples.'
4. Player A must then repeat only the new detail that Player B just added, and append their own new single-sentence detail, for example: 'We need to buy some fresh apples, and they must be crisp and green.'
5. Player B repeats Player A's new detail and adds another: 'They must be crisp and green, and we will bake them into a pie.'
6. Continue this back-and-forth rhythm, ensuring that each turn begins with an exact echo of the partner's new contribution before any new information is introduced.
7. Keep the pace brisk and discourage players from planning their additions while their partner is speaking.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid paraphrasing. If Player A says 'I am angry,' Player B must say 'You are angry,' not 'You are mad' or 'You're upset.' Exact repetition builds discipline.
- Watch out for players planning ahead. If you notice a lag or a glazed look, remind them to focus entirely on the words being spoken in the moment.
- Encourage simple, mundane additions. Players often try to make the story exciting too fast; remind them that the first thought is a gift and small details are highly effective.
- If a player forgets the exact phrasing, have them pause, look at their partner for a gentle non-verbal cue, and try again rather than guessing.

## Variations
- Emotional Echo: Players must repeat the line using the exact same emotional tone, volume, and physical posture as their partner.
- Group Circle: Run the game in a single large circle where Player A makes an offer to Player B, Player B repeats and adds, then turns to Player C to repeat and add, moving the chain around the room.
- Physical Echo: Players must repeat a physical gesture or object-work action along with the spoken line before adding their own physical and verbal offer.

## Debrief
- How did repeating your partner's exact words change how closely you had to listen to them?
- Did you find yourself planning your next line while your partner was talking? How did that affect your ability to repeat their line?
- How did it feel to have your exact words spoken back to you? Did it make your ideas feel valued?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of vocal volume and physical proximity, allowing partners to adjust distance if needed for comfort or hearing accessibility.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to repeat their partner's words verbatim, the game bypasses the brain's tendency to plan ahead. It physically enforces the 'Yes' of 'Yes, And' by requiring literal agreement and validation of the offer before the 'And' can be delivered. This builds trust and ensures both players are operating in the exact same reality.

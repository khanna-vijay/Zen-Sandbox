# Resonance Canvas

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Paint a living, shifting environment together using silent physical actions and intuitive emotional escalation.

## Overview
A collaborative, non-verbal physical exercise where an ensemble builds and transforms a shared environment. Players start with a single physical action, expand its context, and collectively escalate the scene's stakes and atmosphere. The result is a fluid, wordless landscape that shifts organically through shared intuition.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Serve the Piece; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Support Work; Suggestion Deconstruction (A-to-C); Thematic Synthesis; World-Building; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Stage-picture exercises; Playing architecture/objects; A-to-C drills; Object work
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop Group Mind and master non-verbal A-to-C suggestion deconstruction, training players to read subtle physical cues and collectively escalate a scene's emotional stakes without speaking.

## Setup
An open, moderate-sized playing space free of physical obstacles. Players stand in a loose circle or semi-circle, starting in a relaxed, neutral physical posture. No props or chairs are needed.

## How to Play
1. Begin with all players standing in a neutral posture, silent and focused on the shared space.
2. One player steps forward to initiate a single, clear, non-verbal physical action (the 'A' offer) that establishes a basic environmental detail, such as feeling a light breeze or wiping a surface.
3. A second player immediately joins, adding a complementary non-verbal action (the 'B' offer) that expands the context or deepens the physical reality of the first action.
4. The remaining players enter the space one by one, using peripheral awareness to integrate the established 'A' and 'B' elements into their own physical choices.
5. As the ensemble fills the space, players collectively introduce 'C' elements—escalating the physical reality to shift the emotional tone, stakes, or genre of the environment.
6. Maintain strict silence throughout the exercise, relying entirely on physical weight, tension, facial expressions, and non-verbal breath or abstract sounds.
7. Allow the environment to shift organically; once a collective atmosphere is established, any player can introduce a subtle new physical offer that transitions the group into a new setting.
8. Conclude the round when the environment reaches a natural, high-stakes peak or when the ensemble collectively fades back into a silent, neutral circle.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't just mirror what you see; justify why it is happening and show us what happens next.'
- Pitfall: Players default to literal miming or pantomime guessing games. Fix: Encourage players to focus on the emotional weight and sensory reaction to the environment rather than just object manipulation.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Expand your peripheral vision. Feel the temperature of the room change as a collective unit.'
- Pitfall: Two players dominate the scene while others watch. Fix: Remind the ensemble that this is a tapestry; every player must actively support and adjust their physical posture to balance the stage picture.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Look for the third step. If we have cold and wind, what is the high-stakes consequence of that cold and wind?'

## Variations
- Atmospheric Shift: Assign a secret starting emotion to one half of the group and a secret ending emotion to the other half, challenging them to transition the environment's mood silently.
- Aural Resonance: Play the game using only abstract, non-verbal vocal sounds and breath, removing physical movement entirely to focus on auditory group mind.
- Living Backdrop: Once the environment is fully established, the facilitator calls out two players to begin a spoken scene, while the remaining players continue to physically maintain and react to the environment around them.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to transition from a simple physical action (A) to a high-stakes environment (C) without speaking?
- At what point did you feel the group mind click and align on a single, shared reality?
- What non-verbal cues were the most effective in signaling a shift in the scene's emotional stakes?
- How can we bring this level of environmental awareness and support into our verbal scene work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards. Encourage players to adapt physical actions to their own comfort and mobility levels, emphasizing that subtle facial expressions or small gestures can carry as much weight as large physical movements.

## Why It Works
By stripping away dialogue, players are forced to rely on deep physical listening and peripheral awareness. The structured A-to-C progression prevents the scene from stagnating, teaching players how to logically escalate a premise by reacting to the emotional implications of their partners' physical offers.

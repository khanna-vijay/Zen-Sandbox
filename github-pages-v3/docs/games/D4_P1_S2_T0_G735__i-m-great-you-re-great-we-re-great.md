# I'm Great, You're Great

![I'm Great, You're Great — infographic](img/D4_P1_S2_T0_G735__i-m-great-you-re-great-we-re-great.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy, rapid-fire celebration of self, partner, and ensemble connection.

## Overview
This fast-paced, high-energy exercise gets the entire room moving, laughing, and connecting through shared positive affirmations. Players move dynamically through the space, pairing up to exchange synchronized declarations of mutual appreciation before high-fiving and seeking out new partners. It serves as an excellent warm-up or high-note closer to build ensemble trust and banish self-doubt.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Make Your Partner a Genius; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Support Work; Active Gifting; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To cultivate Group Mind and Support Work by practicing active gifting, building immediate eye contact, and fostering a joyful, low-stakes environment where players celebrate themselves and each other simultaneously.

## Setup
An open room cleared of obstacles to allow safe, rapid movement. No props or materials are required. All players stand and spread out across the space.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to begin moving dynamically around the room, filling the empty spaces and maintaining a high energy level.
2. Explain that when they make eye contact with another player, they must immediately step toward each other to form a temporary pair.
3. Once paired, both players must look each other in the eye and simultaneously chant three distinct phrases with matching gestures: 'I'm great!' (pointing to themselves), 'You're great!' (pointing to their partner), and 'We're great!' (raising their hands in the air).
4. Immediately following the final phrase, the partners must deliver an enthusiastic high-five.
5. After the high-five, both players must instantly break apart, resume moving through the space, and seek out a new partner.
6. Encourage players to keep moving and repeating this cycle rapidly, aiming to connect with as many different people in the room as possible within the time limit.
7. Call an end to the activity after approximately two to three minutes, once the room reaches a peak of high energy and laughter.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the rhythm: Encourage players to speak in unison rather than taking turns. The magic of the game comes from the shared, simultaneous declaration.
- Pitfall: Players moving too slowly or hesitating to find partners. Fix: Side-coach them to 'move with urgency' and 'make eye contact to lock in your next partner instantly.'
- Pitfall: Low energy or mechanical delivery. Fix: Model the enthusiasm yourself, encouraging big physical gestures and loud, proud vocal delivery.
- Encourage physical safety: Remind players to be mindful of their surroundings and to offer gentle, safe high-fives rather than aggressive slaps.

## Variations
- Silent Celebration: Perform the entire sequence using only eye contact, exaggerated facial expressions, and physical gestures, culminating in a silent high-five.
- The Wave: Instead of high-fiving, partners must invent a unique, instantaneous two-second handshake or physical celebration before parting.
- Vocal Shift: Change the phrases to match a specific theme or emotional tone of the workshop (e.g., 'I failed!', 'You failed!', 'We failed!' to practice failing joyfully).

## Debrief
- How did it feel to declare your own greatness and your partner's greatness at the exact same time?
- What happened to the energy in the room as we repeated this simple, positive interaction?
- How does this exercise help us practice 'making our partner look good' on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
For players with limited mobility, encourage the group to adapt their movement speed so everyone can participate safely. If high-fives or physical contact are uncomfortable or inaccessible for any participant, offer alternative non-contact gestures like a double thumbs-up, a bow, or air-fives.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to speak simultaneously, the game bypasses the analytical brain and taps directly into unfiltered spontaneity. The physical action of the high-five combined with positive verbal reinforcement triggers an immediate release of collective energy, reinforcing the core improv principle of supporting your partner and celebrating failure or success as a unified ensemble.

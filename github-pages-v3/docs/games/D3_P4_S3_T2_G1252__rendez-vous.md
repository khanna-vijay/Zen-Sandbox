# The Summons

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> An atmospheric narrative format where isolated characters face a mysterious, high-stakes confrontation.

## Overview
The Summons is a structured narrative game played in two distinct acts. In Act I, we establish the everyday lives of several distinct characters before an ominous antagonist enters to invite them to a remote location. In Act II, these characters gather at the designated spot, trapped together, to uncover their shared connection and survive the antagonist's dark ultimatum.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story; Serve the Piece
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; Raising the Stakes; Format Literacy
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Story Spine; Platform/Tilt; Stakes-escalation reps; Longform vs. shortform mechanics
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master narrative architecture by establishing a clear platform (everyday life) and executing a dramatic tilt (the mysterious invitation), while collaboratively raising stakes and serving the overarching story.

## Setup
An active stage area with chairs available for staging. The facilitator gathers an audience (which can be off-stage players) to provide suggestions. One player is cast as the Antagonist, and 3 to 4 players are cast as the Guests. The audience suggests a remote, inescapable location (e.g., an abandoned lighthouse, a locked museum, a high-altitude observatory).

## How to Play
1. Cast the roles: Select one player to be the Antagonist and 3 to 4 players to be the Guests. Obtain a suggestion for an isolated, multi-room location from the audience.
2. Begin Act I (The Invitations): Run a series of short, two-person scenes. Each scene features one Guest in their normal environment, establishing their routine, desires, and flaws (the platform).
3. Introduce the Tilt: Mid-way through each introductory scene, the Antagonist enters. The Antagonist must deliver a formal invitation to the remote location at a specific time, offering a customized lure (e.g., money, blackmail, a long-lost secret) that forces the Guest to accept.
4. Transition to Act II (The Gathering): Once all Guests have been invited, transition to the remote location. The Guests arrive one by one, establishing the physical space through object work and interacting with each other as strangers.
5. Deliver the Ultimatum: Once all Guests are present, the Antagonist reveals themselves, locks the exits, and delivers the high-stakes twist (e.g., 'Only one of you will leave here alive,' or 'One of you is a murderer, and you have until midnight to find them').
6. Navigate the Climax: The Guests must work together or against each other to discover their hidden connection, search the environment, and attempt to escape or appease the Antagonist.
7. Resolve the Narrative: Conclude the story when the mystery is solved, the Antagonist is overcome, or a dramatic sacrifice is made, bringing the narrative arc to a satisfying close.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching the Antagonist: Remind the Antagonist to tailor their invitation to each Guest's specific vulnerability established in Act I, rather than using a generic bribe.
- Pacing Act I: Keep the introductory scenes brief. The goal is to quickly establish who the Guest is, show the Antagonist's intrusion, and move on.
- Pitfall - Low Stakes: If the Guests don't have a strong reason to stay in Act II, the tension evaporates. Side-coach the players to physically and narratively commit to being trapped (e.g., 'The bridge is washed out' or 'The doors are magnetically sealed').
- Collaborative Mystery: Encourage the Guests to actively invent connections between their backstories during Act II. If one Guest mentions a past scandal, another Guest should 'yes-and' how they were affected by it.

## Variations
- The Unseen Mastermind: The Antagonist who gathers them is actually just an employee or hostage of an even more powerful, unseen figure who communicates via intercom.
- The Hidden Traitor: One of the invited Guests is secretly working in league with the Antagonist, sabotaging the group's escape efforts from within.
- The Countdown: Introduce a physical timer or a clear narrative countdown (e.g., the tide rising, a bomb ticking) to force rapid decision-making and escalate the physical energy.

## Debrief
- How did establishing a strong 'platform' in Act I make the 'tilt' of the invitation more impactful?
- What techniques did you use to build tension once all the characters were trapped together?
- How did you balance individual character goals with the collective need to serve the overall story?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this format deals with captivity, high stakes, and potential character death, establish clear boundaries before playing. Use a 'no-harm' rule for physical play, and ensure players agree on the tone (e.g., campy horror vs. psychological mystery) to keep the space safe and comfortable for everyone.

## Why It Works
This format works because it perfectly mirrors classic narrative architecture. Act I builds a series of mini-platforms and tilts, establishing immediate investment. Act II merges these separate threads into a single, high-stakes pressure cooker. By forcing characters into an inescapable space, players must rely on dialogue, active gifting, and collaborative world-building to resolve the tension, rather than physically running away from the conflict.

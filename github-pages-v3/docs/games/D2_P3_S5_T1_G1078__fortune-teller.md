# The Oracle's Vision

> Help a clueless psychic discover their clients' bizarre futures through physical and verbal clues.

## Overview
One player steps out of earshot while the audience provides specific, unusual future predictions for the remaining players. When the psychic returns, the other players act as clients, using physical endowment, verbal hints, and emotional reactions to help the psychic guess their exact fortunes.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Offer Reception; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment chains
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To practice active gifting and endowment, making the guessing partner look brilliant by feeding them highly specific, clear, and progressive clues without outright saying the answer.

## Setup
One player (the Oracle) stands at the back of the room or covers their ears. The remaining players (2 to 5 clients) stand in a line facing the audience. No props are needed, just a clear playing space.

## How to Play
1. Designate one player as the Oracle and have them step out of the room or cover their ears so they cannot hear the audience.
2. Ask the audience to provide a highly specific, unusual future prediction or destiny for each of the remaining players.
3. Bring the Oracle back into the room and set up the scene: the Oracle is in their parlor, and the clients are visiting one by one to have their fortunes read.
4. The first client approaches the Oracle, who begins the reading with vague, open-ended psychic statements.
5. The client must actively gift the Oracle by reacting physically, emotionally, and verbally to these vague statements, steering the Oracle toward the correct prediction.
6. The client uses 'Yes, And' to accept whatever the Oracle says, treating the Oracle's wild guesses as incredibly accurate steps toward the truth, adjusting their physical behavior to match.
7. Once the Oracle successfully guesses the first client's prediction, that client reacts with amazement and steps back, allowing the next client to step forward.
8. The game continues until the Oracle has successfully guessed the fortunes of all the clients in the room.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Make your partner look like a genius! If they guess yellow, don't just say no—react to how close yellow is to orange, or start peeling an imaginary banana.'
- Coaching Cue: 'Show, don't tell. Use your body, your posture, and your emotional reactions to paint the picture of your future.'
- Pitfall: Clients sometimes freeze up and just say 'no' when the Oracle guesses wrong. Fix: Remind them that in improv, every guess is correct in some way. They must 'Yes, And' the guess and pivot it toward the target.
- Pitfall: The Oracle asks too many direct questions. Fix: Encourage the Oracle to make bold, declarative statements instead of asking questions, allowing the clients to endow those statements.

## Variations
- Spectral Whispers: Players who have already had their fortunes read can stand behind the Oracle as 'spirit guides,' miming clues over the Oracle's shoulders to help them with the remaining clients.
- Reverse Oracle: The Oracle knows the fortunes, and the clients must guess their own futures based on the Oracle's highly cryptic, physical clues.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to make your partner look like a genius instead of trying to stump them?
- What kinds of physical gifts (endowments) were the easiest for the Oracle to interpret?
- How does 'Yes, And' help move a guessing game forward compared to simple 'Yes/No' responses?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical mimes are accessible to all players. If a player has mobility limitations, focus on vocal tone, facial expressions, and verbal endowment rather than heavy physical movement.

## Why It Works
This game shifts the burden of 'being funny' or 'knowing the answer' away from the guesser and onto the clue-givers. By focusing on making their partner look brilliant, players learn the power of active gifting and endowment, turning a standard guessing game into a collaborative, supportive scene.

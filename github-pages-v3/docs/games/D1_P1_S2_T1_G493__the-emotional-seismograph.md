# The Emotional Seismograph

> Ride the waves of shifting feelings by dialing emotional intensity up and down instantly.

## Overview
A dynamic circle drill where players pass a chain of word associations, each paired with a specific, declared emotion and numerical intensity from 1 to 10. Participants must fully embody their chosen state using their entire body and voice, then pass it on. The next player absorbs the impact before transforming or deepening the emotional energy.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Vulnerability; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Silence & Stillness; Self-Recovery; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Word-association; First Thought drills; The Emotional Dial (1→10); Emotional substitution; Character Walks/Centers; Projection & breath support; Vocal characterization; Hold-the-beat reps
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To build emotional fluidity and total physical commitment by practicing rapid, deliberate transitions between diverse emotional states and varying intensities.

## Setup
Players stand in a comfortable circle facing inward with enough space to move their arms and shift their stances. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Begin with the group standing in a circle, establishing a shared focus and a supportive, non-judgmental atmosphere.
2. The first player initiates by calling out a single, evocative word.
3. Immediately after the word, the initiator declares an associated emotion and an intensity level from 1 to 10.
4. The initiator must instantly embody this state, using their entire body posture, facial expression, and vocal tone to express the emotion while repeating their word.
5. The player to their left must receive this offer by holding a silent, still beat for two seconds, letting the emotional energy land and affect them physically.
6. After the silent beat, this second player says a rapid word-association based on the previous word.
7. The second player then chooses to either 'Sustain' the previous emotion (adjusting the intensity up or down) or 'Shift' to a completely new emotion inspired by their new word.
8. They declare their word, chosen emotion, and intensity level, and immediately step into that physical and vocal expression.
9. Play continues clockwise around the circle, with each player receiving, associating, choosing to sustain or shift, and fully committing to their physicalized state.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't think, just feel! Let your first physical impulse dictate the emotion.'
- Pitfall: Players rushing past the receiving beat. Fix: Actively coach the two-second silence, reminding players that stillness is where the emotional transfer actually happens.
- Pitfall: Intellectualizing or acting the emotion from the neck up. Fix: Encourage players to lead with their physical center, adjusting their weight, spine, and breathing to match the declared intensity.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Commit 100% to the number you chose. If you said 10, let it shake the room; if you said 1, let it simmer in your pinky toe.'

## Variations
- Group Echo: After a player presents their word and emotion, the entire circle silently mirrors their physical posture and facial expression for one collective breath before the next player begins.
- No Repeats: Challenge advanced players by banning the repetition of any emotion during a single rotation around the circle.
- Non-Verbal Association: Instead of speaking a word, players must make a non-verbal sound or abstract physical gesture, then declare and embody their emotion.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to transition instantly from a low-intensity emotion to a high-intensity one?
- What did you notice about the relationship between your physical posture and your actual internal emotional state?
- How did the two-second silent receiving beat affect your ability to respond spontaneously?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game explores high-intensity emotions, establish a cool-down signal (like crossing wrists) if a player needs to step out or lower their intensity. Remind players they are in control of their dial and can choose safe, non-triggering emotional states to explore.

## Why It Works
By combining rapid word association with the explicit 1-10 emotional dial, the game bypasses the analytical brain. Forcing players to physically and vocally commit to a specific number prevents half-hearted choices, while the mandatory receiving beat trains active listening and emotional resonance.

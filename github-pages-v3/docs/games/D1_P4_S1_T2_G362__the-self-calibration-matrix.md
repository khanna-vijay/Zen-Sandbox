# The Tri-State Reset

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Instantly fuse emotion, movement, and voice, then wipe the slate clean on command.

## Overview
A high-energy, individual training drill where players rapidly embody composite prompts combining a specific emotion, physical quality, and vocal sound. By instantly committing to these complex states and immediately returning to a neutral baseline, players build a highly responsive connection between mind, body, and voice. The experience is fast-paced, visceral, and deeply liberating, stripping away the hesitation of self-censorship.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Self-Recovery
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; The Emotional Dial (1→10); Character Walks/Centers; Weight & resistance mime; Projection & breath support; Gibberish
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and rapid self-recovery by training players to instantly integrate physical, vocal, and emotional impulses without intellectual delay.

## Setup
Players scatter throughout a moderate, open room, standing far enough apart to move and make noise without colliding. No props are needed. The facilitator stands where they can be heard clearly by all participants.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to find a personal space in the room, standing comfortably and facing inward or in a neutral direction, focusing entirely on their own physical presence.
2. Explain that you will call out a three-part 'Tri-State' prompt consisting of an internal emotion, a physical texture or movement quality, and a vocal sound.
3. Direct players to instantly and simultaneously embody all three elements of the prompt the very moment they hear it, with absolute, 100% physical and vocal commitment.
4. Allow players to explore and sustain this combined state for three to five seconds, letting the physical, vocal, and emotional qualities feed into one another.
5. Call out the command 'RESET!' in a clear, sharp voice.
6. Instruct players to immediately drop the entire characterization on the reset command, returning to a completely still, silent, and neutral standing posture within one second.
7. Have players hold this neutral, empty state for two seconds to release all physical tension and emotional residue.
8. Immediately call out a brand-new, contrasting three-part prompt to trigger the next instant transformation.
9. Repeat this cycle for several minutes, gradually shifting the combinations to challenge the players' range and speed.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't plan! Let the sound and the movement happen at the exact same time as the feeling.'
- Pitfall: Players lag or 'warm up' into the state. Fix: Remind them that the transition must be instantaneous, like flipping a light switch rather than turning a dial.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Find absolute stillness in the reset. Let the previous character completely evaporate before the next prompt.'
- Pitfall: Players look at others for validation or copy their peers. Fix: Encourage players to focus on a fixed point on the wall or close their eyes during the neutral phase to maintain internal focus.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Commit to the weirdness. If the prompt feels contradictory, let your body figure out how they fit together.'

## Variations
- Paradoxical Prompts: Introduce deliberately conflicting combinations (e.g., 'Ecstatic, frozen solid, with a mournful sigh') to force creative physical problem-solving.
- The Dial: While players are holding a state, call out 'Volume Five!' or 'Volume Ten!' to have them instantly scale the intensity of their physical and vocal expression.
- Two-Part Warm-up: For less experienced players, start by prompting only two elements (e.g., Emotion + Physicality) before adding the vocal layer.

## Debrief
- Which part of the prompt did your brain want to prioritize, and how did you bring the other two elements online?
- How did the brief moment of absolute neutrality help you let go of the previous character state?
- What did you discover about your ability to generate intense emotions without needing a narrative or scene context?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of their physical boundaries; remind them to adapt physical prompts (like jumping or heavy stomping) to suit their personal mobility and joint comfort. Encourage vocal safety by advising against throat-straining screams, suggesting rich resonance or breath-based sounds instead.

## Why It Works
By demanding three distinct, simultaneous modes of expression (vocal, physical, emotional) under time pressure, the brain's analytical filter is overwhelmed. This forces the player to rely entirely on their immediate, instinctual 'first thought' impulse. The mandatory neutral reset trains the nervous system to quickly shed emotional and physical tension, building resilience and preventing performance fatigue.

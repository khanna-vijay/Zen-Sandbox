# Self-Rhyming Couplets

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Speak in self-contained rhyming couplets to discover unexpected narrative choices through immediate word association.

## Overview
A scene-based game where players must speak in rhyming couplets, with the twist that each player must complete their own rhyme. Instead of rhyming with the previous player's line, a player delivers a line, then immediately delivers a second line that rhymes with their first, before handing the scene over. This forces players to trust their first instinctual word association to complete their thoughts.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Fail Joyfully; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Vocal Craft; Active Listening; Game Identification
- **Technique(s):** Word-association; Vocal characterization
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and active listening by using the constraint of rhyming to bypass the analytical mind, treating the first rhyming word that comes to mind as a gift.

## Setup
Two to three players stand in the performance space. No props or special staging are required. The facilitator asks the audience or remaining workshop participants for a simple relationship or location to start the scene.

## How to Play
1. Two or three players step forward to begin a scene based on a simple suggestion like a location or relationship.
2. The first player initiates the scene by speaking a single line of dialogue.
3. The same player must immediately follow up with a second line of dialogue that rhymes with their first line, completing a couplet.
4. Once the first player completes their rhyming couplet, the next player responds.
5. The responding player must start a brand-new rhyme with their first line, and then complete it with their own second line.
6. Players must not try to rhyme with the previous player's lines; each player is solely responsible for rhyming with themselves.
7. The scene continues back and forth, with every turn consisting of a self-contained rhyming couplet that advances the story or relationship.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to prioritize speed over perfect poetry. If a rhyme is imperfect or silly, celebrate it and keep moving.
- Watch out for players writing ahead while their partner is speaking. Remind them to listen actively to the partner's couplet before formulating their own first line.
- If a player gets stuck on a rhyme, encourage them to make a nonsense sound or a slant rhyme rather than pausing to think. The goal is unfiltered spontaneity.
- Remind players that the second line must still advance the scene or reveal character, rather than just being a throwaway line for the sake of the rhyme.

## Variations
- Shakespearean Verse: Perform the scene with heightened, dramatic theatricality, using grand gestures and archaic language while maintaining the self-rhyming rule.
- AABB Quatrains: Each player must speak four lines per turn, following an AABB rhyme scheme, increasing the complexity of the narrative constraint.
- Rhythm Keepers: Introduce a steady, slow physical beat like snapping or clapping that players must speak in time with, heightening the pressure and bypassing the analytical filter.

## Debrief
- How did the pressure of finding a rhyme affect your internal editor? Did it make you more or less spontaneous?
- When you had to find a rhyming word, did it lead your character or the story in an unexpected direction?
- How did you handle the moments when a rhyme didn't work out perfectly? Did you fail joyfully?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players feel safe making mistakes by establishing a supportive, low-stakes environment where imperfect rhymes are met with enthusiasm. Encourage physical comfort and allow players to sit if standing for the duration of the scene is challenging.

## Why It Works
By forcing the brain to solve a linguistic puzzle like rhyming under time pressure, the analytical mind is occupied, allowing the subconscious to deliver unfiltered, spontaneous choices. The constraint of rhyming with oneself ensures that players do not have to anticipate their partner's rhyme, preserving active listening during the partner's turn.

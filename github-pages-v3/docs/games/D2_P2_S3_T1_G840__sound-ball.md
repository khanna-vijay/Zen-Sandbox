# Sound Ball

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Pass an invisible ball of energy across the circle using synchronized sound and physical movement.

## Overview
A high-energy circle warm-up where players pass an imaginary ball across the room. Each pass consists of a unique physical gesture and an accompanying vocal sound, which the receiver must instantly mirror before transforming it to pass to someone else.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; The First Thought Is a Gift; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Active Listening; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise; Object work; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep partner connection, active listening, and physical mirroring by practicing immediate, unfiltered agreement with a partner's vocal and physical offer.

## Setup
Players stand in a comfortable circle facing inward with clear sightlines to everyone else. No props or materials are needed, but ensure there is enough space for players to move their arms and take wide stances.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle facing inward, ensuring everyone can make eye contact with all other participants.
2. The facilitator initiates the game by holding an imaginary ball of energy, shaping it with their hands, and establishing eye contact with a specific player across the circle.
3. The sender throws the imaginary ball to the chosen receiver, accompanying the throw with a distinct, abstract vocal sound and a clear, committed physical gesture.
4. The receiver must immediately catch the ball by mirroring the exact sound and physical gesture of the sender as closely as possible.
5. Once the catch is completed and mirrored, the receiver instantly transforms the ball, creating a brand-new sound and physical gesture to pass to a different player in the circle.
6. The play continues in this manner, with each player catching and mirroring the incoming offer before immediately sending a new, spontaneous offer to someone else.
7. Keep the momentum fast-paced, encouraging players to rely on instinct rather than planning their sounds or movements in advance.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to catch first: Ensure players fully mirror the incoming sound and gesture before they think about what they want to send next.
- Watch out for intellectualizing: If players hesitate to invent a sound, remind them that the first thought is a gift and any abstract noise is perfect.
- Encourage full-body commitment: Remind players to use their whole body for the gesture, not just a limp hand wave, to build physical energy.
- Address the drop: If a pass is ambiguous, encourage the closest player to claim it dynamically rather than letting the energy stall.

## Variations
- Heavy/Light Ball: Introduce physical weight to the ball, requiring players to mirror the gravity, size, or speed of the object, such as a heavy bowling ball or a floating balloon.
- Emotional Ball: Instead of abstract sounds, the sender passes a specific emotional state which the receiver must mirror and then shift to a new emotion.
- Multi-Ball: Once the group establishes a steady rhythm, the facilitator introduces a second or third imaginary ball into the circle simultaneously to heighten peripheral awareness.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to immediately mirror your partner's sound and movement without judging it first?
- What happened to the group's energy when we stopped planning our next move and focused entirely on the person throwing to us?
- How does catching the offer before throwing a new one relate to active listening in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards. Players with vocal or physical limitations can adapt the gestures and sounds to their comfortable range of motion; the group's job is to mirror the intent and energy of the offer, not to demand physical perfection.

## Why It Works
This game physically embodies the core improv principle of Yes, And. The act of mirroring is the physical Yes (unconditional acceptance of the partner's offer), while the act of transforming and passing is the And (contributing something new). By bypassing the analytical brain through rapid sound and movement, players build trust and enter a state of shared group mind.

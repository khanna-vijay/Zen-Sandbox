# The Story Chronometer

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build a complete, high-stakes narrative arc driven by timed, escalating external disruptions.

## Overview
The Story Chronometer is a structured narrative exercise where two to four players build a complete story arc under strict time pressure. A facilitator acts as the timekeeper, injecting sudden, escalating plot disruptions at precise intervals. Players must instantly accept, justify, and integrate these external twists, driving the scene from a stable platform to a high-stakes climax and resolution.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Justification; Stakes / The 'Want'; Raising the Stakes; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Story Spine; Justify the absurd; Stakes-escalation reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master narrative architecture by practicing the deliberate progression of a scene through distinct phases (Platform, Tilt, Complication, Climax, and Resolution) while rapidly justifying unexpected external offers.

## Setup
This game is played virtually. Two to four active players turn their cameras on, while the facilitator keeps their camera on to act as the timekeeper. The facilitator needs a stopwatch and a list of pre-prepared, escalating narrative prompts (ranging from subtle environmental shifts to catastrophic climaxes). The remaining participants act as an active audience with cameras off.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator obtains a simple, grounded suggestion (such as a mundane location or relationship) to initiate the scene.
2. Phase 1 (The Platform): Two to four players begin the scene, focusing on establishing a stable base reality, clear character relationships, and a shared physical environment for the first 60 seconds.
3. Phase 2 (The Tilt): At the 60-second mark, the facilitator verbally injects the first prompt—a disruptive event or revelation that challenges the established routine.
4. The players must immediately accept this tilt, justify its existence within their reality, and adapt their characters' immediate desires to address this new obstacle.
5. Phase 3 (The Complication): After another 60 to 90 seconds, the facilitator delivers the second prompt, which significantly escalates the stakes or introduces a deeper, more urgent complication.
6. Players apply the 'if true, what else is true' principle, heightening their emotional responses and physical urgency to match the growing crisis.
7. Phase 4 (The Climax Catalyst): At the next interval, the facilitator delivers the final prompt—a high-stakes 'point of no return' that forces an immediate, difficult decision.
8. Phase 5 (The Resolution): The players immediately execute their climactic choices and rapidly guide the scene to a logical, earned resolution, at which point the facilitator ends the scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep prompts concise and active: Deliver prompts in a single, clear sentence so players do not have to pause their momentum to process complex instructions.
- Match the established tone: Ensure your pre-prepared prompts align with the genre or emotional reality the players established in Phase 1, rather than forcing a comedic scene to suddenly become a sci-fi thriller.
- Avoid premature problem-solving: If players try to resolve the conflict too early, use your next prompt to reveal that their solution actually made things worse or uncovered a deeper issue.
- Virtual delivery tip: Speak clearly over the audio to deliver prompts, or use the chat function if players prefer visual cues, ensuring the interruption is clean and unmistakable.

## Variations
- Silent Prompts: In a virtual setting, the facilitator types the prompts into the chat window instead of speaking them aloud, forcing players to split their focus and integrate visual text cues seamlessly.
- Character-Specific Twists: The facilitator directs specific prompts to individual characters (e.g., 'Player A, you suddenly realize Player B is lying'), forcing internal relationship shifts rather than external environmental crises.
- Audience-Driven Chronometer: The virtual audience types the escalating complications in the chat at the facilitator's signal, crowdsourcing the narrative disruptions in real time.

## Debrief
- How did the strict time pressure change your willingness to accept and justify bizarre or difficult offers?
- What strategies did you use to ensure the platform was strong enough to support the subsequent tilts and complications?
- How did your character's core desire ('want') shift or intensify as the facilitator escalated the stakes?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because the prompts introduce escalating stakes and crises, players should establish a 'pause' or 'skip' signal before playing. If a facilitator-introduced prompt inadvertently crosses a personal boundary or triggers discomfort, any player can call 'skip' to receive an immediate alternative prompt with no explanation needed.

## Why It Works
By outsourcing the plot twists to an external 'chronometer' (the facilitator), players are freed from the cognitive load of inventing the plot. This allows them to focus entirely on the core skills of platform-building, deep justification, and emotional heightening. The timed intervals prevent scenes from stalling in comfortable patterns, forcing a healthy, dramatic narrative momentum.

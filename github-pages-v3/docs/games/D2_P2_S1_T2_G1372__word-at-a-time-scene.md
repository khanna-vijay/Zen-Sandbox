# Word-by-Word Scene

![Word-by-Word Scene — infographic](img/D2_P2_S1_T2_G1372__word-at-a-time-scene.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Co-create a physical narrative one word at a time, instantly physicalizing every spoken detail.

## Overview
In this active duo exercise, players build a cohesive story by alternating speaking exactly one word at a time. Instead of just talking, players must physically act out the environment and actions they describe as they speak them. This forces players to step out of their heads and into their bodies, creating a rich, shared physical reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Narrative Architecture; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Last Word Response; Yes, And… sentence games; Story Spine; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** Develops hyper-focused active listening, immediate offer acceptance, and physical commitment by removing the ability to plan dialogue in advance.

## Setup
Two players stand in the center of the playing space. The remaining participants sit as active observers. No physical props are used; all objects and environments must be established through physical mime.

## How to Play
1. Two players stand in the performance space, facing each other or looking out into the shared environment.
2. The players begin the scene by establishing their shared identity or location, alternating one word at a time (e.g., Player A: 'We', Player B: 'are', Player A: 'exploring', Player B: 'a', Player A: 'cave').
3. Once the setting or relationship is established, players must immediately begin physicalizing their actions and environment through mime.
4. Players continue to alternate speaking exactly one word at a time to build their dialogue and describe their discoveries.
5. Every single word spoken must be treated as an absolute truth that both players instantly accept and physically react to in real-time.
6. Players must avoid merely narrating their actions; instead, they should use their physical movement to live in the space while using words to discover new details.
7. The dialogue should flow at a steady, rhythmic pace, avoiding long pauses to plan grammatically perfect sentences.
8. The scene continues until a natural narrative resolution is reached, or the facilitator calls 'scene.'

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Show, don't just tell! If you say there is a heavy box, your body must immediately show the weight of that box.'
- Pitfall: Players pausing to plan the perfect sentence. Fix: Encourage rapid-fire delivery and remind them that grammatical perfection is secondary to immediate physical commitment.
- Coaching Cue: 'Listen to the very last syllable. You cannot plan your next word until your partner has completely finished speaking theirs.'
- Pitfall: One player trying to control the direction of the scene by forcing specific grammatical structures. Fix: Remind players to 'make your partner a genius' by offering open-ended words like 'and', 'but', or 'suddenly'.

## Variations
- The Director's Cut: A facilitator stands offstage and occasionally shouts physical complications (e.g., 'The floor is lava!' or 'Gravity reverses!') which the players must immediately integrate into their physical play and word-by-word dialogue.
- Three-Player Split: Introduce a third player. Two players speak word-by-word to represent one single character, while the third player plays a separate character speaking normally.
- Emotional Shift: Every 30 seconds, the facilitator calls out a new emotion (e.g., 'Fear', 'Ecstasy', 'Suspicion') which the players must instantly adopt in both their physical movement and the tone of their word-by-word delivery.

## Debrief
- How did the strict constraint of speaking one word at a time affect your ability to plan ahead?
- What happened to your physical acting when you stopped focusing on generating long speeches?
- How did you support your partner when a sentence took an unexpected grammatical turn?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards, as players will be moving around while focusing heavily on their partner's words. Encourage players to scale physical movements to their own comfort and mobility levels.

## Why It Works
By stripping away the ability to plan monologue-length responses, players are forced into a state of absolute presence. They must listen to the very last letter of their partner's word to understand the context, embodying the Last Word Response technique. The physical requirement prevents the scene from becoming a dry, intellectual exercise, anchoring the verbal restriction in visceral, shared play.

# Whoosh Wave

![Whoosh Wave — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G1366__whoosh.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy circle warm-up that sharpens group focus, rapid reflexes, and collective rhythm.

## Overview
A rapid-fire physical warm-up where players stand in a circle and pass a kinetic charge using specific vocal cues and physical gestures. As the energy moves, players must instantly adapt to sudden changes in direction, cross-circle jumps, and full-group physical resets.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Commit 100%; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Timing exercises; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop peripheral awareness, active listening, and group mind by training players to track multiple physical and vocal threads in real-time without hesitation.

## Setup
Players stand in a clear, open circle facing inward, ensuring everyone has clear sightlines to all other players. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle facing inward, ensuring everyone has clear sightlines to all other players.
2. The facilitator initiates the game by sending a 'Whoosh' to the player on their left or right, accompanied by a sweeping two-handed gesture pointing in that direction.
3. The receiving player must immediately pass the 'Whoosh' to the next player in the same direction using the same gesture, keeping the momentum going around the circle.
4. Introduce the 'Wow' command: Any player receiving a 'Whoosh' can block it by sweeping both arms downward and saying 'Wow!', which reverses the direction of the flow.
5. Introduce the 'Zap' command: Instead of passing to a neighbor, a player can point both hands directly at someone across the circle and say 'Zap!', sending the energy directly to them.
6. Introduce the 'Groovelicious' command: Any player with the energy can wave their hands and say 'Groovelicious!', prompting the entire circle to bend down, wiggle, and chant 'Groovelicious!' in unison before the initiator restarts the flow.
7. Introduce the 'Freakout' command: A player can wave their hands wildly and yell 'Freakout!', causing everyone to run to the center, scream, scramble, and reform a brand-new circle, with the initiator restarting the game.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep the tempo high; encourage players to make mistakes with joy and keep the energy moving rather than stopping to apologize.
- Use the side-coaching cue: 'Commit to the physical gesture! Big movements make it easier for the group to track.'
- Address the common pitfall of players hesitating or overthinking their next move by reminding them that 'the first thought is a gift' and to react instantly.
- Use the side-coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes on the whole circle, not just your immediate neighbors, to maintain peripheral awareness.'

## Variations
- Sound and Motion Swap: Replace the default words and gestures with custom sounds, such as sports car sounds like 'Vroom' for passing, 'Screech' for reversing, and 'Beep' for zapping.
- Silent Mode: Play the entire game using only the physical gestures and eye contact, removing all vocal cues to heighten visual awareness.
- Elimination Speed Run: Increase the speed progressively, challenging the group to maintain a flawless flow for 60 seconds without any pauses or errors.

## Debrief
- How did your focus shift as we added more commands to track?
- What did it feel like when the entire group instantly committed to 'Groovelicious' or 'Freakout' without hesitation?
- How does keeping your eyes on the entire circle help you react faster to sudden changes?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards, especially for the 'Freakout' phase. Players with mobility limitations can adapt the gestures (such as a seated 'Freakout' or 'Groovelicious' using upper-body movement only) and the group should adjust their pacing to ensure everyone is safely included.

## Why It Works
This game builds group mind by demanding total presence and shared rhythm. By combining physical gestures with vocal cues, it engages multiple sensory channels, forcing players out of their analytical minds and into a state of pure, spontaneous reaction.

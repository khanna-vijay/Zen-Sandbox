# Viscosity Walk

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Experience and physicalize the changing resistance of the space around you.

## Overview
Viscosity Walk is a whole-group physical exploration where players move through an open space that dynamically changes in density. By imagining and reacting to different imaginary mediums—from light mist to thick honey and solidifying cement—players activate their entire bodies and practice weight and resistance mime. The exercise builds physical commitment and spatial awareness in a highly energetic, low-stakes environment.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players spread out so they have room to move freely without colliding. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to find a personal starting spot in the open space, standing comfortably with enough room to stretch their arms.
2. Explain that they are wearing an invisible, weightless oxygen mask that allows them to breathe perfectly regardless of the surrounding environment.
3. Direct the players to begin walking slowly through the space, first experiencing it as normal, thin air.
4. Call out the first transition: the air is turning into a thick, damp mist. Prompt them to feel the moisture on their skin and the slight drag on their movements.
5. Progressively call out increasingly dense substances: warm water, heavy cooking oil, thick honey, and sticky gelatin.
6. Instruct players to adjust their entire physical posture, speed, and muscle tension to match the resistance of each new medium.
7. Transition the environment into wet cement, which slowly begins to dry and harden around them, restricting their movement further and further until they are completely locked in place.
8. Once the cement is completely hard and everyone is frozen, count down from three and instruct the group to explosively shatter the cement to break free.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't just use your hands; feel the resistance in your shoulders, hips, and knees.'
- Side-coaching cue: 'How does this substance change your center of gravity? Where is the weight shifting?'
- Pitfall: Players moving too fast through thick substances. Fix: Remind them that true resistance slows us down; challenge them to move in slow motion to show the density.
- Pitfall: Disconnection between upper and lower body (e.g., moving hands like they are in honey but walking normally). Fix: Coach them to feel the substance against their shins and feet.

## Variations
- Temperature Shift: Add temperature elements to the substances (e.g., freezing slush, hot lava) to explore sensory reactions.
- Blind Viscosity: Players close their eyes (if safe) to focus entirely on internal physical sensation and balance.
- Shared Substance: Two players must move through the substance together, reacting to how their partner's movement displaces the medium.

## Debrief
- How did changing the imaginary substance change your physical commitment and focus?
- Which substance was the hardest to mime, and what physical adjustments helped make it feel real?
- How does physicalizing resistance help you build believable environments in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players maintain peripheral awareness to avoid physical collisions, especially as their movement becomes more labored or explosive. Offer low-impact alternatives for the final 'shatter' moment for players with joint or mobility sensitivities.

## Why It Works
By systematically increasing the imagined density of the room, players are forced to engage muscle groups they normally ignore. This builds muscle memory for weight and resistance mime, transforming abstract space work into a concrete, felt physical reality.

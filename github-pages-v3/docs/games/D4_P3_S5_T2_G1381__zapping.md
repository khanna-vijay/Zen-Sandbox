# Channel Surfing

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Switch between distinct television channels that are all hilariously connected by a single audience suggestion.

## Overview
In this fast-paced comedy game, players represent different television channels, each with a unique genre or format. A host uses an imaginary remote control to 'zap' between channels, forcing players to instantly continue their broadcast while weaving a single, shared theme into their programming.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Piece; Serve the Story; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Thematic Synthesis; Pacing & Rhythm; World-Building; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Weave the threads; Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light); First Thought drills
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops thematic synthesis, active listening, and narrative weaving by challenging players to adapt a single core theme into diverse genres on the fly.

## Setup
Five players stand in a line facing the audience. One player is designated as the Host (holding an imaginary remote), while the other four players represent the television channels. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign one player to be the Host and the remaining four players to be the Channels, standing side-by-side facing the audience.
2. Ask the audience to assign a distinct television genre or format to each of the four Channel players (e.g., home shopping, nature documentary, soap opera, sports broadcast).
3. Obtain a single, mundane theme or object from the audience (e.g., 'paperclips' or 'gardening') that must feature in all broadcasts.
4. The Host begins the game by pointing their imaginary remote at one player and 'clicking' it, prompting that player to immediately start their show, incorporating the theme.
5. The active player performs their genre enthusiastically until the Host 'zaps' to another channel by pointing the remote at a different player.
6. When zapped, the active player must freeze, and the newly selected player must instantly begin or resume their broadcast.
7. As the game progresses, players must listen closely to the other channels and weave details, plot points, or characters from those channels into their own broadcasts.
8. The Host varies the speed of the zapping, building to a fast-paced climax where all channels are deeply interconnected before calling 'Scene!'

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Pick up the thread!' Encourage players to reference details dropped by the channel immediately preceding them to build a cohesive universe.
- Pitfall: Players restarting their scene from the beginning every time they are zapped. Fix: Remind them to freeze in physical and narrative suspense, picking up mid-sentence if possible.
- Coaching cue: 'Commit to the genre!' The comedy relies on the contrast between a serious format (like a documentary) and a silly format (like a game show) treating the same theme with absolute sincerity.
- Pitfall: The Host zapping too slowly. Fix: Side-coach the Host to keep edits sharp and unpredictable, sometimes zapping after just a single word to create high-energy comedy.

## Variations
- Cross-Pollination: Characters or events from one channel physically cross over or get reported as breaking news on another channel.
- Commercial Break: The Host shouts 'Commercial!' and all four channels must instantly unite to perform a joint, high-energy advertisement for a product related to the theme.
- Foreign Dub: One channel speaks in a completely gibberish language, and another channel acts as the live English translator when zapped.

## Debrief
- How did listening to the other channels help you expand your own channel's content?
- What did it feel like to let go of planning and rely entirely on your first instinct when zapped?
- How did we manage to weave the different threads together to make the television universe feel connected?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are standing comfortably; players with mobility needs can sit in chairs representing 'TV screens.' Remind players to keep genres respectful and avoid punching down on cultural or linguistic stereotypes.

## Why It Works
By keeping the central theme constant but changing the delivery format, this game forces players to practice thematic synthesis. The unpredictable 'zapping' mechanic bypasses the analytical brain, encouraging unfiltered spontaneity and teaching players to serve the larger piece rather than just their individual scene.

# Retroactive Reality Warp

![Retroactive Reality Warp — infographic](img/D3_P4_S6_T2_G433__the-chrono-warp-justifier.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Instantly rewrite the past of your scene to justify a sudden, reality-altering twist.

## Overview
Retroactive Reality Warp is a high-stakes narrative game where players perform a standard scene until a facilitator injects a reality-altering twist. Players must immediately accept this new truth and retroactively justify everything that happened before, making the twist feel like it was the secret truth all along. It is a highly cerebral, thrilling exercise in narrative agility and deep listening.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Reincorporation-as-justification; Justify the absurd; Platform/Tilt; Endowment-acceptance; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop advanced justification skills, specifically using reincorporation to retroactively align past actions with a newly introduced reality, thereby serving the overall story.

## Setup
Played virtually over a video conferencing platform. Two to three active players start with their cameras on, while the remaining participants and the facilitator keep their cameras off. No physical props or special space are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign two or three players to start an on-camera scene, establishing a clear platform with defined relationships, a specific location, and an active conflict.
2. The facilitator, acting as the Warp Master, keeps their camera off and closely monitors the scene's progression, looking for established details, physical choices, or emotional beats.
3. At a pivotal moment—typically 60 to 90 seconds in—the facilitator calls out 'WARP!' followed by a concise, reality-altering prompt that redefines a core element of the scene.
4. The active players must immediately accept this new prompt as absolute truth without pausing or breaking character.
5. Players must immediately re-examine their previous dialogue, physical actions, and emotional choices, finding creative explanations that make those past events perfectly logical under the new reality.
6. Through dialogue, players explicitly state how their prior behavior was actually a symptom, cover-up, or direct result of this newly revealed truth.
7. Once the past is successfully justified, players must pivot their characters' immediate objectives and stakes to align with the new reality, driving the scene to a natural conclusion.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Tip: Encourage players to avoid denying the past. Instead of saying 'Oh, I guess we weren't actually in a kitchen,' they should say 'Of course we are in a kitchen, because that is where the simulation always starts!'
- Pitfall: The Warp Master issues prompts that are too minor or purely additive. Fix: Ensure prompts fundamentally redefine the scene's core, such as changing a character's identity, the stakes of an object, or the true nature of the environment.
- Coaching Tip: Remind players to use 'reincorporation-as-justification.' Every weird physical choice or throwaway line from the first half of the scene is a goldmine for explaining the new reality.
- Pitfall: Players freeze up trying to find the 'perfect' logical explanation. Fix: Side-coach with 'The first thought is a gift! Say the first justification that pops into your head and commit to it fully.'

## Variations
- Double Warp: For highly advanced players, introduce a second 'WARP!' prompt near the end of the scene, requiring a double-layered retroactive justification.
- Audience Warps: Allow non-active players in the virtual chat to type in Warp prompts, which the facilitator selects and reads aloud.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have the history of your scene completely rewritten mid-way through?
- What strategies did you use to quickly connect your past actions to the new reality?
- How does retroactively justifying a twist make the story feel more cohesive to an audience?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game involves sudden shifts in reality and identity, establish beforehand that prompts will not touch on personal trauma, real-world phobias, or sensitive identity traits. Players can use a designated virtual 'stop' gesture if a prompt inadvertently crosses a personal boundary.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to look backward instead of forward to solve a narrative disruption, this game exercises the 'reincorporation-as-justification' muscle. It proves that any offer, no matter how disruptive, can be seamlessly integrated into a story if players treat their past choices as deliberate clues rather than mistakes.

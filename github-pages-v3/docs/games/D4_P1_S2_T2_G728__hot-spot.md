# Hot Spot

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Rescue your teammates from the spotlight by jumping in with a new song.

## Overview
A high-energy circle game where players take turns singing in the center while the rest of the group enthusiastically backs them up. The core mechanic relies on rapid support, as players must jump in to replace the singer by launching into a new song inspired by a word, theme, or melody of the current one.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Fail Joyfully; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Support Work; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Tap-ins; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops group mind, rapid support work, and unfiltered spontaneity by practicing immediate, active rescue of ensemble members in high-pressure moments.

## Setup
Players stand in a shoulder-to-shoulder circle with an open space in the center. No props or instruments are needed.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle with all players facing inward.
2. One player steps confidently into the center of the circle and begins singing any recognizable song.
3. The players in the outer circle immediately join in, singing along, clapping, and dancing to support the center singer, regardless of whether they know the exact lyrics.
4. To rescue the person in the center, another player must run into the middle, gently tap the current singer out, and immediately start singing a new song.
5. The new song should be inspired by the previous song, such as sharing a lyric, a theme, an artist, or a musical vibe.
6. The previous singer returns to the outer circle and immediately joins the chorus of support for the new center singer.
7. Keep the transitions rapid, aiming to keep each singer in the center for no more than five to ten seconds.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't let them hang! If you see someone struggling for lyrics, run in and save them immediately!'
- Pitfall: Players waiting too long to enter because they are trying to think of the perfect song. Fix: Encourage players to jump in on their very first instinct, even if they only know one line or a chorus.
- Coaching cue: 'Loud, enthusiastic backup! The circle is the engine that powers the person in the middle.'
- Pitfall: The center singer staying in too long because the outer circle is hesitant. Fix: Side-coach: 'Three, two, one, next song! Go, go, go!'

## Variations
- Word Association: The incoming singer must use the exact last word sung by the previous singer as the first word of their new song.
- Gibberish Opera: Instead of real songs, players must sing passionate, improvised operatic songs in gibberish, with the circle matching their emotional intensity.
- Genre Swap: The facilitator calls out a musical genre and the person in the center must instantly adapt their song to that style.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to be in the center when the circle was loudly backing you up?
- What did you notice about the timing of your rescues? What made you hesitate, and how did you overcome it?
- How does this game illustrate the concept of having your partner's back on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Singing can provoke high anxiety. Emphasize that vocal quality, pitch, and lyric accuracy do not matter at all. Encourage physical support like clapping and swaying to make the space feel warm and non-judgmental. Allow players to step in with simple chants or rhythmic spoken-word if they are highly uncomfortable singing.

## Why It Works
It bypasses the analytical brain by demanding instant action. The physical act of stepping into the center to save a teammate builds a visceral sense of trust and mutual support, while the collective backup singing removes the fear of individual failure.

# The Orbit

> Silently coordinate with a secret partner to encircle a target before the group detects you.

## Overview
The Orbit is a high-tension, silent group game where two secretly designated players must use non-verbal cues and peripheral vision to coordinate and physically encircle another player. The rest of the group must remain highly observant, attempting to identify the secret pair before they can complete their trap. It creates an electric atmosphere of shared focus, silent teamwork, and hyper-awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops peripheral awareness, non-verbal communication, and group mind by forcing players to read subtle physical shifts and coordinate actions without speaking.

## Setup
Players stand in a spacious circle facing inward. The facilitator needs enough space to walk around the outside of the circle. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Have all players stand in a circle, facing inward, and close their eyes.
2. The facilitator walks around the outside of the circle and silently taps two players on the shoulder to designate them as the Orbiters.
3. Instruct all players to open their eyes and remain standing in the circle, keeping their hands to themselves but staying highly alert.
4. The two designated Orbiters must silently communicate using only eye contact and micro-movements to select a target player in the circle.
5. Once a target is chosen, the Orbiters must leave their spots in the circle, move across the space, and join hands to form a complete ring around the target player.
6. While this is happening, any player in the circle can call out a suspicion by pointing at a player and shouting 'Orbiter!'
7. If a non-Orbiter correctly identifies one of the Orbiters before they complete their ring, that Orbiter is eliminated and the round resets.
8. If the Orbiters successfully join hands around their target before being identified, the target player is captured and eliminated from the next round.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to keep their eyes moving and avoid staring directly at one person, which helps build true peripheral awareness.
- Enforce a strict 'walking speed only' rule to keep the focus on stealth, tension, and awareness rather than physical speed or running.
- To prevent random guessing, limit players to one guess per round, or rule that an incorrect guess results in the guesser's immediate elimination.
- Encourage the Orbiters to use misdirection, such as looking at one player while planning to capture another.

## Variations
- Blind Orbit: The target player must keep their eyes closed during the round, relying on auditory cues, while everyone else watches.
- Multi-Orbit: For larger groups, designate three or four Orbiters who must all join hands to encircle a target, requiring even greater coordination.
- Silent Accusations: Instead of shouting, players must make eye contact with another non-Orbiter and nod toward the suspect to build a silent consensus before pointing.

## Debrief
- How did you detect who the secret partners were without them speaking?
- As an Orbiter, how did you establish connection and agree on a target without giving yourselves away?
- How does this level of hyper-awareness translate to supporting your scene partners on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game involves physically encircling someone, establish a clear boundary that no physical contact is allowed when forming the ring. The ring should be formed with a comfortable buffer of space, and the floor must be completely clear of tripping hazards.

## Why It Works
This game forces players to look at the entire group as a single system rather than focusing on individual faces. To succeed, both the secret partners and the observers must tap into group mind, reading micro-expressions, posture shifts, and weight transfers that betray intent.

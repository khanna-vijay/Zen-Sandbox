# Ripple and Resonance

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build a tight, escalating narrative by exploring the cascading consequences of a single disruptive event.

## Overview
This exercise challenges players to construct a cohesive narrative arc by focusing entirely on cause-and-effect progression. It begins with a stable platform and a clear character desire, which is immediately disrupted by an external catalyst. Players then enter sequentially to play out the logical, escalating consequences of that disruption, transforming a simple setup into a high-stakes story.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; Justification; Raising the Stakes; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; If this is true, what else is true?; Stakes-escalation reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master narrative architecture by practicing the Platform and Tilt technique, learning to establish a stable baseline, introduce a disruptive catalyst, and build a justified chain of escalating consequences that serves the story.

## Setup
Players stand in a semi-circle facing the performance space. No props or special materials are required. The facilitator stands to the side to guide the pacing and call out transitions.

## How to Play
1. Establish the Platform: One player steps into the space to establish a character, a specific setting, and a clear, simple, and active core desire or 'Want'.
2. Introduce the Tilt: A second player enters briefly or calls out from offstage to introduce a sudden, external 'Catalyst' event that directly threatens or alters the first player's established desire.
3. The Immediate Ripple: The first player reacts physically and emotionally to this catalyst, letting the stakes land in their body and making an immediate, active choice to address the new reality.
4. The First Resonance: A third player enters the scene as a new character or an existing element of the environment, introducing a choice or piece of information that is a direct, logical consequence of the immediate ripple.
5. Escalating the Chain: Subsequent players enter one by one, each adding a new 'Resonance' that builds directly on the previous player's action, applying the 'if this is true, what else is true?' principle to raise the stakes.
6. Justifying and Driving: Each entering player must ensure their contribution does not merely comment on the situation, but actively drives the narrative forward by introducing new, justified complications.
7. Finding the Resolution: Once four to five layers of consequences have been established, the active players work together to bring the high-stakes situation to a narrative climax or temporary resolution.
8. Facilitator Edit: The facilitator calls 'scene' once the narrative reaches a satisfying peak or a natural resolution of the cascading events.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: Remind the first player to let the Catalyst truly affect them. They should not brush it off; the emotional reaction is what establishes the stakes for the rest of the scene.
- Pitfall and Fix: A common pitfall is players entering with completely unrelated problems. Fix this by side-coaching: 'How does your character's arrival directly result from the event that just happened?'
- Coaching Cue: Encourage players to use the 'if this is true, what else is true?' framework to find logical but surprising escalations rather than random, absurd twists.
- Pacing Tip: Keep the entries rapid. Each Resonance should be a sharp, decisive injection of consequence rather than a long, drawn-out monologue.

## Variations
- The Silent Ripple: Run the entire exercise through physical action and object work, forcing players to communicate the cause-and-effect chain without relying on dialogue.
- The Reverse Cascade: Start with the final, chaotic climax and work backward step-by-step to discover the original, simple desire and the catalyst that caused it.
- Tag-Team Resonance: Instead of adding new characters, players use tag-outs to play different perspectives of the same escalating event in different locations.

## Debrief
- How did focusing on direct consequences help the story feel organic rather than forced?
- What was the difference between an offer that merely commented on the chaos versus one that actively drove the narrative forward?
- How did the initial 'Want' anchor the scene even as the stakes escalated to extreme levels?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that physical reactions to high-stakes catalysts remain safe and controlled within the space. If a catalyst involves a physical hazard, players should mime their reactions safely without actual running or reckless physical contact.

## Why It Works
This game works because it operationalizes the Platform and Tilt narrative structure. By separating the platform, the tilt, and the rising action into distinct, sequential steps, players learn to value cause-and-effect logic over random invention, resulting in highly satisfying and cohesive stories.

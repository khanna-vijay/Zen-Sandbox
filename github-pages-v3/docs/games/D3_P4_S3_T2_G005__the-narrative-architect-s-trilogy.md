# Platform, Pattern, Pivot

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Establish a stable reality, find its rhythmic pattern, and execute a perfectly justified narrative tilt.

## Overview
A structured three-phase scene-work exercise where players build a rich base reality, discover a recurring behavioral pattern, and then introduce a justified disruption (the tilt). This systematic approach helps proficient improvisers move past random plot twists to construct satisfying, organic narrative arcs.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Show, Don't Tell; Base Reality First; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes; World-Building; Game Identification; Justification; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Stakes-escalation reps; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Finding & Playing the Game; Reincorporation-as-justification
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master the Platform/Tilt technique by establishing a solid base reality (the platform) and executing a logical, high-stakes disruption (the tilt) that is fully justified by prior details.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with two chairs. The remaining players sit in an audience arc to observe, listen actively, and analyze the narrative architecture.

## How to Play
1. Two players take the stage and receive a simple, mundane location suggestion that implies a routine activity (e.g., 'a dry cleaner' or 'a community garden').
2. Phase 1 (The Platform): Players spend the first 60 seconds establishing a grounded base reality using specific sensory details and physical object work, deliberately avoiding any immediate conflict.
3. Phase 2 (The Pattern): Players identify and lean into a repetitive behavioral loop or relational dynamic that naturally emerges from their interaction (e.g., one player constantly seeking approval, or a repetitive physical task).
4. The players repeat and heighten this pattern two to three times to firmly establish the scene's status quo for the audience.
5. Phase 3 (The Pivot/Tilt): On the facilitator's cue 'Pivot!' (or self-initiated once proficient), the player currently speaking introduces a significant piece of new information or a radical choice.
6. This pivot must be fully justified, drawing directly from the established details or character dynamics of the platform rather than introducing an arbitrary external event.
7. The players immediately accept the pivot, letting it permanently disrupt the pattern and escalate the emotional stakes of the scene.
8. The scene continues under this new, heightened reality, driving toward a logical resolution based on the consequences of the pivot.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Ground the platform first!' If players rush to conflict, remind them to build the environment and relationship before introducing problems.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Find the loop!' Help players notice their own repetitive behaviors or dialogue patterns and repeat them deliberately.
- Pitfall: The 'Deus ex Machina' Pivot. Players often introduce random external events (e.g., an alien invasion or a sudden lottery win) instead of a justified internal shift. Fix: Pause the scene and ask, 'What is already true in this room that can cause this shift?'
- Pitfall: Ignoring the Platform. Players sometimes abandon all their initial object work and environmental details once the pivot occurs. Fix: Coach them to physically interact with the same objects, but with a completely different emotional energy reflecting the new stakes.
- Coaching cue: 'Escalate through consequence.' Ensure the pivot leads to immediate, unavoidable consequences for both characters, rather than being talked about in the abstract.

## Variations
- Self-Directed Tilt: Remove the facilitator's verbal cue. Players must actively listen to each other and decide on the optimal narrative moment to initiate the pivot themselves.
- The Detail Drip: During Phase 1, the facilitator calls out 'Detail!' at random intervals, forcing the active player to immediately name a highly specific sensory element (smell, texture, temperature) that must be integrated into the platform.
- The Double Pivot: For advanced players, introduce a second 'Pivot!' cue later in the scene, requiring them to adapt to a secondary escalation that builds on the consequences of the first.

## Debrief
- How did establishing a detailed, conflict-free platform make the eventual pivot feel more impactful?
- What clues or behaviors in the pattern phase made the pivot feel earned and justified rather than random?
- How did your physical relationship to the space and objects change after the tilt occurred?
- For the audience: At what exact moment did you feel the narrative engine shift, and why did it work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that physical object work and environmental details respect physical boundaries. If players choose to pivot into high-emotion territory, remind the group that emotional intensity should never compromise player safety or cross pre-established personal boundaries.

## Why It Works
By separating scene construction into distinct phases—Platform, Pattern, and Pivot—this game demystifies narrative structure. It forces players to slow down and build a stable base reality first, ensuring that when the 'tilt' occurs, it has a solid foundation to disrupt. This directly trains the brain to look for organic, justified escalation points rather than relying on cheap, unearned plot twists.

# The Soundtrack Shop

![The Soundtrack Shop — infographic](img/D3_P1_S2_T1_G982__cd-shop.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Pitch absurd custom albums and bring their hilarious hit songs to life on the spot.

## Overview
A high-energy musical comedy game where a customer seeks the perfect audio gift for a highly specific occasion. A clever shopkeeper pitches increasingly bizarre custom albums, prompting a team of backing singers to instantly perform the improvised tracks.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Show, Don't Tell; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind; Play for the Back Row
- **Skill(s):** Heightening & Exploration; Active Gifting; Support Work; Vocal Craft; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** The 'ladder' (escalating beats); Endowment-gifting drills; Walk-ons; Vocal characterization; Projection
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To master the 'ladder' technique of escalating comedic beats, while practicing active gifting and immediate physical/vocal commitment to musical styles.

## Setup
An performance space with a clear stage area. Two chairs or a counter setup for the shopkeeper and customer. A designated 'stage' area nearby for 2-3 singers (and an optional keyboardist/musician). The rest of the group acts as the audience.

## How to Play
1. Ask the audience for a specific, slightly unusual occasion or person someone might buy a gift for (e.g., 'a cousin graduating from dental school' or 'a retirement party for a professional mime').
2. Establish the scene with two players: the Customer, who enters looking for the perfect musical gift, and the Shopkeeper, who runs the boutique store.
3. The Customer explains their highly specific gifting dilemma to the Shopkeeper, setting the emotional stakes.
4. The Shopkeeper enthusiastically offers a custom album solution, inventing a fictional artist, a music genre, and a highly specific song title that perfectly (or terribly) matches the occasion.
5. Upon hearing the song title, the off-stage singers immediately step forward, strike a physical pose reflecting the genre, and perform a short, high-energy chorus or verse of the song.
6. The singers must 'show, don't tell' by instantly committing to the vocal style, choreography, and emotional tone of the pitched genre.
7. After the song concludes (usually 30-45 seconds), the Customer and Shopkeeper react, and the Shopkeeper pitches a second, more escalated album and song, raising the comedic stakes.
8. Repeat this cycle for 3 to 4 songs, with each pitch and performance climbing the 'ladder' of absurdity, before the Customer enthusiastically purchases their favorite option to close the scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Give them a gift!' Remind the Shopkeeper to provide clear, inspiring details (genre, artist name, song title) to set the singers up for success.
- Coaching Cue: 'Climb the ladder!' Ensure each successive pitch escalates in intensity, absurdity, or emotional stakes rather than repeating the same comedic beat.
- Pitfall: Singers hesitate or discuss what to sing. Fix: Encourage immediate physical movement. Striking a physical pose together instantly aligns the singers' energy and style.
- Pitfall: The songs go on too long. Fix: The facilitator or musician should edit the song with a clear musical resolution or a physical 'button' after one strong verse and chorus.

## Variations
- The Vinyl Revival: The Shopkeeper must physically 'play' the record by dropping a needle, allowing them to speed up, slow down, or skip the track to challenge the singers.
- The Algorithm: Set in a futuristic streaming service headquarters where a digital assistant pitches custom-generated playlists to a user.

## Debrief
- How did the Shopkeeper's specific 'gifts' (genre and title) make it easier for the singers to commit instantly?
- How did we use the 'ladder' technique to make each successive song feel bigger and more satisfying than the last?
- What physical choices did the singers make to immediately communicate the genre before even singing a word?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles for quick entrances. If players are uncomfortable singing, they can perform spoken-word poetry, rap, or dramatic soundscapes instead of melodic singing.

## Why It Works
This game works because it relies on a clear, repeating structural engine (the 'game engine') that handles the narrative heavy lifting, freeing the performers to focus entirely on commitment and escalation. By using the 'ladder' technique, the comedy naturally builds, while the 'show, don't tell' requirement forces singers to bypass intellectualizing and jump straight into physical and vocal expression.

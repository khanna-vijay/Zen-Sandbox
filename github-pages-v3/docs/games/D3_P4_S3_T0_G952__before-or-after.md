# Before or After

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Explore a story's timeline by letting the audience jump backward or forward in time.

## Overview
Players improvise a series of interconnected scenes where the chronological order is dictated entirely by the audience. After each scene, the audience decides whether the next segment takes place immediately before or immediately after the events just witnessed, forcing players to rapidly construct a cohesive, non-linear narrative.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Room Reading; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops narrative architecture, pacing, and room reading by training players to explore cause-and-effect relationships out of chronological order.

## Setup
An in-person performance space with a clear stage area and an audience. A facilitator acts as the host to moderate the timeline choices. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Select two to four players to step onto the stage to perform the initial scene.
2. Ask the audience for a simple suggestion, such as an unusual location or a mundane activity, to inspire the opening scene.
3. The players improvise a short, self-contained scene of approximately one to two minutes, establishing clear characters, relationships, and an active conflict.
4. The facilitator calls 'Freeze!' to pause the action at a high point of tension or a natural narrative beat.
5. The facilitator turns to the audience and asks: 'Do you want to see what happened immediately before this scene, or what happens next?'
6. Based on the audience's decision, the players immediately initiate the new scene, maintaining character consistency and honoring the established reality.
7. If 'before' is chosen, players must show the context, choices, or events that led directly to the initial scene. If 'after' is chosen, they must show the immediate consequences.
8. Repeat this process for three to four chapters, allowing the narrative to expand both forward and backward in time.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage players to make bold, clear choices in the first scene so there are plenty of narrative hooks, unexplained objects, or secrets to explore later.
- If the audience chooses 'before,' players must avoid rewriting history; they must honor the established facts of the first scene and show how those facts came to be.
- Watch out for players trying to explain too much in exposition. Remind them to show the action and relationships rather than talking about what they are about to do.
- Keep the transitions tight. The facilitator should prompt the audience quickly and get the next scene started within seconds to maintain high energy and momentum.

## Variations
- The Butterfly Effect: Introduce a rule where a 'before' scene can slightly alter the reality of the 'after' scene, forcing players to adapt to a shifting timeline.
- Multi-Character Mosaic: Allow off-stage players to enter as new characters in the past or future scenes, expanding the world beyond the original pairing.
- Time Jump Extremes: Instead of 'immediately' before or after, the facilitator can offer options like 'ten years before' or 'ten minutes after' to test the players' physical and emotional range.

## Debrief
- How did knowing we might see the past or future change how you played the very first scene?
- What was more challenging: justifying the past ('before') or advancing the future ('after')? Why?
- How did the audience's choices help shape the pacing and tension of the overall story?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that when jumping back in time, players respect established physical boundaries and character consent. If a past scene explores a sensitive relationship dynamic, players should check in briefly off-stage or use non-contact physical choices to represent conflict safely.

## Why It Works
By giving the audience control over the timeline, players are forced to abandon rigid, pre-planned plots. They must focus on the immediate narrative architecture, treating every established detail as a sacred truth. This builds a deep understanding of cause and effect, as players must instantly synthesize past actions with future consequences to serve the larger story.

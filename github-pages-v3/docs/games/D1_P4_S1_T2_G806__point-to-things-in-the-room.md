# Point and Name

> Bypass your internal editor by pointing at objects and rapidly renaming your reality.

## Overview
A high-energy, solo-simultaneous warm-up where players move through the space pointing at physical objects and calling out names. By shifting from accurate naming to completely random naming, and finally to delayed naming, players disrupt their internal editor and practice instant, unfiltered spontaneity.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Fail Joyfully; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To train unfiltered spontaneity and the principle that the first thought is a gift by using physical movement to outrun cognitive hesitation.

## Setup
An open room free of obstacles where players can safely walk around. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Have all players disperse throughout the room and begin walking at a brisk, energetic pace.
2. Introduce Round 1: Instruct players to point directly at objects in the room and loudly state exactly what they are, ensuring the physical point happens a split-second before the word is spoken.
3. After one minute, transition to Round 2: Instruct players to point at objects but loudly name them as anything except what they actually are.
4. Encourage players to avoid logical associations or patterns, aiming instead for completely unrelated words.
5. Transition to Round 3: Instruct players to point at an object and say nothing, then point to a second object and call out the name of the first object, continuing this one-step-behind pattern.
6. Instruct players to keep moving rapidly throughout all rounds, maintaining high physical energy and vocal volume.
7. End the drill by calling freeze and having everyone celebrate the mental chaos with a round of applause.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to let their hand lead their mouth. If they hesitate, encourage them to point first and let whatever sound comes out be the answer.
- Watch out for patterning in Round 2, such as naming everything a fruit or a color. Challenge them to leap to completely unrelated categories.
- When players freeze or make a mistake in Round 3, coach them to fail joyfully by laughing, throwing their hands in the air, and immediately continuing.
- Keep the pace fast. The exercise loses its efficacy if players slow down their walking speed to give their brains more time to think.

## Variations
- The Two-Step Delay: Increase the difficulty in Round 3 by naming the object pointed to two steps prior.
- Emotional Pointing: In Round 2, players must deliver the incorrect name with an extreme emotion dictated by the facilitator.
- Partnered Pointing: Players work in pairs, where Player A points to an object and Player B must instantly name it.

## Debrief
- How did it feel when your brain desperately wanted to find the perfect wrong word instead of just any word?
- What physical sensations did you notice when you successfully bypassed your internal editor?
- How does the fear of making a mistake change our physical posture and movement speed?
- How can we bring this sense of pointing first and figuring it out later into our scene work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the walking path is clear of tripping hazards. Players with mobility differences can participate at their own comfortable pace, or perform the exercise seated by pointing at items within their field of vision.

## Why It Works
By forcing the physical action of pointing to precede the cognitive action of naming, the game disrupts the brain's standard editing loop. The rapid pace and simultaneous play remove the pressure of an audience, allowing players to experience the creative freedom of their very first impulse without judgment.

# Time Warp Scene

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Direct a scene through time by calling out fast-forwards and rewinds to build a story.

## Overview
In this narrative-driven game, an off-stage director actively manipulates the timeline of an ongoing scene. By calling out jumps forward or backward in time, the director challenges the actors to instantly adapt to new realities. The players must immediately show the consequences of past actions or explore the origins of their current relationships.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Serve the Piece
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** Develops narrative architecture, pacing, and the ability to raise stakes by exploring cause-and-effect relationships across different points in time.

## Setup
A performance space for two to three active players, with the remaining players acting as the audience. One player (or the facilitator) is designated as the Time Director and stands off-stage.

## How to Play
1. Ask the audience for a simple suggestion, such as a relationship or a mundane daily activity, to inspire the opening scene.
2. The active players begin performing a grounded, realistic scene, establishing their characters, setting, and initial conflict.
3. At any point during the scene, the Time Director calls out a temporal command, such as 'Fast forward ten minutes' or 'Rewind to three years ago.'
4. Upon hearing the command, the actors freeze briefly to process the jump, then immediately resume acting in the new time period.
5. Actors must instantly adapt their physicalities, emotional states, and relationships to reflect the passage or reversal of time, maintaining narrative continuity.
6. The actors should avoid explaining what happened during the time jump; instead, they must show the immediate aftermath through their actions and dialogue.
7. The Time Director continues to manipulate the timeline, using jumps to skip mundane details, highlight dramatic consequences, or reveal backstory.
8. The scene concludes when the Time Director calls 'Scene' after a satisfying narrative arc or climax has been reached.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Show us the physical toll of that time jump! Don't just talk about what happened in the gap—show us the immediate aftermath!'
- Pitfall: Players often spend too much time verbally explaining what happened during the time jump. Fix: Coach them to start the new segment in the middle of an action or high-stakes moment rather than narrating the transition.
- Pitfall: The Director makes jumps too rapidly, not letting scenes breathe. Fix: Remind the Director to let a beat play out long enough to establish a platform before shifting time again.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Director, look for the unsaid moments. Jump to the moment they regretted making that promise!'

## Variations
- Butterfly Effect: The director rewinds a scene to a critical decision point, orders a different choice, and then fast-forwards to see the altered future.
- Audience-Led Jumps: Instead of a single director, the audience or off-stage players call out the time jumps.
- Micro-Macro Jumps: Restricting the jumps to either very small increments (e.g., 'three seconds later') or massive increments (e.g., 'one thousand years later') to test different comedic and dramatic scales.

## Debrief
- How did jumping forward or backward help you discover new stakes for your characters?
- What was the difference between explaining what happened during a time jump versus showing it through your behavior?
- As the director, how did you decide when a scene needed to jump to serve the overall story?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that time jumps do not bypass player boundaries or force characters into traumatic future or past scenarios without consent. Players can use a standard check-in or pause if a temporal jump lands them in an uncomfortable narrative space.

## Why It Works
This game acts as a powerful narrative engine by stripping away the slow, linear transitions of traditional scenes. It forces players to focus on cause and effect, instantly highlighting how choices in the past dictate the reality of the present, which builds strong narrative architecture and sharpens pacing.

# Double Endowment

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Guide your partner to perform a secret physical action using only environmental and physical clues.

## Overview
Two players enter a scene, each holding a secret physical task they must get the other player to perform. Without explicitly stating the command, players must use environmental staging, physical endowment, and suggestive dialogue to lead their partner to the action while remaining highly receptive to their partner's hidden agenda.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; World-Building; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment chains; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop active gifting and offer reception by training players to read subtle physical cues, accept endowments instantly, and justify unexpected physical choices to make their partner look brilliant.

## Setup
Two players stand on stage. The facilitator secretly assigns a specific, physical action to each player that they must get the other player to perform (e.g., Player A must get Player B to drink from an imaginary puddle; Player B must get Player A to polish a statue's shoe). The facilitator also establishes a shared location. The rest of the group acts as the observing audience.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator pulls both players aside individually to assign their secret physical tasks and establishes a common, high-stakes location.
2. The scene begins with both players in character, immediately establishing the environment and their relationship.
3. Players must actively work to guide their partner toward their secret task using physical endowment, spatial manipulation, and suggestive dialogue, without ever stating the action directly.
4. Players must remain highly sensitive to their partner's physical offers, looking for clues about what their partner is trying to get them to do.
5. When a player senses their partner is guiding them toward an action, they must boldly commit to that physical choice, accepting the physical setup.
6. Once a player successfully performs the secret action, the other player must subtly acknowledge it in character, and the scene continues until the second action is also completed.
7. The scene concludes when both secret actions have been successfully performed and justified within the narrative.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid direct commands. If a player says 'Pick up that sword and lick it,' the game loses its magic. Encourage them to describe the sword's delicious honey coating instead.
- Remind players to be 'easy to read but also easy to lead.' They should actively try to guess what their partner wants and lean into it, rather than resisting or playing dumb.
- Pitfall: Players get so focused on their own secret goal that they completely ignore their partner's physical setups. Fix: Side-coach with 'Receive before you give! What is your partner putting in your hands right now?'
- Encourage justification. If a player suddenly finds themselves licking the floor, they must instantly justify why their character would do that in this environment.

## Variations
- Blind Endowment: The audience knows both secret tasks, but neither player knows their own or the other's, relying entirely on audience reactions and partner physical guidance.
- Verbal Taboo: Players are given a specific emotional state or relationship dynamic they must endow their partner with, rather than a physical action.
- Hot and Cold: The audience hums softly when a player gets closer to performing the desired action, helping guide the physical choices.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to balance your own agenda with the need to be completely receptive to your partner's physical cues?
- What specific physical endowments made it easiest for you to understand what your partner wanted you to do?
- How did justifying an absurd physical action help ground the scene and make it feel like a cohesive story?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical tasks are safe, accessible, and respect personal boundaries. Avoid tasks that require intense physical strain, unwanted physical contact, or compromising positions. Players can always modify a physical action to fit their comfort and mobility levels.

## Why It Works
This game works because it reframes the partner's success as your primary objective. By focusing on making your partner perform an action, you must gift them clear, usable physical information. Conversely, to make your partner look like a genius, you must be hyper-aware of their physical offers and enthusiastically accept whatever physical reality they endow you with.

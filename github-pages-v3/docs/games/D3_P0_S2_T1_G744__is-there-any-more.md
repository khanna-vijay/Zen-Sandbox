# Is There Any More?

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Amplify a single physical and vocal offer step-by-step through enthusiastic partner encouragement.

## Overview
A high-energy pairing exercise where one player introduces a simple physical movement and sound, and their partner coaxes them to escalate it step-by-step. It builds comfort with physical expression, vocal variety, and the concept of heightening in a highly supportive environment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Active Gifting; Heightening & Exploration
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; The 'ladder' (escalating beats)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To master the 'ladder' technique of escalation by incrementally increasing the intensity, size, and emotional stakes of a physical and vocal offer, while practicing active support and validation.

## Setup
Players stand in pairs facing each other. No props or special staging required; can be played simultaneously across the room.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have partners stand facing each other with comfortable space to move.
2. Designate Player A as the Performer and Player B as the Supporter for the first round.
3. Player A initiates the cycle by performing a small, simple physical gesture accompanied by a distinct vocal sound.
4. Player B immediately validates the offer with enthusiastic praise, saying, 'That's great!'
5. Player A accepts the validation by saying, 'Thank you.'
6. Player B then prompts the escalation by asking, 'Is there any more?'
7. Player A enthusiastically replies, 'Yes!' and repeats the original gesture and sound, but increases its intensity, size, or emotional volume by one clear step.
8. Repeat steps 4 through 7 for several rounds, allowing the gesture and sound to escalate to an absolute peak of physical and vocal commitment.
9. Swap roles so Player B becomes the Performer and Player A becomes the Supporter, repeating the process.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Take small steps up the ladder! Don't jump from a whisper to a scream instantly; find the intermediate levels.'
- Pitfall: Players jumping straight to maximum intensity on the second turn. Fix: Remind them to leave room to grow, treating the escalation like volume levels 1 through 5.
- Coaching cue: 'Supporters, make your partner feel like a genius. Your praise must be genuine and high-energy to give them the confidence to grow.'
- Pitfall: Performers changing the gesture entirely instead of heightening the original one. Fix: Side-coach: 'Keep the same shape and sound, just make it bigger, faster, or more intense.'

## Variations
- Emotional Escalation: Instead of just physical size, escalate the underlying emotion of the gesture, starting with mild annoyance and escalating to absolute fury.
- Group Circle: Run the game in a single large circle where one person performs, the entire circle supports and prompts them, and then the offer is passed to the next person.
- Gibberish Storytelling: Apply the same escalation structure to a short gibberish phrase, increasing the dramatic stakes of the language with each step.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your partner enthusiastically validate every single step of your escalation?
- What strategies did you use to find 'more' of the action without completely changing what it was?
- How does this incremental escalation help us build sustainable comedic or dramatic games in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of their physical limits and vocal health; encourage scaling the intensity within their own comfortable range of motion and volume.

## Why It Works
By stripping away narrative complexity, this drill isolates the mechanic of heightening. The repetitive, supportive dialogue loop removes the fear of making 'bad' choices, allowing players to experience the power of committing 100% to a single physical and vocal offer.

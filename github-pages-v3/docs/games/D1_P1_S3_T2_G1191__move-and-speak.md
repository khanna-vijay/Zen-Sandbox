# Create to Speak

> Unlock your voice only by physically building the world around you.

## Overview
A two-player scene-work drill where speech is strictly rationed by physical action. Players can only speak when they are actively introducing and interacting with a brand-new physical object in the space, forcing a deep commitment to environment and object work.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Object work; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To break the habit of 'talking heads' scenes by tying verbal communication directly to physical world-building and 100% commitment to object work.

## Setup
A clear performance space with no actual props. Two players stand ready to perform, while the rest of the group observes as an active audience.

## How to Play
1. Two players step into the performance space to begin a standard scene.
2. The facilitator or audience provides a simple location suggestion to inspire the scene.
3. Players may only speak when they are actively creating and interacting with a brand-new physical object in the environment.
4. Once an object is created, it remains fixed in the imaginary space; players must respect its physical location throughout the scene.
5. Interacting with an already established object (e.g., drinking from a cup that was already poured) does not grant the player permission to speak.
6. To speak again, a player must find, define, and interact with a completely new object in the environment (e.g., opening a drawer, picking up a new tool, adjusting a painting).
7. The speech must directly relate to or be influenced by the physical action being performed, ensuring the movement and dialogue are integrated.
8. The scene continues for 3 to 5 minutes, with players balancing silent physical play with moments of object-activated dialogue.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players who start talking without moving: gently call out 'Freeze! What new object are you creating?'
- Watch out for 'ghost objects' where players walk through previously established items. Remind them to respect the physical layout.
- Encourage players to take their time. Silence is a powerful tool in this game; there is no need to rush to create objects just to speak.
- Ensure the objects created make sense within the established environment to maintain narrative cohesion.

## Variations
- Emotional Objects: The physical object created must trigger a specific, intense emotional shift in the character when touched.
- Shared Construction: Players must collaborate to build a single complex object together (like a machine or a tent) before either can speak.

## Debrief
- How did restricting your speech to physical creation change the pacing of your scene?
- What did you notice about your partner's physical commitment when they were forced to show rather than tell?
- How can we bring this level of environmental awareness into scenes where speech is not restricted?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the performance space is clear of actual physical obstacles to prevent tripping while players navigate their imaginary environments. Players with mobility limitations can adapt the physical actions to their comfort level, focusing on fine motor object work or seated environmental interactions.

## Why It Works
By linking the ability to speak to the physical creation of new objects, this game bypasses the analytical brain and forces players into their bodies. It enforces the 'Show, Don't Tell' principle by making physical world-building the literal currency of communication, resulting in rich, grounded environments and highly committed physical choices.

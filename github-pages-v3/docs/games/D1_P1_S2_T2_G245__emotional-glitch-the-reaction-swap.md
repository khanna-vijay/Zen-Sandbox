# Emotional Glitch

![Emotional Glitch — infographic](img/D1_P1_S2_T2_G245__emotional-glitch-the-reaction-swap.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Instantly swap your emotional state mid-scene to build radical versatility and physical commitment.

## Overview
A fast-paced virtual scene-work drill where players must instantly pivot their emotional state upon a facilitator's cue. Moving from a natural initial reaction to a highly contrasting, counter-intuitive emotion, players learn to bypass intellectual processing and fully commit to physical and vocal transformation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Vulnerability; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Self-Recovery; Justification
- **Technique(s):** The Emotional Dial (1→10); Emotional substitution; Projection & breath support; Vocal characterization; Do nothing exercises; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop rapid emotional fluidity and physical commitment by practicing emotional substitution under pressure, training players to instantly inhabit unexpected feelings without verbal rationalization.

## Setup
Designed for a virtual platform. Three to five players participate. Two players start on camera in a split-screen setup, while the remaining players and the facilitator keep their cameras off but audio on. No physical props are needed, but players should adjust their cameras to show their upper bodies to allow for physical expression.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator assigns a simple, neutral scenario to two active players, such as folding laundry or waiting for a bus.
2. The players begin the scene, reacting with their immediate, unfiltered first thoughts and natural emotional impulses.
3. After twenty to thirty seconds of established reality, the facilitator calls out 'GLITCH!' followed by a specific player's name, a highly contrasting emotion, and an intensity level from one to ten.
4. The named player must instantly drop their previous emotion and fully embody the new state using their voice, facial expressions, breath, and posture.
5. The player must not verbally explain or rationalize the sudden change; they must simply exist in the new emotional reality as if it were completely authentic.
6. The scene partner must continue the scene, reacting naturally to this sudden shift in their partner's behavior, maintaining their own current state unless they also receive a 'GLITCH!' command.
7. The facilitator may call 'GLITCH!' multiple times, targeting different players or adjusting the intensity levels to stretch the players' emotional range.
8. After ninety seconds, the facilitator calls 'SCENE' to end the round, rotating new players into the active slots.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't explain it, just feel it!' If players start saying 'I just remembered something happy,' remind them to let the emotion live in their body and voice instead of their dialogue.
- Pitfall: Players often lower their physical energy in virtual settings. Fix: Encourage them to use their hands, lean into the camera, or alter their posture dramatically to convey the new emotion visually.
- Use the one-to-ten scale dynamically. If a player is playing an emotion safely, side-coach them to 'dial it up to an eleven' to break through self-consciousness.
- Ensure safety by establishing that while emotions can be extreme, they should remain playful and grounded in the scene's fictional reality rather than tapping into personal trauma.

## Variations
- Double Glitch: The facilitator calls 'GLITCH!' for both players simultaneously, giving them opposing emotions.
- The Silent Glitch: The player who is glitched must express their new emotion entirely through non-verbal physical choices and vocal sighs/sounds, while their partner continues speaking normally.
- The Dial Sweep: Instead of a sudden jump, the facilitator slowly counts from one to ten, and the player must gradually scale up the intensity of their assigned emotion in real-time.

## Debrief
- How did it feel physically to transition to an emotion that contradicted the logical context of the scene?
- What happened to your internal critic when you were forced to commit one hundred percent to a 'wrong' reaction?
- How did your partner's sudden emotional shift change your own impulses and choices?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game demands rapid emotional shifts, remind players before starting that they have full agency over how they express an emotion. If an assigned emotion feels personally triggering, they can instantly substitute it with a different high-energy emotion of their choice, no questions asked.

## Why It Works
By forcing an immediate emotional swap without allowing time for intellectual justification, the game bypasses the analytical brain. This directly trains emotional substitution, forcing the body and voice to lead the mind. When players commit one hundred percent to the physical expression of an emotion, the psychological feeling naturally follows, proving that emotion is a physical choice.

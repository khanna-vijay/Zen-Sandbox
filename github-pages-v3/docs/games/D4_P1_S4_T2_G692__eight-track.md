# Stereo Loop

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Pass a rhythmic wave of custom sounds and synchronized claps around a collaborative circle.

## Overview
Stereo Loop is an ensemble-building warm-up where players establish unique, synchronized sound-and-gesture pairings with their immediate neighbors. Once every pair has their distinct track set, the group passes a continuous rhythmic pulse around the circle, requiring intense focus, active listening, and split-second timing. The result is a rapid-fire, organic soundscape that demands total group alignment.

## Setup
Players stand in a comfortable circle facing inward. No props or materials are required. Ensure there is enough space for everyone to turn and clap comfortably.

## How to Play
1. Have the group stand in a circle. Explain that each adjacent pair of players will share a unique sound and a simultaneous clap.
2. The facilitator starts as Player A. Turn to your neighbor on your right (Player B), make a distinct, high-energy sound or word (e.g., 'Zing!' or 'Boing!'), and clap your hands together in perfect synchronization with them.
3. Player B then turns to their right neighbor (Player C), creates a completely different sound/word, and claps simultaneously with Player C to lock in their unique connection.
4. Continue this process around the entire circle until every adjacent pair has established their own specific sound and synchronized clap. The final player will connect back to Player A to close the loop.
5. Once all pairs are set, initiate the active loop. Player A turns to Player B, executing their shared sound and clap.
6. As soon as Player B receives the clap/sound from Player A, they must instantly turn to Player C and execute their shared sound and clap with Player C.
7. This creates a continuous wave of sound and movement traveling around the circle. Keep the rhythm steady and rapid, like a playhead reading a track.
8. Once the group masters a single direction, allow players to 'reverse' the flow by immediately sending the sound back to the person they just received it from, requiring the entire circle to stay highly alert.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep the pulse steady! Don't let the gap between pairs stretch out.'
- Common pitfall: Players trying to plan their sounds ahead of time, which stalls the setup. Encourage them to use the very first sound that pops into their head.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Make eye contact before you clap. Lock in with your partner to ensure the clap is perfectly simultaneous.'
- Common pitfall: The rhythm slowing down as players struggle to remember their sound. Remind them that a confident, incorrect sound delivered in rhythm is better than a hesitant, correct one.

## Variations
- Double Pulse: Introduce a second wave traveling in the opposite direction, forcing players to manage two different rhythms and sounds when they cross.
- Blind Loop: Have players close their eyes, relying entirely on auditory cues and the sound of the claps to pass the pulse.
- Emotional Tracks: Instead of nonsense sounds, each pair establishes a specific emotion and facial expression to pass along with the clap.

## Debrief
- What did it feel like when the rhythm clicked and flowed effortlessly around the circle?
- How did you balance focusing on your immediate neighbors while staying aware of the entire circle's momentum?
- What happened when a mistake occurred, and how did the group mind help recover the rhythm?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players who may have hearing or visual impairments are paired with partners who can offer clear, accessible physical or vocal cues. Adjust the clapping gesture to a wave or nod if physical clapping is a barrier for any participant.

## Why It Works
This game works because it strips away intellectual planning and forces players into a state of pure reaction and presence. By anchoring the rhythm in shared, physicalized agreements (the unique claps), players must rely on their peripheral awareness and trust their partners to maintain the collective momentum, embodying the core of group mind.

# The Guest List

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Help your host look brilliant by gifting clues through physical and verbal character choices.

## Overview
One player acts as a party host while the remaining players enter one by one as eccentric guests with secret identities or bizarre quirks. The host must figure out who each guest is based entirely on their behavior, physical choices, and dialogue. The game is a masterclass in active gifting, as guests must offer clues that are challenging yet solvable, ensuring the host succeeds.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner

**Objective:** To practice active gifting and endowment, helping your scene partner succeed by delivering clear, physical, and contextual clues without explicitly stating your secret identity.

## Setup
An in-person performance space with a designated party room area. One player (the Host) leaves the room or plugs their ears. The remaining 2 to 4 players (the Guests) receive unique, secret identities, habits, or quirks suggested by the audience. The Host then returns to the performance space to begin the party.

## How to Play
1. Select one player to be the Host and have them temporarily step out of hearing range of the stage and audience.
2. Gather distinct, active quirks or secret identities from the audience for each of the remaining 2 to 4 Guest players (such as being allergic to gravity, thinking they are a secret agent, or being obsessed with lint).
3. Bring the Host back to the stage to set up the party space, initiating solo object work like cleaning or preparing drinks to establish the environment.
4. The first Guest knocks or enters the party, immediately committing to their physical and vocal quirk, and begins interacting with the Host.
5. The Guest must actively gift the Host with clues by reacting to the environment, treating the Host in a specific way, or using physical space to highlight their quirk.
6. After a minute of interaction, the next Guest enters, bringing their own unique quirk into the mix, forcing the Host to manage multiple distinct dynamics simultaneously.
7. The Host must accept all offers, interact naturally, and voice their realizations out loud to test their guesses.
8. Once the Host correctly identifies a Guest's quirk, that Guest acknowledges the discovery, makes a polite excuse, and exits the stage.
9. The game continues until all Guests have been successfully identified and have left the party, leaving the Host alone to conclude the scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach Guests to show, not tell. Instead of saying they love cats, they should purr, rub against furniture, or try to catch imaginary mice.
- If the Host is struggling, encourage Guests to turn up the volume of their physical and verbal gifts, making their behavior more extreme and obvious.
- Pitfall: Guests sometimes ignore the Host and just play their quirk to the audience. Remind them that their primary job is to make the Host look like a genius by tailoring their gifts directly to them.
- Encourage the Host to voice their observations early and often. Even incorrect guesses help the Guests adjust their clues.

## Variations
- Emotional Quirks: Instead of physical habits or identities, guests enter with extreme, hidden emotional triggers, such as being terrified of the word yes or instantly falling in love when someone touches their elbow.
- The Reverse Party: The Host has a secret quirk or condition, and the guests must figure it out by observing how the Host treats them.
- Status Party: Each guest is assigned a specific numerical status from 1 to 10, and the Host must guess their exact social hierarchy rank through their interactions.

## Debrief
- As a Guest, how did you balance making your quirk obvious enough for the Host to guess without just telling them outright?
- As the Host, what specific physical or verbal gifts from your partners made it easiest for you to understand their identity?
- How does committing 100% to a physical choice make it easier for your partner to read your character's intent?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical quirks are safe to perform in the space and do not rely on offensive stereotypes or mock real-world physical or mental health conditions. Encourage players to choose playful, imaginative, or object-based quirks.

## Why It Works
This game succeeds because it gamifies the core improv principle of making your partner look good. The Guests cannot win on their own; they can only win by successfully transferring information to the Host. This forces players to move away from self-oriented play and focus entirely on how their partner is receiving and processing their physical and verbal offers.

# Trunk Tag

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A high-energy tag game where getting tagged triggers a joyful, vocal release.

## Overview
Players navigate a shared space trying to tag others' lower backs with one arm (their 'trunk') while protecting their own lower back (their 'target' hand). Whenever a player is tagged, they must immediately shout a silly, dramatic, or made-up exclamation, transforming mistakes into moments of shared celebration. It is a fast-paced, laughter-inducing warm-up that breaks the ice and gets players out of their heads.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self

**Objective:** To cultivate a playful relationship with failure (failing joyfully) while sharpening physical agility, spatial awareness, and spontaneous vocal expression.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players stand scattered throughout the space. No props required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to stand in the open space with plenty of room to move safely.
2. Have each player place one hand flat against their own lower back, palm facing outward; this hand is their 'target.'
3. Have players extend their other arm forward to act as their 'trunk' (the tagging instrument).
4. Explain the objective: players must move around the space attempting to gently tap other players' target hands with their trunk, while simultaneously protecting their own target hand.
5. Establish the 'failure trigger': whenever a player's target hand is tapped, they must instantly shout a loud, dramatic exclamation (such as a silly made-up word, a safe pseudo-swear, or a theatrical gasp).
6. Emphasize that getting tagged is not a loss, but a cue to celebrate loudly and immediately return to play.
7. Start the game with a clear signal, encouraging players to keep moving, stay light on their feet, and maintain a playful spirit.
8. Call 'freeze' after 1 to 2 minutes, as the game is highly physical and exhausting.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Celebrate the touch! Let out a giant, ridiculous sound the moment you get tapped!'
- Pitfall: Players might get overly competitive or aggressive. Fix: Remind them that tags must be gentle, flat-handed taps, not slaps or shoves. The focus is on the fun of the reaction, not winning.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes moving! Use your peripheral vision to spot incoming trunks.'
- Pitfall: Players standing against walls to protect their backs. Fix: Ban 'wall-hugging' and encourage constant movement through the center of the space.

## Variations
- Sound Effects Only: Instead of words, players must make a specific cartoon sound effect (like a slide whistle or a spring boing) when tagged.
- The Slow-Motion Trunk: Run the entire game in exaggerated slow motion, which emphasizes physical control, balance, and dramatic vocalizations.
- Trunk Transfer: When tagged, the player must switch which hand is the trunk and which is the target, adding a quick cognitive challenge.

## Debrief
- How did making a loud, silly sound change your reaction to getting tagged?
- How did you balance focusing on your target (defense) while looking for opportunities to tag (offense)?
- What did you notice about the energy in the room once everyone started shouting their 'failures'?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players understand that physical contact must be light and respectful (no grabbing, pushing, or hard slapping). Offer a low-impact variation for players with mobility limitations, such as allowing them to play at a walking pace or designating a stationary 'safe zone' where they can participate using only upper-body movement.

## Why It Works
By pairing a physical 'defeat' (getting tagged) with an immediate, loud, and silly vocalization, the game re-wires the brain's threat response to mistakes. It transforms self-consciousness into playfulness, building the 'fail joyfully' muscle while demanding high physical engagement and peripheral awareness.

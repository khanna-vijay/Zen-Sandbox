# Sing It!

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Turn ordinary dialogue into spontaneous, high-energy musical numbers at the shout of a single command.

## Overview
In this high-energy game, players perform a grounded, character-driven scene. At any moment, the facilitator shouts a command that forces the last speaker to instantly burst into an improvised song based on their last line. Once the song concludes, the players seamlessly transition back into the spoken scene.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Yes, And; Base Reality First; Play for the Back Row
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Vocal Craft; Active Listening; Game Identification; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Vocal characterization; Last Word Response; Finding & Playing the Game; Projection
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops unfiltered spontaneity, vocal commitment, and the ability to leap into the unknown without hesitation by practicing first-thought drills.

## Setup
Three to five players stand in the performance space. A facilitator stands off-stage to call out cues. No props or instruments are required.

## How to Play
1. Begin a standard, spoken scene based on a simple suggestion like an everyday chore or occupation.
2. Focus on establishing a clear base reality with strong physical object work and defined relationships.
3. At any point, the facilitator interrupts by shouting 'Sing it!' immediately after a player finishes speaking a line.
4. The player who spoke that last line must instantly transition into an improvised song or rap, using their last spoken line as the lyrical starting point.
5. The singing player commits fully to the performance, determining the song's rhythm, style, and length.
6. The other players on stage immediately support the singer by providing physical backup, simple choreography, or rhythmic beats without stealing focus.
7. Once the singer brings the song to a natural conclusion, all players instantly return to the spoken scene, accepting the musical outburst as part of their reality.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to start singing instantly without pausing to think of a clever rhyme; the first sound out of their mouth is the right sound.
- If a player freezes, encourage them to start with a simple physical movement or a repetitive vocal chant to find their rhythm.
- Remind non-singing players that their job is to make the singer look like a rockstar through physical support and active listening.
- Ensure the scene returns to a grounded reality after the song rather than becoming entirely absurd; the song should reveal character depth.

## Variations
- Genre Swap: The facilitator specifies a musical genre when calling the cue, such as 'Sing it country style!' or 'Sing it opera style!'
- Duet Challenge: The facilitator calls 'Duet it!' requiring two players to instantly collaborate on a shared song.
- Beatbox Backup: One designated player off-stage or in the scene provides a vocal beatbox rhythm as soon as the song begins.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to jump into a song without planning the lyrics first?
- What strategies did you use to find a rhythm or structure when you felt stuck?
- How did the physical support of your scene partners affect your confidence while singing?

## Safety & Inclusion
Singing can provoke vulnerability and stage fright. Ensure a supportive, high-energy environment where 'bad' singing is celebrated as a joyful failure. Allow players to rap, chant, or speak-sing if they are uncomfortable with traditional singing.

## Why It Works
By forcing an immediate transition from prose to song, the game bypasses the player's internal editor. The high stakes of musical improvisation demand absolute commitment and force players to rely on their very first instinct, turning panic into playful, comedic energy.

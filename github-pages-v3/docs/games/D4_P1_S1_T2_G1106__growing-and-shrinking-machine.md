# Nested Narratives

![Nested Narratives — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G1106__growing-and-shrinking-machine.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Build a stack of distinct scenes, then peel them back in reverse chronological order.

## Overview
A progressive ensemble exercise where players build a sequence of nested scenes, adding one actor at a time to establish entirely new realities. Once the maximum number of players is reached, the process reverses: players exit one by one, requiring the remaining cast to instantly resume the previous scenes, advanced forward in time.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Serve the Story; Group Mind; Serve the Piece
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Narrative Architecture; Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Thematic Synthesis
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light); Weave the threads; Platform/Tilt
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** Develops advanced thread-tracking, group mind, and peripheral awareness by forcing players to hold multiple narrative worlds in memory and seamlessly transition between them.

## Setup
An open performance space. A line of 4 to 6 players stands offstage or at the back of the space, ready to enter. No props or chairs are needed, though physical space should be clear for movement.

## How to Play
1. Player 1 enters the playing area alone and begins a silent or spoken solo scene, establishing a clear environment and activity.
2. Player 2 enters the space. Instantly, the reality shifts: Player 1 and Player 2 are now in a completely new two-person scene with different characters and a new setting.
3. Player 3 enters next, triggering another instant transformation. All three players are now in a new three-person scene, dropping the previous context entirely.
4. This expansion continues until all designated players (typically 4 or 5) have entered, culminating in a high-energy multi-person scene.
5. The contraction phase begins when the last player to enter (e.g., Player 5) makes a justified physical exit from the scene.
6. The moment Player 5 exits, the remaining players instantly snap back into the four-person scene, resuming their previous characters and situation, but advanced forward in time (e.g., 'ten minutes later' or 'the next day').
7. Players continue to exit one by one in reverse order of their arrival. Each exit triggers an immediate return to the previous, smaller scene, always progressed forward in time.
8. The game concludes when Player 2 exits, leaving Player 1 alone to finish their original solo scene, bringing the narrative arc to a satisfying, time-lapsed resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to make clean, physical transformations when a new player enters; the shift in reality should be instantaneous and obvious to the group.
- Pitfall: Players trying to connect the different scenes narratively. Fix: Remind them that each step of the expansion must be a completely unrelated universe.
- Encourage players to make their exits active and justified within the fiction of the current scene, rather than just walking off.
- Remind players that when returning to a previous scene, they must jump forward in time. Avoid repeating the exact moment they left off; instead, show the consequences of what happened next.

## Variations
- Emotional Carryover: When returning to a previous scene, players must carry over the physical or emotional energy of the scene they just left, justifying it within the resumed reality.
- Non-Sequential Exits: Instead of exiting in strict reverse order, any player can exit at any time, requiring the remaining players to instantly identify which scene they are now in based on who is left.
- Word Count Limits: Each nested level has a strict word-per-line limit (e.g., Level 1 is monologues, Level 2 is 2-word lines, Level 3 is 3-word lines) to add a structural constraint.

## Debrief
- What mental strategies did you use to keep track of the different characters and situations while waiting in the wings?
- How did jumping forward in time help move the narrative forward instead of getting stuck in a loop?
- How did physical posture and spatial positioning help you instantly recall your character when a scene resumed?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles, as players will be entering and exiting quickly. Encourage players to use clear verbal or physical cues before exiting to prevent physical collisions.

## Why It Works
This game exercises the ensemble's collective memory and spatial awareness. By forcing players to drop and retrieve narrative threads instantly, it builds 'group mind' and teaches players to trust that their partners will hold the same reality. The time-jump mechanic prevents scenes from stagnating, encouraging active narrative progression.

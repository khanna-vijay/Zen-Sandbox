# Here Comes the Legend

> Instantly embody extreme character endowments announced by your scene partners.

## Overview
A fast-paced character endowment drill where players summon off-stage performers into a scene by giving them highly specific, descriptive, or absurd titles. The summoned player must instantly enter the space fully embodying the physical, vocal, and psychological traits of that title.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; First Thought drills; Character Walks/Centers; Vocal characterization; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** Develops rapid offer reception, physical and vocal commitment, and the ability to enthusiastically accept extreme endowments.

## Setup
A clear stage area with a designated 'off-stage' or 'backstage' zone where players wait. The remaining players start a basic scene in the active space. No props needed.

## How to Play
1. Two players begin a simple, grounded scene in the performance space.
2. At any point, one of the active players makes a verbal announcement introducing a new character who is about to enter, using a highly descriptive title (e.g., 'Ah, here comes Dr. Always-Whispers!').
3. A player waiting off-stage must immediately step into the scene, fully committing to the physical, vocal, and behavioral traits implied by that title.
4. The active players must instantly treat this new character as a genius, validating their traits and integrating them into the scene's reality.
5. The newly entered character must accept the endowment completely, using their first physical and vocal choices to establish the character.
6. After a brief interaction that explores the new character, one of the players can announce another character's arrival, or a player can exit to keep the stage balanced.
7. Continue rotating players in and out, ensuring everyone gets multiple opportunities to both endow others and be endowed.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't think, just move! Let the physical shape of the name take over your body before you speak.'
- Pitfall: Players waiting off-stage hesitate or try to plan their character. Fix: Encourage them to step forward on the very last syllable of the announcement, forcing an unfiltered physical reaction.
- Coaching cue: 'Make your partner a genius. If they are "Mr. Heavy Footsteps", react to the floor shaking before they even arrive.'
- Pitfall: The endowing player gives a name that is too vague or mean-spirited. Fix: Remind players to gift traits that are fun to play, focusing on physical quirks, vocal patterns, or bizarre habits.

## Variations
- Status Swap: The entering character must have the exact opposite status of the person who announced them.
- Emotional Endowment: Instead of physical or behavioral traits, the titles must endow extreme emotional states (e.g., 'Lady Uncontrollable Glee').
- Chain Reaction: Each entering character must immediately announce the next character within three lines of their own entrance, creating a rapid-fire relay.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your character completely defined for you before you even stepped on stage?
- What strategies did you use to instantly translate a verbal title into physical and vocal choices?
- How does treating your partner's endowment as an absolute truth make scene-building easier?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure physical endowments are safe for all bodies; players should adapt physical traits to their own physical comfort and mobility levels. Avoid endowments that rely on offensive stereotypes or mock real-world disabilities.

## Why It Works
By removing the burden of 'what character should I play?' and replacing it with a direct, external command, the player's analytical mind is bypassed. This forces them to rely on unfiltered spontaneity and immediate physical/vocal commitment, demonstrating that any offer can be successfully accepted and played.

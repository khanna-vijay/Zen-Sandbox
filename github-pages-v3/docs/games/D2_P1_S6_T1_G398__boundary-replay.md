# Boundary Swap

![Boundary Swap — infographic](img/D2_P1_S6_T1_G398__boundary-replay.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Step into your partner's shoes to navigate boundaries, practice check-ins, and discover authentic responses.

## Overview
Two players begin an scene until a moment of tension, physical contact, or boundary negotiation arises. The facilitator freezes the action, and the players physically swap roles to replay the exact moment from their partner's perspective. This exercise builds deep empathy and practical boundary-navigation skills by letting players experience both sides of an interaction.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Consent & Boundaries; Yes, And; Truth Over Pandering; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Boundary Navigation; Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring
- **Technique(s):** Check-ins; Cut calls; Negotiating physical contact; Emotional-echo drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop active listening, single-partner empathy, and practical boundary navigation. Players learn to execute real-time check-ins, negotiate physical contact, and prioritize authentic character truth over polite scene agreement.

## Setup
A clear, open performance space with no props. The facilitator stands close to the stage to observe closely. The remaining participants sit as active observers.

## How to Play
1. Initiate the Scene: Two players step into the space and begin a scene based on a simple, low-stakes relationship prompt.
2. Establish the Dynamic: Players build the scene naturally, allowing a moment to emerge where a boundary is introduced, a power dynamic shifts, or physical contact is proposed.
3. The Freeze: Once a key moment of boundary negotiation or physical offer occurs, the facilitator calls 'Freeze!' and pauses the action.
4. The Physical Swap: The players physically swap places on stage, stepping into each other's exact physical posture and taking over the other's character.
5. Rewind and Replay: The facilitator instructs the players to rewind the scene to the moment just before the freeze.
6. Embody the New Perspective: The players replay the moment, but this time, the player now receiving the offer must respond with absolute honesty based on their new character's felt truth, rather than trying to 'save' the scene.
7. Integrate Consent Tools: During the replay, players are explicitly encouraged to use active check-ins (e.g., 'Is it okay if I step closer?') or call a brief out-of-character 'Pause' to negotiate physical boundaries.
8. Conclude and Reset: The replay runs for another minute to explore the new dynamic, then the facilitator calls 'Scene' to transition to the debrief.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep the Stakes Low Initially: Start with subtle boundary moments (e.g., personal space, verbal pressure) rather than high-intensity conflict or heavy physical contact to keep the learning environment safe and nuanced.
- Normalize the 'Pause' or 'Cut': Explicitly frame calling a 'Pause' or 'Cut' during the replay as a successful application of boundary navigation, not a failure of the scene.
- Focus on Empathy, Not Correction: Ensure players understand the swap is not about 'fixing' what the first player did wrong, but about experiencing how that offer felt from the receiving end.
- Side-Coach 'Truth Over Pandering': If a player in the replay immediately capitulates to make the scene 'easy,' side-coach them with: 'What does your character actually feel right now? Honor that truth.'

## Variations
- The Non-Verbal Swap: Run the same exercise but focus entirely on non-verbal cues, physical proximity, and eye contact, swapping roles to feel the weight of silent physical pressure.
- The Multi-Player Swap: In a three-person scene, swap all three players in a circle to experience how a third-party observer's presence affects the boundary dynamic.

## Debrief
- How did your perception of the boundary shift when you physically stepped into your partner's role?
- What did it feel like to have explicit permission to check in or negotiate during the replay?
- How did prioritizing 'Truth Over Pandering' change the narrative direction of the scene?
- For the initiator, what did you learn about the impact of your original offer once you became the receiver?

## Safety & Inclusion
Establish a clear, universal 'Cut' or 'Time Out' signal before starting. Because this game deals directly with personal boundaries and physical comfort, players must know they can stop the exercise at any moment for any reason without explanation. Remind players that their real-world comfort always overrides the theatrical needs of the scene.

## Why It Works
By physically swapping roles, players bypass intellectual analysis and experience the somatic reality of an offer. This embodied perspective-shifting builds deep empathy, while the structured replay provides a low-stakes laboratory to practice verbal check-ins and boundary setting, transforming abstract safety concepts into muscle memory.

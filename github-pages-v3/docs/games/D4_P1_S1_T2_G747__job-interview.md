# Three-Headed Job Interview

![Three-Headed Job Interview — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G747__job-interview.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Speak as one collective mind to land the job of your dreams, one word at a time.

## Overview
In this high-focus comedy game, three players stand shoulder-to-shoulder to portray a single, multi-headed job applicant. An interviewer asks them standard or bizarre interview questions, which the applicant must answer speaking only one word at a time in rotation. The result is a hilarious exercise in surrender, active listening, and spontaneous grammatical cooperation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Yes, And; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Active Listening; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Word-association; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop deep peripheral awareness, group mind, and thread-tracking skills by forcing players to surrender individual narrative control and build a single cohesive voice.

## Setup
One player is designated as the Interviewer, sitting or standing opposite a panel of three players (the 'Candidate') who stand shoulder-to-shoulder. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign roles: one player acts as the Interviewer, and three players stand close together to form the three-headed Candidate.
2. Ask the group or facilitator for a suggestion of a job title (e.g., 'dinosaur paleontologist', 'professional sleeper', or 'chief executive officer').
3. The Interviewer begins the scene by welcoming the Candidate and asking an opening interview question related to the job suggestion.
4. The Candidate must answer the question by speaking exactly one word at a time, rotating sequentially from left to right.
5. The Candidate must strive to form grammatically correct, logical, and entertaining sentences, matching each other's vocal tone and physical posture.
6. The Interviewer listens actively, reacts to the bizarre nature of the three-headed candidate, and asks follow-up questions based on the Candidate's previous answers.
7. The scene continues for 3 to 5 minutes, concluding when the Interviewer either hires the Candidate, rejects them, or the interview reaches a natural comedic climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Listen to the sentence, not just your next word!' Remind players to track the grammatical thread rather than planning their own funny word.
- Pitfall: Players speaking too fast or overlapping. Fix: Encourage a steady, rhythmic pace. A slight pause between words is better than chaotic overlapping.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Share the body!' Encourage the three heads to move, gesture, and react physically as a single organism to enhance the comedic effect.
- Pitfall: The Interviewer asking simple yes/no questions. Fix: Coach the Interviewer to ask open-ended questions that require elaboration (e.g., 'Tell me about a time you failed').

## Variations
- Emotional Shift: The interviewer can trigger emotional shifts in the candidate (e.g., 'Why are you suddenly so sad about this experience?').
- Physical Sync: The three heads must share a single pair of hands (the outer two players use one hand each) to gesture while speaking.
- The Panel Interview: Two interviewers and a four-headed candidate to increase the complexity of thread-tracking.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to surrender your individual idea of where the sentence was going?
- What strategies did you use to keep the sentence grammatically coherent?
- How did physical alignment (standing close, matching posture) affect your mental alignment?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players standing shoulder-to-shoulder are comfortable with close physical proximity; players can stand slightly apart or sit in chairs if needed for physical comfort or accessibility.

## Why It Works
It forces players to abandon premeditation. Because you only control every third word, you cannot plan a punchline. You must practice absolute 'yes-and' by accepting the exact word before yours and setting up the next player with a word that keeps the grammatical thread alive.

# The Advice Ladder

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Climb the ladder of comedic heightening by offering increasingly terrible solutions to simple problems.

## Overview
Three players stand before the audience to solve a mundane life dilemma. Each player must offer a different tier of advice, systematically escalating the helpfulness of the first response into absolute, chaotic absurdity by the third.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Heightening & Exploration; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** The 'ladder' (escalating beats); Make the choice readable
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To master the 'ladder' technique of comedic heightening, learning how to listen to a premise and escalate its stakes in clear, sequential steps.

## Setup
Three active players stand in a line facing the audience. The remaining players form a queue offstage or sit in the front row, ready to rotate in. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Ask the audience to suggest a simple, everyday problem that someone might need advice on (e.g., 'I keep losing my keys' or 'I don't know what to wear to a job interview').
2. The first player in line steps forward and delivers 'Good Advice'—a sensible, realistic, and genuinely helpful solution to the problem.
3. The second player steps forward and delivers 'Bad Advice'—a solution that is clearly unhelpful, mischievous, or counterproductive, but still directly related to the original problem.
4. The third player steps forward and delivers 'Outrageous Advice'—taking the premise of the bad advice and escalating it to a catastrophic, absurd, or surreal extreme.
5. The facilitator evaluates the escalation: if the third player's advice successfully heightened the second player's premise, the line is celebrated; if the escalation missed the mark, that player is playfully rotated out.
6. Rotate the line-up so that players take on different roles (Good, Bad, or Outrageous) for the next audience suggestion.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach active listening: 'Listen to the previous step! You cannot write your joke in advance; your bad advice must build on the good, and your outrageous advice must explode the bad.'
- Watch out for players jumping straight to absurdity. If the second player gives apocalyptic advice, the third player has nowhere to go. Remind them to keep the second rung of the ladder grounded.
- Encourage high stage presence and confidence: 'Deliver your terrible advice with absolute conviction, as if it is the most logical and professional solution in the world.'
- If a player struggles to heighten, pause the action and ask the group: 'What is the logical next step of absurdity for this specific bad idea?' to make it a collaborative learning moment.

## Variations
- Blind Roles: The players do not know which tier of advice they are giving until the facilitator points to them mid-round, forcing them to adapt instantly.
- The Infinite Ladder: Add a fourth or fifth player to the line, challenging the group to see how many distinct levels of escalation they can climb before the premise collapses.
- Tag-Team Advice: Play in pairs, where each pair must co-present their tier of advice, building physical and verbal agreement before passing it to the next pair.

## Debrief
- How did listening closely to the 'Good' advice help the 'Bad' advice land more effectively?
- What makes an escalation feel earned and funny rather than random and confusing?
- How does maintaining a serious, confident delivery affect the comedy of absurd advice?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure audience suggestions remain lighthearted and avoid heavy personal trauma or sensitive topics. Players should avoid advice that promotes genuine self-harm, keeping the 'outrageous' advice firmly in the realm of cartoonish, physical absurdity.

## Why It Works
This game works because it provides a clear, structured framework for heightening. By constraining the players to specific rungs of a ladder (sensible to flawed to chaotic), it removes the pressure to be instantly funny and instead focuses their energy on the mechanical relationship between setup and escalation.

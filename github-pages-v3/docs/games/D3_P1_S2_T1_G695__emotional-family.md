# The Emotional Household

> Escalate a single shared emotion to its absolute peak within a domestic family scene.

## Overview
A group of players portrays a family gathering where every member shares the exact same underlying emotion. As the scene progresses, players use physical and verbal choices to escalate this shared feeling, climbing an emotional ladder together. The comedy and drama emerge from how mundane domestic tasks are filtered through this extreme, unified emotional lens.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Show, Don't Tell; Commit 100%; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Heightening & Exploration; Emotional Fluidity; Support Work; Game Identification
- **Technique(s):** The 'ladder' (escalating beats); The Emotional Dial (1→10); Walk-ons; If this is true, what else is true?
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop emotional heightening, physical commitment, and group mind by practicing the 'ladder' technique—escalating a shared emotional state in progressive, unified beats without relying on verbal explanation.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with a designated 'stage' area representing a domestic setting (e.g., a living room or dining table). 4 to 6 players participate. Two players start on stage in a neutral domestic activity; the remaining players wait off-stage, ready to enter. No physical props are required.

## How to Play
1. Ask the group for a suggestion of a specific, distinct emotion and a mundane family activity.
2. Two players begin the scene on stage, engaging in the suggested activity while fully embodying the chosen emotion through their posture, facial expressions, and tone of voice.
3. The players must show, not tell, their emotional state, using the physical environment and domestic tasks to express their feelings rather than naming the emotion directly.
4. As the scene establishes, a third player enters from off-stage, immediately adopting the same emotion but at a slightly higher intensity level, stepping up the emotional ladder.
5. The players on stage must match this new, higher level of intensity, supporting each other's choices and raising the collective stakes of the scene.
6. Subsequent players enter one by one, each time stepping onto a higher rung of the emotional ladder, forcing the entire household to escalate the shared feeling to its absolute peak.
7. Players can exit and re-enter the space, but they must always return at the current, heightened emotional level of the room.
8. The scene concludes when the emotion reaches an unsustainable, hilarious, or dramatic peak where the physical and vocal expression cannot go any higher.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to focus on physicalization: 'Show us the anger in how you butter that toast!' This prevents them from just talking about their feelings.
- Watch out for 'emotional plateaus' where players stay at the same level of intensity. Prompt them with: 'Take it up one rung on the ladder!'
- Ensure players are listening and reacting to each other. If one player escalates, the others must immediately match that new baseline rather than fighting for individual focus.
- Pitfall: Naming the emotion directly. Fix: Encourage players to express the emotion through their relationship to the objects and tasks around them instead.

## Variations
- The Emotional Interloper: Introduce a late-arriving character (like a neighbor or delivery person) who enters with a completely contrasting emotion, forcing the family to defend their shared state.
- Emotional Shift: Start the family with one emotion, and have a specific domestic event (like dropping a plate) instantly shift the entire family to a completely different, shared emotion.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to step onto a higher rung of the emotional ladder when entering the scene?
- What physical choices helped you communicate the emotion without explicitly naming it?
- How did sharing the exact same emotion affect your sense of 'group mind' and support on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries as emotions escalate. High-energy emotions can lead to frantic movement; remind players to maintain spatial awareness and keep physical contact consensual and controlled.

## Why It Works
By forcing all players to share the same emotional state, the game eliminates conflict based on disagreement and instead focuses on collaborative escalation. Using the 'ladder' technique teaches players how to build a scene's intensity incrementally, ensuring that every entrance serves to heighten the established game rather than resetting it.

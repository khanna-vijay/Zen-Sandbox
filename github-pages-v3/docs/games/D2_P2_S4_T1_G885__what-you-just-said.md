# What You Just Said

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build deep emotional connection and radical validation by justifying every single word your partner speaks.

## Overview
A paired exercise where players stand face-to-face and begin every single line with a specific, validating prompt. By forcing players to immediately justify why their partner's last statement is crucial, it strips away planning and fosters deep emotional connection and active listening.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; The First Thought Is a Gift
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Yes, And… sentence games; Last Word Response; Emotional-echo drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop profound offer reception and active listening. Players practice unconditional acceptance ('Yes, And') by treating their partner's words as the most important thing in the room, bypassing intellectual planning to access genuine emotional responses.

## Setup
Divide the group into pairs. Partners stand facing each other in a comfortable space with enough room to focus on one another without excessive distraction from other pairs. No props or stage setup required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct players to stand facing their partner, maintaining comfortable eye contact.
2. Player A begins the exercise by making a simple, mundane statement about themselves, their day, or their immediate environment (e.g., 'I had oatmeal for breakfast').
3. Player B must immediately respond by starting their sentence with the exact phrase: 'What you just said is very important to me because...' and then completing the sentence honestly.
4. Player B's completion must directly address and emotionally justify the specific detail Player A just shared (e.g., '...because it means you are finally taking care of your health').
5. Player A then responds using the exact same opening phrase: 'What you just said is very important to me because...' and completes it based entirely on Player B's last statement.
6. The dialogue continues back and forth, with both players strictly adhering to the opening phrase for every single turn.
7. Encourage players to focus on the emotional relationship and the immediate impact of the words, rather than trying to build a complex narrative or plot.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Listen to the end of the sentence before you start formulating your response. Let the words land first.'
- Common Pitfall: Players using the prompt as a mere formality and then changing the subject. Fix: Remind them that the 'because' must directly unpack and elevate the partner's specific words, not pivot to their own agenda.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Allow yourself to feel the importance. If they say they bought shoes, why does that genuinely matter to your relationship with them?'
- Common Pitfall: Intellectualizing or trying to be funny. Fix: Encourage players to lean into sincerity and simple, grounded emotional truths rather than clever jokes.

## Variations
- Physical Mirroring: Have players subtly mirror each other's physical posture and facial expressions as they speak to deepen the empathetic connection.
- Varying the Prompt: Change the opening phrase to 'What you just said makes me feel... because...' to focus more directly on emotional vulnerability.
- The Silent Beat: Require a full three-second pause of eye contact after a player finishes speaking before the partner is allowed to say the opening phrase.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have every single thing you said treated as deeply important?
- What happened to your urge to plan your next line when you were locked into this specific structure?
- How did the emotional stakes of the relationship shift as the conversation progressed?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game fosters high emotional intimacy and eye contact, remind players they can set boundaries on the personal depth of their statements. If eye contact feels overstimulating or inaccessible, players may focus on their partner's forehead, hands, or general presence instead.

## Why It Works
By mandating a repetitive, highly validating verbal constraint, the game bypasses the analytical brain's defense mechanisms. It forces players to practice radical 'Yes, And' by explicitly stating why an offer is valuable, which instantly builds high-stakes relationships and trains players to receive offers with empathy rather than judgment.

# Shared Memory Swap

![Shared Memory Swap — infographic](img/D2_P2_S1_T0_G655__change.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Seamlessly trade ownership of a single personal story at the clap of a hand.

## Overview
Two players collaborate to tell a single, cohesive first-person story about a past event. As a facilitator cues them to swap, players instantly trade roles, taking over the narrative as if it were their own memory. The result is a fast-paced exercise in deep listening and radical acceptance of narrative offers.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; The First Thought Is a Gift; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Narrative Architecture
- **Technique(s):** Yes, And… sentence games; First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop active listening and immediate 'yes-and' acceptance by forcing players to seamlessly adopt and build upon a partner's spontaneous narrative details.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other. No props or special staging are required. The facilitator stands nearby to act as the cue-giver.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and designate Player A and Player B.
2. Ask Player A to begin telling a true or realistic personal story (e.g., 'My worst vacation' or 'A memorable birthday') in the first person.
3. Player B must listen with absolute focus, tracking every detail, character, and emotional beat of Player A's story.
4. After 20 to 30 seconds, the facilitator claps their hands and calls out 'Change!'
5. Upon hearing the cue, Player B must immediately pick up the story mid-sentence, continuing the narrative in the first person ('I') as if it happened to them.
6. Player B must not correct or rewrite what Player A said; they must treat Player A's details as absolute truth and build directly upon them.
7. The facilitator continues to call 'Change!' at unpredictable intervals, gradually shortening the time between swaps to increase the challenge.
8. The game concludes after 2 to 3 minutes of rapid swapping, ideally ending on a satisfying narrative resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid pausing or saying 'uh' when the swap occurs; encourage them to speak their very first thought.
- Pitfall: Players trying to correct the story to match their actual life. Fix: Remind them that once the story starts, it belongs to the duo, not the individual.
- Side-coach: 'Pick up mid-word if you can!' to emphasize seamless vocal handoffs.
- Vary the rhythm of the 'Change!' cue—sometimes let a player speak for a while, other times swap them after just three words.

## Variations
- Trio Swap: Run the game in groups of three, where the cue-giver points to the next storyteller, requiring even broader focus.
- Emotional Shift: When the swap occurs, the new speaker must also shift the emotional tone of the memory (e.g., from joyful to paranoid).
- Physicalized Memory: Players must use physical gestures to represent the memory, and the partner must mirror and continue those physical actions upon swapping.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your 'personal' story altered by your partner?
- What strategies did you use to listen actively enough to take over mid-sentence?
- How does this game illustrate the core principle of 'Yes, And' in a narrative context?

## Safety & Inclusion
Remind players to choose lighthearted or low-stakes memories (e.g., a funny trip, a weird meal) rather than deeply traumatic or highly sensitive personal histories to keep the space safe and comfortable for everyone.

## Why It Works
By forcing an instant handoff, players cannot pre-plan their next line; they must exist entirely in the present moment. It strips away the urge to control the narrative, teaching players that the story is a shared entity that thrives when both partners treat every contribution as absolute truth.

# Slam Poetry Showdown

![Slam Poetry Showdown — infographic](img/D1_P1_S1_T2_G805__poetry-slam.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Step up to the microphone and unleash passionate, unedited verses with absolute, unapologetic commitment.

## Overview
Two teams face off in a high-energy, competitive poetry slam where players must instantly improvise passionate poems based on audience suggestions. The atmosphere mimics a real slam poetry event, filled with snapping, cheering, and intense, dramatic delivery. It pushes players to bypass their internal editors and fully commit to their first vocal and physical impulses.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; The First Thought Is a Gift; Play for the Back Row
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Vocal Craft; Support Work; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Vocal characterization; Projection
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity, vocal variety, and physical commitment by forcing players to speak immediately without planning, trusting their first thoughts, and projecting their presence to the back of the room.

## Setup
Divide the group into two equal teams. Position the teams on opposite sides of the stage (Left and Right). The facilitator acts as the host/MC. The remaining players or a designated group act as the active, highly supportive audience. No props are needed, though a physical space representing a center-stage microphone is established.

## How to Play
1. Divide the players into two teams and have them stand on opposite sides of the stage, creating a clear performance space in the center.
2. Ask the audience to provide a team name for each side to establish a playful, competitive atmosphere.
3. The facilitator, acting as the MC, solicits a single-word suggestion, theme, or title from the audience for the first round.
4. One representative from each team steps forward to the center-stage microphone to face off.
5. The first poet immediately begins delivering an improvised poem based on the suggestion, focusing on high emotional commitment, vocal variety, and physical presence.
6. The opposing poet then delivers their own improvised poem on the same suggestion, aiming to contrast the style, tone, or rhythm of the first poet.
7. Team members on the sidelines must actively support their performing teammate with physical encouragement, rhythmic snapping, and vocalized agreement.
8. After both poets have performed, the audience votes on the winner of the round through applause, cheers, or finger-snapping.
9. Repeat the process with new suggestions, ensuring every player steps up to perform at least once.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: Don't think, just speak! Let the first word out and find the rhythm as you go.
- Side-coaching cue: Play for the back row! Use your entire body and project your voice to fill the space.
- Pitfall: Players trying to make their poems perfectly rhyme, which causes them to freeze. Fix: Remind them that free verse, spoken word, and abstract rhythmic prose are highly encouraged; rhyming is completely optional.
- Pitfall: Teammates standing passively on the sidelines. Fix: Encourage active physical and vocal support (snapping, nodding, echoing key words) to build a safe, high-energy environment.
- Side-coaching cue: Go one verse further than you think you can. Push past the comfortable ending!

## Variations
- Tag-Team Slam: Poets can tag their teammates in mid-poem to continue the verse seamlessly, maintaining the established rhythm and emotional tone.
- Style Challenge: The MC calls out a specific poetic style or genre mid-performance (e.g., Shakespearean, Hip-Hop, Gothic Melodrama) that the poet must instantly adopt.
- Musical Backing: A teammate provides a live, improvised beatbox or rhythmic physical percussion (clapping, chest-patting) to back the performing poet.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to step forward without any plan for what you were going to say?
- What strategies helped you bypass your internal editor and keep the momentum going?
- How did the active support (snapping, cheering) from your teammates affect your confidence and commitment on stage?
- How does committing 100% to a weak or absurd choice make it successful in the eyes of the audience?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the competitive element remains lighthearted and collaborative. Encourage players to use non-verbal physical gestures or vocalizations that match their comfort and physical accessibility levels. If a player is uncomfortable speaking solo, allow them to perform as a duo with a teammate for support.

## Why It Works
By combining the high-pressure environment of a slam with the immediate demand for verse, the game bypasses the analytical brain. The rhythmic and emotional nature of poetry encourages vocal variety and physical expression, while the mandatory sideline support creates a psychological safety net that rewards bold, unfiltered choices.

# Satellite Echo

> Speak your partner's words on a one-beat delay to build hyper-focused listening and rhythm.

## Overview
A high-concentration pairing exercise where one player tells a spontaneous story one word at a time on a steady physical beat, while their partner echoes those exact words precisely one beat behind. It creates a hypnotic, rhythmic challenge that demands deep auditory focus and absolute trust.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise; Timing exercises
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening, rhythmic synchronization, and the ability to split focus between speaking and receiving information simultaneously.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other in a shared space. No props or special staging required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and have partners stand facing each other.
2. Establish a slow, steady physical beat, such as a gentle thigh-slap or finger-snap, that both partners maintain together.
3. Designate Player A as the Broadcaster and Player B as the Satellite.
4. Player A begins telling an improvised, coherent story, speaking exactly one word on every beat.
5. Player B repeats Player A's words exactly, but delayed by exactly one beat (Player A speaks word one on beat one; Player B repeats word one on beat two while Player A speaks word two).
6. Both players must maintain the steady physical beat throughout, keeping the rhythm consistent even when mistakes happen.
7. After two minutes of play, signal the pairs to switch roles so Player B becomes the Broadcaster and Player A becomes the Satellite.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Keep the physical beat steady. Do not slow down the rhythm to make it easier; trust the pattern to carry you.
- Coaching cue: Broadcasters, keep your sentences simple and grammatically logical to help your partner succeed.
- Pitfall: Players often stop the physical beat when they get confused. Fix: Emphasize that the physical beat is the anchor; if a word is missed, leave a blank space and jump back in on the next beat.
- Coaching cue: Satellites, do not try to guess the next word. Just receive the sound and echo it immediately.

## Variations
- Two-Beat Delay: Increase the difficulty by having the Satellite echo the words with a strict two-beat delay.
- Gibberish Echo: The Broadcaster speaks in nonsense syllables, and the Satellite echoes the exact sounds on a delay, focusing purely on phonetics and tone.
- Physical Mirroring: Add a low-intensity physical movement on each beat that the partner must also mirror with a delay.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have to speak one word while simultaneously listening to the next word?
- What strategies did you use to make your partner look like a genius?
- How did maintaining a physical beat help or hinder your verbal focus?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure partners establish a comfortable distance and eye-contact level. If direct eye contact is too intense or distracting, players may focus on their partner's forehead or hands.

## Why It Works
It forces the brain to bypass analytical overthinking. By occupying the conscious mind with a rhythmic physical task and a delayed vocal echo, players must rely on pure, unfiltered auditory reception and immediate vocalization, embodying the principle of making their partner look good by maintaining a steady, predictable pace.

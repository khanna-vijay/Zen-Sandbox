# Gaze and Scream

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Lock eyes, scream in mock terror, and drop out in this high-energy elimination warm-up.

## Overview
A fast-paced, high-energy circle game where players look down, then look up to lock eyes with a peer. If two players make direct eye contact, they must instantly scream in dramatic surprise and step out of the circle. The game continues in rapid rounds until only one or two players remain standing.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To build intense focus, rapid reaction times, and a shared group rhythm while practicing unfiltered spontaneity and embracing sudden failure with joy.

## Setup
Players stand in a shoulder-to-shoulder circle in an open space. No props or materials are required. Ensure there is enough room for players to step back safely when eliminated.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to stand in a closed circle and look down at the floor in front of them.
2. Explain that on the facilitator's count of 'One, Two, Three, Look!', everyone must lift their head and look directly at the eyes of another player in the circle.
3. Emphasize that players must choose a target immediately and cannot change where they are looking once their head is raised.
4. If a player looks at someone who is looking elsewhere, nothing happens; both players remain in the game.
5. If two players make direct, mutual eye contact, they must immediately let out a loud, dramatic scream of terror and step backward out of the circle.
6. The remaining players close the circle to fill in the gaps, look back down at the floor, and prepare for the next round.
7. Repeat the cycle rapidly, with the facilitator calling the cue each time, until only one or two players are left.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep the tempo brisk; the transition between looking down and looking up should be snappy to maintain high energy.
- Side-coach players to commit fully to the scream. A half-hearted gasp slows down the momentum; encourage a full, joyful release of energy.
- Pitfall: Players changing their gaze mid-round to avoid eye contact. Fix: Remind them that 'dying' is the fun part, and they must lock onto their first choice instantly.
- Pitfall: Hesitation or debate over whether eye contact was actually made. Fix: If you think you made eye contact, you did. When in doubt, scream and step out.

## Variations
- Silent Startle: Instead of screaming, players who make eye contact must strike a silent, exaggerated pose of absolute horror and freeze in place as statues.
- The Compliment: Instead of screaming, players who lock eyes must instantly shout a genuine, rapid-fire compliment at each other before stepping out.
- Continuous Play: Instead of elimination, players who make eye contact scream, spin around 360 degrees, and immediately rejoin the active circle.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to make eye contact and immediately 'fail' by having to scream and step out?
- How does this game encourage us to commit 100% without overthinking our choices?
- What did you notice about the energy of the room as the circle got smaller?

## Safety & Inclusion
For players with vocal strain or hearing sensitivities, offer the option to make a dramatic physical gesture (like throwing hands in the air) instead of a loud scream. Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards behind the players so they can step back safely when eliminated.

## Why It Works
This game works by turning the fear of failure into a highly celebrated, loud, and joyful event. It demands absolute presence and eye contact, forcing players out of their heads and into a state of pure, unfiltered reaction. The shared rhythm of looking down and looking up builds a strong sense of group mind and collective timing.

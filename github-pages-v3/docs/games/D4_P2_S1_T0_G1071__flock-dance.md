# Flock Movement

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Move as a single, organic unit by shifting leadership based on the direction you face.

## Overview
In this physical, non-verbal warm-up, the entire group forms a tight, diamond-like grid. As the group rotates, the person at the front of the movement direction automatically becomes the leader, requiring players to constantly shift their focus and follow the follower.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Character Walks/Centers; Mirror exercise
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops peripheral awareness, non-verbal connection, and the ability to seamlessly pass and receive leadership without verbal cues.

## Setup
A large, open space free of obstacles. Players stand close together in a tight, diamond or wedge formation, all facing the same direction initially.

## How to Play
1. Have the group stand in a tight, organized wedge or diamond formation, all facing the same direction.
2. Designate the player at the very front of the wedge as the initial leader.
3. The leader begins to move their body slowly and expressively, using clear, repeatable physical gestures while remaining in place.
4. All other players mirror the leader's movements simultaneously, using their peripheral vision to maintain the shape and timing of the group.
5. To shift leadership, the current leader slowly rotates their body to face a new direction.
6. As the entire group rotates to face this new direction, the player who is now at the front of the formation automatically and silently becomes the new leader.
7. The new leader immediately takes over, initiating new movements, while the previous leader now follows from their new position in the grid.
8. Continue rotating and shifting leadership dynamically, aiming for a seamless, organic flow where no single person dominates for too long.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep movements slow and deliberate at first to allow the group to stay synchronized.
- Avoid looking directly at the leader; instead, soften your gaze and rely on your peripheral vision to sense the movement of the whole group.
- If the group gets out of sync, pause, re-establish the grid, and resume with slower, simpler movements.
- Encourage leaders to make clear, decisive turns so the rest of the group knows exactly when the leadership has shifted.

## Variations
- Soundscapes: Add abstract vocalizations or sounds that match the physical movements, which must also be mirrored by the flock.
- Tempo Shift: Introduce sudden changes in speed, from slow-motion to high-energy, testing the group's reaction time.
- Locomotion: Allow the entire flock to travel through the space while maintaining their relative grid positions and shifting leadership.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to transition from being a leader to a follower instantly?
- What did you have to do with your vision and awareness to keep the group synchronized?
- How does this exercise relate to supporting your scene partners on stage without speaking?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical boundaries and personal space, keeping a safe distance to avoid accidental collisions during rotations. Offer low-impact movement options for players with physical or mobility limitations.

## Why It Works
By removing verbal communication and relying entirely on physical cues, players must tap into Group Mind. The constant shifting of the leader based on physical orientation perfectly embodies Follow the Follower, training players to yield control and support whoever is currently leading.

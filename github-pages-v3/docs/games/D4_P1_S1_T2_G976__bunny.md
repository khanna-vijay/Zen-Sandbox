# Bunny Bunny

> A high-energy, fast-paced circle game that demands intense peripheral awareness and collective rhythm.

## Overview
Players stand in a circle and pass a rapid-fire, three-person physical puppet around the group. One central player acts as the body while their immediate neighbors instantly form the ears, requiring everyone to stay highly attuned to their physical surroundings. The result is a chaotic, joyful burst of group energy that forces players out of their heads and into the present moment.

## Setup
Have all players stand in a clear, open circle with enough space to move their arms freely. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Gather the group into a standing circle, ensuring everyone can see each other clearly.
2. Demonstrate the 'bunny body' pose: hold your elbows tight to your ribs, forearms forward, and hands limp like paws, while chanting 'bunny bunny bunny bunny' rhythmically.
3. Demonstrate the 'bunny ears' pose: the players directly to the left and right of the 'body' must immediately raise their outer hands to the 'body's' head, mimicking floppy ears, and join in the chant.
4. Begin the game with one player initiating the 'bunny body' pose and chant, prompting their immediate neighbors to instantly provide the 'ears' and join the chant.
5. To pass the focus, the 'body' player makes clear eye contact with someone else in the circle, pushes their paws forward, and 'throws' the bunny to them.
6. The recipient immediately assumes the 'body' pose and starts chanting, while their new left and right neighbors must instantly drop whatever they were doing to become the new 'ears'.
7. The previous bunny trio immediately drops their poses and returns to an active, neutral stance, ready to react if the focus shifts near them again.
8. Keep the tempo fast, encouraging the group to maintain a continuous, unbroken rhythm of chanting and passing.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep your eyes wide! Don't just watch the bunny; watch your relationship to the bunny!'
- Common Pitfall: Neighbors freezing or forgetting to make ears. Fix: Remind players that their job is to make their partner look good; if they see their neighbor become the body, they must instantly support them.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Pass with clear intention. Use your eyes and body language to send the energy cleanly across the circle.'
- Common Pitfall: The chant dying out or losing rhythm. Fix: Encourage the entire circle to quietly pulse or bounce to the rhythm, keeping the collective energy high even when they aren't directly involved.

## Variations
- Slow-Motion Bunny: Play the entire game in extreme slow motion, stretching out the physical movements and vocalizing the chant in deep, drawn-out tones to emphasize precision and physical control.
- The Zoo: Introduce different animals with unique sounds and physical shapes (e.g., an elephant with a trunk and big ears, or a cow with horns and udders) to increase the cognitive load.
- Silent Bunny: Play the game in complete silence, relying entirely on physical synchronization, eye contact, and breath to maintain the rhythm.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to transition instantly from a passive observer to an active supporter (the ears)?
- What did you have to pay attention to in order to keep the rhythm from dropping?
- How does this game demonstrate the concept of 'making your partner look good'?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of personal space when gesturing near their neighbors' heads. If physical proximity or rapid arm movements cause discomfort, players can agree to make 'ears' further away from the center player's head, or use a non-contact visual cue.

## Why It Works
This game works because it splits the responsibility of a single character across three distinct people, forcing them to operate as a single organism. Because the focus shifts rapidly and unpredictably, players cannot plan ahead; they must maintain constant peripheral awareness and rely on the collective rhythm of the group mind to succeed.

# Triple Substitution

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Decode absurdly modified activities using only committed physical mime and expressive gibberish.

## Overview
One player steps out while three performers are assigned a mundane activity modified by two surreal substitutions. When the guesser returns, they must deduce all nine elements within a strict time limit. Communication is strictly limited to physical object work and gibberish, requiring high-commitment physical play and deep active listening.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius

- **Technique(s):** Object work; Gibberish; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills

**Objective:** Develops absolute physical commitment, precise object work, and expressive vocal craft to convey complex, absurd ideas without relying on literal language.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with a clear stage area. One player (the Guesser) leaves the room or wears noise-canceling headphones. The remaining players stand in a line on stage. No physical props are used.

## How to Play
1. Select one player to be the Guesser and have them temporarily leave the room or step out of earshot.
2. Assign each of the three remaining stage players a base activity and two absurd substitutions, such as shaving with a banana and whipped cream.
3. Recall the Guesser to the stage and start a three-minute timer.
4. The Guesser approaches the first stage player, who immediately begins performing their modified activity using committed physical mime and vocalizing only in gibberish.
5. The Guesser must interact, using physical mimicry and gibberish to test their understanding of the activity and its two substitutions.
6. Once the Guesser believes they have grasped the concept, they must physically join in the activity with the performer to signal they are ready to move to the next player.
7. Repeat this process for the second and third players before the three-minute timer expires.
8. When time is called, the Guesser must formally guess the base activity and both substitutions for all three performers.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid writing in the air or using charade-like spelling; keep the focus on genuine, committed object work.
- Encourage the performers to isolate the three elements: establish the base activity clearly first, then highlight the substituted tool, and finally the substituted material.
- If the Guesser is struggling, allow the other stage players to physically jump in and support the scene, miming the same absurd reality to offer a different physical perspective.
- Pitfall: Players dropping their gibberish or using English-sounding words. Fix: Remind them that gibberish relies on emotional tone, pitch, and physical gesture, not secret codes.

## Variations
- Abstract Substitutions: Instead of physical objects, substitute one element with an abstract concept or emotion, such as chopping wood with a feather where the wood chips are actually pure joy.
- Silent Mode: Remove the gibberish entirely, forcing players to rely 100% on pure physical object work and eye contact.

## Debrief
- How did committing 100% to the physical weight and texture of the absurd objects help your partner understand what they were?
- What did you notice about the transition from confusion to clarity when you stopped trying to solve the puzzle and started physically playing along?
- How did the emotional tone of the gibberish convey information that physical movement alone could not?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards. Since players are moving dynamically with their eyes on each other, establish a clear boundary line for the stage. Allow players with physical limitations to adapt the activities to seated or low-impact movements, focusing more on facial expression and vocal gibberish.

## Why It Works
By stripping away literal language, players are forced to rely on the precision of their physical choices and the emotional truth of their gibberish. This high-commitment environment bypasses intellectual overthinking, turning a guessing game into a highly collaborative, physical comedy scene where partners must make each other look brilliant to succeed.

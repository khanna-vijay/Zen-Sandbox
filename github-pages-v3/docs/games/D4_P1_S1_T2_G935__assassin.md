# Shield and Assassin

![Shield and Assassin — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G935__assassin.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Keep your chosen protector positioned directly between you and your secret pursuer at all times.

## Overview
A high-energy, silent movement game where every player secretly designates two other people: one as their assassin and one as their shield. Once play begins, everyone must constantly adjust their physical position to keep their shield directly between themselves and their assassin, resulting in a dynamic, self-organizing group dance.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Stage-picture exercises
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep peripheral awareness, spatial tracking, and the follow-the-follower dynamic by forcing players to monitor multiple moving targets simultaneously without verbal communication.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players stand scattered throughout the space, ensuring there is plenty of room to move safely. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Gather all players in a large, open space and have them stand scattered around, facing different directions.
2. Instruct everyone to silently select one person in the room to be their Assassin and another person to be their Shield, keeping these choices completely secret.
3. Explain the core objective: each player must constantly position themselves so that their chosen Shield is physically directly between them and their Assassin, forming a straight line.
4. Emphasize that players must not touch, grab, or speak to anyone; all movement must be non-contact and silent.
5. Signal the start of the game, prompting players to begin moving immediately to establish their safe alignment.
6. As soon as one person moves, it triggers a chain reaction, forcing others to adjust their positions to maintain their own alignments.
7. Allow the chaotic, self-correcting movement to continue for two to three minutes as the group naturally speeds up, slows down, or swirls.
8. Call Freeze to end the game, then have players point to their secret Assassin and Shield to reveal the hidden web of connections.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: Keep your eyes soft. Use your peripheral vision to track both targets at once rather than staring directly at them.
- Pitfall: Players running or colliding. Fix: Remind them that this is a game of precise positioning, not tag. Encourage swift walking instead of running, and emphasize safety.
- Coaching cue: Notice how a single step by one person ripples through the entire room. You are all interconnected.
- Pitfall: Players getting stuck in corners. Fix: Encourage players to use the entire space and avoid clustering against walls.

## Variations
- Slow Motion: Run the entire exercise in extreme slow motion, which heightens the physical control and spatial awareness.
- The Swarm: Reverse the objective so players must keep themselves directly between their Shield and their Assassin, effectively acting as a protector.
- Blind Tracking: Play with eyes half-closed or squinted to rely entirely on soft focus and shadow movement.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to realize that your movements were directly dictating someone else's path, even if you didn't know it?
- What strategies did you use to track two different people at the same time without losing your balance or colliding?
- How does this game illustrate the concept of Group Mind and Follow the Follower in an improv scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is clear of trip hazards. Remind players to maintain a safe physical distance and avoid running, jumping, or physical contact. Offer a walking-pace-only rule to accommodate players with varying mobility levels.

## Why It Works
The game creates a complex, interconnected system out of simple, individual rules. Because every player's position is dependent on two others, the entire group becomes a single, living organism. This perfectly models Group Mind and Follow the Follower, as no single person is in control, yet everyone is deeply responsive to the collective movement.

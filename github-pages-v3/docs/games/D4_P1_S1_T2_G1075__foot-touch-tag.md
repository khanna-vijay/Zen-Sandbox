# Sole Swap Tag

![Sole Swap Tag — infographic](img/D4_P1_S1_T2_G1075__foot-touch-tag.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A high-energy, mind-bending tag game of swapped identities, rapid reactions, and physical coordination.

## Overview
Players form pairs touching foot-to-foot and swap names with their partners, while a central caller tries to summon runners. It is a fast-paced physical warm-up that forces players to track multiple threads of information—their temporary identity, their partner's identity, and their physical proximity—under pressure. The result is a chaotic, laughter-filled exercise in group focus and rapid adaptation.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop peripheral awareness, active listening, and split-second cognitive processing by decoupling physical self-identity from verbal cues while maintaining physical readiness.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players form pairs and spread out across the space. One player is designated as the "Caller" and stands alone in the center of the room. Within each pair, partners stand side-by-side, touching the outer edge of one foot to their partner's adjacent foot.

## How to Play
1. Have players pair up and stand side-by-side, pressing the side of their inside foot against their partner's inside foot. One player stands alone in the center as the Caller.
2. Instruct each pair to look each other in the eye and verbally swap names. For the duration of their pairing, Player A is addressed as 'Player B,' and Player B is addressed as 'Player A.'
3. The Caller in the center calls out any player's real name (e.g., 'Alex').
4. The player currently holding the name 'Alex' (who is actually Alex's partner) must immediately break contact and run to the Caller in the center.
5. The player whose actual name is 'Alex' must attempt to tag their partner before they escape. The tagger must keep their pivot foot planted and cannot chase the runner.
6. If the runner reaches the Caller without being tagged, they form a new pair with the Caller, touching feet and immediately swapping names. The abandoned partner in the original pair now becomes the new Caller in the center.
7. If the tagger successfully tags the runner before they escape, the runner must return to their partner, re-establish foot contact, and the Caller must call a different name.
8. If a player runs when their temporary name was not called, or if a tagger attempts to tag when it isn't their turn, they must reset, and the Caller calls again.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Listen for your new name, react with your old body!'
- Coaching cue: 'Keep your pivot foot anchored! No chasing, just reaching!'
- Pitfall: Players forgetting their swapped names. Fix: Have pairs loudly state their 'new' names to each other before each round begins, or do a quick check-in where the Caller points and asks a few players, 'Who are you right now?'
- Pitfall: Safety hazards from sudden lunging. Fix: Remind players to keep a safe distance from walls and to lunge safely without throwing their entire body weight onto their partner.

## Variations
- Double Swap: After a successful escape, the new pair swaps names, but all other pairs must also instantly find a new partner, touch feet, and swap names again, escalating the cognitive load.
- Action Swap: Instead of swapping names, partners swap physical gestures or signature sounds. The Caller performs a gesture, and the person holding that gesture must run.
- Silent Sole Swap: Play the entire game in complete silence. The Caller must make eye contact and point or use a silent gesture to summon a runner, requiring intense peripheral vision and group mind.

## Debrief
- How did it feel trying to override your lifelong habit of responding to your own name?
- What physical cues did you rely on to anticipate when your partner was about to bolt?
- How does this game mirror the 'thread-tracking' we do in an active improv scene where we must track our character, our partner's character, and the overall plot?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players with mobility or balance challenges can participate by modifying the physical contact (e.g., touching elbows, holding a shared object like a pool noodle, or simply standing within arm's reach). Remind players to be mindful of physical boundaries and to tag gently on the shoulder or torso, never the face or head.

## Why It Works
This game works because it forces a cognitive split-screen: players must maintain physical connection (proprioception) while tracking a linguistic shift (identity swap). By requiring players to monitor both their partner's physical tension and the Caller's voice, it builds high-level peripheral awareness and trains the ensemble to operate as a single, highly responsive organism.

# The Consequence Cascade

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Drive narrative momentum by instantly transforming every major action into an escalating, high-stakes complication.

## Overview
A structured narrative scene-work game where a facilitator's verbal cue forces players to immediately introduce and justify escalating consequences. By transforming simple actions into compounding complications, players learn to avoid narrative plateaus and build high-stakes, logically connected stories.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes; Justification; Game Identification; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Stakes-escalation reps; Justify the absurd; If this is true, what else is true?; Endowment-acceptance
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop narrative architecture and master the Platform/Tilt technique by training players to instantly accept unexpected narrative shifts, raise stakes, and logically justify escalating complications using the 'if true, what else is true' principle.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with two to three active players on stage and the remaining group acting as active observers. A facilitator stands to the side, ready to call out prompts. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Gather a group of six to twelve players, selecting two to three to start on stage while the rest observe.
2. Ask the observing players for a simple, low-stakes suggestion of a location and a mundane task, such as folding laundry in a basement or fixing a toaster.
3. Instruct the active players to begin a scene, establishing a solid base reality (the platform) by defining their characters, relationship, and a clear, simple objective.
4. Once a stable platform is established and a significant action, choice, or statement is made, the facilitator calls out 'CONSEQUENCE!'
5. Upon hearing the cue, the players must immediately introduce a direct, logical, yet escalating complication (the tilt) that stems from their last action.
6. The players must seamlessly integrate this new complication into the scene without breaking character, immediately treating it as absolute truth.
7. Continue the scene under this heightened reality, with players using the 'if true, what else is true' principle to justify and explore the immediate ripple effects of the consequence.
8. The facilitator calls 'CONSEQUENCE!' multiple times throughout the scene, typically three to five times, with each call demanding a progressively higher level of stakes and complication.
9. End the scene after a final, high-stakes consequence is successfully integrated and either resolved or pushed to a satisfyingly dramatic climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- Timing the Cue: Call 'CONSEQUENCE!' right after an active choice or physical action, rather than during passive exposition, to ensure the consequence feels tied to character agency.
- Pitfall - Absurdity Overload: Players may jump to wild, disconnected sci-fi elements that break the scene's logic. Side-coach them to find consequences that are grounded in the established reality.
- Pitfall - Denying the Consequence: Players might acknowledge the complication but immediately try to fix or dismiss it. Remind them to sit in the discomfort of the complication and let it change their emotional state or relationship.
- Coaching Cue: Encourage players to use physical object work to make the consequence tangible. If the lights go out, their physical movement must immediately reflect the darkness.

## Variations
- Targeted Consequences: The facilitator can call out specific types of consequences, such as 'EMOTIONAL CONSEQUENCE!' to force a relationship shift, or 'PHYSICAL CONSEQUENCE!' to force an environmental or bodily change.
- Silent Cascade: Instead of the facilitator calling the cue, players must trigger the cascade themselves by internally identifying when their partner makes a major move and immediately offering a self-imposed consequence.

## Debrief
- How did the forced consequences change your character's original objective or relationship with your partner?
- What was the difference between a consequence that felt organic and one that felt completely disconnected?
- How did applying 'if true, what else is true' help you justify the more extreme escalations?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because escalating consequences can naturally raise tension, remind players before starting that consequences do not need to involve physical violence or trauma to be high-stakes. Encourage them to find high stakes in relationships, status, and environmental complications. Establish a clear non-verbal stop signal if the tension becomes genuinely uncomfortable for any participant.

## Why It Works
This game works because it operationalizes the Platform/Tilt technique. By establishing a stable base reality first, any subsequent disruption (the consequence) carries genuine weight. Forcing players to immediately justify these disruptions prevents narrative stalling and teaches them that story progression is driven by the compounding ripple effects of character choices, rather than random, disconnected events.

# Choreographed Consensus

![Choreographed Consensus — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T1_G309__the-choreographed-consensus.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Achieve a complex physical objective in total silence by mirroring intentions and echoing emotional subtext.

## Overview
A non-verbal, physical partner exercise where pairs must collaborate to complete a complex, imaginary task under a shared emotional state. By stripping away spoken dialogue, players must rely entirely on physical clarity, emotional attunement, and explicit physical acknowledgments to build a shared reality. The experience is deeply focused, demanding intense concentration and mutual trust as partners negotiate leadership and support without words.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Assume Competence

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening and non-verbal attunement by practicing physical offer reception, active gifting of clear intentions, and functional status modulation, directly embodying the principle of making your partner look like a genius.

## Setup
An open, moderate-sized physical space. Divide the group into pairs. One pair performs in the center of the space while the remaining participants act as focused, silent observers. No physical props are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign each pair a specific, multi-step physical objective (such as assembling a delicate glass sculpture or navigating a laser grid) and a shared emotional spine (such as a desperate race against time or a fear of being detected).
2. Instruct the players that they must work together to achieve this objective in complete silence, relying entirely on physical movement, eye contact, and body language.
3. Introduce the 'Covenant of Sounds': each player is allowed a maximum of two non-verbal vocalizations per minute. These must either be an 'Affirming Sound' (like a soft hum of agreement) to signal 'I understand and accept,' or a 'Clarifying Sound' (like a questioning grunt) to signal 'I need more physical information.'
4. The player initiating an action (the Initiator) must use 'Active Gifting'—exaggerating their physical intention, weight, and spatial relationship to make their exact action and desired partner response unmistakable.
5. Before responding with a new action, the receiving player (the Reactor) must perform a 'Physical Echo' or 'Intention Handshake.' This is a brief, physical mirroring of the partner's posture, tension, or hand placement to explicitly signal that the offer has been received and understood.
6. If an offer is ambiguous, the Reactor must apply 'Genius Interpretation'—assuming competence and executing the most positive, helpful, and collaborative response possible rather than stopping the action.
7. If the Initiator notices their partner is confused (either through a hesitant Physical Echo or a Clarifying Sound), they must immediately refine and clarify their physical offer with greater physical precision.
8. Players must fluidly swap roles between Initiator and Reactor, naturally modulating their status as they alternate between leading a physical step and supporting their partner's movement.
9. The scene continues until the physical objective is successfully completed or the facilitator calls 'scene' (typically after 3 to 5 minutes).

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the 'Physical Echo': If players rush to add their own ideas without acknowledging their partner's physical offer, call out 'Echo first!' to slow them down and ground them in Meisner-style repetition.
- Monitor the non-verbal sounds: Ensure players do not use their allowed sounds as a crutch or a substitute for actual physical clarity. Remind them that sounds are a rare safety valve, not a language.
- Pitfall: Players often lose the shared emotional spine as they focus too hard on the physical task. Side-coach them to re-engage their emotional state: 'Remember the stakes—you are terrified of being heard!'
- Encourage physical commitment: Remind players to treat the imaginary objects with consistent weight, size, and resistance, which helps make their intentions clear to their partner.
- If a physical misunderstanding occurs, coach the players to embrace it as a happy accident. The Reactor's interpretation becomes the new reality, and the Initiator must adapt to it.

## Variations
- Blind Consensus: One partner must keep their eyes closed, and the other partner must guide them to complete the physical task using only touch and the allowed non-verbal sounds.
- Status Shift: Assign one partner a high physical status and the other a low physical status, requiring them to maintain this dynamic while collaborating on the task.
- Varying Tempos: The facilitator calls out different physical tempos (such as slow motion or double time) during the scene, forcing players to maintain their attunement under changing physical constraints.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to explicitly acknowledge your partner's physical offer (the Physical Echo) before building on it?
- When your partner's intention was unclear, how did you apply 'Genius Interpretation' to keep the momentum going?
- How did the shared emotional spine affect the way you handled the physical objects and communicated with your partner?
- In what ways did stripping away verbal dialogue make you a more active listener?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of actual obstacles before starting. Encourage players to be mindful of their partner's physical boundaries and comfort levels with proximity and light touch. Offer alternative, low-impact physical objectives for players with mobility constraints.

## Why It Works
By removing verbal communication, this game forces players to engage in pure physical active listening, mirroring the core mechanics of Meisner repetition. The mandatory 'Physical Echo' acts as a physical 'Yes, And,' ensuring that offers are fully received before they are built upon. This builds a deep, somatic trust, teaching players to make their partners look like geniuses by gifting clear, unambiguous physical information and assuming competence in every response.

# Time Jump

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Leap forward and backward through time to instantly discover the high-stakes moments of a story.

## Overview
A narrative scene-work game where players explore a relationship or situation across different eras. An off-stage caller disrupts the linear flow of time by shouting out temporal leaps, forcing the actors to instantly adapt, establish a new platform, and discover how their characters have evolved.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Start in the Middle; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Edits (Sweep, Tag-Out, Sound/Light)
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop narrative agility and pacing by bypassing transitional filler and practicing starting in the middle of new, high-stakes platform-and-tilt moments.

## Setup
Two to three active players stand in the performance space. One player or the facilitator acts as the Time Caller off-stage. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. Begin with a simple suggestion of a location or relationship to establish the initial platform.
2. The active players start a grounded, character-driven scene, establishing their dynamic and current reality.
3. Once a clear platform is established, the off-stage Time Caller shouts a specific time jump (e.g., 'Ten years later!' or 'Two minutes earlier!').
4. The players must instantly freeze, process the new temporal context, and immediately begin the next beat of the scene in that new time period.
5. In the new time period, players must rapidly establish what has changed (the tilt) and what has stayed the same, building on the established narrative history.
6. The Time Caller should vary the scale of the jumps, alternating between micro-jumps (e.g., 'Three seconds later') and macro-jumps (e.g., 'Five centuries in the past').
7. Players can introduce new characters or descendants if the time jump is extreme, while maintaining the core thematic thread of the original scene.
8. The scene concludes after four to five jumps, ideally landing on a satisfying narrative resolution or a dramatic full-circle moment.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Don't explain how you got here!' Encourage players to show the results of the time jump immediately through their physical state, status, or environment rather than talking about the transition.
- Pitfall: Players spending the first 30 seconds of a jump explaining the backstory of what happened during the gap. Fix: Side-coach them to make an immediate, bold choice or physical offer that reveals the current state.
- Coaching Cue: 'Vary the scale.' If the caller only uses 'one year later,' the narrative can become predictable. Mix in extreme jumps like 'ten seconds ago' to capture immediate consequences, or 'one thousand years ago' to explore ancestral origins.
- Pitfall: Losing the core relationship. Fix: Remind players that even if they jump 100 years into the future, the emotional dynamic or core conflict of the original platform should still echo in their new characters.

## Variations
- The Butterfly Effect: Every time jump must show a radical, unexpected consequence of the action taken right before the jump, emphasizing cause and effect.
- Flashback/Flashforward: The caller alternates strictly between the deep past and the far future, forcing players to construct a multi-generational family saga.
- Object-Focused Jumps: The scene centers around a single physical object (e.g., a pocket watch or a painting) that must remain in the scene across all time jumps, even as characters and settings change.

## Debrief
- How did jumping over the 'in-between' moments help you find the most interesting parts of the story?
- What strategies did you use to instantly communicate how your character changed after a massive time jump?
- How does skipping transitions prevent us from stalling or 'bridging' in our regular scene work?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that extreme time jumps (especially into the past or future) do not rely on harmful historical stereotypes or culturally insensitive tropes. Encourage players to focus on universal human dynamics.

## Why It Works
By removing the transition periods, players cannot rely on slow, logical exposition to move a story forward. It forces them to practice the 'Platform/Tilt' technique repeatedly in a short span, teaching them that the most compelling narrative beats happen right after a major shift, not during the slow climb toward it.

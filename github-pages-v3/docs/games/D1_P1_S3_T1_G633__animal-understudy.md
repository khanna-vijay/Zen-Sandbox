# Animal Understudy

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Swap seamlessly between human drama and wild animal instincts to master physical commitment and justification.

## Overview
Two players initiate a grounded, relationship-driven scene. At any moment, the facilitator calls 'Understudy!', prompting two offstage players to instantly swap in and continue the scene as highly physicalized animals. When 'Actors!' is called, the original players return, freezing in the animals' exact physical positions and justifying their new postures to continue the human narrative.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; Justification; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Character Walks/Centers; Justify the absurd; Tap-ins
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop 100% physical commitment and character embodiment using animal centers, while training players to joyfully justify unexpected physical offers and spatial shifts.

## Setup
An open performance space with room to move safely. Two players stand in the center to begin the scene; the remaining players wait on the wings as the designated 'understudies.' No props or chairs are needed.

## How to Play
1. Ask the group for a simple, everyday relationship and location to initiate a standard, grounded scene.
2. Two players enter the space and begin playing the scene, focusing on realistic human interactions and clear physical environment work.
3. Two offstage players secretly decide on a specific animal pairing to prepare for their entrance.
4. At a high point of action or a sudden pause, the facilitator calls out 'Understudy!'
5. The original actors immediately freeze and step out of the space, while the two understudies rush in, instantly adopting the physical postures, movements, and vocalizations of their chosen animals.
6. The animal understudies continue the scene's emotional dynamic and narrative thread, but express it entirely through their animalistic physicality and sounds.
7. After 30 to 45 seconds of high-energy animal play, the facilitator calls out 'Actors!'
8. The animal players freeze instantly and exit. The original human actors must rush back in, assume the exact physical poses and spatial locations left by the animals, and immediately resume the human scene.
9. The human actors must instantly justify why they are in these bizarre physical positions and weave that justification into the ongoing story.
10. Repeat the cycle two or three times within a single scene to build physical momentum and comedic escalation.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Commit to the animal's physical center!' Encourage players to lead with their chests, noses, or hips to avoid half-hearted animal work.
- Common Pitfall: Original actors returning to their comfortable standing positions instead of freezing in the exact animal poses. Fix: Remind players to 'shadow' the animals' final shapes precisely before speaking.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Justify the shape, don't ignore it!' If an actor is left crouching on all fours, they must explain it as a sudden back spasm, looking for a lost contact lens, or a sudden burst of passion.
- Encourage the animal understudies to maintain the scene's core relationship and emotional stakes, rather than just making random animal noises.

## Variations
- Hybrid Understudies: The understudies play the scene as human-animal hybrids (e.g., a corporate lawyer who has the physical tics and instincts of a squirrel).
- Audience Choice: The audience or offstage players shout out the specific animals right as 'Understudy!' is called, forcing instant physical adaptation.
- Tag-Team Rotation: Every time 'Understudy!' is called, a different pair of players from the wings jumps in, introducing new animals each round.

## Debrief
- How did embodying an animal change your physical presence and lower your self-consciousness?
- What strategies did you use to instantly justify a bizarre physical posture when you returned to the scene?
- How does committing 100% to a physical shape make it easier to find the comedy in a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing space is clear of tripping hazards. Remind players to be mindful of physical boundaries when playing high-energy animals; avoid physical contact unless explicitly negotiated beforehand. Players with mobility limitations can adapt animal physicality to upper-body movements or vocalizations.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to instantly transition into extreme animal physicality, the game bypasses the analytical mind and taps directly into unfiltered spontaneity. When the original actors return, the physical shapes they inherit act as a powerful engine for justification, proving that physical offers can drive narrative and character development just as effectively as verbal ones.

# The Unseen Companion

![The Unseen Companion — infographic](img/D1_P1_S3_T2_G1135__invisibility.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Bring an invisible character to life through precise physical reactions and shared spatial commitment.

## Overview
In this scene-based exercise, a group of players interacts with a character who is completely invisible to the audience but fully visible to the actors on stage. The players must use highly detailed physical reactions, eye contact, and object work to define the invisible character's size, movement, and actions, making them a tangible presence in the room.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening; Active Gifting; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Object work; Endowment-gifting drills; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop absolute commitment to physical space work and active gifting. Players learn to 'show, don't tell' by reacting to imaginary physical stimuli, establishing consistent spatial coordinates, and treating an empty space as a living, breathing scene partner.

## Setup
A clear performance space with no physical props. 3 to 4 players stand ready. One player is designated as the 'Invisible Character' (who will stand off-stage or speak from the sidelines), while the remaining 2 to 3 players start on stage.

## How to Play
1. Select one player to be the voice and mind of the Invisible Character; this player stands just off-stage or at the edge of the playing area where they can see the stage clearly.
2. The remaining players on stage establish a grounded, everyday environment using clear object work.
3. Introduce the Invisible Character into the scene. The on-stage players must immediately establish where this character is by tracking them with their eyes, reacting to their physical presence, and adjusting their own physical positions accordingly.
4. The off-stage player speaks for the Invisible Character, delivering lines and describing their own physical actions.
5. The on-stage players must instantly commit to these physical declarations, adjusting their eye-lines to the new height or location and physically reacting to any shared objects.
6. On-stage players can also initiate physical actions involving the Invisible Character, such as handing them an object, hugging them, or blocking their path, which the off-stage player must accept and voice in return.
7. Maintain strict consistency in the invisible character's physical dimensions throughout the scene, ensuring that all on-stage players look at the exact same spot in space when addressing them.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Track the eyes!' Remind players that if two people look at the invisible character, their lines of sight must intersect at the exact same point in space.
- Pitfall: Vague physical reactions. If the invisible character sits on a chair, the chair shouldn't look empty. Fix: Have an on-stage player react to the chair shifting, or try to sit in it and realize it's occupied.
- Coaching Cue: 'React first, speak second.' Encourage physical reactions to the invisible character's movements before verbally acknowledging them to build physical tension.
- Pitfall: The off-stage player becomes a disembodied voice with no physical reality. Fix: Side-coach the off-stage player to narrate physical struggles or spatial movements that force the on-stage players to move.

## Variations
- Silent Unseen: The invisible character is completely silent and does not speak. The on-stage players must entirely invent and react to their actions, translating their 'dialogue' through one-sided conversation.
- The Heavy Guest: The invisible character is extremely large or heavy, requiring the on-stage players to show immense physical effort whenever they interact, pass objects, or get bumped into.

## Debrief
- How did tracking the invisible character's physical location affect your focus and listening?
- What physical cues from your partners helped you instantly agree on where the invisible character was standing?
- How does committing 100% to an imaginary physical presence change the stakes of a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the stage is clear of actual tripping hazards. Since players will be moving while tracking an imaginary point in space, establish a slow, controlled physical pace to prevent accidental collisions.

## Why It Works
This game works because it forces players to externalize their imagination. By requiring absolute agreement on an invisible entity's location and actions, players must practice hyper-vigilant active listening and physical gifting. It strips away the ability to rely on lazy verbal exposition, demanding that the reality of the scene be built entirely through committed physical reaction and shared spatial awareness.

# Proximity Tag

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A high-energy chase game where players actively court danger by staying as close as possible.

## Overview
This is a high-octane physical warm-up that subverts the classic playground game of tag. Instead of running away to safety, players are challenged to play on the edge of danger, staying as close to the tagger as possible. The result is a fast-paced, laughter-filled exercise that demands intense physical commitment and spatial awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; Fail Joyfully; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Peripheral Awareness
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop physical commitment, rapid decision-making, and a playful relationship with failure by leaning into risk rather than playing it safe.

## Setup
A large, open room cleared of all obstacles, chairs, and tripping hazards. All players stand scattered throughout the space.

## How to Play
1. Gather the group in the center of the space and establish clear physical boundaries to ensure safety.
2. Designate one player as the initial tagger ('It').
3. Explain the core twist: the goal for the non-taggers is not to hide or run away, but to see how close they can get to the tagger without actually getting caught.
4. When the facilitator calls 'Go,' the tagger attempts to tag other players using a gentle, open-handed touch on the shoulder or upper back.
5. If a player is tagged, they immediately become the new tagger and must loudly announce 'I am It!' to notify the group.
6. Encourage players to celebrate the moment they get tagged as a joyful failure, immediately pivoting into their new role with high energy.
7. Run the game for three to five minutes, keeping the pace fast and the energy high.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Court the danger! How close can you get before you have to dodge?'
- Side-coaching cue: 'If you get tagged, celebrate it! Fail joyfully and keep the momentum going!'
- Pitfall: Players running to corners or playing too defensively. Fix: Remind them that the goal is proximity, not survival; challenge them to stay within a three-foot radius of the tagger.
- Pitfall: Physical collisions or overly aggressive tagging. Fix: Enforce a strict 'gentle touch only' rule and remind players to maintain peripheral awareness of the walls and other players.

## Variations
- Slow-Motion Tag: The entire game is played in exaggerated slow motion, requiring intense physical control, balance, and dramatic tension.
- Freeze Tag with a Twist: Tagged players freeze in dramatic, high-commitment physical poses and can only be unfrozen when another player mimics their pose for two seconds.
- Multi-Tag: Start with one tagger, but every time someone is tagged, they also become a tagger, rapidly increasing the chaos and requiring extreme peripheral awareness.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to actively move toward the threat instead of running away from it?
- How does the concept of 'failing joyfully' apply to getting tagged in this game?
- How did your awareness of the physical space and other players change as the speed increased?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is non-slip and free of tripping hazards. Establish a strict 'no running blindly' rule to prevent head-on collisions. Offer a fast-walking variation for groups with varying mobility levels to keep the game accessible and safe for everyone.

## Why It Works
By reversing the instinct to avoid danger, this game trains improvisers to lean into high-stakes situations with commitment. It shifts the mindset from self-preservation to active play, transforming 'failure' (getting tagged) into a fun, collaborative transition that keeps the group mind synchronized.

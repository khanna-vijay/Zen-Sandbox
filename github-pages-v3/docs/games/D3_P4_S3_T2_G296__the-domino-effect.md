# The Domino Effect

![The Domino Effect — infographic](img/D3_P4_S3_T2_G296__the-domino-effect.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Escalate scene stakes instantly by declaring and justifying the inevitable, cascading consequences of established facts.

## Overview
A narrative-driven scene-work exercise where players use a verbal trigger to inject sudden, logical, yet game-changing consequences into an active scene. By calling out a domino effect based on a prior detail, players practice the art of tilting a stable platform and immediately justifying the new, heightened reality. The result is a fast-paced, highly collaborative story that escalates organically rather than wandering aimlessly.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Game Identification; Narrative Architecture; Justification; Raising the Stakes
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; If this is true, what else is true?; Platform/Tilt; Justify the absurd; Stakes-escalation reps
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master the 'if this is true, what else is true' principle, specifically training players to identify the platform of a scene, introduce a narrative tilt, and use immediate justification to raise stakes and drive narrative architecture.

## Setup
An open performance space. Two to three active players stand in the playing area, while the remaining players observe as an active audience, ready to rotate in. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Begin a standard scene with two or three players, establishing a clear platform: who they are, where they are, and their basic relationship.
2. Once a stable platform is established, any player (or the facilitator) can call out 'Domino!' to pause the action momentarily.
3. The player who called the trigger must immediately state a new, undeniable consequence that stems logically from a detail already established in the scene.
4. This consequence must be framed as an absolute fact (a tilt) that raises the stakes, complicates character goals, or reveals a hidden tension.
5. The other players in the scene must instantly accept this new fact as absolute truth, without hesitation or denial.
6. The active players must immediately justify this new reality by explaining why it makes sense and how it affects their characters' current motivations.
7. The scene resumes with the players actively playing out this heightened reality, letting the new stakes dictate their physical actions and emotional choices.
8. Repeat this process two to three times throughout the scene, allowing subsequent 'Domino' calls to build upon previous consequences, creating a cascading chain of escalation.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Keep the connection tight!' Remind players that the consequence must logically link back to something already said or done, rather than being a random, disconnected shock.
- Coaching Cue: 'Play the gravity, not just the joke.' Encourage players to react with genuine emotional stakes to the new consequence, which grounds the absurdity and keeps the narrative compelling.
- Pitfall: Over-triggering. If players call 'Domino!' every ten seconds, the scene collapses into a chaotic list of facts. Fix: Instruct players to let each new consequence breathe and be fully explored for several lines before introducing another.
- Pitfall: Soft acceptance. Players might say 'Yes, that's true,' but continue acting exactly as they did before. Fix: Side-coach with 'How does this change what you are doing right now?' to force physical and tactical adjustments.

## Variations
- Audience Dominoes: The observing players sit on the sidelines and are the only ones allowed to call 'Domino!', forcing the active players to constantly adapt to external narrative shifts.
- Silent Dominoes: Instead of calling the word aloud, players introduce the escalating consequence purely through a physical action or a sudden, high-stakes character choice, requiring the partner to identify and justify the unvoiced domino.
- The Micro-Domino: Run the game with a strict rule that each consequence must be small and personal rather than catastrophic, training players to find high stakes in subtle relationship shifts.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have the reality of your scene suddenly shifted by an undeniable new fact?
- What made a consequence feel satisfyingly logical versus jarringly random?
- How did immediate justification help you discover new depth in your character's motivations?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game introduces sudden, non-negotiable narrative shifts, players should establish a red light or pause signal beforehand. If a called consequence inadvertently crosses a personal boundary or introduces sensitive themes, any player can call a pause to adjust the offer without penalty.

## Why It Works
This game works because it operationalizes the 'if this is true, what else is true' concept into a concrete, gamified mechanic. By forcing a clear Platform and then demanding a Tilt via the Domino trigger, it prevents scenes from plateauing. The requirement of immediate justification ensures that the narrative remains cohesive and character-driven, turning potentially disruptive offers into powerful engines of story progression.

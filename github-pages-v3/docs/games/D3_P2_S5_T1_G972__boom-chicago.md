# C.R.O.W. Build

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Rapidly construct a complete base reality and resolve a narrative arc in three simple steps.

## Overview
A fast-paced, structured drill where three players sequentially establish the environment, relationship, conflict, and resolution of a scene. By limiting each player to a single, highly specific contribution, it teaches players to build a solid base reality instantly and collaborate to find a satisfying narrative conclusion.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Physicality & Space Work; Active Gifting; Narrative Architecture
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Object work; Platform/Tilt; Endowment-gifting drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To master the rapid establishment of C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where) and practice collaborative narrative architecture by cleanly separating the setup, conflict, and resolution phases of a scene.

## Setup
Players stand in a semi-circle facing the performance space. No props or chairs are used. The facilitator stands to the side to coach and prompt transitions.

## How to Play
1. Player 1 steps into the performance space and immediately begins silent, detailed physical object work to establish a specific location (the 'Where').
2. Once the location is clear to the group, Player 2 enters the space and establishes a clear relationship with Player 1 using a single, direct line of dialogue (the 'Who' and 'Relationship').
3. Player 1 immediately responds by introducing a specific problem or objective related to the environment or relationship, using either a single line of dialogue or a clear physical action (the 'What' or 'Objective').
4. Player 3 steps into the scene and performs a single action or delivers a single line of dialogue that directly resolves the established problem.
5. Immediately upon the resolution, all three players freeze, throw their hands in the air, and enthusiastically shout 'Tada!' to conclude the scene.
6. The players return to the semi-circle, and the next trio steps up to begin a new round with a fresh environment.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach Player 1 to focus on heavy, specific physical details (e.g., weight, size, texture of objects) rather than generic pacing around, to make the 'Where' undeniable.
- Ensure Player 2's line is an active gift that names the relationship directly (e.g., 'Dad, the principal is on the phone') rather than a vague question.
- Pitfall: Player 3 trying to prolong the scene or add more drama. Fix: Remind them their sole job is to be the hero who cleanly and simply fixes the problem so the scene can end.
- Encourage the final 'Tada!' to be loud and synchronized, celebrating the collaborative completion of a miniature story.

## Variations
- Silent Build: Run the entire exercise completely silently, relying purely on physical object work, body language, and pantomime to establish the C.R.O.W. elements and the resolution.
- Blind Entrances: Player 2 and Player 3 turn their backs to the stage and only turn around when it is their turn to enter, forcing them to read the physical environment and relationship instantly.

## Debrief
- How did limiting your contribution to a single line or action change how you listened to your scene partners?
- What made a relationship or problem easy to build upon versus difficult?
- How does establishing a clear physical environment (the 'Where') make finding the relationship and problem easier?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of obstacles for quick entrances. Players with limited mobility can participate fully by establishing environments that allow for seated or stationary positions, and the final 'Tada!' gesture can be adapted to any comfortable physical expression.

## Why It Works
By breaking a scene's foundation down into discrete, sequential steps, this game removes the pressure of simultaneous creation. It forces players to rely on their partners' offers, demonstrating that a complete, satisfying narrative arc can be built effortlessly when everyone contributes exactly one clear piece of the puzzle.

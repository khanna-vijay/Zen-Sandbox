# Circle Crossings

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Trade places across the circle by tracking names, eye contact, and collective physical rhythm.

## Overview
In this high-focus ensemble warm-up, players navigate a shared space by calling out names and walking across the circle. The challenge lies in maintaining a continuous, uninterrupted flow of movement as players vacate and occupy spots in real time.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Timing exercises; Mirror exercise
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To build peripheral awareness, active listening, and group mind by training players to track multiple physical and verbal threads simultaneously.

## Setup
Arrange all players in a spacious, standing circle facing inward with a clear center. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Begin with all players standing in a circle facing inward.
2. Player A makes clear eye contact with Player B, calls Player B's name aloud, and begins walking at a steady pace toward Player B's spot.
3. Before Player A reaches them, Player B must quickly select another player (Player C), call Player C's name, and begin walking toward Player C's spot.
4. Player A steps into the spot just vacated by Player B, completing their crossing.
5. Player C must now select Player D, call their name, and begin walking before Player B arrives, continuing the chain of movement.
6. Maintain a steady, deliberate walking pace to ensure everyone has time to react and keep the chain unbroken.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to walk at a consistent, moderate pace; rushing ruins the rhythm and causes panic.
- If a player freezes, encourage the group to support them silently with eye contact rather than shouting names.
- Remind players that the verbal name call is the 'key' that unlocks the next person's movement—do not start walking until you have called the name.
- Watch for 'tunnel vision' where players only look at their target; encourage them to soften their gaze and use peripheral vision to see the whole circle.

## Variations
- Silent Connection: Play without speaking. Players must establish clear eye contact and receive a physical nod of acknowledgment before they can begin walking.
- Multi-Thread: Introduce a second, independent walking chain into the circle at the same time, forcing players to track two moving threads simultaneously.
- Emotional Mirroring: The walker adopts a specific physical posture or emotion, which the receiver must mirror before they set off toward their own target.

## Debrief
- What did it feel like when the rhythm of the circle became smooth and continuous?
- How did you use your peripheral vision to anticipate when you needed to move?
- How does this game demonstrate the principle of 'making your partner a genius'?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is clear of tripping hazards. For players with mobility challenges, the game can be played at a slower pace, or players can use a seated circle where physical crossing is replaced by pointing or throwing a soft object.

## Why It Works
This game physically instantiates the concept of 'follow the follower.' Because players must vacate their spot before the incoming walker arrives, they are forced to look ahead and support the next person in line. This builds a shared physical rhythm and a heightened sense of ensemble connection.

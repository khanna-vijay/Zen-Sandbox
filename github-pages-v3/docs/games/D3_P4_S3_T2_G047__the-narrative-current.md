# The Narrative Current

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Steer scenes to deeper waters by pausing to voice and integrate hidden narrative currents.

## Overview
A dynamic, facilitated scene-work exercise where a side-coach periodically pauses a two-player scene to inject critical narrative elements. When called upon, a player steps out of the action to explicitly state a hidden driver—such as their character's core desire, the stakes, or a sudden complication—before stepping back in to immediately play out this new reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Stakes / The 'Want'; World-Building; Justification; Raising the Stakes; Offer Reception; Active Gifting
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; What do they stand to lose? reps; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Justify the absurd; Stakes-escalation reps; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master narrative architecture by consciously identifying, articulating, and integrating structural story elements like platform, tilt, stakes, and subtext to drive a scene's progression.

## Setup
Two players stand in the performance space to initiate a scene. The remaining players observe as the audience. One facilitator acts as the Director, positioned where they can easily observe the players and call out prompts.

## How to Play
1. Begin with a simple suggestion of a location or relationship to establish a basic platform (the who, what, where).
2. The two active players start a grounded, natural scene, focusing on establishing their environment and connection.
3. Every 30 to 60 seconds, or when the scene reaches a plateau, the Director calls out 'Freeze!' and names a specific player along with a 'Story Current' prompt.
4. The designated player steps slightly out of the scene's physical space, breaks character eye contact, and directly states the requested narrative element clearly and concisely.
5. The player then steps back into the scene, immediately embodying the stated element through their physical choices, tone, and dialogue.
6. The scene partner must instantly accept this new reality ('Yes, And') and adjust their own behavior to justify and support the partner's revealed truth.
7. The Director guides the scene through a sequence of prompts, which may include: 'My Want' (immediate objective), 'The Stakes' (consequences of failure), 'Relationship Shift' (a change in how they view each other), 'Environmental Truth' (a discovery about the space or an object), 'Hidden Truth' (unspoken subtext), and 'Complication' (an external force or tilt).
8. After the final prompt is integrated, the Director allows the scene to play out organically for another minute, letting the players drive the heightened stakes toward a natural climax and resolution.

## Facilitation Notes
- Designating Players: Vary which player receives the prompt. If Player A is driving the action, prompt Player B with 'The Stakes' or 'Relationship Shift' to force them to react and share the narrative load.
- Coaching Specificity: If a player gives a vague or weak response (e.g., 'I want to have fun'), gently challenge them to make it high-stakes and actionable (e.g., 'I want to prove I can handle this job so my father finally respects me').
- Immediate Integration: Ensure players don't just talk about the prompt; they must immediately show it. If a player states a 'Hidden Truth,' their next line of dialogue or physical action must actively reflect that secret.
- Handling Resistance: If the non-prompted player struggles to accept the new reality, remind them of 'Make Your Partner a Genius.' They must treat the partner's stated current as absolute, pre-existing truth.

## Variations
- Blind Currents: The Director writes the prompts on cards. When called, the player draws a card, reads it silently, steps out to state it, and integrates it, keeping the partner on their toes.
- Peer Conducted: A third player from the audience acts as the Conductor, practicing their own directorial eye for narrative structure.
- Internal Monologue: Instead of structural prompts, the Director calls 'Subtext!' and the player must voice their character's actual, unspoken thoughts in that exact moment before resuming.

## Debrief
- How did explicitly naming your character's want or stakes change your physical and emotional commitment to the scene?
- For the receiving partner, what was the experience of having the scene's reality shift instantly, and how did you justify those shifts?
- How does consciously identifying these narrative elements mid-scene help us build stronger story arcs without a director's prompts?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they can choose narrative elements that are dramatic or comedic without crossing personal boundaries. If a prompt like 'Hidden Truth' or 'Relationship Shift' touches on sensitive themes, players are encouraged to steer toward creative, safe, and collaborative choices.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to step out and articulate structural elements, the game breaks the habit of passive scene-work. It uses a structured narrative engine to teach the 'Platform/Tilt' technique: establishing a baseline, introducing a disruptive tilt (via stakes, shifts, or complications), and immediately justifying the new reality to serve the story.

# Linked Chase

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A high-energy, fast-paced chase game where safety lies in linking arms with a partner.

## Overview
In this dynamic physical warm-up, players form linked pairs scattered across the space while one runner flees a chaser. The runner can find safety by linking arms with any pair, which instantly bumps the player on the opposite end into becoming the new runner. The result is a chaotic, joyful exercise in constant vigilance and rapid role-switching.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Commit 100%

- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops peripheral awareness, rapid physical adaptation, group mind, and the ability to transition instantly from bystander to active participant.

## Setup
A large, open room free of obstacles. Players pair up, link inside elbows, and scatter themselves evenly across the space. One pair is broken up to create one active Runner and one active Chaser.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players except two to form pairs, link elbows, and stand scattered throughout the space.
2. Designate one of the remaining players as the Runner and the other as the Chaser.
3. On your signal, the Chaser pursues the Runner, navigating safely around the stationary pairs.
4. The Runner can secure safety at any moment by linking elbows with either partner of any stationary pair.
5. Once the Runner links arms, the player on the opposite end of that newly formed three-person chain is immediately bumped.
6. The bumped player instantly becomes the new Runner and must flee from the Chaser.
7. If the Chaser tags the active Runner before they can link to a pair, their roles instantly reverse: the Runner becomes the Chaser, and the Chaser becomes the Runner.
8. Continue play at a high energy level, encouraging frequent linking to keep everyone involved.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to keep their eyes on the active chase even when they are stationary, anticipating when they might get bumped.
- If the same two players run for too long, coach the runner to 'link quickly' to keep the energy circulating.
- Remind players to use light, safe tags (a gentle tap on the shoulder) rather than pushing or grabbing.
- Ensure players maintain a safe running speed appropriate for the size of the room to avoid collisions.

## Variations
- Role Reversal: When a runner links to a pair, the bumped player instantly becomes the Chaser, and the old Chaser instantly becomes the Runner.
- Multiple Chases: Introduce two runners and two chasers simultaneously to double the cognitive load and demand extreme peripheral awareness.
- Slow Motion: Run the entire game in slow motion to emphasize physical control, spatial tracking, and dramatic tension.

## Debrief
- How did you maintain awareness of the chase when you were standing still in a pair?
- What did it feel like to transition instantly from standing still to sprinting for safety?
- How does this game demonstrate the concept of 'Group Mind' in a physical way?

## Safety & Inclusion
Establish clear boundaries regarding physical contact: arm-linking should be cooperative, and tags must be light. For players with mobility challenges, the game can be played at a walking pace, or they can remain as stationary anchor pairs who do not get bumped.

## Why It Works
This game forces players to step out of their individual heads and merge into a collective consciousness. Because the active threat can shift to anyone at any second, players must track the thread of the chase continuously, building deep peripheral awareness and instant physical commitment.

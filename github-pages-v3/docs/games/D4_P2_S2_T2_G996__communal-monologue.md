# Shared Monologue

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Speak with one voice by seamlessly stepping in to continue a single, unfolding story.

## Overview
A group exercise where players collaborate to deliver a single, cohesive monologue from the perspective of one character. One player stands in the center to speak, while circle members dynamically swap in to take over the narrative mid-sentence, maintaining the established voice, emotion, and physical posture.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Peripheral Awareness; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Emotional-echo drills; Tap-ins
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening, physical mirroring, and the ability to seamlessly support and build upon a partner's offer without hesitation.

## Setup
Players stand in a comfortable circle with enough space in the center for one person. No props or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Form a standing circle of 6 to 12 players, leaving the center clear.
2. One player steps into the center of the circle to begin a monologue in character, establishing a clear emotion, physical posture, and narrative premise.
3. The players in the outer circle listen intently, observing the speaker's physical stance, vocal cadence, and emotional state.
4. At any moment, an outer circle player can glide into the center to take over the monologue.
5. The transition must be seamless: the incoming player physically mirrors the outgoing player's posture and continues the exact sentence or thought without a pause.
6. The outgoing player quietly slips back into the outer circle, immediately becoming an active listener.
7. The monologue continues with frequent, smooth hand-offs, maintaining a single, unified character perspective throughout the exercise.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Match the physical posture before you speak.' This physical mirroring helps the brain instantly adopt the character's emotional state.
- Coaching cue: 'Pick up the sentence mid-word or mid-thought—don't wait for a clean sentence boundary.' This prevents polite waiting and keeps the flow dynamic.
- Pitfall: Players waiting for the speaker to 'finish' their point before stepping in. Fix: Encourage rapid, unexpected tap-ins to force spontaneous adaptation.
- Pitfall: Changing the character's emotional state or narrative direction abruptly. Fix: Remind players to 'yes-and' the established emotional reality rather than trying to steer the story to their own pre-planned ideas.

## Variations
- Emotional Shift: The facilitator calls out a new emotion (e.g., 'joy', 'paranoia') during a transition, and the incoming player must justify the shift within the monologue.
- Physical Tag-Out: Instead of gliding in, the incoming player gently taps the speaker on the shoulder to trigger the swap, practicing precise physical hand-offs.
- Subtext Monologue: The character is saying one thing but clearly feeling another; players must maintain this double-layered tension across swaps.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to step in mid-sentence? What helped you make that transition seamless?
- What did you have to pay attention to besides the words being spoken?
- How does this exercise build trust and a sense of 'group mind' within the ensemble?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards. Players should move at a safe, controlled pace when entering the center. If physical touch is used for transitions (like a shoulder tap), establish consent beforehand or stick to the non-contact 'glide-in' method.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to step into the physical and vocal shoes of another, this game bypasses intellectual planning and demands absolute presence. It embodies 'follow the follower' because the speaker must surrender control of their creation, and the incoming player must fully support and adopt the existing reality.

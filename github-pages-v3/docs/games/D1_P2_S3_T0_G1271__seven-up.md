# Seven-Count Shakeout

![Seven-Count Shakeout — infographic](img/D1_P2_S3_T0_G1271__seven-up.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Shake off tension and embrace mistakes in a high-energy, synchronized physical countdown.

## Overview
A high-energy physical warm-up where players stand in a circle and shake out their limbs in a descending count. As the numbers decrease, the pace accelerates, leading to a chaotic, joyful release of physical tension and mental overthinking.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Fail Joyfully; Commit 100%; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Timing exercises
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To build physical presence, establish a shared group rhythm, and practice failing joyfully when physical coordination breaks down at high speeds.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle with comfortable spacing between them, ensuring everyone has enough room to safely shake their arms and legs.

## How to Play
1. Gather the group into a standing circle and explain the sequence of limbs: right arm, left arm, right leg, left leg.
2. Begin the first round by shaking the right arm vigorously while counting aloud from one to seven.
3. Immediately switch to the left arm, shaking it while counting aloud from one to seven.
4. Transition to the right leg for a count of seven, followed by the left leg for a count of seven.
5. Repeat the entire four-limb sequence counting down from six, then five, then four, all the way down to one.
6. Maintain a steady, collective tempo that naturally accelerates as the numbers get smaller.
7. Culminate the game with a rapid-fire, single-count shake of each limb (one, one, one, one) and a collective high-energy cheer.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'If you lose count or shake the wrong limb, laugh it off, commit 100%, and jump right back in!'
- Coaching cue: 'Project your voice and match the group's volume to build collective energy.'
- Pitfall: Players may get self-conscious and drop their energy. Fix: Lead by example with high physical commitment and loud, enthusiastic counting.
- Pitfall: The group's rhythm desynchronizes. Fix: Encourage players to make eye contact across the circle to lock back into a shared tempo.

## Variations
- Silent Shakeout: Perform the entire countdown in complete silence, relying purely on visual cues and group mind to transition between limbs.
- Gibberish Countdown: Replace the numbers with nonsense sounds or gibberish syllables while maintaining the same rhythmic structure.
- Double Speed: Challenge the group to complete the entire sequence at double the initial tempo once they have mastered the basic pattern.

## Debrief
- How did it feel when you made a mistake or lost the rhythm during the fast rounds?
- How does loud vocalization and physical movement affect your mental readiness to improvise?
- What did you notice about how the group synchronized as the numbers got smaller and the pace got faster?

## Safety & Inclusion
Encourage players to modify the movements to suit their physical comfort and balance. For example, players can nod their head, shrug their shoulders, or tap their feet instead of shaking full limbs if they have mobility or balance constraints.

## Why It Works
By combining high-energy physical movement with a simple, descending cognitive task, this game bypasses the analytical brain. When mistakes happen at high speeds, players have no choice but to laugh and keep going, directly training the 'fail joyfully' mindset while building group cohesion.

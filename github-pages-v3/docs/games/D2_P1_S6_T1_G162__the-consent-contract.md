# Consent Coins

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Navigate physical and emotional boundaries in real-time using physical tokens to pause and check in.

## Overview
This structured scene-work drill uses physical tokens to gamify and normalize boundary negotiation. Players must spend a token to initiate any high-stakes physical contact or deep emotional intimacy, triggering a mandatory pause for their partner to check in and respond. The resulting experience shifts consent from an abstract rule to an active, playful, and deeply respectful improvisational dialogue.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Consent & Boundaries; Truth Over Pandering; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Boundary Navigation; Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Check-ins; Cut calls; Negotiating physical contact
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop real-time boundary navigation and active check-in techniques, training players to prioritize personal comfort over scene preservation ('Truth Over Pandering') while maintaining collaborative play.

## Setup
An in-person playing space with a clear stage area. Distribute three physical tokens (such as poker chips, pennies, or bottle caps) to each participant. These represent 'Consent Coins.' No other props are required.

## How to Play
1. Pair up two players and assign them a simple relationship and scenario with potential for physical or emotional intimacy.
2. Each player begins the scene with three physical tokens, representing their 'Consent Coins' for initiating high-stakes offers.
3. To initiate a significant physical action (e.g., a hug, holding hands) or a deep emotional offer (e.g., sharing a dark secret), the initiator must explicitly state their intention and hand over or place down one Consent Coin.
4. Upon the coin being spent, both players must immediately pause the scene's action for a brief, silent internal check-in.
5. The receiving player must respond with one of three choices: Accept (the action proceeds), Decline (the action does not happen, and the scene continues), or Negotiate (proposing an alternative, such as a hand on the shoulder instead of a hug).
6. If a negotiation occurs, the initiator can accept or decline the counter-offer without spending an additional coin.
7. At any point, if a player feels uncomfortable or senses a boundary violation, they can call 'Cut!' to immediately stop the scene with zero penalty or judgment.
8. If a player runs out of Consent Coins, they can no longer initiate high-stakes physical or emotional offers, though they can still receive and negotiate their partner's offers.
9. End the scene after five to seven minutes, or immediately if a 'Cut!' is called, to transition into a structured debrief.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Pause and check in. What does your body actually want right now?' Use this to disrupt the automatic urge to 'yes-and' physical contact.
- Pitfall: Players using coins for minor, incidental contact (like a casual shoulder tap). Fix: Clarify before play that incidental contact is free; coins are reserved for high-stakes, boundary-testing choices.
- Pitfall: Players feeling like calling 'Cut!' or saying 'No' ruined the scene. Fix: Actively celebrate 'No' and 'Cut!' calls as masterclasses in boundary navigation and successful game play.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Negotiate the offer.' Remind players that a counter-offer is a highly creative, collaborative way to keep the scene moving safely.

## Variations
- Silent Coins: Run the same exercise but perform the check-in and negotiation entirely through non-verbal cues and eye contact, testing players' physical awareness.
- The Blind Map: One player is blindfolded (or closes their eyes) and must navigate a physical space guided by their partner, using the coin mechanic to negotiate every physical touch or guiding hand.

## Debrief
- How did having a physical token and a mandated pause change your ability to assess your own comfort level in the moment?
- What did it feel like to say 'no' or negotiate an offer, and how did it affect the creative direction of the scene?
- How did managing a limited number of coins change the intentionality and specificity of your physical and emotional offers?
- How can we apply this practice of active check-ins to standard scenes where physical tokens are not present?

## Safety & Inclusion
This game is highly safety-sensitive. Participation must be entirely voluntary. Ensure players know they have absolute autonomy over their bodies. If a player calls 'Cut!', the facilitator must immediately pause the room, check on the players with care, and avoid assigning blame or forcing an explanation.

## Why It Works
By introducing a physical currency and a mandatory pause, the game disrupts the subconscious pressure to unconditionally 'yes-and' physical or emotional escalation. It transforms boundary setting from a potential scene-killer into an active, gamified mechanic, proving that respecting a partner's boundaries actually enhances trust and creative collaboration.

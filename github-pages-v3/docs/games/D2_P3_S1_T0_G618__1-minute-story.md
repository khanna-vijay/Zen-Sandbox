# Sixty-Second Biography

![Sixty-Second Biography — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T0_G618__1-minute-story.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Listen deeply to a partner's life story, then repeat it back with absolute accuracy.

## Overview
Sixty-Second Biography is a low-energy, high-connection pairing exercise where players practice deep, non-judgmental listening. One partner shares their life story in exactly one minute, while the other listens without interrupting, only to immediately retell that story back to them with as much detail and respect as possible. It is an intimate, grounding experience that builds immediate trust and focus.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** This exercise develops active listening, offer reception, and vulnerability by training players to absorb information without planning their next line, embodying the principle of making their partner a genius.

## Setup
Players form pairs and stand or sit facing each other in a comfortable space. The facilitator needs a stopwatch or timer to track the intervals.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and designate one person as Partner A and the other as Partner B.
2. Instruct Partner A that they will have exactly sixty seconds to share their life story, starting from their earliest memories up to the present day.
3. Instruct Partner B that their sole job is to listen actively and silently, without interrupting, asking questions, or nodding excessively.
4. Start the timer and let Partner A speak continuously for sixty seconds, sharing whatever details they feel comfortable disclosing.
5. When the timer sounds, immediately instruct Partner B to retell Partner A's story back to them, aiming to capture as many specific details, emotions, and chronological events as they can remember.
6. Give Partner B sixty seconds to complete the retelling, ensuring they speak in the third person (e.g., 'You were born in...').
7. Swap roles so Partner B shares their life story for sixty seconds while Partner A listens, followed by Partner A retelling the story.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the listeners: 'Listen to absorb, not to reply. Turn off your internal commentary and visualize their story like a movie.'
- Watch out for listeners who try to memorize facts like a test, which makes them look tense and disconnected. Remind them to relax and connect eye-to-eye.
- If storytellers run out of things to say, coach them to focus on small sensory details, childhood hobbies, or even how they feel in the present moment.
- Ensure the retellers honor their partner's story by avoiding jokes, embellishments, or adding their own commentary.

## Variations
- The Emotional Echo: Instead of focusing on chronological facts, the reteller must focus on and highlight the emotional states the storyteller expressed.
- Specific Prompt: Instead of a whole life story, share a specific memory, such as a memorable trip or a first day at school, to lower the vulnerability threshold.
- The Three-Sentence Summary: After the retelling, the listener must distill the essence of the partner's story into exactly three sentences, practicing synthesis.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to listen without the pressure of having to formulate a response or ask a question?
- What strategies did you use to remember the details of your partner's story?
- How did it feel to hear your own story reflected back to you? Did you feel seen and heard?

## Safety & Inclusion
While this game is low-physicality, sharing personal history can occasionally touch on sensitive or vulnerable topics. Remind players that they are in complete control of their narrative. They can share a completely mundane, fictionalized, or highly curated version of their life. No one is obligated to share deeply personal trauma.

## Why It Works
By removing the pressure to respond, improvisers bypass their internal editor and practice true offer reception. Retelling the story forces the brain to actively process and value the partner's words, embodying the principle of making your partner look like a genius by treating their words as precious, memorable gold.

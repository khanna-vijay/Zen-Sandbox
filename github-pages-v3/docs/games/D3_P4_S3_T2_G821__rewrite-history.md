# Rewriting History

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Uncover the hilarious, unrecorded truth behind famous historical events by tilting the timeline.

## Overview
In this game, players recreate a well-known historical event, starting with a recognizable setup before introducing a sudden, unexpected twist. The fun lies in treating this absurd new reality with absolute sincerity, showing the audience what 'really' happened behind the scenes. It is a playful exploration of narrative disruption and collaborative justification.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Justification; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop narrative architecture skills, specifically mastering the Platform/Tilt technique by establishing a clear baseline and successfully justifying a major story disruption.

## Setup
An open performance space suitable for 2 to 5 players. No props or special materials are required. The facilitator or group should have a few famous historical events ready as suggestions.

## How to Play
1. Obtain a suggestion of a well-known historical event or milestone from the group.
2. Two to three players step into the performance space to establish the 'Platform' by portraying the historical figures in their correct setting.
3. Players spend the first minute of the scene behaving exactly as history dictates, establishing their relationships, environment, and immediate goals.
4. One player introduces a 'Tilt'—an unexpected, ahistorical choice, secret, or obstacle that completely derails the expected timeline.
5. The other players must immediately accept this new reality using 'Yes, And' and justify why this bizarre turn of events is actually the truth.
6. The players collaborate to build a new narrative arc based on this tilt, treating the high-stakes situation with genuine commitment.
7. The scene concludes once the players reach a satisfying, alternative resolution to the historical event.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Establish the normal first!' Ensure the players build a solid, recognizable platform before introducing the twist.
- Coaching cue: 'Play it straight!' The comedy and narrative tension rely on the characters treating the absurd tilt with high stakes and sincerity.
- Pitfall: Tilting too early. If players twist the story before the audience understands the setup, the impact is lost. Encourage at least 30 seconds of historical accuracy first.
- Pitfall: Denying the tilt. If a player tries to correct the history or ignore the twist, remind them to accept and justify the new reality immediately.

## Variations
- The Secret Agent: One player is secretly assigned to introduce the tilt, while the others must try to keep the scene historically accurate until it happens.
- Anachronism Alert: The tilt must involve introducing a highly advanced modern technology or modern social concept into an ancient historical setting.
- The Textbook Version: A narrator offstage reads the 'official' history, while the onstage players physically and verbally contradict the narration to show the 'truth'.

## Debrief
- How did establishing a strong, recognizable platform make the eventual tilt more satisfying to play?
- What strategies did you use to justify the absurd twist without breaking character or ruining the scene's reality?
- How does knowing the 'expected' historical outcome help us play with and subvert audience expectations?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players avoid mocking real-world tragedies, sensitive cultural events, or marginalized groups. Encourage them to focus on lighthearted scientific, political, or cultural milestones rather than historical traumas. Provide physical accommodations for players who need to remain seated during the scene.

## Why It Works
This game perfectly demonstrates the Platform/Tilt narrative structure. By using a shared historical baseline as the platform, players don't have to invent a world from scratch. The sudden tilt forces immediate offer reception and justification, teaching players how to pivot a story dynamically while maintaining narrative cohesion.

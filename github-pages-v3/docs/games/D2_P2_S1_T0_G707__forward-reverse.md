# Forward and Rewind

![Forward and Rewind — infographic](img/D2_P2_S1_T0_G707__forward-reverse.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Navigate a scene's timeline backwards and forwards on command, testing memory, physicality, and active listening.

## Overview
Two players improvise a narrative scene while a facilitator controls the flow of time. When 'Rewind' is called, players must step-by-step reverse their dialogue and physical actions; when 'Forward' is called, they resume the narrative trajectory. It is a high-focus exercise that transforms simple scene work into a playful, physical memory puzzle.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Narrative Architecture; Peripheral Awareness; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Object work
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening, precise physical recall, and narrative tracking by forcing players to commit to and remember every word and movement their partner makes.

## Setup
Clear a moderate performance space. Two players stand in the playing area, while a third player or the facilitator acts as the 'Time Controller' standing off-stage. The remaining players watch as active observers.

## How to Play
1. The Time Controller prompts the two active players with a simple, action-oriented suggestion to start a scene.
2. The players begin improvising a scene, ensuring they use clear, distinct lines of dialogue and deliberate physical actions.
3. At any moment, the Time Controller calls out 'Rewind!' to alter the flow of time.
4. Upon hearing 'Rewind,' the players must immediately stop their forward progress and begin repeating their dialogue and physical actions in reverse order, line-by-line.
5. During a rewind, the words within each individual line of dialogue remain in their normal, forward-reading order, but the sequence of lines is reversed.
6. The Time Controller can call 'Forward!' at any point during the rewind, prompting the players to resume the scene's narrative from the exact moment they have rewound to, creating new story developments.
7. The Time Controller can also introduce other temporal commands like 'Slow Motion' (stretching out voice and movement), 'Fast Forward' (speeding up voice and movement), or 'Pause' (freezing completely).
8. The scene continues for 2 to 3 minutes, cycling through different time states, before rotating in new players.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Keep your physical actions simple and distinct! If you pick up a cup, remember exactly how and when you did it so you can put it back down in reverse.'
- Common Pitfall: Players trying to reverse the actual spelling or word order of a sentence (e.g., saying 'bomb the diffuse' instead of 'diffuse the bomb'). Fix: Remind them that only the sequence of lines reverses, not the grammar of the lines themselves.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Listen to your partner's last line. In rewind, you are waiting for them to finish their previous line before you deliver yours.'
- Common Pitfall: Losing the narrative thread because of the cognitive load of remembering. Fix: Encourage players to slow down the pace of the scene and use shorter, punchier lines of dialogue.

## Variations
- Extreme Rewind: Challenge the players to rewind all the way past the very first line of the scene, inventing the 'prequel' actions and dialogue that happened before the scene officially started.
- Physical Only: Run the scene entirely in gibberish or silently, focusing 100% on the rewind and fast-forward of complex physical comedy and object work.
- Group Time Warp: Increase the player count to 3 or 4, requiring intense group mind and peripheral awareness to track the sequence of lines across multiple people.

## Debrief
- How did having to remember your partner's exact lines change how closely you listened to them?
- What strategies did you use to remember your physical positions and object work during a rewind?
- How did the 'Rewind' moments actually help inspire new narrative choices when you went 'Forward' again?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing space is completely clear of tripping hazards, as players will be moving backward with limited rear visibility. Facilitators should actively watch the boundaries of the stage and call 'Pause' if a player is about to collide with a wall or object.

## Why It Works
This game forces players out of their own heads and into a state of hyper-presence. By making the past state of the scene a concrete set of rules that must be recalled, players cannot plan ahead; they must 'Yes, And' the physical and verbal reality that has already been established, reinforcing the connection between active listening and narrative co-creation.

# Stupid T-Shirt

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Instantly caption absurd imaginary graphics to bypass your inner editor and embrace your first thought.

## Overview
A high-energy, rapid-fire circle game where players take turns describing a bizarre graphic on their neighbor's imaginary shirt, and that neighbor must instantly supply the caption. Driven by a rhythmic group chant, this drill forces players to bypass their analytical minds and commit to the very first idea that pops up.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and trust in one's immediate impulses by practicing rapid offer reception and instant commitment without self-censorship.

## Setup
Players stand in a circle facing inward. No props or physical materials are required. The facilitator should establish a light, high-energy atmosphere before beginning.

## How to Play
1. Have the entire group stand in a circle and establish a steady, rhythmic clap or bounce to build energy.
2. Lead the group in chanting the chorus together: 'T-shirt, T-shirt, what's on your stupid T-shirt?' while gesturing toward a designated starting player (Player A).
3. Player A immediately turns to the player on their left (Player B), points to Player B's chest, and describes a bizarre, imaginary visual graphic on Player B's 'T-shirt' (e.g., 'It's a picture of a squirrel wearing sunglasses riding a skateboard').
4. Without pausing to think, Player B must instantly deliver the slogan or caption that goes with that graphic (e.g., 'Too fast for nuts!').
5. Immediately after Player B delivers their caption, the entire group restarts the chant: 'T-shirt, T-shirt, what's on your stupid T-shirt?'
6. Player B now turns to the player on their left (Player C), describes a new imaginary graphic on Player C's shirt, and Player C must instantly caption it.
7. Continue this pattern around the circle, keeping the rhythm fast, supportive, and continuous.

## Facilitation Notes
- Keep the tempo high. If a player hesitates, encourage the group to gently prompt them or have them make a sound rather than overthinking.
- Side-coach players to accept whatever image they are given without trying to make it logical or 'good.' The weirder and more nonsensical, the better.
- Watch out for players trying to pre-plan their captions or graphics. Emphasize that the magic lies in the surprise of the present moment.
- If a player gets stuck on a caption, encourage them to shout the very first word that comes to mind, even if it's just a sound or a single unrelated word.

## Variations
- Reverse Direction: Change the flow of the circle mid-game on a facilitator's cue to keep players on their toes.
- Physicalized T-Shirts: Have the player captioning the shirt strike a physical pose that matches the energy of the graphic and caption.
- Double Trouble: The player describing the graphic must describe a front and a back graphic, requiring two rapid-fire captions from the wearer.

## Debrief
- How did it feel when you had absolutely no time to plan your caption?
- What happened when you let go of trying to make your caption 'funny' or 'clever'?
- How did the group's rhythmic chant help support your spontaneity?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are comfortable with light, non-contact gesturing toward each other's chests. Players can point generally at the person or gesture to their own chest if preferred to maintain personal boundaries.

## Why It Works
By combining a rhythmic, collective chant with a strict time constraint, the game overloads the analytical left brain. This forces the player to rely entirely on their subconscious, demonstrating that their 'first thought' is often the most creative, surprising, and delightful option available.

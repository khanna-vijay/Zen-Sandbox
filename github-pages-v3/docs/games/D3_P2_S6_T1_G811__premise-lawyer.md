# The Absurd Advocate

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Defend the indefensible by finding the logical core behind the wildest claims.

## Overview
A rapid-fire pairing exercise where one player presents a completely bizarre or nonsensical belief, and their partner immediately defends it with structured, logical reasoning. By treating the absurd as absolute truth, players learn to ground unusual choices in a believable reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Yes, And… sentence games
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To master the art of justification, transforming bizarre or accidental choices into logical, character-driven truths that support a stable base reality.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs spread out across the room. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and designate Player A and Player B for the first round.
2. Player A begins by stating a completely absurd, nonsensical, or counter-intuitive belief as an absolute fact (e.g., 'We should replace all traffic lights with disco balls').
3. Player B immediately adopts the role of the 'Advocate,' enthusiastically agreeing with the premise without hesitation.
4. Player B must then deliver a structured, logical justification explaining why this premise makes perfect sense, drawing on pseudo-science, history, psychology, or common sense.
5. Player B concludes their defense with a strong summary statement, cementing the absurd premise as a rational truth.
6. The partners swap roles, with Player B presenting a new absurd premise and Player A acting as the Advocate.
7. Run multiple rounds, encouraging players to increase the speed of their agreement and the depth of their logical justifications.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to avoid saying 'Yes, but...' or stalling. The agreement must be instant and absolute.
- Pitfall: Players often try to make the justification wacky or silly. Fix: Encourage them to use a serious, academic, or highly professional tone; the contrast between the absurd premise and the grounded logic is where the comedy and skill lie.
- Encourage advocates to use real-world frameworks (like economics, biology, or history) to ground their arguments.
- Remind players that a good justification doesn't change the premise; it explains the 'why' behind it.

## Variations
- Cross-Examination: After the Advocate finishes their defense, Player A asks one challenging follow-up question, which the Advocate must immediately justify.
- The Tag-Team Defense: Play in groups of three. Player A states the premise, Player B starts the justification, and Player C must seamlessly take over mid-sentence to finish the defense.

## Debrief
- How did finding a logical reason for a wild claim change how 'real' the premise felt?
- What strategies did you use to make your justifications sound authoritative and believable?
- How does this skill help us when a scene partner makes an unexpected or unusual choice on stage?

## Why It Works
By forcing players to immediately accept and logically defend an absurd premise, the game bypasses the instinct to judge or block unusual offers. It trains the brain to treat every choice as an intentional, justifiable truth, which is the core mechanism of building a stable base reality in comedic scenework.

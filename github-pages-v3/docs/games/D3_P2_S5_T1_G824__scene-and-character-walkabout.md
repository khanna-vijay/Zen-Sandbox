# Parallel Worlds Walkabout

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Build instant base realities simultaneously with a partner in a low-pressure, high-energy environment.

## Overview
In this dynamic, low-stakes warm-up, the entire group moves through the space and pairs up on a facilitator's cue to immediately launch into simultaneous scenes. Because every pair plays at the same time, there is no passive audience, allowing players to freely experiment with establishing a solid base reality without self-consciousness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Yes, And; Commit 100%
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Unfiltered Spontaneity; Emotional Fluidity
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Endowment-acceptance; The Emotional Dial (1→10)
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To rapidly establish a clear Base Reality using the C.R.O.W. framework (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where) through active listening and immediate offer reception.

## Setup
An open room with cleared floor space allowing all participants to walk around freely. No props, chairs, or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to disperse and walk around the room at a moderate pace, filling the empty spaces and maintaining soft eye contact.
2. Call out 'Freeze and Pair!' to signal players to immediately stop and partner with the person closest to them.
3. Provide a specific prompt that targets one or more elements of C.R.O.W., such as a location and a relationship (e.g., 'You are two competitive chefs in a messy kitchen').
4. Instruct players to immediately begin a two-person scene based on the prompt, focusing on establishing who they are, where they are, and what they want right away.
5. Allow all pairs to play their scenes simultaneously for 60 to 90 seconds, encouraging them to use physical object work to define their environment.
6. Call out 'Thank your partner!' to end the scene, prompting players to express gratitude to their partner with a high-five or a quick thank you.
7. Instruct players to resume walking around the room, mixing up the space and preparing for the next round.
8. Repeat the process for 3 to 4 rounds with new prompts, progressively challenging players to establish all four elements of C.R.O.W. within their first three lines of dialogue.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Show us where you are! Use your hands to touch the environment and define the physical space.'
- Side-coaching cue: 'Establish how you feel about each other in your very first line of dialogue!'
- Pitfall: Players might wait for their partner to make the first move, leading to awkward silence. Fix: Remind them that both players are 100% responsible for the scene; initiate with a physical action or a clear emotional statement immediately.
- Pitfall: The room gets too loud, and players struggle to hear. Fix: Encourage players to lean in close and lower their vocal volume while keeping their physical energy and emotional commitment high.

## Variations
- Silent C.R.O.W.: Run the simultaneous scenes entirely in gibberish or complete silence, forcing players to rely purely on physical world-building and emotional expression to establish the base reality.
- The Hot-Potato Element: Give the pairs only one element of C.R.O.W. (e.g., 'Where: A submarine') and challenge them to discover and agree upon the other three elements within thirty seconds.
- Tag-In Walkabout: After a minute of simultaneous play, freeze the room and have one pair perform their established scene for the rest of the group for 30 seconds, highlighting strong base realities.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to play a scene when there was no audience watching you?
- What was the fastest way you and your partner established 'Where' you were without explicitly naming it?
- How did physicalizing your environment (object work) help you discover your character's objective or relationship?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the walking pace is accessible to all physical abilities. If someone has mobility challenges, players can pair up by raising a hand or making eye contact rather than rushing physically. Remind players to respect physical boundaries during rapid pairing.

## Why It Works
By removing the spectator element, players lose their stage fright and bypass their internal editors. The simultaneous noise and action create a safe 'wall of sound' where players can commit 100% to their choices, immediately practicing the rapid construction of a shared base reality through physical and verbal agreement.

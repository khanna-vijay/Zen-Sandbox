# The Partner's Pedestal

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Elevate your partner to legendary status through relentless, reciprocal gifting and physical attunement.

## Overview
A two-player scene-work exercise that trains improvisers to aggressively support and celebrate their scene partner. Players begin with a silent, somatic attunement phase before transitioning into a spoken scene where every line and physical choice is engineered to make the other person look brilliant, competent, and high-status.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And; Assume Competence; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Active Listening; Status Modulation; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Offer Reception; Emotional Fluidity; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Give them the answer; Mirror exercise; Emotional-echo drills; Meisner Repetition; Endowment-acceptance; Status Seesaw; High/low-status walks
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To master active gifting and endowment-gifting, training players to instinctively elevate their partner's status, assume their absolute competence, and gracefully accept high-status endowments.

## Setup
Two active players stand facing each other at a comfortable conversational distance in the center of the space. The rest of the group acts as active observers. The facilitator prepares a few neutral relationship prompts that do not imply pre-existing status dynamics.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator provides a neutral relationship prompt, such as two colleagues waiting for critical news or two old friends meeting after a long journey.
2. Begin with a sixty-second silent Echo Start where Player A initiates a subtle, authentic physical gesture or micro-expression reflecting an internal emotional state.
3. Player B observes intently and mirrors the emotional essence of Player A's gesture, internalizing and embodying the feeling rather than just mimicking the movement.
4. Player A builds on Player B's response by deepening or shifting the physicalized emotion, and Player B mirrors this new layer, establishing a shared somatic foundation.
5. Transition to the spoken scene where Player A speaks the first line, directly translating the non-verbal emotional connection into a verbal relationship.
6. Engage in the Status Symphony where both players must actively gift status and genius to their partner by attributing high intelligence, expertise, or historical success to them.
7. Strategically lower your own status when appropriate to create space for your partner, framing your vulnerability as a way to highlight their strength and competence.
8. Incorporate non-verbal gifting by yielding physical space, looking up with genuine admiration, and reacting with visible awe to your partner's statements.
9. Practice Gracious Offer Reception where the receiving player must instantly accept all high-status endowments without self-deprecation, fully stepping onto the pedestal.
10. Maintain reciprocal gifting where every time you receive an elevating endowment, immediately build on it and offer a new gift of genius back to your partner.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach immediately if a player tries to play modest or deny a compliment, prompting them to say 'Thank you, and...' to force acceptance.
- Watch out for self-deprecation that diminishes the scene; remind players that lowering their own status is a strategic gift to empower their partner, not a way to make themselves useless.
- During the Echo Start, ensure players are not pantomiming or performing; coach them to find genuine, small, internal impulses like a shift in weight or a soft sigh.
- If a player struggles to invent a genius endowment, coach them to look at what their partner is already doing and label it as a masterclass in that action.

## Variations
- The Silent Pedestal: Run the entire scene without words, relying purely on physical status shifts, spatial yielding, and emotional mirroring to establish who is the genius.
- The Blind Pedestal: One player is designated as the genius but does not know why; the other player must endow them with specific, brilliant achievements until the genius figures out their expertise.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to immediately accept and embody the high-status genius attributes your partner gifted you?
- What physical adjustments did you make to visually place your partner on a pedestal?
- How did lowering your own status strategically help to elevate your partner's presence in the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players establish comfortable physical boundaries before the Echo Start, as close-proximity eye contact and mirroring can feel intense. Allow players to adjust the physical distance as needed for comfort.

## Why It Works
By forcing a hyper-focus on the partner's brilliance, this game short-circuits the improviser's self-conscious internal monologue. When players are relieved of the pressure to be funny or clever themselves, and instead channel all energy into making their partner look like a genius, the scene naturally gains high emotional stakes, rich character detail, and deep collaborative trust.

# Mundane Crisis Heroes

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Rescue partners from trivial crises by gifting them ridiculous superhero identities they must instantly physicalize.

## Overview
A high-energy, fast-paced game where players summon one another to solve an incredibly minor household problem using absurd, improvised superhero personas. Each new hero enters with grand, high-status theatricality, utterly fails to solve the problem, and then gifts the next player an even more ridiculous superhero moniker to save the day.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Yes, And; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Active Gifting; Status Modulation; Offer Reception; Heightening & Exploration; Physicality & Space Work
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Status Seesaw; Endowment-acceptance; The 'ladder' (escalating beats); Character Walks/Centers
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To practice active gifting and endowment, forcing players to instantly accept and heighten absurd identities (making their partners look like geniuses) while mastering rapid status shifts from high-energy confidence to humbled defeat.

## Setup
Four to six players stand in a line upstage (the 'hero pool'). One player steps forward to the playing area. The facilitator asks the audience for a trivial, everyday problem (e.g., 'static cling' or 'a stuck drawer') and a random household object (e.g., 'a spatula' or 'a sponge').

## How to Play
1. The first player begins a solo scene, physicalizing the trivial problem with high stakes and dramatic struggle, establishing that they cannot solve it alone.
2. Desperate, the first player calls out for a superhero named after the suggested household object (e.g., 'Spatula Man, save me!').
3. The second player from the line must instantly leap into the scene as that superhero, striking a high-status, theatrical pose and physicalizing their unique 'powers' based on the name.
4. The superhero attempts to solve the problem using their absurd powers, but their actions must actively make the situation worse, escalating the chaos.
5. Realizing their failure, the superhero experiences a sharp status drop (from confident savior to embarrassed failure) and calls out for a new, completely improvised superhero to help (e.g., 'This is too much for me! I must summon... Velcro Girl!').
6. The third player leaps in as the newly named hero, repeating the cycle: high-status entrance, physicalized powers, spectacular failure, status drop, and gifting a new name to the next player.
7. The final player (usually the fourth or fifth) enters as the last-summoned hero and must find a ridiculous, triumphant way to actually resolve the crisis, ending the scene on a high note.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Leap first, think later!' Encourage players to enter with physical commitment before they even know what their powers are.
- Pitfall: Players trying to be actually helpful. Fix: Remind them that comedy comes from making the problem worse. The superhero's 'help' should always escalate the disaster.
- Coaching Cue: 'Give a clear gift!' When summoning the next hero, speak the name clearly and give them a juicy, specific title that suggests physical action.
- Pitfall: Soft status transitions. Fix: Ensure there is a stark, visible contrast between the hero's booming, confident entrance and their humbled, apologetic exit.

## Variations
- Villainous Intervention: Introduce a supervillain halfway through who represents the source of the mundane problem (e.g., 'The Dust Bunny King'), requiring the heroes to battle them physically.
- Tag-Team Powers: Instead of exiting, failed heroes remain in the space, physically helping (or hindering) the subsequent heroes, creating a chaotic, crowded stage of useless super-beings.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to be gifted a highly specific, absurd superhero name? Did the specificity make it easier or harder to physicalize?
- What physical choices helped you instantly communicate high status upon your entrance?
- How does making the problem worse actually 'make your partner a genius' in this game format?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing space is clear of tripping hazards, as players will be leaping into the scene with high physical energy. Offer low-impact physical alternatives for players who prefer not to jump or strike high-impact poses.

## Why It Works
This game thrives on the 'Yes, And' engine of endowment. By naming a partner as a specific hero, you gift them a clear physical and character starting point. The rapid status shift (high to low) teaches players to embrace failure joyfully, while the escalating stakes turn a trivial prompt into an epic comedic narrative.

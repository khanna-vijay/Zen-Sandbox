# Ha So Ka

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Pass a high-energy, three-part physical rhythm around the circle without breaking the chain.

## Overview
A fast-paced, high-energy elimination game where players pass three distinct vocal and physical cues around a circle. As the speed increases, players must maintain intense focus, peripheral awareness, and a joyful attitude toward mistakes. It builds group mind by demanding absolute synchronization of voice, movement, and attention.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** Timing exercises

**Objective:** To develop rapid-fire physical and vocal coordination, heighten peripheral awareness, and cultivate a playful resilience to failure through a shared rhythmic structure.

## Setup
Players stand in a shoulder-to-shoulder circle in an open space. No props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Gather all players into a standing circle facing inward.
2. Introduce the three distinct sounds and gestures that must always occur in the strict sequence: 'Ha', then 'So', then 'Ka'.
3. The first player initiates 'Ha' by placing one hand horizontally above their eyes like a visor and pointing their fingers either to the immediate left or right neighbor, passing the turn to that neighbor.
4. The receiving neighbor must immediately perform 'So' by placing one hand horizontally under their chin, pointing their fingers either to their left or right neighbor to pass the turn.
5. The player receiving 'So' must immediately perform 'Ka' by bringing both palms together in a chopping motion, pointing directly at anyone across the circle.
6. The player targeted by 'Ka' must immediately restart the sequence with 'Ha', choosing a direction to pass it to.
7. If a player hesitates, makes the wrong gesture, says the wrong word, or speaks out of turn, they are out, and the rest of the circle rhythmically chants, 'You are out of this game!' to celebrate the mistake.
8. Eliminated players step outside the circle and are encouraged to playfully distract the remaining active players using sounds, faces, or gestures without physical contact.
9. The game continues, accelerating in speed, until only two players remain, who are declared the co-champions.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage players to fail joyfully; the elimination chant should feel like a celebration, not a punishment.
- Keep the tempo brisk. If the circle slows down too much to avoid mistakes, challenge them to double the speed.
- Watch for double-pointing or ambiguous gestures. Gestures must be crisp and deliberate so neighbors know exactly who is being targeted.
- Remind eliminated players that their job is to be active distractors from the outside, which keeps the energy high and trains the remaining players' focus.

## Variations
- Non-Elimination Mode: Instead of stepping out, players who make a mistake do a quick spin or a jump-squat and the game immediately resumes with them starting a new 'Ha'.
- Sound Swap: Replace 'Ha', 'So', and 'Ka' with three thematic words related to a current show or topic, keeping the same physical rules.
- Blind Pass: Players must close their eyes, relying entirely on vocal cues and spatial memory, best played without elimination.

## Debrief
- How did the energy shift in the room as the speed of the game increased?
- What did it feel like to make a mistake and hear the group chant? How can we bring that joyful failure attitude into our scene work?
- How did you use your peripheral vision to stay ready even when you were not the immediate target?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical gestures, especially the 'Ka' chop, are kept at a safe distance from others. For players with limited mobility, gestures can be adapted, such as head nods or eye contact instead of hand placements. Distractors must remain at least two feet away from the active circle and avoid any physical contact.

## Why It Works
By combining strict physical rules with a rapid vocal sequence, the game forces players out of their analytical minds and into a state of pure reaction. The elimination mechanic, paired with a supportive group chant, reframes failure as a fun, shared event, which lowers performance anxiety and builds ensemble trust.

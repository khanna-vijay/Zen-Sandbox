# The Emergency Gift

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Solve your partner's sudden crisis instantly using the random object in your hand.

## Overview
Two players face off, each declaring a personal crisis and a completely unrelated object they happen to be holding. They must then immediately swap roles to solve their partner's crisis using their own random object, celebrating the first absurd idea that comes to mind. It is a fast-paced, high-energy drill that builds rapid justification and supportive play.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Active Gifting; Justification
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Give them the answer; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop active gifting and rapid justification by forcing players to immediately 'give the answer' to their partner's problem, making their partner look brilliant and relieved.

## Setup
Two players stand facing each other in the center of the playing space. The rest of the group watches as the active audience. No physical props are needed; all objects are mimed or declared.

## How to Play
1. Two players step forward to face each other.
2. Player A initiates by stating a personal crisis they are facing, immediately followed by naming a random, unrelated object they are holding (e.g., 'My house is flooding, and all I have is this stapler!').
3. Player B immediately responds with their own crisis and unrelated object (e.g., 'I have a job interview in five minutes, and all I have is this live lobster!').
4. Without pausing to plan, Player A must solve Player B's crisis using Player A's object (e.g., 'Use my stapler to hem your pants so you look sharp for the interview!').
5. Immediately after, Player B must solve Player A's crisis using Player B's object (e.g., 'Throw my lobster into the floodwaters to pinch the pipe shut!').
6. Both players enthusiastically accept the solutions as perfect, genius fixes, ending the round with a high-five or a shared moment of triumph.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to respond instantly: 'Don't think, just offer! The first solution is the best solution!'
- Encourage enthusiastic acceptance: 'Make your partner's solution sound like pure genius!'
- Pitfall: Players stalling to find a logical solution. Fix: Remind them that absurd, surreal, or highly impractical solutions are preferred over realistic ones.
- Pitfall: Rejecting the solution or arguing it wouldn't work. Fix: Coach the receiver to react with absolute relief and gratitude, validating the gift instantly.

## Variations
- Rapid Fire Line: The group forms two lines facing each other. Pairs step up, do the exchange in under 15 seconds, and rotate to the back of the line.
- Emotional Stakes: Players must deliver their crisis with extreme emotional intensity (grief, panic, joy), and the solution must match or shift that emotional state.
- Three-Way Crisis: Three players stand in a triangle. Player A solves Player B's crisis, Player B solves Player C's, and Player C solves Player A's.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your crisis solved with something completely useless?
- What happened when you stopped trying to find the 'perfect' logical answer and just said the first thing that popped into your head?
- How does treating your partner's bizarre solution as a stroke of genius change the energy of the scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Keep the 'crises' lighthearted and low-stakes (e.g., lost keys, bad haircuts, minor inconveniences) to avoid triggering real-world trauma. Encourage players to steer clear of heavy personal tragedies.

## Why It Works
By pairing a random crisis with an unrelated object, the brain is forced to bypass logical filters to find a connection. This trains unfiltered spontaneity and justification. Because players must accept the absurd solution as a perfect fix, it directly reinforces the principle of making your partner a genius and active gifting.

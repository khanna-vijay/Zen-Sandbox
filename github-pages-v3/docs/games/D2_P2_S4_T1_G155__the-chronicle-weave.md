# The Narrative Loom

> Weave a seamless collaborative story using virtual chat cues and spotlighting to eliminate audio overlap.

## Overview
A fast-paced, virtual storytelling game where players build a cohesive narrative one or two sentences at a time. By using the platform's chat box to claim the next turn and the spotlight feature to focus on the active speaker, players bypass the lag and interruption common in online spaces. The result is a highly focused, deeply collaborative storytelling experience that feels like a single mind at work.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Narrative Architecture; Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Last Word Response; Yes, And… sentence games; Story Spine; Thread-tracking drills
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop immediate offer reception and active listening in a virtual environment, training players to build directly on the last line spoken without pre-planning.

## Setup
Players join a virtual video meeting with gallery view enabled and the chat panel open. No physical props are required. The facilitator acts as the 'Conductor' who will monitor the chat queue and manage the platform's spotlighting or pinning features.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator welcomes the group and instructs everyone to turn on their cameras, open the chat window, and mute their microphones.
2. The facilitator provides an open-ended narrative prompt to kick off the story, such as 'A deep-sea diver discovers a mysterious door at the bottom of the ocean.'
3. To claim the next turn in the story, players must type the keyword 'LOOM' into the chat box.
4. The facilitator monitors the chat and calls out the name of the very first person who typed the keyword.
5. The facilitator immediately spotlights or pins that player, who unmutes and delivers exactly one or two sentences to advance the story.
6. The active speaker must immediately mute themselves as soon as they finish their sentence, signaling they are done.
7. The next player to type the keyword is called upon, spotlighted, and continues the story, practicing strict 'Yes, And' by building directly on the previous line.
8. If a player is called but has a sudden block, they can say 'Pass,' and the facilitator immediately calls the next person in the chat queue.
9. After 10 to 12 minutes, the facilitator announces the 'final thread,' prompting the next speaker to bring the story to a satisfying conclusion.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: 'Listen to the very last word spoken, not the idea you had three turns ago!'
- Pitfall: Players typing the keyword before the current speaker finishes. Fix: Remind players that they can only type the keyword after the speaker has finished their sentence to ensure true active listening.
- Coaching Cue: 'Keep your contributions to one or two sentences. Pass the baton quickly to keep the momentum high.'
- Pitfall: Facilitator lag in spotlighting. Fix: If spotlighting is too slow, the facilitator can simply call out the name verbally to keep the pace, updating the spotlight as soon as possible.
- Coaching Cue: 'Use non-verbal reactions (thumbs up, physical nods, expressive faces) while muted to support the active storyteller.'

## Variations
- Genre Shift: The facilitator periodically types a new genre (e.g., 'Sci-Fi', 'Noir', 'Soap Opera') into the chat, and the next speakers must instantly adapt the story's tone.
- Word Count Constraint: Limit each speaker's contribution to exactly seven words, forcing extreme economy of language.
- Character Renaming: Before starting, players rename themselves on the platform to represent a specific perspective or emotional state (e.g., 'The Skeptic', 'The Enthusiast') and must speak from that perspective when called.

## Debrief
- How did the chat queue change how you listened compared to a normal virtual conversation?
- What did it feel like to let go of a pre-planned idea because the speaker right before you took the story in a new direction?
- How did the spotlighting affect your focus as both a listener and a speaker?
- What strategies helped us keep the narrative cohesive rather than chaotic?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since this game relies on rapid-fire typing and verbal delivery, ensure players with physical or cognitive processing differences are supported. The 'Pass' option should be normalized as a low-stakes, shame-free choice. Alternatively, players can use a simple emoji in chat instead of typing a word if that is faster or more accessible for them.

## Why It Works
This game solves the 'virtual lag' problem by turning the chat into a structured, self-regulating queue. Because players cannot predict exactly when they will speak, they cannot pre-plan their lines. This forces them into a state of pure offer reception, where they must accept the immediate reality of the previous line and 'Yes, And' it to keep the narrative engine running.

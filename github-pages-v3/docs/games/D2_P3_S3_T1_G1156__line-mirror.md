# Linked Mirror Lines

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Move as one collective reflection by linking hands and sharing the physical lead.

## Overview
Two parallel lines of players face each other while holding hands within their respective rows. As they begin to move, the lines must mirror each other's physical actions in real-time, creating a fluid, synchronized dance where leadership is shared and invisible.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind; Follow the Follower
- **Skill(s):** Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Peripheral Awareness
- **Technique(s):** Mirror exercise
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops deep physical empathy, peripheral awareness, and the ability to distribute leadership across a connected group.

## Setup
Clear a moderate space. Divide players into two equal lines. Have the lines stand facing each other, about three to four feet apart. Within each line, players stand side-by-side and hold hands.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into two equal lines facing each other, standing close enough to see micro-movements but far enough to move safely.
2. Instruct players within each line to hold hands with the neighbors on their left and right, forming two solid, connected chains.
3. Explain that Line A and Line B will act as mirrors of one another, where every movement made by one line must be instantly reflected by the other.
4. Begin the movement in silence, with no designated leader. Any player in either line can initiate a slow, deliberate movement.
5. Allow the physical impulse of a movement to ripple through the hand-connection of one line, while the opposing line mirrors the visual shape of that movement.
6. Encourage players to use their peripheral vision to stay in sync with their own line while maintaining soft-focus eye contact with the line opposite them.
7. Challenge the group to make the transition of leadership so seamless that an outside observer cannot tell who is initiating any given movement.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Soft eyes. Look at the whole line opposite you, not just the person directly in front of you.'
- Coaching cue: 'Feel the physical pull and push in your hands. Let the physical connection guide the speed of the movement.'
- Pitfall: Players moving too fast, causing the mirror connection to break. Fix: Remind them to move as if underwater or in slow motion.
- Pitfall: One dominant player hijacking the movement. Fix: Side-coach to 'pass the leadership' or 'let the movement start from the elbows or knees of someone else.'

## Variations
- The Horseshoe: Form a single U-shaped line where the two ends of the U face each other and mirror, while the curve of the U acts as the physical bridge transmitting the movement.
- Sound Mirroring: Add abstract, low-volume vocalizations that must be mirrored in pitch and tone along with the physical movement.

## Debrief
- How did holding hands change your ability to anticipate or follow a movement compared to a standard one-on-one mirror?
- When did you feel leadership shift? How did you know it was your turn to lead or follow?
- How did you balance looking at the person directly in front of you with watching the entire opposing line?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game involves holding hands, always ask for consent before starting. Offer alternatives for players who prefer not to touch, such as touching elbows, holding a short piece of rope/fabric between them, or standing close without physical contact while maintaining the same spatial gap.

## Why It Works
By physically linking the players, the exercise creates a literal feedback loop. The physical touch acts as a haptic channel of communication, reinforcing the visual mirroring. This forces players to rely on peripheral awareness and group mind, embodying the principle of making their partner look like a genius by instantly validating and matching every physical offer.

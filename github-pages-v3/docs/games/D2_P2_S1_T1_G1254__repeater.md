# Echo Dialogue

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Hear every word, repeat the line, and build the scene one echo at a time.

## Overview
A foundational two-player scene-building drill designed to enforce deep, active listening. Players must repeat their partner's exact last line of dialogue before they are permitted to add their own new contribution, creating a rhythmic and highly focused exchange.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception
- **Technique(s):** Meisner Repetition; Last Word Response
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To cultivate absolute presence, eliminate pre-planning, and ensure players fully receive and process their partner's offer before formulating a response.

## Setup
Two players stand or sit facing each other in the center of the space. The rest of the group observes as active listeners. No props or special staging are required.

## How to Play
1. Pair up players and have them stand or sit facing each other.
2. Player A initiates the scene with a single, simple line of dialogue (e.g., 'I think we are lost in this forest.').
3. Player B must first repeat Player A's line exactly as spoken, matching the words precisely (e.g., 'I think we are lost in this forest.').
4. Immediately after repeating the line, Player B adds their own new line of dialogue to advance the scene (e.g., 'And the sun is starting to set.').
5. Player A must now repeat only Player B's new line (e.g., 'And the sun is starting to set.') and then add their own new line (e.g., 'We should build a shelter before it gets dark.').
6. Continue this cycle of 'Repeat partner's new line + Add your own new line' for two to three minutes.
7. Keep lines relatively short and manageable to prevent cognitive overload and maintain focus on the emotional connection.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't plan your next line while they are speaking. Just listen to the words.'
- Pitfall: Players repeating the entire history of the scene. Fix: Remind them to only repeat the single new line added by their partner, ignoring the echo part.
- Pitfall: Paraphrasing instead of exact repetition. Fix: Gently pause the scene and have them try the line again with the exact words used.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Notice how repeating their words changes your emotional state or physical posture.'

## Variations
- Emotional Echo: Players must repeat the line matching the exact vocal tone, volume, and emotional subtext of their partner before adding their own line.
- Physical Echo: Players must mirror the physical posture or gesture of their partner while repeating the line.
- Three-Way Echo: Run the exercise in groups of three, where the active speaker repeats the line of the person who spoke immediately before them.

## Debrief
- How did having to repeat your partner's line affect your ability to pre-plan your next line?
- Did you notice any moments where repeating their words changed how you felt about the scene?
- How does this level of listening differ from how we normally listen in everyday conversations?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players speak clearly and at a moderate pace to support players with auditory processing differences or hearing impairments. Allow players to sit if standing for long periods is a barrier.

## Why It Works
By forcing the physical act of repetition, the brain is prevented from jumping ahead to write the next line. It grounds the player in the present moment, embodying Meisner's repetition technique to build authentic, responsive chemistry.

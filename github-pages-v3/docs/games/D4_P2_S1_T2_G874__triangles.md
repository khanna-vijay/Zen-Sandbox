# Equilateral Triangles

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A silent, kinetic group exercise where players dynamically balance the space using secret physical relationships.

## Overview
In this high-focus movement exercise, players silently select two peers and continuously adjust their own position to form a perfect equilateral triangle with them. Because everyone's targets are also moving to satisfy their own secret triangles, the entire room enters a state of fluid, self-organizing motion. It is a physical manifestation of group mind that requires no verbal communication.

- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Follow the Follower; Group Mind

**Objective:** To develop deep peripheral awareness, non-verbal connection, and the ability to track multiple moving elements simultaneously in a shared space.

## Setup
A large, open room cleared of chairs and obstacles. Players stand scattered throughout the space, facing different directions, with comfortable distance between one another.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to stand silently in the space and look around the room.
2. Ask each player to secretly select two other people in the room without making eye contact or indicating their choices in any way.
3. Explain the objective: each player must position themselves so they are always equidistant from their two chosen targets, forming the third point of an invisible equilateral triangle.
4. On the facilitator's signal to begin, players must start moving silently to establish and maintain their individual triangles.
5. Encourage players to keep moving continuously, as their targets will also be moving to satisfy their own secret triangles, creating a chain reaction across the floor.
6. After one to two minutes of continuous movement, call out 'Freeze!' and ask everyone to hold their positions.
7. While frozen, have players point to their two secret targets to visually verify how close they came to forming a perfect equilateral triangle.
8. Reset the game by having everyone choose two new secret targets and repeating the process.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to use 'soft focus' or peripheral vision rather than staring directly at their targets, which helps maintain the secrecy of the choices.
- If the room becomes chaotic or players start running, pause the game and remind them to move at a controlled, deliberate walking pace.
- Remind players that the goal is not to reach a static 'winning' position, but to remain in a state of active, responsive adjustment.
- Encourage players to notice how a tiny step by one person ripples through the entire room, demonstrating how everyone is interconnected.

## Variations
- Bodyguard and Assassin: Players secretly select one 'Assassin' and one 'Bodyguard'. They must constantly move to keep their Bodyguard directly between themselves and their Assassin.
- The Devoted Fan: Players select just one target. They must maintain a direct, unobstructed line of sight to that person at all times while staying as close as possible without touching.
- Slow Motion: Run the standard equilateral triangle version at ten percent speed, emphasizing extreme physical control and hyper-awareness of micro-movements.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to realize that your movements were directly causing someone else to move, even if you didn't know who was tracking you?
- What strategies did you use to keep track of two moving targets without staring directly at them?
- How does this exercise mirror the concept of 'following the follower' in an active, unscripted scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is completely clear of tripping hazards. Remind players to maintain a safe walking speed to prevent collisions, and to be mindful of physical boundaries as they navigate close quarters.

## Why It Works
This game physically demonstrates the concept of 'Group Mind' and 'Follow the Follower.' Because there is no single leader, the system self-organizes through micro-feedback loops. Players must rely on peripheral vision and spatial tracking, which directly translates to tracking multiple narrative and physical threads on stage.

# 185

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A rapid-fire pun game where players embrace their first thoughts and fail joyfully together.

## Overview
185 is a high-energy, fast-paced comedy game where players take turns stepping forward to deliver quick-witted punchlines based on a single suggestion. Using a classic joke template, players must trust their immediate word associations, delivering both brilliant puns and wonderfully terrible groaners with equal confidence. The focus is on speed, support, and celebrating every attempt, regardless of how the joke lands.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Fail Joyfully; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Support Work; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** Word-association; First Thought drills; Walk-ons
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and rapid word-association skills, training players to trust their immediate instincts and overcome the fear of making mistakes.

## Setup
Players stand in a horizontal line facing the facilitator. No props or special staging are required; a clear playing space where players can easily step forward and back is ideal.

## How to Play
1. Ask the group for a suggestion of a profession, object, or animal.
2. Once the suggestion is given, any player can step forward from the line to initiate the joke using the standard setup: '185 [suggestions] walk into a bar...'
3. The player completes the joke with a punchline, which is often a pun, double entendre, or wordplay based on the suggestion.
4. After delivering the punchline, the player immediately steps back into the line, and the rest of the group cheers, laughs, or groans supportively.
5. Another player immediately steps forward to deliver a different joke using the same suggestion and setup.
6. Players are encouraged to step forward quickly, even if they do not have a perfect joke, to keep the momentum high and relieve pressure on their teammates.
7. Once the jokes for that suggestion run dry or reach a natural peak, the facilitator calls out for a fresh suggestion to start the cycle again.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to step forward before they even know the punchline to force reliance on pure word association.
- If a player delivers a terrible joke, encourage them to sell it with absolute, triumphant confidence.
- Address the pitfall of overthinking by reminding players that stepping forward to deliver a weak joke is a generous act that buys time for their teammates.
- Keep the energy high by maintaining a fast tempo between jokes, not allowing long silences to settle.

## Variations
- The Bartender Says: Restrict the format strictly to: '185 [nouns] walk into a bar. The bartender says... and they say...' to add a structural constraint.
- Tag-Team Puns: Play in pairs where one player steps up and says the setup, and their partner must immediately step up and deliver the punchline.
- Alphabetical Order: Players must step forward in a specific order or the punchlines must follow alphabetical order based on the first letter of the key pun word.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to step forward without knowing exactly what you were going to say?
- What happened to the energy of the room when we celebrated the 'bad' jokes just as much as the clever ones?
- How does stepping forward to deliver a weak joke actually support your teammates?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they can pass or step back if they feel blocked, without any penalty or judgment. Keep suggestions lighthearted and avoid professions or topics that might invite offensive stereotypes.

## Why It Works
By forcing a rigid, repetitive structure, the brain is freed from worrying about narrative setup and can focus entirely on rapid word association. The high-speed nature of the game bypasses the internal editor, teaching players that their first thought is a valuable gift. Celebrating failures removes the stakes, fostering a supportive team environment where risk-taking is rewarded.

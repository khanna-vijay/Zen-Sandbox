# Circle Crossing

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Find common ground and swap spaces in a fast-paced game of physical and personal connection.

## Overview
Players form a circle with one person in the center who shares a personal truth or characteristic. Anyone who shares that trait must quickly cross the circle to find a new spot, while the center player attempts to claim a vacant space. The player left without a spot becomes the new caller, fostering rapid connection and spatial awareness.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills

**Objective:** To build group mind and peripheral awareness by navigating a shared physical space while practicing vulnerability and unfiltered spontaneity through personal disclosures.

## Setup
An open room with enough space for all players to stand in a large, clear circle. No chairs or props are needed, though spot markers can be used if helpful to define spaces.

## How to Play
1. Have all players stand in a large, clear circle facing inward, with one designated player standing in the center.
2. The player in the center makes a true statement about themselves starting with the phrase, 'Cross the circle if...' (e.g., '...if you have a dog' or '...if you are afraid of heights').
3. Every player in the circle for whom this statement is true must leave their current spot and move across the circle to find a new vacant spot.
4. The player who was in the center must also quickly find and occupy one of the newly vacated spots in the circle.
5. Players moving across the circle cannot simply move to the spot immediately to their left or right; they must cross the central area of the circle.
6. Once everyone has found a spot, one player will be left standing in the center without a space.
7. The new center player immediately offers a new 'Cross the circle if...' statement, and the process repeats.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach players to make eye contact as they cross the circle to avoid physical collisions and build connection.
- Encourage vulnerability: prompt the center player to share genuine, unfiltered truths rather than just surface-level facts (e.g., 'Cross if you felt nervous today' vs. 'Cross if you are wearing blue').
- Pitfall: Players moving too slowly or hesitating to claim a spot. Fix: Remind the group to move with playful urgency while maintaining physical safety.
- Pitfall: The same few people ending up in the center. Fix: If a player is in the center twice in a row, challenge them to make a statement that applies to almost everyone, ensuring they can easily slip into a spot.

## Variations
- Silent Crossing: Play the entire game in complete silence, using only eye contact, gestures, and physical movement to communicate and claim spots.
- Emotional States: Instead of facts, the center player names an emotion (e.g., 'Cross the circle if you feel ecstatic'). Players must cross the circle while physically embodying that emotion.
- Slow-Motion Crossing: Run the game in exaggerated slow motion, forcing players to hyper-focus on balance, peripheral awareness, and safe physical navigation.

## Debrief
- How did you navigate the physical space when multiple people were rushing toward the same spots?
- What did you notice about the group when people shared more vulnerable or personal statements?
- How does this game require us to use our peripheral vision and read the room's energy?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the floor is clear of tripping hazards. Encourage players to walk or move at a pace that is safe for their physical comfort level. For players with mobility considerations, the circle can be wider, or the rule can be modified so that players point and swap places visually rather than physically running.

## Why It Works
This game physically manifests 'Group Mind' by requiring players to constantly monitor the entire circle's movement. It builds peripheral awareness as players must dodge each other in the center, and it fosters vulnerability by encouraging players to reveal personal truths, instantly showing them who shares their experiences.

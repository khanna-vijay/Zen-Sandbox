# Three-Line Scenes

> Build a complete, compelling platform of character, relationship, and setting in exactly three lines.

## Overview
Two players step up to perform a micro-scene consisting of exactly three spoken lines. This rapid-fire exercise strips away the pressure of sustaining a long narrative, forcing players to focus intensely on establishing a clear base reality immediately. The entire group celebrates every completed scene with enthusiastic applause, building a supportive, low-stakes environment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Yes, And; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** World-Building; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Yes, And… sentence games; Endowment-gifting drills
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To master the rapid establishment of a scene's base reality (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where) using economical, high-impact offers and active listening.

## Setup
Players stand in two parallel lines facing each other, creating a central performance space. No props, chairs, or special materials are required.

## How to Play
1. The first player from each line steps into the center performance space to face each other.
2. Player A initiates the scene with Line 1, establishing a clear physical action, location, or relationship detail.
3. Player B receives the offer, validates the premise, and responds with Line 2, adding a specific detail that deepens the context.
4. Player A delivers Line 3, cementing the dynamic, expressing an reaction, or clarifying the objective.
5. Immediately following the third line, the scene is frozen, and the entire group erupts into supportive applause.
6. The active players return to the back of their respective lines, and the next pair immediately steps forward to begin a new scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach physical engagement: Encourage players to begin with physical object work or a clear posture before speaking Line 1. This establishes the 'Where' and 'Character' non-verbally.
- Pitfall: Players trying to tell a complete story with a conflict and resolution. Fix: Remind them that the goal is only to establish the 'platform' or base reality, not to solve a problem.
- Side-coach active gifting: Remind Player B that Line 2 must fully accept and build upon the reality introduced in Line 1, rather than redirecting the scene.
- Pitfall: Asking questions instead of making statements. Fix: Coach players to turn questions into declarations (e.g., instead of 'What are you doing?' use 'You are painting that wall blue.').

## Variations
- C.R.O.W. Focus: Assign a specific element of C.R.O.W. to each line. Line 1 must establish the 'Where', Line 2 the 'Relationship', and Line 3 the 'Objective' or 'Character'.
- Silent Physical Start: Players must engage in five seconds of silent, cooperative physical action before the first line of dialogue is spoken.
- Emotional Shift: Line 1 starts with a clear, distinct emotion, and Line 3 must show a reaction that shifts or intensifies that emotional state.

## Debrief
- How did knowing you only had three lines change the way you crafted your very first offer?
- What non-verbal cues did your partner give that helped you instantly understand the relationship or setting?
- Why is it more satisfying to establish a clear, simple reality than to try to tell a complex story in three lines?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space between the two lines is clear of tripping hazards. Encourage players to use vocal projection and clear physical choices so that all participants, regardless of hearing or visual acuity, can fully engage with the micro-scenes.

## Why It Works
By limiting the exchange to exactly three lines, the game removes the anxiety of 'what happens next' and forces players to focus entirely on 'what is happening right now.' This constraint highlights the power of economy in language and demonstrates how quickly a rich, believable world can be built when both players commit to mutual agreement.

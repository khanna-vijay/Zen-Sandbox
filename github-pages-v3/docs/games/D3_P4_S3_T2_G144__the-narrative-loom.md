# The Story Loom

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Weave tight, escalating storylines by explicitly calling out triggers and mandating their dramatic consequences.

## Overview
A collaborative scene-work game where players explicitly pause the action to identify a pivotal trigger and dictate its mandatory consequence. By taking turns as external narrative architects, players learn to build tight, cause-and-effect storylines. The active players must then immediately resume the scene and organically justify this new dramatic reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Game Identification; Justification; Raising the Stakes; Offer Reception; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; If this is true, what else is true?; Justify the absurd; Stakes-escalation reps; Endowment-acceptance
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To master narrative architecture and the platform/tilt technique by consciously identifying cause-and-effect relationships, raising stakes, and practicing rapid justification of forced narrative shifts.

## Setup
Conducted in a virtual meeting space with cameras on. Two players start on screen to establish the platform, while the remaining players keep their cameras on to act as the Weavers who will call out narrative shifts. No physical props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Begin a virtual scene with two players establishing a stable platform based on a simple suggestion.
2. At any point once the platform is clear, any off-screen player can call out Freeze to pause the action.
3. The player who paused the scene must state a clear Trigger—a specific action, line of dialogue, or discovery that just occurred in the scene.
4. The same pausing player then immediately dictates a mandatory Consequence—a direct, dramatic result of that trigger that must happen next.
5. The active players unfreeze and must immediately play out this consequence, finding a logical and emotional justification to make it feel organic to their characters.
6. After the consequence is established and integrated, another off-screen player calls Freeze to identify a new trigger arising from the previous action and dictates the next escalating consequence.
7. Repeat this cycle three to four times, with each consequence raising the stakes and complicating the characters situation.
8. To conclude, a player calls Final Weave and dictates a consequence that forces the scene toward a dramatic climax or resolution, which the active players then play out to its natural end.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach weavers to make the consequence active: Ensure consequences are instructions for immediate action or emotional shifts, rather than passive background information.
- Watch out for consequences that completely ignore the established platform. Remind weavers that consequences must logically flow from the Trigger.
- Side-coach active players to justify, not question: Encourage active players to accept the forced consequence instantly as absolute truth, rather than spending time arguing about it in character.
- For virtual pacing, have the Weavers use their microphones to call Freeze clearly, ensuring active players hear the cue instantly despite any minor lag.

## Variations
- The Internal Loom: Only the active players in the scene can call the freezes, taking turns to dictate triggers and consequences for each other.
- The Emotional Loom: Focus the consequences entirely on internal shifts, such as forcing a character to feel overwhelming guilt or sudden joy.
- The Multi-Character Loom: Introduce a third player into the scene as a direct consequence of a trigger, expanding the platform dynamically.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your narrative direction suddenly dictated by an outside force? How did you find the justification?
- What made a consequence helpful for advancing the story versus one that felt like it derailed the scene?
- How does explicitly naming the trigger help us recognize the platform and tilt in our everyday, unscripted scenes?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since players are imposing actions and emotional states on one another, establish a pre-game boundary check. Players can use a simple virtual stop gesture or word if an imposed consequence crosses a personal boundary, allowing the weaver to quickly offer an alternative consequence.

## Why It Works
This game works because it externalizes the internal cognitive process of narrative progression. By separating the trigger from the consequence, players learn to see scenes as a series of logical dominoes. It forces players to practice if true, what else is true in a highly structured, low-stakes environment, building the muscle memory needed to do this fluidly in open scenes.

# The Chat-Cut Director

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Shape virtual scenes in real-time using instant text-based side-coaching and sudden narrative pivots.

## Overview
A dynamic virtual-platform game where a designated Director guides two to three active performers through an evolving scene. By calling out sudden pauses and typing immediate, challenging adjustments into the chat, the Director pushes the actors to instantly adapt. Performers must seamlessly integrate these text-based endowments, keeping the virtual audience highly engaged.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Heightening & Exploration; Justification
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; If this is true, what else is true?; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop rapid offer reception and endowment-acceptance in a virtual environment, training players to instantly 'Yes, And' unexpected narrative shifts without breaking character.

## Setup
Conducted on a video conferencing platform with chat functionality. All players start in gallery view. The facilitator designates one player as the Director and two to three players as the active Actors. The facilitator spotlights the active Actors so they are prominent on screen, while the remaining players act as the audience and suggestion pool.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator designates one player as the Director and two to three players as the active Actors, leaving the remaining players as the audience.
2. The facilitator spotlights the active Actors' video feeds so they are the primary focus on screen, while the Director and audience remain in the background.
3. The facilitator asks the audience to type an unusual location and an unexpected relationship into the chat, holding their 'Enter' key until cued.
4. On the facilitator's count of three, everyone presses enter simultaneously to create a rapid waterfall of ideas in the chat.
5. The Director selects one location and one relationship from the chat waterfall, announces them, and calls 'Action!' to begin the scene.
6. At any point during the scene, the Director can verbally call out 'CUT!' to pause the action.
7. Upon hearing 'CUT!', the active Actors must immediately freeze their physical postures and facial expressions on camera.
8. The Director quickly types a specific, concise instruction or endowment into the chat directed at one or more actors.
9. Once the instruction is typed, the Director calls out 'ACTION!', and the Actors must instantly unfreeze and continue the scene, fully accepting and justifying the new endowment.
10. After several interruptions and a satisfying narrative arc, the Director calls 'SCENE!' to conclude the round and roles are rotated.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage Directors to keep their typed instructions short (under ten words) to maintain the scene's momentum and prevent long, awkward pauses.
- If actors break character or look confused while reading the chat, remind them that reading the chat is part of the game; they should maintain their frozen physical posture and integrate the reading process into their character's focus.
- Remind Directors to build on what the actors have already established rather than completely wiping the slate clean; the goal is to heighten, not reset.
- If technical lag causes delayed freezes, instruct actors to freeze the very moment they hear the verbal 'CUT!', regardless of any visual delay they might perceive.

## Variations
- Genre Shift: The Director's typed instructions must always dictate a change in cinematic genre, such as switching to film noir or a soap opera.
- Subtext Reveal: The Director types secret instructions privately to only one actor, forcing the other actor to discover and adapt to the secret endowment live.
- Audience Director: The audience types the 'CUT!' instructions in the chat, and the Director's job is simply to call the verbal cues and select which audience prompt to apply.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your reality suddenly altered mid-sentence, and what helped you accept the new endowment instantly?
- As a Director, how did you balance letting the actors play versus stepping in to guide the narrative?
- How did the visual constraint of the video frame affect how you communicated your physical and emotional shifts?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since players are freezing in place, remind them to choose sustainable physical postures so they do not strain themselves during the typing pauses. If a player has visual accessibility needs, the Director should read the chat instruction aloud instead of relying solely on text.

## Why It Works
This game leverages the unique constraints of video conferencing to isolate the auditory and visual channels. By using the chat for side-coaching, the Director can inject complex endowments without talking over the actors. This forces the performers to practice pure offer reception and endowment-acceptance, as they must instantly justify the new text-based reality within their physical and verbal choices.

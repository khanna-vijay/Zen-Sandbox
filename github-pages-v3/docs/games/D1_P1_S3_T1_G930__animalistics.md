# Animal Instincts

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Transform animal traits into fully realized human characters through physical, vocal, and status choices.

## Overview
In this exercise, players adopt the physical, vocal, and behavioral traits of a specific animal and translate them into a human character. The resulting scene features high-commitment, grounded human interactions driven by these distinct animalistic baselines.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** Commit 100%; The First Thought Is a Gift; Make Your Partner a Genius; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Status Modulation; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Character Walks/Centers; Vocal characterization; High/low-status walks; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** To develop physical commitment, vocal variety, and status modulation by using animal archetypes as a physical center and behavioral guide.

## Setup
A clear playing space where players can move freely. The facilitator prepares a list of diverse animals or gathers suggestions from the group.

## How to Play
1. Assign each player a specific animal suggestion, either openly or privately.
2. Have players stand in the space and briefly explore the literal physical movements and sounds of their assigned animal.
3. Instruct players to gradually stand upright, translating those literal animal movements into a human posture, walk, and physical center.
4. Guide players to discover a human voice that naturally emerges from this physical state, adjusting pitch, tempo, and tone.
5. Establish a mundane, grounded human scenario, such as an office breakroom or a bus stop.
6. Initiate a scene with two to four players, who must interact as human characters while fully committing to their animal-inspired physicalities and vocal patterns.
7. Maintain the base reality of the scene by ensuring characters do not explicitly mention animals or acknowledge their physical quirks as unusual.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Translate, don't imitate! You are a human who moves like a snake, not a snake wearing a suit.'
- If players drop their physical commitment when speaking, pause the scene and have them reset their physical center before continuing.
- Coaching cue: 'How does this animal's natural status translate? Is a lion high status? Is a mouse low status? Let that dictate your relationship.'
- Pitfall: Making the scene about the animal traits. Fix: Remind players to focus on the base reality of the scene and treat their physicalities as normal personality traits.

## Variations
- Secret Totems: Players draw animal cards secretly, and the audience must guess the animal based purely on their human behavior.
- Status Shift: Players must play against their animal's natural status, such as a low-status lion interacting with a high-status mouse.
- Environmental Shift: Change the physical environment mid-scene to see how the animalistic physicalities adapt to new spaces.

## Debrief
- How did starting with a physical animal center change your vocal choices and status?
- What did it feel like to commit 100% to a physical choice even when the scene's dialogue was completely mundane?
- How did your partner's physical presence help you discover your own character's status and relationship?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players adapt physical movements to their own comfort and mobility levels; there is no requirement to go to the floor or perform high-impact movements.

## Why It Works
By bypassing intellectual character creation and starting with a physical, animalistic gift, players instantly bypass overthinking. It forces 100% commitment to a physical center, which naturally generates unique vocal qualities and status dynamics, establishing a rich base reality.

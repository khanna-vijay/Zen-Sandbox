# Word-Bound Entrances

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Players must instantly enter or exit the scene whenever their secret trigger word is spoken.

## Overview
A dynamic scene-work game where players are assigned unique trigger words. Whenever a player's word is spoken by anyone on stage, that player must immediately toggle their presence in the scene, requiring them to instantly justify their sudden movement within the established reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Make Your Partner a Genius; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Active Listening; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Walk-ons
- **Focus:** mixed

**Objective:** Develops rapid justification skills, active listening, and support work by forcing players to ground sudden, arbitrary entrances and exits in a believable base reality.

## Setup
A group of 3 to 8 players stands in a semi-circle. The facilitator assigns one distinct, common word to each player. Two players start on stage to begin a scene, while the remaining players wait off-stage as active listeners.

## How to Play
1. Assign each player a unique, common word (such as 'water', 'keys', or 'later') as their personal trigger.
2. Two players step into the performance space and begin a scene, establishing a clear, grounded base reality with a defined relationship and environment.
3. The remaining players stand off-stage, listening intently to every word spoken in the scene.
4. If a player's trigger word is spoken by anyone currently in the scene, that player must immediately change their location: if they are off-stage, they must enter; if they are on-stage, they must exit.
5. The entering or exiting player must immediately justify their movement so it fits seamlessly into the scene's logic, rather than breaking the reality.
6. The players already on stage must support the transition, helping to justify why their partner suddenly ran out or why a new person just arrived.
7. The scene continues with players constantly cycling in and out as their trigger words are naturally woven into the dialogue.
8. The game concludes after a few minutes or when the scene reaches a natural, satisfying climax.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't just walk off; tell us why you are leaving!' Encourage immediate, physical justification.
- Coaching cue: 'Support the entrance! Who is this new person and why are they here?' Help your partner look like a genius.
- Pitfall: Players saying their own trigger words on purpose just to force themselves in or out. Fix: Challenge players to only react when others say their word.
- Pitfall: Breaking character or freezing when a word is said. Fix: Remind players that any movement can be justified; even a sudden gasp and run can be explained by remembering a forgotten appointment.

## Variations
- Secret Triggers: The players do not know each other's trigger words, forcing them to listen even more closely to every single word spoken.
- Emotional Triggers: Instead of physical entry/exit, the trigger word causes a sudden shift in emotional state that must be justified.
- Physical Actions: Instead of entering/exiting, the trigger word forces a specific physical action (like sitting down or saluting) that must be justified.

## Debrief
- How did having to justify a sudden entrance or exit affect the depth of your scene's base reality?
- What strategies did you use to make an absurd, sudden exit feel grounded and believable?
- How did active listening change when you were hunting for specific trigger words versus listening for narrative offers?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the off-stage area is clear of tripping hazards, as players may need to enter or exit quickly. Remind players to move safely and avoid running blindly into the playing space.

## Why It Works
This game forces players to prioritize justification over planning. By introducing an arbitrary, external constraint (the trigger word), players cannot pre-plan their entrances or exits. They must accept the sudden shift as an absolute truth and use the scene's established base reality to make the absurd transition make perfect sense.

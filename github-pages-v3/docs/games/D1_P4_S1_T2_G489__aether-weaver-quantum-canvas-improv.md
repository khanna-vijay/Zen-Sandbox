# Rapid Chat Roulette

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Turn a flood of text suggestions into instant, unfiltered solo scene initiations.

## Overview
A high-energy virtual warm-up where players flood the video-conferencing chat with rapid-fire suggestions based on a prompt. One player is spotlighted, instantly grabs the first text offer that catches their eye, and immediately launches into a spontaneous monologue.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Yes, And; Start in the Middle
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Offer Reception; World-Building; Suggestion Deconstruction (A-to-C)
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Endowment-acceptance; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Premise brainstorm rounds
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To train unfiltered spontaneity and the ability to treat the very first thought as a gift, bypassing the analytical mind to initiate scenes instantly from unexpected text prompts.

## Setup
A virtual meeting platform with the public chat window open for all participants. Everyone should be in Gallery View with cameras on. No physical props or materials are required.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator introduces a broad, imaginative category, such as 'Unusual items in a time traveler's pocket' or 'Jobs that shouldn't exist.'
2. The facilitator starts a 60-second timer and calls out 'Go!' signaling all players to type as many short, punchy ideas as possible into the public chat.
3. Players type continuously without editing, filtering, or reading others' posts, creating a rapid scroll of text.
4. When the timer rings, the facilitator calls 'Freeze!' and all typing must immediately stop.
5. The facilitator calls out one player's name to be the active performer and spotlights their video feed.
6. The chosen player has exactly three seconds to scan the chat, read the first suggestion that catches their eye aloud, and immediately launch into a 30-to-60-second character monologue.
7. The performer must start in the middle of the action, fully committing to the chosen suggestion as absolute truth without explaining how they got there.
8. After 45 seconds, the facilitator gently calls 'Scene!' and selects the next player to scan the existing chat and perform.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Don't hunt for the perfect suggestion! Grab the very first word your eyes land on—that is your gift.'
- Coaching cue: 'Start in the middle of the scene. Don't spend time setting up the premise; live in it immediately.'
- Pitfall: Players scrolling endlessly to find a 'good' prompt. Fix: Enforce a strict 3-second countdown for the player to read their chosen prompt aloud.
- Pitfall: Players freezing or overthinking. Fix: If a player gets stuck, have them say 'Pass!' and immediately prompt them with a random word from the chat yourself to keep the momentum high.

## Variations
- Two-Player Tag: The first player starts the monologue, and a second player can unmute and jump in to turn it into a duo scene based on the same chat prompt.
- A-to-C Association: Instead of using the exact word from the chat, the player must read a word, instantly think of a second-degree association (A-to-C), and base their monologue on that new concept.
- Emotional Shift: The facilitator types emotional states into the chat mid-monologue, and the performer must instantly pivot their character's emotional state to match.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to grab the first suggestion your eyes landed on rather than searching for the 'best' one?
- What strategies helped you start in the middle of the scene instead of explaining the premise?
- How does having a massive wall of text options lower the pressure of coming up with a perfect idea?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they can skip their turn by saying 'Pass' with zero penalty or explanation needed. Remind players to keep chat suggestions PG-13 and respectful of all participants.

## Why It Works
By flooding the chat, we remove the pressure of generating a single 'perfect' suggestion. The rapid transition from reading a text prompt to speaking forces the brain to bypass its internal editor, directly exercising the 'first thought is a gift' principle. It leverages the virtual medium's text latency to create a parallel, non-verbal brainstorming space that feeds the verbal performance.

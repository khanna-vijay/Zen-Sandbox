# Creature Chorus

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Pass a high-energy chain of physical and vocal animal signals across a circle without breaking rhythm.

## Overview
Players stand in a circle, each adopting a unique animal persona with a distinct sound and physical gesture. By performing their own signal and then another player's, they pass an invisible current of energy around the room, requiring intense focus and rapid physical commitment.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Commit 100%; Make Your Partner a Genius
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Pacing & Rhythm; Physicality & Space Work; Vocal Craft; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Thread-tracking drills; Timing exercises; Vocal characterization
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops peripheral awareness, active listening, and group mind by forcing players to track multiple physical and vocal threads simultaneously while maintaining a high-energy rhythm.

## Setup
Form a standing circle with 5 to 15 players in an open space. Ensure there is enough room for players to make expressive physical gestures safely.

## How to Play
1. Have each player select a specific animal and design a distinct, repeatable physical gesture and vocal sound to represent it.
2. Go around the circle and have each player demonstrate their animal's sound and gesture twice so the entire group can observe and memorize them.
3. Select one player to initiate the game. This player activates the chain by performing their own animal signal (the 'source') followed immediately by the signal of another player in the circle (the 'target').
4. The targeted player must immediately recognize their signal, perform their own animal signal to accept the pass, and then perform a different player's signal to redirect the energy.
5. Continue this passing pattern rapidly across the circle, maintaining a steady, driving rhythm without pauses or hesitation.
6. If a player hesitates, performs the wrong gesture, or misses their cue, they step back slightly from the circle to become the 'hype chorus,' cheering on the remaining players and keeping the energy high.
7. The game continues, accelerating in speed, until only two players remain, who then engage in a rapid-fire final duel.

## Facilitation Notes
- Encourage players to make eye contact with their target when sending the signal to ensure clear communication.
- Side-coach players to commit 100% to the physical and vocal scale of their chosen animal; half-hearted gestures are harder for the group to recognize.
- If the rhythm slows down, challenge the group to increase the tempo, reminding them that making a confident mistake is better than hesitating.
- Pitfall: Players looking only at the person next to them. Fix: Remind them to use soft focus and peripheral vision to monitor the entire circle.

## Variations
- Silent Kingdom: Play the entire game using only the physical gestures, removing all vocal sounds to heighten visual tracking.
- Evolutionary Shift: After three successful passes, players must swap animals with the person they just sent the signal to, forcing them to learn new patterns on the fly.
- The Echo Chamber: When a signal is passed, the two players standing adjacent to the target must perform a quiet 'echo' of the gesture to support their teammate.

## Debrief
- How did your focus shift as the speed of the passes increased?
- What did it feel like to rely on your peripheral vision to catch your cue?
- How does committing fully to a silly physical choice help your teammates succeed?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players choose physical gestures that are safe for their personal mobility levels. If a player has vocal or physical limitations, encourage them to use simple, comfortable alternatives (like a distinct clap or a specific facial expression) that the group can easily mirror.

## Why It Works
This game builds group mind by establishing a shared physical vocabulary that must be tracked in real-time. Because players cannot predict when they will receive the signal, they must maintain constant, active engagement with the entire circle, training their peripheral awareness and response times.

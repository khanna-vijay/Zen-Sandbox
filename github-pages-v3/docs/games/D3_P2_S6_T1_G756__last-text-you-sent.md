# Last Text Sent

![Last Text Sent — infographic](img/D3_P2_S6_T1_G756__last-text-you-sent.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Weave real, out-of-context text messages seamlessly into a grounded scene through instant justification.

## Overview
Two players perform a scene where they must periodically read aloud real, random text messages collected from the audience. The challenge is to treat these bizarre, modern, or mundane texts as completely logical, in-character statements, integrating them into the scene's base reality.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Base Reality First; Yes, And; The Audience Is the Final Scene Partner
- **Skill(s):** Justification; Offer Reception; Room Reading
- **Technique(s):** Justify the absurd; Endowment-acceptance; Reading the suggestion's intent
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** Develops the skill of justification, offer reception, and establishing a strong base reality before introducing absurd elements.

## Setup
Before the scene begins, ask audience members to write down the absolute last text message they sent on slips of paper. Fold these slips and scatter them face-down across the stage floor. Two players stand ready to perform.

## How to Play
1. Collect the written text messages from the audience on slips of paper and scatter them across the performance space floor, blank side up.
2. Ask the audience for a simple, grounded relationship and location to establish a clear base reality.
3. Begin the scene by establishing the normal routine, environment, and relationship before picking up any papers.
4. At moments of high stakes or natural pauses, a player picks up a slip of paper, unfolds it, and reads the text message aloud as their next line of dialogue.
5. The reading player must deliver the text with absolute commitment, matching their character's current emotional state and vocal tone.
6. The other player must immediately 'yes-and' the line, justifying why that specific statement makes perfect sense in their current situation.
7. Continue the scene, alternating who picks up slips, ensuring that the base reality remains intact and the relationship evolves despite the bizarre interruptions.
8. Conclude the scene once a satisfying narrative resolution or a peak comedic justification is reached.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Establish the room first! Don't rush to the papers.' This ensures a strong base reality is built before the absurdity begins.
- Coaching cue: 'Justify the emotion, not just the words. Why would they say that now?'
- Pitfall: Players treat the text as an out-of-character joke. Fix: Remind them to maintain high stakes and treat the text as a serious, literal statement within their world.
- Pitfall: Picking up papers too quickly, turning the scene into a rapid-fire reading exercise. Fix: Coach them to leave space between papers to explore the consequences of the last line.

## Variations
- Live Phone Pull: Instead of paper slips, the host borrows a willing audience member's phone (unlocked to their messaging app) and hands it to a player, who scrolls and reads live texts.
- The Silent Receiver: Only one player has access to the texts (representing their character receiving actual messages), while the other player must react to these sudden, weird updates.

## Debrief
- How did establishing a strong base reality early on make it easier to justify the weird text messages later?
- What strategies did you use to make a completely unrelated text message sound natural to your character?
- How does treating an absurd line with high stakes and emotional commitment change the audience's reaction compared to treating it as a joke?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure audience members know they should only share texts they are comfortable having read aloud. Avoid collecting highly sensitive, private, or identifying information. If using the live phone variation, explicitly ask for consent and allow the audience member to select the thread or opt-out at any time.

## Why It Works
By forcing players to treat random, modern text messages as canonical dialogue, the game strips away the ability to pre-plan. It demands deep offer reception and immediate justification. When players prioritize the base reality first, the contrast between the mundane setup and the absurd text creates a highly satisfying comedic tension that is resolved through clever justification.

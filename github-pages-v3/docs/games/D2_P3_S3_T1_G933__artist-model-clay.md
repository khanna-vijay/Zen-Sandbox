# The Silent Sculptor

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Recreate a hidden physical pose using only silent, touch-free guidance and deep physical empathy.

## Overview
In this three-player physical communication game, one player acts as a block of clay, another as a hidden model, and the third as the sculptor. Without speaking, touching, or demonstrating the pose directly, the sculptor must guide the clay to mirror the model's exact posture. It builds intense focus, non-verbal connection, and physical awareness.

## Setup
Divide players into groups of three. Each group needs enough space to stand comfortably. No props are required.

## How to Play
1. Assign roles within each trio: one Model, one Clay, and one Sculptor.
2. Position the players: The Clay stands facing the Sculptor. The Model stands directly behind the Clay, facing the same direction as the Clay, so the Clay cannot see the Model but the Sculptor can see both.
3. The Model strikes a distinct, static physical pose and holds it completely still.
4. The Sculptor's goal is to manipulate the Clay's body to match the Model's pose exactly, under strict constraints: no speaking, no physical touch, and no acting out the pose themselves.
5. The Sculptor must guide the Clay using indirect non-verbal cues, such as pointing to specific joints, gesturing directions of movement, or using facial expressions to indicate 'more' or 'less.'
6. The Clay remains relaxed and highly receptive, responding to the Sculptor's gestures with slow, deliberate adjustments, trying to interpret the Sculptor's intent without guessing the final shape.
7. Once the Sculptor believes the Clay matches the Model, they clap once to freeze the scene. The Model then steps forward to compare the 'sculpture' to their original pose.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Sculptors, focus on one limb at a time. Start with the spine or base, then move to the arms and head.'
- Coaching cue: 'Clay, don't try to guess the final pose. Just react to the immediate physical guidance of your sculptor.'
- Pitfall: Sculptors often default to acting out the pose themselves. Fix: Remind them they cannot mirror the pose; they must act as a guide, pointing to the Clay's body parts and gesturing the direction of movement.
- Pitfall: Clay moving too fast or anticipating. Fix: Encourage the Clay to move like heavy, slow-moving material that only shifts when prompted.

## Variations
- Time Attack: Give the Sculptor exactly 60 seconds to complete the sculpture.
- Emotional Clay: The Model holds an emotional expression or attitude rather than just a physical shape, requiring the Sculptor to convey feeling through physical alignment.
- Double Sculptor: Play in groups of four with two Clays and two Sculptors, creating a two-person statue.

## Debrief
- As the Clay, how did it feel to surrender control and rely entirely on your partner's non-verbal guidance?
- As the Sculptor, what strategies did you find most effective for communicating precise physical adjustments without touch or speech?
- How does this exercise relate to 'making your partner look good' on stage?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players are mindful of physical comfort and flexibility when striking poses. Since this is a touch-free game, it is highly accessible for players with physical touch boundaries, but players with mobility limitations should adapt poses to their comfort level.

## Why It Works
By removing touch and speech, the game forces players to develop a shared physical vocabulary. The Sculptor must pay exquisite attention to the Clay's micro-movements, while the Clay must practice deep physical empathy and trust, embodying the principle of making their partner look like a genius through cooperative physical adjustment.

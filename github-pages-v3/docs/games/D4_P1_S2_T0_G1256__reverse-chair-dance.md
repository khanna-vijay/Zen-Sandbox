# Collective Chairs

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> A cooperative twist on musical chairs where everyone wins by lifting each other up.

## Overview
A high-energy, physical icebreaker that flips the classic competitive game of musical chairs on its head. Instead of players being eliminated when chairs are removed, the entire group must work together to ensure every single person is off the floor using the remaining chairs. As the chairs dwindle, the physical puzzle demands deep trust, close contact, and spontaneous physical support.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Commit 100%; Consent & Boundaries

- **Technique(s):** Negotiating physical contact

**Objective:** To build group mind, physical trust, and active support work by shifting the focus from individual survival to collective success through physical cooperation.

## Setup
Arrange a circle of sturdy, armless chairs in the center of the room, using one fewer chair than the total number of players. Set up a music player with upbeat, high-energy tracks. Ensure the surrounding space is clear of tripping hazards.

## How to Play
1. Instruct all players to stand in a circle around the chairs.
2. Start playing the music and direct the players to move or dance around the outside of the chair circle.
3. Pause the music unexpectedly; players must immediately find a way to get their feet completely off the floor using the available chairs.
4. In the first round, one player will find themselves without a chair and must be supported by others (e.g., sitting on a lap or being held up) so that no feet touch the ground.
5. Once the group successfully gets everyone off the floor and holds the position for three seconds, congratulate them and have them step down.
6. Remove one chair from the circle, leaving two fewer chairs than players, and restart the music.
7. Repeat the process, removing one chair each round, forcing the group to find increasingly creative, cooperative, and physically supportive ways to share the remaining chairs.
8. Continue until the group is attempting to balance the entire ensemble on just one or two sturdy chairs, emphasizing that no one is eliminated and everyone must succeed together.

## Facilitation Notes
- Ensure the chairs used are highly durable and stable; avoid folding chairs or plastic chairs that might buckle under collective weight.
- Step back and let the group self-organize; do not direct their physical solutions, as the magic of group mind lies in their emergent problem-solving.
- If the group struggles, encourage them to use side-coaching cues like 'breathe together' or 'find your center of gravity' rather than telling them where to step.
- Pitfall: Players rushing and colliding. Fix: Remind players that this is a cooperative puzzle, not a race, and safety and control are paramount.

## Variations
- Silent Support: Play the entire game in complete silence, forcing players to rely entirely on non-verbal cues, eye contact, and physical weight-sharing adjustments.
- The Floor is Lava: Introduce a rule where players cannot touch the floor at all once the music stops, meaning they must transition directly from dancing to the chairs without stepping on the 'lava' floor (requires tight spacing).

## Debrief
- How did our communication shift as the physical space became more limited?
- What did it feel like to support someone else's weight, or to fully trust others to support yours?
- How does this game redefine what it means to 'win' or 'survive' in an ensemble setting?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game requires close physical contact and weight-bearing, establish a clear 'opt-in' boundary beforehand. Players can choose to be 'anchors' (staying low/stable), 'climbers', or can opt to play as 'spotters' who stand nearby to ensure no one falls, keeping them fully included without forced physical contact. Always check that chairs are structurally sound before playing.

## Why It Works
By reversing the competitive mechanic of elimination, the game instantly aligns the group's goals. It forces players to bypass intellectual planning and rely on physical intuition, weight distribution, and immediate physical support. This rapid, tactile problem-solving bypasses social barriers and fosters an immediate sense of shared vulnerability and trust.

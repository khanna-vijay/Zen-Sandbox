# Echoing Edits

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Sculpt a collaborative virtual story in real-time by dynamically renaming yourself to offer narrative twists.

## Overview
A virtual-native storytelling game where one spotlighted narrator weaves a tale while the rest of the group acts as silent, real-time editors. By dynamically changing their display names to short, evocative words or phrases, the silent players feed the narrator unexpected details that must be instantly accepted and integrated. It transforms standard video-conferencing features into a seamless, low-latency co-creation tool.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Serve the Story; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Narrative Architecture; Support Work
- **Technique(s):** Yes, And… sentence games; Endowment-gifting drills; Story Spine
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop rapid offer reception and seamless 'Yes, And' integration under cognitive load, while training the group to offer high-value, low-friction narrative gifts asynchronously.

## Setup
Conducted on a virtual video-conferencing platform with Gallery View enabled. All participants must have permission to rename themselves. The facilitator acts as the host to manage spotlighting. Everyone starts with their actual first names and their microphones muted, except for the designated starting narrator.

## How to Play
1. Begin with a brief technical warm-up: have all players practice renaming themselves to a random object and back to their real name to ensure technical fluency.
2. The facilitator spotlights one player to be the active Narrator and unmutes them; all other players remain muted in Gallery View as the Echoing Editors.
3. The Narrator begins telling an original, linear story, establishing a clear platform, characters, and setting.
4. As the story unfolds, the Echoing Editors listen actively and silently suggest narrative details, twists, or adjectives by changing their display names to a short phrase of three words or fewer.
5. The Narrator must continuously scan the Gallery View, select at least one renamed suggestion during their turn, and seamlessly integrate it into the narrative as an absolute truth.
6. Once an Editor's suggestion is integrated, that Editor immediately changes their display name back to their real name to clear the visual canvas.
7. Editors can use virtual reactions to upvote another editor's name suggestion, signaling to the Narrator that it is a crowd favorite.
8. Every 60 to 90 seconds, the facilitator shifts the spotlight to a new player, who must immediately unmute and continue the story from the exact point it was left, while the previous narrator mutes and joins the editors.

## Facilitation Notes
- Ensure strict muting: Keep all non-narrating players muted to eliminate background noise and audio lag, keeping the focus entirely on the visual text inputs.
- Coaching cue: If the Narrator gets stuck or ignores the screen, call out 'Look to your editors!' to prompt visual scanning.
- Pitfall: Editors writing long, complex sentences as names. Fix: Remind players that suggestions must be three words or fewer to keep the narrator's cognitive load manageable.
- Pitfall: The narrator trying to integrate every single name on screen. Fix: Coach them to pick just one or two high-quality offers that serve the story, rather than rushing to check every box.

## Variations
- Genre Shift: The facilitator periodically types a new genre into the chat, and the editors must tailor their name-change suggestions to fit that style.
- Blind Integration: The narrator closes their eyes for 10 seconds while telling the story, opens them, and must immediately integrate the first renamed player they see.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have your narrative direction instantly altered by a silent visual cue?
- What made a display-name suggestion easy to integrate versus difficult to integrate?
- How did the asynchronous nature of the edits help prevent the usual chaos of online group storytelling?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players who may have difficulty typing quickly or navigating the interface can participate by using the chat window or pre-preparing a list of words they can copy-paste. Remind players to keep suggestions accessible and avoid flashing or rapid renaming that might cause visual fatigue.

## Why It Works
By separating the verbal channel (the narrator) from the visual channel (the editors), the game bypasses the audio latency and overlap issues inherent to virtual platforms. It forces the narrator to practice 'Yes, And' in its purest form—taking an external, unprompted visual offer and instantly treating it as a narrative reality—while keeping the entire group highly engaged as active, silent writers.

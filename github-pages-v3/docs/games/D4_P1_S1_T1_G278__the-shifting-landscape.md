# Shifting Landscapes

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Collaboratively build and seamlessly transform physical environments using only non-verbal movement and group intuition.

## Overview
A physical, non-verbal ensemble game where players co-create a detailed environment through mime, then collectively transform it. One player initiates a physical shift in an object's properties, prompting the entire group to instantly adapt their physical reality. It builds a deep, unspoken connection and a shared physical vocabulary.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D4 — The Ensemble
- **Principle(s):** Group Mind; Follow the Follower; Serve the Piece; Commit 100%; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Peripheral Awareness; Support Work; Suggestion Deconstruction (A-to-C); Pacing & Rhythm; Thematic Synthesis; Physicality & Space Work; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Stage-picture exercises; Playing architecture/objects; A-to-C drills; Timing exercises; Object work
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop heightened peripheral awareness and group mind by physically committing to a shared space and executing seamless, non-verbal A-to-C environmental transitions.

## Setup
A clear, open playing space with no physical props. Four to seven players stand in a neutral circle or semi-circle, ready to step into the active playing area.

## How to Play
1. Begin with all players standing in a neutral, focused state around the perimeter of the playing space.
2. One player steps into the space and initiates a single, clear physical action that establishes a specific environmental element, such as climbing a tree or sitting on a low bench.
3. Without speaking, other players enter the space one by one to add complementary elements or interact with the established environment, ensuring every physical choice supports the existing reality.
4. Once a cohesive, multi-layered environment is fully established, the group interacts within this space, solidifying their collective agreement through physical commitment.
5. At any moment, any player may initiate a Shift by non-verbally altering the physical properties of an existing element, such as a solid wall beginning to melt or a heavy lever becoming weightless.
6. The rest of the ensemble must immediately observe this change through peripheral vision, interpret the new physical reality, and instantly adapt their own physical behavior to match the transformation.
7. The group continues this cycle of building, establishing, and shifting, allowing the environment to organically evolve through several distinct iterations.
8. The facilitator calls freeze or scene to conclude the exercise after several successful transformations or when a natural narrative arc concludes.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: Keep your eyes wide. Watch the entire space, not just the person next to you.
- Side-coaching cue: Commit fully to the weight and resistance of the objects. If it is heavy, let us see it in your muscles.
- Pitfall: Players make shifts that are too subtle or fast to be noticed. Fix: Coach players to make their initial shift slow, deliberate, and physically exaggerated so the ensemble can register the change.
- Pitfall: The environment becomes chaotic with multiple conflicting shifts. Fix: Remind players of Follow the Follower—if someone else initiates a shift first, yield your own idea and support theirs immediately.

## Variations
- Atmospheric Shift: The facilitator calls out a shifting emotional tone, such as from playful to suspenseful, which players must manifest through their physical interactions with the environment.
- Soundscape Integration: Players can add abstract, non-verbal vocalizations like drips, hums, or mechanical whirs that transform alongside the physical environment.
- Fixed Positions: Players must remain in their physical locations on stage, forcing them to transform the environment entirely through how they manipulate the space from where they stand.

## Debrief
- How did you know when a shift was happening without anyone speaking?
- What did it feel like to let go of your own environmental idea to support someone else's transformation?
- How did peripheral vision and physical commitment help maintain the illusion of the space?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the playing space is clear of tripping hazards. Encourage players to adapt physical movements to their own comfort and mobility levels; physical commitment does not require high-impact exertion.

## Why It Works
By removing verbal communication, players are forced to rely entirely on visual tracking and physical empathy. The Shift mechanic trains the A-to-C mental leap physically, teaching players to accept a new reality instantly and find their place within it without hesitation.

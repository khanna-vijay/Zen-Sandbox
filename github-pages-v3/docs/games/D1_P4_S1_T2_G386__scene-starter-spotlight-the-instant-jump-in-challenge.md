# Spotlight Roulette

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Banish overthinking and leap into action the exact second the virtual spotlight hits you.

## Overview
A high-energy, rapid-fire virtual drill where a facilitator dynamically shifts the video spotlight among muted participants. The moment a player is spotlighted, they must instantly unmute and deliver a 10-to-15-second character monologue or scene starter based on a single broad theme. It is an exhilarating exercise in trust, immediacy, and bypassing the inner editor.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Commit 100%; Start in the Middle
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; World-Building; Peripheral Awareness; Stage Presence & Clarity
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Make the choice readable
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To cultivate unfiltered spontaneity and immediate commitment by training players to treat their very first impulse as a gift, bypassing self-censorship in a virtual environment.

## Setup
Conducted on a video conferencing platform supporting a host-controlled spotlight feature. All participants keep their cameras on, set their view to Gallery View, and mute their microphones. The facilitator acts as the technical director, ready to manually toggle the spotlight.

## How to Play
1. The facilitator introduces a broad, open-ended prompt or scenario (e.g., 'You just discovered something highly unusual in your attic' or 'You are waiting for a train that is three hours late').
2. All players remain in Gallery View with their cameras on and microphones muted, maintaining high physical presence and focus.
3. The facilitator randomly and unpredictably spotlights one participant's video feed for everyone to see.
4. The spotlighted player must instantly unmute and immediately begin speaking in character, starting in the middle of an action or thought without any introductory setup.
5. The player delivers a high-commitment monologue, reaction, or scene starter for 10 to 15 seconds, inspired by the initial prompt.
6. The player's contribution does not need to connect to or build upon what the previous player said; each spotlight is a completely fresh initiation.
7. After 10 to 15 seconds, the facilitator abruptly removes the spotlight and shifts it to a new random player, who must instantly unmute and begin their own initiation.
8. Non-spotlighted players remain muted but actively support the active player by reacting physically and expressively on camera.
9. The cycle continues rapidly for 3 to 5 minutes, ensuring every participant gets spotlighted at least once.

## Facilitation Notes
- Technical Prep: Practice the platform's spotlight hotkeys or interface beforehand to ensure transitions are seamless and rapid, minimizing dead air.
- Side-Coaching Cue: If a player hesitates, call out 'First thought!' or 'Start speaking before you know what you are saying!' to nudge them past their inner editor.
- Pitfall & Fix: Players trying to write a perfect story. Remind them that they only have 15 seconds; they should start in the middle of a high-stakes moment rather than setting up a premise.
- Audio Management: Encourage players to keep their hand near their mute shortcut (like holding the spacebar) to eliminate technical lag when their spotlight begins.

## Variations
- Emotional Shifts: The facilitator calls out a new emotion or physical state (e.g., 'Now you are freezing cold!' or 'Now you are ecstatic!') right before spotlighting the next player.
- Tag-Team Spotlight: The facilitator spotlights two players simultaneously, who must instantly engage in a high-stakes, two-person scene starter for 15 seconds before the spotlight shifts.
- Object Work Focus: Players must immediately begin their spotlighted turn by interacting with an imaginary physical object in their space, establishing the environment before speaking.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to speak before your brain had time to plan a 'good' idea?
- What physical or vocal shifts did you notice when you committed 100% to your very first impulse?
- How did watching your peers' expressive, silent reactions in gallery view affect your confidence when spotlighted?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players know they can use a simple non-verbal gesture (like a brief wave or headshake) if they experience a sudden technical glitch or need to pass, allowing the facilitator to smoothly spotlight the next person without shame.

## Why It Works
By removing the time needed to plan and eliminating the expectation of narrative continuity, this game isolates the act of initiation. The sudden visual cue of the spotlight triggers an adrenaline-fueled leap into action, forcing the brain to rely on subconscious, unfiltered impulses. This directly reinforces the principle that the first thought is a gift, building deep confidence in solo stage presence.

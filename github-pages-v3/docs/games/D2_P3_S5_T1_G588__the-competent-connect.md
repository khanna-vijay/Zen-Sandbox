# The Genius Assumption

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Elevate every partner choice into an act of absolute brilliance through strict, supportive dialogue constraints.

## Overview
A two-player scene-work exercise where players are restricted to responding in ways that frame their partner's actions as masterstrokes of genius. By removing all doubt, hesitation, and negation, players experience a radical shift toward total support and collaborative elevation. The resulting scenes are filled with high-status, highly competent characters who treat each other with profound respect.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Assume Competence; Fail Joyfully
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Status Modulation; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Justification
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment-acceptance; Give them the answer; Last Word Response; Justify the absurd
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To train improvisers in active gifting and radical agreement by forcing them to justify their partner's choices as brilliant, thereby building deep trust and eliminating defensive scene-blocking.

## Setup
Two players stand in the performance space. The facilitator provides a simple relationship (e.g., business partners, siblings) and a location (e.g., a research lab, a bakery). No props or physical materials are required.

## How to Play
1. Two players step forward to begin a scene, establishing a basic relationship and location provided by the facilitator.
2. Player A initiates the scene with a simple action, statement, or choice (e.g., 'I've decided to bake this bread using sea water').
3. Player B must respond using only one of two specific structures: a Competence-Justifying Affirmation or a Competence-Driven Question.
4. To deliver a Competence-Justifying Affirmation, Player B must enthusiastically agree and explain the hidden brilliance or strategic wisdom behind Player A's choice.
5. To deliver a Competence-Driven Question, Player B must ask a question that assumes Player A has a master plan, inquiring about the deeper genius of their strategy without any hint of skepticism.
6. Player A then receives this gift, accepts their newly endowed genius, and makes a new statement or action, which Player B must again validate using one of the two structures.
7. The players alternate, ensuring that every single line of dialogue either explicitly praises the partner's wisdom or asks them to elaborate on their brilliant reasoning.
8. The scene continues for 3 to 5 minutes, maintaining a high-status, mutually supportive dynamic throughout.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't play skeptical! If they spill the coffee, assume it was a calculated move to test the floor's absorption rate.'
- Pitfall: Players asking passive-aggressive or doubting questions (e.g., 'Why would you do something so foolish?'). Fix: Pause the scene and have the player rephrase the question to assume absolute competence (e.g., 'What secret benefit does this apparent mistake bring to our mission?').
- Side-coaching cue: 'Give them the answer! Don't make them defend themselves; explain to them why they are brilliant before they even have to think about it.'
- Pitfall: Over-intellectualizing and slowing down the scene's pacing. Fix: Encourage players to make bold, immediate justifications, embracing the first positive explanation that pops into their head.

## Variations
- The Silent Genius: One player is completely silent, performing physical actions, while the speaking player must continuously narrate and justify those physical choices as acts of absolute brilliance.
- High-Stakes Crisis: Run the same exercise but set the scene during an active, fast-paced emergency (e.g., a failing spaceship), forcing rapid-fire competence gifting under pressure.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to have every single one of your choices treated as an act of genius?
- What was the hardest part about reframing seemingly 'bad' choices or mistakes as brilliant strategies?
- How did this constraint affect the level of trust and connection you felt with your partner?
- In what ways did this exercise change how you modulate status during a scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure players understand that 'assuming competence' does not mean accepting physical danger or boundary-crossing behavior. If a partner makes an offer that crosses a personal boundary, players retain the right to pause the scene or step out, prioritizing personal safety over the game's constraints.

## Why It Works
By stripping away the option to doubt, deny, or play low-status victimhood, this game forces players to actively look for the value in their partner's offers. It transforms the 'Yes, And' principle from a passive agreement into an active, status-elevating gift, proving that any theatrical choice can be made brilliant through committed justification.

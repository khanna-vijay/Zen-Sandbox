# The Symbiotic Pulse

![The Symbiotic Pulse — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T1_G006__the-symbiotic-pulse.jpg){ .game-hero }

> Tune into your partner's unspoken shifts to co-create a deep, subtextual relationship.

## Overview
Two players engage in a low-dialogue, high-attunement scene driven by secret relational agendas. By communicating through micro-movements, breath, and subtle status shifts, partners learn to read and influence each other's emotional states. The result is a deeply connected, silent dialogue where the unspoken relationship is the primary story.

- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** To develop hyper-attuned active listening and precise status modulation by responding to a partner's non-verbal cues while pursuing a secret interpersonal goal.

## Setup
Two players stand facing each other, approximately three to five feet apart, in a neutral stance. The facilitator prepares a list of simple, low-action scenarios (e.g., waiting for a delayed train, standing in an elevator, watching a distant fire) and a set of secret relational agendas written on slips of paper or whispered privately.

## How to Play
1. Assign two players to the performance space and secretly give each player a unique relational agenda, such as making their partner feel protected, making them feel self-doubt, or making them feel like an equal.
2. Provide a simple, low-stakes shared scenario that establishes a basic relationship but contains no inherent plot or high-energy action.
3. Instruct Player A to initiate the scene with a subtle, non-verbal physical offer—such as a shift in weight, a change in breathing pattern, or a micro-expression—that reflects their internal state and aligns with their secret agenda.
4. Direct Player B to actively observe this offer, absorb its emotional and status implications, and physically mirror or acknowledge its core essence.
5. Have Player B immediately build upon that received offer by sending back their own subtle non-verbal response, filtered through their own secret agenda.
6. Continue this continuous loop of non-verbal 'pulses' and 'echoes,' keeping spoken dialogue to an absolute minimum, using only single words or short, reactive phrases when absolutely necessary.
7. Focus the scene entirely on the evolving emotional landscape and status dynamic between the two characters rather than trying to advance an external plot.
8. End the scene after three to five minutes, once a clear, unspoken shift in the relationship has occurred.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't pantomime or act out your agenda. Let it live in your breath, your posture, and where you place your gaze.'
- Common Pitfall: Players intellectualizing the exercise and freezing up. Fix: Encourage them to focus entirely on their partner's physical presence and let their body react instinctively before their brain can plan.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Receive first, then influence. Do not just push your agenda; let your partner's state change you first.'
- Common Pitfall: Moving into heavy verbal dialogue to explain the relationship. Fix: Remind players that words are a last resort; if they must speak, limit them to single-word acknowledgments or sighs.

## Variations
- Pure Silence: Run the entire exercise with absolutely zero verbalization or vocalization, relying entirely on physical presence and micro-expressions.
- The Verbal Filter: Allow players to speak in full sentences, but they must maintain the subtextual agenda, ensuring their physical status work completely contradicts or deepens their spoken words.
- Group Pulse: Expand the exercise to three or four players, where each player has a secret agenda targeting a different person in the group, creating a complex web of unspoken tension.

## Debrief
- What did you perceive your partner's secret agenda to be, and what physical cues led you to that conclusion?
- How did it feel to balance your own secret agenda while genuinely accepting and responding to your partner's offers?
- At what point did you feel a genuine shift in the status or emotional connection between your characters?
- How does focusing on micro-expressions and breath change the way you support your partner in a standard, verbal scene?

## Safety & Inclusion
Because this game requires close physical proximity and sustained eye contact, establish a clear boundary before starting. Players may opt out of direct eye contact by focusing on their partner's forehead or shoulder, and they should agree on physical touch boundaries (defaulting to no touch unless explicitly negotiated).

## Why It Works
This game works because it strips away the distraction of plot-building, forcing players to practice Meisner-style moment-to-moment responsiveness. By focusing on secret agendas, players must treat every micro-movement from their partner as a brilliant, intentional offer (making their partner a genius). This builds deep empathy and teaches players that status and relationship are built through physical attunement rather than dialogue.

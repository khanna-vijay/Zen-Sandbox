# Sixty-Second Biography

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Share your life in one minute, then hear it lovingly gifted back to you.

## Overview
In this paired exercise, one player shares their life story for a continuous minute while their partner listens in absolute silence. Afterward, the listener reconstructs the narrative, highlighting and celebrating the details they remembered to make their partner feel deeply heard and valued.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Vulnerability
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Single-Partner Empathy & Mirroring; Unfiltered Spontaneity
- **Focus:** connection

**Objective:** Develops deep active listening, empathy, and the ability to make one's partner look brilliant by reflecting their narrative with care and accuracy.

## Setup
Players stand or sit in pairs facing each other in a shared space. No props or special staging are required. The facilitator will need a timer.

## How to Play
1. Divide the group into pairs and designate one person as Player A and the other as Player B.
2. Instruct Player A that they will have exactly sixty seconds to share their life story, starting from their earliest memory up to the present day.
3. Instruct Player B to listen in silence, maintaining comfortable eye contact and open body language, without interrupting, asking questions, or offering verbal feedback.
4. Start the timer and have Player A speak continuously for the full minute, embracing whatever details, feelings, or tangents arise.
5. Once the minute is up, instruct Player B to immediately retell Player A's story back to them, aiming to capture as many details, emotional beats, and specific facts as they can remember.
6. Emphasize that Player B's goal is not to test their own memory, but to make Player A feel deeply understood, celebrated, and valued.
7. Swap roles so Player B shares their life story for sixty seconds while Player A listens and then retells it.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching cue: 'Listen to understand, not to reply or memorize.'
- Pitfall: Listeners trying to memorize a list of facts, which makes them look tense and disconnected. Fix: Remind them to listen for the emotional arc and the essence of the person, letting the details land naturally.
- Pitfall: Speakers running out of things to say and stopping early. Fix: Encourage them to fill the silence with whatever comes to mind, even if it is small details or how they feel in the present moment.
- Coaching cue: 'When retelling, treat your partner's story like a precious gift. Elevate their experiences.'

## Variations
- The Emotional Mirror: The listener retells the story focusing entirely on the emotional shifts and feelings expressed, rather than the chronological facts.
- The Third-Person Myth: The listener retells the story to the wider group as an epic, heroic biography, framing their partner as a legendary figure.
- The Sensory Focus: The speaker focuses only on sensory memories (smells, sounds, textures) from their life, and the listener mirrors those sensory details back.

## Debrief
- How did it feel to be listened to for a full minute without any interruptions or verbal agreements?
- What did your partner remember or highlight that surprised you or made you feel valued?
- How does letting go of the need to plan your response change how you receive information?

## Safety & Inclusion
While this is a low-physicality game, sharing personal history can bring up vulnerability or sensitive memories. Remind players that they are in complete control of their narrative; they can share fictionalized elements, focus on mundane details, or set boundaries on how deep they want to go.

## Why It Works
By removing the pressure to respond or steer the conversation, the listener's cognitive load is freed up to truly absorb the speaker. The act of retelling the story back to the speaker acts as a powerful validation tool, embodying the principle of making your partner look like a genius by treating their spontaneous words as valuable, memorable scripture.

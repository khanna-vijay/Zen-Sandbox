# The Platform Tilt

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Establish a stable behavioral routine, then disrupt it with a justified, narrative-shifting choice.

## Overview
A structured narrative exercise where players establish a predictable behavioral pattern (the platform) before introducing a sudden, justified disruption (the tilt). This shift redefines the scene's reality and propels the story toward a compelling climax. It trains improvisers to balance stability with dynamic change.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D3 — The Scene
- **Principle(s):** Serve the Story; Show, Don't Tell; Yes, And
- **Skill(s):** Narrative Architecture; Raising the Stakes; World-Building; Game Identification; Justification; Active Listening
- **Technique(s):** Platform/Tilt; Stakes-escalation reps; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where); Finding & Playing the Game
- **Focus:** narrative

**Objective:** To develop the ability to construct a solid narrative foundation and execute a purposeful, justified disruption that advances the story without breaking reality.

## Setup
Conducted in a virtual meeting space. Two active players have their cameras turned on, while the rest of the group keeps their cameras off to act as the active audience. No physical props are required.

## How to Play
1. Select two active players to turn their cameras on, while all other participants turn their cameras off to focus attention.
2. Obtain a simple suggestion of a mundane location or a relationship between two people to initiate the scene.
3. The active players begin the scene with the goal of establishing a clear, repetitive behavioral pattern or routine (the platform) within the first three exchanges.
4. Once the pattern is established and repeated at least twice, the facilitator calls out 'Tilt!' or 'Pivot!' via their microphone.
5. The player currently speaking or acting must immediately introduce a new, justified piece of information or a character choice that disrupts the established routine.
6. This disruption must build logically from the existing scene details rather than being a random, external shock.
7. The players must accept this new reality and play out the consequences, driving the scene toward a natural resolution under the altered dynamic.
8. Rotate players by having the active pair turn their cameras off and a new pair turn their cameras on for the next round.

## Facilitation Notes
- Coaching Cue: If the initial pattern is weak, side-coach with: 'Find a repetitive behavior or a shared rule you both agree on.'
- Pitfall: Players often introduce a completely random, unjustified tilt (e.g., declaring they are an alien). Fix: Remind them that the tilt must be a logical consequence or a hidden truth of the established platform.
- Coaching Cue: If players stall after the tilt, call out: 'Commit to the new reality—how does this change your immediate objective?'
- Pitfall: The platform phase goes on too long without a clear pattern. Fix: Intervene early to help them identify a simple, repeatable dynamic before calling the tilt.

## Variations
- Self-Directed Tilt: Instead of the facilitator calling the cue, the players must sense the right moment and initiate the tilt themselves once the pattern is fully established.
- The Double Tilt: Introduce a second, even higher-stakes disruption later in the scene to practice escalating consequences.
- Silent Platform: The initial routine must be established entirely through physical object work and non-verbal behavior before the verbal tilt occurs.

## Debrief
- How did establishing a clear routine make the eventual disruption feel more impactful?
- What clues in the platform phase made the tilt feel earned and justified rather than random?
- How did your character's objective shift once the pattern was broken?

## Safety & Inclusion
Since virtual play can sometimes feel disconnected, ensure players establish clear verbal agreement on physical boundaries if their characters are sharing an imagined close space. Encourage players to use self-care and step away from their cameras if any narrative theme becomes uncomfortable.

## Why It Works
By separating the scene into a distinct platform and tilt, players learn that narrative tension relies on contrast. A disruption only has power if there is a stable routine to break. Forcing the disruption to be justified prevents chaotic storytelling and teaches players to mine their established reality for dramatic gold.

# Solo Three-Line Scene

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Play both sides of a rapid-fire, three-line scene to bypass your inner editor instantly.

## Overview
A high-energy solo drill where a single performer rapidly improvises both characters in a complete three-line scene. By physically shifting between two distinct stage positions, the player must instantly react to their own previous line, burning through safe choices to access raw, unfiltered spontaneity.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D1 — The Self
- **Principle(s):** The First Thought Is a Gift; Commit 100%; Base Reality First
- **Skill(s):** Unfiltered Spontaneity; Physicality & Space Work; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** First Thought drills; Character Walks/Centers; C.R.O.W. (Character, Relationship, Objective, Where)
- **Focus:** skill_drill

**Objective:** To develop unfiltered spontaneity and trust in one's first thoughts. By forcing rapid-fire character shifts and immediate responses, players learn to bypass analytical hesitation, establish a clear base reality instantly, and commit fully to physical and vocal choices.

## Setup
A clear performance space where a single player can stand. The rest of the group acts as supportive observers, ready to offer a simple, one-word suggestion (like an object or location) to kick off each round.

## How to Play
1. The active player stands in the center of the playing space, establishing two distinct physical spots on the floor, one for Character A and one for Character B.
2. The facilitator or audience provides a simple, one-word suggestion to inspire the scene.
3. The player steps into Character A's spot, instantly adopts a physical posture, and delivers Line 1, establishing a clear relationship, emotion, or environment.
4. The player immediately steps into Character B's spot, absorbs the impact of Line 1, and delivers Line 2 as a direct, unfiltered reaction.
5. The player steps back into Character A's spot and delivers Line 3, bringing the micro-scene to a satisfying, committed resolution or escalation.
6. The entire sequence should take no more than 15 to 20 seconds, after which the player resets and immediately begins a brand-new three-line scene with different characters.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coaching cue: 'Don't think, just move!' Encourage players to physically step into the new character's space before they even know what they are going to say.
- Pitfall: The player plans both lines before starting. Fix: Have the facilitator call out 'Step!' to force the physical transition immediately after a line is spoken, leaving no time for mental rehearsal.
- Side-coaching cue: 'Let your body lead.' If a player gets stuck, tell them to change their posture, facial expression, or vocal register first, and let the words follow the physical choice.
- Pitfall: Delivering lines to the audience instead of the imaginary partner. Fix: Remind the player to maintain eye contact with the empty space where their partner 'stands' to ground the base reality.

## Variations
- Physical Catalyst: Instead of a verbal suggestion, the player must start with a distinct physical gesture or object work, which dictates the first line.
- Emotional Flip: Character B must feel the exact opposite emotion of Character A, forcing an immediate, high-contrast shift.
- The Gauntlet: The player does five three-line scenes back-to-back without stopping, forcing them to exhaust their stock characters and discover new ones.

## Debrief
- How did the physical act of stepping from one spot to another affect your ability to find a new voice or perspective?
- At what point did you run out of your 'go-to' stock characters, and what happened to your creativity once you did?
- How does reacting to your own line differ from reacting to a scene partner's line?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards, as players will be stepping back and forth rapidly. For players with mobility considerations, the physical 'stepping' can be replaced by a simple turn of the head, a shift in seated posture, or a change in vocal tone.

## Why It Works
This drill works because physical movement bypasses the cognitive filter. By demanding a three-line limit and rapid physical transitions, the brain is forced to rely on muscle memory and immediate instinct rather than premeditated jokes or safe choices. It teaches players that their first physical or vocal impulse is a gift that can instantly build a rich base reality.

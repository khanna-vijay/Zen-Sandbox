# Household Olympiad

<div class="game-hero-ph" markdown>🎨 *Infographic coming soon*</div>

> Turn mundane chores into high-stakes athletic drama through slow-motion physical comedy and play-by-play commentary.

## Overview
Two physical performers compete in a mundane household chore elevated to an elite Olympic sport, executed in hyper-dramatic slow motion. Meanwhile, two commentators sit at a broadcast desk, providing high-energy, play-by-play analysis that endows the physical players with complex backstories, strategic choices, and sudden obstacles. The magic lies in the symbiotic relationship between the physical players' slow-motion commitment and the commentators' rapid-fire narrative gifts.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell; Group Mind
- **Skill(s):** Physicality & Space Work; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; Heightening & Exploration; Pacing & Rhythm
- **Technique(s):** Object work; Weight & resistance mime; Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; The 'ladder' (escalating beats); Timing exercises
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To practice active gifting and endowment. Commentators gift physical players with specific strategies, physical traits, and emotional stakes, while physical players immediately accept and physicalize these gifts, making each other look brilliant.

## Setup
Four players step forward. Two act as the athletes and stand center-stage in a neutral starting position. The other two act as commentators and stand to the side. Ask the group or audience for a mundane household chore to serve as the event.

## How to Play
1. Divide the four players into two teams: two physical athletes on stage and two commentators at the broadcast desk.
2. Obtain a suggestion of a mundane household chore to serve as the Olympic event.
3. The commentators open the broadcast with high-energy sports-anchor personas, introducing the event, the stakes, and the two athletes, endowing them with names, countries, and rivalries.
4. The athletes begin in a neutral, focused stance, warming up physically in slow motion to match the commentators' introductory descriptions.
5. The commentators announce the start of the event, prompting the athletes to begin performing the chore in extreme slow motion.
6. As the athletes move, the commentators narrate their actions, framing ordinary movements as high-risk athletic maneuvers.
7. Commentators actively gift the athletes with challenges, injuries, or secret strategies, which the athletes must instantly physicalize and play out.
8. Athletes can initiate physical choices, such as attempting to sabotage the other competitor, which the commentators must instantly justify and explain to the audience.
9. The commentators build the segment to a dramatic climax, calling the photo finish and declaring a gold medalist based on the physical action.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the commentators to actively gift: 'Commentators, give them a specific physical struggle or a secret weapon!'
- Side-coach the athletes to stay in true slow motion: 'The slower you go, the more room you give your commentators to make you look amazing.'
- If commentators talk over each other, instruct them to adopt the classic play-by-play and color commentator dynamic, taking turns to build on each other's ideas.
- If athletes ignore a commentator's endowment, pause and remind them to immediately physicalize the gift, showing the audience the exact moment the information registers.

## Variations
- Instant Replay: The commentators can call for a slow-motion replay or rewind of a specific moment, forcing the athletes to physically reverse their movements and play them forward again.
- Equipment Failure: The commentators introduce an unexpected equipment malfunction, forcing the athlete to adapt their physical mime to this new constraint.
- Coach Interview: Introduce a fifth player who acts as a coach called in for a quick sideline interview mid-match, offering absurd strategic advice that the athlete must immediately implement.

## Debrief
- How did it feel as an athlete to have your movements instantly translated into high-stakes athletic strategy?
- Commentators, how did watching the physical details of the athletes' mime help you generate commentary?
- What happened when there was a perfect alignment between a commentator's gift and an athlete's physical response?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure the physical space is clear of tripping hazards, as players will be moving with their eyes focused on imaginary objects. Remind players that slow motion is an excellent tool for physical safety, preventing accidental collisions.

## Why It Works
This game is a perfect engine for active gifting and offer reception. The commentators' verbal endowments force the physical players to adapt their bodies to new narratives, while the physical players' slow-motion precision gives the commentators rich visual material to analyze. It builds a tight feedback loop of mutual support, making both pairs look like geniuses.

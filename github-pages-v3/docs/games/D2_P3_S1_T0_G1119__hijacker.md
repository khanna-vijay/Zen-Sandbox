# The Unwitting Hijacker

![The Unwitting Hijacker — infographic](img/D2_P3_S1_T0_G1119__hijacker.jpg){ .game-hero }

> A clueless hijacker must discover their vehicle, weapon, and demands through active listening and physical clues.

## Overview
One player steps out of the room while the group establishes a vehicle, an unusual weapon, and a bizarre demand. When the player returns as the hijacker, the other players must use physical endowment and verbal cues to help them deduce these three elements without spoiling the mystery. It is a high-energy exercise in active listening, offer reception, and making your partner look brilliant.

## What It Trains
- **Domain:** D2 — The Partner
- **Principle(s):** Yes, And; Make Your Partner a Genius; Show, Don't Tell
- **Skill(s):** Active Listening; Offer Reception; Active Gifting; World-Building
- **Technique(s):** Endowment-acceptance; Endowment-gifting drills; Endowment chains
- **Focus:** comedy_game

**Objective:** To develop deep active listening, physical endowment, and collaborative offer-gifting, training players to support their partner's discovery process rather than forcing information onto them.

## Setup
Four players are selected. One player (the Hijacker) leaves the room or covers their ears. The remaining players, guided by the facilitator, select three secrets: 1) A specific mode of transportation (e.g., a hot air balloon, a tandem bicycle), 2) An unusual object to be used as a weapon (e.g., a wet noodle, a feather duster), and 3) A bizarre demand or destination (e.g., to fly to the moon, to get a free vegan donut). The remaining three players position themselves in the performance space, miming the environment of the vehicle.

## How to Play
1. Send one player (the Hijacker) out of the room so they cannot hear the setup.
2. With the remaining players, establish the three secret elements: the vehicle, the hijacking tool, and the ultimate demand.
3. The three remaining players begin the scene on the vehicle, establishing the physical environment and movement of the craft through strong object work.
4. The Hijacker enters the scene, knowing they are the hijacker but completely unaware of the vehicle, their weapon, or their goal.
5. The passengers must react to the Hijacker's physical presence and vague threats, using physical endowment to hand them the invisible weapon and reacting to its specific properties.
6. The passengers drop verbal and physical clues about the vehicle's nature (such as swaying for a boat or complaining about altitude for a balloon) without explicitly naming it.
7. The Hijacker must closely observe the passengers' physical reactions and dialogue, adjusting their physical posture, weapon-holding, and demands based on these clues.
8. Once the Hijacker successfully identifies the vehicle, brandishes the correct weapon, and declares the correct demand, the scene reaches its climax and the facilitator calls scene.

## Facilitation Notes
- Side-coach the passengers to show, not tell. Instead of saying 'Is that a feather duster?', they should sneeze or giggle when the weapon is pointed at them.
- Encourage the Hijacker to make physical choices and try things out. If they pick up an invisible object, the passengers should immediately endow it with weight, shape, and danger.
- Pitfall: Passengers giving away the answers too quickly. Fix: Remind them that the fun is in the struggle and the gradual discovery; they should make their partner work for it but always keep them moving forward.
- Pitfall: The Hijacker getting stuck in their head. Fix: Side-coach them to do something physical or react to the passengers' body language.

## Variations
- Silent Hijack: The entire scene must be played in gibberish or complete silence, relying entirely on physical endowment and pantomime.
- The Helpful Hostage: One passenger is secretly trying to help the hijacker, while the other two are trying to subtly sabotage or confuse them, requiring even sharper listening.

## Debrief
- For the Hijacker: What physical cues from your partners were the most helpful in figuring out your weapon and vehicle?
- For the Passengers: How did you balance giving helpful clues without making it too easy or breaking the reality of the scene?
- How does making your partner a genius apply when you are withholding information from them for a game?

## Safety & Inclusion
Ensure that the weapon and hijacking elements are kept lighthearted, comedic, and non-violent. Encourage players to choose absurd, non-threatening objects (like a banana or a wet sponge) to keep the space safe and comfortable for everyone.

## Why It Works
This game works because it forces the clueless player to abandon pre-planned ideas and rely entirely on active listening and offer reception. Meanwhile, the other players must practice making their partner a genius by carefully calibrating their physical and verbal offers so the guesser feels clever and supported rather than interrogated.
